## Frameworks (business)

- **The Money Model** (2025) — "a deliberate sequence of offers… what you offer, when you
  offer, and how you offer it to make as much money as you can as fast as you can" (MM
  p. 194). Full framework in [[../wiki/topics/business/money-model|money-model]]. Four offer
  types across three stages: [[../wiki/topics/business/attraction-offers|Attraction]] →
  [[../wiki/topics/business/upsell-offers|Upsell]] +
  [[../wiki/topics/business/downsell-offers|Downsell]] →
  [[../wiki/topics/business/continuity-offers|Continuity]].
- **Payback speed beats profit ratio** (2025) — a profitable business still dies if payback
  is slow; get paid fast so cash never limits growth. "Get paid fast and grow as much as
  you darn well please." (MM p. 30)
- **The $100M standard** (2025) — make more profit from ONE customer than it costs to get
  and service MANY customers in the first 30 days; this "eliminates cash as a bottleneck for
  growth" (MM p. 205).
- **Build one stage at a time** (2025) — never implement a finished money model at once, "it
  will collapse on top of you"; measure in quarters, not weeks (MM pp. 195, 200).
- **Raise price until you make less money** (2025) — start offers cheap for feedback, then
  keep raising price to the point where the extra cash no longer offsets the lost yeses (MM
  p. 200).
- **"Simple Scales. Fancy Fails."** (2025) — "it's less about having 100 products to offer,
  and more about having 100 ways to offer your product" (MM p. 200).
- **The trilogy** (2025) — *$100M Offers* = what to sell; *$100M Leads* = how to find
  people; *$100M Money Models* = how to get them to buy (MM p. 204).

## Values

- **Ethics as a moat, not a constraint** (2025) — actually raise prices ("Don't lie"), give
  instant refunds ("Don't Take Blood Money"), let people win, obey tightening advertising
  law, and deliver what you sold, because "you can't file bankruptcy to erase a bad
  reputation" (MM pp. 36, 45, 73, 75).
- **"Hard Selling Is For Weak Products"** (2025) — don't convince people against their will;
  make the offer available at the moment of need. "It's a numbers game." (MM p. 35)
- **Think long** (2025) — "we don't get customers to make a sale, we make sales to get
  customers"; the real money is in customers who succeed and buy again (MM pp. 47–48).
- **Abundance** (2025) — "There's plenty of money. You just have to go get it." (MM p. 29);
  rich person mantra "how will I make this work for my business?" over the poor person
  mantra "this won't work for my business" (MM p. 34).
- **Confidence is earned, not affirmed** (2025) — "You become confident by giving yourself a
  stack of undeniable proof that you are who you say you are. Outwork your self-doubt." (MM
  p. 209)
- **Persistence as the only guarantee** (2025) — "You cannot lose if you do not quit";
  "Success is the only revenge"; "Be one of zero." (MM pp. 210–211)
- **Delegation / partnership** (2025) — "find someone who can build and lead the team that
  makes your vision a reality. When I did, I married her." (MM p. 195)
