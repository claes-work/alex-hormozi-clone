# Email Marketing

Alex Hormozi's email-marketing system, drawn from his solo masterclass
["Learn Email Marketing in 39 Minutes!"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pLhQOYMGa88)
(2024-11-06, source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]). He built the system
around his own newsletter — the **"Mozi Money Minute"** (the acquisition.com newsletter) —
which he launched only "not that many months ago" after avoiding email for the vast
majority of his career. His core framing: email is just another form of *content*, but
with **guaranteed delivery**, and it is one of the highest-ROI, most-ignored channels there
is. See [[marketing]] for the domain hub, [[copywriting-hacks]] for the copy checklist these
emails run through, and [[../content-strategy/content-engine]] for how email fits the wider
content machine.

> ⚠️ Self-reported: waiting so long to email was, on his own on-camera account, "one of the
> biggest mistakes I made in my career" — he says he didn't email Gym Launch until "2 or 3
> years in." Timeline claims are his, not registry-verified here
> (source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]).

## Why email (guaranteed delivery)

Email is content you *own* — the algorithm doesn't gatekeep who sees it, your own past
performance does (source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]):

> "imagine if there was a stock investment that you could 50x in a week — well that's kind
> of like what email is, and people still don't do it, myself included."

- **Same job as all content:** make them buy now, or make them more likely to buy later.
  The difference is delivery — the contact is already acquired, so email is essentially
  free follow-up. The only real cost is software plus whoever writes the emails.
- **Leverage:** *"one email writer can send an email to 10 million people, and that is a
  tremendous amount of leverage."*
- **Durable and universal:** he cites ~61% of people checking email multiple times a day
  (~20% multiple times an hour) and roughly uniform consumption across age brackets
  (15–24 up through 65+).
- **Just start.** Waiting was his self-described biggest mistake — *"it's just saying no to
  money."* Adding email follow-up on [Skool] produced a ~40% lift in conversion (his figure).

> ⚠️ Reported, not verified: the **35–45x average email ROI** ("for every dollar you spend
> you can get like $45 back") is a figure he relays "depending on the source," not an
> independently verified number. Same for the consumption stats above.

## List building & opt-ins

The opt-in is where segmentation data and the first reward loop are set
(source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]):

- **Platform-specific bonus.** Offer a strong lead magnet that *only* subscribers get — e.g.
  a "secret chapter" peeled out of a book. The magnet gets the email; the relationship keeps
  them.
- **Sell the ongoing communication, not just the magnet.** On the opt-in / thank-you step,
  resell *why to stay subscribed* first, then deliver the promised reward near the end.
- **Reply "yes" to get the bonus.** Having them reply does double duty: it's an engagement
  signal that boosts deliverability, and it trains the reward loop from message one.
- **Capture segmentation qualifiers at opt-in** (revenue tier, has-a-business, etc.) — and
  urge people to answer honestly, because those fields decide which email they get later.

## Deliverability

His mental model: there is **no silver bullet** — deliverability is "100 golden BBs,"
i.e. consistently sending emails people open, read, and click. *"Don't try and figure out
hacks — try and simply align what you do with the objective of the platform"* (deliver a
high-quality email experience) (source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]).

**Avoiding the promo tab:**

- Make emails look like plain personal emails — mostly text, ~1–2 links max, minimal or no
  images. More links/images/design = higher promo-tab rate.
- **Strip "money language"** from the body and subject line — the more money talk, the
  higher the promo-tab rate.
- **Get replies.** A reply signals engagement to the mail server (this is why the opt-in
  asks people to reply "yes").

**Sender reputation (protect the domain):**

- **Make unsubscribing easy** and don't keep an uninterested segment captive. Emailing
  dormant people for vanity volume lowers domain deliverability long-term and hurts the
  people who *do* want your emails: *"I don't want to keep sending emails to an uninterested
  segment of my list because it'll actually make my domain deliverability go down long
  term."* Unsubscribes aren't an insult — many of his workshop attendees had unsubscribed.
- **"Shaking the tree":** the first email after a long gap pulls churn forward — you get
  this month's and next month's churn at once. His highest-ever unsubscribe was on his very
  first email (*"just over 1%"*); it's now steady at *"like 0.42 unsubscribe rate per
  email,"* which is fine as long as the list grows faster than that.
- **Mobile optimization:** ~58–60% of traffic is mobile — check every send in both desktop
  and mobile views (especially with images) or click-through plummets.
- **Google-native links** (he calls it a "2011 advanced tactic"): links to Google-owned or
  Google-understood assets (YouTube, Google-Drive-hosted sales letters/VSLs) can earn higher
  deliverability and click-through — sometimes a "duct-taped" asset converts better.

> ⚠️ Reported, not verified: the B2B stat that **~16.99% of marketing emails never reach the
> inbox** is one he relays, not an independently verified figure.

## Copy: the reward loop

The organizing principle behind every email is behavioral: **behavior is driven by what
happens *after* the action, not before.** So reward every step, and the audience is trained
to keep taking the next one (source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]):

- **Reward the open** — a quote/tweet up top gives an immediate payoff for opening.
- **Reward the read** — the "meat" carries a genuinely valuable nugget.
- **Reward the click** — send people out to something worth their click, so they're trained
  to click again.

**The Mozi Money Minute template** (reuse it — *"think about McDonald's… I don't want any
surprises, I'm expecting a Big Mac… it's not the same burger but I want it to still be just
as good"*):

1. **Consistent subject brand** — recognizable so opens become a habit.
2. **A quote/tweet up top** — the reward-the-open payoff.
3. **The "meat," kept under ~200 words (~one minute)** — the reward-the-read nugget.
4. **CTA** — contextual to the content, never a generic copy-pasted mini-ad (this video's
   CTA was about email; beginner content → [Skool], high-level → scaling
   workshops/acquisition.com).
5. **PS statement** — *"beyond the headline it's the most read part of the email, so not
   having a PS statement is PS stupid, so have one."*

**Supporting copy tactics:**

- **Drive clicks, don't hoard them.** Don't dump a full blog into the email — put one
  interesting nugget and link out, training the click habit. (Too *many* links is still bad;
  regularly getting *a* click is the goal.)
- **Preview text.** ~24% of people read preview text before opening; if you don't set it,
  the client grabs the first 150 characters ("hey John…"). Pull a curiosity nugget forward —
  takes ~10 seconds.
- **A/B test subject lines** on a small percentage, then send the winner to the rest (same
  logic as YouTube thumbnail/title testing).
- **Pain-based, moment-specific hooks.** From reviewing 2,500+ Gym Launch testimonials, the
  top-4 hooks were all pain-based and about a *moment*: *"the moments are what make hooks,
  the details are what make them powerful."* Don't ask "how was life before" — ask *"for
  their worst moment."* (Overlaps with [[copywriting-hacks]].)

## Cadence & segmentation

**Cadence** (source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]):

- The best marketers told him **~3x/week is the sweet spot** — *"the more often you email
  the more you will convert,"* up to an annoyance ceiling that quality offsets.
- **Starting from zero? Begin once a month** and build up.
- Hormozi personally **refuses a fixed cadence** and uses a *quality* threshold instead:
  *"if I've got two good ones in me then two is what's going to go out… but if I've got zero
  it's zero."* He batch-wrote 24 emails in half a day.

**Segmentation** — his single biggest lever
(source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]):

> "it's way less about how good the email is and so much more about who's getting it."

- Send the right email to the right people, sorted by the qualifiers captured at opt-in
  (revenue tier, has-a-business, etc.).
- Segmentation is why honest opt-in answers matter, and why the list should be *easy to
  leave* — a clean, interested list segments better and deliverates better.
- **Context (not persona data):** [Leila Hormozi] sends longer, more story-driven emails to
  the most-qualified / highest-revenue segment; Alex's own style is shorter and more
  tactical. Both perform well — different segments, different email.

> ⚠️ Reported, not verified: the **~791% ROI increase from segmentation** is a **HubSpot**
> study he cites, not an independently verified figure.

## Metrics

The market has shifted from open rate to **click-through rate** — Apple privacy laws broke
open-rate tracking (source: [[../../sources/2024-11-06-yt-pLhQOYMGa88]]):

- **His last-3-months benchmark:** open rate ~35.7%, CTR ~8.5%.
- **Optimize CTR over a longer window,** not per-email — *"depositing before we withdraw."*
  Reward loops first, revenue later.
- **Send timing ("golden BBs"):** B2C best days Mon/Tue (~18% open lift); B2B best day
  Wednesday. Best times ~10am–noon and ~1–3pm (before/after lunch); first-thing-morning is
  too competitive with real business email. Timing matters most for single-time-zone / local
  businesses.

> ⚠️ Reported, not verified: the **day-of-week send-timing data is credited to Neil Patel**;
> the **~391% speed-to-contact** figure (call leads within 60 seconds of opt-in) is
> attributed to a **Harvard Business Review** study. Both are figures Hormozi relays, not
> independently verified. (Business-domain email *examples* — the annual-renewal-fee tactic,
> speed-to-contact, testimonial pain-hooks — belong to `business/` topics on a later pass.)
