# Book Launch Affiliate Playbook

How Acquisition.com runs a mass book launch through an affiliate army — reverse-engineered
from the [[../../sources/2025-08-16-affiliate-black-book|Affiliate Black Book]] for the
*$100M Money Models* launch (2025-08-16). This is Hormozi's distribution machine in
document form: the event is free, the book is the product, and the affiliates are the
paid-in-attention salesforce.

## Campaign architecture

- **Free live event as the conversion container.** The launch is a free virtual YouTube
  event; the book rides on it. Registration runs through per-affiliate "magic links",
  making every registration attributable.
- **Live-only scarcity.** The core bribe — a "secret project" 4 years in the making —
  is only granted to people who attend live ("Miss it, and you miss it forever").
  Scarcity is attached to *attendance*, not purchase; the sale comes later.
- **Curiosity gap as the master hook.** The giveaway is never named, only bracketed:
  "better than an NFT but less than a Bitcoin." Every channel repeats this one riddle.
- **Social proof stacking.** "Over 1 million people registered", "5 mystery headliners
  (some billionaires)" — scale claims do the qualifying.

## The affiliate incentive ladder

Prizes escalate from participation to access — access to Alex himself is the top prize:

| Milestone | Reward |
|---|---|
| 10 referrals | *70 Profit Tactics* audiobook + entry in Vegas-HQ-day drawing |
| Top 50 affiliates | Guaranteed day at the Vegas HQ, Q&A recorded for the affiliate's audience |
| Top 10 affiliates | 30-min fireside chat with Alex, live-streamed by the affiliate |

Design insight: the top rewards are **content opportunities for the affiliate** — a day
at HQ or a fireside chat becomes the affiliate's own video material. The incentive pays
in the same currency the affiliate trades in (audience attention), so promoting the
launch is also self-promotion. The mid-tier reward ("everyone who registered gets the
bonus content too") turns each affiliate's audience into co-beneficiaries — the
affiliate's followers *want* the affiliate to win.

## The countdown sequence (21/14/7/4/1/0)

Six emails, each with the identical promise stack (secret project + Money Models
training + mystery headliners + free), varied only by urgency frame and a prize-progress
update ("We're so close to hitting our first milestone!"). The "we" framing makes the
reader a teammate in the affiliate's prize hunt, not a target.

## Channel re-voicing

The same message is rewritten per channel intimacy: formal-ish Meta ads → casual friend
texts ("Hey real quick…") → team-framed Slack messages ("Found something that could help
our revenue goals") → personal-story organic posts. Two YouTube script outlines give
affiliates full 8–12-minute video structures with timestamped beats (problem → personal
story → concept explainer → reveal → tease → CTA).

## Reusable pattern

1. Make the launch itself a free event; gate the best bonus behind live attendance.
2. Give every promoter a tracked link and a public leaderboard with tiered prizes.
3. Pay top promoters in access-turned-content, not cash.
4. Ship done-for-you copy for every channel so promoting costs affiliates near zero effort.
5. One curiosity hook, repeated everywhere, never resolved before the event.
