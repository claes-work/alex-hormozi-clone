# Customer Acquisition Channels & Traffic

How Alex Hormozi thinks about getting customers: the taxonomies of channels /
activities, the financing math that makes acquisition self-funding, why word of
mouth is the only channel that compounds, the daily-volume rule for beginners, and
the naming formula that keeps a single offer marketable forever.

> **Framing note (read first).** Across his talks Hormozi has taught the "channels"
> question with *different counts* — "Big 4", "six sources", and "four ways" — and it
> is easy to mistake these for contradictions. They are not; they count *different
> things* and the framing evolved from 2021 → 2023. This page reconciles them in the
> section [Reconciling the counts](#reconciling-the-counts-big-4-vs-6-vs-4) below.

---

## The channels / activities taxonomy

### Six traffic sources — "never go hungry again" (2021–2022)

In the 2022 "Big 4" keynote, the *traffic* framework is a set of **six customer-
acquisition channels** he calls the "never go hungry again" list: **paid media,
earned media, owned media, outbound, affiliates & partners, and referrals.** He
self-reports having made over $20M from each except earned media, which he was only
just starting at the time [[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]].

The 2021 *$100M Offers* Q&A gives the same six with a cleaner internal split — three
**media** channels and three **people-based** channels:

- **Paid media** — advertising you buy.
- **Owned media** — your own list / audience.
- **Earned media** — organic platform distribution.
- **Manual outbound** — one-to-one reaching out (people-based).
- **Affiliates** — others promoting you (people-based).
- **Referrals / word-of-mouth** — customers selling for you (people-based).

His load-bearing claim about this list: **five of the six are *linear*** — a fixed
input/output ratio that usually diminishes at scale — **and only referrals /
word-of-mouth is *quadratic* (exponential)** [[../../sources/2021-10-25-yt-neTSqOAMgao]].

### The four ways to get customers — the "Core Four" (2023)

By the 2023 gym-owner keynote the taxonomy is compressed to **only four ways to get
customers**, defined by the *activity* rather than the media type:

1. **Warm outreach** (people who know you)
2. **Cold outreach** (people who don't)
3. **Paid ads**
4. **Content**

> "There's only four things that you can do: warm outreach, cold outreach, paid ads,
> content." [[../../sources/2023-06-07-yt-LGq0uVspNms]]

On top of the four activities he layers **leverage through other people** — customers
(referrals), affiliates, employees, and agencies. Each of these "does the four
activities on your behalf," but *acquiring* them still starts with you personally
doing warm/cold outreach, ads, or content [[../../sources/2023-06-07-yt-LGq0uVspNms]].

### Reconciling the counts (Big 4 vs. 6 vs. 4)

These are **not contradictory** — flag as an *evolving framing*, three different cuts
of the same subject:

| Framing | Count | What it actually enumerates | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| **"Big 4"** | 4 | Four *whole-business frameworks* — the math, the offer, the traffic, the money. Traffic is *one* of the four; it is **not** four channels. | [[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]] |
| **"Six sources"** | 6 | Six *traffic sources / channels* by **media + people** type (paid, owned, earned media; outbound, affiliates, referrals). | [[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]], [[../../sources/2021-10-25-yt-neTSqOAMgao]] |
| **"Four ways" (Core Four)** | 4 | Four *activities* you physically do to reach people (warm/cold outreach, paid ads, content), with "other people" (affiliates, employees, agencies, customers) as a leverage layer that does those four for you. | [[../../sources/2023-06-07-yt-LGq0uVspNms]] |

The key reconciliation: the **"Big 4" is not a channel count at all** — it names the
four frameworks of the whole business, of which "traffic" is one bucket. Within that
bucket, the **six sources (media-based, 2021–22) and the four ways (activity-based,
2023)** are two vintages of the *same* channel taxonomy. The six-source list mixes
*what activity* with *who does it* (affiliates/referrals are people, not activities);
the later Core Four cleanly separates the four **activities** from the **people**
(affiliates, employees, agencies, customers) who can perform them for you. Read as a
refinement: 2023 pulls "affiliates" and "referrals" out of the channel list and
re-files them under leverage, leaving four irreducible activities. In **every**
version, **referrals / word-of-mouth is singled out as the exponential one.**

## Client Financed Acquisition (the math that makes it self-funding)

The first of the 2022 keynote's "Big 4" frameworks is *the math*, which he calls
**Client Financed Acquisition** — acquiring customers at a profit *within 30 days* so
growth never needs outside capital.

**Core equation:** in the first 30 days you must generate more gross profit from one
customer than **the cost to acquire two customers plus the cost to fulfill both
(2×CAC + 2×COGS).** Clear that bar and growth becomes unlimited — constrained only by
operations, never by capital [[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]].

- **"30-day cash"** is the metric he tracks: gross profit extractable from a new
  customer in the first 30 days. He uses a **credit card as free financing** — as long
  as the money comes back plus more within 30 days, he never uses his own capital to
  grow (self-reported method) [[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]].
- A closely related earlier KPI: in the 2021 talk he tracked **cash a client
  generates in the first 14 days** (then averaging $15,500/client)
  [[../../sources/2021-10-25-yt-neTSqOAMgao]].

> "I basically know that I have 30 days against the clock that I can spend whatever I
> want, and as long as I have it back plus some in 30 days, I never have to use my own
> money to grow." [[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]]

> "If you can help people actually make the money to pay for your program rather than
> asking them to pay for the program and then get the result… there's no limit on the
> amount of money that you can make." [[../../sources/2021-10-25-yt-neTSqOAMgao]]

The strategic payoff ties acquisition to lifetime gross profit: whoever can make a
customer more valuable than competitors can **outspend everyone**, go deeper into
every channel, and effectively "create a monopoly" by bidding CPMs so high rivals are
priced out — "the arms race of business"
[[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]]. The 2023 keynote restates it plainly:
"If you print cash on the back end, you can get silly on the front end"
[[../../sources/2023-06-07-yt-LGq0uVspNms]]. See [[money-model]] and
[[business]] for the LTGP levers this depends on.

## Word of mouth: the only exponential channel

This is the most consistent theme across all his acquisition talks: of every channel,
**only word of mouth / referrals compounds.** Everything else returns a fixed ratio
per unit of spend or effort.

- **The math of it:** referrals are *quadratic* — "one tells two, two tell four, four
  tell eight." The other five channels are linear and usually diminish at scale
  [[../../sources/2021-10-25-yt-neTSqOAMgao]], [[../../sources/2023-07-16-yt-h2tokrQ4r-0]].
- **It runs whether you like it or not.** Word of mouth is *always* on, positive or
  negative; gurus "fizzle and pop" when the negative quadratic force (enough people
  telling enough people the product sucks) outpaces them
  [[../../sources/2021-10-25-yt-neTSqOAMgao]].
- **Why it matters more every year:** as you scale ad spend you reach colder, lower-
  trust, lower-fit audiences, so **CAC only goes up and conversion only goes down** —
  two forces working against you inevitably. Word of mouth is the one non-linear force
  that counteracts both [[../../sources/2023-07-16-yt-h2tokrQ4r-0]].

> "Word of mouth is the only one that is exponential in nature. One tells two, two tell
> four, four tell eight." [[../../sources/2023-07-16-yt-h2tokrQ4r-0]]

> "The only thing that is still viral is word of mouth. Everything else is linear in
> terms of the relationship." [[../../sources/2023-07-16-yt-h2tokrQ4r-0]]

> "Of those six… five of them are linear in nature… with referrals word of mouth it is
> quadratic which means it's exponential… the hardest one to achieve but once you
> achieve it it is the one that will pay you so handsomely." [[../../sources/2021-10-25-yt-neTSqOAMgao]]

**Two paths to an enduring business** (his tidy binary): "sell stuff that people never
stop buying from you, or build a company where people never stop selling for you"
[[../../sources/2023-07-16-yt-h2tokrQ4r-0]].

**Practical referral tactics:**

- **Referral "golden ratio":** get the % of customers who refer to *exceed* the %
  who churn, and growth becomes limitless with nothing else. He self-reports ~30% of
  customers still come from referrals [[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]].
- **Ask at the "activation point"** — the customer's first magical moment — because the
  favor-doer disproportionately feels goodwill (cites the Ben Franklin effect)
  [[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]].
- **The "marketing gods" thought experiment** he runs on portfolio CEOs: imagine all
  marketing is banned and every customer deleted except one, and every future customer
  must come from that one — how do you treat that customer?
  [[../../sources/2023-07-16-yt-h2tokrQ4r-0]]
- Fix the product first: truly exceptional products compound via referrals; a mediocre
  one just "lets everyone know you're mediocre" if you market it
  [[../../sources/2023-06-07-yt-LGq0uVspNms]].

## The Rule of 100 (daily volume for beginners)

Attached to the 2023 Core Four, the **Rule of 100** is his prescription for *quantity
of action* — pick one of the four activities and hit 100 units of it every day:

- **$100/day** on ads, **or**
- **100** cold outreaches, **or**
- **100** warm outreaches, **or**
- **100 minutes** of content.

Do it every day [[../../sources/2023-06-07-yt-LGq0uVspNms]].

> "You either spend 100 bucks a day on ads, you do 100 cold outreaches, you do 100 warm
> outreaches, or you do 100 minutes of content. You do it every day."
> [[../../sources/2023-06-07-yt-LGq0uVspNms]]

This pairs with his broader beginner advice — **one product, one avatar, one channel**
until ~$3M revenue, because "the issue is not even time, the issue is attention"
[[../../sources/2021-10-25-yt-neTSqOAMgao]].

## MAGIC naming (keeping one offer marketable forever)

From the *offer* framework in the 2022 keynote, the **MAGIC naming formula** is how he
re-skins a single program so marketing stays evergreen without changing operations:

- **M** — Magnetic reason why
- **A** — Announce the avatar
- **G / I / C** — a time interval + "container" words (blueprint, jumpstart, sprint,
  accelerator, challenge, detox)

The same underlying program can be renamed across the calendar — *Fall 6-Week
Challenge → Spring 28-Day Blueprint → Halloween 42-Day Detox* — so the offer feels
fresh every season while the fulfillment behind it never changes
[[../../sources/2022-05-17-yt-XwZH-lOKG9c]]. It sits alongside his other offer
components (promotion, core offer, bonuses, scarcity, urgency, guarantees); see
[[attraction-offers]] for the full offer stack.

## Related context: what makes a channel worth pursuing

Acquisition economics are downstream of *what kind of business you're in*. In a 2024
clip Hormozi argues the best businesses have **low capital expense, high gross margin,
and pricing power** — because high margin is what funds aggressive acquisition in the
first place:

- Test for low capex: can you produce **10× more units without materially changing the
  cost basis?** Services, digital, and (mostly) software pass; inventory /
  manufacturing / heavy-equipment businesses fail [[../../sources/2024-02-19-yt-qku04Qv9fv0]].
- Rule of thumb for a service business: **gross margins above 80% — i.e. charge at
  least 5× cost of goods.** Little margin makes both hiring the best people and
  investing in marketing very hard [[../../sources/2024-02-19-yt-qku04Qv9fv0]].

The chain he draws: **niche down → specific avatar → productize → efficiency → margin
→ money to fund the best people and marketing** [[../../sources/2024-02-19-yt-qku04Qv9fv0]].
This is why he can afford Client Financed Acquisition and deep channel investment at
all — high margin is the enabling condition. See [[business]] and
[[../wealth/wealth]].
