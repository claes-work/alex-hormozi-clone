# Acquisition.com

The Hormozis' family office / holding company — founded with his wife
[[leila-hormozi|Leila]] "to invest in businesses we know how to grow". Publisher and
distribution engine of the $100M book series. Facts self-reported unless noted.

## Facts

- Portfolio revenue: over $200M/year at time of writing (2025), spanning brick & mortar
  chains, software, services, and ecommerce
  ([[../sources/2025-08-16-100m-money-models|$100M Money Models]], PDF p. 21).
- Investment criterion: works with companies over $1M EBITDA (PDF p. 213).
- Legal: Acquisition.com®, its logo, and $100M® are registered trademarks of Bumble IP,
  LLC; the book is copyright 2025 Bumble IP, LLC, distributed by Acquisition.com, LLC
  (PDF p. 8).
- Publishing/media arm: free trainings (Acquisition.com/training/money), free chapter
  "Your First Avatar" (/avatar), podcast (top 5 entrepreneurship / top 15 business in
  the US at time of writing, self-reported), daily short-form content (/media), hiring
  from "#mozination" (/careers/open-jobs) (PDF pp. 212–214).
- Runs mass book launches through an affiliate program — see
  [[../topics/marketing/book-launch-affiliate-playbook|book-launch-affiliate-playbook]]
  ([[../sources/2025-08-16-affiliate-black-book|Affiliate Black Book]], 2025).
- None of his businesses — including Acquisition.com — started with a "finished" money
  model (PDF p. 195).

## Business model / flywheel (2026-04)

Alex's first-person map of how Acquisition.com / ACQ makes money, framed as a flywheel
that turns "raw attention into money" and redeploys the cash. All figures below are
**self-reported** (not registry-verified); Alex notes advisory tier names/prices are
"subject to change"
([[../sources/2026-04-17-yt-hHkdbr6_JJs|How Acquisition.com Makes Money]], 2026-04).

- **Scale & HQ:** described as a "$250 million plus portfolio" (self-reported), HQ in
  **Las Vegas**. Monetization sweet-spot avatar: business owners doing roughly
  **$500K–$50M revenue** (sub-$500K eyeballs monetized diffusely via ad revenue, low-ticket
  products, and book/Audible margins, with the goal of ascending them into the sweet spot).
- **The flywheel (value ladder):**
  1. **Media (top of funnel):** content, on-site courses, podcasts, YouTube — produced
     daily with money, effort, and team.
  2. **Conversion (minor conversions):** the **Mosey Minute** email list (free, itself
     "a product in of itself"); **Skool** (~$9/mo); and **books** (~$30 on-site or Amazon).
     Framed as "a free trial into a $9 thing," "a $30 book," "a free email list."
  3. **Advisory Practice (AP):** in-person workshops at the Las Vegas HQ (associates,
     analysts, directors). Three ascending tiers — **L1 ≈ $5,000**, **L2 ≈ $35,000**,
     **L3 ≈ $135,000** (L3 = more detailed help / access to Alex). **Almost all advisory
     conversions happen over the phone** via a phone sales team.
  4. **Cash redeployment:** profits pushed into the investment arms below.
- **Investment arms:**
  - **ACQ RE (real estate):** buys properties, typically **multi-family**, via a
    partnership through **Arc**; raises funds for deals and can solicit the audience to
    co-invest. Rationale: **tax-efficient** for the business.
  - **ACQ Ventures:** very small bets (**1–5%**) on unicorn-style tech companies, or
    seeding a company at onset to sell through ACQ's distribution. Also a career path for
    top AP operators to launch/run new ventures (insurance, lending, sales AI, etc.).
  - **Private equity:** larger stakes (**30–50%, up to 100%**) in businesses run
    independently, sometimes with ACQ talent inserted. Future PE targets companies that
    can leverage ACQ's distribution, vs. the "legacy portfolio."
- **ACQ Network (forward-looking, 2026):** a more scalable business-owner
  association/membership at roughly **$5,000–$10,000/year**, bundling an **AI business
  consultant** ("trained on all of this in-person experience"), a **community**, and
  industry-specific calls. Priced with enough barrier to stay business-owner-only. Also a
  **buying-group / negotiating moat**: the aggregate distribution base secures
  pre-negotiated agencies, payment providers, and vendor rates for "thousands of businesses
  at once," so accrued savings exceed the price ("no one would ever want to leave").
- **Skool:** named/co-founded **portfolio company** (~$9/mo); ACQ **holds equity and takes
  distributions** from it. Described as "the only big brand association made publicly."
- **Disney analogy:** media brand = Disney; **ACQ Network = Disney+**; **Advisory Practice
  = the parks** ("meeting Mickey" in B2B); **Skool = a franchise universe** at a low ~$9/mo
  barrier; RE + PE = other spokes.
- **The one premise:** free content must beat competitors' paid content, plus "absolute
  premium brand credibility" — the wedge, since high-credibility people mostly won't do the
  teaching/media work.

> ⚠️ Note: the 2026-04 "$250M+ portfolio" figure is a portfolio-valuation framing, distinct
> from the 2025 "over $200M/year" **portfolio revenue** figure in Facts above — different
> metrics, both self-reported; not a direct contradiction.
