# How Do I Handle "We're Already Working With Someone"?

## Summary

This is a multi-question Q&A. Alex answers the title objection by returning to the
prospect's dissatisfaction, then covers search-fund criteria, investing $10K into
active-income skills, apprenticeships, sales-process VSLs, outbound channels, AI in
language education, and how he would open a gym in 2026.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-06)

- Alex says the response to "we're already working with someone" is to ask why they are
  on the call if they are satisfied.
- He self-reports using this frame when a gym prospect said she already had a personal
  trainer.
- He says a first-time business buyer should look for high revenue retention.
- He says a buyer should prefer a word-of-mouth business over one run by a good
  marketer, because goodwill is more likely to survive the handoff.
- He says $10K should be used to learn a skill that can increase active income, not as
  an investment expecting a 10x financial return.
- He recommends apprenticeships in small or family-owned businesses because the learner
  can taste many functions.
- He says if prospects ghost after seeming interested, the seller may be avoiding the
  close and missing pre-sale framing.
- He recommends VSLs before calls to frame the seller, conversation, proof, and buying
  decision.
- He says high-volume outbound usually uses email and calls.
- He says a language-course owner should use AI rather than fear AI.
- He says he would open a gym in 2026 with a small semi-private model for older
  longevity-focused clients, around 100 members at roughly $400/month.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Then why are you here?"

> "The sale begins at the moment of interest."
