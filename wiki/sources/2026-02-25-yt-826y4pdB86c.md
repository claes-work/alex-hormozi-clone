# The Only Metric That Matters for Short-Form Content

## Summary

In this group Q&A (a Skool-community setting with founder Sam Ovens present), Alex
Hormozi argues that the single most predictive metric for short-form content that drives
sales is **saves** — not shares, not likes, not views. He cites a TikTok Shop
meta-analysis that reportedly found saves to be the number-one signal correlated with
purchase intent. He explains the psychology (you only save what you don't already know
and expect to need later), gives a practical content formula for producing save-worthy
posts, and connects saves to trust and eventual buying. The conversation drifts into a
tangent about whether Skool's "Discovery" should add a save/wish-list feature, plus
Alex's aside on learning from creators in other niches and from the smallest business
owners. The video ends with a promo for the free acquisition.com/roadmap 10-stage
scaling roadmap.

★ **L3-candidate** — introduces a specific, sourced, named content metric ("saves =
purchase intent," per a TikTok Shop meta-analysis) plus a repeatable content formula

## Key claims

All claims Alex-attributed, paraphrased unless quoted, dated 2026-02-25:

- If you had **one metric to optimize short-form content toward, it should be saves.** (2026-02-25)
- TikTok Shop, motivated to identify which content actually creates sales, ran a large
  meta-analysis and found the **number-one metric correlated with purchase intent was
  saves — not shares, and not likes.** (2026-02-25)
- Shares and likes serve the platform's objective (views, time-on-platform) but do not
  indicate buying intent: a funny meme you send to 10 people racks up views yet does not
  make you more likely to buy from whoever made it. (2026-02-25)
- **You only save content you don't already know** and expect to need later (e.g. "11
  steps to fix X"). Therefore save-worthy content **cannot be obvious** — if the advice
  were the surface-level answer, people would already have it. (2026-02-25)
- The value is in the **layers underneath the obvious answer**: what ChatGPT / the first
  layer would tell you is not save-worthy; demonstrating you've spent real time with the
  problem across many clients ("why isn't the obvious answer working?") is the next
  level. (2026-02-25)
- Practical content formula: think of **content as removing friction from people's
  lives** — "give the normal advice and then respond to your own advice with why people
  struggle with that." That gap is the content. (2026-02-25)
- Saves signal **trust and accumulation → purchase**: among many creators you consume,
  the one whose material you repeatedly felt compelled to save/"hoard" is the one you
  trust most and are most likely to buy from when you eventually need to act. (2026-02-25)
- Caveat on the metric: many people save content (and products) but **never look back at
  their saved items** — a reason a platform might hesitate to add a save feature, even
  though the save action itself still reveals intent. (2026-02-25)
- Buying is tied to cash cycles: **purchase spikes appear after the average payday**
  because most buyers in this space have jobs and paydays. (2026-02-25)
- Aside (content sourcing): you get **the best insights from people in a completely
  different niche**, and it's foolish to disregard creators who make less than you — the
  person with the least money is often the scrappiest and most creative, and Alex says he
  learned some of his best tactics from the smallest business owners. (2026-02-25)
- Aside (personal practice): Alex is an **"ads collector"** — he saves ads he likes for
  their angles as a swipe file. (2026-02-25)
- Promo: acquisition.com/roadmap offers a free **10-stage roadmap from $0 to $100M+**
  (which "less than 1% of companies finish"), broken down by eight business functions
  with the constraint/symptoms and steps to graduate each stage. (2026-02-25)
