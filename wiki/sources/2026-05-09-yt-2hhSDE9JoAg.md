# How to Take 100 Primary Actions to Reach Your Goal

## Summary

Alex explains the Rule of 100 as doing 100 primary actions every day until a goal is
reached. He applies it to outreach, content minutes, or ad spend, and says behavior
change is the test of whether learning occurred. He recommends using a timer and
judging the day by effort rather than by whether the outcome has arrived yet.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says the Rule of 100 means taking 100 primary actions every day until the goal
  is reached.
- He gives examples such as 100 reach-outs, 100 minutes of content, or $100 per day in
  ad spend.
- He defines learning as changing behavior under the same conditions.
- He says if behavior does not change, nothing has been learned in practice.
- He recommends using a kitchen timer and pausing it when distracted.
- He says the best morning routine is the one that gets a person to work fastest.
- He recommends redefining winning by level of effort and whether anything was left in
  the tank.
