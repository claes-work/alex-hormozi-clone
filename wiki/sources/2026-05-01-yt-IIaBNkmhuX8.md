# Adding More Content Increased His Churn

## Summary

Alex frames a community/newsletter business around four levers: traffic, conversion,
pricing, and churn. He tells a story about a high-ticket print newsletter whose churn was
lowest when the deliverable had only two parts: one monthly call and one newsletter. When
the owner added more calls, themed sessions, Q&A, and community elements, churn rose.
Alex's lesson is that retention often comes from clarity, simplicity, activation, and
consumption rather than adding more content.

## Key claims

- Alex identifies traffic, conversion, pricing, and churn as the main levers for this
  type of business.
- Pricing can often be picked once, and conversion can be improved then maintained; the
  main recurring inputs are front-end promotion and back-end churn reduction.
- A high-ticket newsletter example had its lowest churn with only a monthly call and a
  newsletter.
- Adding more deliverables increased churn in that example.
- Entrepreneurs may add complexity because they are bored, not because customers need it.
- Alex would rather increase the chance customers consume 100% of the offer than add more
  things for them to consume.
- If a founder's day does not include letting more people know about the offer, revenue
  will eventually stop.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The more things he added, the more churn increased."

> "Reduction of churn is about simplicity and making thing and about activation."

> "I would rather increase the likelihood that they consume 100% of my stuff."
