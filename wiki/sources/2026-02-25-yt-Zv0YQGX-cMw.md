# How to Get Anything You Want

## Summary

In this short clip, Hormozi argues that most of what a person wants sits on the far
side of a few hard conversations they have been avoiding, and that people either grow
into their potential or repeat "the same six months of their life over and over."
He frames the core choice as a trade between short-term and long-term pain, coining a
short vocabulary — comfort/fear as short pain or gain, regret/fulfillment as long pain
or gain — and urges trading short pain for long gain. The clip closes with a plug for
his free 10-stage "zero to 100 million" scaling roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap.
It is delivered solo by Hormozi.

## Key claims

- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) Everything you want is on the other side of a few hard
  conversations you have been putting off.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) People either grow into their potential or keep reliving
  the same six months of their life; the difference is how many hard conversations
  you are willing to have and how fast you have them once you realize you need to.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) He personally struggled with hard conversations for a long
  time — he says he traveled across the country before telling his dad he had left.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) If uncomfortable conversations seem hard, the result of not
  having them will be harder.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) The underlying struggle is short-term pain versus long-term
  pain; long-term pain is regret. Comfort is short gain, regret is long pain, fear is
  short pain, fulfillment is long gain. The goal is to trade short pains for long
  gains, not short gains for long pains.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) Choosing the "safe bet" of avoidance is not actually safe —
  it is a guaranteed loss, just deferred.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) He offers a free 10-stage roadmap from zero to $100M+ that
  fewer than 1% of companies complete, broken down across eight business functions —
  what each constraint feels like, its symptoms, and the steps taken to graduate — and
  says he has done it multiple times across software, physical products, service, and
  brick-and-mortar businesses. Available at acquisition.com/roadmap.
