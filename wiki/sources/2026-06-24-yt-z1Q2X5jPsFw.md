# Hire the Influencer, Not the Technician

## Summary

This clip is a Hormozi Hotline call handled by Leila Hormozi while Alex is off-camera. Leila advises a gym/PT business owner who is trying to replace herself with an operator. Leila says the operator should be a people operator first: someone kind but tough, organized, and strong at sales/influence, not merely a technical project-management person. ## Key claims (Leila's advice only; not Alex persona material)

- An operator is often a people operator before being a process operator.
- Influence, leadership, management, and sales ability are higher-leverage for the top operator than technical project-management skill.
- A founder should hire someone who approximates the founder's high-leverage skills if the goal is to take those responsibilities off the founder's plate.
- Hiring the opposite personality can solve a different problem while leaving the founder's actual workload intact.
- At some scale, the founder must decide that the business can no longer scale on the founder's leadership and influence alone.
- A useful operator profile is kind but tough, organized, and equipped with sales skills.
