# Our CAC Is $6,700 and We Don't Know What's Working

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches the same buyers-advisory acquisition problem captured in `yt-RRFXliltxFM`: a property-investor advisory company has meaningful revenue and profit, but unclear channel attribution across PR, organic content, Facebook, YouTube, and other touches. Alex recommends doubling what already works if the business can afford it, then using qualitative first-touch data and a first-touch/last-touch blend to allocate future resources. He also gives a short operating lesson on duplicating top performers by converting observed differences into trainable behaviors.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Imperfect attribution should not stop growth when the current acquisition mix is affordable and appears to work in aggregate.
- Ask customers where they first heard about the business to get directional, qualitative channel signal.
- Channel ROI can be deceptive because assets closest to conversion tend to look best.
- Scientific/blended attribution can use last touch for recent conversion events and first touch for longer journeys.
- Keep middle touchpoints in place for complex services because they may be doing trust and conversion work.
- Top-performer duplication starts with studying what the best people actually do and say differently.
- Behavior standards can raise the team; failure to adhere may reveal work ethic or adherence issues rather than missing knowledge.
