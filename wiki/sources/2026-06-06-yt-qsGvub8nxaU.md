# "My Spiritual Coaching Offer Isn't Converting. Help."

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a spiritual coaching business doing about $500K top line and $300K bottom line. The owner says the offer feels too ethereal. Alex translates the outcome into observable behavior change, recommends removing vague language, adding sales calls to ascend $97 buyers into higher-ticket offers, and using a win-your-money-back offer with store credit, testimonial/promotion requirements, and simpler mechanics.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Spiritual or abstract offers should be translated into observable behaviors the buyer wants to stop or start.
- Marketing should use lowest-common-denominator language so the largest number of people can understand and comply.
- A confused buyer stalls; clarity can increase conversion across ads, funnels, emails, and sales calls.
- A low-ticket buyer list can be monetized by calling buyers and selling a higher-ticket transformation.
- A win-your-money-back offer can work well for behavior change if the refund is store credit toward an upsell.
- The required behaviors in a win-your-money-back offer should include both activation behaviors and advertising/proof behaviors.
- Combining too many attraction mechanisms reduces clarity; one clean mechanism is stronger.
