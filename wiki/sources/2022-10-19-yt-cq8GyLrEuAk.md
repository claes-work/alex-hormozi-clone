# 14 Life Lessons I Wish I Knew Earlier (2022-10-19)

## Summary

A compilation video of life lessons Hormozi says he wishes he'd learned after
college (titled 14; he numbers them 1–14, opening "13 lessons"). The through-line
is compounding, singular focus, ego-suppression, and seeing reality more clearly.
Several lessons restate his recurring frameworks (audience/Goodwill compounds
faster than money; extraordinary = ordinary things for extraordinary periods;
beliefs as an accurate view of reality), while others are sharper standalone
mindset material on humility, status, negotiation, and the failure→learning cycle.

## Key claims

- **Assume you're the dumbest in the room** — you can't learn while talking, and
  letting others edify you positions you higher than self-promotion ever could.
- **Goodwill compounds faster than money**, is audience-based, and grows tax-free;
  you can convert it to money later at a moment of your choosing. Reputation and
  respect must be earned by first respecting yourself and giving to the group.
- **Extraordinary results come from ordinary actions done for extraordinary
  periods** — the commitment to the action, not the action itself, is what
  compounds (workout reps for 23 years; one at-bat is nothing).
- **Negotiate everything except your values.** Nearly everything is negotiable;
  it's not zero-sum. Balance four points: your need today, your need in 10 years,
  their need today, their need in 10 years.
- **Humility = increasing your regard for others, not decreasing regard for self**
  (attributed to Clayton Christensen). You gain status by giving/sacrificing more
  for the group than you take.
- **The entrepreneurial cycle is cyclical, not a destination:** failure → learning
  → success → complacency → failure; the goal is to skip complacency and return to
  learning→success fast. He'd **pay any amount to see obvious truths / true
  reality** ("ignorance tax / ignorance debt").
