# You Need to Work 100x Harder

## Summary

In an interview-style segment, Alex Hormozi explains why extreme effort can create disproportionate outcomes. Although work can have diminishing absolute returns, the relative payoff for being best can be exponential. He says his standards have risen over time, and uses his own presentations and book drafts as examples: he now counts work in hundreds of hours and considers work done only when he no longer knows how to make it better.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The top end of work can create outsized rewards, even when the difference between first and second is small in absolute terms.
- From a relative perspective, being number one can produce exponential returns.
- People usually underestimate how much they can work until they work at a much higher standard.
- Counting in hundreds of hours changes the depth of understanding and quality of output.
- More time can change the whole approach, not just polish the first version.
- Alex considers work done when he no longer knows anything else he can do to improve it.
- He describes the Money Models book process as 19 drafts, four full rewrites, third-grade reading level, and 106 hand-drawn images.
