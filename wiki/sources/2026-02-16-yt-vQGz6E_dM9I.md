# "How to Get High-Paying Clients in 2026"

## Summary

Short clip in which Alex Hormozi describes how buyer behavior has shifted heading into 2026 and what it means for landing high-paying clients. His thesis: with more creators than ever and less trust than ever, buyers now consume far more content before purchasing and are much less likely to buy an expensive offer on first contact. Instead of the old "watch one video, hop on a call, pay $5–10k" motion, buyers first make a small, low-risk purchase (a book, a ~$29 product/community/subscription) to test the quality — and only then ascend to more expensive offers. He frames the modern funnel as top-of-funnel shorts → long-form → low-ticket front-end → a small ascending percentage (5–10%) climbing into higher-priced backends. What is publicly visible is the low-ticket subscription front-end; the bigger-profit businesses still carry premium-priced services or products on the backend. The clip ends with his standard promo for the free acquisition.com/roadmap.

Note: this restates his established value-ladder / ascension model and his "sell to the rich" / high-ticket-backend view, dated to the 2026 creator-economy context (declining trust + rising pre-purchase content consumption). No fresh named framework — treated as a trend-dated application of existing beliefs.

## Key claims (Alex, paraphrased; dated 2026-02-16 — trend-sensitive)

- **[2026-dated]** There have never been more creators and, simultaneously, never been less trust — so people trust others less and consume significantly more content before making a purchase.
- **[2026-dated]** First purchases now skew lower-ticket on average. The behavior of ~10 years ago (see one video, get on the phone, pay $5,000–$10,000) is significantly less likely today.
- **[2026-dated]** Buyers are far more likely to first grab a low-risk item — a book or a ~$29 offer — to try it and judge whether the product / community / subscription quality is what it's supposed to be worth, and only then pay for more expensive things.
- The publicly visible front-end is increasingly the (low-ticket) subscription; but many businesses that post bigger profit numbers still have backends that are more premiumly priced, whether in services, products, or otherwise.
- The modern funnel structure: top-of-funnel shorts → long-form content → low-ticket front-end offer → ascension, where roughly 5–10% (or "whatever percentage") ladder up into a higher-priced level.
- Implication for high-paying clients: you no longer win them cold on the front end — you earn them by proving quality on a small first purchase and ascending the subset who convert into premium backends.
- Closing promo (not substantive): free 10-stage zero-to-$100M+ roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap, broken down by 8 business functions (constraint, symptoms, steps to graduate), claimed applied across software, physical products, service, and brick-and-mortar businesses.
