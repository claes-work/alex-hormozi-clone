# Helping an Online Service Business Get to $100M

## Summary
Live coaching Q&A: the owner runs a fully digital business treating men's sexual/psychological dysfunctions (~$3.5M/yr, ~$800–900K profit, ~70% organic / 30% ads) via group online therapy and e-learning, and wants to go global to $100M. He assumes he needs a new business model; Hormozi says the model is fine — he needs a big brand and aspirational associations, and lays out why an affiliate / centers-of-influence strategy is the realistic path given the taboo nature of the niche.

## Key advice (Alex)
- You don't need a new business model — you need a new way of doing business (a big brand). The delivery vehicle may add AI/operating scale, but one-to-many consulting is an old, proven motion; pricing/packaging are easy wins.
- Brand is built through aspirational associations. A big celebrity endorsement (his Sydney Sweeney / Jessica Alba hypotheticals) would instantly create a $100M brand; the question is which handful of associations elevate perceived value and reach.
- Protect the customer-acquisition mix: keep the ~70/30 organic-to-ads ratio and let organic dictate ad spend — growing organic is the primary lever. He spells out the failure path if ignored: rising ad spend, compressing margins, more headache at the same profit, then bolt-on upsells that add complexity and burn you out.
- Celebrities actually have the weakest pull; go for micro-influencers, live-streamers, and long-form-audience creators — ideally people who quietly struggled with the same issue.
- Because it's a taboo, low-referral category (people won't tell friends, and few will want the brand association), the best route is centers of influence / affiliates: businesses that already serve this avatar for free under a different money model and can send those customers over.
