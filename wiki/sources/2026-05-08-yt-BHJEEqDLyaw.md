# The Question Every Employee Should Ask Their Boss

## Summary

Alex advises employees to connect their job directly to business revenue, then ask what
it would take to earn more by creating more value. He recommends framing the raise
conversation as a problem-solving exercise for the boss, and says smaller businesses
often offer more room to affect revenue than large corporations.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says employees should figure out how their job directly ties to revenue.
- He says if an employee does not know how the role makes money, they should ask.
- He says even customer service can tie to repeat purchases, fewer refunds, word of
  mouth, and future pricing power.
- He recommends asking, "What would it take for me to earn that?"
- He says the employee should make clear that they want to make the business more
  money and receive a small share of the increase.
- He notes that Leila makes more content than he does on raises and negotiation.
- He says some careers will not offer that opportunity, especially in large companies
  where employees can get lost.
- He says a name-brand company can provide a stamp of approval that can later be used
  to get a more impactful role in a smaller business.
- He says entrepreneurs are receptive to people who offer to help them make more money.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I will help you make more money."
