# I built an app and no one's using it

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the founder of a SaaS app for fence contractors doing about $500K/year. The app helps contractors show a prospective customer a fence in their own yard. The founder has high monthly churn, and Alex diagnoses an activation problem: users who sell a fence quickly with the app stick, while those who do not use it churn. Alex tells him not to scale ads until churn is fixed, to separate trial conversion from post-conversion churn, and to incentivize early activation with a free-trial-with-penalty or first-month-charge/second-month-rebate structure.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Do not scale ads while churn is high; otherwise the business pours customers out the back.
- If users stick after they use the product to sell a fence, activation is the core constraint.
- Churn metrics should separate trial churn/conversion from post-conversion churn; otherwise trial behavior pollutes the retention picture.
- The actual CAC should be calculated on paying customers, not trials.
- A free trial with penalty can be used as a front-end offer to force the behavior that predicts retention.
- Another option is to charge the first month up front and make the second month free or rebated if the customer uses the app a target number of times.
- For this product to become compelling, Alex says retention should be roughly 80%+ annually, which implies churn closer to 1-2% monthly.
- All effort and incentives should go toward activation until the customer experiences the product's value.
