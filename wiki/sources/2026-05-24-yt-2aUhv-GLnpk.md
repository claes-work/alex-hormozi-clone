# Manifestation vs Visualization: What's the Real Difference?

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says he dislikes "manifestation" because he has not heard it defined in an
observable way and because it can reward approval without action. He distinguishes it from
visualization, which may have evidence in sports practice and can add risk-free mental reps.
His core rule is to focus on observable behavior because reality changes only when someone
interacts with reality.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex rejects "manifestation" as he defines it: a word people say to get nods and approval
  without necessarily taking action.
- He asks for observable definitions and evidence before using a term.
- Visualization is different from manifestation and may be useful, especially in sports,
  because mental practice can add reps without joint wear.
- Visualization still does not change reality unless paired with action in reality.
- Alex focuses on observable behavior because it makes conversation clearer and predictions
  more accurate.
- He credits much of his content clarity to eliminating what is not observable.
- Undefined words let people nod at each other without changing behavior.
- He is obsessed with what changes behavior because changed behavior changes reality.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I just focus on the observable world."

> "They're not going to change reality until they interact with reality."
