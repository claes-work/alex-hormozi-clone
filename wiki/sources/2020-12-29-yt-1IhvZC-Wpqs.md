# Marketing Mastery - How To Get Your Ideal Clients To Come To You

## Summary

In this 2020 short, Hormozi argues that effective marketing sells people what
they *want* as the entry point in order to later deliver what they *need*. He
illustrates it with a parable he calls the "ham and the garlic" — the garlic is
the real (unpalatable) solution, the ham is the wrapper that gets it consumed —
and applies it to his own history in fitness and to client marketing.

## Key claims

- (2020) Marketing that leads with "the truth" (what you know the customer needs)
  often fails to get responses; he cites his early fitness marketing about
  counting macros and resistance training getting no new customers. Paraphrase.
- (2020) You are not your customer — a business owner's personal taste in
  marketing is a poor guide, because existing "nice teeth" prospects aren't the
  target. Told via a furious dentist client anecdote. Paraphrase.
- (2020) The "ham and the garlic" principle: give people what they want (the ham)
  to get them in the door, then once you've earned trust, deliver what they
  actually need (the garlic). Paraphrase of the parable's moral.
- (2020) Prospects lack the context/perspective you have; you must "go back
  across the bridge" to before you knew what you know, meet them where they are,
  and walk them to where you need them to go. Paraphrase.
- (2020) Applied to fitness: they marketed short-duration challenges and detoxes
  (the quick fix people wanted) as the front end, then after early success
  upsold clients on learning calories, macros, and real training. Paraphrase.
- (2020) Reframe competitors who market "quick fix" offers: it's not that they're
  wrong — it's probably working because they're meeting the market where it is.
  Treat "this is the truth" as "this is *their* truth right now." Paraphrase.
- (2020) Applies to any transformation/coaching business where the customer must
  break a belief or take steps toward you: "begin with the end in mind" but do
  not begin *with the end." Paraphrase.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's a good thing that you're not your customers. You already have nice teeth."

> "You have to give people what they want in order to give them what they need."

> "You have to go back in time to before you knew what you know now and meet
> people where they're at and then walk them across the bridge to where you need

> "It took the ham to get them in the door."

> "You can begin with the end in mind. You do not begin with the end. You have to

> did so that they can get to where they want to go."
