# A World-Class EA Is a Superpower

## Summary
Solo clip in which Alex describes hiring his first "world-class" executive assistant and what made her different. He opens with a self-aware hedge — he's early in the relationship ("I'm almost hesitant to make this because I'm in love"), so he tells viewers to take it "with a gigantic grain of salt." His core diagnosis of why past EAs failed and this one works: "she's a psychopath and I think I had normal people and I'm not normal" — he needed someone whose intensity matched his. Two traits define the fit. First, **speed over everything**: an EA who "will always respond to me within 60 seconds, no matter when I message," because founders go for hours with nothing and then, the moment they reach out, "in that moment, you are my constraint" and need full attention/full resources. Second, **general intelligence / horsepower**, which he admits he did not screen for hard enough previously and only discovered its value by accident — a smart EA earns higher trust, so he can hand off real cognitively demanding tasks ("just do a first draft"), get 80% done, and merely tweak rather than build everything from scratch (de novo). He layers on his time-management philosophy: the founder should be the one who *decides* how their own time is allocated (because your time is your most valuable resource and you are the best allocator of it), even if the mechanical scheduling/"blocking and tackling" is outsourced. He references his maker-vs-manager framing — plan your time according to whether you're a maker or manager and "live and die by that" — and credits proactive time-blocking plus an EA who tracks all his ongoing projects and open loops ("when are you going to close the loop on this?") for some of the most productive weeks of his life, finally feeling "ahead." Practical hiring advice: pay more than you expect, look for someone more intelligent than you'd default to, state that speed is non-negotiable, and have them watch his maker-manager video for context. Closes with the standard acquisition.com/roadmap promo (ignored for claims).
