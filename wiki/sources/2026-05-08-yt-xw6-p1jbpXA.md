# Patience Is Relative to the Outcome That You're Going For

## Summary

Alex distinguishes macro patience from micro speed. The bigger the desired outcome,
the more patience is required, but daily execution should still push deadlines,
remove blockers, and pull the future forward.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says patience is relative to the size of the outcome.
- He uses an Everest-versus-foothill comparison: the same percentage of progress can be
  much larger in absolute terms on a bigger mountain.
- He repeats the macro-patience, micro-speed frame.
- He says managers and operators should ask when work will be done, what blockers
  exist, whether the task is top priority, and how many actual hours remain.
- He recommends asking "why not today?" to compress timelines.
- He describes frequent small confrontations as a way to pull an organization forward.
- He cites Elon Musk as an inspiration for urgency.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Patience is relative to the outcome."

> "Macro patience, micro speed."

> "Why not today?"
