# My Family's Intervention Went Horribly Wrong

## Summary

This clip repeats Alex Hormozi's family-intervention story about a relative saying she
would never live his life. It adds the idea of "punking the game": refusing to continue
playing by someone else's conversational rules when the game itself is not one he agreed
to play.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex repeats that if someone says they would never live his life, his answer is that
  they should not because it is his life.
- He says the family member was likely trying to help rather than acting from envy.
- He describes the event as an intervention-like moment and says he became very angry.
- Leila was present and physically soothed him as he reacted.
- Alex uses "good, don't" and "you're right" as ways to defuse projected criticism.
- Another speaker introduces "punking the game" from improv as refusing a game's rules.
- Alex's example fits that frame: he refused to play the critic's game.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's not your life, it's mine."

> "Good, don't."

> "You're right."
