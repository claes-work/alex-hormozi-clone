# Helping a $5M SaaS Scale to $20M/Year

## Summary
In a live coaching exchange, a Spanish founder describes his AI short-form-content SaaS that grew from $0 to $5M ARR in 12 months and aims for $20M by end of next year. Alex Hormozi presses him on his real constraint, walks through the product and unit economics, and concludes the founder's strategy is fundamentally sound while pointing to churn reduction, activation, and ICP focus as the levers. Alex declines the founder's pitch for a partnership, arguing the business can hit its goal on its established channels without it.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames diagnosis around identifying "the constraint," repeatedly asking why the founder should "bother" partnering when he can grow from $5M to $20M on existing channels anyway.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that a business grown ~50% by word of mouth demonstrably has a good product, and that if growth outpaces churn the model "tracks."
- As of March 2026, Alex treats high monthly churn as normal for consumer/mass-market software, citing Spotify and Shopify as high-churn consumer businesses.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises narrowing to a proven ICP (coaches / people who already make money and therefore don't churn) and expanding into adjacent monetized segments — network marketers, realtors, local business owners, fitness coaches — rather than the undifferentiated mass market.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends every business understand at least one or two distinct customer-acquisition methods, listing door-knocking (one-to-one), content (one-to-many inbound), paid ads, affiliates, and referrals.
- As of March 2026, Alex says ongoing gains come from tactical work — reducing churn, increasing activation, CRO in the funnel — even for businesses with strong metrics, citing Skool's own daily efforts.
- Caller claims (unverified): $0→$5M ARR in 12 months; targeting $20M by end of next year; ~50% of revenue from word of mouth, ~30% from ~7,000 affiliates, rest from SEO/content; pricing $20–$150/month with ~$40 average per user; 45% EBITDA margin; ~15% monthly churn; users across 60+ countries and 60 languages.
