# Why Most Cold Outreach Fails

## Summary

Alex explains when cold outreach is the right acquisition channel. His rule is that the
larger the ticket and the more specific the target, the more important outbound becomes;
lower-ticket, less-specific markets usually fit content and paid ads better. He also
separates organic content as a primary acquisition channel from a minimum viable organic
presence that simply proves legitimacy and nurtures cold prospects.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Cold outreach fit depends heavily on ticket size and target specificity.
- As a rule of thumb, cold outreach becomes more attractive at roughly $10,000 or more.
- Low-LTV or product-led-growth offers need much more volume, making outbound less
  efficient.
- Hunting small deals with a high-touch outbound motion is like hunting minnows with a
  harpoon.
- Organic content can be bolted onto outreach as lead nurture even when it is not the
  primary acquisition channel.
- A bare profile with no posts makes outreach look like a scam.
- A minimum viable organic presence should show the seller is alive, relevant, and able
  to provide value.
- Content that hits should become sales-team assets mapped to avatars, case studies, and
  objections.
- If doing outbound on a channel, the linked profile should be the one the business is
  actually active on.
