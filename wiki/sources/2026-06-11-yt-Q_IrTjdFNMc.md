# Helping a $15M Legal Services Business Hit $20M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a specialized Texas legal-services business doing about $15M with high margins. The owner is choosing between expanding to another state or growing within Texas by increasing share and possibly bringing more high-volume law firms into the market. Alex frames the decision through risk-adjusted return and the cost of change. Because no other state appears as attractive as Texas and the expansion upside is modest, he recommends first increasing share of the existing Texas pie and then growing the Texas pie before considering other states.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When two paths both lead up the mountain, over-optimizing for the perfect path can waste time; good-enough big decisions should be made faster.
- For strategic changes, Alex thinks in terms of risk-adjusted return.
- He has observed about a 20% performance decrement whenever a business changes something meaningful before gains accrue.
- A guaranteed 20% cost of change should require meaningfully more than a potential 20% upside; Alex wants something closer to 50% or double.
- Constant tweaking can leave companies persistently below potential because employees are always adapting to new ideas.
- If no other state can generate Texas-like revenue, the order of operations is to increase Texas share, grow the Texas pie, and only then consider other states.
- Alex prefers not to rely on someone else opening a business and doing a good job as the primary growth path.
- He suggests thinking about monopolistic levers that starve competitors and increase enterprise value.
