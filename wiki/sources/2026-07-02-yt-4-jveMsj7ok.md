# You're Spending Zero Hours Advertising. That's Why You're Broke.

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Amos Craig, whose gray-area psychedelic mushroom e-commerce business relies mostly on Instagram and wants to grow from about $650K to $3-5M. Alex names the real enemy as irrelevance: no one knows the business exists. He recommends much more advertising in the broad sense, starting with short-form content cross-posted across platforms, repurposed into text and infographics, batched through a weekly production system, and connected to a diagnostic/guide lead magnet. He tells the founder to build one advertising brick at a time before adding long-form, live, or interactive layers.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Amos Craig** - the coachee. His product category, legality comments, revenue, channel risk, and account-strike issues are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The fundamental enemy for a small brand is irrelevance: no one knows it exists.
- Content is advertising; the founder should count hours spent making content as advertising time.
- One Instagram reel per day is not enough if advertising is the constraint.
- The first lever is cross-platform distribution: each reel should also go to TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn video, X video, and other relevant video platforms.
- Transcriptions can become text posts, and transcriptions can also be turned into infographics, multiplying one video into many outputs.
- End-to-end production is inefficient. The better system is to batch: plan ideas, research hooks/trends/memes, film in one block, then edit and publish.
- A single diagnostic or guide can serve as the hero lead magnet behind many content hooks; not every piece of content needs a new lead magnet.
- Long-form should wait until short-form is systematized, because long-form is many shorts organized around a theme.
- Advertising channels should stack like a brick foundation: get one channel working, systematize it, then add another layer. Jumping from brick to brick prevents compounding.
