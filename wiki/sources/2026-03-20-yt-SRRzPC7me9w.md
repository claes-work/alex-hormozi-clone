# Helping a $51M/Year E-Commerce Business with Their Marketing

## Summary
In this live-audience consulting segment, Alex Hormozi advises a physician who sells
genetic reports and supplements (self-reported ~$51M revenue this year, ~22% growth,
~22% EBITDA, grown almost entirely by word of mouth). The owner feels his messaging is
unclear across the many conditions he addresses; Hormozi argues the message is actually
clear and reframes the problem as needing a marketing execution team, not a niche.
Hormozi prescribes a quiz funnel plus an affiliate/influencer acquisition model in which
influencers keep 100% of the front-end genetic-test profit while the business monetizes
through back-end segmentation sequences.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex advises that a founder who thinks their message is "confusing"
  is often clear already and instead needs an outside marketing operator to build the
  funnel — clarity of message vs. capacity to execute are different problems.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends a quiz funnel for this business: quiz-based questions
  that decision-tree into ~10 videos for the 10 most common customer issues, then
  recommend products — which he says is cheap and fast to build ("in an afternoon").
- As of March 2026, Alex prescribes an influencer/affiliate acquisition model: give
  influencers 100% of the front-end profit on the low-barrier offer (the genetic test)
  so the business makes zero on the front end, and monetize via back-end segmentation
  sequences and ancillary site purchases.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the genetic test as the ideal low-ticket / low-barrier
  front-end offer to get maximum people in the door, using influencers to "preach the
  gospel" and drive traffic to it.
- The owner's claims are self-reported and unverified: ~$51M revenue this year, ~22%
  growth, ~22% EBITDA, ~22% retail / 40% wholesale / rest Amazon, ~$50–60K/month on
  Google and Meta ads, and that he is "the world's foremost expert" in his area.
- The owner (unverified) states his genetic reports deliberately do not sell his own
  supplements because he wants customers to fix lifestyle first, positioning ~90% of the
  benefit as diet/lifestyle and ~10% as supplements.

> "I will agree with your claim it's word of mouth."
