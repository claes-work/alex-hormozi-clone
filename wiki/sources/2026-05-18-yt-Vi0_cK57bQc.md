# Why Productivity Hacks Are a Waste of Time

## Summary

Alex argues that focus is the core difficulty in entrepreneurship and defines commitment
as eliminating alternatives. In his view, productivity hacks miss the point because
focus comes from removing everything that is not the target, not adding more tools,
rituals, or tactics. He names saying no as the cheapest productivity hack.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says the hardest thing about entrepreneurship is focus.
- He defines commitment as the elimination of alternatives.
- The most focused person does nothing but the target activity, with only survival needs
  added back in.
- Productivity hacks are often a waste of time because they add more activities instead
  of removing distractions.
- Wanting to add something to get more focus is, in Alex's framing, a flawed impulse.
- The number-one productivity hack is saying no.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Commitment, which is the elimination of alternatives."

> "Focusing more is about removing everything that isn't the thing you want to focus on."

> "The number one productivity hack is to say no."
