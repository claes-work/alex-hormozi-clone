# You're Not Making as Much Money as You Want Because You're Not as Good as You Think

## Summary

Alex gives the blunt message that people usually are not making as much money as they want
because they are not as good as they think. He then discusses luck: top outcomes include
skill, drive, intelligence, and luck, but luck can be manipulated by increasing the surface
area for chance and getting around people with higher opportunity flow.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-16, Alex says most people are not making as much money as they want because
  they are not as good as they think.
- He says the universe is often fair, and the fairness is what makes people not make
  money.
- He acknowledges his own luck and says he feels unfairness has favored him.
- He says the absolute top outcomes have drive, intelligence, a huge stack of skills, and
  luck; therefore the top 10,000 richest people may teach more broadly than only the top
  five, who have all of those plus extreme luck.
- He says believing he is lucky lets him take bigger swings because he expects the downside
  will probably be okay.
- He says luck is manipulable: increase the chance of luck by creating more opportunities,
  meeting more people, and being around people who are succeeding more.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're not making as much money as you want cuz you're not as good as you think you are."

> "I have an intrinsic belief that I am lucky."

> "Luck itself is also manipulable."
