# 7 Ways To Get Customers for Free

## Summary

Solo Hormozi video laying out seven tactical, script-level referral plays for
generating customers for free, each tied to a specific moment in the sales process
(a discount request, a holiday, a success moment, an event). His framing claim:
referrals compound with the business — roughly 30% of customers can come from
referrals regardless of company size, and referred customers close at higher rates,
pay more, buy more often, and refer others in turn. He argues referral rate vs. churn
rate sets a business's at-rest growth rate.

## Key claims

The seven free-customer methods (plus framing and a bonus):

- **Framing — why referrals compound.** ~30% of business can come from referrals at
  any scale (whether $100M or $100K), so it scales indefinitely; referred customers
  close higher, pay more (premium versions), buy more often, and refer more. The ratio
  of referral % to churn % is your stable at-rest growth rate (e.g. 20% referrals on
  10% churn = 2-to-1 growth that persists at any size).
- **1. The gift card play.** During a holiday (real or invented — birthday, business
  anniversary, Christmas), sell existing customers gift cards priced at one core unit
  of service (e.g. a $200 or $2,000 unit) at a 90%+ discount. Limit two per customer
  (which nudges them to buy two), redeemable only for other people, not themselves.
  When they buy, ask who to make the card out to — this captures the referred lead's
  name/number. Put a 30-day expiration for urgency. You get paid to acquire leads
  instead of paying to acquire them, then upsell redeemers into higher packages.
- **2. Referral-for-discount.** When a prospect asks "can you give me a discount,"
  respond: "Sure — just give me the name of three friends and I'm happy to give you the
  discount," and make the introductions on the spot. Locks in three leads in exchange
  for a discount he'd have considered anyway. His rule: never discount unless there's a
  change in terms. Willing to give up to ~1x CAC as the discount (1/3 conversion × 3
  referrals).
- **3. Bonus-instead-of-discount (permutation of #2).** Instead of cutting price,
  offer extra service/product of equivalent stated value. A $200 discount costs $200
  hard cost; $400 of service may only cost $40–80 to deliver, so effective discount
  drops while perceived value rises — and it drives more usage of the product.
- **4. "Do you want to know how to get this for free?"** After the sale closes, ask
  this; everyone leans in. Offer real cash per referred customer (e.g. $100 cash check
  per person brought in on a $500 offer) — paid only after the referred person's sale,
  not for the introduction. Produced ~30% of sales on promotions. One nurse/teacher
  referred 10 people, earned $500, brought him 10 customers.
- **5. Referral at success.** At the transformation/success moment (weight lost, garage
  fixed, dog trained), while capturing the testimonial, ask "how many other amazing
  people like you do you know?", take a selfie together on the customer's phone, then
  text yourself from their phone with the photo to make a warm three-way intro.
  Requires existing rapport.
- **6. Handwritten card + event.** Pair handwritten appreciation cards with quarterly
  in-person (or virtual) customer events. Card PS: screenshot this and text it to a
  friend to get them in (food/drinks); do a three-way text so you meet them first.
  30–50% of the room ends up being plus-ones. Don't sell at the event — just set
  follow-up appointments, booked close (not far out) to catch high motivation.
- **7. The spouse request.** For anything a partner could do together (weight loss,
  therapy, paint night), proactively ask "does your husband/wife support you in this?"
  and pitch a "spouse program" backed by a stat (e.g. people who do it with a spouse
  are 3x more successful). Never uses the word "mandatory" but frames attendance as
  near-required for results.
- **Bonus — "bring a friend."** A friend running massive online challenges (million+
  customers) simply added "bring a friend, you'll succeed more" to every piece of
  content/ad/post; 25–30% of customers then came in from word-of-mouth / unattributed
  sources vs. almost nothing when he didn't. Works for any business, not just brick-
  and-mortar.
