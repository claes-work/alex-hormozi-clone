# The Lead Magnet That Will Sell Your Mentorship Program

## Summary

Alex advises a worship-leader mentorship founder that ad targeting restrictions matter
less than message-market match. He says the ad's person, setting, props, language, and
lead magnet should self-select the target audience. He sketches a stage-shot ad that
offers practical pre-stage tips and then invites viewers into a school/community for
feedback.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says platform targeting limits are less important because messaging will select
  the audience.
- He says the person in the ad, background, imagery, props, clothing, and language
  affect who responds.
- He says Christian language and church-stage context will attract Christian prospects
  even if the platform cannot directly target religious demographics.
- He recommends filming on stage with the congregation present to create authentic
  context.
- He sketches a story of being bad before practice, then improving and training others.
- He recommends a lead magnet with six things to do before going on stage.
- He recommends selling a school/community where the founder gives personalized
  feedback and video reviews.
