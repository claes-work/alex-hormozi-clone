# Helping a GoHighLevel Reseller Scale to $1M/Month

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a GoHighLevel / CRM reseller serving online fitness coaches. The owner has improved retention and has some profitable ads and strategic partnerships. Alex recommends testing ads with enough spend to learn, framing the offer around automating Instagram follower-to-customer conversion, and then considering a one-level-up channel: sell to fitness business coaches or agencies whose clients already have the demand problem. He compares this to his experience with Allen/ALAN, where selling to agencies beat selling directly to SMBs.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - GoHighLevel reseller for online fitness coaches. His revenue, churn, LTV/CAC, partnerships, ad spend, funnels, and avatar are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A campaign cannot be meaningfully tested unless spend is at least about 2x target CAC.
- The offer should be framed around a painful outcome: turning Instagram followers into customers without living in DMs.
- A VSL/case-study lead magnet can show several coaches with low follower counts getting clients through automation.
- Case studies should represent different psychographics so prospects believe it can work for their niche.
- Selling directly to fitness coaches may create a second problem: now the seller must help them get leads.
- A stronger path may be going one level up to fitness business coaches, mentors, or agencies that already have the customers.
- The pitch to those partners is stickier recurring revenue that their clients keep paying for after mentorship churn.
- Going one level up creates two customers: the partner/guru and the end customer.
- A two-sided model needs customer success for both sides.
