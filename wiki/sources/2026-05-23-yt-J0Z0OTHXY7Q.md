# Why Entertainers Will Always Beat Educators

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says that if he monetized only through AdSense or content attention, he
would choose a broader audience than business owners. His current niche is intentionally
small because it monetizes through business outcomes, not pure reach. He argues
entertainment scales larger than education because entertainment crosses demographic and
skill boundaries more easily.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If Alex monetized only through AdSense or content attention, he would choose a broader
  niche than business owners.
- His business-owner audience is small compared with mass entertainment audiences.
- He gives rough self-reported niche math: about 9% of Americans own businesses, about
  5% of those are above $1 million, about 1 in 250 are above $10 million, and about 1 in
  2,500 are above $100 million.
- Entertainment is larger because nearly everyone wants to laugh, relax, or be
  distracted.
- Education is naturally narrower because it depends on a person's active goal, problem,
  and skill level.
- A creator's monetization model should determine whether broad reach or narrow buyer
  relevance matters more.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Entertainers will always be bigger than educators as a category."

> "If I was monetizing on AdSense, I would go broad."
