# Helping an E-Commerce Business Owner Make Better Content

## Summary
Live coaching clip. An e-commerce owner selling psychedelic mushroom products online (~$650k revenue last year, wants to reach $3–5M) gets most customers organically from Instagram, but posts only one Reel a day and can't run ads reliably because the product category gets flagged, striked, and accounts shut down. Alex reframes the problem bluntly: "the thing that you're fighting against is irrelevance… no one knows you exist," so the fix is simply advertising more (content = advertising). He then gives a compact **content-scaling playbook**. **Lever 1 — distribution/repurposing:** be cross-platform on everything (Reels → TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn video, X video), then repurpose each piece into text posts (from transcriptions) and infographics — "7 × 3, we 21x output." **Lever 2 — production via chunking:** stop making-and-posting end-to-end (the most inefficient way to produce anything); plan the week (spend ~two days on idea/hook/trend research), batch-record everything in one day, then edit and post on a cadence — "if you just do that, you'll probably triple." On monetization he pushes a **CTA + diagnostic lead magnet** (a "free mushroom guide"/quiz) routed through ManyChat, arguing a diagnostic gives "endless hooks on the front end" so you don't build a new lead magnet per post — you "chunk in, chunk out" of one hero piece, all hyperlinked to the store. On sequencing he refuses to add long-form, podcast, or live before short-form is systematized: master shorts first ("longs is just many of those together on a theme"), do it consistently for 3–6 months, then layer the next channel. His closing principle: scaling advertising is laying a "brick foundation" one channel at a time — most people "jump from brick to brick and never actually get any foundation, so it never stacks."

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames a stalled e-commerce brand's core enemy as **irrelevance** — "no one knows you exist" — so the primary lever is simply to advertise (make content) more.
- As of March 2026, Alex treats **content as advertising** in the formal sense, and counts hours "spent on advertising" as content-production hours.
- As of March 2026, his first content lever is **cross-platform distribution**: every short should go to TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn video, and X video (Facebook and LinkedIn video "blowing up right now").
- As of March 2026, his repurposing math: take transcriptions of each video into standalone text posts and into infographics — roughly 7 platforms × 3 formats = "21x output" from the same core content.
- As of March 2026, his production lever is **chunking the workflow** — plan/research ideas, hooks, trends, memes and formats in a batch; film all in one day; then edit — because going "end-to-end" (make one, post one) is "the most inefficient way to produce anything," and chunking alone will "probably triple" output.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends adding a **CTA to organic content** routed to a ManyChat flow; even flag-sensitive niches can use a soft CTA (e.g. "here's a free mushroom guide").
- As of March 2026, he favors a **diagnostic/quiz as the lead magnet** because it locates where the prospect is and gives "endless hooks on the front end" — one hero piece you "chunk in, chunk out," with everything inside hyperlinked to the store (or the link routes to an article that links to the shop).
- As of March 2026, his **sequencing rule**: don't add long-form, podcast, or live until short-form is running — shorts are easier than longs, and "longs is just many of those together on a theme."
- As of March 2026, he advises systematizing one advertising channel consistently for **3–6 months** before layering the next — "it's just layers."
- As of March 2026, his scaling principle: build advertising like a **brick foundation**, one working channel at a time; people who jump channel-to-channel "never actually get any foundation, so it never stacks."
