# Why I Turned Down Two People Who Offered Me $50K

## Summary

Alex recounts turning down two people at Funnel Hacking Live who each offered $50,000
for a single day of consulting — a decision his wife Leila made on the spot before he
could answer ("No, we don't do that"). He uses the story to argue that focus and
protected attention, not chasing lucrative side opportunities, are the main driver of
their fast growth, and that a $100M business is mostly a $30M business doing more of the
same thing.

## Key claims

- (2020) The better you get, the better the opportunities you get to say no to; saying
  no to attractive distractions has been "the number one thing" that grew their
  business.
- (2020) Attention — not time — is an entrepreneur's most valuable asset; every side
  opportunity has "strings attached" to your attention.
- (2020) Any single business can be as big as you want it to be; diluting attention
  across multiple ventures ensures none of them work.
- (2020) The difference between a $30M/year and a $100M/year business is mostly volume,
  not new verticals — the $100M business does more of the same thing (e.g. 15 sales a
  day instead of 5), same customer avatar, same problem solved better.
- (2020) Mastery is "boring": you get good at one thing and keep doing it better rather
  than adding new things; the entrepreneurs who make the most money do the *least*
  number of different things.
- (2020) Accepting the $50K day would have pulled attention away from fulfilling the
  promise made to their existing gym-owner clients.
- (2020) "Sometimes you have to go slow to go fast" — advice he attributes to a mentor
  who was COO of a $10B company, with whom he worked closely for an extended period.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "My wife, before I even had a chance to answer the question, stepped forward and said,
> 'No, we don't do that.'"

> "The attention that that would take is going to take more from our business than it
> will give us."

> "The better you are, the better the opportunities that you have to say no to are."

> "It's your attention. And so... that's the most valuable asset you have. Not your
> time, it's your attention."

> "The ones who make the most money are the ones who do the least."

> "Focus in on the one thing that makes you money and do that over and over and over
> and over again until you're sick of it, and then do more of it, and I promise you'll
> make money."
