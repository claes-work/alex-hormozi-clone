# The 4 Reasons Employees Don't Do What You Ask

## Summary

Alex presents the Management Diamond as a diagnostic for missed employee execution:
they may not know what to do, how to do it, when it is due, or what is blocking them.
He says managers should diagnose the missing condition before assuming laziness or bad
motivation.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says employees usually fail to execute because they lack clarity on what, how,
  when, or the blocker.
- He says motivation should be diagnosed last rather than assumed first.
- He says the framework helps a manager avoid attacking the person and instead find
  the cause.
- He says he developed the framework from experience, not from heavy inspirational
  consumption.
- He recommends hiring for the smallest skill deficiency the company can afford to
  train.
- He says low-skill roles can prioritize attitude over aptitude, while high-skill roles
  may require more tolerance for attitude problems because rare aptitude matters.
- He says soft skills and hard skills are both trainable skills.
- He defines intelligence operationally as rate of learning.
