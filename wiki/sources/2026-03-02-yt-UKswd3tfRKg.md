# The Type of Content I Watch

## Summary
Short interview clip on Alex's personal media diet. He says he "only follows comedy and philosophy accounts for the most part" — calling them "the only two truth tellers": some make him laugh, some make him think, both "give me context on life." On comedy he's indiscriminate about size ("no matter how big they are") but disciplined about consistency: he follows any comedian whose page is *regular* comedy clips, and unfollows anyone whose page is mostly non-comedy. He names a long list of stand-ups he likes (see quotes), calls himself "a cheap date when it comes to comedy," and says the only thing he dislikes is "lazy comedy" — "crude comedy for the sake of being crude." He does **not** listen to comedy podcasts; he watches routines, goes to live shows, and watches clips. On philosophy he only reads "the ancient stuff" — "anybody who's dead. If you're dead, I like you" — citing the Stoics (Epictetus, Seneca) and "darker" writers (Dostoevsky). His most-active platform by far is X/Twitter, and he stresses he runs it himself ("Everything's me. I manage everything. 100%."). He says he doesn't listen to podcasts in general and isn't a podcaster — the one exception being that he watches Elon Musk every time he goes on Joe Rogan. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
