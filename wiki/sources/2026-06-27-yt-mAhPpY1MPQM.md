# We do $8M and can't get past it without ads

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a B2B frozen cocktail-mix company selling to country clubs and resorts. The founder asks whether to grow awareness or get more accounts. Alex says to mechanize the father's existing relationship-driven sales motion before trying a new brand-awareness strategy, especially because the product likely sells well when buyers can taste it.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - frozen cocktail-mix business operator. His product, customers, revenue, father-led sales, and word-of-mouth context are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If the business was built through B2B sales and word of mouth, Alex would first do more of the proven motion rather than start a brand-awareness motion from scratch.
- The father's existing sales process should be broken into many component parts so other people can execute it.
- Mechanizing outbound requires scripts, first and second messages, openers, offers, and a way to increase the pain gap between the prospect's current and desired state.
- The hardest step is getting the decision-maker to try the product, because tasting it changes perception.
- Conferences and trade shows likely fit well because the product is literally taste-driven and can be sampled in person.
- Alex does not recommend going brand-first yet; he recommends expanding the B2B account-getting motion first.
