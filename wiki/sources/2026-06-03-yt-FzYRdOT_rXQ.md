# He Built a $5M Paint Biz. Now He's Stuck

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a painting-company owner who moved from residential repaints into general-contractor work. The new channel has strong lifetime-value-to-acquisition economics but creates heavy accounts receivable and long collection cycles. Alex's diagnosis is that the model is not broken; the constraint is cash conversion. His recommendation is to solve financing before changing the offer or channel.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When the acquisition economics are extremely strong, the founder should not reflexively abandon the model because the operational pain feels worse.
- The specific constraint is cash conversion cycle: work is won and delivered before cash arrives.
- Receivables financing or a revolving line of credit secured by AR can bridge the timing gap.
- The cost of short-term financing can often be offset with a small price increase if the market already supports demand.
- The first move should be financing the working-capital gap, not changing the business model.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Don't mess with the model."
