# How the ULTRA WEALTHY get loans to buy cool stuff (margin loans)

## Summary

Solo explainer on borrowing against a stock portfolio (margin loans) to access
low-interest, tax-free liquidity — the mechanism behind the ultra-wealthy's
"borrow against appreciating stock, buy stuff, never pay income tax until death"
strategy. Alex demonstrates literally Googling "take loans against stocks" to show
the answer is public (Fidelity, Robinhood Gold, M1 Finance), explains the margin-call
risk, and reveals that he personally runs nearly zero leverage and prefers being
underlevered.

## Key claims

- Established banks/brokerages lend roughly 30–65% of a portfolio's value as
  asset-backed (margin) loans at ~1–2% interest, with no closing costs or fees — you
  just pay interest while the money is out. (Paraphrase.)
- The ultra-wealthy take loans against stock rather than selling, avoid income tax, and
  because the stock appreciates faster than they borrow, they can keep borrowing until
  death and never pay taxes. (Paraphrase — the "buy, borrow, die" mechanic.)
- The risk is the margin call: if the portfolio drops, you must pay back down to the
  allowed ratio, and if you can't, they force-sell your assets at the worst possible
  moment ("double hosed"). Borrow far below the limit (10–20% tops). (Paraphrase.)
- Alex uses margin only for short-term (6–12 month) secured transactions like hard-money
  lending where he earns 12–20% and borrows at 1–2%. (Self-reported.)
- He currently holds zero debt, not even a mortgage, and is deliberately underlevered —
  his objective for money was freedom, not more money. (Self-reported.)
