# The Story Behind the $100M Book Series

## Summary

In this interview clip, Alex Hormozi tells the origin and design story of his three-book "$100M" series (*Offers*, *Leads*, *Money Models*). He recounts that all three were written at once — spun out of a single massive manuscript he originally titled "Lead Generation and Monetization Structures" — after a mentor told him, while he was still running Gym Launch, to crystallize his knowledge before he forgot it. He explains the deliberate meta-concept behind the trilogy: each book both teaches a step (what to sell → who to sell to → how to make money) and demonstrates its own ideas through its launch. He details the launch mechanics, including using all the advertising methods for the *Leads* launch and 12 of 15 monetization mechanisms for the *Money Models* launch that produced a $100M/72-hour result and a Guinness World Record. He claims his books have helped people generate "probably a few billion" dollars.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed only)

- *Money Models* sold ~2.9 million books in 24 hours and holds the **Guinness World Record** for fastest-selling non-fiction book (confirming his prior claim; interviewer cites "2.9 million books in 24 hours").
- All **three books were written at the same time**, spun out of one large manuscript. (Self-reported; note this reframes *Leads* being described elsewhere as co-authored with Trevor over ~2 years.)
- A **mentor**, during his Gym Launch years, told him to "crystallize all your knowledge now" because he'd forget the details — the trigger for writing the manuscript.
- The original manuscript was titled **"Lead Generation and Monetization Structures"**; friends he shared it with said it was "a lot," so he peeled off the offers section.
- He wasn't sure he'd publish it — considered making it an **internal team manual** — until **Leila told him "Publish it."**
- When he published *$100M Offers* he had **10,000 Instagram followers and nothing on YouTube**; it sold via **word of mouth** because it produced results.
- The trilogy's **meta-concept**: each book demonstrates its own ideas, and the series answers a sequence — *Offers* (purple book) = **what do you sell**, *Leads* (blue book) = **who do you sell to**, *Money Models* (third, "the record breaker") = **how do you make money**.
- **Offer value has four elements** on a continuum (not binary): **dream outcome, speed, ease, and risk** — you improve an offer by making it faster, easier, and more risk-free.
- **Advertising core model**: there are only **four things** anyone can do to let others know about something — **paid ads, content, warm outreach, cold outreach** — done toward **four types of people** (affiliates, customers, agencies, employees) for leverage; "a cycle of advertising."
- For the *Leads* (blue book) launch he **used all eight advertising methods**; ~"hundred-something thousand" people were live; he ran a full-stack pitch with everyone ready to buy, then **gave it away for free** (free courses; hard copies still purchasable).
- The book **courses are free**; only physical/hard copies are paid.
- *Money Models* contains **~15 monetization mechanisms**; he **used 12 of them** in the launch to demonstrate the book's ideas, culminating in a **$100M launch in 72 hours**.
- Free copies given at launch did **not** count toward the record sales number.
- Estimated total money his books have helped people generate: **"probably a few billion."**
