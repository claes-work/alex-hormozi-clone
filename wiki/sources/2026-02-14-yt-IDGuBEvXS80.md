# How AI Will Change Social Media in 2026

## Summary

A short, forward-looking clip (mid-thought, picked up in progress) in which Alex
Hormozi lays out how he expects AI to reshape social-media content strategy in
2026 — framed explicitly as what _he_ is changing in his own media operation for
the coming year. His central thesis is a **divergence of long form and short
form on production quality**: long form is going _up_ in production quality while
short form is going _down_, deliberately, toward raw/authentic. His reasoning is
an AI-scarcity argument: because AI can now make everything look highly produced,
production quality is becoming **commoditized**, so value "zigs back" toward
**authenticity and truth** — he asserts "the market always finds truth," and in
an AI world the industry will index harder toward it. He admits his own shorts
have become "too produced" and says a big push for next year is to switch them
back to raw, talk-to-camera, rant-style. He then reprises two themes he says he
raised "last time" and is still committed to — **live** and **interactive**
content — because both are significantly harder for AI to reproduce, making them
the "alpha" (better-than-market return on effort). He closes the media segment
with a **barbell strategy**: be either a polished format people love _or_
super-raw all-in-podcast style — "the middle is not where you want to be." The
clip ends with an acquisition.com/roadmap CTA (his free 10-stage 0→$100M+
roadmap). Solo Alex (an off-camera "Evelyn" is thanked but does not speak
substantively). See [[../topics/business/content-strategy]].

## Key claims

_(All from 2026-02-14; single substantive speaker, Alex Hormozi — paraphrased,
forward-looking predictions/plans for 2026.)_

- ★ **Long form and short form are diverging on production quality: long form
  going up, short form going down.** By "quality" he means _production_ quality.
  His plan for the coming year is to make his short form more raw (talk-to-camera,
  rant-style, authentic) and let his long form go heavier on production and
  structure.
- ★ **In an AI world, value migrates toward authenticity and truth because
  production quality is being commoditized.** AI can make everything look
  super-high-quality, so polish stops being a differentiator; the market "always
  finds truth," and the industry will index harder toward authenticity — so raw
  content "zigs back" into value. (This is the content-strategy expression of his
  broader AI thesis that value migrates to what AI can't cheaply supply.)
- (paraphrase) He judges his **own current shorts as "too produced"** and is
  doing a deliberate big push next year to switch them to raw/authentic.
- ★ **Live and interactive content are the "alpha" because AI has a harder time
  reproducing them.** Live is inherently hard to fake ("if you're live, you're
  live"), and real-time interactivity with the audience is significantly more
  difficult for AI to reproduce — so both give better-than-market return on
  effort. (He notes these are themes he raised previously and is still committed
  to.)
- (paraphrase) On the **long-form side** he considers the call already made: it's
  fine to go heavier on production and be more structured in the format.
- ★ **Barbell / "poles" strategy for 2026 media: win at one of two extremes, not
  the middle.** Either have an awesome polished format people love, _or_ go
  super-raw all-in-podcast style — "the middle is not where you want to be."
- (paraphrase) CTA: his free 10-stage roadmap from $0 to $100M+ (which he says
  fewer than 1% of companies finish and he has done multiple times, broken down
  across eight business functions), available at acquisition.com/roadmap.
