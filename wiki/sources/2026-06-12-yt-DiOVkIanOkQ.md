# Helping a Wildfire Sprinkler Company Scale to $200M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a wildfire-sprinkler company that did about $2.2M in its first year and wants to reach $200M. The company has narrowed to high-net-worth and commercial customers but may receive 16,000 homeowner leads through an insurance-company mandate. Alex frames the decision by whether the opportunity changes day-to-day operations, calculates even a low close rate as a large revenue opportunity, and says the company is missing a sales machine.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When choosing between customer avatars, Alex asks what changes about daily activities; if the opportunity does not materially change delivery or operations, he is more likely to pursue it.
- A partner-mandated list is lead generation, not guaranteed revenue; the company still needs to close the customers.
- Even a weak close rate can be valuable when the lead pool and average order value are large.
- The company does not primarily have an avatar problem; it has no developed sales motion.
- Installing a sales machine against a large incoming lead pool could immediately multiply the business.
- Moving upmarket may have hidden the real issue if the original residential segment only looked bad because of sales inefficiency.
