# Helping a Dirt Bike Coach Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client runs a motocross/dirt-bike training business built on 5-day camps and single-day tour dates, projecting ~$4M for 2026. He scaled single-day dates from 70 (2023) to 140 (2024) — "a ton of operational drag," like a rock band on a small tour — but discovered the bottom ~20 were net-negative and the next ~20 netted under $1,000, while three test 5-day camps each netted well over $100k. He wants to shift toward 25 camps a year but fears that abandoning single-day dates hands that market to a copycat competitor. Alex's first move: reframe the fear — the competitor is racing to "copy the model that makes no money," which is a win, not a threat ("Love this for us"). On pricing (1-day $300, 5-day $1,200), Alex pushes hard: both are underpriced, and the client's reluctance stems from guilt — he came from no money, gives everything free (22M subscribers, ~100 people in the room), and doesn't want to "out-price the people that were me." Alex's counter: you can only ever monetize ~1% of the people you help for free, and you can't expand your footprint or free content without cash flow — so serve the person who can't afford it for free, and charge the correct price to those who can. He warns that a proper sales motion (phone selling vs. a ClickFunnels checkout) is worth getting right the first time, and that real pricing power might be 300→3,000, not 300→400. On which event model to keep, Alex refuses to guess and insists on **unit economics**: the true optimization target is return on capital — human and financial — the "economic arbitrage between what it costs to make more money" and the difficulty of doing it again. He cites a brick-and-mortar client whose small "micro model" had 5x the return on capital of the big-facility model despite lower absolute profit, letting them hit a 10-year goal in 3 years. To land the plane he tells the story of his own most costly mistake: a competitor undercut Gym Launch with cheaper one-on-one coaching and poached ~10 of his top testimonial customers; Alex panicked ("war time"), relaunched to his existing base with a bigger value stack at a *lower* price, cutting top-line ~$500k/month (~$6M/year), raising costs, and losing ~$6-7M/year in profit — with **zero change in churn**, because the cut ($3,000→$2,500/month) was still past the "this is a lot of money" threshold. The chat's first reaction wasn't gratitude but "I can't believe I was paying more." That lost profit, multiplied at sale, cost him roughly $50M. The lesson: don't copy the moron undercutting you — "you will never go out of business focusing on the customer." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

- As of March 2026, Alex advises raising price until willingness to pay stops, then staying there; underpricing ($300 single-day, $1,200 5-day) likely understates true pricing power, which could be closer to 300→3,000 rather than 300→400.
