# How Successful People Use Anxiety as Fuel

## Summary

Alex Hormozi defines anxiety as emerging when there are too many choices and unclear
priorities. He connects anxiety to unresolved decisions, then says anxiety, anger, and
other human emotions can be useful if they drive preparation and action. He uses Leila as
an example of anxiety being channeled into useful output, while describing his own
tendency as more anger-oriented.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex observes anxiety when there are many choices and unclear priorities.
- Anxiety is connected to conflict between options and uncertainty about what to do.
- A decision cuts off options; commitment eliminates alternatives.
- Replaying a familiar video-game level creates less anxiety because the person already
  knows what to do.
- Alex says Leila has a natural proclivity toward anxiety and is very successful.
- He describes his own natural emotional tendency as more anger-oriented.
- He does not frame anxiety or anger as inherently good or bad; he asks what a person gets
  from them.
- Anxiety can serve someone if it causes more preparation and a better performance.
- People may continue to stay anxious or angry as long as those emotions serve them.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Many choices and unclear priorities."

> "Commitment... is the elimination of alternatives."

> "If you can have a negative emotion and make something positive from it, was it a negative emotion?"
