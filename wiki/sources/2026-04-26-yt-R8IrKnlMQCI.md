# Comparison Is the Thief of Joy

## Summary

Alex pushes back on the saying "comparison is the thief of joy." He says comparison is a
valuable feedback tool when separated from insults. Criticism is the gap between expected
and actual or desired and actual; insult is an added character attack. Because comparison
is built into how people orient themselves, the choice is not whether to compare but
whether to compare with envy or study.

## Key claims

- Alex dislikes the quote "comparison is the thief of joy" because comparison can be a
  useful feedback tool.
- He distinguishes criticism from insults: criticism identifies a gap; insult attacks
  character.
- He wants to keep comparison and criticism while eliminating insults.
- Hate or insults can be translated as "he lives his life in a way I would not prefer."
- Alex says criticism is a fixed cost whether someone does nothing or does something.
- Comparison can become either envy or study; study turns the gap into steps for
  improvement.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Comparison is an incredibly valuable feedback tool."

> "Criticism is fact. It is a fixed cost."

> "Comparison as two kind of brothers, study and envy."
