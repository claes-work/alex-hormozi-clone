# Helping a Coaching Business Doing $1M/Year Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. Two young founders run a **soccer training program sold to players who want to go pro** — a ~$1M/year, essentially all-profit, all-organic business built around one of them as the face of the brand, with the other running the back end. They sell a **one-time $397 initial product** to young footballers and want to reach ~$12M ($1M/month) in revenue. They've been told repeatedly it's a "leads issue" (just do more volume), and they worry they're "playing the wrong game" since no one else does it digitally in their space. Alex reframes the whole problem: before touching leads, **fix the offer/monetization**, because they have a one-time-purchase product and want higher LTV — "you either have to make them worth more or you have to sell more of them," and to grow they'll need both. He first surfaces the strategic fork based on what they actually want: **if the goal is an exit, digital "definitely won't sell"** — they'd have to build a real brand and a differentiated physical product (media-heavy, brand-heavy, product-heavy, licensing other soccer faces), which is slower and harder; and building a business *just* to sell it rarely works unless it's a "really boring business" (e.g. HVAC roll-ups). His practical recommendation for making money fast is the **two-triples path**: (1) **add a back end** — call the people who bought the $400 thing and sell a $4,000 higher-touch upsell (maybe 1 in 20, maybe 1 in 4), which roughly triples the business; (2) with the excess cash, **start running paid ads** (they're currently 100% organic) to get the second triple, reaching ~$1M/month. Outcome: profit goes from ~$1M/year on $1M revenue to ~$3–4M/year on ~$10M — "you will not have a sellable company, but you'll make more money." Alternative offer moves he floats: make the $397 recur (annual or six-month), or build a **discount-buying association / network-effect** community (aggregate discounts, ~10x value so a $400/yr fee saves ~$4,000/yr) that gets *stickier and stronger* as it grows, unlike a service business that degrades with scale. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex reframes a "leads issue" as an **offer/monetization issue first**: with a one-time-purchase product, before tackling lead volume you should fix the offer — "you either have to make them worth more or you have to sell more of them," and to grow you probably need both.
- As of March 2026, his fastest, lowest-operational-change path to ~$1M/month is **two triples**: first add a back-end upsell (call existing $400 buyers, sell a $4,000 higher-touch offer), then use that excess cash to fund paid ads.
- As of March 2026, the simplest first move is literally to "call the people who buy the $400 thing and ask them to buy the $4,000 thing" — do the back end before touching the front-end offer or the leads problem.
- As of March 2026, a purely **digital coaching business "definitely won't sell"**; an exit requires building a real brand plus a differentiated (not white-labeled) physical product, which is media-heavy, brand-heavy, and product-heavy.
- As of March 2026, **building a business just to sell it rarely works** unless it's a "really boring business" (e.g. experienced operators rolling up HVAC); the durable path is to consistently provide value people keep paying for, for a long time, until it becomes valuable enough that someone will buy it.
- As of March 2026, sellable/recurring-revenue businesses are more valuable precisely *because* they're "slower and harder to build."
- As of March 2026, alternative LTV moves for the digital product: make the $397 **recurring** (annual or six-month), or add a recurring element to keep it digital.
- As of March 2026, a **discount-buying association** is a strong network-effect model: aim for ~10x value (a ~$400/yr fee saving members ~$4,000/yr) makes it "very, very sticky."
- As of March 2026, the key network vs. service contrast: in a **service business** quality "degrades" as more members are added, whereas in a **network business** the bigger it gets, the more buying/negotiating power it has to secure better aggregate discounts — added members *add* value.
- As of March 2026, if you build a differentiated product and get "any modicum of success," expect China to make dupes and sell the same thing — "that's why brand matters"; people must want to buy because of who you are and what it is, which means not skipping on quality or details.
- As of March 2026, the expected financial outcome of the money-first path: profit rising from ~$1M/year (on ~$1M revenue, near-all profit) to ~$3–4M/year on ~$10M revenue — more money, but a non-sellable company.
