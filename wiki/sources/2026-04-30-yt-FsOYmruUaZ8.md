# Message All 130 Members Personally to Get Engagement

## Summary

Alex gives a new Skool community owner quick engagement tactics. First, personally DM all
130 members and invite them to introduce themselves, tag the owner, and receive a
response, because some people need personal permission and a little status to post.
Second, use Skool's pre-coded event/post formats and picture-based prompts such as
workstations, whiteboards, cars, or other niche-relevant real-world objects.

## Key claims

- Alex recommends personally messaging all members of a small quiet community to prompt
  introductions.
- Some members need personal permission before posting because they feel afraid.
- Inviting members to tag the owner can give them status and encourage engagement.
- Picture prompts help make the digital community feel real.
- Alex recommends using built-in formats such as coffee hour, Q&A, co-working session, or
  happy hour.
- For entrepreneur communities, showing a workstation, laptop, or idea-filled whiteboard
  can work as an engagement prompt.
