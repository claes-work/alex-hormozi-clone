# Double the Work, Eventually You Win

## Summary

Alex contrasts "too big to fail" with becoming too good to fail: exceptional work can
spread from one viewer over enough time. He recommends comparing inputs rather than
views, commissions, or other lagging outputs. A newcomer must exceed the incumbent's
work rate because matching it preserves the existing performance gap.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says sufficiently good work will eventually be discovered if
  each exposed person wants to share it; this is a time-dependent claim, not a guarantee.
- The durable asset is the person's own improved capability, not view counts or status.
- He recommends comparing work per short, calls, practice, tenure, schedule, and weekend
  effort rather than comparing downstream outputs.
- Given a long enough horizon, he expects matched inputs to produce matched outputs.
- A beginner who merely matches an incumbent's input remains behind because both continue
  improving; doubling or tripling repetitions can close and then reverse the gap.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "There's an alternative, which is too good to fail."

> "Measure yourself by how much work he puts into one of his shorts."

> "You have to do twice the work."

## Notes

Reinforces input comparison, improvement rate, and quality-over-views content discipline.
