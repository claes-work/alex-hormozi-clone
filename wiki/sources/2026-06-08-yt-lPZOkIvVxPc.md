# Stop Using a Warm Sales Process on Cold Leads

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a video-testimonial agency selling to mid-market and enterprise B2B software companies. The agency grew through warm inbound and word of mouth, but cold outbound and LinkedIn ad calls close far worse. Alex diagnoses the issue as using a warm sales process on cold leads. He prescribes building for cold: add VSL/pre-frame, set qualification, train salespeople to follow the cold process, and bifurcate the offer into high-quality upfront testimonial production plus an ongoing lower-intensity quantity/UGC review stream.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A warm process used on cold leads underconverts.
- Build for cold and it works everywhere; build for warm and it only works on warm.
- Cold traffic needs a VSL, qualification, and pre-frame before the close call.
- The sales team needs a training cadence to follow the process and improve close rates.
- Testing VSL sections can shorten sales cycles, increase close rates, and improve sales utilization.
- Done-for-you testimonial services can become recurring by splitting quality and quantity offers.
- The upfront "quality" offer captures high-resonance testimonials across key avatars and ICP language.
- The ongoing "quantity" offer keeps fresh proof current through shorter snippets and regular review/UGC-style content.
- This is a big-head, long-tail structure: upfront premium production plus recurring proof maintenance.
