# Helping an $11M Legal Services Business Get to $25M

## Summary

Alex coaches an $11M asset-protection legal-services business trying to reach $25M. He
separates its two constraints: rising PPC costs and hiring salespeople who understand
legal concepts. For acquisition, he says PPC is strong but capped by auction economics,
so growth likely comes from LTV expansion and top-of-funnel education for people not yet
in lawsuits. He recommends social ads into virtual or in-person workshops, especially
local in-person events, then selling from the room.

## Key claims

- Rising PPC costs are an auction problem; winning requires increasing LTV or finding
  additional acquisition channels.
- PPC captures red-hot intent, but preventative legal buyers need more education.
- Alex recommends interruption-based social ads into workshops for complex legal offers.
- For local in-person workshops, he suggests lead generation, phone scheduling, heavy
  reminders, and expecting roughly 20-25% of leads to show.
- He says the business might close about a third of the room after an in-person workshop.
- After dialing in the local pitch, the business could move to online and then national.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "PPC is going to be intent, red hot pain."

> "I would probably go social ads to workshop."

> "Remind the hell out of them."
