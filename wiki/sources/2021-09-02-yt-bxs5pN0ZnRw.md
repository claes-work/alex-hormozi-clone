# This is my plan to become a BILLIONAIRE

## Summary
Solo talking-head video in which Hormozi lays out his stated plan to grow Acquisition.com to a billion dollars a year in revenue and explains why he gives his educational material away for free rather than selling courses. He argues the traditional education system is broken, that demand for high-value skills is unmet, and that his highest-leverage contribution is transferring the skills that built his portfolio at zero cost — announcing the release of the first of ten planned free books/courses, starting with $100M Offers.

## Key claims
- (2021) His plan: help many entrepreneurs reach $1M–$5M/year for free, and once they cross roughly the $5M threshold and want to scale to $30M–$100M+, offer to invest in their businesses — that is the monetization path to a billion-dollar portfolio.
- (2021) He does not sell courses because a $1,000 course would not be meaningful to him; his portfolio companies already fund his lifestyle, so he prices his teaching at zero.
- (2021) The education system is broken: paying $50k–$200k/year for a degree that adds zero marginal earning power is a bad, irreversible, debt-saddled deal.
- (2021) The "guru space" exploded over ~10–15 years because demand for high-value skills is real but unsatisfied; technology "democratizes consumption and consolidates production" (quoting Naval Ravikant), so the best can serve everyone.
- (2021) His thesis: education will become increasingly fragmented and niche (plumbers, gym owners, online trainers, life coaches), and he wants to supply those high-value skills.
- (2021) He plans to release at least 10 books/courses in sequence — offers, lead generation, money models, sales, scaling sales teams, pricing/lifetime gross profit — mirroring the order in which a business is built. All free, no opt-in, on the site and as a YouTube playlist.
- (2021) Product is the number one determinant of a company's success, not marketing; "you make a sale to get a customer" (relationship, repeat purchases), not "get customers to make sales."
- (2021) Value is the discrepancy ("customer surplus") between what a customer pays and what they get; a good deal is about value, not price.
- (2021) His release cadence: a course ships only when it is meaningfully better than what came before ("the difference between good and great is vast").
