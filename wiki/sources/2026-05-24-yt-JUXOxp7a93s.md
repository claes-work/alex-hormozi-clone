# I Don't Gain Confidence by Affirmations, Here's What Works Instead

## Summary

Alex Hormozi rejects affirmation-based confidence and says confidence comes from proof
that the work has been done. He uses the launch preparation for *$100M Money Models* as
his example: drafts, rewrites, presentation rehearsal, and large-scale registration gave
him confidence because he could judge that he had done everything he reasonably could.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Confidence comes from demonstrated evidence, not from repeating positive statements.
- Someone who feels unprepared should reduce self-doubt by doing more preparation.
- Alex says the *$100M Money Models* launch had about 500,000 registrants at the time of
  this clip.
- He self-reports writing 19 drafts of the book, doing 4 full rewrites, and working from
  6 a.m. to noon for about 2 years on it.
- He says the launch presentation had about 900 slides for a 60-minute presentation.
- He rehearsed the presentation mentally every day and recorded full run-throughs to get
  feedback.
- His self-esteem standard is whether he can honestly say there was nothing else he could
  have done.
- The practical antidote to self-doubt is more work, more preparation, and stronger proof.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Outwork your self-doubt."

> "I'm the ultimate judge, jury, and executioner of my own self-esteem."
