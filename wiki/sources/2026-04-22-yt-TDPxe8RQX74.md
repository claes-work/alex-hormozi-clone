# Everyone Who Doubts You Is Right Until They Aren't

## Summary

Alex reflects on the lonely middle of pursuing a hard path. He says people often cheer
at the beginning because the beginner temporarily lowers status, then support fades
after the person passes them but before they reach a higher-status peer group. The pain
comes from not knowing whether the sacrifice will be worth it until much later.

## Key claims

- Alex says people tend to root for others at the beginning and after the finish, but
  not during the middle where support is most needed.
- He says some people were happy for him to try because trying temporarily made him
  lower status than them.
- He says support can be proportional to where the supporter is on the status ladder
  relative to the person succeeding.
- He says the middle is lonely because one leaves the original peer group before joining
  the next group of proven operators.
- He says the hard part is uncertainty: the person does not know whether years of
  sacrifice will pay off.
- He frames the decision as placing a bet whose outcome may only be known years later.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have to master the middle."

> "I needed it in the middle."

> "You just don't know how it's going to finish."
