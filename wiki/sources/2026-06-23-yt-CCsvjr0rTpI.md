# Would You Rather Start Over With Muscle or Money?

## Summary

In this very short Q&A, Alex Hormozi is asked whether he would rather start over with muscle or money if he had nothing. After calling the question difficult, he chooses muscle, not because of ego, but because he says his current training and size likely shorten his life. He frames extra muscle beyond a certain size as additional strain on the organs.

## Key claims (Alex's answer only)

- Alex says he would rather start over with muscle than money.
- His reason is longevity: starting with muscle would, in his framing, let him live longer relative to his current size/training.
- He says his current training shortens his life.
- He says after a certain size, each additional pound of muscle puts more strain on the organs.
- He links that risk to why very large bodybuilders often die young, while noting there are other causes too.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "That might be the best question I've had this year."

> "Muscle."

> "I know that if I started over with muscle, I would live longer."

> "Everything that I do for training literally shortens my life."

> "After certain size, every additional pound of muscle will just put more strain on your organs."
