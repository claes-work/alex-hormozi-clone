# Agencies Charge Wrong (Here's the Fix)

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a Canadian performance-marketing and demand-generation agency doing about $2M. Alex diagnoses the agency as underpriced after moving upmarket: low churn and high close rates suggest that the market can bear a higher price. He recommends raising the base fee and adding non-retroactive ad-spend tiers so incremental spend creates incremental margin without giving back revenue from lower tiers.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Coachee** - performance-marketing agency founder. His revenue, client count, churn, close rate, and pricing are context only.
- **Jean-Francois** - VP of demand generation. Context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- An agency serving mid-market healthcare and medtech companies at $3.5K-$10K monthly retainers is probably mispriced.
- Moving upmarket without changing price creates a cash-flow constraint that makes hiring and marketing investments feel riskier.
- High annual retention and high close rates are evidence that the agency is probably undercharging.
- Alex's rough pricing guide treats about a 35% close rate as closer to the right price; 60%-70% close rates imply at least a possible double in price.
- Being "too cheap" can reduce credibility with legitimate mid-market buyers.
- Founders can sell out of their own wallet and underestimate what larger customers are willing to pay.
- A better model is a higher base fee plus ad-spend-based tiers that decline by tier but are not retroactive.
- The fee should be structured as the greater of a minimum base or the tiered spend fee, with a minimum ad spend requirement for customers.
