# How to Scale a $3M Sports Memorabilia Business to $10M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a UK sports-memorabilia seller stuck around $3M. Alex first separates conversion-rate problems from traffic-quality problems, then identifies the missing skill as media buying. The founder is good at product sourcing and arbitrage, but the business needs a strong media buyer, potentially compensated with cash plus equity, before later building the brand into a household name.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - UK sports-memorabilia seller. His revenue, profit, product sourcing, conversion rate, and target are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A low ecommerce conversion rate can mean the website is weak, but it can also mean the wrong traffic is being sent to the site.
- The easiest way to improve conversion can be to get better prospects.
- The founder appears strong at supply/product sourcing because he can identify memorabilia arbitrage.
- Once ecommerce supply is solved, the core missing skill is usually media buying and then brand building.
- A good media buyer could move the business from $3M to materially higher revenue quickly if the supply side can keep up.
- With only about 12% margin, paying a top media buyer may require using equity or other compensation levers, not just salary.
- Ecommerce owners Alex sees tend to be either product/brand focused or hardcore media buyers; this founder sits between those profiles and needs the missing skill.
- Brand becomes the next skill after more sales and revenue are driven through media buying.
