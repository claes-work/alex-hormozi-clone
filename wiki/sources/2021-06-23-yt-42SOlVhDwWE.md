# 1 SIMPLE mental technique that will SKYROCKET your sales (2021)

## Summary

In this early (2021) sales-psychology talk, Hormozi argues that the single most
underused lever in selling is **conviction** — reframing "do you believe in what
you sell?" from a binary yes/no into a *continuum* ("to what extent do you
believe?"). He models sales as a **transference of belief**: a salesperson can't
pour a full cup of belief into a prospect from a cup that is only one-third full.
He contends conviction outweighs raw sales skill and is built by deeply
understanding the prospect's problems (not the product), and that it counts twice
because belief also drives follow-up and work ethic.

## Key claims (paraphrase; dated 2021-06-23)

- The ONE technique is the **"belief continuum"**: belief in what you sell is not
  binary (believe / don't) but a matter of degree — the real question is *to what
  extent* you believe. (2021)
- Sales is fundamentally a **transference of belief** from salesperson to prospect;
  you cannot transfer belief the prospect doesn't yet have if your own belief is
  low. (2021)
- Closing percentage ≈ **skill of sales × extent of belief (conviction)**. Skill
  effectively ranges ~1–10, but conviction can range ~1–100, so a low-skill rep
  with 100 belief will still close. (2021)
- Conviction is built by understanding the **prospect and their problems**, not by
  studying the product; most sales training wrongly focuses on educating reps about
  the product. (2021)
- Most salespeople don't listen — they just deliver a pitch — which means they
  can't be convicted the product solves a problem they never identified. (2021)
- Total performance has **three variables — skill, conviction, and work ethic — but
  conviction counts twice**, because genuine belief also makes reps follow up faster
  and more persistently. (2021)
- Prospects can **sense** whether the salesperson truly believes; belief is felt, not
  just stated. (2021)
- Analogy to religious movements: they sell an extremely hard product (a whole new
  worldview) largely by instilling wholehearted belief, often without formal
  selling-skill training. (2021)
- Thought experiment: if you went back 5 years to tell your past self to invest
  everything in Bitcoin/Amazon/Tesla, your total conviction would make you follow up
  relentlessly — that is the posture to sell from. (2021)
- Echoing Simon Sinek's "start with why," Hormozi says salespeople need a *why*;
  fix belief first and skill/follow-up largely take care of themselves. (2021)

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It would be much more accurate to say to what extent do you believe... because
> belief is not a binary question, it is a continuum."

> "If you think about what sales is, it's fundamentally a transference of belief."

> "How can you transfer a cup that is only one third full into another empty cup and
> expect it to be over the threshold of the amount of belief that's required for a
> prospect to make a purchasing decision?"

> "You've got skill, you've got conviction, and you've got work ethic, but conviction
> counts twice."

> "People can sense whether you believe what you are telling them is true."

> "If you can get that to 100 you will never have problems selling ever again."

## Topic notes

Relates to [[../topics/business/sales-communication]]. That page (built from
2025 sources) documents the *delivery* layer — tone, script, discovery, closes —
and states an underperforming rep either says the wrong words (script) or the right
words the wrong way (tone). This 2021 source sits *upstream* of both: it addresses
the **internal belief state** that precedes words and tone. Two through-lines are
already consistent across the years:

- **Understand the prospect, not just the product.** The 2025 "discovery" material
  (matching prospect problems to products, "people believe what they say") is the
  tactical execution of the 2021 claim that conviction comes from deeply knowing the
  prospect's problems.
- **Belief transfer.** The 2021 "transference of belief" thesis prefigures the 2025
  emphasis on getting the prospect to state their own problem so *they* supply the
  belief.

Note the evolution: in 2021 Hormozi weights **conviction over skill** ("I don't
think they are equivalent") and even downplays script/tonality drilling as
"monotony"; by 2025 ([[../topics/business/sales-communication]]) he treats a
memorized script as the non-negotiable foundation ("no one is above the script").
The through-line survives — belief still matters — but the relative emphasis shifts
toward disciplined skill/scripting over the years.
