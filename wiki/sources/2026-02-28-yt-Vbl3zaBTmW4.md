# The Fastest Way to Learn a New Skill

## Summary
Solo clip. Alex describes his fastest method for learning a new skill outside of formal courses: **tremendous volume of activity followed by structured pattern-extraction**. Pick the activity (sales calls, outreach, content, customer-success calls) and do ~100 reps. Then examine the **top 10%** of those reps and ask what those winners had in common that the other 90% lacked — say, three shared traits. Deliberately apply those three things across the *next* 100 reps. Because of "the variety of life," new variables surface: in the next batch's top 10% you find two more differentiators. Now do all five. Iterating this way builds a **checklist** for producing "banger" content and videos. He illustrates with a David Perell clip on **Chris Rock's** creative process: Rock's polished 60-minute Madison Square Garden set exists because, unseen, he runs new hour-long sets in small clubs where most of the material bombs; he keeps the ~5 minutes that land, moves them to the front, and tries all-new material for the remaining 55 — repeating until he has 60 uninterrupted minutes of laughs. That, Alex says, is fundamentally the process of learning anything: not one big thing, but **many small things that compound in aggregate** into outsized returns. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (the 10-stage zero-to-$100M+ roadmap).
