# You Don't Need the US Market to Build a $20M Business

## Summary

Alex advises a French HR professional in the short-term-rental staffing niche not to
split focus across French paid/free communities and an English free community. He says to
choose one market, preferably France, because that is where the founder has language,
proof, and context. He pushes back on the assumption that a founder must start in the US:
a smaller national market can still support a very large business before expansion.

## Key claims

- Alex recommends focusing on one market instead of starting with scattered French and
  English community offers.
- For this founder, he recommends France because it is the market she understands and
  where she has proof.
- A founder does not need to start in the US just because it is larger.
- Alex frames France as large enough to support a multimillion-dollar business before
  expansion.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I would focus on one market."

> "Stick to France."

> "You can build a million-dollar business in France."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: one-market focus over US-market FOMO; proof/context beats theoretical TAM.
