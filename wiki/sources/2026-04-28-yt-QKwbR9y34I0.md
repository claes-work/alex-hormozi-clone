# Remove the Emojis From Your Ads

## Summary

Alex reviews manifestation ads and says paid ads should look like native content. Emojis
once looked native but now can signal "marketer," so he suggests removing them. He pushes
the copy toward pain-led hooks, benefit-of-benefit phrasing, simple writing, relevant
analogies, and process-based creative instead of generic AI-style imagery.

## Key claims

- Paid ads should look and feel like content so people do not scroll past them as ads.
- Emojis may now make ads look more like marketer copy because the market has copied the
  tactic.
- Labels such as "manifestors" may fail if the audience does not self-identify with them.
- Alex recommends leading with pain and implied yeses rather than generic prosperity
  claims.
- Copy should turn features into benefits and then into benefits of benefits.
- He recommends third-grade-or-less simplicity, short sentences, fewer commas, and clear
  analogies matched to the audience.
- Ads should provide value before purchase and should explain process/implementation more
  than rely on colorful abstract images.
