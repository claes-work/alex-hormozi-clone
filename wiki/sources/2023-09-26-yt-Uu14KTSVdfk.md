# Alex Hormozi's Top 3 Sales Hacks to Print Money

## Summary
A short sales-tactics clip in which Alex Hormozi lays out three concrete techniques to
close more deals at higher prices: a negative-then-positive "but" reversal (a persuasion
move he says he learned from how Eminem "owns all the negatives"), high-anchoring by
inventing a service level 10x above your most expensive offer, and reframing a price
objection as evidence the buyer will succeed. Alex is the only speaker; the entire clip
is direct persona/sales-craft material.

## Key claims
- **Hack 1 — Own the negatives with a "but" reversal.** Sentences built around "but" pair
  a good and a bad statement, and "but" signals a contrast. Most people say *good* but
  *bad*, which lands negatively. In persuasion you flip it: put the negative first, then
  reverse into the positive — e.g. "our program's really hard, it's really long, it's
  honestly very complicated, but it works better than anything you've ever tried before."
  He credits the "own all the negatives about yourself so no one else can" idea to Eminem's
  rap technique.
- **Hack 2 — Anchor high to shift the buying curve.** Rather than presenting prices as
  usual, invent/create a service level 10x higher than your most expensive thing. One of
  two outcomes follows: a rare "whale"/luxury buyer picks the most expensive option (run to
  the bank), or — far more commonly — the buying curve shifts up and more people take the
  second-highest option (formerly your highest) because it was anchored against the new most
  expensive thing. The first number a person sees becomes the psychological reference point;
  every later number is compared to it and seems smaller by relation.
- **Hack 3 — Align the objection with buying behavior.** When a prospect objects (e.g.
  "this seems like a lot of money"), agree, then re-label the objection as a success
  indicator: the people who succeed most are the ones who make the biggest relative
  investment. If the amount means a lot to them, that is why he "knows" they'll be
  successful; if it meant nothing, they'd be less likely to succeed. Whatever the objection
  is, associate it with the label of someone who is going to win.
