# If You Post on Instagram, Do This

## Summary

Short mastermind/room Q&A clip. Alex Hormozi polls the room on who uses Instagram as a primary customer channel and who uses ManyChat (Instagram comment-to-DM automation). He states his view that ManyChat is currently the most efficient way to convert an eyeball into a CTA — the "comment a keyword, I'll DM you the link" mechanic. An audience member (owner of a "creative AI education company" that drives Skool/community signups) then shares a tactic upgrade they tested across ~500 posts: instead of DMing a bare link, DM a **PDF document of actionable resources** (hosted on Drive) that contains the hyperlink to whatever you're selling, plus a bottom-of-PDF CTA pointing to more education (their Skool). They report the PDF "does way better than just a link." Alex restates the full mechanic back for the audience and endorses it. The clip closes with Alex's standard plug for his free acquisition.com/roadmap resource.

> ⚠️ **Attribution**: The concrete PDF-lead-magnet upgrade is the AUDIENCE MEMBER's tactic and result, not Alex's — do NOT feed it into `persona/` as Alex belief/voice data. Only Alex's own ManyChat framing (below) is persona-eligible.

## Key claims

**Alex-attributed (persona-eligible):**
- ManyChat (Instagram comment-to-DM automation) is, in his current view, the most efficient conversion of an eyeball into a CTA — the mechanic being: audience comments a keyword, the tool auto-DMs them the link. (paraphrase; see verbatim)

**Audience-member-attributed (context / tactic, NOT Alex persona data):**
- They have run ManyChat on their last ~500 posts; people find "just sending a link" the easiest action to take. (paraphrase)
- Upgrade that beat a bare link: DM a **PDF of actionable resources** with a hyperlink to the thing you're selling embedded in it; at the bottom, a CTA to their Skool ("check out our school for more education"). (paraphrase)
- Concrete instance: their creative-AI-education company DMs the images and prompts used in a given video, so people can access them, with the school CTA at the bottom. (paraphrase)
- Reported result: the PDF approach "does way better than just a link" and is "killing it." (paraphrase — self-reported by the audience member, uncited, unverifiable)
