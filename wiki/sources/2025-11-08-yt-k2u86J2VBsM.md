# How To Use $100M Leads To Get More Real Estate Deals

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi answers Richard, a real estate developer
running value-add / teardown-and-land deals who asks how to apply the $100M Leads
principles to source more deals (not more investor capital). Alex reframes the problem
as a targeted advertising challenge: because the buy-box is so narrow, outreach — not
broad content — should be the primary channel, backed by just enough content to signal
legitimacy and a broker network built patiently over years. He stresses in-person
conferences and warm introductions as the highest-leverage move in small, top-heavy
industries, while candidly noting there is no single secret method.

## Key claims (Alex's advice)

- Real estate development is analogous to scaling a private-equity firm like Acquisition.com;
  the same leads thinking applies.
- First distinguish which "lead" you actually need — deals or investors. Richard already
  uses his own capital, so the goal is **more deals**.
- For a highly specialized buy-box (specific size, specific area), this is fundamentally an
  **advertising problem**: build a brand around the niche.
- **Outreach should be the primary method** of attracting deals; don't expect content alone
  to generate volume because the target is so narrow.
- Maintain a **base level of content** — enough that people can see you're legit and do this
  for a living — as support, not as the main engine.
- In small industries that are "very aggregate at the top," **attending conferences** and
  meeting as many people as possible is one of the most effective tactics; Alex would aim for
  roughly one conference a week if starting over.
- The real value of networking is the **second-degree introduction**: "it's not the person
  you meet, it's the person that person introduced you to."
- After conferences, **outbound and follow-up** are the next priorities.
- A few big wins compound — good deals attract more attention and more capital.
- Deal flow largely comes from **building a broker network** over time; Alex's own team,
  after focusing on one market for ~six years, now has brokers bringing off-market deals to
  them first because they know the team can close.
- It's a long-horizon business; there is no one-secret shortcut.
