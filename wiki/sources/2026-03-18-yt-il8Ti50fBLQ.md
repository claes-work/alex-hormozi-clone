# Helping a Nail Salon Build a Franchise Model

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A premium nail-salon owner in San Antonio, TX runs one location doing ~$1.5M revenue at ~25% net margin (labor ~50% of revenue, down from ~70% before two price increases); she's also a social-media influencer who has never spent a dollar on ads in five years and wants to build an "empire/ecosystem" — educate nail techs, sell her gel/product line, help them open their own salons — while asking how to juggle these layers without burning out. Alex's diagnosis: **she's doing too much, trying to "protect an entire block" alone.** He separates *vision* (which he says isn't wrong) from *plan*, and endorses **franchising the salon model** using her media as lead gen — "just franchise the model out… have people directly buy franchises of nail salons from you, as long as you nail the model." His key economic caution: the model isn't actually "nailed" yet because her margins and demand are propped up by her personal influencer following. A normal brick-and-mortar running paid ads spends **10–30% of revenue on new-client acquisition**; strip out her free social traffic and force the model to stand alone (as a buyer would have to) and "your margin would be zero." So the requirements before franchising: get net margin to **30%+**, make the salon acquire customers **without** her social media, and **pull herself completely out of operations** so it truly runs on its own — then she pours time into a national social sales motion to sell the model. His franchising thesis: reframe from "coach" to **"educator online"** who sells a **~$250k franchise** and takes **a percentage of sales forever** — this aligns incentives, attracts higher-quality operators, and "fundamentally franchising is just a capital play" to open 100–1,000 locations over ten years using *other people's capital*. On quality control (her worry that franchisees need her exact detailed technique): the answer is **training** — "that's your hard problem… and then you'll get paid a hundred million dollars for solving it." He cites a five-location nail-salon chain he did diligence on running **43% net margins** with a **gamified system** getting technicians (many without high-school diplomas) to six-figure incomes, loyal, no founder magic, no social media — proof that far better economics are achievable. He closes noting the business is "AI-proof" in the near term but that humanoid robots may eventually do nails (train once, finance for ~$200/month, run 24/7). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (excluded from claims).
