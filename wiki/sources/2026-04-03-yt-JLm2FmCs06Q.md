# You Need to Be Thinking Bigger

## Summary

Alex argues that a sufficiently larger goal forces a different solution set. Running
harder may reach one block faster, but reaching five blocks suggests using a car. In the
same way, a $100,000 target often assumes selling more hours, while a $1 million or $10
million target forces the learner to identify leverage and higher-value skills.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says bigger goals can be easier because they require a different
  tool rather than more intensity within the existing method.
- A person can improve running form, clothing, and footwear only until physical effort
  reaches its ceiling; a more distant destination changes the vehicle.
- He calls $100,000 a particularly hard income level because many pursue it through
  hourly wages or salary and consume nearly all available time.
- A $1 million or $10 million constraint makes the inadequacy of pure time-trading obvious.
- The practical task is identifying and learning the skills required for the larger
  outcome rather than merely increasing hours.
- "Easier" is a strategic framing about leverage, not a claim that high income requires
  little effort or carries guaranteed results.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The magic of thinking bigger is that you change your solution set."

> "You use different tools to accomplish the objective."

> "Start solving for the skills required to make a million or ten million dollars."
