# "If you think like this, you will stay poor and unhappy…" (2022-03-02)

## Summary

An 11-minute cut from Hormozi's ~2-hour interview on Graham Stephan's podcast, framed
around money, happiness, and meaning. Hormozi argues that stating a desire is "a contract
we make with ourselves to be unhappy until we get what we want," and pushes a nihilist
position: exit the equation of labeling yourself (worthy/unworthy, happy/unhappy) rather
than trying to flip negatives into positives. The other participants (Graham and a second
interviewer) push back throughout, making this a debate, not a monologue.

## Key claims

- **Desire creates a happiness deficit.** Naming a desire ("I want to be happy," "I want
  meaning") sets an expectation against reality and guarantees dissonance; better to let
  things "just be" — pure acceptance without judgment. (paraphrase)
- **Exit the equation, don't flip it.** For someone who feels worthless, the move is not
  positive affirmations ("I am worthy") but declaring worthiness itself irrelevant —
  removing the label entirely. (paraphrase)
- **Self-professed nihilism as freedom.** He states he is "100% a nihilist"; believing
  nothing ultimately matters releases you from "the chains of the expectations of others"
  and enables risk-taking and clarity most people can't access. (paraphrase)
- **Deservingness is decoupled from success.** No one deserves anything; a terrible person
  can still do the things that produce success. Success follows actions, not merit. (paraphrase)
- **Money changed nothing internally.** He reports feeling the same after acquiring wealth
  as he did with nothing — "I felt nothing" when the money hit the account. (paraphrase)
