# 'I Do Client Acquisition for High-Ticket Agencies. How Do I Get to $1M/Month?'

## Summary

Short live-coaching clip from @MoreMozi. An audience member (not Alex) runs a
done-for-you client-acquisition business for high-ticket marketing agencies:
he contracts with agencies and runs their sales (managing sales teams, not the
ads/delivery) to acquire agency clients. He reports $5M topline, 31 clients, and
wants to scale to $1M/month with a ~$30M EBITDA exit as the end goal. He names his
constraint as talent — specifically **sales directors** — claiming the qualified
talent pool (TAM) is shrinking.

Alex reframes the diagnosis. Given the questioner's own unit economics (~$60K to
acquire a sales director vs. ~$600K/year gross profit each), the recruiting math
already works ~10:1, so Alex pushes on why he isn't simply doing far more of it.
Alex's core argument: the "shortage of unicorn sales directors" is a self-imposed
belief, not a real constraint. He suggests (a) recruiting from adjacent talent pools
like door-to-door salespeople, who already build and manage hard-selling teams, and
(b) refining the sales script so the process is less consultative — which lowers the
skill bar, removes the need for a $60K specialist hire, and massively widens the pool
of viable candidates ("the arbitrage gets juicy"). He notes sales directors are
primarily culture and training, not phone work, so they need *less* product knowledge
than frontline reps. He closes on span-of-control (15 reps per director is high; he
prefers ~10, splits teams around 7–8) and points the questioner to the sales handbook
from the event.

The questioner's business-model numbers are self-reported and unverified; only Alex's
answers are treated as persona data below.

## Key claims
*(All dated 2026-02-21. Alex-attributed unless marked. Paraphrased unless quoted.)*

- The named constraint (not enough qualified sales directors) is not a real constraint
  — it is a belief/self-imposed limit.
- When acquiring a role earns ~10x its cost (questioner self-reports ~$60K to acquire a
  sales director worth ~$600K/year gross profit), the correct question is "what stops
  you from doing far more of that?" — recruiting spend should scale, not be treated as
  capped.
- A shrinking talent pool ("TAM is dwindling") is usually solvable by widening the
  candidate pool rather than fighting over the same narrow one.
- Adjacent talent pools are undervalued: door-to-door salespeople already build teams
  and sell harder, in worse conditions, than selling from home on a computer — so the
  only real learning curve is teaching them the category's language, not media/delivery.
- A too-consultative sales process forces reliance on rare "unicorn" salespeople; a more
  refined, repeatable script lets ordinary trained reps succeed and expands the hirable
  TAM. ★ (specific arbitrage framing — L3-candidate for topics/sales)
- Objection handling should loop questions back to the "straight line" and keep the
  prospect moving, rather than opening consultative digressions.
- Salespeople do not need to know more about the prospect's business than the prospect
  does; they only need to know how to sell how the company can help. (Alex cites his own
  team's average sales rep age being ~23 as evidence the skill is the selling, not deep
  product mastery.)
- A sales director's job is primarily maintaining culture and driving training daily —
  not taking calls; they arguably need *less* product knowledge than frontline reps
  because "teaching sales is teaching sales."
- Span of control: ~15 reps per director is on the high side; Alex prefers ~10 and would
  start splitting a team once two managers each have ~7–8 reps.
- If the sales numbers are already healthy, still address the scripts — the goal is to
  remove the dependency on hard-to-find specialists, not to fix a broken funnel.
- **Questioner self-reported (audience, unverified):** ~$5M topline; 31 clients;
  clients pay ~$12–15K/mo (one at ~$27K); serves "middle-market" agencies (~$1–5M/yr)
  that lack an in-house sales director; ~$15K/mo recruiting spend across organic, paid
  (Indeed), and LinkedIn; each director manages ~3 clients / 3 sales pods of 5–6 reps.
