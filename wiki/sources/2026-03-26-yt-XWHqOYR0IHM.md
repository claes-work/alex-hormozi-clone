# Champions See Their Past as Their Origin Story

## Summary
A short mindset/reframe clip in which Hormozi contrasts "victims," who see their past as their fate, with "champions," who see the same past as an origin story — the only difference between the two being the actions they choose to take now. He extends this into a "the past doesn't exist" frame (crediting Joe Rogan), arguing that the past lives only as chemicals in the brain and that the sole thing anyone controls is present action, which makes the present feel light and baggage-free. A second voice introduces Tim Urban's "tree of time" illustration (one black branch already taken, many unrealized green branches, and an unlimited fan of future possibilities) as a visual for the same idea. The clip closes with a standard promotional read for his free "$100 scaling roadmap" at acquisition.com/roadmap.

## Key claims
- Victims see their past as their fate; champions see their past as their origin story — the same situation can produce either.
- The only difference between the two characters is the actions they choose to take.
- Using "Joe Rogan's frame," when you wake up as the main player character in the present moment, the past does not exist — it is "just gone."
- The only thing we absolutely control is the actions we take right now.
- The past exists only as chemicals in the brain, not in the present — an "eraser of time" reframe.
- The frame is freeing: you get to start with no baggage, which makes the present feel light because you carry nothing into it.
- Tim Urban's "tree of time" shows one path actually taken (black branch) among many that could have been (green branches), then, at "now," an open fan of potential and possible futures.
- "It doesn't matter what happened yesterday" or in childhood — "it only matters if you make it matter."
