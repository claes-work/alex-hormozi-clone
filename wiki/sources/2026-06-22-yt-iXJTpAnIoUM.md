# $4M Gym Owner Asks Hormozi What's Stopping Him From $10M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a gym-growth-platform founder doing about $4M revenue and $2.2M profit. Alex walks through metrics, estimates current revenue ceiling from sales velocity and LTV, and says the founder should build fulfillment capacity before scaling ads harder. The call then turns personal: Alex reflects on why he moved on from Gym Launch, saying he was no longer in love with the avatar, became resentful, and did not yet see how large Gym Launch could have become.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - gym-growth-platform founder. His revenue, profit, CAC, churn, ads, fulfillment constraint, and acquisition offers are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Current sales velocity and LTV imply a hypothetical max near the current business size, so growth depends on scaling CAC and sales velocity.
- If fulfillment is already strained, the founder should build capacity before pushing demand much harder.
- Building capacity protects reputation and ethics even if it reduces short-term profit.
- At a capacity constraint, the founder is choosing between sacrificing profit or sacrificing reputation/metrics.
- Alex says the founder appears to have a nine-figure-plus opportunity in the current gym-growth-platform business.
- Alex says he moved on from Gym Launch when he was no longer in love with the avatar.
- He describes becoming resentful of "what have you done for me lately" dynamics with clients.
- He says he did not see at the time how Gym Launch could become bigger, and that is why he sold it.
- In hindsight, Alex says Gym Launch could have exited for $400M, $500M, or even a billion-dollar range if he knew then what he knows now.
