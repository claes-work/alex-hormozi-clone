# Don't Wish for Strong Character and an Easy Life

## Summary
In this short clip, Alex Hormozi argues that hardship is the price of a strong character and that many perceived "problems" are simply facts of life one must habituate to. He offers a time-based reframe: because today's problems will look trivial in ten years, the only thing separating the present from the future is perspective. He recasts problems that come with success — such as lawsuits — as evidence of being on the chosen path rather than as obstacles.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that a strong character and an easy life are mutually exclusive — each is the price of the other.
- As of March 2026, Alex suggests that many things people experience as "hard" are not actually problems but their own sensitivity, and that the difference between struggling now and being at peace later is only perspective.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames certain problems as requisites of success: he cites that Tesla has hundreds of lawsuits and large legal departments at any given time, and argues no one becomes the wealthiest person in the world without getting sued.
- As of March 2026, Alex reframes getting sued (and problems generally) as a point of evidence that one is on the path they originally chose, rather than as a setback.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You cannot wish for a strong character and an easy life. Each is the price of the other."

> "What if what you're going through isn't hard? What if you're just sensitive?"

> "10 years from now Alex will look back at the problems that I'm dealing with today and feel the same way ... the only difference is the perspective that he has that I don't have."

> "When you're in business long enough, you make enough money, you're just going to get sued. It is what it is."

> "You cannot become the wealthiest man in the world and not get sued. Getting sued is a requisite for being there. No person has been there without this."
