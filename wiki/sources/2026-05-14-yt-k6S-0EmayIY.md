# How to Stop Overcomplicating Simple Success

## Summary

Alex uses the midwit meme to argue that many domains return to simple basics after a
period of overcomplication. In fitness, he summarizes the basics as genetics, protein,
and progressive overload. In marketing, he says persuasion happens in specificity: if a
marketer can articulate a customer's pain more accurately than the customer can, the
customer will infer the marketer can solve it.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Many domains move from simple basics to overcomplicated theory and then back to simple
  fundamentals.
- In fitness, Alex reduces the fundamentals to genetics, protein, and lifting heavier
  over time.
- In marketing, persuasion happens in the specific rather than the vague.
- A prospect is more likely to buy when the seller articulates the prospect's pain in
  more detail than the prospect can.
- Pain is more motivating than promise.
- The pitch is the pain when that pain is specific, emotionally resonant, and easy to
  process.
- Great marketers can sometimes advertise pain they have not personally experienced, but
  most good marketers know the avatar because they are or were the avatar.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "No persuasion occurs in the vague."

> "Pain is more motivating than promise."

> "The pain is the pitch."
