# 7 Ways to Get People to Buy More Times

## Summary

Solo talk in which Alex frames business growth as two levers — doubling customer
count or doubling customer worth (LTV) — and focuses on the second. He shares seven
retention/purchase-frequency tactics distilled from surveying the top 1% lowest-churn
gyms in Gym Launch (5,000+ licensed locations), plus two his own team added, which cut
Gym Launch's churn from 11% to 6% monthly and made the company sellable. He closes with
the LTV math (halving churn doubles LTV and more than doubles profit) and a
bang-for-buck implementation order.

## Key claims

The seven tactics to get customers to buy more / stay longer:

1. **Exit interviews** — obligate a cancellation interview up front ("just FYI, when
   you choose to cancel, we ask you do an exit interview"). Frame it as improving the
   product. Use the "angry boat" rule (get angrier at the *problem* than the customer
   is) to validate them; then offer to credit money already paid toward a saved stay,
   or upsell them into a higher tier (e.g. silver → gold) because they actually needed
   more help. Saves ~half of cancellers and can turn a churned customer into a more
   valuable one, killing "revenue churn."
2. **Reach outs** — systematized personal check-ins (every 14–21 days) that approximate
   a real relationship; not about selling, just "how's life." Beats Dunbar's number
   (~150 relationships) by assigning ~40 customers per trainer (10–15 reach-outs/week).
   "It's a lot easier to quit a membership than it is to quit a relationship." Also acts
   as an early-warning system / faster front-line feedback.
3. **Member events** — events show you care and double as lead gen (customers bring
   friends). Make a crazy offer to the friends; referrals are worth 2–3x a cold
   customer. Requires an **open loop**: always announce the next event during the
   current one, or people extract the value and leave.
4. **Handwritten cards** — sent roughly every ~6 weeks (coincides with event cadence).
   Signed, personal; the key is a **P.S.** telling them to photograph the card and text
   it to a friend in a three-way thread who gets invited to the (themed) event — ties
   the referral mechanic to the event.
5. **Attendance tracking** — track consumption to get an *early indicator* before churn
   (churn is a lagging indicator). "The easiest correlation with cancellation is lack of
   consumption." When someone drops a session, reach out — and with customers you
   *escalate* contact (1 time, then 2, then 3) rather than de-escalate like a cold lead.
   "A gym membership you never use is never worth it," no matter how cheap.
6. **Onboarding** — a "silent killer." Add a step after the close: reconfirm the buying
   decision (first 24h is where cold feet/chargebacks happen), set and meet expectations
   repeatedly, and give a roadmap + walkthrough. Set-and-meet in small increments (daily)
   rather than one big weekly report. Can add one or two months of recurring revenue.
7. **Unlockables / milestones** — bonuses unlocked by doing the things you want customers
   to do: activate, leave a testimonial, send a referral, ascend/buy more. Define your
   **activation** point (what long-stayers did early — e.g. Gym Launch: a $2,000 sale in
   the first 7 days; weight-loss: lose 5 lbs week one; "first five clients"). Unlockables
   can be activity-based or tenure-based (month 3, 8, 15), placed just past the current
   cancel point, with rewards getting bigger over time. Selling a customer something else
   (Ascension) is cited as the single greatest retention driver.

Supporting economics:

- Growth = double customers OR double what customers are worth; he sold Gym Launch for
  $46.2 million.
- Example: $100/mo, 10% monthly churn → 10-month average → $1,000 LTV. Cutting churn to
  5% → $2,000 LTV. Doubling LTV more than doubles profit (no re-acquisition cost) and
  lets you outspend competitors on CAC (e.g. $600 vs $300 at 3:1), unlocking far higher
  ad-spend caps.
- One portfolio company: 1.9x'd LTV ($2,000 → $3,800), moving it from $2M/month to
  $2M/week.
- Implementation order by bang-for-buck: (1) onboarding, (2) unlockables, (3) exit
  interviews, (4) attendance tracking, (5) reach outs, (6 & 7) events + handwritten
  cards together.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "There's two ways that you can double a business: one is you double the amount of
  customers that you sell every month, the other is you double how much they're worth."
- "It's a lot easier to quit a membership than it is to quit a relationship."
- "The easiest correlation with cancellation is lack of consumption. So if you want
  people to stay and pay, you got to get them to use it."
- "No matter how cheap a gym membership is, a gym membership you never use is never worth
  it."
- "Churn — people canceling — is a lagging indicator. It means it happens after the bad
  thing happened. We want to find out the moment the bad thing happens so we can turn
  something from tragic to magic."
- "The only way that you can actually solve the problem is get more angry than them — but
  you're getting angry not at them but at the problem."
- "The best way to get people to think that you care about them — this is a really key
  trick — is to actually care about them. That's it."
- "It has to be systematized, not automated. If it's automated, like how many times do
  you respond to the green text? ... Never."
- "Sometimes the best way to retain someone is to sell someone again and sell them
  something else or bigger."
- "With retention, it's not the bullet, it's all of these things put together — and when
  we did this it took our churn from 11% to 6% monthly."
