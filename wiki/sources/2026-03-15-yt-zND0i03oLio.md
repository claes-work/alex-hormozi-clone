# Helping a Holistic Health Practice Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The owner runs a functional-medicine (root-cause) telehealth practice out of Texas doing ~$1.6M/year at 70% margins, almost entirely word-of-mouth with no reliable customer-acquisition system. She is both the practitioner selling and fulfilling, and wants to 10x to $10M. She has hired a second practitioner and is trying to move existing clients over — but clients resist leaving her — while also narrowing her ideal client (a "menopausal, highly successful woman who is under-served") and just starting Google ads plus building a webinar. Alex reframes the client-attachment problem as a **feature, not a bug**: people love her *because* she's good, so of course they're upset they can't get her; the choice is between a painful short-term revenue dip and staying "stuck doing this forever." The real question that "ladders up" is whether she is confident she can acquire customers without it being her — and her past growth was all *without* ad spend, so that's unproven. On acquisition, Alex's core teaching: the biggest mistake is **running a warm process on cold leads** — "the reverse always works and the inverse does not." A warm process only closes the easy warm leads; a cold-built sales process, applied to *every* lead, converts warm leads at higher rates too. This usually fails from a lack of skill or a lack of energy to put every lead through the same cold process. He cites his own book launch (~8 weeks prior) as the example: the webinar was built for people who didn't know him, and doing the full sell — rather than just emailing the offer — produced roughly 3x the sales (two-thirds came from "actually selling like we meant it"). Her funnel is "missing friction": click-to-close is currently just an intro consult with no steps in between, so he'd add a VSL/selling layer, especially since local Google-ads traffic carries some trust but national/webinar (meta ads) traffic is "a different game" that needs a real pitch. On the transition risk, he'd **not hand off 100%** — instead change the service-delivery ratio to get ~10x leverage on her time (she moves "up," the new practitioner handles components, cadence drops to ~5x less frequent, shared internal notes, soft-touch messaging, no more calls) so clients still feel proximity to her. The nightmare he wants to avoid: churn from the handoff compresses top-line while marketing costs rise before she's good at marketing, margins collapse "from 70 to zero," she panics and reverts — or the new practitioner leaves and takes the whole business. Ends with the roadmap promo (ignored for claims).
