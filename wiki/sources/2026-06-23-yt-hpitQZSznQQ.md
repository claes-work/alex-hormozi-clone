# The $10M Trucking Owner Who Wants to Sell the Product Too

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a trucking owner delivering chemicals to fracking companies who wants to also sell the chemicals to end users. Alex warns that the product-selling opportunity adds complexity and a second cash-flow constraint to an already growing transportation business. He uses his Gym Launch and Prestige Labs experience as a cautionary parallel: selling another product to an existing distribution base can look obvious but slow the main business.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - trucking/last-mile chemicals transportation owner. His revenue, receivables, customer history, possible chemical sales, and exit concerns are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Selling the product in addition to transporting it would add another cash-flow constraint to the business.
- If the existing trucking model is already growing from $5M to $7M to $10M, Alex asks why not simply double the existing business.
- Side opportunities can prevent a company from becoming great at the main opportunity.
- Alex says Prestige Labs slowed Gym Launch because selling supplements to the existing gym distribution base looked smart but consumed attention and operational energy.
- The larger the distribution base becomes, the more tempting unrelated or adjacent opportunities become.
- If the natural extreme of the current business could be a $100M trucking business, then everything that is not trucking risks that outcome.
- Selling at $15M-$20M is not mandatory; founders can keep owning if they do not want the exit.
- Alex speculates that many owners sell around $5M EBITDA because the proceeds are enough to be done, not because the business cannot grow further.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're going to add another cash flow constraint to the business."
