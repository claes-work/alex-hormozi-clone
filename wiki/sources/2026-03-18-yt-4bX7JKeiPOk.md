# Helping a Marketing Agency Get to $20M/Year

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client runs a **video-testimonial agency** selling to mid-market and enterprise B2B software companies (sales-enablement / social-proof use case; buyer is usually a director of marketing; ~$15k average order value; some contracts up to $100k). They are at **$6M in revenue** and want to reach **$20M**. Growth so far has come from **inbound word-of-mouth** — warm leads already in the buying cycle that they close well — but they've recently gotten **outbound and LinkedIn ads** working (booking ~100 sales calls/month, ~6% close on LinkedIn-ad leads, roughly half their warm close rate). Alex's diagnosis is immediate: **"You're using a warm process on a cold lead."** His rule of thumb — **build for cold and it works everywhere; build for warm and it only works on warm.** He illustrates with his own recent book launch: he could have blasted an email offering bonus books + AI tools and captured maybe a third of the sales, but treating the *entire* audience that way would have under-converted the warm buyers by ~two-thirds — different processes for different temperatures. His prescription for the cold motion: **put a VSL in place, a set qualification step, and a pre-frame for the close call, plus a training cadence** so the reps actually follow the process; then test/iterate the VSL portions to shorten sales cycles and raise close rates. The payoff loop: fewer calls on the back end → higher sales-team utilization → fewer reps closing more deals → ability to attract better reps ("a virtuous loop rather than a vicious one").

The client raises a second problem: the business is **project-based, not recurring** — clients buy a $15k package and re-buy sporadically ("~$9k per one testimonial"; option tiers $4k–$9k). Alex (who "assumed this was continuity") reframes it: testimonial collection should be **ongoing** — you want fresh reviews every month, not a one-time blast that dies. His fix is to **bifurcate the offer into a quality and a quantity offer** ("big head, long tail"): (1) the **quality / emotional** offer is the upfront project — capture a representative group of avatars / all the ICPs in their own language ($15k–$25k), the front-end big-head purchase; (2) the **quantity / logical** offer is ongoing — since reviews go stale after ~18 months and companies must show they're still current, run a recurring package that collects **lots of five-stars, snippets and short UGC-style clips** on a regular basis. That recurring long-tail package becomes the continuity the business is missing (the client had struggled to get continuity because at the premium per-testimonial price customers only buy three or four at a time). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (excluded from claims).
