# The 6 Stages of Scaling | Dave Ramsey

## Summary
Interview clip in which **Alex Hormozi plays interviewer** and **Dave Ramsey is the guest**. Alex asks Dave to walk through the stages-of-scaling framework from Dave's book *Building a Business You Love*. The framework is entirely **Dave Ramsey's** (CONTEXT — it does not train the Alex persona). Dave explains that after coaching ~10,000 small businesses over 30+ years, he and his team observed that businesses move through **five stages** of growth (the on-screen title says "6 stages," but Dave describes five stages plus a separate set of **six drivers**). The five stages: **Treadmill** (solopreneur; you own a job, not a business — "if it's to be, it's up to me"), **Trailblazer** (10–15 people, chaotic, "herding cats"), **Pathfinder** (strategic thought, first leadership layer, systems and planning in place — "you're baling money now"), and finally the **Legacy** stage (succession, replacing yourself, family-office mentality — where Ramsey says his company is today). Dave notes revenue levels don't map cleanly to stages because it varies by industry. Underneath the stages run **six drivers** (personal growth — "I am the problem and I'm the solution"; purpose; people/culture/core values; profit; planning; then cycle back), which a company revisits repeatedly (Ramsey estimates 10–15 times) as it grows. Dave frames the value as giving owners a "clear path," reducing paralysis-of-analysis (his Total Money Makeover "baby steps" analogy — 12M copies). Alex closes by thanking Dave, noting Dave "owns a $600 million campus in cash," then delivers his own promo for the **acquisition.com/roadmap** ($100M scaling roadmap — a quiz mapping a business to **10 stages** across product, marketing, sales, customer success, recruiting, IT, HR, and finance).

## Key claims
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, businesses move through **five stages** of scaling — Treadmill, Trailblazer, Pathfinder, (an intermediate stage he references loosely), and Legacy — an observation drawn from coaching ~10,000 small businesses over 30+ years.
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, in the **Treadmill** stage you're a solopreneur who owns a job, not a business ("if you don't work, nothing happens"); it's fun and invigorating but unsustainable — "you don't meet 10-year treadmill operators." Leveling up requires time management (work *on* the business, not just *in* it — crediting Michael Gerber / *E-Myth*) and making the first hires so production and revenue don't depend solely on the owner.
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, the **Trailblazer** stage (the middle stage, ~10–15 people) is chaotic — "herding cats" — with high passion but eventually too frenetic; leveling up requires actual planning, less tactical reflex, and developing the first layer of leadership.
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, strategic thought (a "30,000-ft view") is what lets an owner "go around the wall" instead of running into it; Dave says his MBA hires taught him strategic thought while he "taught them how to work."
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, the **Pathfinder** stage is where systems, processes, and planning are in place and the business is highly profitable.
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, revenue levels do **not** map cleanly onto the stages — it changes industry to industry (an HVAC operator with 20 trucks and 70 employees isn't necessarily Pathfinder vs. Trailblazer).
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, the **Legacy** stage — where Ramsey is today — centers on succession, end-game, handoff, bench depth (ultimately replacing yourself), and family-office mentality.
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, **six drivers** move a company through the stages — personal growth ("I am the problem and I'm the solution… holding the business back at every stage"), purpose, people/culture/core values, profit, and planning — and a company cycles through them repeatedly (Ramsey estimates 10–15 times) as it grows and its owner changes.
- **[Dave Ramsey — CONTEXT]** As of March 2026, a clear path reduces anxiety and paralysis-of-analysis; this is the same insight behind the Ramsey "baby steps" (*Total Money Makeover*, ~12M copies), now applied to business in *Building a Business You Love*.
- **[Alex Hormozi]** As of March 2026, Alex offers a free **$100M scaling roadmap** (at acquisition.com/roadmap) — ~200 hours distilled from his portfolio companies' growth into **10 stages**, with a quiz that maps a business to its stage and prescribes next actions across **product, marketing, sales, customer success, recruiting, IT, human resources, and finance** — framed on the premise that "no matter what you're struggling with, someone else has already struggled with it and solved it."
