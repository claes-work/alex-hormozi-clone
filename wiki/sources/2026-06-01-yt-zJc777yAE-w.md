# Free Car Wash Made More Than Paid Ones

## Summary

Alex Hormozi reads from a "Lost Chapters" section about a COVID-era car wash that advertised a free wash, then let customers choose what to pay as a staff-support donation. He generalizes the tactic into a "pick your price" attraction offer: market the first thing as free, preframe that people can pay whatever they want, add bonus rungs for higher payment levels, and preserve goodwill while setting up future upsells.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A "pick your price" offer attracts with free but lets the buyer choose a payment level at checkout.
- The seller should preframe the payment choice early so the close does not feel awkward at the end.
- Bonus rungs should make higher voluntary payment levels feel valuable without forcing a purchase.
- The free version must have low operational cost so the business can honor it without burning out the team.
- In service businesses, the free/pick-your-price frame can also test reciprocity and commitment before accepting a long-term client.
- The close should still assume payment method after explaining the options.
- The model works especially well in low-trust environments because it loads the offer with goodwill and choice.
- A money model is a sequence of offers, not one isolated offer.
