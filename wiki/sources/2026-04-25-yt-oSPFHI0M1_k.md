# Why Your Ads Stop Scaling

## Summary

Alex coaches a 21-year-old founder running a relationship-niche VSL funnel at about
$600K/month with a 3:1 ratio, wanting to reach $1M/month. Alex says scaling requires
better and more creative across awareness levels, conversion-rate optimization on the VSL,
and more back-end LTV. He recommends testing the first five minutes of the VSL and adding a
high-ticket phone upsell rather than only low-ticket upsells.

## Key claims

- Alex says most advertisers wildly underestimate how much better creative must become as
  spend scales.
- He uses Eugene Schwartz's awareness ladder: unaware, problem aware, solution aware,
  product aware, and most aware.
- Hooks should match the audience's awareness level; most-aware buyers can receive a
  direct offer, while unaware audiences need curiosity or entertainment.
- He says many ad accounts hit an artificial limit where the difference between current
  spend and the next level is the quality of creative.
- For a VSL funnel, he recommends testing the first five minutes: tighter intro, more proof
  up front, and clearer "you are in the right place" framing.
- He says front-end VSL tests can produce 20-30% CAC improvements.
- He separates lowering CAC (creative and CRO) from raising allowable CAC through a better
  back end.
- He says low-ticket upsells are insufficient in this case and recommends a $5K-$10K
  high-ticket phone upsell.
- He says adding phone sales often doubles or triples LTV.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Most people underestimate wildly... how much better the creative needs to be to continue to scale."

> "The hooks are based on the audience that you're going after and the quality of the creative itself."

> "The difference between here and here is just the quality of creative."

> "You need a high-ticket upsell."
