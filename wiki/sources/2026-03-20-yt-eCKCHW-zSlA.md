# How Do Ads Work?

## Summary
Alex explains the mechanics of paid ads as a way to reach beyond the tiny fraction of an audience that organic content touches. He argues that running ads against your existing best content plus a handful of direct-to-camera offers can multiply revenue, and details a paid-plus-organic content volume mix. He closes by explaining how ManyChat DM automations dramatically outperform "link in bio" calls to action.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex claims only about 1-2% of an audience sees a given piece of organic content (subscribers/followers), while a much larger pool knows about you but hasn't followed — organic posting only capitalizes on that smaller slice, whereas ads reach all of it.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims creators earning "two, three, five" units of revenue from organic can typically reach "25 or more" by paying to play with ads.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends an ad strategy that reuses existing media: run your best organic short content as ads, paired with additional direct-to-camera offer recordings, since Meta's system already knows who your audience is.
- As of March 2026, Alex describes a content volume mix of roughly 35 short pieces/week as ads plus the organic output, adding about 5-10 direct-to-camera straight offers.
- As of March 2026, Alex identifies ManyChat's three most common automations: new-follower DM, story CTA (keyword reply), and post-comment keyword triggers — each kicking off a DM that can branch into logic trees for lead-gen and info collection.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims ManyChat DM automation works orders of magnitude better than "click the link in my bio," citing that a normal video gets 1,000-2,000 comments while a "link in bio" ask yields roughly 50 clicks.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "A very small percentage of your audience is going to see that content, maybe 1 or 2%."

> "The magic is like anybody who I see who's doing, you know, two, three, five, whatever money from organic, they usually can get to 25 or more just by basically paying to play with ads."

> "The meta world, they already know who your audience is. The ads is going to be basically all of this short content. So fundamentally, you're making 35 pieces of content a week that are going to be ads."

> "It works significant like orders of magnitude better than saying click the link in my bio. It's like not even close."

> "If I make a video that's just like a normal video, I'll get like 1,000 or 2,000 comments. If I were to make a video and say, 'Hey, click the link in my bio,' I'd probably get like 50 clicks."
