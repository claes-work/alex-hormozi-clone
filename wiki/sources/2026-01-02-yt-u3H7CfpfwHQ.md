# We Broke The Guinness World Record In 24 Hours

## Summary

A behind-the-scenes documentary of Alex Hormozi's `$100M Money Models` book-launch
event, in which he set a Guinness World Record for the fastest-selling non-fiction book
in 24 hours, surpassing the prior holder (Prince Harry, ~1.43M copies). The video
intercuts Hormozi's own reflections on strategy, preparation, and the "proof above
everything" thesis with team members narrating the operational build-out (set design,
sales-floor staffing, launch-day choke points). Much of the footage is other speakers;
only Hormozi-attributed lines are marked as persona data below.

## Key claims

- The `$100M` series was originally written as a single book containing offers, leads,
  and money models, then split into three stacked books (offer → advertise → monetize).
  (Hormozi)
- The launch itself was designed as living *proof*: "the best way to get someone to
  believe is to have already done it." (Hormozi)
- Hormozi frames the whole enterprise as a demonstration that skills alone — not brand —
  produce results, pointing back to his Gym Launch era. (Hormozi)
- `$31 million` of consulting work reportedly went into the Money Models book; offered
  "for free" for a `$200` donation, or a bundle for `$5,998`. (Hormozi, stage)
- 300+ phone reps were hired and trained to help buyers check out. (Hormozi, stage)
- Prior record framing: Prince Harry ~1.4M/day (later cited on-stage as 1.43M),
  Michelle Obama ~750,000; the goal was to beat all of them live. (Hormozi)
- The record was confirmed broken early inside the 24-hour window; the record continues
  measuring for the full 24 hours. (Guinness judge — non-Alex)
- Stated stretch goal beyond the record: `$100M` in sales over the launch weekend
  ("$100M money model — you have to make 100 million"). (team + Hormozi)
- Peak sales velocity cited on-camera as ~1,000 books per second. (unattributed team)
