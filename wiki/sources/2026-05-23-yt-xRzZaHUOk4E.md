# I Make YouTube Videos - How Do I Get More Sponsors

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a profitable travel-food YouTuber whose constraints are key-person
dependence and under-sold sponsorship inventory. Alex says the sponsorship upside is a
modest lift, while the real constraint is manpower and quality leverage: hire a high-level
YouTube strategist or media operator who is intellectually equal or better in the domain,
and give the existing COO solution time to work.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex first reframes the business as two levers: make more videos or make more per
  video.
- If maxed sponsorships add about $800,000 on a $3 million profit base, that is only
  about a 20% lift and not the primary constraint.
- A high-level YouTube strategist from a larger or similar channel could remove a large
  share of the founder's workload.
- Paying one expensive person can be worthwhile if it buys mental bandwidth and operating
  leverage.
- Talent-led YouTube businesses get large leverage from the founder being exceptional on
  camera.
- Alex is almost not involved in his own pre-production and post-production; he mainly
  shows up for the content.
- The founder's current COO/media-ops hire may already be the right solution, so the
  founder should not solve the same problem twice before the first solution has time.
- High-level hires should begin thinking and dialoguing before their official start date.
- The higher the role, the more surface area the founder should get with the person across
  different conditions before trusting the hire.
- For this business, the output that matters most is quality, and quality should be
  reverse-engineered through talent.
