# How to Train Your Sales Team to Close More Deals

## Summary

In this coaching answer, Alex Hormozi gives a gym owner a tactical supplement-sales playbook. He says trainers may not sell supplements because they lack confidence and personal experience, so the owner should have them complete a 30-day challenge using the products and posting through the process. He then outlines the menu-upsell script from *$100M Money Models*: seed the expectation that supplements will come up, ethically unsell what the person does not need, prescribe exactly how to use what remains, and close through simple choices and default payment.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - gives the supplement-sales playbook. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Questioner / gym owner** - UK-based gym owner with a supplement-sales problem. His business context is minimal and context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Salespeople may fail to sell supplements because they lack confidence in the products and general supplement knowledge.
- The team should experience the product through a 30-day challenge with meal plan, supplements, before/after photos, and social posting.
- A free 28-day transformation can create the context for a nutrition orientation where the supplement package is sold.
- Trainer commissions align incentives when supplements are the only thing being sold in the free transformation.
- Menu upsells use four steps, starting with unselling what the customer does not need.
- The script should seed that supplements will be discussed while truthfully emphasizing food first.
- Low-margin or inappropriate products can be crossed off or referred elsewhere to build trust.
- Prescription means telling the customer exactly when, where, and how to use each product, integrating it into existing routines.
- The close uses simple flavor choices and asks whether to use the card on file.
- Alex claims this process can close a very high percentage of supplement buyers.
