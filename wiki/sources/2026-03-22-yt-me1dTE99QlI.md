# I Have a Large YouTube Channel, How Do I Make Money?

## Summary
A caller who built a 1.5M-subscriber YouTube channel teaching DIY epoxy countertop remodeling (and exited an earlier company for over $20M) asks Alex how to monetize his audience of viewers who want the work done for them rather than doing it themselves. His plan is a trade school that trains people to become contractors, with the caller supplying leads; he is unsure whether to charge tuition up front or train for free and take ownership/a cut of the resulting businesses. Alex advises charging for the school while playing with the price and financing terms, keeping tuition so students have skin in the game, and controlling the sales/lead distribution centrally rather than relying on trainees to pay for leads afterward.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex advises the caller to charge for the trade school rather than train for free, because free delivery to people expected to perform typically produces "freebie seekers" who fail to follow through; students need skin in the game.
- As of March 2026, Alex reframes "or" into "and": the caller could charge tuition (e.g. lower it from $20K to $5K or $10K with financing) AND take a percentage of the business he sends trainees.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the free content the caller already publishes is itself the help for "the kid who has nothing"; paid help is for those willing to save up and invest.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends the caller control the sales function centrally — build a phone team that closes deals, collects payment, then distributes to trainees — instead of expecting trainees to pay him for leads (enforceability of a back-end "you pay me" contract is a "nightmare").
- As of March 2026, Alex uses a Harvard/school analogy: students pay to attend but are not guaranteed graduation, and the school should retain standards to expel or fail underperformers.
- Caller claims (unverified): built over 1.5M YouTube subscribers with epoxy-countertop DIY content; previously exited a company for over $20M using Alex's content/books; estimates ~$20K cost to train one contractor; says he has already proven the lead-gen side.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Instead of or. Like maybe I want them to pay 20,000 dollars to make sure that they're legit. And I'm going to get a percentage of all the business that I send them."

> "To help the kid who has nothing you can just make all the content free, right, which you do now. So that kid already has been helped from you. If he wants more help from you he can pay and he can save up his shackles."

> "It's very tough to go free and deliver a lot to people expecting that they're going to do something. They typically will need to have some skin in the game. If you're going to put skin in the game you're going to want them to put skin in the game."

> "Otherwise you're just going to have a bunch of freebie seekers who are going to come, and then also the enforceability of the back-end contract and then we're paying you is probably going to be a little bit of a nightmare."

> "If it were me I would control the sales and then hand them the deals so that I could collect the payment and then distribute payment to them rather than expecting them to pay me."

> "Think of it like Harvard. You pay to go, you don't get guaranteed a graduation. So I would just let them know it's like we will not graduate you if you're not good. Or we kick you out just like a school. Real schools have standards and you pay and you might not graduate."
