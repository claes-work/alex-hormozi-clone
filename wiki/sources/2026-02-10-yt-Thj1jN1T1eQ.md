# The Simplest Way to Advertise Your Business

## Summary
In a live Q&A on @MoreMozi, a life coach (Tracy) building a free community around goals and life transitions asks how to advertise and grow a paid online community when starting from a low social-media following. Alex frames it as a simple advertising question about traffic — the first of "the four levers" (getting more eyeballs). He says the operator just needs to pick a channel: post on social media, post in targeted subreddits and niche forums (noting Reddit has a community for virtually any interest), or engage on Twitter by commenting back and forth on niche-focused feeds and DMing people already engaged. As long as the operator adds value to the community, targeted people will be interested. He reduces the whole set of options to three fundamental moves — reach out, make content, or run ads — and gives a resource-based rule for choosing between them: reach out if you're poor, make content if you're less poor, run ads if you're the least poor.
