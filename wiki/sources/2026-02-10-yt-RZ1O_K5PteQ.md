# Helping an Agency Owner Scale

## Summary
In a live Q&A on @MoreMozi, an owner of a "SaaS-agency hybrid" (~$150K in its first year, with three service tiers spanning DIY, done-with-you, and done-for-you plus a community) asks Alex whether to include an inspirational personal story (a criminal record, since expunged, where the homeowner became a client) in his marketing and how to grow now that a non-compete has lifted. Alex first separates the two issues — the personal story is a human/branding matter, distinct from the business-growth question — and pushes the owner to massively simplify. He forces a single positioning: the business is a normal SMB digital agency that white-labels GoHighLevel and runs Facebook ads and SEO for local business owners. Alex tells him to pick one model (SaaS or agency, not both) and to lead with a crisp one-line pitch stating what he does, who for, and his true constraint (more demand). His core scaling recommendation is to make the low-churn, high-gross-margin, high-revenue-retention piece (white-label software + SEO at ~$300–$500/month) the primary product and drop the higher-ticket ad-management services, because volatile SMB clients churn out of the expensive services and become a pain — the durable play is heavy front-end acquisition (ads + outreach) feeding a scalable, low-churn back end.

- (2026-02-10) The tools are provided for ~$100/month; some clients spend up to ~$5,000/month, with the pricier line item (~$1,500) being the owner running their ads. [guest-attributed] [self-reported figures]
- (2026-02-10) The business currently has roughly 10–15 clients in services. [guest-attributed] [self-reported figures]
- (2026-02-10) The personal story and the business-growth question are two separate things: the story is a human/branding matter, while the immediate priority is the business itself, which is "a very normal business."
- (2026-02-10) The owner should massively simplify and set the inspirational story aside to focus on the business first.
- (2026-02-10) You should pick one model — SaaS or agency — right off the bat rather than running both; if the "SaaS" is just white-labeled software, then the real business is an agency.
- (2026-02-10) In the future, lead with a crisp, honest one-line pitch: "I white-label GoHighLevel, I run ads for small business owners, I can't grow as much as I'd like, and the constraint I'm dealing with is that I need more demand."

- (2026-02-10) The white-label software plus SEO is the lowest amount of work in terms of gross profit; it can probably be sold at $300–$500/month with relatively low churn given the SMBs being brought in.

- (2026-02-10) Growth here is a demand problem: to scale, the owner needs to do more of whatever ads and outreach he is currently doing to get more of those low-churn customers.
- (2026-02-10) The owner should pick either ads or outreach as the acquisition channel rather than trying to do everything.
- (2026-02-10) The owner believes he could roughly double his current customer load as long as he focuses on systems and processes along the way. [guest-attributed]
