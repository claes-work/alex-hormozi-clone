# Why You Keep Repeating the Same Bad Habits

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains repeated "bad habits" through reinforcement history. He says people
repeat behaviors that have been rewarded, unpunished, or less costly in the past. The
trigger is not the root cause; it is the recurring circumstance in which the previously
rewarded behavior appears again. He uses relationship conflict and child-tantrum examples
to explain extinction bursts and delayed rewards.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- People do what they have been rewarded for doing in the past.
- A behavior someone labels "bad" was likely rewarded, unpunished, or less punished than
  alternatives at some earlier point.
- Triggers are circumstances that precede behavior, not necessarily the true cause.
- Repeated anger or crying in conflict may persist if it previously produced relief,
  attention, concession, or avoidance of a harder conversation.
- When a reinforced behavior stops working, it may intensify before it disappears.
- A child's tantrum can worsen when a parent first stops rewarding it, because the child
  escalates a previously effective behavior.
- People can become skilled at avoidance, delay, or distraction if those behaviors are
  rewarded.
- Delayed reward makes causality harder to learn because the payoff arrives much later
  than the behavior.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The trigger is not the cause."
