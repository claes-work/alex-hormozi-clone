# Deleting Work Life Balance for a Season

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says work-life balance should be judged over a chosen timeline, not only a
day or week. He frames life as seasons: compressing work and pain into a few intense years
can create more freedom across a lifetime, while copying a billionaire's current calendar
ignores the season that built the optionality.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Work-life balance is incomplete unless the timeline is specified.
- Alex thinks in seasons rather than daily or weekly microcosms.
- Sacrifice today can be appropriate depending on the season, goal, and risk/reward.
- Questions such as how much to consume versus invest have no universal answer.
- The right answer depends on the person's current season and goals.
- The frame can give people permission to work weekends or use the hours before and after
  a normal job for a season.
- Alex says he has not seen hyper-successful people who avoided a significantly
  unbalanced season.
- People should not copy what billionaires do now without understanding the season that
  made them billionaires.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Everyone talks about work life balance but no one talks about it over what timeline."

> "I think about life in seasons."

> "I am going to be unbalanced and that's okay."
