# I Built a $1.5M Business I Secretly Hate

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a cloud-computing consultant who builds custom cloud solutions and then sells managed services on top. The founder wants to skip the long custom-build work and sell managed services on environments he did not build. Alex frames the model as a front-end/back-end or big-head/long-tail business and suggests a hybrid: sell a smaller paid assessment or improvement patch that creates value upfront, lets the company understand the customer's system, and naturally leads into managed services.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - cloud-computing/managed-services founder. His revenue, current service model, technical constraints, and personal brand are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The current model looks like a front-end/back-end business: build the customized thing, then sell the natural managed-service tail.
- Managed services likely have strong gross margins because they attach to systems the customer already depends on.
- The main question is why the initial build is so constrained: whether nobody can do it or whether the founder has not attracted the right people.
- A hybrid offer may solve the issue better than jumping straight to unmanaged third-party systems.
- Alex suggests inspecting an existing cloud solution and offering a roughly 20% improvement or high-impact patch.
- The upfront patch can function like a paid assessment the company would need to do anyway before managing the system.
- This approach may capture most of the benefit of a shorter front end while preserving stickiness for the managed-service back end.
