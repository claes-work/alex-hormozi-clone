# How I Scale Companies I Invest In

## Summary
Interview clip (an off-camera host asks; Alex answers). Alex lays out the Acquisition.com / ACQ Ventures investing lens as **three legs of the stool** he can contribute to a company: **money**, **time/work**, and **brand/reputation/distribution**. He prefers to lean on the first two because they are more scalable — brand, by contrast, is "limited in how many," not in power: a brand is built on **associations and pairings**, so pairing yourself with too many disparate things dilutes its potency (many associations within *similar* buckets, however, could give "more shots on goal"). On the operational side, he describes the holdco team he built: he **marries old-school and new-school** by hiring bright, hard-working ex-McKinsey/Bain consultants (5–10 years in) who hate being "a cog in a machine," many of whom came from his own content world, and pairs **big-business strategy with small-business tactics**. He argues big-consulting work (e.g. supply-chain analysis for Walmart LatAm) is heady and not useful to a small business, but the **problem-solving mind** transfers — "consultants are a lot like engineers" — which feeds the advisory practice. The back half addresses **investing in content creators**: he wouldn't fund a de-novo idea (creators are big-idea people; their constraint is **execution and business acumen**, and he'd lack majority control → high risk); he'd prefer a structure where the creator owns ~20% and ACQ owns ~80% and fronts the capital and builds everything, but even then it must clear his own **deal-flow supply/demand** and prove more **alpha** than existing deals. For a later-stage creator (a "Logan Paul / Jake Paul" type), investing "isn't really investing — it's a joint venture," i.e. starting another business; he'd only do it if ACQ already owned a huge share of the required **core competencies (knowledge *and* manpower)**. Net: with less-savvy operating businesses he brings distribution or operational expertise; with creators who already have distribution but no operations, he'd have to **build the company** — so the honest translation ("would you build a company for a creator?") is "probably not." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap CTA.
