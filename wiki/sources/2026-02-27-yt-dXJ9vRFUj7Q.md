# Risk Rejection Now or Guarantee Rejection Later

## Summary
Short solo clip on the psychology of rejection and regret. Alex frames a universal choice: **risk rejection now or guarantee regret later** — "losers fear rejection, winners fear regret." He argues failure is a prerequisite for success ("greatness rejects all first-time applicants") and mocks the person who claims they "can't get a job" after applying to exactly one place. He reframes job-seeking as a rejection-tolerance exercise directly analogous to building a business: the volume of outreach, interviews, resumes, and job ads a person did to land a job produced what he'd call "one sale" — one person saying yes and paying for their work. Business is the same mechanism; anyone who could handle that level of rejection to get hired can handle it in business, so the real question is whether they can handle the *regret* years later of not trying. He closes with an observation on champions: everyone else gets excited by the idea of winning, but consistent champions are *relieved* when they win because they "hate losing more than they love winning" and expected to win from superior preparation. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of February 2026, everyone eventually must choose whether to "risk rejection now or guarantee regret later"; "losers fear rejection, winners fear regret."
- As of February 2026, failure is a prerequisite for success — most attempts at anything fail — captured in his line "greatness rejects all first-time applicants."
- As of February 2026, the effort of landing a job (outreach, interviews, resumes, job ads) is functionally the same as making "one sale" — getting one person to say yes and pay you for your work.
- As of February 2026, business works the same way as job-hunting; anyone who could tolerate the rejection required to get hired can tolerate the rejection of building a business — "it's not actually that different."
- As of February 2026, the decisive variable is regret tolerance: whether you can handle the regret years later of not having done it.
- As of February 2026, the difference between champions and everyone else: others are *excited* by winning (and rarely win); consistent champions are *relieved* when they win because they hate losing more than they love winning, and they expected to win from superior preparation.
