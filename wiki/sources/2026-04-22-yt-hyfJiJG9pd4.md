# Helping a $1.8M Roofing Company Get to $70M

## Summary

Alex coaches a young roofing company considering franchising, multi-location ownership,
or other scaling paths. He says the owner has not yet nailed the model, so the right
next move is to learn the model before choosing a scale vehicle. He frames franchising
as an impatient and expensive form of capital that can create many low-profit locations.

## Key claims

- Alex says the roofing company should continue with the insurance-driven model if that
  is already 95% of the work.
- He says the owner should not decide between corporate expansion and franchising before
  the core model is nailed.
- He says once the model is nailed, the right path becomes clearer based on returns on
  capital, required oversight, and patience.
- He says he often sees franchising as impatience and as an expensive form of capital.
- He says operating 20 franchise locations can require similar administrative work as 20
  owned locations, while producing less money.
- He self-reports buying out 18 franchisees in a teeth-whitening chain so the company
  could own all 32 stores and simplify operations.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Once you nail the model, then the path will become really clear."

> "I kind of see franchises as like being impatient."

> "The most expensive form of capital."
