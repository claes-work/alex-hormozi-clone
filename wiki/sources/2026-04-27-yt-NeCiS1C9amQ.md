# Why Paying for a Course Is Almost Always Worth It

## Summary

Alex says free learning on YouTube is possible, but paid help buys speed,
troubleshooting, and avoided mistakes. He gives his own example of paying an ads expert
$750 per hour for about eight sessions because he wanted to understand how the expert
thought through his specific ad account, not just how to click buttons. He frames skill
acquisition as stacking: ads plus sales can multiply earning power.

## Key claims

- Alex says what people pay for in courses, workshops, or coaching is speed.
- Paid help can also provide troubleshooting and reduce the number of mistakes a learner
  makes.
- Alex paid roughly $5,600 for one-on-one Facebook ads help to learn how an expert
  thought through his own ads.
- He wanted personalized thinking, not generic button-click instruction.
- He says a single valuable skill can create large earning power and become more valuable
  when stacked with other skills like sales.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What you pay for is speed."

> "I wanted to learn how he would think through running my ads."

> "They just keep multiplying and multiplying and multiplying."
