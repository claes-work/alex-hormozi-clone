# The 6 Levels of Making Money

## Summary

Solo framework video. Alex Hormozi argues there are only four ways to get money
(steal, inherit, marry into it, trade for it), and that for most people trading
is the only realistic option. Within trading, he ranks six ways to structure the
trade in reverse order of "bestness," from ordinary W-2 employment up to two
"god-tier" setups. The organizing through-line: you are compensated in proportion
to the (perceived) risk you take on, and the biggest rewards come from taking
risks the market misprices as larger than they actually are.

## Key claims

The four ways to get money: **steal, inherit, marry into it, trade for it.** Most
people only realistically have the trade option. Within trading, there are six
structures (ranked worst-to-best):

- **Level 1 — "I work, then you pay."** Classic W-2 employment. Paid no matter what
  unless fired; you trade risk for reliability. Lowest risk, most reliable, least
  upside.
- **Level 2 — "You pay as we go."** Work and payment happen in parallel
  (some now, some during, some at end; half now/half later). Typical for
  independent contractors and vendors. Front-loads some money, but vendors get
  fired far faster than employees.
- **Level 3 — "You pay, then I work."** Paid in full up front, then delivery
  begins (surgeons, attorney retainers). Only possible when you own the business /
  have leverage. Includes his "business-owner layaway" tactic: let the customer set
  their own payment plan, and work only starts once they've paid up.
- **Level 4 — "When X happens, you pay me."** Compensation divorced from time and
  tied to outcomes: rev share, profit share, outcome-based bonuses, accelerators,
  equity, agency % of spend. Pays on ability to create an outcome (skill), not
  hours.
- **Level 5 — "Buy and sell risk itself."** Insurance and its cousins (warranties,
  guarantees, AppleCare). When nothing happens, you still get paid, and that becomes
  profit. No delivery beyond the agreement to carry the risk.
- **Level 6 — "No matter what, you pay me."** The god-tier / S-tier level:
  government taxation, backed by a monopoly on violence, gets paid regardless.
  Business analogues that control the money flow: franchisors who collect and remit,
  and payment processors who take their slice before remitting.

Supporting claims and evidence he cites:
- Almost half of business owners make no money at all; the median business owner
  earns about California minimum wage — so "employee is riskier than owner" is false.
- Employee average tenure 3.9 years (US Bureau of Labor Statistics) vs. 3–12 months
  for an independent contractor and 1–3 months for temp/gig — ~5x the turnover for
  vendors.
- The higher up the pyramid, the more leverage you have and the more you can command
  your own terms.
- Reward tracks **perceived** risk; pros make deals very risky for the other party
  and not risky for themselves — "mispriced bets" (cites Howard Marks / distressed
  debt).
- You can shift risk even as a W-2 employee (e.g. offer to work for $60k instead of
  $100k in exchange for $180k upside if targets hit).
- Move-up tactics for a business owner/solopreneur: **royalties/licensing** (paid off
  the top, revenue not profit — "top line's top line"); **selling guarantees/
  warranties** (sell insurance-like risk without being an insurer); and **controlling
  the money flow** (franchisor/payment-processor model).
- Prefer rev share over profit share, because profit can be manipulated by overspending;
  revenue cannot.
