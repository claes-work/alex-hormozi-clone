# The First Lesson to Get Out of Poverty

## Summary
Interview clip (with an unnamed host — Alex references "Chris," likely Chris Williamson). Alex argues that the **first lesson of poverty is two words: "my fault."** At 19 he was angry at his parents and blamed them for the person he had failed to become; he realized he had handed the people he hated power over his own success, which disgusted him enough to point the blame finger inward and take ownership. His core mechanic: **"power follows the blame finger"** — wherever you point blame is where the power goes (blame the government → the government has the power; blame your spouse for being out of shape → you've given them all the power). Even when something genuinely is *not* your fault, his response is "**And?**" — you can either never try, or "put the metal thing on" (prosthetic leg metaphor) and do it anyway. He concedes real disadvantages (racial/gender inequality, being born in poverty, abuse) are legitimately justifying, but argues you can instead choose to be a **local "point of proof"** for others who suffered the same and succeeded despite it. The clip's second half answers the host's question — how does the "aphorism-on-the-whiteboard" person move from *mental masturbation* to action? Two things: (1) know the **input-output equation** (define the most basic first action that produces the outcome you want — post the content, send the cold DM, run the ad, press go), and (2) know **what your fuel is going to be**. On fuel, his "first rule of entrepreneurship": **use what you have** — most people lack the big passion/vision/carrot but do have pain, anger, and shame, so use *that* as gas in the beginning until you escape the loop and find real passion. He recounts learning the word "wantrepreneur" from a self-help book, hating that he *was* one, and taking six months to quit his job. He ties leaving to killing his father's image of him ("sometimes your parents' dreams have to die in order for yours to live"), the Tony Robbins threshold ("when the pain of staying the same is greater than the pain of change"), and his preference for **negative visualization / future-casting** (imagine 10 more years of the current path to magnify present pain over the action threshold). The clip ends with the $100M scaling roadmap promo.
