# Alex Hormozi 10xed His Income

## Summary

Despite the clickbait title, this interview clip contains **no income figures or income-scaling method** — it is entirely about ego, humility, competitiveness, and legacy. Alex Hormozi describes ego as "the enemy" in business and life, a source of unhappiness he has been actively fighting since age 19, estimating he now has "40 percent less ego" than he used to. He recasts human legacy through the lens of shared mitochondrial ancestry (everyone as "distant cousins," his rationale for giving long, deliberate hugs) and links shedding ego to how far his message can spread. The host (Lewis Howes) contributes a parallel story about de-centering his own show — that material is context, not Alex.

## Key claims (the "income-10x method")

- **No income-10x method is present in this clip.** The title is not supported by the transcript; the content is a mindset/ego discussion. (Flagged so the ledger title is not taken as a factual claim.)
- Ego is "the enemy" in business because "it gives us too much confidence when we don't deserve it"; Alex says there is "just no benefit to Ego."
- He treats ego reduction as a lifelong, incremental project — "defusing a bomb" key by key — that "no one ever conquers," only chips away at.
- Reframing method for staying humble amid growing success: consciously **link** the good outcomes to the *decrease* in ego, so success reinforces humility instead of amplifying ego.
- His competitiveness came from being an athlete, where winning felt necessary to feel "worthy" or "good enough"; he carried that need-to-compete into business "for many years."
- He argues that if his ego were at its highest, his message would spread "much slower or not at all," because "the message wouldn't be about the message, it would be about me."
- Legacy reframe: mitochondrial DNA traces back to one common maternal ancestor, so all humans are effectively "distant cousins" — a reason to embrace people warmly and be present.
