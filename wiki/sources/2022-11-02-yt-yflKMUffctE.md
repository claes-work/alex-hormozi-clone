# How They Keep You POOR Forever

## Summary

Despite the clickbait title, this is a solo talk on singular focus and why entrepreneurs stay poor by chasing new opportunities ("the woman in the red dress") instead of doing the boring basics on one business for an uncommonly long time. Hormozi lays out his "better, more, new" priority order and argues execution — not strategy — is the real risk, illustrated by a "backyard football / two fat guys in the middle" story from a mercenary CEO.

## Key claims

- Distraction (new shiny opportunities) is "the woman in the red dress" from The Matrix; at every level of business she gets "hotter," so you must re-learn to say no to more and bigger opportunities repeatedly. (paraphrase)
- Priority order is "better, more, new": max out doing the current thing better (lowest risk), then more, and only then add new — because "more is riskier than better." Innovation already happened when you hit product-market fit. (paraphrase)
- Being advanced means you never stop doing the basics; big businesses do the same things small ones do, just better, and "fill the holes in the bucket." (paraphrase)
- Business is not complicated, it's hard, because people can't stay focused on one thing for a long period; splitting focus across five businesses to out-compete someone fully focused on one is arrogant. (paraphrase)
- He had nine businesses before Gym Launch took off; you can force any single business to succeed if you focus, rather than launching many and hoping the market picks the winner. (paraphrase, biography)
