# You Don't Have a Margin Problem, You Have a Brand Problem

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a DTC skincare/e-commerce founder doing about $10M revenue with roughly 15% margins and a goal of $100M. Alex says the margin problem is really a brand problem: the company is trapped in direct-response/media-arbitrage thinking, where scaling ad spend reaches colder audiences and compresses margin. He says the founder must learn brand as the core skill of physical-product e-commerce, shift a majority of advertising toward brand-building over time, and think like a capital allocator over a 6-12 month attribution horizon rather than a 30-60 day performance marketer.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Coachee** - DTC skincare founder. His revenue, margin, brand structure, rebrand, subscription idea, and exit goal are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Low margins in this DTC case come from not having a brand.
- Scaling spend in direct response increases revenue while compressing margin because the business reaches less-profitable audiences.
- A long-term e-commerce company may need most advertising to be brand-driven rather than direct-to-buy.
- Brand building includes founder story, narrative, differentiation, and associations the ideal avatar finds positive.
- A media-arbitrage white-label business is hard to sell.
- A physical-product e-commerce owner cannot fully outsource brand; brand is the core skill.
- Subscription, AOV, and CRO work can help, but they are not the same as solving the front-end brand problem.
- CRO is like a one-time steroid pop: useful, but not the long-term growth engine.
- Brand creates premium pricing, repeat buying, higher CTRs, and higher conversion rates.
- Brand requires a longer attribution window, often 6-12 months, and capital-allocation thinking.
