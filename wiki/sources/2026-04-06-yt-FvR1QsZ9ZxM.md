# Why Better Content Doesn't Get More Views

## Summary

Alex says the message accounts for only a small part of content consumption; the larger
factor is context around the speaker, especially identity, track record, and proof. He
does not assume his content is intrinsically better than lower-view alternatives. The
durable strategy is to earn evidence by doing the thing, then speak with certainty from
that proof.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex estimates message quality at 10% and speaker context at 90% of
  content consumption; treat the percentages as a rhetorical heuristic.
- He uses Elon Musk as an illustration that status and proof change how a trivial message
  is received.
- Alex says many people produce better content than he does but receive fewer views
  because they lack comparable context and evidence.
- Proof creates subject competence, audience belief, and resilience to criticism.
- He self-reports crossing $100 million net worth at age 32; this remains an unverified
  self-report rather than registry evidence.
- Content longevity comes from certainty grounded in completed work rather than confidence
  in generic prescriptions.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The message is 10% of how people consume it. 90% is the context around the message."

> "Solve for the proof and then the content will take care of itself."

> "Be certain based on what you've done."

## Notes

Reinforces context-carries-message, proof-first authority, and domain evidence.
