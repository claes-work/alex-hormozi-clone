# $2.9M CFO Advisory Stopped Taking Clients to Hit $20M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a CPA/CFO advisory founder doing about $2.9M who wants to reach $20M. The clip repeats the same case as nearby CFO advisory uploads: the founder wants to market books and courses, but Alex says the real issue is that the service business stopped taking new customers because fulfillment is hard. He recommends fixing supply and operating leverage before creating more demand.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - CPA/CFO advisory founder. Her revenue, books, courses, organic content, churn, and capacity issues are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A business that is taking zero new monthly clients will not grow from more marketing.
- Books and courses are not the current constraint; they can become marketing assets later.
- A sticky service business is more valuable than starting a course business just because fulfillment is hard.
- The business is supply constrained, not demand constrained.
- The founder should improve operating leverage with offshoring and service-delivery redesign before adding more lead generation.
- AI-first requires a data-first company with complete data architecture before the AI layer can be useful.
- The correct sequence is operating leverage, data layer, AI leverage, then more demand.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Well, that will not grow the business."

> "You're supply constrained."

> "How do I market more? I'm like, 'You can't even take people.'"

> "It just never stops being hard."

> "The first thing that you have to do in order to have an AI first company is you have to be a data first company."

> "You'll make more money if you sell people."
