# You're Not Working Hard, You're Just Tired

## Summary

Alex distinguishes feeling tired from actually exhausting one's work capacity. The
tactical marker is what happens after a hard block of work: drink water, look at the next
large task, and start again. He argues that proximity to exceptional people teaches these
otherwise hard-to-learn lessons faster than abstract content. He cites a new marketing
director's first week, when they shared a screen for eight hours and then immediately
moved to the next task, as a live perception shift.

## Key claims

- Most people can try much harder than they think, and many are not really trying.
- A practical test of work capacity is whether you can start the next large task after
  finishing a demanding one.
- Working side by side with exceptional people changes one's felt standard for effort.
- People under 30 should move, live cheaply, work for free if needed, and get proximity
  to exceptional performers because lived exposure teaches faster than content.
- He credits Angela Duckworth with the idea that the most consistent way to become a
  champion is to join a team of champions.

## Notable verbatim quotes
