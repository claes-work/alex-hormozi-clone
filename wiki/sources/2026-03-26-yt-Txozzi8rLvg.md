# Helping a Real Estate Developer Doing $46M/Year Hire Better Talent

## Summary
In a live Q&A, a real-estate developer who sells micro-development ("infill") housing to investors describes doing $46M/year in revenue with an ambition to reach $500M, bottlenecked by capital scaling and a team structure that relies heavily on him and his business partner. He asks how to attract high-net-worth (accredited) investors without leaning on his personal network. Alex maps out the finite set of customer-acquisition paths and recommends that, in the caller's shoes, he would prioritize conferences and in-person events for the highest leverage and fastest speed-to-trust.

## Key claims
- (Caller, unverified) The caller's company sells micro-development / infill housing to investors, does $46M/year in revenue, wants to reach $500M, and is bottlenecked by scaling capital and a team structure dependent on the two founders.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames raising capital from high-net-worth investors as functionally identical to an enterprise sale — it "feels different" or "quacks different" but follows the same process.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that there are only a finite number of ways to get people to find out about your offer: (1) go to someone who already has the audience — the affiliate model (which he groups trade shows and paying for audience access under), (2) run paid ads targeting accredited investors, (3) make content regularly, or (4) do outbound.
- As of March 2026, if Alex had no money and had to start in the caller's position, he would prioritize conference and event activity, because being face-to-face with investors makes speed-to-trust much faster and is super high leverage.
- As of March 2026, Alex notes an exception: he would lean into paid media instead only if the person already had a background in media buying — which this caller did not.
- Alex mentions his team has a detailed "playbook" for this high-end acquisition process but declines to share it fully on-air due to time.
