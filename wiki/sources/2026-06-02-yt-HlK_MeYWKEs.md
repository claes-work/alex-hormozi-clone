# He Sold $125M in Real Estate After I Fired Him

## Summary

Alex Hormozi revisits a real-estate operator he previously advised to stop running a lead-generation agency and get good at real estate. The operator later sold roughly $125M in real estate and built tax-focused acquisition, setup, and management services. Alex's current advice is to lop off the low-leverage Airbnb-management burden, focus on the core economic arbitrage of helping high-income clients buy tax-advantaged real estate, and expand the CPA/strategic-partner channel from a few partners to many.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A founder should distinguish the core thing buyers value from the add-on services that merely make the offer easier to sell.
- Closing 95% is a sign that prices are likely too low or the offer is too strong relative to the ask.
- If backend management creates low-dollar work and client escalation, it may be better to refer it out while preserving leverage through relationship control and expectation setting.
- The front-end promise should focus on the core economic outcome, such as buying the right property to create the desired tax treatment.
- If most new business comes from a few strategic partners, the main growth lever is increasing the number of those partners rather than adding unrelated channels.
- More strategic partners reduce dependence, let the founder turn down bad-fit business, and free time to sell the high-margin core service.
