# Helping a Home Services Business Scale

## Summary
In a live Q&A, a business owner who runs a dog-waste-removal ("poop scooping") company doing roughly $2M/year asks Alex Hormozi how to acquire competitors and integrate them without wasting money while maximizing enterprise value for investors. Hormozi advises structuring deals with big post-integration incentives for operators, breaking each acquired business into components (routes, customer list, labor, admin) with an SOP per component, and building an ever-growing integration checklist. He recommends studying roll-up playbooks (citing the book "How to Make a Few Billion Dollars" and Waste Management) and rebranding acquisitions under one brand to earn a valuation premium.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends keeping acquired operators on with a large post-integration bonus so incentives are aligned ("if this doesn't work then you're not going to get paid"), which he considers fair especially for small deals.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises separating an acquired business into its core components (routes, customer list, scooping labor, administrative functions) and building an SOP for each component.
- As of March 2026, Alex says integration is fundamentally "the world's longest checklist" that improves with every deal until it covers everything and lets you integrate faster.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends the book "How to Make a Few Billion Dollars," noting the author did three roll-ups each sold for about $10 billion, and points to Waste Management as the model for route-based roll-up growth.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises rebranding acquired businesses under one brand to command a premium valuation ("the big play").
- Caller claims (unverified): his dog-waste-removal company does ~$2M/year, aims for $10M/year, has near-zero cancellations, and roughly doubles acquisition each first quarter as snow melts.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "I would have some sort of big incentive for them to have bonus basically post integration. And so that way they're going to be highly incentivized... like we're in this together. If this doesn't work then you're not going to get paid."
