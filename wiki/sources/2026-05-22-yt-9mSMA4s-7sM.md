# How to Be Articulate & Charismatic

## Summary

Alex Hormozi answers a question about becoming articulate and charismatic by playing an
old awkward content clip from his gym days, then pointing to repetitions. He says he has
made ads for roughly 14 years, often at 50 or more per week, and estimates he has made
25,000 to 30,000 ads. The lesson is that speaking skill comes from trying, starting, and
doing far more reps for longer than expected.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says the answer to poor articulation and charisma is practice and repetition.
- He plays an old gym-era content clip to show that he was not naturally polished.
- The old clip was from about 16 years earlier, according to Alex.
- He says he has personally made 50 or more ads per week for about 14 years.
- He estimates he has made roughly 25,000 to 30,000 ads.
- After enough reps, his team can put a camera on, give him the hook or angle, and he can
  speak.
- People need to do much more than they think and for much longer than they expect.
- People may be paid less than expected for longer than expected, but the process can
  still be worth it.
- A lot of markets are winner-take-all, so the returns often arrive late in the skill
  curve.
- Many people never start, and many who start quit early.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Of course you're going to suck."

> "You have to do a lot more than you think you do."

> "When you do something 30 thousand times you get decent at it."
