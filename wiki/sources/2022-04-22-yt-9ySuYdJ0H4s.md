# REPROGRAM your mind to be rich in 22 minutes (28 ways to stay poor)

## Summary

Solo talk in which Alex Hormozi runs "28 ways to stay poor" as an exercise in
Charlie Munger's inversion thinking: list every way to sabotage an outcome, then do
the reverse. He walks all 28 poverty "rules" (start tomorrow, take advice from poor
people, wait for perfect conditions, look rich instead of being rich, spend more than
you make, etc.), then closes by flipping every one into a rule for building wealth.

## Key claims

- Inversion thinking works because human brains are wired to find threats/problems
  more easily than solutions; you harness that to list failure modes, then reverse
  them into strategy. (Paraphrase; credits Charlie Munger's "how to live a miserable
  life" talk.)
- "You are successful the moment you say you are" — success is a self-defined process,
  not an external number; chasing a number just moves the goalpost. (Paraphrase.)
- Prioritizing looking rich over being rich is a core poverty trait; being rich means
  spending less for a long time and being willing to look poor. (Paraphrase.)
- Do "what it takes," not "your best" — the marketplace only rewards what is required,
  so become good enough that your best exceeds what is required. (Paraphrase.)
- Regardless of income level, spending more than you make keeps you poor; wealth is
  controllable via lifestyle. (Paraphrase.)
