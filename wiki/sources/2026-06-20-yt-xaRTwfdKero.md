# $625K/Year Working 90 Minutes a Week — How to Scale to $3M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a commercial property owners association management company that has grown through haphazard broker, developer, and owner referrals. Alex reframes the business as a center-of-influence play: the associations are not the real customer acquisition target; brokers and other referral sources are. He recommends reverse-engineering how prior brokers became referrers, deliberately farming existing referrers, hunting new ones, and building a customer journey for brokers as the real marketing audience.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - commercial association management owner. His revenue, pay, workload, client count, and acquisition history are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- This business is fundamentally a center-of-influence strategy.
- Existing brokers who have referred business should be farmed with a deliberate communication cadence.
- New brokers should be hunted by measuring the cost and process of acquiring an activated broker.
- What previously happened accidentally with broker referrals should be made deliberate.
- The company should see brokers and COIs as the real customer for marketing purposes.
- Alex compares this to his ALAN experience, where agency owners, not SMB end users, became the true customer.
- A channel partner's underlying clients may churn, but the partner can remain stable and keep sending replacement clients.
- The operator should design the broker's customer journey, activation point, re-engagement, events, and reciprocity-building.
