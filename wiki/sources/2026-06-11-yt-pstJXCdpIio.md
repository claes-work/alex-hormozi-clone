# Helping a Pet Care Coaching Business Scale to $3M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi gives the same pet-care coaching founder advice captured in [[2026-06-11-yt-EbcqyxLLWN4]]. She has organic demand and industry-conference traction, but cannot take more customers because she is the only coach and has set high-access expectations. Alex says the constraint is delivery, not demand, and tells her to change customer expectations, route requests to resources or scheduled windows, and frame delivery reduction as integrity to the larger mission.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Do not change the selling model when the business is supply-constrained rather than demand-constrained.
- The founder-created high-touch model can become a monster because customers are trained to expect immediate access.
- The founder sets expectations and can reset them.
- A delivery reduction should be sold as a better fulfillment of the underlying mission, not as taking value away.
- The founder must "sell herself" on the new model instead of defending the model she says she hates.
- If the mission is to help more people, the current model may be out of integrity because it cannot scale.
- Some churn from old expectations is acceptable if it opens capacity for better-fit customers.
