# How To Raise Your Prices [plus the letter I actually sent]

## Summary
In this solo video Hormozi lays out a tactical method for raising prices on *existing* customers, arguing that price is the single biggest lever on profit and walking through the exact structure of the price-increase letter he uses. He illustrates the leverage with margin math and with a personal story from one of his first businesses (a gym/facility) where tripling prices cost him a third of customers but doubled revenue and sharply raised profit — his team called it "the culling" because it removed the problem customers. He also gives delivery tactics: announce via video and direct mail, turn off comments, break the price down to daily/weekly figures, and don't chicken out once committed.

## Key claims (2021; paraphrase unless quoted)
- Pricing is the number-one lever on profit. At a 20% net margin: a 20% price increase doubles profit, a 50% increase yields ~3.5x profit, and doubling price 6x's profit. (Alex)
- There is a right and wrong way to raise prices on existing customers; the wrong way is a blunt "we're raising prices" email, which drives cancellations and leaves you worse off. (Alex)
- The letter's framing is about the customer, not you. Its structure: (1) state why you're writing; (2) re-sell the value you currently provide and surface costs they don't realize you incur (talent, continued education, software/equipment upkeep) to get them thinking like an entrepreneur; (3) describe the future improvements you want to add (specialty programs, higher service levels, events); (4) tie the increase to a non-contestable *value/integrity* promise — you cannot honestly claim to be the best unless you invest, and that requires a price increase. (Alex)
- Communicate the price attractively by breaking it down: "an extra $100/week" or "$15/day" lands lighter than "$5,000/year" — the same reason insurance is quoted per day. (Alex)
- Add a P.S. for perspective: if the increase would materially affect their life (mortgage, groceries for their kids), invite them to reach out privately. This anchors everyone to the reality that no one goes homeless over it, while opening a door for genuine hardship cases. (Alex)
- Announce via video and direct mail, and turn comments off on the post — framed as not wanting a "gossip forum" and preferring to handle concerns one-on-one out of respect. (Alex)
- Expect "squeaky wheels" whose interest is in stopping the increase; once committed, stick with it. Finding an alternative provider is a bigger pain for customers than owners realize, and the customers who leave are usually your worst. (Alex)
- Bio detail: at his facility (one of his first businesses) he tripled prices, lost ~1/3 of customers, kept 2/3 at 3x price, doubled revenue, and raised profit further by servicing a third fewer customers; employees called it "the culling." (Alex, self-reported)
- The exact gym price-increase letter is included in his *Gym Launch Secrets* book (a business book applied to gyms), which he repeatedly notes is "not how I make money." (Alex; promo)
