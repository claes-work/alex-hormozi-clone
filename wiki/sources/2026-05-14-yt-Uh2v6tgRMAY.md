# Why Small Business Owners Always Undercharge

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains gross margin as the relationship between price and delivery cost,
then argues that gross margin dictates the rest of the business. He says small business
owners undercharge because they sell from their own wallet or undervalue what comes
easily to them. For service businesses, he treats 80% gross margin as a minimum rule of
thumb and links mispricing to commoditized offers and weak sales processes.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Business owners need to learn the language of business, including core financial
  terms and ratios.
- Gross margin is price minus cost to deliver.
- Net margin cannot exceed gross margin, so low gross margin caps the whole business.
- Small business owners often undercharge because they empathize from their own wallet.
- They also undercharge because they know how to do the work and therefore undervalue it.
- Pricing should be based on the value of life without the problem, not the seller's ease
  of delivery.
- Alex's rule of thumb is at least 80% gross margins for service businesses.
- A service that seems impossible to price at 80% gross margin is often commoditized.
- Thirty-day cash collected should cover cost of goods sold plus cost of acquiring the
  customer so the business can acquire the next customer.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have to learn the language of business."

> "I will not get into a business with less than 80% gross margins."

> "It's usually because your margins are off. You're mispriced."
