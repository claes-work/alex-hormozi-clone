# "I Have an In-Person Service Business. How Do I Switch to Online Only?"

## Summary

Short Q&A/coaching clip. An audience member — brand new to Skool, content creation, and
making money online — asks Alex Hormozi how to convert their one-to-one in-person coaching
service into a digital course with weekly coaching, given they have neither a course nor
content yet. Alex advises against overthinking the product and instead transitioning the
existing acquisition channel to remote delivery first. He lays out a staged path (sell
in person / deliver remotely → sell and deliver remotely), notes that in-person selling
enjoys higher conversion and lower lead costs because of inherent local trust, and stresses
that the real first priority for a beginner is advertising — letting people know the offer
exists — pointing to his own leads book for the steps.

## Key claims

_All paraphrased from the 2026-02-25 clip unless quoted below. The questioner is an
audience member (not Alex); their business description (in-person coaching, wanting a
digital course + weekly coaching) is self-reported and the question attribution is
`audience / uncertain`. Only Alex's answers are persona data._

- (2026-02-25) Whatever channel already gets you clients in person, just transition it to
  remote — don't wait on building a new digital product first.
- (2026-02-25) "Hybrid model": you can sell in person and then deliver remotely. Alex says
  this is what they did with gyms during COVID — one-on-one appointments could still be
  sold in person, but one-to-many delivery had to move remote (e.g. remote classes).
- (2026-02-25) In-person selling has meaningfully better conversion rates and lower lead
  costs than online because of inherent trust — people register you as "a real person who
  lives in my city" and assume you're not going anywhere.
- (2026-02-25) Staged path: the "1.0" version is to sell the way you currently do
  (in person) but sell *remote services*; the "2.0" version is to make both acquisition and
  delivery remote (sell remotely and deliver remotely).
- (2026-02-25) For someone just getting started, priorities number one and two are both
  advertising — letting people know about your stuff — because otherwise nobody buys no
  matter how good the offer is. Alex points to his leads book as the step-by-step for this.
