# Helping an SEO Agency Scale Past $1M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the founder of a large SEO/AEO agency who wants to build a personal brand to get more stages and clients. Alex says the brand should be built around the highest-value content for the exact buyers the founder wants to attract, not around views or broad edutainment. He recommends keeping the content mostly business-focused, with personal or philosophical material anchored back to the business lens.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - SEO/AEO agency founder. Her revenue, clients, personal-brand goal, and crowded-market concern are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The purpose of the personal brand determines the content strategy.
- If the goal is to get clients and speaking opportunities, the founder should make content for the exact people who buy the service.
- Views, likes, and broad popularity should be deprioritized when the goal is high-value B2B demand.
- The content should deliver the highest value per second to the target audience.
- Packaging still matters: hooks, visual hooks, pacing, and format are table stakes.
- Alex suggests roughly 80% straight-down-the-barrel business content, with only some personal or philosophical content.
- When Alex covers philosophy, relationships, fitness, or hardship, he anchors it back to business.
- If starting again, Alex says he would use the same business-centric content approach.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What is the objective of the brand?"

> "Disregard views, likes, whatever."

> "The absolute highest value per second that you can make for that audience."

> "The core meat of what you make should be as focused as possible on the thing that the people who are buying your stuff want."

> "I would probably go 80/20 on that."

> "I still try to anchor just about everything to business."

> "I would do exactly the exact same thing."
