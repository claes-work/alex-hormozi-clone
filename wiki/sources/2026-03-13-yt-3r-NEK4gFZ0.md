# Ads Have Changed Forever

## Summary
Short solo clip in which Alex argues that paid advertising has fundamentally shifted: winning now is about the **volume of different creative** rather than manual audience targeting. He cites a launch for which the portfolio produced "1,000 or 2,000 ads" before it even started and "3,000 by the time of the launch" — deliberately generating creative in the thousands. He frames where advertising is headed as **hyper-personalization at scale**: with AI you can create "way more permutations much faster," so ads move toward personalization for each viewer. He dates the call explicitly — speaking at end of 2025, calling it for 2026 — and says he does not think the shift will reverse ("this is the future"), citing "crazy returns" already seen across the portfolio companies. His strategic prescription goes beyond volume: build a **machine that self-proliferates creative** — "how do I get the customers to make creative that gets us more customers that make more creative?" — rather than just endlessly hiring a bigger team. He calls this the "self-licking ice cream cone" and names it his big lesson from the year that he's carrying into the next. Ends with the standard acquisition.com/roadmap ($100M Scaling Roadmap) promo.
