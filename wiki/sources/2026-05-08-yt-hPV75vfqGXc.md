# Helping a $1.35M Education Business Scale Past Its Lead Cap

## Summary

Alex coaches a Russian-speaking citizenship-prep business that wants to grow from
about $1.35M to $4.5M. He diagnoses the limit as a mix of creative volume, narrow
market size, and low LTV, then recommends a "self-licking ice cream cone" for customer
generated ads plus a real backend or adjacent language/job-skills offer.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says the business must realistically size the eligible market, not just the
  broad naturalization market.
- He says Meta's Andromeda-style ad system needs many fresh creatives because it will
  select only a small percentage to run.
- He recommends turning new customers into ad assets through lifecycle ads and
  testimonials.
- He recommends in-person events primarily to capture testimonials and media.
- He says a backend offer can increase revenue per customer and allow more ad spend.
- He distinguishes upsells from a true backend offer.
- He suggests job-related language or job-placement skills as a possible adjacent
  backend, subject to avatar fit.
- He says the business may be capped by the small Russian-speaking eligible market
  unless it raises LTV or expands into adjacent language verticals.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You need to create a self-licking ice cream cone."

> "You don't have a back end, you have upsells."

> "Do the ad thing first."
