# How Do I Hire Better Sales Reps Without Overpaying?

## Summary
In this short clip, Alex Hormozi advises a caller named Anthony, who runs a solar sales business doing ~$500,000 in revenue at ~78% profit and wants to reach $3 million. Anthony blames his BDR/SDR team's performance and retention; his reps are remote (globally sourced) and paid a small $500 base every two weeks plus uncapped commission. Hormozi diagnoses the compensation plan as the primary lever and lays out how to fix it, including a "try before you buy" 30-day contractor structure and the argument that remote reps compare the opportunity against all jobs, not just other solar jobs.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that fixing the compensation plan is the biggest lever for the caller's business: better comp attracts better SDRs, which increases sales, which increases cash flow, which then funds the later move to remote/virtual selling.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends a "try before you buy" onboarding: bring reps on as contractors for 30 days on a slightly lower base, and lock in their full base only after they pass the KPIs — reducing repeated hire-and-fire churn.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the financial downside of a bad hire as small: paying roughly $50,000/year (~$1,000/week), a rep who fails within two weeks costs only about $2,000 — trivial against 75-80% net margins, so it is worth giving up some margin to attract better people.
- As of March 2026, Alex says remote SDRs benchmark the offer against every other remote opportunity available to them (not just solar roles), so a $500-per-two-weeks base is uncompetitive.
- As of March 2026, Alex notes that for a transactional seller, pipeline typically starts building within roughly 14 to 30 days, so a reasonable base to bridge that ramp is justified.
- As of March 2026, Alex outlines the sequencing: fix comp first, grow cash flow, then invest in enabling remote selling to scale a rep from ~4-5 consults a day to 20+, and eventually duplicate that sales process in others.
- Caller-reported (unverified): Anthony sells solar to homeowners, does ~$500,000 in revenue at ~78% profit, uses globally-sourced virtual reps, and pays a $500 base every two weeks with uncapped commission; he wants to reach $3 million.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "One thing you can do is do like a try before you buy type situation which is like we're going to make you a contractor for 30 days and then if you pass the KPIs then we'll roll you in. That way it's way less of a like hey hire and fire, hire and fire."

> "If you pay somebody like $40,000 a year in terms of what you'd actually pay them. Call it 50 for simple math. So it's 1,000 bucks a week. If you pay someone for two weeks and you don't think they're good, you lost two grand."

> "Maybe give up some margin so that you can scale and just get better people in."

> "The remote SDRs that are potentially working for you are comparing your opportunity not to other solar opportunities, but to all other opportunities. And so getting $500 every two weeks is like, yeah, 100%, tough."

> "Fix the comp. That gets the better SDRs in. SDRs increase sales. Increase sales increase cash flow. Increase cash flow then we can start investing the extra time into getting the virtual sales."
