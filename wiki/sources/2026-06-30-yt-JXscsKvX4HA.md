# Doctor or $7M Brand? Pick One.

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a fitness-coaching founder deciding whether to enter medical school while also building a preventive-health brand. Alex says the founder's problem is not tactics but lack of an optimization target. If the founder wants the MD path, he should accept six years of under-earning as the trade. Alex frames decision-making as cutting off futures and asks which future the founder would regret more at age 85.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Fitness-coaching founder / future medical student** - the coachee. His business, medical-school plan, assets, family pressure, and health-brand ambition are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The real question is whether the founder should forego income today to earn MD credibility that may help later.
- The fair comparison is not "me with an MD vs. me without an MD"; it is "me with an MD after a six-year delay" vs. "me without an MD with a six-year head start."
- A decision is hard when the founder has nothing to optimize against: money, impact, or another clear objective.
- Figuring out what to do is the hard part; getting there is easier once the target is chosen.
- Many people stay stuck for years because they are afraid of making a decision and mourning the path not taken.
- Decision means cutting off other futures.
- If the founder would regret not becoming a doctor more at age 85, he should choose medical school and accept the six-year under-earning trade.
