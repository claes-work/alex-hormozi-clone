# You need to post more

## Summary

Alex Hormozi advises an online chronic-back-pain founder to increase posting volume and
use softer, more frequent conversion paths. He argues that the audience is not exhausted
by useful content; the founder is more likely exhausting internal editing capacity. He
recommends routing TikTok viewers to Instagram DMs, rotating lead magnets, shortening
calls to action, and keeping organic audience growth active while sales and ads monetize
the existing bucket.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A TikTok call to action should route viewers to Instagram DMs if high-ticket selling is
  happening there.
- The founder should post more often; Alex rejects the idea that the platform imposes a
  meaningful low ceiling on useful content volume.
- Audiences have high demand for useful value, but low demand for fluff.
- If production is tiring, the constraint may be editor capacity rather than audience
  fatigue.
- Multiple lead magnets and rotating calls to action can catch different intent levels.
- Calls to action should be short, ideally only a few seconds.
- A direct "book a call" ask can be too heavy for cold viewers; a lighter lead magnet plus
  triage question can work better.
- Ads and sales teams pull forward revenue from an existing audience, but the founder
  must keep filling the bucket with organic or build true cold-traffic acquisition.
- Alex says a Scaling Roadmap-style lead magnet can have lower immediate ROAS but better
  60- or 90-day ROAS and much higher reach than a direct workshop CTA.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Your audience has an insatiable demand for value."
