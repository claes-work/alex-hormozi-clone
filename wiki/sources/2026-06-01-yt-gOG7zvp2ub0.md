# Scared of Debt? Your Business Will Stall

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a Mexican rental-equipment company owner who wants to grow but is psychologically uncomfortable taking on more debt. Alex says one side of the tradeoff has to give: in a capital-intensive business, faster growth often requires capital. The solution is not emotional reassurance but math: model volatility, cash flow, covenants, coverage, and default risk. He validates appropriate risk aversion if the owner wants to survive and compound for decades.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- In a capital-intensive business, wanting faster growth while refusing the capital required for growth creates an unavoidable tradeoff.
- Debt increases risk because volatility has more power to impair payments and control.
- The right question is how much risk the owner is willing to take, not whether debt is universally good or bad.
- The owner should model seasonality, cash flow, debt covenants, payment coverage, and plausible adverse events.
- Good lenders usually do some of this diligence before extending business debt.
- If the founder refuses debt, growth can still happen, but it will be slower.
- Slower growth can be rational if the company wants to survive for 20 or 30 years and avoid a zero-out event.
- The goal is to remove emotionality and make a quantified bet.
