# My 12 Hour Work Day (in 15 Minutes) [Ep 003]

## Summary

A vlog walking through a working day at acquisition.com, structured around one recurring
theme: **praise/reinforcement over punishment** as a management and life philosophy. Alex
covers his morning routine, his productivity stack, the company's dual vision, a pipeline
review (Bettors vs. Builders investing), the economics of self-funding his free book, and
why he now makes higher-produced content ("the band that reaches the most people").

## Key claims

- **Reward beats punishment** — the day's spine. Reinforced behavior captures
  "discretionary effort"; punishment-based cultures (McKinsey, Goldman) get performance
  only by raising the bar and burning out anxiety-driven high performers. Timing matters:
  reward/punish too late and you teach the wrong lesson (kid cries → gets cookie → learns
  to cry; punish a kid for coming home late → kid stops coming home).
- **Two visions in a company:** internal (relevant to team/vendors/customers) and external
  (impact on the greater community) — acquisition.com's mission is "to make real business
  education available to everybody," built "our way, which is praise over punishment."
- **Two schools of investing:** Bettors (find a mispriced company, Warren Buffett style) vs.
  Builders (templatized playbooks/SOPs deployed to 3–5–10x companies). acquisition.com
  leans Builder — "we just try and find companies that we know our playbooks will
  immediately 10x." 95% of deals that reach the pipeline are passed on.
- **Branding via association** (shirt story): a teammate wearing acquisition.com merch got
  VIP treatment — value from the book → reward → associate with the book → with Alex → then
  "remove me and you just keep the thing"; someone else in the shirt gets the same feeling.
- **Self-funds the free book to keep it free:** publishers won't let you give a book away
  free; "Uncle Alex foots the bill... probably like a million two into this book negative."
  Finished 16 weeks before release (12 weeks is printing); uses multiple printers for
  quality; books fund Mosey Media.
- **Higher-produced content = reaching more people:** editing/thumbnails aren't "selling
  out" — "the band morphs into the thing that reaches the most people"; audience "votes
  with clicks and eyeballs" and thereby trains what he makes.
