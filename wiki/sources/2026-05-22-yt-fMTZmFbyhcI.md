# The Tim Ferriss Quote That Changed How I Live

## Summary

Alex Hormozi repeats two internal refrains: he will do what is required, and even if the
outside world praises the outcome, he will know whether he could have done better. In the
same conversation, he describes a simple good-day blueprint: work hard on something worth
doing, work out, and spend time with people he enjoys.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says some work simply needs doing and no one else will do it.
- In early content sessions, he says he recorded about 100 direct-to-camera shorts at a
  time because that was the available slot.
- "I will do what's required" is a useful internal refrain when he knows what needs to be
  done.
- Another internal refrain is "but I'll know": even if others praise the outcome, he will
  know if he could have done better.
- He says he has never regretted working harder.
- A former boss's idea that happiness is stringing together as many good days as possible
  became a blueprint for him.
- His birthday, Tuesday, and Sunday ideally look similar because he tries to build a life
  of repeatable good days.
- His good-day checklist is working hard on something worth doing, working out, and
  spending time with people he enjoys.
- He says many people hear "work" as "pain," while successful people may define work
  differently.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I've never regretted working harder ever."
