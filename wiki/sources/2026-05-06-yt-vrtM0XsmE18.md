# How to Funnel a LinkedIn Audience Into a Paid Community

## Summary

Alex advises a creator with a LinkedIn audience and newsletter to use simple CTAs into
a School community. For LinkedIn, he recommends a first-comment or postscript CTA
because outbound links may be downgraded. For email, he recommends a short hook,
immediately useful tactical nugget, example, and a CTA for either more depth or a more
personalized version.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-06)

- Alex says small LinkedIn followings can still do well on School.
- He recommends continuing the existing LinkedIn content if it is working.
- He recommends first-comment or PS-style CTAs for LinkedIn because links can be
  downgraded.
- He says email has some of the best marketing returns relative to cost.
- He structures emails with a short pithy hook, tactical nugget, example, and CTA.
- He says not to hold back in the tactical nugget.
- He says the two core CTA types are "more" and "more personal."
- He says "more personal" can work better, but requires actually personalizing the
  help.
- He says the CTA should be contiguous with the email, not pasted on generically.

## Notable verbatim quotes
