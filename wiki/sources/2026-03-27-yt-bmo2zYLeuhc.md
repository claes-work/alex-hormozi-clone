# Why Isn't My Business Scaling?

## Summary
In this call, Alex Hormozi advises Mike Nathan, founder of an in-home cellular/IV-infusion therapy business ($1M revenue, ~50% EBITDA, targeting $25M and an eventual sale). Nathan's team excels at B2B doctor-referral (affiliate) sales but has no B2C experience and wonders whether to add online advertising to scale. Hormozi argues the business is nowhere near tapped out on the channel it already has: the fix is to reactivate and properly manage the existing ~200 affiliate physicians rather than start a new B2C playbook from scratch.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex advises squeezing an already-working acquisition channel to its limit before starting a new one; he would not chase B2C ads while the B2B affiliate channel remains "completely unoptimized."
- As of March 2026, Alex frames affiliates as "a second tier of customer" that must be actively managed by a dedicated affiliate manager who "stokes the fire" so they keep referring.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites his Leads book (p. 240, affiliates chapter) "launch and integrate" model: run a big joint promotion to reach an affiliate's whole customer base (launch), then integrate so referrals happen automatically without reminders (integration).
- As of March 2026, Alex says the first referred patient is a "trial" — a test run for the affiliate — so that first experience must be flawless, and the referrer should be looped back afterward ("We blew Sandy away, by the way").
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends systematizing patient-outcome follow-up to the referring doctor and asking "which patients would be a good fit?" rather than "do you have any?"
- As of March 2026, Alex says once a B2B playbook works he would just replicate it in new metros rather than solve B2C first.
- Caller-reported (unverified): business does ~$1M revenue at ~50% EBITDA; ~$500 cost to acquire a referring physician who refers ~$1,000/year (a ~12:1 return); price is $6,000 per treatment; ~200 affiliates recruited across the Twin Cities, most referring only one patient.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "So the way that I think about affiliates is it's basically a second tier of customer. And so you need to have somebody who's regularly kind of like stoking the affiliate fire to keep them activated and continue to get them to continue to refer business."

> "There's so much juice left in this thing. I don't want to now start something new. It's like I barely got this one going. I want to like crush this."

> "That first patient's the test run for them for you... one, it's like Sandy's got to be blown away, right? She's got to come back and be like, 'Oh my god, that place was amazing.' And then you also have to go back to the doc and be like, 'We blew Sandy away, by the way.'"

> "Rather than saying, 'Do you have any?' it's 'Which ones would be?' is the question. Small training stuff, but it matters."

> "If I crush my B2B play, then I just run my B2B playbook in a new market. Like once I find something that works, I just want to just do more."

> "Imagine harnessing the horsepower of 200 businesses of advertising and forcing it through one."
