# Reacting to My First Videos 10 Years Later

## Summary

Present-day Alex Hormozi reacts to a compilation of his own earliest fitness/gym
content from roughly 10-13 years ago, most of it self-recorded talking-head clips
made while he owned and operated a gym. The old clips cover fitness coaching (a free
6-week challenge, a "mind game series" on love vs. discipline, body-fat measurement
via bioelectrical impedance) plus early business musings on planning vs. executing. A
pivotal clip captures the moment his free "go in and launch gyms and split the upside"
service was born — the seed of what became his gym-services business. The video ends
with a present-day promo for his book bundle (all three books plus 30 days of Skool
for $16). Alex is the sole/primary speaker in both the old footage and the
reactions.

## Key claims

- The compilation first "took off" on his highlights channel; this upload re-shares it. (self-reported)
- His earliest content was fitness/gym coaching recorded largely by himself; production and appearance were rough ("I didn't even put a shirt on... I don't look good"). (self-reported)
- He ran a gym under the brand **United Fitness**, describing himself on camera as "the owner of United Fitness." (self-reported)
- Early offer mechanics already present: a free 6-week challenge, screening for "super motivated" people, custom nutrition plans, body-fat tracking. (self-reported)
- On business philosophy (early clip): success among high earners is mostly execution, not secrets — "you set out with a plan and then you execute"; plans need not be detailed because "no plan survives first contact." He says he changed his own approach ~6 times before finding what worked. (self-reported, Alex-attributed opinion, dated ~10-13y ago)
- Origin of his gym-launch service: he began going into gyms for **free** to launch them and **split** the additional revenue he brought in; a prospect who saw him speak asked to buy something before he even had an offer, and that "weirdest, funniest sale from the stage" sprouted "the entire new business." (self-reported)
- In that early period he launched **three different gyms in one month**. (self-reported)
- Present-day bookend: 3.6M copies of his book(s) were donated by other entrepreneurs in his book launch; he offers all three books ("what to sell," "get people to find out about it," "make money from it") + 30 days of Skool free + shipping for **$16**, deliberately at a loss. (self-reported, present-day promo)
