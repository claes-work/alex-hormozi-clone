# Nobody Buys Because You Sound Like Everyone Else

## Summary

In this short teaching clip, Alex Hormozi responds to a creator who says he cannot hire because nobody can do the work as well as he can. Alex reframes the bottleneck as the creator's inability to train, then recommends breaking the creator's work into constituent parts and delegating components rather than trying to replace the full rare skill at once. The goal is to keep the founder on the highest-value, rarest part while others prepare research, hooks, offer context, and other leverage work.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - sole substantive speaker. The teaching below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Founders should reframe external bottlenecks as their own inability to perform or train the missing function.
- If salespeople, Facebook, or hires "do not work," the better frame is that the founder has not learned how to train or use them.
- A rare creator skill should be decomposed into smaller trainable components.
- Delegation can start with research, hook gathering, creative preparation, or other pieces that consume most of the time.
- The goal is not immediate self-replacement; it is replacing components of the founder's work one layer at a time.
- Eventually the founder should do only the highest-value and rarest skill, while support staff create leverage around it.
