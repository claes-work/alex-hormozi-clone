# How to Improve Email Open Rates

## Summary
In a live Q&A, a caller reports that their free lead magnet converts well on opt-in but their subsequent email flow only sees roughly 20–25% open rates, and asks how to raise it. Alex Hormozi answers with two levers: improving the actual quality of the lead magnet (value delivered reinforces future consumption), and split-testing subject lines regularly. He digresses into how he prioritizes tests by cost versus expected output and into his "big list of ideas" practice for managing a high idea-generation rate. The caller states they are not yet in charge of email marketing; only Alex is quoted.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that email open rate is driven first by the underlying quality of the lead magnet: value delivered reinforces the audience consuming more, and you grow an audience by giving them more than it cost them to consume it. He frames this as how he built his own brand.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends split-testing email subject lines on a regular basis, and notes that many questions he gets are answerable by a test rather than by his opinion (he cites testing pricing the same way).
- As of March 2026, Alex describes a "pendulum of testing" — from testing everything constantly to never testing — with a sweet spot, because every test carries a guaranteed cost (cost of change, mess-ups) while the upside is not guaranteed.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises ordering candidate tests by lowest cost / highest potential output; low-cost tests (like a subject-line split test) sometimes have massive output, while high-cost tests (changing an offer, retraining team and fulfillment) do not always pay off.
- As of March 2026, Alex says he generates roughly one new business idea per day while Leila's capacity is about one per quarter; he manages the surplus by (a) making content as a creative outlet and (b) maintaining "Alex's big list of ideas," letting ideas sit so emotional freshness fades and only durable ones get tested.
- As of March 2026, Alex's concrete tactical suggestion for the caller: Google "split test headlines for subjects," collect ~500 examples, and filter for those that fit your niche.
- Caller claim (unverified): their lead magnet is free and good and converts well on opt-in, but the email flow only sees ~20–25% open rates; caller is not yet responsible for the email marketing.
