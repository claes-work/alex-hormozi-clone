# Why Your Restaurant Marketing Doesn't Matter

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a restaurant group doing roughly $25M across several concepts. The owner asks about restaurant LTV:CAC and how to justify marketing spend. Alex says restaurants and high-tech converge on the same long-run truth: product is what matters most. In restaurants, food, service, environment, and overall experience drive word of mouth and reviews. Marketing matters most for grand openings, but only after the operator knows the product can turn initial attention into repeat demand.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Restaurants are unusually product-driven; if the food or total experience is not noteworthy, marketing cannot compensate long term.
- Word of mouth and reviews dominate restaurant discovery.
- Paid/promotional marketing is most useful around grand openings.
- A grand-opening playbook should include overstaffing, ad spend, and promotional giveaways or invite-only equivalents for premium concepts.
- Brick-and-mortar openings should not be pushed hard until the operator knows the model can reach full capacity from the initial attention wave.
- Premium paid into ingredients, service, systems, and experience can be treated as marketing if it increases word of mouth.
- In restaurants, data collection is harder and gross margins are smaller, so product quality matters even more.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Product is the only thing that matters."
