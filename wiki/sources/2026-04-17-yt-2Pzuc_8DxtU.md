# Change Who You Compare Yourself to

## Summary

Alex argues that reference groups are the people whose opinions influence hard decisions,
not necessarily the five people someone spends the most time with. Proximity only matters
if it changes behavior. The useful move is to think about people closest to the goal, learn
their behavior through observation, and reinvest in rooms where behavior models are better.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-17, Alex says that as he has made more money, he has fewer mentors and more
  heroes.
- He rejects the simplistic "average of the five people" framing; the real reference group
  is whose opinion affects hard decisions.
- He says proximity does nothing by itself; it matters only insofar as it changes behavior.
- He says people learn many small behaviors unconsciously through observation.
- Self-reported: he says a private-equity meeting around the Gym Launch deal made him see
