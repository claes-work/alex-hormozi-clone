# The Simple Framework That Solves 99% of Business Problems

## Summary

In this short teaching answer, Alex Hormozi says a done-for-you high-ticket sales company should first determine whether it is demand constrained or supply constrained. If delivery is the concern, the business needs a parallel funnel for talent that mirrors the customer journey: applications, application nurture, interviewing, onboarding, retention, and ascension. The key point is that delivery capacity requires marketing, sales, onboarding, and retention for employees or contractors just as customer acquisition does for buyers.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - sole substantive speaker. The framework below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The first diagnostic is whether the company is constrained by customer demand or delivery supply.
- If the business can only take a limited number of clients, it should increase demand only up to current delivery capacity first.
- Delivery talent requires its own marketing funnel.
- The customer journey can be mapped as leads, nurture, sales, activation, retention, and ascension.
- The talent journey mirrors that process as applications, application nurture, interviewing, onboarding, retention, and ascension.
- Interviewing is a sale, and onboarding employees is analogous to onboarding customers.
- The practical fix is to build a parallel process for delivery talent.
