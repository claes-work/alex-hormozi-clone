# Helping a $5M E-Com Brand Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches the founder of a safety-jewelry company doing about $5M/year after roughly
$20M in lifetime sales. The company has DTC social-media-driven sales and large B2B
distribution partnerships. Alex's first diagnostic is cohort LTV: segment customers by
where they came from and identify which cohort stays longest and has the highest lifetime
value. He says the business fundamentally needs more advertising/distribution, and the
easiest path may be closing more B2B distribution deals because the founder already has
that skill set.

## Key claims

- Alex asks whether the best current customers are one-time purchasers or subscribers, and
  how long they stay.
- He wants the founder to segment customers by acquisition source and compare lifetime
  value by cohort.
- He says both DTC and distribution opportunities could work, but the right focus is where
  the best customers come from.
- He diagnoses the business as needing more advertising/distribution.
- He says the ultimate version of the business probably has a killer marketer who can drive
  traffic.
- Given the founder's current skill set and existing ADT/Telus deals, he says more B2B
  distribution deals may be the highest-likelihood path of consistent growth.
- He also says the business has strong direct-response potential: one excellent ad story
  could materially scale DTC if the team can build the required marketing skill set.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Segment your customers by cohort to figure out where do those customers come from, the ones that are staying the longest."

> "Fundamentally you need more advertising aka distribution."

> "This is like a national infomercial waiting to be made."

> "The ultimate version of this business would have a killer marketer who really knows how to drive traffic."
