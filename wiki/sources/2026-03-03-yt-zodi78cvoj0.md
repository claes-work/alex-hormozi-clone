# Stop Making Excuses

## Summary
Talk/Q&A clip (audience laughter audible, but all substantive speech is Alex). Alex attacks the habit of manufacturing reasons for failure. His Q&A tactic: when someone offers "here's why that won't work for me," just agree — "You're right. You'll never succeed." — which flips the person into fighting *for* their own success ("the fighter showed up… you were fighting on the wrong side"). He ties this to a sales move he calls the **"reason close"**: whatever objection a prospect gives ("my husband doesn't support me," "I don't have time," "I don't have the money"), reframe it as "that's the perfect reason you should do this." His underlying claim: reasons are irrelevant because "the doing just needs doing" — it doesn't care whether you have a reason, or whether you're a good or bad person. He shares a personal note: for a long time he considered himself "a very bad person" / "the villain," and found it freeing to realize that "bad guys can win too" — success is behavior-driven and under your control, not gated on being a good person. This is why he boils everything down to **behaviors** and has spent near-zero time on manifesting/affirmations/psychology: "if you do 100 reach outs, more people will find out about your stuff" regardless. He closes with a two-part definition: **learning** = "same condition, new behavior" (red card → slap → eventually you duck), and **intelligence** = "rate of learning," a speed (fewer reps to change = smarter). The practical frame: if you attend an event and your behavior in the same conditions doesn't change, "it means you learned nothing" — either the source was worthless or you failed to receive it. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues people invent "contrarian" excuses "for no reason to give ourselves excuses"; his counter in Q&A is to simply agree ("you're right, you'll never succeed"), which provokes the person into asserting they *will* succeed.
- As of March 2026, his sales "reason close": take whatever reason/objection the prospect gives and reframe it as "that's the perfect reason you should do this" — he calls it "insane how effective this is cuz it's so silly sounding."
- As of March 2026, he claims reasons are basically irrelevant because "the doing just needs doing" — action doesn't care whether you have a reason, or whether you're a good or bad person.
- As of March 2026 (personal/dated), Alex says for a long time he considered himself "a very bad person" and "the villain"; the freeing realization was that "bad guys can win too" — you can do what successful people do and win anyway, keeping success under your control.
- As of March 2026, he says he has spent "very little or zero time" on manifesting, affirmations, thoughts, and psychology, because behaviors are what move outcomes ("if you do 100 reach outs, more people will find out about your stuff… whether you set affirmations in the morning… it doesn't matter"); focusing on behaviors is his "razor to cut through the noise."
- As of March 2026, he claims this behavior frame lets you discard ~90% of content from people "who haven't been there and never did it," who "fill lots of fluff… because they have no substance."
- As of March 2026, definition: **learning = same condition, new behavior** (red-card-then-slap example: eventually you duck).
- As of March 2026, definition: **intelligence = rate of learning**, a speed — the person who changes behavior after fewer reps is more intelligent; defining it this way "makes intelligence under our control."
- As of March 2026, his consumption test: if you attend/consume something and your behavior in the same conditions doesn't change, "it means you learned nothing" — so either the source wasn't valuable or you need to change; "red card, slap. You don't change… how smart are you?"
