# How the Top 1% of Customers Make 51% of Your Profit

## Summary

In this short teaching clip, Alex Hormozi applies Pareto / power-law logic to customer profitability. He says 20% of customers can create 80% of profit, 4% can create 64%, and the top 1% can create about 51%, then connects this to wealth distribution and premium offer design. The practical point is that a business model must allow high-value customers to pay more, because under-offering to a high-budget buyer can destroy more value than over-offering to a low-budget buyer.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - solo teaching. The claims below are Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.

## Key claims (Alex's teaching only)

- Big companies grow by going where the money is.
- Pareto's principle appears in business as a minority of customers creating the majority of revenue or profit.
- Alex presents a nested version: 20% of customers create 80% of profit, 4% create 64%, and 1% create 51%.
- Power-law customer economics explain why serving one high-value customer can be much more profitable than serving many low-value customers.
- This only works if the business model lets high-value customers pay more.
- Businesses should expect most customers to say no to expensive products and services.
- When a high-budget "whale" appears, the business should have an offer capable of capturing that willingness to pay.
