# 6 Hacks To Make More Money [Increase LTV] Without More Customers (2021-04-20)

## Summary

Hormozi lays out a proto version of his lifetime-value framework, redefining
lifetime value as **lifetime gross profit (LTGP)** rather than the "amorphous"
standard term. He frames all business growth as two levers — get more customers,
or make each customer worth more — and gives six ways to increase per-customer
gross profit, ending with pricing as the single most powerful lever. This is an
early ancestor of the money-model / LTGP:CAC thinking later formalized in his
books.

## Key claims

- (2021) There are only two ways to grow a business: (1) get more customers, or
  (2) make those customers worth more. **Proto money-model dichotomy.**
- (2021) He redefines "lifetime value" as **lifetime gross profit (LTGP)** —
  gross margin (revenue minus cost of delivery), not net profit and not revenue.
  He dislikes "LTV" as too loosely/amorphously defined. Example: a $10 meal
  costing $9 to deliver yields only $1 gross profit, so a $5 acquisition cost
  is actually spending $5 to make $1 of gross profit — a bad deal despite the
  "2-to-1" revenue optics. **Proto LTGP:CAC.**

### The 6 hacks to increase LTV (lifetime gross profit)

1. **Increase the price.** Price itself creates allure and can reposition a
   product into a higher category (a Chanel purse at $20 wouldn't feel cool).
2. **Decrease the cost (of fulfillment).** Invest in technology, processes, or
   outsourcing to deliver the same thing for less (e.g. a robotic process
   dropping meal cost from $9 to $2) — raising gross profit without changing
   anything the customer sees.
3. **Increase purchase frequency.** Get people to buy again and again; getting
   every client to buy one additional time lifts LTGP across the whole business.
4. **Upsell.** Sell the *same* thing in a more valuable version — more of the
   same or better of the same (Honda → Lexus; 20oz → 32oz soda).
5. **Cross-sell.** Sell an *adjacent* need — the next natural need after the
   first purchase (burger or fries with the Coke). Often where the majority of
   the profit lives; enables loss-leader models if you know your numbers.
6. **Downsell.** Monetize the traffic/people who say no to the main offer — the
   "sawdust" / excess capacity. Either refer out to a partner's product for
   affiliate/referral commissions (everybody wins), or offer a lower-cost-to-
   fulfill version solving the same need (done-for-you → done-with-you /
   do-it-yourself; personal training → group or at-home version).

- (2021) Pricing is the single most powerful and most underutilized lever. Most
  people price by averaging the marketplace — but "everyone else is broke."
  Small businesses stay small because they think like small business owners;
  model the pricing of *mature* big businesses that have survived past the
  growth phase (with a caution that funded market-penetration pricing is a
  special case).
- (2021) Price can multiply LTGP where other levers can't: on a $10-to-fulfill
  item, moving price from $20 to $30 *doubles* the lifetime gross profit. Few
  other mechanisms can double/triple/quadruple LTGP the way price can — which is
  also why people carry the biggest limiting beliefs around raising it.
