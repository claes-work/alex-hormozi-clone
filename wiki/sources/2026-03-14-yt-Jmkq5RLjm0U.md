# If I Wanted To Grow An Audience In 2026, I'd Do This

## Summary

A long compilation video stitching together several Alex Hormozi solo presentations on building a personal brand and audience. It opens with a branding keynote (branding as the deliberate pairing of your business with good outcomes for your ideal customer), then walks through the six data-driven shifts he made to his own content, his "agency method" for buying skill on a new platform, the SPCL influence framework, four ways to monetize an audience, and a behind-the-scenes reveal of the metrics that resulted from the six shifts. Throughout, the thesis is that in 2026 he is going all-in on education (over entertainment), on longs and live streams, and on making narrow business content for business owners rather than chasing views.

## Key claims (his 2026 audience-growth playbook)

- **Branding is "a deliberate pairing of things through an outcome."** Advertising = letting people know about your stuff; branding = the pairing (association) that occurs as a result. Branding always happens; the goal is *good* branding — deliberately pairing your business with good outcomes for your ideal customer.
- What you pair your brand with determines (1) who pays attention and (2) whether they move toward or away from you. A strong brand turns a commoditized product (a $5 white t-shirt) into a premium one and lets you charge many times more, get higher ad response, and win repeat/loyal customers.
- Recover from a bad pairing (e.g. a DUI, a "dead rose" in the bouquet) not by trying to erase it but by **overwhelming the audience with much more of the stuff they like** until the bad pairing shrinks into irrelevance (cites Kanye).
- Brand has three metrics: **influence** (likelihood it changes behavior), **direction** (toward or away), and **reach** (how many people it changes it for). Strong brands do *not* have to be polar — Taylor Swift, Mother Teresa, and Apple are large/strong/positive without being polarizing; you don't have to trade on controversy.
- **Claimed results (40-ish months):** ~7.8M subscribers across platforms, ~2–3B impressions, 8M+ books sold; built on $4M in team/equipment/studios/software, 1,000+ hours on camera, and **35,000 pieces of content**. Original "fancy plan" was just: make as much good stuff as I could, post it everywhere, learn as much as I could.
- **Six shifts he is doubling down on:** (1) edutainment → **education**; (2) for us (his team) → **for you** (the ideal customer/business owner); (3) wide → **narrow** (only business models, leverage, selling); (4) views → **ad revenue** as the primary metric; (5) shorts → **longs**; (6) assume more → **assume nothing**.
- **All views are not created equal.** The funnel theory (entertainment viewers graduate to education, shorts viewers graduate to longs) is wrong in his data: entertainment viewers want more entertainment, education viewers want more education, shorts viewers watch more shorts, longs viewers watch more longs, and customers buy more. These are largely different audiences (though a person can be a shorts viewer on one platform and a longs viewer on another).
- **Ad revenue (RPM × views) is his new north-star metric** because it's a fast, paired quality-and-quantity metric — RPM proxies audience quality (business owners have high spending power, so high RPM = right people). The month with highest RPM/ad revenue also had the most book sales, opt-ins, and applications, independent of raw view count. In a 90-day experiment with edutainment/entertainment, views 2–3x'd but ad revenue dropped by half — wrong people.
- **"For you" tactics (5):** clearer (not vague) packaging/thumbnails/headlines; different intros built on **Proof, Promise, Plan** (lead with the one that matters most — for a business educator, usually proof); "meat" that emphasizes language over "razzle" (in 30 days, ~75% of top videos emphasized message over production); effective (not over-) production, replacing visual effects with data visualization; and "pre-search" over post-production ("an ounce of pre-work is worth a pound of post" — front-loading research cut ~90–95% of edit hours and ~4x'd output).
- **"Assume nothing" tactics:** title content as if viewers don't know you; introduce yourself and say why they should listen *every time*; explain every inside joke ("if you tell an inside joke and someone doesn't understand it, guess where they are? On the outside"); re-post your greatest hits because new audience members never saw them (TV theme songs / Simpsons chalkboard gag as precedent — same intro with small variety serves both new and old fans).
- **Agency method for learning a new platform:** decide if you have more money or more time; if money, "pay down your ignorance." Phase 1: hire a cheap ($3–5k/mo) agency just to force a posting cadence and learn basics. Phase 2: move to an advanced ($15–30k/mo) agency and tell them up front you want to learn their process over ~6 months, document it into SOPs, co-create, then transition them to a lower-cost consulting retainer and eventually decouple amicably. This builds enterprise value/an owned asset and avoids the "agency trap" (the 11-step cycle from $100M Leads where a senior rep gets swapped for a junior and results decay).
- **SPCL influence framework** — Status (control scarce resources/reinforcers in an environment), Power (say-do correspondence: you say, they do, a good thing happens, so they comply next time — his top pick if forced to choose one), Credibility (third-party validation, e.g. paying Guinness judges to verify the book-launch record), Likeness (relatability / "just be you"). Parents max out all four, which is why they have so much influence. Influence = likelihood of compliance with a request; stack all four to "min-max" it. Reverse-engineer SPCL into every piece of content.
- **Live + interactive is his stated media strategy going forward** ("ACQ 3.0 / Mozi Media 3.0"). At a Mr. Beast soccer event the applause escalated A-listers → shorts creators → long-form creators → **live streamers** (biggest). Longer formats allow far more "reinforcing cycles" (his "podcast election" argument re: Trump/Rogan and Elon/Tucker). The internet trends toward truth/rawness, which favors live.
- **Volume is linear/near-mechanical:** ~1 in 10 shorts is a 2–3x outlier; 100x the volume ≈ 100x the prospects ("volume negates luck"). Rogan, PBD, Dave Ramsey all post hours daily.
- **"Social media is becoming interest media": the content IS the targeting.** The algorithm reads your topic, background, and clothing and shows it to people with matching interest history, so make content for your avatar. Judge content by IRL responses (texts from business owners you respect) and quality of prospects, not views. Only ~9% of the US owns a business; ~95% of those are under $1M revenue; ~0.4% (1 in 250) do $10M+; ~1 in 3,000 hits $100M — so a 10k-view video watched by the right business owners can beat a 400k-view one. Set view expectations against your true market size.
- **Four ways to monetize an audience** (a continuum of rising difficulty/risk/value): **affiliates** (fastest, low risk, paid after the sale, but low exit value), **sponsorships** (paid first as a traffic source; scalable via whitelisting; low exit value unless you're a true media/talent company), **partnerships** (usually equity; structure with up-front + performance + time-based vesting; the public association is permanent so get something up front), and **starting your own brand** (white-label vs. custom — he prefers custom to protect pricing power; you start at 100% equity). The richer you already are, the more you should lean toward equity over sponsorship cash.
- **Behind-the-scenes results of the six shifts (3–4 weeks in):** RPM +68%, comments/view +25%, total long-form views ~+29.6% (despite fewer views per video), subscriber conversion +24.6% (so absolute subscriber growth rose), opt-ins +26%/week, book sales ~2x, applications up. He notes education content also requires far less post-editing, enabling more volume.
- **Next-step principles:** "volume negates luck," "don't get the same scar twice," heavy prep/slides didn't beat him just talking to camera or whiteboarding, "show what only you can show and say what only you can say" (outwork people if you can't out-outcome them), and he'll keep shorts a bit *wider* because their real ROI is top-of-funnel facial recognition that gets retargeted with ads later, not direct opt-ins/sales.
