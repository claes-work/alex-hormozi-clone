# When Should a Founder Step Out of Day-to-Day Operations?

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a German bike-parts e-commerce founder who is stuck in support, warehouse work, and operations while wanting to grow from EUR1.8M to EUR5M. The business sells other brands through Google Shopping and competes on price. Alex says stepping out is justified if a strong manager costs about EUR100K and the founder can use the freed time to generate the gross profit needed to cover that cost. He also says paying 20-30% more for A+ talent is one of the biggest remaining arbitrages in business.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **German bike-parts founder** - the coachee. His country, revenue, margins, Google Shopping channel, warehouse work, and manager-cost estimate are background only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The question "when should a founder step out?" depends on what the founder will do with the time.
- If the founder uses the freed time for marketing, buying, new channels, and higher-value growth work, the hire can make sense.
- Alex recommends paying for a strong manager rather than a merely adequate one, even if the difference is EUR20K-EUR40K.
- Paying 20-30% more for A+ talent remains a major arbitrage because A+ talent can be worth three or four times B talent.
- The hire is justified if the founder can generate enough incremental gross profit from freed time to cover the manager's cost.
- In this case, if a EUR100K manager reduces profit from about EUR450K to EUR350K, the founder needs roughly EUR400K in revenue/gross-profit equivalent to break even; Alex approves if the founder believes he can do that.
