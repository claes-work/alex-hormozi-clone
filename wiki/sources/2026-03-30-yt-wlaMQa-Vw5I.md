# How To Build Revenue Retention in a 1-Time Purchase Business

## Summary
In this short coaching exchange, Alex Hormozi advises a caller who runs a permanent
holiday/accent lighting installation business (aluminum track with LEDs under home
soffits) that has no revenue retention because the product is a one-time purchase. Hormozi
reframes the problem: rather than forcing a recurring subscription, the business should
generate *reoccurring* revenue through seasonal impermanent-lighting campaigns, upgrades,
and maintenance upsells. He argues that simply tracking and demonstrating that a meaningful
share of customers buy again each year makes the business substantially more valuable.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex distinguishes "recurring" revenue (memberships/subscriptions) from
  "reoccurring" revenue (reliable repeat purchases with no subscription), citing Coca-Cola
  as a business with essentially no memberships but reliable repeat purchase.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends a one-time-purchase business run two to three seasonal
  campaigns per year (e.g. Easter, Christmas) to stay top-of-mind and create upsell openings.
- As of March 2026, Alex says maintenance check-ins ("double check the lights are working")
  create natural upsell and upgrade opportunities, including showing prior work done for a
  neighbor as social proof.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims that merely having the data to show that roughly half of
  customers buy something again each year makes the company "very viable" and much more valuable.
- Caller-reported (unverified): average ticket ~$5,000; ~60% margins depending on season;
  CAC ~$300; single location; customers acquired via Facebook/Meta ads; customer base split
  between elderly and "keep up with the Joneses" buyers; caller owns the ~2-year-old business.
