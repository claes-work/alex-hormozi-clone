# Helping a $4M Tattoo Business Scale to $35M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a $4M tattoo business owner who wants $35M and may build a Gym Launch-style education business for tattoo studio owners. Alex separates two paths: a corporate/studio path that is easier to sell but slower, and an education/coaching path that can make more cash sooner but is harder to exit. He warns that artist-dependent tattoo studios are hard to sell and that education needs sticky consumables to retain value after the buyer learns the core tricks.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- An exit goal conflicts with a face-driven education business because education is usually hard to sell.
- A studio/brand path is more sellable, but usually takes longer and involves more personnel and locations.
- A Gym Launch-style education path may generate more annual income, but the terminal exit may be lower or harder.
- Shutting down a business or path can be more painful than starting one, but focus requires killing alternatives.
- Proof from owning current studios is not required for the education model; Alex says Gym Launch had no owned gyms when it scaled.
- Education businesses need consumables or other stickiness because customers may leave once they learn the core material.
