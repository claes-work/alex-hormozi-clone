# You Need to Hire Better Talent

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A home-services owner from Bozeman, Montana (HVAC/technician trade; runs on the ServiceTitan CRM; ~$1.5M revenue to date this year, ~$700k net; just himself, four techs, and his wife) asks how to build a "dream team" — more technicians and salespeople. Alex diagnoses with his **order-of-operations question** ("What breaks when we do more?"), lands on techs as the constraint, then runs his **management diamond** to isolate *why*: people don't act because they don't know they need to, don't know how, don't know when, have something blocking them, or have a motivation problem. The owner is motivated and knowledgeable, so the block is internal — he *chooses* to be involved in every aspect of the business (high-end clients at the Yellowstone Club, ego of letting go). Alex reframes this as **key-man risk — "the number one risk of every business"** — dangerous both because no one will buy a key-man business and because the owner eventually burns himself out. His prescribed fix is a **time study**: log activity every 15 minutes for a week, rank tasks by revenue value/uniqueness, and hand the bottom half to an existing or newly hired person. Because premium customers pay premium prices, the owner has excess margin to buy **premium talent** and must **lower his tolerance for mediocrity**. On recruiting specifically, Alex says the unlock is to be willing to spend money (e.g. ~$50k signing/relocation on a $300k-profit tech), and rather than a recruiting firm, **run national ads plus a generous relocation package** (structured as a signing bonus, e.g. $25k now / $25k at month six). He shuts down the owner's idea of switching techs from W-2 to 1099 — if they show up at set times and do the work your way, they're employees; **"go more legit, not less legit."** Closes with the leverage question: if you got half your time back, could you double the business? — Yes. "That's the game." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex diagnoses growth constraints by asking "What breaks when we do more?" — identify the single bottleneck (here, technicians) before solving anything.
- As of March 2026, his **management diamond**: when someone isn't doing a needed action, it's one of — they don't know they need to, don't know how, don't know when, something is blocking them, or a motivation problem; isolate which before prescribing.
- As of March 2026, **key-man dependency is "the number one risk of every business"** — for two reasons: no one wants to buy a business that depends on the owner, and the owner eventually burns himself out and wants to quit.
- As of March 2026, the owner's real blocker is a choice, not a necessity — Alex presses "have to or choose to?" and "can't or won't?" to separate behaviors from feelings; ego about serving high-end clients keeps him from letting go.
- As of March 2026, the delegation method is a **time study**: set a 15-minute alarm, log what you did each interval for a week, then rank activities by revenue value and uniqueness — delegate the bottom half of the list.
- As of March 2026, the highest-leverage owner activity is almost never manual/hands-on work — more likely the design work or getting and managing the client relationship.
- As of March 2026, **serving premium customers → charging premium prices → excess margins → ability to afford premium people**; the owner should therefore lower his tolerance for mediocrity on the team.
- As of March 2026, recruiting difficulty is largely a willingness-to-spend problem — on a tech worth ~$300k/year in profit, spending ~$50k to acquire a good one is clearly worth it.
- As of March 2026, his recommended recruiting unlock is to **run national ads and offer a generous relocation package** (structured as a signing bonus, e.g. $25k now and $25k at month six) rather than only using a recruiting firm.
- As of March 2026, on W-2 vs 1099: if workers must show up at set times and do work your specific way, they are employees, not vendors — keep them W-2 and "go more legit, not less legit."
- As of March 2026, the payoff logic: freeing half the owner's time by delegating enables doubling the business — "that's the game."
