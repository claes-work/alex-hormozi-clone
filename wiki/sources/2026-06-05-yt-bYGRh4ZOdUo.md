# Scaling a $50M Pest Control Company to $1B

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a third-generation pest-control company doing about $50M revenue and targeting $1B. The owner says the limiting constraint is manpower and retention of average delivery talent. Alex recommends expanding the career ladder from a few large jumps into many small rungs, alternating pay, title, and perks so employees are always close to another win.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Repeated delivery roles need more granular advancement than a few big promotions.
- A three-step career path can often become nine or eighteen rungs by splitting each role into junior/base/senior and plus levels.
- Alternating title bumps, pay bumps, and perks creates more frequent reinforcement without making every step expensive.
- New hires should see the career path on day one, with objective measures tied to advancement.
- The first advancement should be easy enough to reach quickly, because early churn often happens in the first 30-45 days.
- The reinforcement cycle should lengthen gradually as the employee gains momentum.
- Give average performers more ways to win instead of only promoting the obvious A players quickly.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Create career advancement."

> "Always three to six months away from another promotion."

> "Give them more ways to win."
