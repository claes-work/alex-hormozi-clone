# My Biggest Ongoing Challenge

## Summary
In this short interview clip, Alex Hormozi names focus as the challenge he still has to beat every day, even with abundant talent and capital at acquisition.com. He argues that having more resources forces him to be more selective, that commitment means eliminating alternatives (like marriage), and that the "better" opportunity that appears is usually just an early, less-understood one. He also frames the case for sticking with a single skill or path: returns concentrate at the top, so the biggest payoff comes at the end, not from restarting elsewhere. An interviewer poses questions and reacts, and the clip closes with a promo for the acquisition.com scaling road map.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex says focus is still the thing he has to beat every day, calling it extremely hard (self-reported).
- As of March 2026, Alex reports that acquisition.com has many very smart people and substantial capital to pursue opportunities, which makes him more selective about what is worth doing (self-reported).
- As of March 2026, Alex says he is not interested in a $100M-cap idea or even a $1B idea; he wants something with deca-billion-dollar potential minimum before he will say no to everything else (self-reported).
- As of March 2026, Alex frames commitment as marriage: saying yes eliminates the alternatives, and you stick with the original commitment even when someone/something seemingly better appears.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the seemingly-better option is rarely actually better; it just looks better because it is early and you do not yet know its bad parts.
- As of March 2026, Alex says this past year was the first year in his career that he did not feel FOMO, which he counts as a huge accomplishment (self-reported).
- As of March 2026, Alex says that for the first decade-plus of his career, hearing that someone else made money would push him to abandon his path and chase theirs (self-reported).
- As of March 2026, Alex argues you must compare a new path against where your current path will be by then, not against its current level, because you keep improving and incremental gains compound into outsized returns.
- As of March 2026, Alex uses the runner analogy: the gap between first and second place is tiny in time but everything in real-world reward, and the same winner-take-most dynamic applies to skills like marketing.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims moving from top 10% to top 5% may be only a 5% absolute difference but can triple income, because "all the fruit is at the end."
