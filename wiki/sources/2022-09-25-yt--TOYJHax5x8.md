# How I Wrote A #1 Bestseller with $0 and No Publisher (2022-09-25)

## Summary

A solo deep-dive by Alex Hormozi on his book-writing and self-publishing process, framed around the one-year anniversary of *$100M Offers*. He breaks down how he picks a single narrow problem to solve, builds storytelling into every chapter, and grinds through many full drafts before releasing. He also lays out the launch philosophy: make the book so good people share it by word of mouth rather than through paid promotion.

## Key claims (self-publish book-launch method)

- **Word-of-mouth is the launch engine.** A book good enough that readers share it on their own doesn't need advertising or a publisher; if people aren't sharing it, it isn't worth promoting because it isn't good enough. (paraphrase)
- **Solve one narrowly-defined problem completely.** "A good book is a complete solution to a narrowly defined problem." Most books name hundreds of problems and solve none. (verbatim key line)
- **Book series maps to the customer's sequence of problems.** *Offers* solves "what do I sell" (the offer); the next problem is "who do I sell it to" (leads) → *$100M Leads*. (paraphrase)
- **Position the book as the solution someone reaches for at the moment of pain** (e.g. "I wish I had more leads" → "you have to read this book"), not just a generic recommendation. (paraphrase)
- **Structure:** one big problem → 3–6 supporting "buckets" → a personal story showing how he learned each → a framework per chapter. (paraphrase)
- **Storytelling framework:** setting, character, desire, struggle, Eureka moment, victory, resolution; weave the external (what you'd see) with the internal (emotional) journey. (paraphrase)
- **Stakes and struggle make a story interesting** — same Uber Eats story becomes gripping when a diabetic could die without food. (paraphrase)
- **Seven factors of newsworthiness** to make writing/copy more interesting: recency, impact, prominence, proximity, conflict, unusualness, and update-ability. The more you tie in, the more of a story it is. (paraphrase; credited to a textbook from "my buddy Dr. Kashi")
- **Craft rules:** use all five senses; show, don't tell; be selective about details (only include a detail if changing it would change the story).
- **Editing:** translate from "12th grade language to third grade language with more pictures"; the average American reads at fifth-to-seventh grade. Use [hemingwayapp.com](https://hemingwayapp.com) for immediate feedback. Cut any sentence the paragraph doesn't need, prefer shorter sentences/words, eliminate adverbs (an adverb usually means the wrong verb — "I walked quickly" → "I ran"), use simple tenses.
- **Feedback loop:** send draft to 4–8 ideal readers who will tell the truth; diverse feedback = good, uniform feedback = fix it (attributed to Stephen King). Then an editor for grammar/typos = finished book.
- **Model validity:** frameworks must be useful and valid; validity = the number of different situations it still works in. Test in new situations, adjust to incorporate failures, repeat until you can't find where it's wrong.
- **Process order (longest to shortest):** ideating all the pieces is longest, then writing the first draft, then heavy editing. Restart from the beginning whenever the model shows a logical inconsistency.

## Biography facts (dated, self-reported)

- **$100M Offers** crossed its one-year anniversary as of this video (published 2022-09-25), implying an **~September 2021 launch**. (self-reported)
- **Sold over 200,000 copies** with **no publisher, no advertising campaign**, launched **before he had an audience** — "truly off of word of mouth." (self-reported)
- He spends **~20–25 hours a week** on his book, and writes for "four or five hours" at the start of his day rather than the end. (self-reported)
- He spends **roughly 1,000 hours on a book.** (self-reported)
- *$100M Offers* went through **eight full drafts** — about **600 pages of writing** compressed into the **~200-page** version 8. (self-reported)
- **$100M Leads** announced as the sequel, "coming out in the next 12 months, realistically probably six months"; the *Offers* early-bird list "sold out in less than an hour." (self-reported; Leads later published Aug 2023)
- Stated goal of **one book per year.** (self-reported)
- Attended a **charity event at Arnold Schwarzenegger's home** (invited) where he observed scarcity in action — items normally worth ~$10k selling for ~$100k. (self-reported)
- Cites **Stephen King's *On Writing*** as a formative influence ("my hero for writing").
