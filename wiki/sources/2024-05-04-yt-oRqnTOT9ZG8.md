# If I Wanted to Monetize An Audience in 2026, This is What I Would Do

## Summary

Solo talk in which Alex Hormozi lays out a shared "construct" for monetizing an
audience — useful to both the influencer with eyeballs and the business owner who wants
to buy, license, or rent that audience. He frames **four ways to monetize an audience**
(affiliates, sponsorships, partnerships/equity, own brand) sitting on a **continuum of
increasing difficulty, risk, and exit value**, and walks through how compensation, deal
leverage, and time/vesting terms differ across them. The content is essentially the same
talk as [[2025-02-13-yt-9PV0eIXSyjI]] (same 4-ways framework, same examples and quotes).

## Key claims

- Four ways to monetize an audience, on a continuum of rising difficulty, risk, and exit
  value: **(1) Affiliates → (2) Sponsorships → (3) Partnerships (equity) → (4) Start your
  own brand.** Behaviors across the first three are similar; the difference is **how you
  get compensated**. They are **not mutually exclusive** — one deal can blend several.
- **Affiliates:** paid *after* you make a sale. Fastest way to make money immediately,
  low-risk for both parties, no cap on upside, and lets you prove audience fit. Programs
  have predetermined standard terms you sign up for automatically. **Low exit value** — a
  multiple of cash flow discounted by how reliable the underlying business is (US-govt
  affiliate = sound; disappearing arbitrage info product = near-zero value).
- **Sponsorships / endorsements:** business **pays you first** to advertise; you are a
  **traffic source** paid on **CPMs**. Two forms — posting to your own audience, or
  **whitelisting** (business runs your posts as their own ads to audiences beyond yours).
  Harder to land than affiliate deals (more à la carte), governed by the **leverage
  balance** between influencer size and company size. Exit-valuable only if you build a
  **true media company** (army of influencers under umbrella deals ≈ talent agency).
- **Partnerships (equity):** take equity in a business that already has product and
  infrastructure. Minority or majority (0.1%–99%), fully negotiable, often
  **performance-based** (e.g., 1% per 1,000 customers, capped at ~10%). The brand
  association is **permanent**, so **always get something upfront**. Deals commonly add a
  **time/vesting** component (e.g., 25% upfront, 25% performance, 50% vesting over ~4
  years, forfeited on breakup). Define deliverables up front via the W's
  (who/what/where/when/why/how): posting cadence, links in bio, long-form mentions,
  short-form posts/stories, monthly whitelisting ads.
- **Start your own brand:** highest risk — you begin with 100% equity. Two routes:
  **white label** (put your logo on an existing manufacturer's product; faster, cheaper)
  or **custom formulations** (custom product/materials/cuts no one else has; costs more).
  Hormozi personally prefers **custom** so no one can sell an identical product and hold
  pricing power over him.
- **Deal-structuring rule:** the more money you already have, the more you should lean
  toward **equity** over sponsorship cash (cash is taken "at the expense of equity"); more
  risk demands a disproportionate reward. Navigate any deal by balancing **exit value,
  cash in/out, amount of work, and risk exposure**.
- **Self-reported track record** (opening): Prestige Labs built 5,000+ affiliates
  generating $20M+/year; ALAN software 1,000+ affiliates (agencies white-labeling it);
  the *$100M Leads* book launch used 30,000 affiliates.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "Most people don't know how to, quote, monetize an audience."
- "Affiliates, which is where you are getting paid after you make sales for a business.
  Sponsorships, where they pay you first to advertise on behalf of the business."
- "With sponsorships you get paid first, and so that means that you are a traffic source
  for the business."
- "The business pays money to advertise your brand, so they make you bigger... but at the
  same time they get higher conversion rate because you're probably better on camera,
  you're better spoken."
- "If I'm an affiliate of the US government... and I recruit people for the military, then
  I probably can say that my business model is sound and the US government is not going to
  go away."
- "The association that you make with their brand is permanent. As soon as you make that
  public declaration you transfer all of that goodwill, you transfer that association to
  them."
- "You don't want to just be like all of a sudden some shill — that's not good. You want
  to tastefully be able to integrate that promotion into the content that you have."
- "By taking on more risk you want to make sure that you're getting a disproportionate
  reward in the form of stock, shares, options, equity and things like that."
- "If you're going to take the risk on of starting your own brand, I might as well get all
  the benefits of doing it."
