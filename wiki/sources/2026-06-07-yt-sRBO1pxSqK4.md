# Stop Building 10 Businesses. Build One to $10M.

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a two-person business that builds offers, marketing, sales, and operations for established entrepreneurs who want to teach their expertise. The founders repeatedly build clients from scratch, including million-dollar launches, but remain capacity-constrained and under-leveraged. Alex tells them to stop repeatedly building zero-to-one businesses for small percentages. Instead, pick the highest-X-factor partner or build their own business, negotiate from the leverage they already have, and choose terms they would be happy with rather than resent.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- It is usually easier to grow one strong business from $1M to $10M than to build ten separate zero-to-one businesses.
- If a service provider truly runs the client's business engine, they have leverage: stopping work reveals the real dependency.
- Negotiation terms should be backed into from what the provider would be happy with, not what they would begrudgingly accept.
- When a former client returns after rejecting terms, preserve the person's ego so the relationship can restart profitably.
- Risk matters in partnership splits; the person signing checks or holding liability deserves compensation for that risk.
- A compelling repositioning can simply tell the truth: the founders built many businesses for others, then chose to build for themselves.
