# How to Get Ahead of 99% of People

## Summary

A long whiteboard keynote on why only 1 in 250 businesses ever passes $10M/year, split into
two levers: the **entrepreneur** (skills + beliefs; character traits are just bundles of
trainable skills) and the **opportunity vehicle / leverage** (getting more out for what you
put in). Alex walks his own trajectory up the "leverage ladder" — employee → self-employed
→ gyms → turnarounds → licensing → capital/acquisition.com — mapped onto Naval's four
leverage types (his four C's: Collaboration, Capital, Code, Content), and closes on
"ignorance debt," getting paid to learn, the side-hustle transition, and infinite vs.
finite games.

## Key claims

- **Two things make a $10M+ business:** the best entrepreneur (skills + non-limiting
  beliefs) in the best vehicle (leverage). "Character traits" collapse into skills —
  patience is just many trainable micro-skills; "hard vs. soft skills" only means easy vs.
  hard to measure.
- **Unknown unknowns limit us most.** The vehicle you don't know exists is the most
  expensive gap. His own turning point: a mentor told him "you have a level 10 skill set
  and a level 2 opportunity — I don't think you should be running gyms," opening
  licensing/B2B he "didn't know was possible."
- **Four types of leverage (renamed from Naval as four C's):** Collaboration (people),
  Capital (other people's money), Code, Content. You can max just one to 1000x; his ladder:
  self-employment → collaboration (gyms/turnarounds, six figs/mo) → content (licensing,
  seven figs/mo) → + capital (acquisition.com, eight figs/mo); adding time (or code) →
  nine figs/mo.
- **"It's not about how hard you row, it's about what boat you're in"** (Buffett's smarter,
  harder-working classmate who went into steel and did merely "okay"). Leverage = speed:
  people who "move faster" just get more per input.
- **Ignorance debt:** the woman paying "$950,000 a year not knowing how to make a million
  dollars." Education can't be taxed, taken, or sued away (ties to his father fleeing Iran
  with $1,000 and rebuilding on skills) — invest in skills over the S&P; a $1,000 sales
  course that takes you from $40k → $220k/yr beats an S&P return "every f***ing time."
- **Get paid to learn, then transition:** be willing to go from earning to learning (he
  went from top-secret-clearance DC consultant to a $14/hr trainer). Then live below your
  means, stack cash, run a side hustle until it matches your income, sustain 6 months, then
  go full-time. Credibility gate on content: document → demonstrate → duplicate; don't
  educate on what you haven't done.
- **Infinite vs. finite games:** business, health, marriage are infinite games — "the day
  you start playing is the day you win and the day you stop playing is the day you lose."
  Separate identity from the business; stay in the same game longest to compound reps.
