# In-Person vs. Remote Work | Dave Ramsey

## Summary
A conversation between Alex Hormozi and **Dave Ramsey** (recorded at Ramsey Solutions' Tennessee headquarters) on why both run fully in-person companies. **Most of the framework talk is Dave's, and is context only.** Dave explains his 100%-in-person policy: everyone relocates to Tennessee, because in-person raises the quality of communication ("85% of communication is body language and tone… not words"), which builds trust and productivity — "organizations move at the speed of trust." He teaches "management by walking around" (crediting the 1982 book *In Search of Excellence*) — walking the floor to feel the energy, and "drive-by communications" (a shoulder-touch to an employee whose mother has cancer) that "does not occur if they're remote." He describes subsidizing the company cafeteria by several million dollars a year so people "break bread" together, learn from each other, and build trust; he concedes a purely tactical task "is fine for remote" but says Ramsey wants "more out of the interaction than the simple task," and has "lost some good people" over the policy. **Alex's own contribution** is narrower and more nuanced: he cites a research study (he attributes it to Uber or Airbnb) that found an "it depends" answer, but argues that the first third of a career (~18–35) gets a *disproportionate* upside from being in person because of the **unstructured, osmotic learning** that happens off your formal career path — e.g. a salesperson seated near marketing overhears organic/paid, funnels, and conversion rates, and later sounds knowledgeable on a sales call. In a Zoom-only environment "you lose out on all of that." He notes his own company (Acquisition.com) is in-person with a big Las Vegas headquarters, cites research that in-person workers get more promotion chances, and directly encourages his largely early-20s audience to work in person. Ends with his $100M scaling-roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex advocates in-person work **especially for the first third of a career (~18–35)**, because those workers get a disproportionate upside from unstructured learning that isn't on their formal career path.
- As of March 2026, Alex's mechanism: proximity creates osmotic learning — a salesperson seated near marketing overhears organic/paid strategy, funnels, landing pages, and conversion rates, and later draws on it in sales calls; in a Zoom-only environment that is lost.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites a research study (he attributes it to Uber or Airbnb, unsure) that concluded in-person vs. remote is an "it depends" question.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites research that in-person workers get more chances at promotion than remote workers who only join calls "commensurate to the task assigned."
- As of March 2026, Alex's own company (Acquisition.com) is in-person with a large Las Vegas headquarters, and he directly encourages his (mostly early-20s) audience to work in person early in their careers.
- (Context — **Dave Ramsey**, not Alex) As of March 2026, Ramsey Solutions is 100% in-person; everyone relocates to Tennessee, on the reasoning that in-person communication quality (body language, tone) builds trust and productivity, and "organizations move at the speed of trust."
- (Context — **Dave Ramsey**) He practices "management by walking around" (crediting *In Search of Excellence*, 1982) and "drive-by communications," and subsidizes the company cafeteria by several million dollars a year so employees eat and bond together; he grants that a purely tactical task "is fine for remote."

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Especially for the first third of someone's career — so basically, like, call it 18 to 35 — is where those people get a disproportionate upside for being in person, because of all of the unstructured learning that occurs that's not on your quote career path."

> "If you're sitting right next to marketing and you're in sales, you overhear what's going on in terms of the organic stuff, you overhear what's going on on the paid side. You start learning a little bit more about funnel, about landing page and conversion rates… And then all of a sudden, when you are on a sales call with a business owner or whomever, and they bring something of that up, you're like, 'Oh, I actually know something about that.'"

> "But if you were in an only Zoom environment, you lose out on all of that."

> "Because I obviously have a big percentage of the audience that I have that's in their early 20s, beginning their career, I would encourage you all to work in person. I really mean that."

> "I did read one piece of research that you get more chances at promotion in person than if you're sitting at home and you're only on the calls commensurate to the task assigned to you."
