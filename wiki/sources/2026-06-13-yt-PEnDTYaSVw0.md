# AI Ads Agency Charging $25K Per Commercial

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the founder of an AI commercial studio that has generated about $250K in revenue and charges roughly $25K per ad. Alex tells him not to sell "AI" as the product; customers buy more customers, not the technology. He suggests repositioning toward high-volume ad creative for brands already spending heavily on ads, where the AI advantage is lower production cost and faster creative output rather than novelty.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- "AI" should not be the main thing sold; the buyer cares about whether the ad gets customers.
- If a company can sell a $500K commercial and produce it for $18K, Alex says it should sell the $500K outcome rather than discounting around the production method.
- The likely stronger wedge is not one expensive bespoke commercial, but becoming the creative engine for advertisers who need large creative volume.
- Good target customers are brands already spending at least about $100K/month or $1M/year on ads with a proven funnel.
- Advertisers need many creative variations because neither the agency nor the client knows in advance which creative will work.
- The agency-style value proposition is operational relief: remove the burden of managing many creatives, agencies, freelancers, and performance testing.
- Alex frames media buying as the easier part compared with creative throughput.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

> "Don't sell AI. I don't want AI. I want customers."

> "AI is how I do the magic, but it's not what I sell."

> "I need 100 pieces of creative cuz you don't know and I don't know which one's going to work."

> "Aren't you tired of managing creatives?"

> "The media buying is the easiest part."
