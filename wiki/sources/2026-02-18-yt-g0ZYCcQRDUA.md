## Summary

A short, reflective solo clip (@MoreMozi) in which Alex answers the question "What is your
experience of loneliness?" He candidly says he enjoys being alone and does not get very
lonely. He distinguishes *loneliness* from *isolation*: isolation is the feeling that "no
one else is dealing with this," which he frames as a species of shame — it "only exists in
the dark" and dissolves once you speak the problem aloud (marriage, weight, business),
because millions of others face the same thing. He quotes a line from his mother — "the
news is always the same, it's just the names change" — and generalizes it: headlines
(bank defaults, rate hikes, housing crises, even crimes) recur across time; only the
people involved change. He notes that intellectually knowing you're not alone is one thing,
but actually *meeting* someone going through the exact same struggle is what makes people
feel less isolated. He reiterates that, for him personally, solitude is one of his most
valued states. The clip closes with a standard promo for his free acquisition.com "0 to
$100M+" 10-stage scaling roadmap.

## Key claims

_All dated 2026-02-18, paraphrased unless quoted below._

- He personally likes being alone and does not get very lonely.
- He separates loneliness from isolation: isolation is the sense that no one else is
  dealing with what you're dealing with.
- He frames isolation as a form of shame that "only exists in the dark" — speaking a
  problem out loud dissolves it, because countless others share the same struggle
  (in marriage, weight, or business).
- He credits his mother with the maxim that the news is always the same and only the
  names change — the same headlines (bank defaults, rising interest rates, housing
  crises, even crimes) recur over time; only the people involved change. Humans doing
  human things.
- Knowing intellectually that others share your problem is weaker than meeting someone
  going through the exact same thing; the personal encounter is what actually reduces
  the feeling of isolation.
- Spending time alone is, for him, one of his most valued/enjoyable states.
- (Promo) He offers a free 10-stage roadmap from $0 to $100M+ that fewer than 1% of
  companies finish, broken down across eight business functions — what each constraint
  feels like (its symptoms) and the steps his teams took to graduate through it, applied
  across software, physical products, service businesses, and brick-and-mortar
  (acquisition.com/roadmap).
