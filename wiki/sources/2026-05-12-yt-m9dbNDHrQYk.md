# How Do I Build a Marketing System From Scratch When I Have Never Done It

## Summary

A garage-door business owner with a prior auto-body background asks how to build a
marketing system from scratch while reducing reliance on home-warranty lead channels.
Alex treats customer acquisition as a core business function, alongside delivery and
operations. He recommends using PPC agencies first, building local social-media ad
creative in-house, and using agencies as temporary teachers when entering a new channel.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex says every business needs customer acquisition, delivery, and operations as core
  legs of the stool.
- He says generating leads is a core function, not an optional side project.
- He likes that the caller has multiple warranty lead sources and suggests expanding
  that source count where possible.
- He says local PPC can be harder to crack at first but stable once working.
- He recommends starting with a vendor for local PPC because good agencies can run a
  repeatable local playbook.
- He says local Meta/social ads likely require an in-house person because creative in a
  local market can fatigue in two to three weeks.
- He recommends finding a younger, tech-savvy local paid-ads operator and giving that
  person an exciting compensation path.
- He says SEO is more skill-dependent than PPC, though local SEO is easier than national
  SEO.
- He describes his channel-learning playbook as hiring an agency first, telling them he
  will take over in about six months, and having them help train the internal person.
