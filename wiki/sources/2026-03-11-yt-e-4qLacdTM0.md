# What I'm Focused on Right Now

## Summary
Solo talk in which Alex names the two skills he is personally working on as of March 2026, both tied to running a company that keeps scaling. **First: reinforcing cultural norms across a scaling organization.** He defines culture as "the rules that govern reinforcement in an organization" — both spoken and unspoken, where doing X earns "a pat on the butt" and doing Y earns a "ding." The hard part is that culture is a thousand unspoken rules, which is why newcomers take time to learn it, and it drifts because reinforcers change with the people managing them — so a sales team can have a different culture than marketing, finance, or HR. His current focus is to **standardize the rules that govern reinforcement across the org** and get everyone aligned to that style of work. **Second: the highest level of recruiting and talent.** He frames talent (like focus) as a skill you never master — you just discover more levels; the mist lifts and you see the next peak. As you scale, the mediocrity you tolerated at one level becomes intolerable at the next, so you raise the bar on who you bring in and on the process of getting them to buy into your culture, vision, and opportunity. Underpinning this is his belief that **"the best talent is always yet to come"** — predicated on the idea that if *he* gets better, the opportunity gets better, which attracts more and better people. The through-line: reinvestment in learning skills never ends (he cites Charlie Munger on being a lifelong student). He closes with a mindset contrast drawn from a recent one-on-one: an employee asked whether the org could eventually build a role that only used his one favored skill; Alex told him he'd instead ask "how could I learn 10 more skills?" and let his skills meet the demands of the existing business rather than asking the business to meet him — "asking reality to meet you where you're at rather than meeting reality where it is." Expecting the world to be more convenient for you is, he says, a "poverty lens" rooted in entitlement; the wealthy accept the world as it is and choose to maximize and adapt, while everyone else stomps their feet about what's unfair (and they may be right — "and so what?"). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (the $100M Scaling Roadmap quiz across the 10 stages and 8 business functions).
