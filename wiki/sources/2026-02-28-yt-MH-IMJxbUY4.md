# Building a $12,000,000 Business for a Stranger in 25 Minutes

## Summary

Live coaching build in which Alex diagnoses Joel McDonald's "travel hedging" coaching/course business (a book-funnel operation, ~$6.4M trailing-12-month revenue, ~30% margin, heavily dependent on Meta ads). Alex frames it as a demand-constrained business and focuses on two levers: lowering CAC through better creative (a decentralized UGC content loop plus AI/kaleidoscope variation) and improving sales conversion through lead scoring, a parallel dialer, more reps, and front-loaded damaging admissions. Joel's situation is context; only Alex's advice below is persona data.

## Key claims (Alex's advice/frameworks only)

- **First diagnostic question: supply-constrained or demand-constrained?** For a demand-constrained business, the fixes are: new channel, lower CAC (via CRO, better creative, or offer), or simply spend more.
- When someone hits a wall every time they spend more, the unlock is usually **better creative**, not more budget.
- **UGC loop** is the highest-leverage move for a visual product: incentivize customers to post reels/montages of their trips in exchange for a gated checklist or training, then run the winners as ads — a "decentralized content machine" producing 20-30 community videos a week.
- **Kaleidoscope** the winning creative: once you find a winner, generate many variations (AI filters, black-and-white, sepia, cartoon, Ghibli, 3-second video from a still, re-films/remakes) to extract many angles from one proven asset.
- **Good video beats images, but images beat bad video.** Statics "work better" for many businesses only because those businesses are bad at video. Same logic applies to content generally: good content works, bad doesn't.
- Hire "one hyper-caffeinated 20-year-old" native to the platforms to run short-form — they instantly spot a bad hook, missing trending audio, or over-professional (vs. iPhone-selfie-style) videos.
- **Content-to-ad double dip**: post organically, find winners, add a 5-second CTA, then run the same post as a paid ad.
- **Copy vs. creative cadence**: winning copy/messaging rarely needs changing (cites hooks run 3-6 months, and one company with only three winning hooks after 5 years at hundreds of thousands/day); creative changes constantly. When you find the winner, just run it.
- **One great ad can convert an entire market** (Old Spice "man on a horse" example): if everyone could buy your product, there exists one ad good enough to convert everyone. Meta serves the most-interested first; CPA rises as you push into adjacent, less-interested pools, and only better creative breaks through to the bigger market.
- **Avatar diversity in creative**: the algorithm shows ads to lookalikes of the people in them ("likeness is one of the strongest predictors of conversion"); varying the avatars in ads lets you reach and saturate new customer segments with existing creative quality. AI avatars are "just about good enough to be used."
- **Lead scoring for outbound**: score by the two qualifying factors (credit-card spend and annual vacation spend), weave both into the opt-in, and have the dialer call two-for-two prospects first, one-for-two second, zeros last.
- **Parallel/power dialer** at scale: dials ~10 numbers at once per rep, auto-connects on ~5% pickup, maximizes talk time; the bigger the team, the better it works. Suggests ~6 reps with a KPI around $300/day each.
- **Front-load damaging admissions** on the sales call: state everything that sucks about the offer before pitching the good, so prospects self-qualify ("you can have what you want, just not when you want"). Offering a too-good-to-be-true claim as less-good makes it more believable.
