# Stop Selling From Your Own Wallet

## Summary

Alex Hormozi warns founders not to price based on their own buying power or the opinions of broke friends and family. He argues that many customers have far more money than the founder imagines, and that low prices can reduce credibility for high-value promises. His practical rule is to make upsells 5x to 10x the core price, expect far more no's, and optimize for absolute profit rather than the highest close rate.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Founders should imagine that prospects are richer than they are, rather than pricing from their own wallet.
- A price can be too low to be credible for the promise being made.
- Advice from people without money is often poor input for selling to people with money.
- Upsells should be 5x to 10x the core price and valuable enough that the seller is happy to deliver them.
- If the delivery feels like too much work, adjust value or price; Alex prefers charging more when the value is real.
- The sweet spot is not the most yeses; it is the most money.
- A high anchor can help the rest of the offer stack look more attractive.
- Absolute profit matters more than relative margin; one high-ticket sale can equal hundreds of low-ticket sales.
