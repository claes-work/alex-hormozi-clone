# The Mental Trick That Killed My FOMO

## Summary

Alex says people keep finding new problems as they level up because deprivation moves to
the next discrepancy. His FOMO-killing frame is to fast-forward the present: even if he
took the opportunity, made the bet, or pursued the alternate path, he would probably be
about as happy or unhappy as he is now. That reduces attention spent on paths not taken.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Changing goals shifts deprivation to a new discrepancy.
- Solving money pain does not create a pain-free life; it creates different problems.
- Alex says his subjective well-being is not materially different from two years ago or
  two years before that.
- To reduce FOMO, he fast-forwards the present and assumes he would be roughly as happy
  or unhappy on the alternate path.
- This frame reduces attention allocated to paths not taken.
- Regret about missed opportunities is often unproductive because the emotional state
  would likely normalize anyway.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You just get different problems as you level up."

> "Fast forward the present."

> "You'll probably be about as happy or unhappy as you are right now."
