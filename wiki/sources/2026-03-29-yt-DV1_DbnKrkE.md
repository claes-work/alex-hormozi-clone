# Helping a Dental Practice Create Recurring Revenue

## Summary
In a short coaching exchange, a dental-clinic owner tells Alex Hormozi that the practice's core offer is single-visit full-mouth work (e.g. veneers), a one-time transaction that leaves no lifetime value afterward. Hormozi advises converting the one-off into recurring revenue by selling a paid maintenance/"insurance" program priced at 5-10% of the original purchase, framing it as protecting the investment the customer just made. He stresses selling the maintenance plan immediately after the big-ticket sale, while the customer is still emotionally attached to the result.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends that businesses selling expensive one-time purchases add a recurring "insurance"/maintenance program priced at roughly 5-10% of the total purchase price to generate ongoing revenue and extend customer lifetime value.
- As of March 2026, he advises timing the maintenance-plan pitch for immediately *after* the main sale closes (not before or during), when the customer is looking at the fresh result and is most motivated to protect it and least price-sensitive.
- He frames the maintenance offer around protecting/extending ROI — e.g. claiming maintenance can "double how long these teeth are going to last."
- He cites a fur-coat dealer (his early job, a fourth-generation family business) that invented "summer storage" as the historical example of turning a one-time luxury buyer into a repeat customer.
- As of March 2026, he claims 90% of luxury buyers are "actually poor people who want to look rich" — i.e. that luxury purchases are largely aspirational rather than made by the wealthy. (Unverified generalization; asserted without a cited source.)
- Caller claim (unverified): the clinic's unique offer is single-visit full-mouth dentistry, and after that one transaction customer LTV ends.
