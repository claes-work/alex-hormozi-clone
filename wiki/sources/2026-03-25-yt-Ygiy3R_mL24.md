# Helping a Real Estate Broker Get to $50M

## Summary
In this live-audience coaching exchange, Alex Hormozi diagnoses an apartment broker who does roughly $250,000 in revenue (self-reported) and wants to grow to $50 million but lacks clarity on the single highest-leverage action. Hormozi walks the broker through the bottleneck (demand / lead generation via cold calling), the time-vs-management trade, and the "swamp" ($1–3M) where an owner must bet their whole profit on a great hire. He argues the broker's real block is that he doesn't believe in his own recruiting pitch, and coaches him to poach a proven "stud" rather than train burnout-prone juniors, and to audit every activity by dollars-per-hour.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the core growth bottleneck for the broker as demand — getting more people to hire him to sell their buildings — and notes his only acquisition channel is cold calling.
- As of March 2026, Alex describes the "trade 40 for two": trade 40 hours of doing (sales) for two hours of managing a hire, and keep repeating that trade until you are all management and no doing, then trade your management away too.
- As of March 2026, Alex calls the $1–3M revenue band "the swamp" and argues it is the monetarily hardest stage because an owner's two options both suck: work more (or on things that matter more), or bet essentially all of the business's profit on one great hire.
- As of March 2026, Alex illustrates the swamp with rough math: a business at ~$2M at 25% margins keeps ~$500K, split between the owner's income and the business's profit, so hiring a game-changing person means betting nearly 100% of that profit on them being good.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises hiring one proven "stud" you feel you must have — including by poaching — rather than the industry-standard model of bringing in junior agents, ~90% of whom (per the broker) burn out within a year.
- As of March 2026, Alex tells the broker his block is belief: he can't recruit agents because he doesn't ethically feel good about the offer, and must first "sell himself" on why joining him beats going solo (learning from him, existing lead flow, brand, and pre-built systems/back-end).
- As of March 2026, Alex's activity-audit rule: for everything you spend time on, look at how much you make from it and how many hours it takes, then peel off the two-to-four hours a day spent on things that don't grow the business.
- The broker states (unverified caller claims) that he did $2M in revenue in 2022 with little change since, that he previously hired someone and lost money ("negative CAC"), and that the market was "a lot better" in 2022.
- Alex plugs the free $100M Scaling Roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap — a quiz built from ~200 hours reviewing portfolio companies, breaking growth into 10 stages across product, marketing, sales, customer success, recruiting, IT, HR, and finance.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You trade 40 for two. 40 hours of sales for two hours of management. You keep making that trade over and over again till you're in 40 hours of management and no doing. Then you say, okay, can I get somebody else and trade my 40 hours of management to somebody else's two hours? You just keep making that trade."

> "Right now, you're in what we call the swamp, which is 1 to 3 million."

> "You have two options and they both suck. One is that you just somehow work more than you currently are, or work on things that matter more, or you find somebody else who can help you and you basically bet all your profit on it. That's why it's so tough."

> "You're doing 250, which sounds like you've got stuff in between your ears more than something in the market."

> "Instead of hiring people, hire somebody who's a stud that you feel like you have to get them."

> "I think you need to sell you on this. Like you can't sell them because you don't believe it's a good idea."

> "The doingness — sometimes you have to do twice the effort to get the same outcome."

> "Basically everything that you're spending your time on, you should just look at how much am I making from this activity, and then how many hours would it take me to get this thing, and you just peel off the two or three or four hours a day that you're spending doing something that's not the thing that's going to grow the business."
