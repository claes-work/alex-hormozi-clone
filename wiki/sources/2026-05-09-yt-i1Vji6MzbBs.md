# If It Won't Work in 20 Years, End It Today

## Summary

Alex advises someone stuck in a podcast partnership to have a direct conversation about
why the partnership should continue or end. His core rule is that if the partnership is
not likely to work over the next 20 years, it is better to end it now and start moving.
He says partnerships can end gracefully if both people explain the different directions
and direct the audience to follow both.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says the partner should have a direct conversation about why the partnership may
  need to end.
- He says the issue could be work imbalance, money imbalance, or another unresolved
  problem.
- He says if the partnership will not work over the next 20 years, one may as well end
  it today.
- He says ending a partnership does not have to be combative.
- He recommends a graceful public frame: the partners had a good journey and are now
  moving in different directions.
- He says both audiences can be directed to follow both former partners.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you don't think the partnership's going to work out over the next 20 years, it's like you might as well end it today."

> "It doesn't have to be this combative thing."
