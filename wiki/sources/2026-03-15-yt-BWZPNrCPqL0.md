# Helping a Paid Ads Agency Build a Leadership Team

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client runs a paid-ads and growth advisory selling to eight- and nine-figure e-commerce brands (~$3.5M, wants to reach $10M then $20M). His constraint is talent, and he has just stood up a leadership team over the past 12 months — two team leaders, a head of growth and a head of strategy — all on salary, and wants an incentive plan to carry the company as it 2–3x's headcount. Alex's core answer is a **profit-share pool**: allocate a fixed 10–20% of profit to a leader pool, from which each leader takes "slices of that slice," so the owner's ~80% is protected and the plan self-caps. This makes leaders "think like owners" — every new leader they bring in gives up some pie but should grow the pie. He then delivers his **equity philosophy**: if you might never sell, don't give real shares away, because the only thing guaranteed ten years out is that *you'll* still be in the business. Equity provides four things — **cash flow, sale bonus, risk, control** — and he uses that grid to negotiate: most people don't want risk and he won't give control, so what remains is cash flow (profit share off the bottom line) plus a **sale bonus** in the event of an exit. He notes a US sale bonus is taxed as income rather than capital gains, but the alternatives (them buying in, or being gifted equity with tax and risk, plus agreeing a valuation) are a "mess." His summary line: "I'll give you some money today, and if we sell, you get money tomorrow, and if you leave, you get neither." For apportionment he advises leaving ~2/3 of the pool for **future** talent ("the best talent is in the future, not right now"), making the sale bonus proportional to the profit share (1% of profit → 1% of sale), and — from personal experience — writing it as a percentage of *cash received*, not of total sale value. On the head of growth (their first non-founder salesperson, also backfilling growth/marketing), Alex warns that **giving equity to sales people is a day-one mistake** — the salesman already participates via commission — and advises gating the leadership table behind a quantifiable, earned milestone (e.g. mastering demand gen that produces a set number of deals). Ends with the standard roadmap promo (ignored for claims).

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends a leadership incentive plan built as a **profit-share pool**: allocate a fixed 10–20% of profit to all leaders as a group; each leader gets a slice of that pool, so the plan self-caps and the owner always retains ~80%.
- As of March 2026, a capped shared pool makes leaders "think like owners" — bringing in a new leader means giving up some pie, but they should grow the pie, mirroring how the owner reasons when adding people.
- As of March 2026, if you may never sell the business, do not give real shares away: "the only thing guaranteed 10 years from now is that you will still be in the business" — everything else is uncertain.
- As of March 2026, equity provides four things — cash flow, sale bonus (sale dollars), risk, and control — and Alex negotiates by elimination: the person usually wants no risk, the owner won't give control, so what's left to offer is cash flow (profit share) plus a sale bonus.
- As of March 2026, a US sale bonus is taxed as income rather than capital gains, but the alternatives are worse/messier — the person taking on risk without control and paying tax on a gift, or buying in (requiring money and an agreed valuation).
- As of March 2026, Alex's summary structure: "some money today, money tomorrow if we sell, and neither if you leave" — the interest reverts to the owner on departure.
- As of March 2026 (apportionment): leave ~2/3 of the leader pool for **future** talent if you want to grow big, "because the best talent is in the future, not right now" — don't blow the whole slice on current people.
- As of March 2026, make the sale bonus **proportional** to the profit share: a leader with 1% of profit (10% of a 10% pool) gets 1% of the sale.
- As of March 2026, write the sale bonus as a **percentage of cash received**, not calculated on the total value of the sale — Alex says he was burned on exactly this before.
- As of March 2026, giving equity to sales people is "one of those day-one mistakes" to avoid, because a salesperson already participates through commission.
- As of March 2026, gate the leadership table behind a quantifiable, earned milestone — tell an ambitious salesperson "I don't think you're ready yet; this is what I'd need to see," e.g. mastering demand gen that produces a defined number of deals, so that "if he actually did this, you'd be like you earned it."
- As of March 2026, if selling, the profits interest / sale bonus can be included in the sale proportional to each leader's interest, and reverts to the owner if the leader leaves.
