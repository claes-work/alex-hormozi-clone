# The 5-a-Day Rule for Being Present

## Summary

This excerpt develops a practical presence frame: rather than expecting continuous
mindfulness, aim for five brief moments per day of being where one's feet are. Alex
connects presence to noticing details, such as a sip of coffee, peripheral vision, or
the corner of a room during a hard moment. He says a single vivid detail can make a
moment memorable even inside difficulty.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Presence can be practiced through small moments rather than an idealized permanent
  meditative state.
- One goal is to capture five moments a day of being where one's feet are.
- Seeing peripheral vision can cue present-moment awareness.
- A hard macro situation can contain a small beautiful detail that makes the person
  present.
- If life feels too fast, Alex says to look for a detail.
- A single physical sensation can crowd out other thoughts because attention can hold
  only one thought at a time.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Be where your feet are."

> "Just look for a detail."

> "You can only have one thought at one time."
