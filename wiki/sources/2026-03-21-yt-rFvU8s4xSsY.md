# Helping an Online Service Business Get to $5M/Year

## Summary
In a live coaching exchange, Alex Hormozi advises the owners of an online golf-coaching business (two coaches, ~10 employees, ~$1.4M revenue, down from a 2021–22 peak of $2.6M) who want to reach $5M in three years. The business has two arms: a membership/subscription community they are passionate about but struggle to grow, and a media/brand-sponsorship arm that "holds the business" with near-100% margins. Hormozi presses them to grow the media arm rather than the shrinking membership, arguing media is more scalable, more sellable, and uniquely efficient because the way you advertise is also the way you deliver.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the three things that most determine a business's value/sellability are strong gross margins, fast growth, and revenue stickiness; if all three hold, the model is very strong even accounting for key-man risk.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims media/ad-space buyers are stickier customers than consumer golf-community subscribers, and that media margins are "virtually 100%" (roughly a wash with the community's margins, so the differentiator becomes growth rate).
- As of March 2026, Alex describes media as uniquely efficient because the method of advertising is also the method of delivery: you get attention, sell the attention, get more attention, and the flywheel compounds.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises picking the growing, high-margin, more-scalable arm (media) over the passion arm (membership) that is shrinking and harder to scale, framing it as the classic outsider-perspective correction.
- As of March 2026, Alex notes far more $50M media businesses exist than $50M golf-coaching businesses, and calls golf an excellent niche because golfers are disproportionately high spenders (one of the few categories men reliably spend on, alongside cars, watches, shooting, prepper gear).
- As of March 2026, Alex frames outside eyes / portfolio-company coaching via a Yiddish saying about being unable to smell your own bad breath — smart CEOs still miss what an outsider sees immediately.
- Caller claims (unverified): the business does ~$1.4M revenue with ~10 employees, peaked at ~$2.6M in 2021–22, lost subscription revenue while brand revenue rose after over-scaling the team post-COVID, and believes the membership is the "best product" and the path to $50M.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "One of the beautiful things that I love the most about media business is very unique to media, which is that the way that you advertise is also the way you deliver. So, you just have to do one thing, which is get attention. And then you sell the attention, then you just get more attention, then people find out about your business cuz you got attention, and then you get more attention."

> "You're like, I've got this thing that I'm not trying to grow that's growing that's super valuable and it's high gross margin than the other thing that's really hard that I haven't done really well, and it's shrinking. How do I grow that one?"

> "Golf is an awesome niche. Huge spenders. It's one of the few things men spend their money on. Cars, watches ... And so you are one of the outlets that men spend money on in consuming a big way."

> "The margins on media are virtually 100%."
