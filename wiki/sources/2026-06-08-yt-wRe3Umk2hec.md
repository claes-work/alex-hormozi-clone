# Why Most Med Spa Owners Stall at $7M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a med-spa / wellness business doing about $6.7M across three Austin-area locations with roughly 15% profit. The owner wants to reach $20M but is unsure which lever to pull. Alex argues that the business has likely over-expanded relative to its talent and margins. He recommends fixing the existing locations first: improve margins, build a membership sales motion, increase utilization, develop a talent-retention model for injectors, and only then make the next location a repeatable model.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The business exists to satisfy customers, not the owner's entrepreneurial urges.
- Multi-location businesses often lose profit when founders split attention before backfilling talent.
- Opening more locations before current units reach strong margins can create more liability without much more owner income.
- A three-location med-spa at 15% profit should likely fix the current units before opening another location.
- Membership sales motions can increase revenue retention, utilization, scheduling efficiency, and revenue per square foot.
- For med spas, injector retention is a major enterprise-value risk because customers can attach to the provider rather than the brand.
- Talent career paths, pay, and model design must make providers loyal to the brand rather than only to themselves.
- Paid customer acquisition can beat local organic dependence in markets where many competitors rely mostly on reputation and SEO.
