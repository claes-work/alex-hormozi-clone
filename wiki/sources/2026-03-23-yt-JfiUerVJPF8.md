# Helping a $25M/Year Private Equity Firm Scale

## Summary
In this coaching-style clip, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a family-office-funded
private equity firm (self-reported ~$25M/year across the group, aiming for over $1B
enterprise value) who wants to build "institutional-quality" deal-making capability.
Alex reframes the problem as the universal PE trap of raising more money to do the same
number of deals, and argues the real lever is talent: identify and extract the one or
two winners inside acquired companies, pull them into the HoldCo, overpay for elite
operators, and treat recruiting as a primary value driver. The caller's firm details
are self-reported and unverified.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames most private equity firms as falling into a trap: they
  raise more and more capital but only do "fewer bigger deals" limited by their
  bandwidth, rather than truly scaling deal volume.
- As of March 2026, Alex reports that acquisition.com did roughly 24 deals in 24 months
  (roughly 2021 to end of 2023), found many weren't worth the bandwidth, and that he
  eventually gave some portfolio owners their equity back — letting them keep the money
  and equity — because he'd rather not keep talking to them. (Self-reported.)
- As of March 2026, Alex says star performers so massively outperformed the rest of the
  portfolio that divesting time from small deals was worth it.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises that inside a mediocre acquired company there is
  usually only one or two genuinely good people (e.g. a strong marketer or sales
  leader); the highest-return move is often to pull those "diamonds" into the HoldCo
  where their skill compounds across the whole portfolio, and "forget the business."
- As of March 2026, Alex states acquisition.com is "in the talent business" and
  deliberately pays at the 90th percentile for all roles because paying above market
  generates outsized "alpha" return.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims recruiting was acquisition.com's primary value driver
  early on, and he keeps a recruiting team roughly the same size as his sales team; he
  recommends the caller hire a director of talent experienced in executive recruiting.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the two ways to win with a portfolio company as: pull
  the winners out, or put more winners in until it's a full company.
- Alex references Constellation Software as an example of a firm whose entire thesis is
  doing ~600 deals a year at ~$3M each, requiring a purpose-built model around that
  volume.
- (Caller, unverified) The firm runs on family office money, does deals deal-by-deal,
  and has done deals in a "few hundred million" size range.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "A lot of money and a lot of time."

> "It's almost more valuable to just pull out the diamonds, forget the business, and then put it into a better business vehicle."

> "As a company, like internally, we're in the talent business. When people are like, what business — we're in the talent business, 100%."

> "We pay 90th percentile, which is pretty high, for all of our roles, because I just want the best."

> "If you have a sales director that you think is an absolute savage and they want $400,000 a year, you will make $4 million a year extra by getting the savage."

> "The core function that we ran as basically almost our primary value driver in the very beginning of acquisition.com was recruiting. We were just very good at it. And that's how you scale stuff."

> "There's only two winners. Either we can pull the winners out or we can put three more winners in and now we have a full company."
