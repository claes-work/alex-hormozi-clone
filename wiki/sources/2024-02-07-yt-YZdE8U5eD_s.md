# If You Want to Start a Business in 2024, Watch This Interview

## Summary

Alex interviews Harley Finkelstein of Shopify about entrepreneurship, Shopify's origin, and the lower modern cost of starting a business. Harley gives the Shopify/company-history material; Alex adds founder-oriented lessons about imperfect first businesses, solving one's own problem, staying product-agnostic, and making a business known before expecting customers.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2024-02-07 - First-business realism**: Alex says aspiring entrepreneurs over-romanticize the first business; the first business mainly teaches the next step rather than needing to be a dream company.
- **2024-02-07 - Solve your own problem**: Alex says every really good business he has had came from scratching his own itch or solving a problem he understood firsthand.
- **2024-02-07 - Idea generation**: Alex recommends telling many people that you are thinking of starting a business so the environment starts returning problems, introductions, and ideas.
- **2024-02-07 - Advertising definition**: Alex restates his Leads-book view that advertising is making something known; if no one knows, no one can buy.
- **2024-02-07 - Low-cost experimentation**: Alex agrees with the theme that the cost of entrepreneurial failure has fallen dramatically, which makes repeated attempts more rational.

## Context / guest-attributed claims

- Harley Finkelstein gives the Shopify origin story, including his own early businesses, meeting Tobi Lutke, and Shopify's mission to make entrepreneurship more accessible.
- Harley says the cost of starting/failing at a business has fallen sharply through products such as Shopify; he frames this as a major democratization of entrepreneurship.
- Harley discusses examples such as Gymshark and broad growth in business applications. Treat these figures as guest/context unless separately verified.
