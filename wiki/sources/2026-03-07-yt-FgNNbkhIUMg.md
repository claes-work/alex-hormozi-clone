# What Is "Value"?

## Summary
Interview clip (an off-camera host asks questions; only Alex-attributed speech is treated as voice data). Prompted about the "five business strategies that actually work" and when brand becomes higher leverage, Alex reframes everything back to his **Value Equation**: the components of value are speed/latency, ease (removing friction), perceived likelihood of achievement (which he ladders to consistency/reliability/risk), and the meaningfulness of the impact itself to the customer. His core argument here is about **where brand fits inside value**: brand does *not* overcome or sit separately from the value components — it is "a high lever on risk," influencing only the perceived-likelihood-of-achievement / risk-free dimension. People buy a brand because it has rewarded them in the past, and the product then either reinforces or deteriorates that cycle. He connects this to creator-world "jab jab jab, right hook" / "give give get" thinking: that advice is only true when your products aren't great; if products are consistently excellent (his Apple / Steve Jobs example — nobody lining up for a new Apple product felt "right-hooked"), then the product itself deposits goodwill. He collapses the whole idea into a single rule: **always reward your customers at every interaction.** Consistently rewarded customers have less friction, buy faster, and pay premium prices because the purchase feels risk-free (his Chipotle example: 20 good bowls buy forgiveness for one bad bowl). Asked whether that means "all jabs, no right hooks," Alex agrees — all gives, then you get. The back half applies this to his own Skool promotion: he says the "I have nothing to sell you" positioning worked because he'd spent years only giving value, and that he only puts his name behind a company (Skool) when it's the best product in its category and appeals to a large share of his audience — otherwise (e.g. a hypothetical teeth-whitening chain) there's no point promoting it personally. Ends with the standard acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
