# The Trap of Productizing Your Service Business

## Summary

Alex coaches a founder selling tech and operations consulting to law firms. The founder
asks about productizing a service business and taking $500k of investment. Alex warns
that productizing a high-skill professional-services firm can make the business harder
to sell if it dodges the real supply constraint, and he argues that this service business
does not need outside capital because it can sell work before hiring delivery capacity.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Professional-services businesses are often supply constrained: selling is easier than
  finding high-skill delivery people.
- If the founder wants to sell the company, Alex advises against productizing in a way
  that creates a harder-to-sell business.
- The hard part of a high-skill service business is the trade for building a viable
  company.
- Outside capital is rarely needed in a service business that makes money from current
  work.
- A service business can often sell the work before it has the person to fulfill it.
- Taking $500k can mean giving up ownership and adding a permanent stakeholder for the
  equivalent of one deal.
- Choosing between high-touch and lower-touch offers should be based on LTV-to-CAC,
  gross margin, revenue retention, and how many units can be sold.
- If gross margins are equal and the lower-touch offer can sell more than four times as
  many units, Alex says to prefer that offer.
