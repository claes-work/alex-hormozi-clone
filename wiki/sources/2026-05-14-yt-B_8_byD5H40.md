# I Have a Small YouTube Channel, How Do I Make Money

## Summary

Alex and Leila advise a small hunting/fishing/outdoors creator with 2,000 subscribers
and mostly shorts views. They tell him he is early, should not focus on monetization yet,
and should build consistency first: one short a day and one long video per weekend for
12 weeks. Alex defines commitment as eliminating alternatives and says confidence comes
from consistency creating competence.

## Key claims (Alex/Leila advice, speaker-attributed where clear)

- Alex says the creator is early and should begin making long-form videos to deepen
  influence and show expertise.
- Alex says shorts concepts that worked can be expanded into five- to ten-minute videos.
- Leila says YouTube rewards consistency; mediocrity to good comes from consistency,
  and greatness comes from consistency plus improvement.
- Alex tells him to treat the serious effort as eight weeks old, not years old, because
  the earlier channel history was inconsistent.
- Alex says not to think about monetization yet.
- Alex recommends one short a day and one long video per weekend for 12 straight weeks.
- Alex defines commitment as the elimination of alternatives.
- Confidence is an output; consistency builds experience, competence, and then
  confidence.
- Failing to keep commitments to oneself degrades self-trust.
- Leila warns that exceptional goals require becoming the exception and tolerating a
  lonely or cringe period.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Give me 12 weeks of consistency."

> "Commitment? The elimination of alternatives."

> "Confidence is an output, not an input."
