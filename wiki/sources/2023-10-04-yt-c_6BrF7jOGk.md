# I Doubled A Business in 60 Days to Show It's Not Luck (Part 1)

## Summary

Alex walks through a portfolio-company turnaround: one of his 13 [[../entities/acquisition-com|Acquisition.com]] holdco companies (a young-founder team selling a mid-priced consumer service via a two-call close) whose sales output he nearly doubled in ~60 days by diagnosing and fixing five problems. He first defines the five sales-funnel metrics he tracks (show rate, offer rate, close rate, % cash collected up front, units sold), then narrates the before-state, the root causes across ad targeting / sales process / org structure, the fixes applied in a deliberate order, and the after-numbers. The thesis is that doubling a business is repeatable process, not luck — "when you do things that work, they work." Part 1 covers sales; product/customer-success problems are teased for Part 2.

## Key claims

**The diagnostic — five metrics he tracks on any appointment-based sales business:**
- **Show rate**: % of booked appointments that show up. Benchmark ~70% for any appointment-type business.
- **Offer rate**: % of people who show that you actually make an offer to; an indicator of lead-flow quality (you shouldn't offer unqualified prospects).
- **Close rate**: % who buy (can be measured off shows or off offers — he tracks both). Highly variable by sale type: 80%+ for low-ticket in-person, ~5% for an investment sold over the phone.
- **% cash collected up front**: cash collected today vs. total deal size; a measure of buyer conviction and sales-team strength.
- **Units sold**: the output of the four multiplied together.
- Tracking both close-off-shows and close-off-offers, plus per-rep stats, exposes reps who game their close rate by only offering sure-things. High-quality data is what lets you localize a problem (bad leads vs. bad closers) and fix it.

**Before-state (prior month, 56 units sold):** 49% show rate · 83% offer rate · 27% close rate · 47% cash collected.

**Problem 1 — Low show rate (49% vs. 70% benchmark, a ~40% gap):**
- Root cause: ad **targeting** was off — optimizing for lowest-cost leads/appointments instead of **cost per sale**; media buyer was targeting 18–24 y/o instead of the intended 25–35 y/o gainfully employed who love their job.
- Number-one driver of show rate: total time-slots available. Other drivers: targeting and the offer itself.
- Sales reps were canceling 75% of appointments to hit even the 49% show rate — "the closest thing to literally burning money."

**Problem 2 — Setter multitasking / show-rate mechanics:** setters were both dialing new leads and doing nurture/reminders (two different flow-states). Three issues: (1) no double-dialing, (2) speed-to-contact too slow (leads sitting 30 min–3 hrs), (3) no morning-of nurture / reminders.

**Problem 3 — Low close rate (27% vs. ~40% benchmark, ~50% improvement needed):**
- Surface-level discovery: reps only asked "how much money do you want to make" instead of digging into **intention** (the "why" questions below the iceberg — what changes, who else it affects, why it matters).
- Objections/obstacles blowing up in the close (objections come after the price, obstacles before) because discovery was shallow; "smoke screen" objections are fake reasons to get off the phone.
- Reps talking more than prospects; argumentation and hard-closing; tonality off. Tone ("childlike curiosity," raising voice at the end) and emphasis change the message ("I didn't say he hit his wife" example).

**Problem 4 — People / org-structure issues:**
- CEO was the sales manager because he was the best closer — but best-closer ≠ good sales-manager; sales-team churn was through the roof.
- Companies over-educate reps on the product and under-educate on the prospect ("sell me this pen" is about the prospect, not the pen).
- Setting-team standards set too low (2 sets/day when 3 was achievable — a 50% lift; "if you set the bar low people shrink to that level").

**The five fixes, applied in deliberate order:**
1. **Hired an experienced sales director** first (freeing the micromanaging CEO) — solve the *who* before the *what*/*how*; a recurring problem under the same *who* is often a who-problem.
2. **Fixed ad targeting** — also a personnel fix (replaced a negligent media buyer running a side hustle); back to 25–35 y/o, optimizing for cost per sale.
3. **Reduced sales-team size and reset setter expectations** — cut the lowest-utilization/lowest-close reps; fewer, better people raises team close rate and morale (a low performer demotivates high performers). Setter target 2→4/day.
4. **Promoted one setter to lead-nurture specialist** — bridges setting and closing teams; equipped with a checklist (three-way intro between setter and closer via iPhone; morning-of personalized voice-memo/video-text from the closer).
5. **Optimized sales scripts** — deeper discovery, "killing zombies" (surface objections early / defuse the bomb before it goes off), added a pre-call decision-maker question, and drilled the team on **looping** (resolve concern, ask again, resolve, ask again).

**After-state (~60 days):** show rate 49%→70% · offer rate ~80% (roughly unchanged — they had been offering unqualified people) · close rate 27%→41% · cash collected 47%→82% · units 56→93/month. Framed as ~40% improvement in one lever, ~50% in another, near-doubling of cash collected, ~4x cash-flow effect.
