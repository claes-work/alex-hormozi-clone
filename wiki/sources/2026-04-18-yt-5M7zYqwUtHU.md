# Helping an E-Com Brand Turn One-Time Buyers Into Monthly Subscribers

## Summary

Alex advises a children's sticker-book company selling roughly 1,000 Amazon units per day.
Instead of using the QR code on the back of the book for a value-free email opt-in, he
suggests turning it into a continuity offer: "three free books" for joining the membership,
then one or two books per month depending on normal usage.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-18, Alex says an existing buyer QR code should offer immediate value, not just
  ask for an email.
- For a sticker-book continuity offer, he suggests leading with three free books for
  joining the membership.
- He says the cost of the books is low enough that the company should do more than collect
  a value-free email.
- He recommends matching monthly continuity volume to normal product usage.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "They bought a book. Give them three books for free the moment they join the membership."

> "Three free books. Scan here. It'll crush."

> "Might as well do more."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: buyer QR code to continuity offer; free product as membership join
incentive; match continuity cadence to usage rate.
