# Most People Abhor Work Because They Misunderstand It

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says people misunderstand high-workload people because they define "work" as
pain, while the high-workload person may define it as challenging, worthwhile activity.
He repeats his good-day blueprint and says work addiction becomes easier when the person
chooses work worth doing.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex's good day includes working very hard on something worth doing, working out, and
  spending time with people he enjoys.
- He says many people define work negatively, so they hear the word "work" as "pain."
- Understanding successful people requires understanding that they may define work
  differently.
- Work that is worth doing can be challenging without being experienced as mere pain.
- The uncertainty of whether the work will succeed is part of what makes it engaging.
- People who are "addicted to work" make it easier to be addicted by choosing things
  worth doing.
- Life is short enough that people should spend their limited time on the few things worth
  doing.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "One person says the word work and the other person hears the word pain."

> "Do things worth doing."
