# You're Stuck Because Your Pricing Model Is Killing Profit

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an Australian/New Zealand wholesale-and-DTC product founder whose major pharmacy/channel partners drive volume but hurt margin and price integrity. Alex separates wholesale economics from DTC advertising, identifies pricing/cost structure as the profit issue, and argues that raising wholesale price may both increase profit and force low-integrity distributors either to raise price or stop selling. He ends by telling the founder to be bold whichever path she chooses.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Product founder** - the coachee. Her product category, geography, wholesale footprint, DTC channel, and distributor details are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When wholesale is the primary revenue driver, the founder should diagnose wholesale profitability separately from DTC advertising spend.
- If price architecture and wholesale terms are compressing margin, the levers are price up, cost down, or both.
- A 10% price increase can more than double profit when current margins are thin.
- If distributors undercut the brand and destroy price integrity, raising wholesale price can force them either to raise retail prices or stop selling.
- Losing some low-margin wholesale volume can still leave the business making more money if the remaining volume is much more profitable.
- If the founder already removed a distributor to regain pricing control, Alex treats that as a valid strategic path; the key is to commit rather than stay timid in limbo.
