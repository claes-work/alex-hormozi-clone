# How Do I Become an Authority in the Personal Brand Niche?

## Summary
A member Q&A: a questioner who has "been building brands globally for 20 years" asks Alex how to position that expertise and whether to open with a high price or a low price to bring new people in. Alex reframes the problem as an avatar/business-model problem rather than a pricing one. He argues "building brands" is too vague and that most people don't even know what a brand is, then lays out three candidate sub-avatars: (1) an enterprise marketing agency serving big companies, (2) helping individuals build personal brands — where having your own personal brand is essential to demonstrate authority, or (3) professional development for people working in branding (career advancement). He stresses matching the audience to the business model: enterprise/Fortune-100 work is an enterprise sale for which a community is probably not the vehicle, while personal-brand and professional-development audiences suit a community. On a follow-up clarifying the person helps successful entrepreneurs with global brand strategy, Alex warns branding is very hard to sell to small and medium-sized businesses — it's the most valuable thing you can develop but takes a very long time and requires a customer who understands the value of brand, both rare, which is why (in his framing) mainly Fortune 100 companies buy it.

## Key claims

- (2026-02-09) Three candidate sub-avatars for this expertise: (a) an enterprise marketing agency that helps big companies build brands; (b) helping people build personal brands; (c) professionals in the branding space seeking career advancement (professional development).

- (2026-02-09) Selling to Fortune 100 / Fortune 500 (implied by "global") is an enterprise sale, which changes the appropriate business model.
- (2026-02-09) Match the audience to the business model: for enterprise services a community is probably not the solution; a community does work for a personal-brand audience or a professional-development audience.
- (2026-02-09) [guest-attributed] Follow-up clarification: the person specializes in helping successful entrepreneurs build their global brand strategically, not the startup/design phase.
