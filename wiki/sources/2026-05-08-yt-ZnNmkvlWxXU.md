# The Trick to Never Being Insulted Again

## Summary

Alex explains why insults do not have to land. He treats labels as partial, non-binary,
and often irrelevant; a person can agree with some truth in the label or reinterpret
the comment entirely. He uses Leila's response to a stranger calling her "skinny" as an
example of choosing the meaning of an intended insult.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says he does not give off much "let's fight" energy.
- He says he often ends insults by agreeing with them instead of trying to prove the
  other person wrong.
- He says being called an asshole does not bother him because he has probably behaved
  that way at some point.
- He says traits are degrees, not binaries; the question is not whether someone is
  patient, but how patient.
- He says many people react to insults because they think a label from one individual
  means something.
- He says an intended insult becomes insulting only when the receiver accepts it as
  insulting.
- He says he cannot know another person's real intention, only the observable fact that
  the person said certain words.
- He self-reports walking with Leila on the Las Vegas Strip when a stranger called her
  "skinny"; Leila took the word as a compliment.
