# Social Media Is Changing

★ **L3-candidate** — durable reframe ("social media → interest media" / "the content is the targeting") plus a reusable market-sizing framework for calibrating content expectations. Worth promoting into `content-strategy` topics.

## Summary

Solo Alex Hormozi (@MoreMozi). His thesis: social media is turning into **interest media** — the algorithm has become good enough that it targets your content for you based on what you actually talk about, so *the content itself is the targeting*. The practical consequence for business owners (educators, not entertainers): stop judging content by raw view counts, which reward broad entertainment, and instead make content specifically for your ideal avatar and judge it by the *quality* of the audience it attracts. He walks through US market-size math (only ~9% of people own a business; 95% of those are under $1M revenue) to argue that a "small" view count of the right people is a huge opportunity, and that comparing yourself to MrBeast-scale entertainment is a category error. He closes with proof-points: businesses doing seven figures on tiny followings.

Trend-sensitive claim dated 2026-02-22 — the "social → interest media" framing and the "algorithm is so good now" argument are anchored to this moment in the platform/algorithm landscape.

## Key claims (paraphrased unless quoted; all dated 2026-02-22)

- **Social media is turning into "interest media."** The distribution is no longer primarily social-graph driven; content is shown to people based on their demonstrated interests. (He notes "some of you guys may have heard this" — presenting it as a circulating concept, not solely his coinage.)
- **Judging content by views is "dumb"** for business creators. A viral gag (e.g. a grandma slapping him) gets views by being shown to people interested in *humor* — a large audience, but not your customers.
- **Most business people are educators, not entertainers.** Regardless of vertical (e-commerce, SaaS, services, brick-and-mortar), if you sell to people you're trying to provide value that changes behavior and walks them closer to buying.
- **"The content is the targeting."** The algorithm can now infer what you're talking about — even judging your background, clothing, and who you are — and shows it to people with a history of finding similar content valuable. So making content *for your avatar* auto-targets that avatar.
- **Low view counts on niche content are expected and fine.** A piano-repair creator getting ~1,000 views/video is reaching a genuinely small buying market; comparing that to entertainment views is meaningless.
- **Prioritize IRL / quality response over view count.** Hormozi says he cares far more about real-world responses — e.g. respected business owners texting him "that was fire" — than raw views. When business owners visit his Vegas HQ, their favorite content is consistently the more "educational"/niche format, not the broad-appeal stuff, so he makes more of it.
- **Market-sizing math (US, "census bureau data," his figures):**
  - ~9% of people own a business → ~32–33 million US business owners.
  - 95% of that 9% are below $1M revenue; only 5% are over $1M (~1.5 million people).
  - Over $10M: ~0.4% (one in 250).
  - Nine figures ($100M/yr): roughly one in 3,000 businesses (varies by data source).
- **Calibrate expectations to your true market size.** If ~1.5M people are $1M+ business owners, you'll never get 100% of them; 100,000 views on content made for that tier means "you're crushing it." Don't measure against MrBeast — "we have different games."
- **His own early trajectory as proof of small-number value:** started at zero, once dreamed of 10,000 views in 24 hours when he was getting ~1,000/day; before that, in-person "lunch and learns" drew ~15 people — so a thousand of the *right* views is enormous by comparison. (Biographical, self-reported.)
- **You can make serious money with a tiny following.** He cites two businesses doing $1M+/year with under 5,000 followers, and a B2B insurance company he invested in doing "many millions" on about 10,000 followers — provided the content is directly valuable to that following.
- **Soft promo:** he's a co-founder of Skool ("school.com/…mozi"), positioned as a low-cost way to start a business online — $9/month after a 14-day trial. He also references the "cash cows" format (a business owner presents their business and he coaches improvements) and asks viewers to comment whether they like it and whether they're business owners.
