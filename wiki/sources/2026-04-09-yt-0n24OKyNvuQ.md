# Helping an $810K Home Service Business Get to $3M

## Summary

Alex advises a pressure-washing and gutter-cleaning owner to scale the proven local
door-to-door channel rather than add digital complexity. Subscription customers should
first build predictable cash flow; a well-paid or commission-only canvasser can then
replace the founder. Centralized online lead generation makes more sense only after the
company has enough trucks and teams to absorb it.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex first asks what prevents doing ten times more of an already
  profitable acquisition activity.
- The owner's reported 10:1 LTV:CAC excludes the founder's free labor and should be
  recalculated with a paid salesperson; all figures are owner-reported and unverified.
- He recommends stacking annual subscriptions until their cash flow funds a replacement
  canvasser.
- A generous per-sale commission may attract a commission-only salesperson and remove
  fixed hiring risk.
- Adding Facebook, Instagram, or Google creates a separate lead-calling system and is
  premature while a simpler door-to-service workflow works.
- Digital awareness and centralized phone sales become more sensible after the company
  has many trucks and delivery teams in the city.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What stops you from doing 10 times more of that?"

> "I don't want to add complexity."

> "I'd rather you keep it easy."

## Notes

Reinforces more-before-new, subscription-funded capacity, and local-channel simplicity.
