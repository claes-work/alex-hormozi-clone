# How to Get More Customers

## Summary
Monologue built on the Rule of 100 and the volume argument. Alex says he's "never seen a business not grow" when a starter implements the rule of 100 — 100 primary actions/day, ideally for 100 days ("100 and 100" = 10,000 actions in one direction). Most people get their first customer by ~week three, but you must commit to the full 100 days ("heart of a missionary versus the mercenary"). For bigger businesses, apply the rule of 100 each time you expand to a new channel/platform. His central diagnosis of small-business "feast or famine": what feels like inconsistent lead flow is really *insufficient volume* — the timeline you're measuring is too small. If you average one sale per 10 days, that means there's enough haphazard advertising in a 10-day window for one sale; compress that same advertising into one day *deliberately* and you get one sale per day. "Companies doing 30 times more sales are typically doing 30 times more advertising." He contrasts his ~450 pieces of content/week (~25–30k/year) with a $1M business's ~1/day (~365/year) and defends volume against the optimization mindset: diminishing returns are still returns; "you can get the most for the least, but you're not going to get the most." Ends with the roadmap promo.
