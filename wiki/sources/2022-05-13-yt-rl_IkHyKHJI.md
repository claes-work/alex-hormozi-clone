# Why losing friends is normal (on friendship and the climb)

## Summary

A personal, reflective solo video in which Alex, having fallen out of touch with his
last two friends from high school/college, argues that losing friends during the climb
toward your goals is a natural sign of growth, not a character flaw. Sparked by a
conversation with his close friend Dean Graziosi about the price of one's dreams, he
reframes friendship as an exchange of value over a time horizon and encourages viewers
to tolerate the empty space rather than settle for false friends.

## Key claims

- "Real friends talk to your face and praise you behind your back; fake friends do the
  opposite" — the true test of a friend is whether they root for you behind your back.
  (Paraphrase / near-verbatim; from a viral tweet of his.)
- People root for you until you outdo them, then stop; many from your past personalize
  the gap and resent you as a reminder of choices they didn't make. (Paraphrase.)
- Relationships persist because of an exchange of value; depth is measured by how long
  you're willing to wait for the next positive exchange (intermittent reinforcement).
  (Paraphrase.)
- Getting comfortable being alone makes you more selective — like dating, you can hold
  out for the best people instead of filling a vacuum out of fear. Two or three good
  friends is enough. (Paraphrase.)
- If you grow, you change; people who say "you've changed" often haven't grown
  themselves. Losing such friends is the price of becoming who you want to be.
  (Paraphrase.)
