# Invest First, Then Spend the Rest

## Summary

Alex reverses the usual savings remainder: automatically invest first, then spend what
remains. He recommends removing investment money before it is seen, holding lifestyle
spending at a fixed amount as income rises, and creating friction around withdrawals and
purchases. For beginners, the investment may be procedural or declarative knowledge in
their own skills.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex recommends prioritizing the future self by investing before
  discretionary spending.
- He cites Switzerland's millionaire rate as evidence of different money rules, but the
  source provides no supporting data; treat it as an unverified illustration.
- Automate investment through payroll, apps, or separate accounts and create manual
  friction around spending.
- Holding spending to a fixed amount as earnings rise makes all incremental income flow
  to investment.
- Early investment should distinguish declarative knowledge about a subject from
  procedural ability to perform it.
- Alex self-reports owning no Lamborghinis, Rolexes, or jets despite being able to buy
  several monthly; this is dated self-report.
- He recommends manual withdrawals, deleting shopping apps, and removing stored cards to
  make spending harder.
