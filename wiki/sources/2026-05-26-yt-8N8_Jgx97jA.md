# Why Most Businesses Fail: No Standards

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says the businesses people love have standards, and the businesses people
resent usually reflect an owner who lacks standards. He emphasizes that standards must be
communicated as observable actions and modeled by the owner; a leader cannot demand early
arrival, discipline, or consistency while failing to embody those standards.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Businesses with excellent customer experiences usually have clear standards.
- Bad businesses often trace back to an owner who does not hold or model standards.
- Standards have to be communicated as observable actions, not vague preferences.
- Owners lose credibility when they demand behavior they do not model.
- Leading by example is required before the standard has moral force inside the company.
