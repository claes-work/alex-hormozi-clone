# Why Most People Hijack Their Own Direction

## Summary

This clip is mostly another speaker's warning about using resentment to play someone
else's game, followed by Alex's father story. Alex says that around age 28 he realized he
had been trying to prove himself to his father. He repeats the father-apology story and
notes that he and his father are now on good terms.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says he was fortunate to realize around age 28 that he had been trying to beat or
  prove himself to one central person: his father.
- He says he and his father are now on good terms.
- After Alex left and his father disapproved, they did not talk much for about 5 years.
- His father later called and apologized "for everything."
- Alex frames the apology as culturally significant because his father was born in Iran
  and came from a different father-son expectation.
- Alex says he had more awareness later than he had at the time.
- The clip reinforces the danger of letting someone else's status game redirect one's
  goals.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I had been trying really hard to beat... my father."

> "We're on good terms, by the way."

> "For everything."
