# Helping a $200M Info Product Portfolio Survive AI

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a Brazilian portfolio owner with minority positions in multiple online-course / mentorship businesses. Alex says it does not make economic sense to build an Acquisition.com-style specialist support team for small minority stakes because the needed talent would consume too much of the economics and the positions are illiquid. When asked how to protect info-product businesses from AI as customers increasingly want answers rather than learning, Alex says he would not protect them; he would make as much as possible and look for a different vehicle.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Brazilian portfolio owner** - the coachee. His revenue in reais, number of invested businesses, minority ownership, and product categories are background only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- An Acquisition.com-style specialist team does not make sense for small companies where the investor owns only 20-30%.
- The economics fail because the quality of talent required would consume all or more of the available economics.
- The bet is worse because small minority positions are illiquid, so the investor cannot rely on easy exits to recover the cost.
- For info products exposed to AI substitution, Alex would not try to protect the business.
- His recommendation is to harvest as much as possible while looking for a different business vehicle.
