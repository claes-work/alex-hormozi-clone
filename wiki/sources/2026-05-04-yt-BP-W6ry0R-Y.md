# How to Sell a Community Business for the Most Money

## Summary

Alex says a Skool/community business can be sold if it has the traits buyers pay for:
recurring revenue, low risk, customer longevity, reliable acquisition, profitability, and
low owner dependence. He frames a business sale as selling an annuity, with the price
discounted by risk. The more a buyer must personally operate the community, the less the
business looks like an asset and the more it looks like a job.

## Key claims

- Alex sees no inherent reason a community business on Skool could not be sold.
- Buyers are effectively purchasing an annual recurring revenue stream and discounting
  its value by perceived risk.
- Retention, predictable acquisition, diversified traffic, profit, and low owner
  dependence increase exit value.
- If customer stay is short and acquisition is unclear, Alex says the business might sell
  for a small fraction of annual revenue.
- The sale value depends on how much the business is an asset versus a job for the buyer.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Any asset is sellable if it has the characteristics of a sellable business."
