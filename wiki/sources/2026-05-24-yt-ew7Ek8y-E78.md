# How to Build a Network When You Have Nothing to Offer

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains his give-first networking strategy. Early in a career, he recommends
generalized giving because future needs are diverse and unpredictable. He describes joining
a peer group, meeting every member, offering help with sales and marketing, and later being
able to call people years afterward for deep industry information. As skill and demand rise,
the giving strategy narrows.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Early in a career, give broadly because future needs are unpredictable.
- Give actual value, not just an attempt or an offer to help.
- Alex says he won an invented "member of the year" award in a peer group because he met
  with everyone and helped with sales/marketing.
- Giving without immediate ask creates future access to expertise and relationship capital.
- Later, when skills and demand increase, the person can narrow where attention goes.
- Alex frames his current stage as more exploitation than exploration: he knows his strengths
  and path more clearly, so he interacts regularly with fewer people.
- People integrate prior value subconsciously; he does not need to remind them of favors.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "In the beginning I had nothing. I had only the only thing I had was skills."
