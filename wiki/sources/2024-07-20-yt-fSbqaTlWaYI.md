# Learn Paid Ads in 30 Minutes!

## Summary

A solo Alex Hormozi paid-ads masterclass structured as live Q&A: business owners at
sizes from ~$600K to $10M/year present their paid-ads situation and Alex prescribes a
strategy on the spot. The through-lines are consistent across every business: lead with
the prospect's pain in the hook, use customer-list lookalike audiences on Meta, add or
remove funnel friction to tune lead quality, lean on proof over promise, and (once ROAS
is healthy) simply spend more. He also details his creative-scaling doctrine — spend
80% of resources reskinning proven winners — and a 70/20/10 budget split between the
core ad, adjacent variations, and wild new bets. Repeated tie-ins to group funnels on
Skool and his `$100M Leads` acquisition-channel thinking.

## Key claims (paid-ads framework)

**Creative & hooks**
- Lead every ad with the prospect's **pain**, not with yourself — articulate the
  specific pains of the target avatar (e.g. for dentists: don't want to work weekends,
  don't want to sell to private equity and be forced into years of servitude).
- ~80% of the ads his companies have run over the years are about **proof**, not about
  him personally. "No one cares" about the founder; buyers want to see people like them
  who got the result.
- Proof sits on a continuum of believability: in-person > virtual, live > recorded,
  looks-like-me > anonymous, exact-result-I-want > close result. The most compelling
  proof is the kind hardest to fake.
- **"Your promise is not unique; your proof is."** Everyone in a market promises the
  same outcome; differentiation comes from proof and a believable mechanism, not a
  different promise.
- You only lose trust in the marketplace when you say something untrue — if it's just
  the prospect's fact of life, no one can fault you.

**Targeting**
- Default to **Meta (Facebook + Instagram)** first because its targeting is easiest to
  figure out; TikTok targeting is "getting much, much better." Google/PPC is
  intent-based (higher quality); Meta is interruption-based (lower intent but huge reach,
  and skews older — good for dentists, roofers, homeowners).
- Upload your **customer list → build a lookalike audience** off it. If "dentist" is in
  the copy and the video, Meta's AI will also serve it to dentists.
- If you have an email list over ~1,000, seed targeting with a lookalike of that list.

**Funnels & friction**
- A sales process doesn't create qualified customers so much as **filter** for good ones.
  More good filters = more expensive but better leads. Bad filters (e.g. a page that
  loads slowly) add cost without quality. A VSL is *good* friction that raises lead
  quality.
- Perfect-funnel rule: too many bad leads → **add friction** (e.g. an application); not
  enough leads → **remove friction**; near-zero friction and still bad leads → the
  **targeting** is off.
- Standard local/lead funnel: ad → free (or low-ticket $19–29) offer → opt-in → thank-you
  page → scheduler → **still call every lead yourself**. For local businesses trust is
  already high, so ads are very **offer-driven** and brand/value-building matters much less.
- **Global retargeting** ("the shadow funnel"): install every platform's pixel and
  retarget site visitors across GDN, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube. It's the first,
  cheapest, almost-always-positive ROAS because you already paid for that traffic.
- **Own your PPC branded terms** — a PPC campaign on your own terms always makes money;
  he's "never seen it not."

**Metrics & scaling ROAS**
- Two levers to improve ROAS (besides just spending more): drive **CAC down** or push
  **LTV / average cart value up**.
- Drive CAC down via creative: pull your **top 5–10% of ads**, study the **first 3
  seconds**, and graft those winning 3-second openers onto 100+ other ads — 10–50x more
  creative per proven winner.
- Push LTV up by **bundling** recurring consumables with a high-ticket purchase (the
  "buy a year of ink with the printer" logic) — even 30% take-rate adds meaningful ACV,
  and a max-cart bundle can nearly double spend.
- When ROAS is strong and you have capacity (e.g. 7:1, 10:1, 20:1), the answer is
  **spend more** — dial spend to your fill rate; if seats/capacity aren't filling, crank
  spend then throttle off.
- Raise capital / get investors to open more locations faster when a location model is
  printing high ROAS.

**Creative-scaling doctrine (the core distinct framework)**
- His teams spend **~80% of resources taking winners and reskinning them**, not making
  net-new ads.
- The "kale ad" story: one perfect testimonial spawned ~100 versions — black-and-white,
  sepia, reordered back end, swapped hooks/headlines/subheads/fonts/copy. "Use and abuse
  it until it doesn't work at all." Winners should be run longer, harder, in more
  variations.
- **70/20/10 time & budget split**: 70% on the main proven ad, 20% on close adjacent
  permutations, 10% on wild new ideas. (He credits Google's experimentation as validating
  this.)

**Compliance (regulated niches)**
- In health/wealth/relationship spaces, claims get ads auto-shut-down. Hire a
  **compliance-audit firm** (~$5–6K), check their post-audit approval rating (90%+ for
  good ones); they audit sites/videos/collateral and often have a Google back-channel to
  push compliant accounts through.

**Group funnels (Skool)** — four conversion mechanisms from a free group:
1. Free onboarding call auto-added for every new member → clarify/qualify → pitch.
2. A 5-video VSL series inside the group (opportunity/core story/big idea → three
   belief-breaking issues → CTA video with stack + sizzle).
3. Weekly value webinar with a soft CTA; setters DM engaged attendees and pull them off.
4. DM setters engage anyone who likes/comments/posts and set the call.
- Benchmarks he cites: good operators convert **~10%** of group opt-ins into the next
  level; get on calls with ~1/3 of opt-ins and close 30–50%. Group funnels are dropping
  cost-per-call "from ~$250 to ~$40" across industries.
