# The Power of Moments: Why 95% of Our Year Goes Unnoticed

## Summary

Alex says one of his themes for the year is focusing on moments. He argues that people
remember only a small number of moments from a year, then often let a few negative
moments define an entire season. He connects the same threat-detection habit to business
strategy: instead of only asking how to grow a business, ask how to destroy it in the
fewest moves and then do the opposite.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex says he has been focusing on moments as a theme for the year.
- He says people likely forget about 95% of a normal year because routine does not get
  recorded as notable memory.
- He argues that a small number of bad moments can be mentally expanded into a bad
  season or bad year.
- He says the same negative-detection machinery can help in business when aimed at risk.
- He attributes the "invert, always invert" principle to Charlie Munger and also
  references Einstein.
- He recommends asking how to destroy the business in the fewest possible moves, then
  doing the opposite.
- He says some of his best creative business ideas came from identifying obvious ways the
  business could die and reversing them.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I don't remember probably 95% of the year."

> "Invert, always invert."

> "How would I absolutely destroy this business in the fewest possible moves?"
