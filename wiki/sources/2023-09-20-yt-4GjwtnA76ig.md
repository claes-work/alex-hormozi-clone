# This video will make you more money than anything else on the Internet

## Summary

A live keynote on offers — "how to sell the same thing for more money by making offers so good people feel stupid saying no." Five parts: nailing the offer, selling to the right people, charging a lot, reverse-engineering value, and a live scarcity/urgency/guarantee demonstration. Built around case studies where changing only the offer or only the price multiplied returns.

## Key claims

- Nailing the offer is the single strongest lever. Agency case study: changing only the offer (from $1k down + $1k/mo to $4k one-time setup, pay-when-they-show, implied guarantee) gave 2.5x response rate, 2.3x close rate, 4x price → 5 sales became 28, $112k cash collected on $10k spend (11.2:1, ~22x more effective). His career ROAS is 36:1; portfolio ~$200M/yr. (paraphrase)
- Sell to people valuable because of *who they are*, not who you are. Same 10% CRO uplift on a $1M business = $36k fee; on a $100M business = $3.6M for the same work (100x pay). "The boat you're in" matters more than how hard you row (Buffett's steel-industry classmate). Market characteristics to target: pain, purchasing power, easy to find, growing. (paraphrase)
- Charge a lot (Dan Kennedy: "charge as much as you can without cracking a smile"). Compete on value, not price; price-value discrepancy is what matters — you can only cut price to zero but raise value infinitely. Sell "in a vacuum" so there's nothing to compare to. (paraphrase)
- The **virtuous cycle of price**: higher price → more emotional investment → higher perceived value → better results → less demanding clients → more margin to reinvest. Wine experiment: identical wine rated better when labeled more expensive — price itself is a component of value. (paraphrase)
- Reverse-engineer value with the value equation: dream outcome × perceived likelihood of achievement ÷ (time delay × effort/sacrifice) — good, fast, easy, likely. (paraphrase)
- Sales conviction/belief trumps training; charging enough that it "hurts" gives clients skin in the game (gym-launch six-week challenge, $500 refundable, 78% hit the goal). (paraphrase)
