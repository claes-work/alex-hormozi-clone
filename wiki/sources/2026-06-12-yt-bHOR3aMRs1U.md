# Helping a $20M Fence Company Scale to $50M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a fencing-company owner who has grown from $3M to about $20M, wants $50M, but only nets about 2%. Alex rejects industry averages as the standard and diagnoses the issue as execution consistency across locations, not a new growth problem. He pushes the owner to compare location-level performance, identify what the best locations and leaders do differently, and replicate the already-proven model before opening more fronts.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A low-net-margin service business should not excuse itself with "industry average" if some locations or operators already perform better.
- The problem is not always growth; sometimes it is making every unit perform like the units that already work.
- Revenue per location and variance between locations are diagnostic questions for multi-location service businesses.
- When location performance varies widely, the owner needs location-level P&Ls and a clear comparison of what differs.
- Experienced sales or operations leaders may explain why one branch outperforms another; the answer may be talent and attention, not market demand.
- The practical path is to copy what is already working inside the business across the rest of the footprint.
