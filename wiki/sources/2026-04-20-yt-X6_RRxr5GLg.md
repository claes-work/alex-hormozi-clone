# You're Making Business Harder Than It Has to Be

## Summary

Alex explains why he chose personal-brand content for Acquisition.com: not mainly fame,
but to create unlimited deal flow, recruiting leverage, and reduced failure probability.
He says content currently has more pros than cons, but he would stop if that changed,
while acknowledging that fame trades away privacy and is a one-way door.

## Key claims

- Alex says he was reluctant to build a public content brand and is not sure he will do
  it forever.
- He cites celebrity and creator-led examples such as Teremana, Huda Beauty, Proper
  Twelve, Nomad Capitalist, and Kylie Jenner as inputs that made him take brand leverage
  seriously.
- He says the core strategic reason was making Acquisition.com hard to fail by creating
  unlimited deal flow from people who wanted to work with him.
- He says the biggest pros are recruiting elite talent and deal/customer flow.
- He says he does not think he is someone who loves attention or fame.
- He says his content now feeds thousands of families through Acquisition.com, Skool, and
  related distributed effects, so he would try to backfill that responsibility before
  quitting.
- He says the price of fame is privacy and that public attention is a one-way door.
