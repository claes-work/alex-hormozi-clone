# For people who believe in themselves (2024-12-12)

## Summary

Interview clip in which Alex Hormozi explains why he invested in and attached
his personal brand to Skool, framing it as a bet on the risk/opportunity
relationship. He describes the three archetypes of communities that thrive on
the platform (shared pain, shared interest/passion, professional skills) and
argues personal brands are just one cheap channel of advertising, not a
requirement for a community to work.

## Key claims

- Opportunities look like risk today and only look like opportunities in
  retrospect; once all the risk is gone, the upside is competed away because
  everyone can see it. (paraphrase)
- Communities form around a shared something — a pain overcome/being overcome
  together, a niche interest/passion too rare to find locally, or a
  professional skill; those are the three main buckets. (paraphrase)
- Skool wins like Wikipedia beat Encarta: by crowdsourcing communities (rather
  than topics) instead of one entity trying to assemble everything. (paraphrase)
- A personal brand isn't required for a community — brands emerge inside
  communities; fundamentally you only need people to find out about it, so you
  advertise via content, outreach, or ads (all three work). (paraphrase)
- He timed the investment to a "sweet spot" — big enough to see compounding
  growth and referrals, small enough that he could still buy a meaningful
  chunk and add the most value. (paraphrase)
