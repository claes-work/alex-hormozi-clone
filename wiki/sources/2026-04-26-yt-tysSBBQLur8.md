# Why Starting B2B Is a Distraction

## Summary

Alex coaches a profitable career-coaching business that wants to move from B2C into B2B.
He pushes back: the company already has a proven consumer product, a profitable channel,
and only modest ad spend, so the lower-risk move is to do more of what already works. He
frames the B2B idea as a likely escape from sales-efficiency and margin problems that
would also exist on the new path.

## Key claims

- Alex says consumer markets are large, and a proven B2C product with profitable paid
  acquisition should usually be scaled before changing avatars.
- He asks why the company cannot increase spend on the existing channel; the answer to
  that question is the constraint.
- He says the problems in the current B2C model -- sales efficiency, conversion, delivery
  cost, and margin -- will still exist in the B2B model.
- He says the only proper constraint for changing avatar is running out of the existing
  avatar.
- For enterprise sales, he says conversion and stickiness rise when more stakeholders on
  both sides are involved, but deal velocity per salesperson falls.
- If the owner wants diversity, Alex prefers adding a second channel before changing the
  customer.
- He lists likely levers for scaling paid spend: better lead magnet, better creative,
  better conversion-rate optimization, better sales efficiency, or all four.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I will exhaust more before I look at anything new."

> "Why can't we do more and the answer to that question is the constraint."

> "The lowest risk-adjusted return move in any business is to do more of what's already working."

> "The riskiest thing you can do is a new business basically."
