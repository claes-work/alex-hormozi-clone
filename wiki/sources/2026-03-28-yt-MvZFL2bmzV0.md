# I Started 4 Businesses. How Do I Know Which One to Quit and Which One to Scale?

## Summary
In this MoreMozi clip, a Las Vegas luxury real-estate agent tells Alex Hormozi he runs four overlapping ventures — his brokerage (self-reported ~$1.3M revenue last year, on track for ~$1.5M), a client-funded investment fund, an agent-teaching agency built on paid ads, and an end goal of becoming a property developer — but is stuck on focus and can't scale toward $10M. Hormozi draws the org chart, points out the brokerage is the money-maker while the rest are distractions, and diagnoses the real block as a lifestyle/comfort issue rather than a strategy one. His prescription is to consciously trade short-term income (the realtor role) for the long-term goal (development) by leaning on the existing client base for capital.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the caller's situation as one-founder-per-business overload: the caller does most of the work himself across four ventures and has only one strong "right hand," which caps growth.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the core business (brokerage) makes the money and the other ventures are distractions — the caller must decide which to commit to rather than chasing all of them.
- As of March 2026, Alex reframes the obstacle from "focus" to "golden handcuffs" / lifestyle: the caller isn't stuck strategically, he's unwilling to give up current comfort, so the question is how badly he wants the goal.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises consciously giving up the short term (steady realtor income) for the long term (development, which he expects to pay more over time), including using the existing client base to raise development capital.
- As of March 2026, Alex reiterates his "50% of a lot is a lot; 50% of zero is zero" point — the caller's attempt to hand luxury clients off to cheaply-trained agents cost him clients, so replacing himself that way doesn't work at the high end.
- Caller claims (unverified, self-reported): his brokerage did ~$1.3M in revenue last year and is tracking to ~$1.5M this year; wealthy clients have already handed him cash for a fund because they believe in him; he has no debt/payments; he previously tried to replace himself with junior staff and lost clients, so he reversed course.
