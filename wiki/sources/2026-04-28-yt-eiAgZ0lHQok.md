# Specificity Gets Past the Spam Blocker in People's Brains

## Summary

Alex explains why specific copy beats broad claims. Generic promises like "lose weight
fast" trigger banner blindness because people have heard them and been disappointed.
Specific lived details bypass the mental spam filter by making the claim concrete and
recognizable. He uses entrepreneurs missing dance recitals, baseball games, and stressing
about payroll and taxes as an example of replacing "entrepreneurs work hard" with
specific texture.

## Key claims

- Copy becomes stronger through specificity.
- Broad phrases create banner blindness because audiences have learned not to trust them.
- Specific details bypass the mental spam blocker by describing what the claim actually
  looks like.
- The right specifics should come from the audience's real life, not generic examples.
- Specificity is especially useful for making common claims feel true again.
