# How Do I Date While Building a Business

## Summary

Alex advises DJ, a single father restarting a virtual staffing company, about a serious
long-distance relationship. Alex's answer is to let the partner experience the founder's
real life early, without accommodation or performance. He frames the relationship with
one's goals as the core relationship because goals reflect the self; the practical test
is whether a partner loves the actual life the founder wants to live.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex tells DJ to give his partner a "try before you buy" by having her experience his
  actual life for two weeks.
- The founder should not cater, perform, or pretend during that trial.
- If the partner likes the real lifestyle, the relationship has a better chance of
  working with low effort.
- If the partner dislikes the real lifestyle, learning that early prevents years of
  delayed mismatch.
- Alex says the most important relationship is the relationship between a person and
  their goals, because goals reflect the self.
- He would not sacrifice his relationship with himself for another person, especially if
  single.
- The relationship should be moved as far forward as possible into the state where both
  people stop acting.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Give her a try before you buy."

> "I'm not going to cater to you. I'm going to live my life."

> "The most important relationship that you have as you with your goals."
