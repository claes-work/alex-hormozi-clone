# Stop Consuming Content and Start Taking the Action

## Summary

Alex says someone who only listens to the episode will live the same tomorrow; the
listener only proves learning by doing a different action. He defines the useful takeaway
in terms of behavior, such as recording a selfie video and texting it to 100 people. He
also says the early learning curve is exciting because going from terrible to proficient
happens quickly, but mastery takes much longer.

## Key claims

- Alex says passive listening is entertainment unless it changes behavior.
- He says the listener should define the lesson in terms of specific actions.
- He gives an example action: record a selfie video and text it to 100 people.
- He says the first 20 hours can produce more visible progress than later periods because
  the jump from terrible to proficient is fast.
- He distinguishes fast early proficiency from the long path from proficient to master.
- He says the only way to reach the fast learning curve is to start.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If someone just listens to the episode tomorrow will be the same for you."

> "This was just entertainment for you."

> "You can prove to yourself that you learned something... by simply doing something different."

> "The only way you can do that is starting."
