# Helping an Online Coaching Business Scale to $50M/Year

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an advertising-training business owner with high EBITDA who wants to scale to $50 million and is capacity constrained. Alex reframes delegation as return on invested capital inside the business: spending $200,000 on a person who creates $1 million of incremental profit can be better than taking all profit out. He says the founder has talent debt, should recruit ahead of the current team, and should be willing to pay much more for exceptional operators.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - paid-ad training business owner. Her revenue, EBITDA, lack of employees above $100,000, content-time question, and capacity constraint are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- At this profit level, the founder should think in terms of return on invested capital within the business.
- Taking all profit out is valid, but reinvesting in talent can create a higher return.
- Alex says the biggest arbitrage opportunities in business are still in talent.
- "Your best talent is always in the future" means the current team will likely look underpowered from the perspective of the future business.
- The goal is to shrink the gap between current talent and future required talent as fast as possible.
- The founder has talent debt: choosing more owner profit now can create a human-capacity debt later.
- Businesses can incur different debt types: financial, talent, management, or technical debt.
- Service businesses are capped by culture and the people who proliferate that culture.
- If nobody earns more than $100,000, the founder is probably personally creating most of the business's output.
- Alex recommends continuing to search until a candidate creates a strong "I have to have this person" reaction.
