# A Shift in My Views on Happiness

## Summary
Asked what beliefs he has updated most over the past 12 months, Alex Hormozi names two mindset shifts. The first is a reversal on happiness: he no longer treats being useful and being happy as mutually exclusive, and is now open to holding both at once. The second is a discipline of confining experiences to specific moments — spreading a good moment wide, shrinking a bad one small, and refusing the "second arrow" of added self-inflicted suffering.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex has shifted his view on happiness: he used to dismiss happiness (his prior stance was, in effect, to reject/deprioritize it in favor of usefulness), and now is "open to the idea that I can be both useful and happy" — framing it as no longer a trade-off between the two. (OLD view: usefulness vs. happiness is a trade / happiness deprioritized. NEW view: both are possible simultaneously.)
- As of March 2026, Alex describes his second updated belief as being very specific about moments and not extrapolating a single moment out to days, weeks, months, or years — deliberately spreading one good thing wide and shrinking one bad thing into the smallest possible box.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites avoiding the "second arrow" (added suffering piled on top of an initial painful event) as an active practice in how he sees reality.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Instead of saying [expletive] happiness, I am open to the idea that I can be both useful and happy."

> "I'd say that's probably the biggest one, that it's not a trade between the two."

> "getting very specific about moments and not extrapolating moments to days, weeks, months, years. This one good thing, can I spread that wide and this one bad thing? Can I shrink it into the absolute smallest possible box? And making sure that I don't suffer the second arrow."

> "those are probably the two biggest things that I would say I have been actively working on for how I see reality."
