# How My Childhood Bullying Became a Business Advantage

## Summary

Another speaker describes how childhood social exclusion became an adult ability to read
social dynamics. Alex responds by expanding the frame: painful events can become large
macro wins if the person doubles down on the skill or adaptation those events produced.
He cautions that not every painful event automatically becomes useful, but says time
horizon can transform the interpretation of many experiences.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says negative events can be seen as happening for a person rather than only to a
  person when the time horizon expands.
- He explicitly cautions that some people experience terrible events that simply damage
  them.
- His takeaway is that negative micro-events can become major macro wins if doubled down
  on.
- Some small wins do not offer the same ability to compound into a major win.
- Alex treats the reframe as a time-horizon issue rather than denying the pain of the
  original event.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "They happen for us, not to us."

> "All of the negative things that happen on the micro have the opportunity... to be huge wins in the macro."
