# 13 Rules to Win at Anything

## Summary
Solo talk. Alex recounts a pep talk he gave to a hand-picked cohort — 100 of 500+ applicants selected for a 90-day sprint to start a Skool community — when the group's operator asked him for "words of wisdom" before kickoff. The result is a numbered list of 13 rules, mostly execution-and-mindset discipline for beginners doing the work. The spine of it is his familiar **Rule of 100** (reach out to 100 people, spend 100 minutes on a post, spend $100+ on ads, spend 100 minutes/day making and improving ads) plus the observation that "no one sticks with anything" — the day-60 people report more business than they can handle while most quit by day 4/20/30. Around that he stacks his standard mindset material: excitement fades and *that's* where the work begins; "your work works on you more than you work on it" (you are the product being built); everything is unscalable at the start and that's how you learn every piece and become an expert; the pain of repetition is what forces improvement (citing bodybuilding coach Milos Sarcev's "find the pain / suffer"); complaining makes you "dead to me" (zero-agency, net loss of energy and respect); thousands have already succeeded so "you are not special" — repeat the activities, repeat the outcomes; reframe "X doesn't work" (Facebook ads, cold outbound) into "I'm not good enough to make it work yet"; write down your reasons for sticking with it and revisit them; "business is shockingly simple but surprisingly hard," the hard being consistency; and finally "just win," because the hardship fades from memory disproportionately — he only has a few glimpses of sleeping on the gym floor, and remembers the words he told himself more than the suffering. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames winning as 13 rules he gave a 90-day Skool-launch cohort (100 selected from 500+ applicants).
- As of March 2026, Rule 1: follow instructions; Rule 2: when you hit something you don't understand, Google it first; Rule 3: when you figure it out, post it — others may have struggled too.
- As of March 2026, Rule 4 is the Rule of 100: daily reach out to 100 people, make a post you spent 100 minutes on, spend $100+ on ads, and spend at least 100 minutes making/improving/researching ads; do it consistently and get better and you will see results.
- As of March 2026, most people quit early (day 4/20/30) while day-60 Rule-of-100 practitioners have "more business than they can possibly handle" — "no one sticks with anything."
- As of March 2026, Rule 5: you'll be excited for a week, then excitement ends, and that is where the work begins.
- As of March 2026, Rule 6: "your work works on you more than you work on it" — you are the product being built more than your business is.
- As of March 2026, Rule 7: everything is unscalable in the beginning, and that's the point — it's how you learn every piece; once you know every piece, people call you an expert.
- As of March 2026, Rule 8: the pain of repetition forces you to seek improvement; gaining more output for the same work is skill; he cites bodybuilding coach Milos Sarcev's "find the pain / suffer" — leaning into the "it's not working" pain is what forces the improvement.
- As of March 2026, Rule 9: "if you complain, you are dead to me" — complaining is the lowest-agency act, a net loss of both your improvement-energy and others' respect; people remember only that you complained, not what about.
- As of March 2026, Rule 10: thousands have already succeeded, so "you are not special" — repeat the same activities to repeat the same outcomes; before concluding something "doesn't work," state its objective truth (Facebook's ~$400B/yr ad spend, mass cold-outbound firms) and reframe to "I don't know how to make it work / I'm not good enough yet."
- As of March 2026, Rule 11: write down every reason you're going to stick with it, put it in front of you, and revisit it when you need reminding.
- As of March 2026, Rule 12: "business is shockingly simple but surprisingly hard" — the hard is consistency; the day you don't want to do it or skip is when you learn what hard feels like; learn to endure.
- As of March 2026, Rule 13: just win — the hardship fades and memory disproportionately keeps the good over the bad; he recalls few glimpses of sleeping on the gym floor and remembers the words he told himself more than the experiences.
