# Scaling a $22M Hemp Distributor to $50M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a hemp distributor doing about $22M who is hesitant to expand because of regulatory uncertainty. Alex says regulation is a real concern, but usually provides warning through bills, votes, effective dates, and phase-ins. His advice is to capitalize on the current opportunity until information changes, rather than staying frozen by a possible future. If regulation does change, the distributor can pivot products within the same customer base.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Regulatory risk is a major concern in regulated markets such as hemp and healthcare.
- Even when regulation changes, businesses often get more warning than they think through legislative process and phase-in periods.
- The business should operate with current information until current information changes.
- Staying frozen by uncertainty can last forever and waste the existing opportunity.
- The distributor should keep aggressively expanding while the market exists.
- If regulation changes, the company can pivot to other products through the existing distribution base.
- Trying to guess the future is less useful than changing behavior when facts change.
