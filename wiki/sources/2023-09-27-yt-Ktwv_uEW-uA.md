# "What now?" [Ep 009]

## Summary

A vlog-style episode filmed as Hormozi does a "four-podcast day" tour right after the launch of his second book, `$100M Leads`, intercut with interview clips and personal texture (packing habits, Chick-fil-A order, comedy consumption). Threaded through is his big personal "unlock" — redefining winning as doing the hardest possible work on a few things — plus the introduction of his "one of zero" brand/philosophy and a preview of his next two years of projects (a third book and "one of zero").

## Key claims

- On the `$100M Leads` launch he deliberately used the book's own methods to promote the book: ~137,000 people from ads, ~104,000 from affiliates, ~27,000 affiliates promoting it, plus a customer-referral incentive (get 10 people = two bonus chapters). (self-reported)
- His marketing rule: "say what only you could say, show what only you can show" — one-of-one content; "one of zero" escalates this to doing what no one else *would* do (mathematically 1/0 is undefined; he identifies with work no one else will do).
- His personal breakthrough of the past year was redefining "winning" as putting the maximum sustainable effort into a project — measured against a future version of himself who knows what perfect work looks like; this standard means he can only pursue a few projects.
- He operationalizes amorphous terms to make them useful: patience = "figuring out what to do in the meantime"; love = "how much you're willing to sacrifice to maintain a relationship."
- Environment change beats willpower for changing behavior (cites Vietnam-heroin vs. US-relapse data via `Atomic Habits`).
- Next two years of projects: a third book ("a lot of people are going to make a lot of money from it") and "one of zero"; plus more companies at acquisition.com.
