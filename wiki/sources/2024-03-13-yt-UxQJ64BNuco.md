# The Alex Hormozi Guide to Haters [REVEALED]

## Summary

Solo video in which Hormozi lays out his complete playbook for dealing with
haters, critics, and defamation as a public figure. He presents two mental
frameworks: (1) a three-step response ladder — **ignore, agree, or agree-and-one-up
(self-deprecate)** — and (2) a decision box based on whether the attacker is
bigger or smaller than you and whether the claim is true or a lie. The through-line:
you never beat hate by fighting it, you drown it out by getting louder and continuing
to deliver value; humor and self-mockery neutralize almost any attack, and ~99% of
his own response is simply ignoring and amplifying his own message.

## Key claims

- When attacked, there are three options in escalating order: **1) ignore**,
  **2) agree** ("get in the angry boat with them"), **3) agree + one-up / insult
  yourself better than they can** ("Ninja level"). This handles ~95% of attacks.
- **Ignoring is his all-time favorite** response: it costs the least time and effort,
  and haters are effectively advertising you for free — "when people direct their
  customers towards you, some of them buy."
- Conflict only continues as long as there is an opposing force — "there's only one
  person allowed in the angry boat." Agreeing removes the thing to push against.
- The **Extinction curve**: an unreinforced behavior dies out. If you don't respond
  (be "a rock"), haters escalate a couple times then disappear. "No one keeps
  attacking someone who does not reply."
- **Hate only comes from below**, never from above — if someone hates on you it signals
  they are beneath you and concede that they follow you.
- Decision box by attacker size and claim type: if it's **true, lean into it**; if
  it's **false and they're smaller, ignore**; if it's **false and they're bigger,
  make them seem petty** (deflect/joke). Only reply to people bigger than you.
- **"Never attack back."** You cannot win by attacking; you win by deflection or by
  amplifying. In direct conflict over reputation, even if you win you lose (Pyrrhic
  victory, lost credibility).
- **The pink-elephant / brand-association principle**: repeatedly denying a claim
  ("I'm not X") makes the audience associate you with X and feeds the narrative you're
  trying to disprove. Brand is built on associations.
- **Law of large numbers**: 500 hateful comments against a million-person audience is
  ~0.1%; humans aren't built to process this much feedback, so it feels bigger than it is.
- You only get canceled if you **choose to accept it** or an external power fully
  deplatforms you; short of that, keep amplifying value and negativity shrinks by
  proportion over time.
- **Humor — especially self-mockery — is the one thing that counters hate above all
  else.** "We should always be better at mocking ourselves than anyone else does...
  we have more material."
- Hate is a prerequisite for success (cites Churchill: "You have enemies? Good."). The
  only people who get no hate are invisible / do nothing.
- If people learn they can control your behavior with a button (provoking a response),
  more people press it; refusing to react makes you "very hard to beat."

- "There's only one person allowed in the angry boat, and so what you can do is you
  get in the angry boat with them and you say I know I suck, and then they have to get out."
- "Insult yourself better than they can, and you should know how to do it better than
  they can 'cause you know you better than they do too."
- "You are greater than — hate only comes from below. In my entire life it has never
  come from above."
- "The only way you beat hate is you don't beat hate, you drown it out."
- "If I stand next to something and say I am not a pink elephant, people will, over
  enough time, associate me and the pink elephant."
- "You don't win on logic, you win by force, because you'll never convince them. You
  can make them so small they cease to matter."
- "The one thing that counters hate above everything else is humor. And one of the
  all-time funny things that you can always do is make fun of yourself."
- "Hate is a prerequisite for success... The only people who get no hate are people
  who are invisible."
- "If people know that they can control your behavior by pushing this button to get
  this response from you, more people hit that button... but if they can hit any
  button wherever they want, as hard as they want, and you're going to keep doing you,
  you become very hard to beat."
