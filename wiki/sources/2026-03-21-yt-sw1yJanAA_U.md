# How to Get More Leads in a Local Service Business

## Summary
In this MoreMozi coaching clip, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of an outdoor-lighting business (~$880K revenue, ~40% in the holiday/Christmas-lights season) who struggles to generate leads for permanent lighting during the off-season. Hormozi frames the core decision as either running a seasonal-only operation or keeping the team busy year-round, then diagnoses the owner's Meta-ads lead problem as a creative and offer issue rather than a landing-page one. He coaches the owner toward a stronger, partly-free lead-magnet offer (e.g., lighting a small element like the driveway) and using home-value data to prioritize leads.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues a local service owner faces one "existential decision": be full-time seasonal (like a Halloween store that thrives on ~2 months a year) or equip the existing team with year-round skills; he personally prefers keeping the seasonal business selling year-round.
- As of March 2026, Alex describes the ideal business as one that sells all year and delivers in a short burst: "collect cash, collect commitments" for ~10 months, then earn the living over ~45-60 days of delivery, because it reduces the operation to one focus (marketing and sales) and produces strong cash flow.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that in local markets, results are "very offer driven," so improving the front-end offer matters most.
- As of March 2026, Alex diagnoses that the owner's problem is likely weak ad creative, not landing pages, because permanent lighting is easy to sell visually (before/after of a house in the dark).
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends starting with a "banger" offer and then adding friction/scarcity — e.g., only giving the free element to homes above a certain value, having prospects enter their address, running a Zillow API to rank leads by home value, and prioritizing high-value homes.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises pricing the offer to break even on the first small job (mixing markup between lights and labor), knowing a share of first-buyers will purchase a ~10x-bigger package — "that's just math."
- As of March 2026, Alex references the leads book's framing that a lead magnet can be a trial, one step in a multi-step process, or a third type; he classifies the lighting demo as one step in a multi-step process.
- (Caller claim, unverified) The owner reports doing ~$880K revenue, running the lighting season ~3 months / 45 days, having dropped lawn care to focus on Christmas for better margins, and now selling project-based permanent lighting with the same team, running Meta ads with a "free demo" lead magnet.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The perfect business in the world is you sell all year and then you don't have to deliver for this much. It's an amazing business. You just collect cash all day long. Collect cash, collect commitments, collect cash, collect commitments all year round and then you boom and you basically earn the living over the next 45 or 60 days and then you go back into the sales cycle."

> "The nice thing is in local markets it's very offer driven."

> "I'll prefer to start with a banger offer and then add friction, because the thing is just like you can say I'm going to give this away, but you don't have to give it away to everybody. You can say I only give it away to houses that are over X."

> "You can have people put their address in and then run an API to Zillow, look at home value and then rank order the leads based on value of home and then basically prioritize those leads ahead of time."

> "If I'm struggling to get leads, I just need them to say they want lighting. I don't really care how they say they want lighting. I just need them to say they want it and then the sales process takes care of the rest."

> "You'll just know that one out of two or whatever it is who buy the first thing buy the 10 times bigger package and then that's just math."
