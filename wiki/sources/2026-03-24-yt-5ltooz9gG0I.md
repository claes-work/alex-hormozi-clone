# How Do I Date While Building a Business?

## Summary
In a live Q&A, a caller (a single father named DJ Christopherson, restarting a zero-revenue virtual staffing company) asks Alex Hormozi how to prepare his new long-distance partner for life with a type-A entrepreneur. Hormozi's advice is "try before you buy": have her spend two weeks living his real, unaccommodated life to see whether she genuinely loves how he lives, rather than both people performing and compromising. He frames the most important relationship as the one with your own goals, and argues that testing compatibility early — before years of "acting" — de-risks the whole thing.

## Key claims
- (2026-03-24) Rather than coach a partner on how to tolerate an entrepreneur's lifestyle, invite her to spend ~two weeks living that life fully un-catered-to; if she is thrilled by it, the relationship has a high chance of working with little ongoing effort.
- (2026-03-24) The most important relationship a person has is the one with their own goals, because goals are a reflection of the self; Hormozi says he would not sacrifice that relationship with himself for someone else — stated hypothetically as "especially if I were single" (self-reported / hypothetical framing).
- (2026-03-24) Compromise where neither person gets what they want is a worse target than finding someone who authentically loves how you already live; being yourself requires no pretending and no "putting on a show."
- (2026-03-24) The goal is to get as far forward as possible into what the relationship would look like "if neither of us acted," then behave that way early — discovering incompatibility in two weeks beats discovering it in two years after both people stop acting.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You should give her a try before you buy."

> "Fly out, spend two weeks with me. I'm not going to cater to you. I'm going to live my life. If the way that I live my life, the way this works, this is how it's going to be. If you like that, let's rock and roll. If you don't, I don't want to change."

> "I see the most important relationship that you have as you with your goals, because your goals are really just a reflection of yourself."

> "If being you is enough that she's stoked, then you probably have a good life ahead of you."

> "At least you didn't have to wait two years to find out, after you both stopped acting, that this is how you really are."

> "How do I get as far forward in the relationship as possible of what would it look like if neither of us acted, and then let's just behave that way — and if it still works, that feels pretty good."
