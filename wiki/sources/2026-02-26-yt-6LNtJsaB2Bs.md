# How I'd Sell Physical Products Using TikTok Shop

## Summary

In this short Q&A clip, Hormozi answers a viewer ("Junior") who sells purpose-driven natural-stone jewelry with his wife and asks what content to post to advertise on TikTok. Because the product is highly visual and (he assumes) high-margin, Hormozi recommends selling through **TikTok Shop**, funding **aggressive affiliate commissions** to other TikTokers who repost the product, and doubling the same content onto Instagram via collab posts with influencers. His central point is that a commodity like jewelry sells far better when paired with a **narrative** — the story and meaning behind each piece (where the stone was sourced, why it was designed that way, what each chain/metal does). He illustrates with his own book-launch hat sale and a Dave Ramsey example arguing that you need far less content *variety* than you think: one core framework or product, with variety coming only from the examples you apply it to.

## Key claims

_All claims below are paraphrased from Alex Hormozi unless quoted; dated 2026-02-26._

- For selling physical products that are visual (like jewelry), you should be on **TikTok Shop** — if you're not doing that, you should be. (paraphrase)
- Jewelry likely has very good margins, which means you can afford to pay **aggressive affiliate commissions** to other TikTokers who repost your product. (paraphrase)
- Look for small, cheap items you can give away for free to affiliates/creators to seed reposts — it feels costly ("heavy") but people will be willing to promote it. (paraphrase)
- **Double the same content onto Instagram**, doing as many **collab posts** as possible with other influencers trying on different pieces. (paraphrase)
- The biggest lever is to **tell the narrative / story behind the jewelry** — pair the commodity with ascribed meaning. (paraphrase)
- Analogy: a plain logo t-shirt isn't compelling, but if you tell the story behind why the logo or saying is meaningful, it sells. The same principle applies to jewelry. (paraphrase)
- Concrete content ideas: show the jewelry, show unboxings, show people picking between different pieces, and narrate the story of each — where the stone was sourced, why it was designed that way, what one chain does vs. another, what the metals do. (paraphrase)
- **Don't be afraid to repeat stuff** in your content (stated twice for emphasis). (paraphrase)
- You need **significantly less variety** in your content than you think. (paraphrase)
- Dave Ramsey example: for ~35–40 years his core message has been "spend less than you make / don't go into debt / cut up your credit cards / pay off your loans" (plus his seven baby steps), yet people still tune in daily because they wonder how the single message applies to different situations. (paraphrase)
- Principle: a **single framework, core product, or concept** can carry your content — the variety comes from the **examples** you use, not from changing the message. (paraphrase)

### Biographical / self-reported data point
- At his leads-book launch "two years ago" (i.e., ~2024), he sold "like millions of dollars of hats"; people paid essentially $100 for a hat (bundled with two other books, hat nominally free). He credits spending four or five minutes explaining what the hat means to him — after which far more people bought it than if he'd just asked "do you want to buy a hat?" (self-reported)
