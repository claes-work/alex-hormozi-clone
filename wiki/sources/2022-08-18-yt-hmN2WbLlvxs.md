## Summary

A Gym Launch tutorial, presented by CEO Kale, teaching gym owners a repeatable
system — the "Legion Scrambler" — for generating an endless supply of fresh
Facebook/Instagram lead-generation ads. The method treats an ad as four
interchangeable components (headline, copy, image-or-video, format variations)
that can be recombined to produce many distinct ads without running out of
ideas. Kale walks through several live winning ads (as of July 2022) that were
producing leads for roughly $4–$10 each.

## Key claims

- Ads are built from four components — **headline, copy, image OR video, and a
  fourth interchangeable element** — and swapping any component yields a new,
  fresh ad. This framework is called the **"Legion Scrambler."**
- Gym Launch uses this same framework on both the B2B side (their agency running
  ads for clients) and the B2C side (gym-owner clients' ads).
- **Headline angles** include duration, gender call-out (subject to current
  Facebook rules), curiosity, benefit, status, fear/negative, or simply naming
  the challenge.
- **Copy angles** include short vs. long copy, "free" framing, newness (grand
  opening/reopening, new program, beta test), a stated reason why, benefits,
  status ("so that you can X"), describe-don't-tell, scarcity ("only 8 spots
  left"), urgency ("closing Monday"), and implied authority ("worked with over
  1,000 local residents").
- **A reason why is mandatory** in every ad — "there always has to be a reason
  why you're doing something."
- **Image variations**: contrast/black-and-white, banners, diagonals, group
  pics, selfies, workout pics, people in class, sweating, memes, etc.
- **Video variations**: flash cards, changing background/scenery (in gym, out of
  gym, local landmark), pro camera vs. iPhone — **cell phones actually perform
  better** — and always add subtitles/captions.
- **UGC (user-generated, selfie-style client testimonials) are the best type of
  ad, "hands down."** Be careful with before/after pictures on Facebook/TikTok.
- **Deliberate/select typos can improve performance** — they make the ad feel
  like it came from a real person and read as more reasonable.
- Gym Launch runs "**post ads**" — posting the creative to their page first and
  boosting it, rather than building it in Ads Manager — because it looks more
  organic (no button).
- **Jump cuts** in video keep viewer attention longer.
- Cited results: the shown ads were mostly getting leads at **$4.00–$4.15**,
  with the priciest around $8, all under $10; a B2B video ad was pulling leads
  at ~$15 vs. their typical ~$40 range.
