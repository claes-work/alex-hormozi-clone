# How to Think About Your Own Insignificance Without Losing Your Ambition

## Summary

Alex reflects on a post paraphrasing Richard Dawkins: organisms eventually normalize
back into their environment. He applies the biological image to ambition and identity:
the surrounding world pressures people to dilute themselves and regress to the mean, so
they must fight that natural force. He also uses mortality and bodily insignificance as a
way to lower the perceived stakes of ambition.

## Key claims

- Alex endorses the metaphor that everything around a person tries to make them merge
  with their surroundings.
- He says regression to the mean is natural and inevitable, but still worth fighting.
- Thinking about death and physical insignificance gives him emotional perspective on
  goals that otherwise feel overly important.
- He treats humility-inducing thoughts as a way to exhale rather than as a reason to stop
  striving.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Everything around you will try and force you to dilute yourself."

> "This force is natural and it's inevitable and yet we have to fight it."

> "I could be lunch."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: insignificance as ambition-pressure release; fight regression to the mean.
