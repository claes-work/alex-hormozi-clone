# The Business Model That's So Simple, Anyone Can Try It

## Summary

Alex Hormozi uses a fictional "$10 million trash collecting business" as a running gag to teach the **Core 4** — the four ways any one person can let other people know about their stuff: warm outreach, posting free content, cold outreach, and paid ads. He role-plays each method (a phone call, two content skits, three door-knocks, and a paid ad) in a "game footage" format, then breaks down the tactics play-by-play. The lesson is that customer acquisition is simple and skill-based: anyone can follow a script and get customers by talking to people they know, making content, contacting strangers, and running ads. Framed as beginner/small-business education; no hard product pitch, though it references his `$100M Leads` book.

## Key claims (the business model)

- **The Core 4** = the four advertising methods for one person to reach others: (1) **warm outreach** (talk one-to-one to people you know), (2) **posting free content** (one-to-many to people who know you), (3) **cold outreach** (talk one-to-one to strangers), (4) **paid ads** (one-to-many to strangers). These are 4 of "8 ways in total."
- **Warm outreach**: pull up your phone contacts, offer your service **for free** to 5–10 people in exchange for a review, and ask for referrals via a **three-way text intro**.
- **Free work makes money four ways**: the free person can (1) become a paying customer, (2) leave a review that gets you a customer, (3) send you a friend/referral, or (4) give you feedback that makes you better long-term ("Secret Door Number Four").
- Frame free work as *"you're helping me out"* — not as putting the customer in your debt. You have no reputation/track record yet, so giving it away free is correct.
- **No-based questions** convert better than yes-based questions: ask "would you be opposed to...", "would it be a huge inconvenience..." because people more readily say no; you get the yes by having them say no.
- **Content = hook, retain, reward**. First **3 seconds are the most important** (≈80% decide whether to keep watching there). Retain with lists, steps, or stories in a give-step-then-explain-why cycle. Reward = something funny/shareable or a call to action.
- Two limits of warm outreach: only so much **time** (it's just you) and only so many **people you know** (~1,000 contacts = ~10 days at 100/day).
- **Cold outreach** channels: email, phone, DM, SMS, voicemail, door-knocking. Solve three problems: get contacts (**build a list** via software / brokers / DIY scraping of Yelp, Google Maps, Facebook groups, Reddit), what to say (**script + offer**, and **BFV = Big Fast Value**), and not enough replies (**volume**, via automation + collaboration).
- **Name-dropping leverage**: give one neighbor (the "Johnsons") free service, then use them as social proof at every other door to build instant trust.
- **Once you get the yes, stop talking** — everything said after the yes only raises the risk they reverse it.
- **Paid ads** = hook + meat/retain + call to action (like content but always ends with a CTA). Use **call-outs** (visual or auditory, e.g. "Las Vegas homeowners") before the hook. Agitate multiple **pain points** because different ones hit different people. The **person and setting in the ad** determine which avatar you attract more than anything; put multiple/different people in ads to multiply reach.
- **Guarantee** placement is flexible (beginning, middle, or end of the ad). Example offer: miss even one pickup and you get 3 free months.
- Selling is a base life skill (he cites Bezos, Gates, Cuban, Buffett starting in door-to-door / face-to-face selling). "No is the price of yes."
- **Reframe views as room size**: 17 views = a full classroom you'd have been thrilled to teach — don't compare to million-view clips.
