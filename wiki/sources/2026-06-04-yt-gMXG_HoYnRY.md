# He's at $1.2M Solo and Walking Away From It

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a real-estate advisory owner moving from Seattle to Phoenix while serving high-level executives and business owners. Alex frames the go-to-market problem as a high-trust, local-market acquisition problem: keep doing direct outreach, referrals, events, and face-to-face networking, but also test local paid ads because local services can sometimes profitably concentrate multiple channels in a single market.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- For executive-level real-estate advisory, outreach, referrals, events, and face-to-face work remain the primary trust-building motions.
- Local markets are an exception where a business can often run several channels at once because the geography concentrates the target audience.
- A small local ad budget can be justified even when the main channel is relationship-based, especially when the offer is specialized and high-value.
- The offer should be stated around the buyer's concrete problem, such as tenant or buyer representation and lease negotiation, rather than generic real estate help.
- Proof should show before/after economics. Dollar amounts usually communicate better than percentages when the buyer cares about savings or value captured.
