# Be Careful Who You Take Advice From

## Summary
Short solo clip on **advice filters**. Alex's core rule: judge advice by the life of the person giving it — "if you don't like their life, don't listen to what they say." If everyone around you has a life you don't want, don't take any of their advice; you have to "put blinders on." He warns of a lonely middle path: while climbing, you're not yet successful enough for the next tier of friends but already too different from your old ones — and the upside of that loneliness is time, which is exactly what a beginner needs to learn skills (skills → money → more money). He then lays out his sequence for someone broke who wants out: (1) **own that everything about your life is your fault** — many reject this, and he flatly tells those people they're right and they'll stay poor; (2) **use what you have** — circumstances, connections, money, or even anger, shame, and pain — rather than waiting for the passion/purpose/resources you wish you had; (3) expect that once you start taking action, the people around you will "collapse and try to pull you down," so protect yourself by **increasing your own approval of self and decreasing how much their opinion matters** — be proud of yourself for the step, and actively look at their lives and ask "do I want what they have?" If no, don't listen to them. Then keep walking, no matter how painful, and never stop. Ends with the standard acquisition.com/roadmap ($100M Scaling Roadmap) promo.
