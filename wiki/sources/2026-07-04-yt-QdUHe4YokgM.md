# Do I Keep Selling Pizza or Sell the Software I Built?

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Angelo, a pizza operator who runs three stores and built an internal "Pizza OS" operating system that other operators now want. Alex lays out multiple viable paths: use the tool as a proprietary edge to buy and operate more pizza stores, build a franchise-light / revenue-share model around the tool, or become a full software founder. His core advice is that either path can work, but the founder should understand that at scale all paths converge into the same people-and-leadership problems; if he chooses software, he should sell the stores and focus fully out of respect for focused competitors.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Angelo** - the coachee. His pizza stores, revenue, margins, tool, and goals are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A proprietary operating system can be used as an edge for a roll-up or chain: buy pizza stores cheaply, improve them with the tool, and build a sellable operating business.
- The same tool can also support a B2B model, such as free adoption plus revenue share from stores that process through it.
- Full software is a different life than operating pizza stores; the founder becomes a software founder, not a pizza operator.
- Either opportunity could make the founder as much money as he wants if he executes well.
- "The view's going to be the same": at sufficient scale, pizza and software both become people businesses with heads of marketing, sales, IT, legal, and the same leadership issues.
- Different feels fun because it is new, but mature businesses converge in management complexity.
- If the founder chooses the software path, Alex recommends selling the pizza stores so all attention goes to the new company.
- A founder should not expect to beat focused competitors while competing with one arm tied behind his back.
