# Why I Read 15 Books in 2 Weeks to Solve One Problem

## Summary

Alex says he grows by identifying the current constraint, consuming heavily around that
constraint, and taking violent action until it is no longer the constraint. He gives the
example of starting a family office and reading 15 books in two weeks on family-office
structures, investment theses, and holding companies. He emphasizes consistency, volume,
and focus as hard-to-see but decisive separators.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice)

- Alex says he did everything and had little ego about trying things and listening.
- Self-awareness is valuable because it helps someone choose advice and resources that
  fit their current skills and constraints.
- Alex says he is maniacal about the current limiting constraint.
- When starting a family office, he read 15 books in two weeks and did little else.
- If sales were the issue, Alex says he would read many books, watch videos, hire someone
  good at sales, and get real-time review until sales was no longer the issue.
- People often either misidentify the constraint, take insufficient action, or both.
- Commitment and focus are both forms of eliminating alternatives.
- Consistency and volume are hard to understand unless one witnesses them over time.
- Alex says his operation puts out 35,000-plus pieces of content a year.
- The amount of volume required is often far more than people are prepared for.
