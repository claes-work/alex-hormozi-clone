# Rate of Progress Matters More Than Where You Start

## Summary

Alex argues that starting advantages such as money, connections, appearance, and
intelligence can be overcome by an insatiable desire to improve. He rejects fairness as
a useful frame: the starting point is outside a person's control, while the rate of
learning and behavioral change can be influenced.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says sustained desire to improve can beat substantial starting
  advantages.
- He frames life as a race in which people begin at different points but move at
  different speeds; focusing on the starting gap distracts from controllable progress.
- He defines education as a change in behavior and intelligence as the rate of learning.
- He says defining oneself by what happened to oneself, rather than what one makes
  happen, preserves a losing identity.
- He recommends removing fairness and "should" from one's decision frame and asking
  only what needs to be done.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The starting point you can't control, but the rate of progress you can."

> "Education is a change in behavior."

> "Intelligence is rate of learning."
