# How wealthy people avoid paying taxes..my new plan (2021-11-26)

## Summary

Hormozi explains (2021) how business owners can pull cash out of a company tax-free by
borrowing against it (debt) instead of selling equity — the same buy-borrow strategy he
says the wealthiest people and public-company CEOs use. He frames it as a de-risking
tool: refinance a growing business at intervals, take out loans (not taxable income),
and redeploy the cash while still owning and growing the asset. He presents it
explicitly as his "new plan" going forward.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- (2021) Selling a business is far less glamorous than the fantasy: transition
  documents, earn-outs, seller financing, required equity rollover, transaction and
  financing costs, and ~20% tax — the cash "X" you keep is often far below enterprise
  value.
- (2021, self-reported) He has exited six businesses and says the reality of an exit is
  "not nearly as inspiring" as the story of one.
- (2021) The wealthiest people he knows (10-figure) buy and build and mostly *don't*
  sell; entrepreneurs usually want to sell to escape pain, so the better move is to fix
  the pain (e.g. hire one or two people) rather than "kill a mosquito with a cannon."
- (2021) Core mechanism: a loan against your business is not income, so it is tax-free.
  You can take out (e.g.) a 70% loan against a $1M business, keep 100% ownership, and
  still grow both the business and the redeployed loan proceeds.
- (2021) Worked example: a $700K loan against a growing $1M business (heading to $2M),
  proceeds redeployed to grow to ~$2M, leaves net worth ~$3.3M vs. ~$2M without — and
  vs. ~$560K net after a taxed cash sale keeping 20%.
- (2021) Methods named: minority or majority debt, and dividend recapitalization; a
  privately held business can "refinance at regular intervals" like an apartment
  building as its value grows.
- (2021) This is how public-company CEOs live — they rarely sell equity; they live off
  asset-based loans against stock that compounds tax-free, then refinance later once the
  asset is worth more, effectively getting the money at a discount (no income tax).
- (2021, self-reported) A PE managing partner ("Andy") told him some of his companies
  have "negative leverage" (cash on the balance sheet) and that he should take loans
  against equity to redeploy it.
- (2021, self-reported) Acquisition.com is a portfolio of companies doing about
  $85M/year.
