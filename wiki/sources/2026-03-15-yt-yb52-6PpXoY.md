# Helping a Medical Practice Scale

## Summary
Live consulting clip. The owner of a local medical practice (HRT — hormone replacement therapy) nearing $3M revenue asks the "most overlooked operational system" to add leverage in scaling, and the single hire to implement it. Alex refuses to answer abstractly and instead diagnoses the business live through his standard sequence: **local vs. national → primary lead source → CAC vs. LTV → capacity → what breaks when you double.** The numbers: local, walk-in; leads roughly 60% referral / 30% Meta ads (client also runs Google/AdWords); LTV ~$4k; ~$25k/month ad spend yielding ~150 new patients (~$225 CAC), so an LTV:CAC of ~20:1 with good margins. The client already has spare square footage and can absorb 100–200 more patients/month — i.e. can double immediately — and nothing breaks if they do. Alex's first move is therefore blunt: **just spend twice as much money**; the real constraint is the owner's self-imposed mental cap (~$1,000/day ad spend). But the client's own instinct — that efficiency, not more spend, is the lever — leads Alex to the deeper diagnosis. He reframes to the one thing that matters long-term: **keep people forever.** Churn is ~40–50% annually (they keep only ~50–60% of patients per year), which is severe for a product as sticky as HRT ("hard to get off... once you're on you kind of stay on"). Because the practice is **local**, high churn burns through the local reputation/reputation pool. So the fix in order is **churn first, then the sales motion** — expectations set across marketing, the sales call, and top-of-funnel offers, plus the "handshake" between funnel steps and key activation points. The hire: someone from the **software / customer-success world** to build a customer-success pathway and map the patient journey, backed by a bit of tech stack to automate it. Payoff math: cutting churn from ~50%/yr to ~20% or less roughly doubles the business on its own within ~2.5 years doing nothing else, and disproportionately grows profit. He cites **We Whiten** (a teeth-whitening chain they own) where doubling lifetime revenue per customer produced 2.4x revenue and 7.5x profit per location. On the growth path, he'd expand to 4–5 local locations before going nationwide, reverse-engineering "forever stick." Notes the owner came through Gym Launch — "you learned how to market and sell, you just didn't learn how to product." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (excluded from claims).
