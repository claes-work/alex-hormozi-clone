# How to Keep A-Players Without Just Paying More

## Summary

Alex distinguishes attracting A-players from keeping A-player performance. Money gets
talent in the door, but culture keeps talent because real-time social reinforcement is
more frequent than financial rewards. He argues that high performers want to be on winning
teams where the culture reinforces winning behavior in the moment.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-18, Alex says money attracts talent, but culture keeps talent.
- He says financial incentives matter as a minimum bar, but are too infrequent to create
  the strongest day-to-day behavior change.
- He uses addiction/reinforcement latency as the operating principle: behavior changes
  faster when the reinforcement happens immediately.
- He says a team of A-players can self-manage because members reinforce one another for
  behaving like A-players.
- He says winners want to win more than they want to make money, so being on a winning
  team can be more motivating than high pay on a losing team.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Money attracts talent. Culture keeps talent."

> "Latency beats intensity every day and twice on Sunday."

> "Money is what will get them in the door but winning is what keeps them there."
