# Helping an Online Fitness Coach Get Customers Through Content

## Summary

Live Q&A / coaching exchange from the @MoreMozi channel. An online fitness coach
(the questioner — NOT Alex) explains he sells online fitness programs to
Spanish-speaking customers, has been stuck at ~$300K/year "since the pandemic,"
can produce viral/engaging content but lacks a framework to convert views into
sales. His funnel: ManyChat lead gen → landing page → low-ticket digital product
(~$30/month continuity, ~$140 LTV), plus retargeting ads. He resists high-ticket
(has real estate as his high-ticket vehicle) and resists calling buyers to upsell.

Alex diagnoses two things. First, the conversion mechanics: use a **sorting
question** in the chat funnel to separate freebie-seekers from buyers ("Do you
want the free thing or do you want lasting results?"). Second, and primary — the
coach's real constraint is **advertising volume**. Getting lots of views is
worthless if the viewers aren't buyers; viral views that don't produce buyers are
a vanity problem. Alex tells him 30 ads/month is "nothing" and to scale to ~100
ads/week (videos and images combined). The organic pieces that actually generate
buyers should be repurposed into ads with a direct CTA driving to the funnel.
Alex also runs an impromptu audience poll on his own four YouTube content formats
(cash cows, honest life/mindset, live-stream Q&A, direct-to-camera training) —
"partially just for me."

## Key claims (2026-02-16, paraphrased unless quoted; Alex-attributed)

- ★ **Sorting question in the chat funnel.** When someone comments to get a free
  lead magnet, reply with a question that separates intent — e.g. "Are you here
  for the free thing or do you want lasting results?" Many will self-select into
  "I want help / lasting results," and those are the ones to push toward the
  offer. (Alex)
- **Views mean little if the viewers aren't buyers.** High view counts on viral
  content are near-worthless when the audience it attracts is not the audience
  that purchases; the content that gets the most views is often not the content
  buyers watch. (Alex)
- **His actual constraint is advertising, not content.** If the conversion process
  is already automated and working, the single constraint on the business is ad
  volume. (Alex)
- **30 ads/month is far too few.** Alex calls it "nothing" and recommends scaling
  to ~100 ads/week — videos and images combined — to "make way more." (Alex)
- **Repurpose proven-organic winners into ads.** Take the organic pieces that
  actually generate buyers (not just views), add a more direct CTA at the end, and
  drive them through the funnel. (Alex)
- **Keep making content anyway.** Scaling ads doesn't mean stopping content —
  do both. (Alex)
- **Ad performance decaying over ~a month is normal.** When the coach notes ads
  are profitable "maybe for a month or less," Alex responds "that's how ads work"
  — implying continual fresh creative is required. (Alex)
- **Consider a call-and-upsell to existing buyers.** Alex probes whether the coach
  would phone buyers of the low-ticket product to offer something more expensive;
  the coach declines (prefers real estate as his high-ticket). Alex presents this
  as a straightforward path to more revenue. (Alex, as a suggestion)
