# Don't Open a Paid Community. Do This Instead

## Summary

Alex advises an Etsy seller with a 320-member free community to convert the existing
community into a free trial instead of starting a second paid community. He says a
separate paid group creates management work and can drain the best members out of the
free group, weakening the original asset. He explicitly frames the advice as reputation-
driven because Skool would benefit from users opening more communities.

## Key claims

- If a free community is already engaged and clearly provides value, flipping it into a
  free trial can be better than opening a second paid community.
- A second paid community adds operating complexity.
- Pulling the strongest members into a separate paid group can cause the free group to die.
- Alex says his reputation matters more than the platform incentive to recommend opening
  more communities.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Just flip it to a free trial."

> "My reputation matters more to me."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: convert engaged free community to free trial instead of splitting into free/paid groups.
