# The Pricing Rule That 5x'd My Profit Overnight

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains a pricing-tier rule for upsells: each higher tier should be roughly 5x to 10x the price of the prior tier, with the expectation that a small percentage of customers will buy it. The logic is that spending power is extremely concentrated, and the top tiers often contribute disproportionate profit. He illustrates the rule with simple subscription math and with Acquisition.com's own advisory ladder, then advises founders to add tiers one at a time and to start as high on the ladder as they can operationally support.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A small share of customers has far more spending power than the rest, so businesses under-earn when they price only for the median buyer.
- A higher tier should usually be 5x to 10x the price of the prior tier, and a rough expectation is that about 20% of the prior tier can qualify/take it.
- A new tier should be large enough to add a full additional block of revenue; otherwise, the operational complexity may not be worth it.
- Profit contribution often concentrates at the top because the base tier covers overhead and premium tiers drop more dollars to the bottom line.
- Buyers with larger net worth can be less demanding relative to the transaction size than low-dollar buyers who are spending a larger share of their resources.
- Add tiers one at a time because the full ladder requires operational capability.
- Starting higher can be easier to operationalize and stronger for brand position than starting at the lowest tier.
