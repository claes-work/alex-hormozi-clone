# Helping a $2.8M Catering Biz Hit $8M (Seasonal Problem)

## Summary

Alex coaches a premium catering owner who sees seasonality as a constraint. He says the
seasonality is predictable volatility, not true risk, and therefore may not need a new
business model. If the current model is profitable year-round, the higher-return move
is to do more of what works, especially PPC and Meta, rather than chase an adjacent
corporate-delivery business.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-06)

- Alex says seasonality can be a feature of an industry rather than a bug.
- He distinguishes volatility from risk: volatility changes over time, but risk is
  uncertainty that cannot be planned.
- He says predictable cycles can be planned around.
- He says a profitable business with stronger and weaker months may not have a real
  problem to solve.
- He says the owner should focus on doubling what already works rather than solving
  seasonality.
- He recommends doubling PPC and cracking Meta ads rather than starting a new corporate
  catering line.
- He says theory of constraints is about focus and saying no to distractions.
- He says competitors can stay distracted by shiny objects while the owner keeps
  executing the current model.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "This is a feature not a bug."

> "Volatile but not risky."

> "If you can double the business doing one thing, why do four?"
