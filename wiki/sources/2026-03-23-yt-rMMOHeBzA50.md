# Helping a Marketing Agency Get to $1M/Month

## Summary
In this live coaching exchange, Alex Hormozi advises a marketing-agency owner who sells services to two unrelated avatars — restaurants and chauffeur/transportation companies — and wants to grow from ~$1M/year to $1M/month. The owner reports much cheaper customer acquisition on the chauffeur side ($30–50/client) than the restaurant side (~$600), but only two weeks of data. Hormozi counsels waiting roughly a month to get real LTV-to-CAC before acting, warns that easy-to-acquire customers can turn out to be low-value headaches, and frames the ultimate choice as focusing on one avatar rather than running two entirely different ones.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues it is unwise to run two entirely different avatars; scaling to $1M/month likely requires giving up one (here, roughly half the small-business restaurant clients).
- As of March 2026, Alex advises the owner to make no changes yet and let the chauffeur experiment run about a month, so real LTV-to-CAC data exists before deciding.
- As of March 2026, Alex warns that a low acquisition cost alone is misleading: a segment can look like "printing money" early, then reveal low LTV and high servicing pain (his example: personal trainers who acquire cheaply but "have no money and are such a pain in the ass").
- As of March 2026, Alex's decision rule: if the new (chauffeur) avatar's LTV matches the existing business, it can make sense to sunset the other business and consolidate onto the cheaper-to-acquire, focused avatar.
- Caller claim (unverified): the owner does ~$1M/year, has ~35 restaurant clients mostly from referrals, and acquires chauffeur clients for $30–50 versus ~$600 for restaurant clients, via a low-ticket case-study offer ($1 first month tripping into $197/month, ascending to agency services).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "I don't want you to actually make any changes, which is not normal for me to say right now, because we need to see this play out for like a month-ish."

> "If I ran an ad for personal trainers, I would have my gym LTVs and see my personal trainer acquisition cost and be like, oh my god I'm going to print money. And then you quickly see, oh my god, personal trainers suck and have no money and are such a pain in the ass. Why bother?"

> "It's unwise to have two entirely different avatars."

> "The price of the business that you want at a million a month is giving up the very small business, half of the small business that you have today. If you're willing to pay that price, then it's an easy decision."
