# Why Warren Buffett Calls This Industry a Scam

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the same nonprofit health-benefits business covered in a nearby clip. The founder sells savings-based benefits to employers and relies on affiliates such as CPAs and financial advisors. Alex reframes the pitch from a suspicious large-dollar savings claim to a mechanism-based nonprofit margin explanation, then recommends focusing sales efforts on centers of influence, improving the weak close rate, and integrating into affiliate onboarding.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When a pitch sounds too good to be true, the seller should explain the mechanism that makes the claim believable.
- For affiliate-driven B2B sales, the center of influence can be the true acquisition unit.
- The seller should build sales messaging, onboarding, and metrics around acquiring and activating those partners.
- Launches can generate internal case studies; integrations can make the offer part of every partner's future customer flow.
- A 10% close rate on a strong offer suggests the sales process itself may be a high-leverage constraint.
- Fixing sales process could materially grow the business before adding more top-of-funnel complexity.
