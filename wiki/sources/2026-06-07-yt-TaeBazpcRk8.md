# He Can Double His Business Tomorrow and Won't

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a local HRT / medical practice approaching $3M in revenue. The owner asks which operational system and hire would add the most leverage. Alex first notes that the practice has strong CAC:LTV and apparent capacity to double by spending more, but the owner reveals high annual churn. Alex then agrees the better constraint is retention: fix customer success, expectation-setting, activation points, and the journey before pouring more demand into a leaky back end.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A business with strong CAC:LTV and available capacity can often double by spending more, unless retention is leaking badly.
- Local medical practices have to avoid burning reputation by scaling acquisition into poor retention.
- For sticky products like HRT, 40%-50% annual churn is a serious product/customer-success issue.
- Cutting churn can double the business over time without increasing front-end acquisition.
- Retention improvements can multiply profit more than revenue because much of the extra revenue falls to the bottom line.
- A customer-success hire from software can help map the journey, activation points, expectations, conversations, and tech stack.
