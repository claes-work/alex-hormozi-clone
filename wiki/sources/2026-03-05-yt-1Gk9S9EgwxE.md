# You Need to Learn How to Manage Your Time

## Summary
Short solo clip on time management as the prerequisite for money management. Alex's core claim: **"If you never learn to manage your time, you're never going to learn to manage your money."** Everyone gets an equal amount of time each day, so the people who get the best returns on their money are those who best invest their time. His mental model: **money is "an encapsulated version of time"** — since everyone trades time for money, each dollar represents a certain amount of time for each person, and the wealthier you get, the *less* time a given denomination represents (for one person $100 = 4 hours; for a wealthier person $100 = 4 minutes). His actual method is a **constant appraisal of the calendar**: look at what's on it and ask, "What things do I have here that I could trade my time for for more money?" He frames every use of time as a transaction with an opportunity cost — you *can* spend time with your girlfriend, but there's a transaction cost to it; as long as you'd willingly make the trade knowing the cost, "trade it all day." **Bad trades** are the ones you'd reject once you actually price them out ("that's a bad deal"). The compounding principle: if you keep **"trading up"** how much time you invest for what it earns you, your income rises year after year. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (free $100M scaling roadmap quiz + optional call). Note: this clip is framed explicitly around *making money*, not life balance overall.
