# Helping a Software Business Break Into Enterprise Sales

## Summary

Alex coaches a Theory of Constraints software business moving from perpetual licenses to
SaaS and considering enterprise site licenses. He says the product sounds like an
enterprise consultant product and recommends whale hunting, choosing who to sell to,
charging the biggest plausible number, and making the legal/contract friction worth the
price.

## Key claims

- Alex says the company first needs clarity on who it sells to, how much it charges, and
  how it will get those customers.
- He says if the product already has enterprise use cases, the company should go whale
  hunting rather than minnow hunting.
- He says enterprise buyers such as large contractors may not even believe a very low
  price.
- He recommends saying the biggest number the seller can think of because price can move
  down more easily than up.
- He says legal and procurement friction is not worth it for small contracts, but can be
  worth it for very large annual contracts.
- He says the company may be one enterprise deal away from fixing cash flow.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Go whale hunting rather than go minnow hunting."

> "You can always go down, can't go up."

> "One deal away from fixing our cash flow."
