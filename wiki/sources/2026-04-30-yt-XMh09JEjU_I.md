# Helping an Online Education Business Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches a Ukrainian crypto-education company doing over $1M per month. He says the
simple first growth lever is ascension in the existing market. For new geographies, he
diagnoses the likely issue as cultural and language mismatch in the marketing, not a lack
of exotic funnel types. He also labels the business a cash-flow media-arbitrage business
rather than one with durable enterprise value: growth comes from increasing LTV, improving
creative and funnels, and repeating many small optimizations.

## Key claims

- For a profitable education business, Alex says adding an upsell/ascension path in the
  existing market is the simplest high-likelihood growth lever.
- When expanding to Poland or other markets, Alex thinks marketing language, analogies,
  visuals, and cultural norms are likely the conversion gap.
- He does not recommend focusing on people who are not on Google or Meta for a crypto
  education offer because likely buyers are already online.
- Alex frames the business as a cash-flow arbitrage model: buy media, monetize gated
  media, and scale as long as LTV exceeds acquisition cost.
- He says this type of business is unlikely to have durable 10-year enterprise value and
  is more of a "smash and grab" opportunity.
- Growth comes from raising LTV, improving ads, split-testing funnel pieces, and doing
  many small improvements rather than expecting one order-of-magnitude tactic.
- Alex gives a creative production heuristic of about 50 pieces of creative per $10,000
  per day of ad spend.
