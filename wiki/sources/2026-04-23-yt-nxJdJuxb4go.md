# How to Know When to Fire a Vendor/Employee

## Summary

Alex says vendor values alignment matters, though less than for full-time hires, and
that a vendor who does not match Acquisition.com's standards will stop being a vendor
quickly. He frames vendors as fractional capacity for work the business should
eventually understand or own, used either to fill a temporary skill gap or to add speed
when internal headcount is constrained.

## Key claims

- Alex says values alignment matters for vendors, but not as much as it does for
  full-time hires.
- He says Acquisition.com expects vendors to meet standards for excellence,
  professionalism, timeliness, and quality.
- He says growing businesses should continually ask whether they would hire the same
  vendor again if starting over.
- He says vendors usually supply either missing expertise or temporary bandwidth.
- He recommends building work in-house where possible once the company understands the
  function and can recruit for it.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You will outgrow vendors."

> "If I were to start over again would I still bring this person on?"

> "We try to build it in-house if we can."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: vendor values bar; vendors as fractional skill-gap or bandwidth; start-over appraisal; build in-house when possible.
