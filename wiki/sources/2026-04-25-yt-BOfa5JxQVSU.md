# Some People Just Don't Hate Their Current Life Enough

## Summary

Alex says early change often came from hating his current existence, not from a clear
positive vision. He argues negative motivation is underrated: anger, sadness, and pain can
be used as fuel rather than letting them use the person. He also explains why he does not
answer "do you believe in yourself?" with a simple yes: he bases confidence on evidence
that he repeats activities he has repeated before, not on charisma or affirmation.

## Key claims

- Alex says some people do not hate their current existence enough to create change.
- He says people need either a strong reward they believe in or a clear recognition that
  their current life is painful enough to leave.
- He frames change as movement away from pain or toward pleasure.
- He says negative motivation is criticized too much and can be useful if directed.
- He describes early anger as something he wielded in a direction.
- He says he did not need to get the first move exactly right; he wanted to be
  directionally correct and adjust.
- He avoids saying he believes in himself as a charisma statement; instead, he says he has
  evidence that he is likely to repeat activities he has done in the past.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I hated my current existence."

> "I think negative motivation is poo-pooed too much."

> "If you are angry use it. If you are sad use it."

> "I just want to be directionally correct."

> "I have a high likelihood of repeating activities that I've done in the past."
