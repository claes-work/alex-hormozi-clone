# It just takes work...

## Summary
Short interview clip (with an off-camera host asking questions) on work ethic and why Alex out-works people. Asked whether he ever dislikes work, Alex says he works a lot and cites a Joe Rogan clip (relayed via Andy Frisella) about "psychopaths" who just keep working and will beat you at whatever game they choose to grind. He sorts people into three tiers: most people **do nothing**; a cohort of **optimizers** who want the most output for the least input (max leverage — "a great goal"); and a handful of **maximizers** who accept that "diminishing returns are still returns." He uses the Olympics analogy: first vs. second place is a tenth of a second on the clock but "a lot" in reality — and he wants to win. He frames both himself and Leila as products of the personal-development space: he wants to be the best version of himself, and sees **entrepreneurship as one of the best vehicles for personal development** because it gives real-time feedback (if you suck at content/books/marketing, you find out fast). After selling the company and being "done financially," his mission became making real business education accessible for everyone — and to separate himself from the "swamp" of business educators he has to have **proof**: he's worth more and is better at the game they claim to be good at, and he backs it up with volume. Closes on his core lesson: he's been rewarded for working more than other people, and once he realized he could simply do more, he learned that doing more tends to make him win more.
