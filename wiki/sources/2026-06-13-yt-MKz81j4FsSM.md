# Cut the Lower-Margin Customer and Just Win

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a cleaning business doing about $1.2M and aiming for $3M in 18 months. The owner serves both homeowners and vacation-rental hosts. Alex compares the two customer groups and tells him to focus on homeowners because that segment is already the larger, higher-LTV:CAC, broader market with less price sensitivity. He frames the vacation-rental side as a second business that should be allowed to fade rather than splitting effort.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A business should keep paying attention to **LTV:CAC** when choosing which customer segment to prioritize.
- If one segment is larger, has higher LTV:CAC, contains more available customers, and is less price-sensitive, the answer is to focus there.
- Serving homeowners and vacation-rental hosts can behave like **two businesses**, because the customer journey and buying criteria differ.
- Small companies should usually avoid attacking multiple verticals until they are much larger.
- Growth can happen slowly and then suddenly; Alex describes business as "nothing, nothing, nothing, all at once."
- The practical move is to say no to the weaker segment "for now," not necessarily forever.
