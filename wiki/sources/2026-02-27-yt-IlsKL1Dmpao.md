# "'I Do $200K/Month Selling STEM Cells. I Need More Qualified Leads'"

## Summary
Live "Hormozi hotline" coaching clip. The caller is a medical doctor running a brick-and-mortar men's sexual-function clinic in Fredericksburg, Virginia (stem cells, shockwave therapy, hormone/peptide replacement), doing ~$200k/month. Services run $15k–$25k; ~70% of patients travel to the clinic (many international — Hong Kong, UK). All leads come from a 150,000-subscriber YouTube channel plus word of mouth, via an application funnel that books a call with the sales team, which converts at 65%. His stated constraint: not enough *qualified* leads who can afford the services.

Alex's diagnosis: **the conversion process is not the constraint — lead volume/quality from content is.** At 65% close rate, the funnel is fine; focus 100% on making more and *better* YouTube content. He frames two paths — more content vs. better content — and, because the caller is an experienced YouTuber already at ~10k views/video (with ~10 videos near a million), argues the bigger wins are on **"better rather than more."** Prescriptions: (1) do the "five CTAs" throughout each video (caller was at four, now five — an easy ~20–25% lift); (2) pick thumbnail/headline **packaging from proven outliers** (mentions a tool "1of10.com") rather than what the creator personally finds interesting; (3) match thumbnail + headline + scripted introduction to those outliers; (4) script the intro **both verbally and visually** — use the physical setting (lab coat, tools, machines, anatomical props) to set the frame; (5) structure the intro with **proof, promise, plan** (optionally add pain) — a sentence for each P, plus a picture giving a visual roadmap to aid retention.

On the caller's worry that pivoting content toward high-ticket "executive" clients tanked views (from thousands to hundreds), Alex's rule: **never threaten the core business with a test.** Keep the two proven videos/week, and *add* a third experimental video for the new high-ticket angle — so you guarantee more volume while safely testing. Private-jet advertising goes in the same "experimental bucket." Ads are the next lever after packaging, but this business is so trust-driven that organic YouTube is the right primary engine. His ambition for the caller: grow toward a million subscribers / 100k–250k views per video, and **"repeat your winners."**
