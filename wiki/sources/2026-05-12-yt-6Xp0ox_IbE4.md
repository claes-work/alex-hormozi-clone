# $250M CEO Explains How to Build A Brand in 2026

## Summary

Alex advises an e-commerce operator that more products and AI influencer content do not
create a brand. He distinguishes arbitrage businesses from brands: arbitrage buys clicks
for less than it sells, while a brand creates trusted distribution, repeat purchase, and
premium associations. His recommended path is to use current cash flow to fund a better
product, recruit two or three premium human associations, align all content to a narrow
aspirational avatar, and increase email revenue.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex says the caller is lacking a brand, not more products.
- He says premium associations, not product proliferation, create brand value.
- He says AI influencers can create cheap clicks, but they do not carry trust or
  influence.
- He recommends recruiting two or three premium human influencers who embody the desired
  avatar and can transfer trust.
- He says a strong product is necessary because reputable people will not promote a poor
  product long-term.
- He says the caller's current model resembles arbitrage: buy clicks for X, sell for Y,
  and keep the spread.
- He says brands are valuable because they build a trusted distribution base that returns
  to buy more products.
- He says revenue retention, not subscription arbitrage or one-time bundle expansion,
  should be the North Star.
- He recommends considering a new brand if premium people see the current brand as a
  scam or reputational risk.
- He says brand messaging must be narrow; trying to represent everyone makes the brand
  stand for no one.
- He says aspirational brands prove an extreme ideal so the customer discounts it down
  to a believable personal use case.
- He says e-commerce should email more, with stronger flows and regular emails, and that
  mature e-commerce can get a large share of revenue from email.
