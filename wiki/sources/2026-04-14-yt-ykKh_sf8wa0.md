# How I Think About Money

## Summary

Responding to a hostile comment about wealth, Alex describes money as neutral potential
that amplifies what its holder chooses to do. He argues that condemning an outcome one
privately wants can reinforce behavior that prevents achieving it.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex describes money as potential rather than inherently good or bad.
- He says money can fund hospitals and skyscrapers as readily as harmful movements; the
  holder's use supplies its moral direction.
- He argues that attacking people for having money resembles mocking fit people while
  wanting to become fit and can make the desired outcome harder to pursue.
- He treats discomfort with another person's wealth as evidence about the critic's own
  beliefs around money.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Money is just potential."

> "Whatever you do with it is a dictation of whatever you're going to amplify."

> "It's just potential. That's all it is."
