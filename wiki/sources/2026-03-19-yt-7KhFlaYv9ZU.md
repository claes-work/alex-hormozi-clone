# Embrace uncertainty...

## Summary
Short monologue on regret, trade-offs, and uncertainty. Alex argues the most-rewarded people are those willing to embrace not knowing what will happen. He reframes life as "happening for you, not to you," saying you can live without regret if you always believe you *had* to go through what you went through to get where you are. He rebuts the "if I'd known this earlier I'd be so much further" fantasy — you couldn't have known it then, and even if you had, you'd likely be about as satisfied/dissatisfied now anyway. His warning: many people sit in "decision purgatory," trading their years but getting nothing in return; be conscious of *what* you're trading and *what for*, and ignore those who decry your trades unless you'd actually want their life. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex claims "the people who are rewarded most in life are the ones who are willing to simply embrace uncertainty" — accepting that you don't know what will happen.
- As of March 2026, he restates the "life happens for you, not to you" frame as the route to a regret-free life: believe "you had to go through that to get to where you are now."
- As of March 2026, he rebuts hindsight regret: even had he known things earlier, "I'm just better off and I'm still going to be probably about as dissatisfied and satisfied as I am right now."
- As of March 2026, he warns against "decision purgatory" / "decision limbo": "you're trading the years of life, but you're not getting anything for it."
- As of March 2026, his two-part prescription: be conscious of (1) what you're trading and (2) what you're trading it for; and when others speak against your trades, ask "do you want their life?"
