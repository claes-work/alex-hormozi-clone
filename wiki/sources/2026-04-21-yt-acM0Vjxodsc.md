# How to Decide If You Should Start Making Content

## Summary

Alex says not every business should add content; the decision depends on the business
constraint. More attention can grow businesses that are not supply constrained, but if
the constraint is delivery capacity or recruiting, content may be irrelevant or should
target supply instead of customers.

## Key claims

- Alex says he has strong reservations about the word "should" because content depends
  on the business constraint.
- He says a company with a working outbound channel may not need content if another
  activity would create more enterprise value.
- He says attention generally helps businesses only if they are not supply constrained.
- He says some service businesses do not need more demand; they need more plumbers,
  cleaners, or delivery capacity.
- He says if supply is the constraint, content might target workers or providers rather
  than customers.
- He reframes content as another advertising channel that should be added only when it
  addresses the constraint.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It really just depends on what the constraint of the business is."

> "No, unless it's the constraint."

> "More attention in general tends to grow businesses."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: content only if it solves constraint; attention helps only if not supply constrained; content can target labor supply.
