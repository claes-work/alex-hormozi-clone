# How to Bounce Back From Anything Fast

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains resilience as the time between an aversive stimulus and return to
baseline behavior. He uses "cosmic relevance" and the "veteran" frame as tools for making
the rebound more V-shaped: specify what will actually happen, shrink the event against the
scale of life and the universe, and ask what the thousandth repetition of the same
inconvenience would feel like.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Vague emotion gets weaker when the feared event is made specific.
- Most people move on after even major public deaths; that macro perspective can shrink
  acute self-concern.
- Resilience is the time it takes to return to baseline behavior after a bad thing happens.
- Cosmic relevance is one of Alex's default tools for reducing the emotional size of a
  problem.
- The "veteran" frame asks how the event would feel on the thousandth repetition; if it
  would feel normal then, one can choose that frame sooner.
- Building many rebound tools makes recovery after disruption more V-shaped.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Someone's going to argue over what appetizer they're going to serve when I die."

> "Resiliency is the amount of time after an aversive stimulus... that you return back to baseline behavior."
