# Helping a Real Estate Company Scale

## Summary

Alex advises a real-estate/wholesaling business owner whose deal flow dropped from ~70 to ~40 deals a month after his two best closers left (one promoted to sales manager, one departed for a competitor). The owner is weighing whether to hire a sales director on top of the existing sales manager or to get personally involved. Alex argues the CEO of a sales-driven business must own sales himself, that the real fix is a simple, universally-followed script plus disciplined role-play training, and that the best closer is usually the wrong person to coach the team.

## Key claims

- As of March 2026, Alex advises that in a sales-driven business the CEO should personally know how sales works, because whoever runs sales otherwise gains leverage over the owner (e.g. threatening to leave unless pay doubles).
- Alex claims most companies are bad at sales training: he estimates ~95% of companies have no idea how to train sales, hiring reps and hoping for the best with no measurable improvement.
- Alex's training method: people get better by watching a lot of "game tape" of people who sell well (modeling), plus immediate corrective role-play — drilling one part of the script repeatedly until the rep can "breathe it" and no longer thinks about what to say next, then moving to the next part.
- Alex claims the best closer / "sales killer" is usually the wrong choice for sales manager; the better coach is a different persona — an attentive, caring closer who genuinely wants to help others win (he cites "Jacob" from Gym Launch as an example).
- Step one for turning around a sales team is making the script "the Bible" — everyone follows it exactly; without universal adherence, training and script tweaks are meaningless.
- If a rep follows the script perfectly and still doesn't close, Alex frames that as success, because a followed script can then be measured and tweaked to change how everyone sells.
- Alex advises making the script as simple as possible; he says when his team reviews a script they typically cut half of it.
- Alex recommends the owner personally take some sales calls (he suggests as few as five) to learn where the script can be trimmed and to better teach and iterate it.
- (Unverified caller claims) The owner states deal volume fell from ~70 to ~40/month, that lead flow is unchanged, that the remaining team closes at the same percentage, and that the newly promoted sales manager was previously paid "under the table" by the prior manager to do call reviews.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have a sales-driven business. You're the CEO. You should know how it works. You're going to keep running this issue and whoever runs sales is going to have leverage over you."

> "I honestly just think everyone sucks at sales training... I think literally 95% of companies have no idea how to train sales and they just hire sales people and hope for the best."

> "People get better at sales by watching a ton of game tape of people who sell well. One, by modeling just like children learn anything. And then two, by corrective, very immediate role play."

> "You just really drill one aspect of the script until they breathe it... That way they can actually listen on the call because they're not thinking about what they're going to say next."

> "You need somebody who like really really loves sales people and like wants to teach and invest in people, which is not usually the personality of a sales killer."

> "Everyone follow the script. The script becomes the Bible. It's like, if this is word of God. Now, that means that if you follow the script perfectly and they don't close, you crushed it."

> "Most times when we look at a script, we cut half of it."
