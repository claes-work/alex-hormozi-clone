# Most Problems Can Be Solved With Advertising

## Summary
In a live Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a custom luxury-home builder (10M revenue, wanting to reach 30M) on how to recruit a top salesperson so the owner can get his time back. Hormozi reframes the hiring problem as an advertising problem: every business is demand-constrained either for customers or for talent, and the same outreach and ad methods that find customers find talent. He argues the builder's failed attempts stem from insufficient volume, and recommends LinkedIn outreach to top producers, expanding the search region, and offering relocation packages.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames every business as demand-constrained — either for customers or for talent — and holds that "most problems can be solved with advertising," the only question being who you advertise to.
- As of March 2026, Alex asserts that talent is found the exact same way customers are found; recruiting top players/leaders is done primarily through outreach.
- As of March 2026, Alex attributes most recruiting failures to insufficient volume, arguing the owner who contacted ~15 candidates simply needs to "add two zeros to effort."
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends recruiting via LinkedIn as the primary method — targeting top producers in the area, expanding the region, and offering relocation packages for strong candidates.
- As of March 2026, Alex promotes acquisition.com/roadmap, a free "$100M scaling roadmap" quiz built from ~200 hours reviewing portfolio companies across 10 growth stages and eight business functions.
