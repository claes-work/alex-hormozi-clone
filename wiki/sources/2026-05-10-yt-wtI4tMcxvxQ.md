# How to Become a Millionaire Before 30

## Summary

Alex gives a personalized answer to Caleb about becoming a millionaire. He says one path
is staying at Acquisition.com long enough as the company grows; another is starting a
business, either around a core skill such as media or by pairing that skill with
co-founders. The broader framework is skill stacking: each additional skill makes the
existing skills more valuable.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says there are many paths to becoming a millionaire and the right path depends on
  the game someone wants to play.
- He says one path for Caleb is staying at Acquisition.com on a long enough time horizon.
- He says his goal is for people at Acquisition.com to become very wealthy.
- He distinguishes being an artist from being an entrepreneur: one can raise price and
  stay scarce, or build a system that scales production.
- He says he likes the game of business across industries rather than one particular art.
- He says he is good enough across business functions to prevent any one of them from
  becoming the constraint.
- He defines talent stacking as combining skills so that one plus one can equal five.
- He says each skill added to the tool belt makes the rest of the skills more valuable.
- He says Acquisition.com's mission is to make business accessible to everyone.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Do I want to be the artist or do I want to be the entrepreneur?"

> "Many skills like 1 plus 1 equals five when you put them together."

> "Every skill you add to your skill tool belt makes the rest of your skills more valuable."
