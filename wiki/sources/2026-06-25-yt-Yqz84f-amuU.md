# Don't Reinvent What's Already Working

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the CEO of an established trading/charting platform doing about $84M top line. The CEO asks how to find the next "lightning in a bottle" product. Alex says not to break a fast-growing business in search of novelty. Product excellence comes from removing friction from the outcome customers already want, and growth capital may be better reinvested into the existing machine when the enterprise-value math is favorable.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - CEO of a trading/charting tools and education company. His role, revenue, product category, customer economics, and founder ownership context are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A business that has been growing for years should be cautious about searching for novelty that could break what already works.
- Excellent products are built by looking at the customer's end state and removing what sucks about getting that outcome.
- Alex distinguishes marketing from product: marketing is about new and exciting, while product is the thing customers already want with friction removed.
- If customers stick and reinvested marketing spend creates enterprise value, the founder's decision becomes mostly risk tolerance.
- Alex compares taking profit out and investing it elsewhere against reinvesting into advertising that can create more top-line enterprise value tax-free.
- A product-development hack is to map the customer's path like a course, then build tools that remove steps from that path.
- Watching users stumble through the product can reveal tiny frictions that block success.
