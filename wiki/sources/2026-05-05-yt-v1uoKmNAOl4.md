# Why You Keep Pivoting (And How to Stop)

## Summary

Alex explains constant pivoting as a doom cycle: uninformed optimism, informed pessimism,
valley of despair, then a new shiny object that restarts the loop. The only way out is to
push through the valley until informed optimism and achievement. He uses Suzanne
Schiffler's line that every business has problems and argues that new opportunities look
easy only because the founder has not yet paid the debt required to understand them.

## Key claims

- Each new idea feels rational because the founder is comparing the new idea's uninformed
  optimism against the old idea's known pain.
- The loop is: uninformed optimism -> informed pessimism -> valley of despair -> shiny
  object -> repeat.
- Every business has problems; a business that seems easy is usually one the founder does
  not understand yet.
- Self-reported: around 2024 he noticed he had much less shiny-object pull than during
  the first 13 years of his career.
- Switching opportunities ignores compounding: year one of the new thing is still year
  four or five of the old thing.
- Durable success comes from staying long enough to see the texture and ridges of the
  opportunity, then modeling what successful people do and outworking them over a long
  horizon.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Every single business that feels like it's easy is only a signal that you don't know enough yet."

> "You need to stick with it."
