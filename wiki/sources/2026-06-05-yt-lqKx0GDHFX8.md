# Never Give Real Equity (Do This Instead)

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a $3.5M paid-ads and growth advisory business serving eight- and nine-figure e-commerce brands. The owner wants incentive plans for a newly established leadership team. Alex recommends a capped profit-share pool, possible sale bonus / profits interest, and avoiding real shares unless necessary. He breaks equity into four components: cash flow, sale proceeds, risk, and control.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A simple leadership incentive can be a 10%-20% profit-share pool, with leaders receiving slices of that pool.
- The pool should cap so the owner always keeps a known majority of profit.
- Future talent should be reserved for; do not give away the whole incentive pool to today's team.
- If the owner does not want to sell, real shares are usually a mistake.
- Equity provides four things: cash flow, sale dollars, risk, and control.
- If the employee does not want risk and the owner will not give control, the practical substitutes are profit share and a sale bonus.
- Sale bonus terms should be based on cash received, not merely total deal value.
- Giving equity to a salesperson too early is a common day-one mistake; leadership participation should be tied to quantifiable demand-generation mastery.
