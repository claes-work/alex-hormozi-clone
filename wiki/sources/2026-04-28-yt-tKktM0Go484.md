# Stop Seeing Character Traits as Traits

## Summary

Alex says character traits should be treated as skills. A trait is a bucket of behaviors
performed under specific conditions, so improvement starts by defining the trait,
identifying the conditions where the desired behavior fails, and practicing the specific
replacement behavior. He gives his own example: after feedback that people discounted
"Alex numbers" because he was optimistic, he made "state the facts and tell the truth" a
practice around metrics.

## Key claims

- Character traits are better understood as skills and behaviors, not fixed traits.
- A desired trait should be broken into specific conditions and desired behaviors.
- "Patience" can mean figuring out what to do in the meantime, including in specific
  trigger conditions such as traffic.
- Improvement comes from practice and evidence in the moments where the condition appears.
- Alex says he corrected optimistic exaggeration in numbers by reporting current facts
  exactly.
- "State the facts and tell the truth" became a marketing mantra partly from that
  feedback.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Patience in and of itself is figuring out what to do in the meantime."
