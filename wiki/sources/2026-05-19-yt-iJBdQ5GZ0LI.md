# The Math Behind Quitting Your Job Safely

## Summary

Alex Hormozi gives the rational math for quitting: save 3 to 6 months of personal
expenses, start the side business, and show 3 to 6 months of income that matches or
replaces the job. He then says math is rarely the real blocker; people must compare a
guaranteed bad path with a chance at a good one and make vague fears specific.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The conservative math for quitting is 3 to 6 months of personal savings.
- A person should start something on the side before quitting.
- The side income should match or replace job income for 3 to 6 months.
- That math is reasonable but often not the true reason people do not quit.
- Alex frames some decisions as "guaranteed bad, chance at good."
- Existing paths can be guaranteed outcomes someone does not want, only delayed.
- Fear exists in the vague, not in the specific.
- Playing out the worst case often reveals less catastrophic outcomes: a story, shame,
  savings loss, a temporary worse living situation, or a different career story.
- Going from vague to specific can disengage fear and bring logic back.
- Shameful experiences often become funny with enough time.
- The same play-it-out method applies to business decisions and hard conversations.
- People discount a lifetime of guaranteed misery because the pain is delayed.
