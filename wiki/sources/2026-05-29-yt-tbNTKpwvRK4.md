# The Real Reason You Don't Have What You Want in Life

## Summary

Alex Hormozi defines resilience as a time measure: after a bad event changes behavior, how long does it take to return to a new baseline? He contrasts a person who recovers in minutes with someone who stays off baseline for years. The point is not whether the reaction spike is intense, but how quickly the person comes back.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Resilience is one of the most workable traits for people who do not have what they want.
- Formally, resilience is how long it takes to return to baseline after a bad event changes behavior.
- In simpler terms, it is how long it takes to stop acting out after something bad happens.
- A person can react intensely and still have high resilience if they recover quickly.
- Low resilience is when a bad event throws someone off for months or years.
- Resilience is different from fortitude; someone can have low fortitude but high resilience if they return quickly.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Resilience is a measure of time."
