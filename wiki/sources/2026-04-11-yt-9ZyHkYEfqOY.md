# Helping a $5M Plumbing Business Double Revenue

## Summary

Alex advises a plumbing-business buyer to consider dropping the construction half and
focus on the preferred service business. The two operations run separately,
construction consumes working capital and attention, and a future buyer would likely
carve the businesses apart. He treats the original purchase as sunk cost and asks
whether the owner would choose the same structure today.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says separately operated construction and service divisions are
  effectively two businesses even when they share an income statement.
- If the owner would not start the combined structure today, the prior acquisition cost
  should not control the current decision.
- Construction's receivables and working-capital burden make its cash flow less
  attractive than service in this case.
- A focused service company is cleaner for a future acquisition because buyers may not
  want both operations.
- Removing the operational headache can accelerate the remaining business despite
  short-term pain and regret.
- Alex self-reports nine failed businesses and losing all his money twice; these claims
  are not independently verified here.
- All company revenue and contribution figures are owner-reported and unverified.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's sunk cost."

> "If I were to start this today, would I do it this way?"

> "You'll be amazed at how much faster the other one will grow by getting rid of the headache."
