# How to Grow an Audience if You Have 0 Followers

## Summary

Solo talk in which Alex argues that growing an audience from zero is a matter of
years and consistency, not a 90-day sprint. He uses his own podcast (started 2017,
400+ episodes, only just cracked top 10 after ~6 years) as proof that early content
is bad and narrow — his first 150 episodes were "just me talking about how to run a
gym better." The core method: make the action the goal, post and reach out daily,
and divorce the activities from the outcome so consistency survives when results
lag or spike.

## Key claims (the zero-to-audience method)

- Just start — having no following/audience is not a reason to wait; discouragement
  over a small audience is the wrong frame.
- Expect a long ramp: the first ~year is spent "finding your voice," learning to
  teach, entertain, present, and speak; subject matter and delivery skill improve
  together over years.
- His own timeline as evidence: podcast started 2017, ran 1–3 episodes/week for six
  years without missing, hit top 10 (entrepreneurship) only recently; content
  evolved gym → marketing → sales → business/M&A.
- Think in decades, not days: "the time's going to pass no matter what," so set
  goals on a decade horizon.
- Make the action the goal, not the outcome: if you made the post and made the
  reach-outs each day, you succeeded — you check both boxes.
- Volume of long-form content compounds: 400 long-form pieces would make almost
  anyone's following bigger; how long it takes "is as long as it takes."
- The consistency trap: people ramp content → get sales/money → slow down → hit
  scarcity → restart. Break it by focusing on the process, not the money.
- Divorce activities from outcome so self-esteem stays stable whether income rises
  or falls, because you're committed to what you can control.
- Cites Atomic Habits: "every winner and every loser have the same goals" — goals
  don't make you unique, the activities do.
- Reach-outs (direct outreach) run in parallel with content — content brings
  attention, reach-outs convert it.
