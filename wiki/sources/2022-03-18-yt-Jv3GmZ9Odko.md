# "What Millionaires Know That You Don't" (2022-03-18)

## Summary

A solo video answering why two entrepreneurs starting at the same time diverge wildly — and
why someone who loses it all rebuilds far faster. Hormozi's answer is **pattern recognition
in people**: speed of scaling comes from knowing what "ideal scene" looks like for each role
and hiring against that pattern. He illustrates with his own decade-long struggle to find a
good executive assistant, and argues most people's real bottleneck is too-high tolerance for
non-excellent people plus a wrong picture of what each role should look like.

## Key claims

- **Strategy is simple; execution differentiates.** Most business strategy is basic (find
  something people want, charge a lot, deliver so well they refer). Two lawn-care companies —
  one worth a billion, one barely making $5k/month — differ on execution, not strategy. (paraphrase)
- **Speed comes from pattern recognition in people.** Scaling fast means "paying down the
  ignorance tax" of not knowing what an excellent hire looks like; outside eyes who already
  know ideal scene let you skip steps. (paraphrase)
- **A-players are visible immediately.** Every A-player he's had, he knew within the first
  couple of days; he's had A-players decline, but "if someone is not an A-player up front,
  I've never had someone become an A-player." (paraphrase)
- **Two ideal scenes to learn.** For each role you must know (1) the right *sequence* to hire
  heads (marketing, sales, HR, finance, etc.) and (2) what an *excellent* person in that role
  actually looks like. (paraphrase)
- **Mis-hires are doubly costly.** They cost time to onboard, time to discover they don't
  fit, and time to off-board — during which that department stalls instead of growing. Don't
  take advice from people one step ahead of you. (paraphrase)
