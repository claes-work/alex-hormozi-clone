# Why Your Content Gets No Views After 4 Years

## Summary

In a coaching exchange with a tax-strategy founder, Alex rejects the idea that tax is too
boring for content. He argues the founder is making it boring by teaching generic
hypotheticals rather than demonstrating expertise in real, high-stakes client situations.
His prescription is to build a monthly delivery mechanism that records 20-50 fast tax
diagnosis calls, structured around the client's stakes and one to three actionable tactics.

## Key claims

- "Boring industry" is usually a content-process problem, not a market law; legal,
  finance, and business advice can all become compelling through stakes.
- Demonstration of expertise is especially important in an AI world because real
  diagnosis and live help are harder to fake.
- Content needs stakes. Hooks create artificial stakes; real client problems create
  actual stakes.
- The founder should study ten better creators in the space, model hook structure, and
  deliver a different solution rather than copying content.
- For the tax business, he recommends recording 20-50 client/audience audits per month,
  starting with the stakes, then giving one to three tactics.
- If possible, do the first version in person because it is easier to make work than
  remote.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I don't think tax is boring. I think you might make tax boring."

> "What makes content interesting is stakes."
