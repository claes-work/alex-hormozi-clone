# Helping a $30M Online Service Business Get to $100M

## Summary

A live coaching Q&A in which Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a ~$33M online fitness-info/coaching business (low-ticket-to-high-ticket ascension for athletes) that wants to reach $100M. The owner's LTV:CAC has compressed to 2:1 and margins are thin after scaling from 4 to 40 phone-sales reps in three months. Alex diagnoses the problem as pure operations — specifically a sales recruiting-and-training problem — and reframes the growth lever as raising dollars-per-lead (via a better sales function, front-end conversion optimization, and back-end renewals) rather than "increasing LTV" abstractly. Only Alex's advice and framing are persona-relevant; the owner's business details are context.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- **The whole margin is coming from the free/organic traffic.** At a 2:1 LTV:CAC with ~20% margins, if you pulled out the ~30% of clients coming from organic, the business would probably not be profitable — the paid side is roughly breaking even and the free traffic is carrying the profit.
- **This is pure ops — a sales problem, not an ads problem.** The fix is the sales motion, dialing it in, and adding education prior to the close so reps can convert with less ramp. Ad creative and funnel conversion can add incremental lift, but the sales function is where the alpha is.
- **Fundamentally it's a recruiting-and-training business.** "You have a sales business with direct response media on the front end." Solving the business means getting better reps and training them.
- **Scale through training, not through selection.** ~95% of sales orgs scale by hiring as many people as possible and letting the bad ones die out (scaling through selection). Scaling through training lets you scale at higher margins because you don't have to overpay "mercenaries" — you can take anybody off the street and have them sell, pay them less, ramp them faster, and get more of them. That is the competitive advantage.
- **Don't frame the goal as "increase LTV" — increase dollars per lead.** When the owner said the 80/20 was a better sales team to raise LTV, Alex corrected: "You're not going to increase LTV, you're going to increase dollars per lead."
- **Target ~5:1 LTV:CAC as a good goal** (10:1 is unrealistic here). Fixing the sales function gets to ~3.5, maybe 4; another ~20–25% lift comes from front-end conversion-rate optimization and media, plus improving the back-end renewal rate.
- **The two most profitable segments are free customers and renewals.** Customers acquired for free (essentially the entire current margin) and customers who renew (no CAC to re-pay) are what drop to the bottom line. "Everything in the middle is literally just to facilitate these two things." Double down on both, lift the rest, and you unlock the path to $100M.
- **Getting to 4–5:1 is what makes $100M reachable** — the work is ops-heavy, but the payoff is ~$70M more revenue per year.
- **Context (owner, not persona):** ~$33M revenue targeting $100M; low-ticket-to-high-ticket coaching/info for athletes (mobility, injury prevention); ~70% of clients from paid, big organic following; 20% margins; 20% close rate (dragged down by reps still in ramp, "should be" 30–35%); scaled 4→40 reps in 3 months; structure of 40 reps, 4 pod leaders, 1 sales director; sales training built by the owner and sales director.
