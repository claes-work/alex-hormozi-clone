# How to Recruit Elite Sales Directors for Your Agency

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a done-for-you client acquisition business for agencies doing about $5M top line. The owner says the constraint is finding sales directors who can manage sales teams for client agencies. Alex reframes the problem: the pool is not truly scarce, and the business may be over-specifying the role because its process depends too much on specialized knowledge. He says sales directors are primarily culture and training, and that better scripts and training widen the talent market.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A sales director in this context is mostly responsible for **culture and training**, not closing calls.
- Door-to-door sales leaders can be a useful talent pool because they have built teams and sold harder products in worse conditions.
- If a sales process requires "unicorn" people with special knowledge, the script/process is probably too consultative.
- A refined process should let less specialized salespeople follow the script and loop prospects back to the core path.
- The apparent talent constraint is partly a belief constraint: there are many people who can train sales if the role is defined correctly.
- For team size, Alex says around 10 reps is a preferred point to start splitting sales teams; 15 reps under one director feels high.
