# Why I'm Rolling Up 50 Med Spas Instead of Exiting

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a med-spa / body-contouring owner with two businesses: operating med spas and selling consulting to med spas. The owner wants to coach but also sees a possible med-spa roll-up. Alex says a roll-up could make everything else irrelevant if executed, but the owner must get out of day-to-day facility management first. He recommends installing an intelligent operator with profit/equity-like upside and a clear "phone does not ring" responsibility test.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Coaching can generate cash, but the owner should recognize that it may not create an exit the way a roll-up could.
- A med-spa roll-up is easy to say and harder to do; deal structure and tax treatment matter.
- If the roll-up is the real opportunity, the current locations should be delegated even at the cost of some margin.
- An operator can be paid salary plus a profit share or sale-upside component so they own the facility outcome.
- The operator responsibility test is simple: when customer complaints, leaks, or ordinary crises happen, any answer except "call the founder" is the right answer.
- Giving the operator status, visible ownership, and upside helps transfer accountability away from the founder.
