# Why I Hate the Word 'Mindset

## Summary

Alex Hormozi criticizes vague "mindset" language because it often hides undefined terms and social signaling. He frames language itself as behavior: people repeat words because those words produce acceptance, agreement, or other social reinforcement. He uses an example from his marriage to Leila to show how responses can reinforce the wrong behavior, then connects the lesson to his problem-solving practice of defining terms before arguing about them.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex dislikes the term "mindset" because people load it with vague mythology instead of defining what they mean.
- Language is behavior because words solicit responses and get socially reinforced.
- People may believe or repeat undefined words because the community rewards them for saying those words.
- Reinforcement can create harmful loops even in close relationships.
- Alex says he learned the wrong lesson when anger stopped Leila from crying, then had to relearn the behavior pattern.
- Definitions matter because unclear words prevent clear problem solving.
- His problem breakdown starts with: what does it mean, how do we know, and why does it matter.
- Those three questions map to logic, evidence, and utility.
