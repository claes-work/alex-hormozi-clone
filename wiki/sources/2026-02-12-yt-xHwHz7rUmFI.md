# The Biggest Mistake Entrepreneurs Make

## Summary
Asked what once worked for him but stopped serving him as he grew, Alex points to early-stage risk-taking. When you quit your job and start out, you get massively rewarded for taking existential risks and doing something different — but that reward creates a lingering craving for the "hit" of trying new things. His reframe: entrepreneurs have to almost immediately unlearn that instinct and instead stay on their current path, getting better at the thing in front of them and confronting the specific problem they don't know how to solve to reach the next level. Chasing new ventures because novelty paid off before just splits focus across "two or three masters" and gets them nowhere. He closes by promoting his free 10-stage zero-to-$100M+ roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap.

## Notable verbatim quotes
_Alex Hormozi unless noted._

> "When you quit the job and you start entrepreneurship, you get rewarded really big for taking a risk, for doing something different. But you don't need to take those kind of existential risks all the time."

> "And then you almost have to immediately unlearn that and then stick with this current path for an extended period of time. But you kind of always remember the hit of trying something new."

> "So many entrepreneurs tend to always want to do lots of new things when most of the time they need to just get better at the thing that's in front of them and confront whatever problem they don't know how to solve so they can get that thing to the next level."

> "They think that by doing something new, because that's what worked for them in the past, that's going to get them to the next level. When in reality, they just end up distracting themselves and trying to chase two or three masters at a time and never getting there."
