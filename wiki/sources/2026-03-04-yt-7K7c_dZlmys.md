# You only need 3 things to win

## Summary
Short solo motivational clip. Alex's thesis: winning requires only **three things — "the balls to start, the brains to learn, and the heart to never give up"** — and everyone already has all three. From this he draws his central "ask": make the commitment to never stop. He is careful to distinguish this from committing to *be successful* — he asks only for a commitment to "keep running… even if you have to pant and you have to slow down. You just can't stop." He then attacks the habit of waiting for a "special occasion" (New Year's, etc.) to begin self-improvement, arguing that waiting proves you don't think *today* is worth improving, and that today is the only day you control. He deliberately posted this before year-end to make the point that waiting for January 1st is "stupid." He reframes the future you want as a lottery ticket you'd cash in as fast as possible. He rejects the "you're young, you've got time" comfort as a lie that only delays the realization that "tomorrow isn't guaranteed." The upside: his oft-repeated **"20 hours of focused effort to get decent at anything"** claim — people wait years before starting their first hour, so many could start hitting next year's goals (learning ads, content, video editing, or sales) before this year ends; his team can onboard a new sales hire in a few days. He closes on a tangent about the fear of the unknown: people dislike even *good* surprises (surprise parties) because the unknown carries a possibility of a negative outcome — but over a long horizon "85-year-old you isn't even going to remember it." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (the $100M scaling roadmap quiz).
