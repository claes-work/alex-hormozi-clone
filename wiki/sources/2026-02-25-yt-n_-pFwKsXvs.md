# Helping a SaaS Owner Find His Ideal Customer

## Summary

Short Q&A/coaching clip. An audience member (a founder starting a SaaS for property
due-diligence / inspection services aimed at serious property buyers) asks Alex Hormozi
whether he should hire inspectors and run a platform/marketplace model, or build a B2B
SaaS product. Alex advises the safer B2B SaaS bet over a platform, notes that platforms
earn higher multiples precisely because they are so hard and expensive to build, and
observes that your true ideal customer often only reveals itself after you launch and
place a bet. He illustrates with his own experience at Gym Launch/ALAN and at Skool.

## Key claims

_All paraphrased from the 2026-02-25 clip unless quoted below. The questioner is an
audience member (not Alex); their business description and numbers are self-reported and
attribution of the question is `audience / uncertain`. Only Alex's answers are persona data._

- (2026-02-25) You often can't predict your ideal customer in advance — you place a bet
  by launching, then discover who actually buys. Alex says with ALAN he expected lead
  nurture for small businesses to be "a home run," but found agency owners were the best
  customer.
- (2026-02-25) A B2B SaaS (product) model is a much safer bet than a platform/marketplace
  model; platforms are "significantly harder to build."
- (2026-02-25) Platforms earn the multiples they do precisely because they are so
  expensive and so hard to build — the difficulty is the source of the valuation premium.
- (2026-02-25) The hardest part of building a marketplace is the early phase — years where
  customers keep asking to "white label this" and you have no real value prop beyond
  "someday this will be valuable when it's a marketplace." Alex says (as a Skool
  co-founder) Skool is already through that hardest part, having built the product so good
  that the marketplace component can follow.
- (2026-02-25) Framing on the bet: "your life equals one" — you only get to live one life,
  not 10 or 20, so pick the safer bet (B2B SaaS). The exception: if the explicit goal is
  extreme upside ("I have to be a trillionaire"), then you have to go platform.
