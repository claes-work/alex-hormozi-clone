# You're Not Burned Out, You're Just Focused

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that work is not wasted because the person doing the work becomes the
output. He uses losing the proceeds from selling five gyms as the key example: the money
disappeared, but the skills built during the five years enabled much larger earnings in
the next year. He connects work capacity to training capacity: if someone does not die or
get injured, they adapt.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says hard work is often misunderstood because people assume it does not create
  progress.
- The work works on the person more than the person works on the work.
- Even if a project fails, the person becomes more capable through doing the labor.
- Alex spent 5 years building a chain of six gyms, then sold five and shut one down.
- He says he put the proceeds into the next venture, and a partner took the money,
  disappeared, filed bankruptcy, and sent it to a girlfriend in Sweden.
- In the following 12 months, Alex says he made more profit than in the prior 5 years
  multiplied several times.
- His retrospective conclusion is that the real output of the five years was him, his
  skills, and his experiences.
- No work is wasted if the person becomes more capable.
- Work capacity is trainable in the same way recovery from training volume is trainable.
- People who do more volume can increase their ability to withstand volume.
- Experts become experts because they learn to be rewarded by the work itself.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The work works on you more than you work on it."

> "I am the output of the work, not the outcome."

> "No work is wasted."
