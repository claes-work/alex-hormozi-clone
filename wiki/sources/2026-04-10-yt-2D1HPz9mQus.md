# Helping a CFO Turn Construction Expertise Into a Consulting Business

## Summary

Alex advises a finance-and-strategy consultant to specialize in construction, where he
already has credibility and experience. A niche creates more relevant case studies,
higher response and pricing, and more customers rather than fewer. Alex dismisses boredom
as a reason to stay broad because successful businesses eventually give the owner a
similar job managing five to seven functional leaders.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says niche positioning can produce more customers than broad
  positioning because the message and proof feel specifically relevant.
- A specialist can charge niche prices, build directly applicable case studies, and
  create easier sales.
- He says even a general track record may attract fewer construction customers than a
  construction-specific promise.
- At sufficient scale, businesses converge toward an owner managing five to seven
  leaders across functions, so the founder's day-to-day becomes similar across industries.
- Alex recommends focusing on the market where the consultant has the deepest track
  record and can create the strongest results.
- The consultant's career and company credentials are self-reported and unverified.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You'll have more customers in a niche than if you go broad."

> "Every business, if you succeed at it at the highest level, ends up becoming the same thing."

> "Focus on the thing that you have this massive track record in."
