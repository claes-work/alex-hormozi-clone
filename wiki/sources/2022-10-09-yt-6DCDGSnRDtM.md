# Get Rich in the New Economy (2022-10-09)

## Summary

Hormozi's thesis that **attention is the new oil** — the last century's fortunes
were built on energy, but four of the five biggest companies today are built on
capturing the scarce resource of collective human attention (24 hours × every
person's eyes and ears). He narrates his own belief shift from "rich and unknown"
(just run ads, no content) to building a media audience, triggered by seeing
younger creators (Kylie Jenner at 20) out-earn him, and by learning Naval
Ravikant's four types of leverage (labor, capital, code, media). The core mental
model: **content is the input, the audience is the output**, and media/audience
is a compounding, tax-free leverage vehicle.

## Key claims

- **Attention is the new oil.** Scarce resources are most valuable; oil was
  scarce, now attention is. Platform owners are the new landowners — they don't
  haggle for the resource, they just take it.
- **Naval's four types of leverage:** labor, capital, code, and media. Code and
  media are permissionless — one-time input, infinite output. Media was the
  leverage vehicle Hormozi decided he could win at.
- **Content is the input; the audience is the output.** The audience is what
  compounds and sticks; building an audience is **tax-free**, and you can choose
  the "conversion event" (monetization) later.
- **Mega-brands monetize late and on-brand.** The Rock built his brand ~20 years
  before Under Armor; celebrity endorsements sell *status* (Kim → Dolce & Gabbana
  → the buyer). Cross-pollinating multiple deep interests (Rogan: UFC, comedy,
  fitness, aliens) compounds the audience faster.
- **The new billionaires will capture, hold, and multiply attention** — and the
  best time to start is now because media/audience only gets more expensive.
