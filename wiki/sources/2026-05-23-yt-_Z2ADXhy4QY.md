# You're Distracted by the Woman in the Red Dress

## Summary

Alex Hormozi uses the "woman in the red dress" scene from *The Matrix* as an internal
metaphor for distracting opportunities. He says better operators get better distractions,
so the ability to say no becomes less about refusing everything and more about saying yes
to the one thing that matters most.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex treats attractive opportunities as "women in the red dress" that pull attention
  away from the training objective.
- The more successful or able someone becomes, the more attractive the distractions
  become.
- A mentor told him after selling Gym Launch that if an opportunity was not worth a
  billion dollars, it was not worth his time.
- He uses that billion-dollar threshold as a decision razor.
- Acquisition.com receives about 3,000 company deal inquiries per month, or about 100 per
  day, according to Alex.
- He says Acquisition.com had done 13 deals in 2 years at the time of this clip.
- A strong "no muscle" is really a yes muscle for the thing that matters most.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's a woman in the red dress."

> "If it's not a billion, it's not worth your time."

> "Your no muscle isn't really a no muscle. It's just a yes muscle for the one thing that matters most."
