# The first 5 hires EVERY entrepreneur should make...or go broke...

## Summary

Alex explains early hiring as buying back the founder's time in reverse order of market value. The first hires are extensions of the founder who take repeated, lower-dollar-per-hour tasks off the founder's plate: admin, customer support/delivery help, sales, marketing/prospecting/content help, fractional bookkeeping, and often a tech/CRM person. He also introduces startup "debt" categories: operational, management, financial, technology, and life debt.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2022-02-23)

- Alex says early hires should help the founder do more before the company has mature departments.
- He recommends replacing the lowest-value, most repeatable tasks first.
- He lists common early roles: administrative help, customer support/success, salesperson or setter, marketing assistant/prospector/content help, fractional bookkeeper, and possibly a tech/CRM person.
- He says the sequence depends on the founder's own dollars-per-hour in each function.
- He warns that buying back time lowers profit if the founder does not reinvest the freed time into higher-value work.
- He says individual contributors often struggle to become managers; hiring people who can grow into the next role is learned with experience.
- He frames every startup as incurring debt: life, management, financial, technological, cultural, and operational debt.
- He says venture/funding is not fake entrepreneurship; it is choosing financial debt to avoid some other debts.
- He says later businesses can grow faster because the founder can spend money to skip debt that previously cost quarters or years.
