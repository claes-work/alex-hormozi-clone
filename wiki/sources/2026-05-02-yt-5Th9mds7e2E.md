# You Have 300 Free Users and You've Called 3 of Them

## Summary

Alex coaches a founder with a free rental-relocation tool and weak paid conversion. He
says 300 free users is still low volume and the founder has talked to far too few of
them. The immediate move is not more software tinkering but manual onboarding and calls:
learn what users want, test whether a cheaper offer fits students, or sell adjacent value
such as moving-service leads. He frames demand as something to channel rather than create.

## Key claims

- A freemium tool with 300 users still has low volume; the founder needs more direct user
  conversations before deciding the paid offer.
- Alex treats getting only 3 calls from 300 users as an avoidable mistake.
- In freemium, free users can be treated as low-cost acquisition while effort or capital
  goes into ascension.
- The founder should add manual onboarding and outbound quickly after sign-up, accepting
  that some booked calls will no-show.
- Alex says marketers should channel existing demand rather than try to create demand.
- Serving a cheaper demographic first can force automation and reveal a low-cost leader
  path; alternatively, the founder may discover adjacent monetization such as selling
  warm leads to moving companies.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "We want to channel demand. We do not want to create demand."

> "Your marketing spend, if freemium is done well, is on ascension."

> "Tinkering on the computer is not going to be the solution."
