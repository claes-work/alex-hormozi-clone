# How to Pitch Your Paid Offer on a Free Group Call

## Summary

Alex coaches a free fitness group owner on soft-pitching a paid group. The weekly call
should teach deeply enough that members feel the founder knows the craft, then the CTA
should sell personalization: if members want the training personalized for them, they join
the paid offer. The pitch should stack features by explaining what each feature does, why
it matters, and the time, money, pain, or mistakes it saves.

## Key claims

- Alex likes the specificity of helping men bench over 225 and run ultra-marathons in
  under six months without training more than 10 hours per week.
- A weekly free-group call can be a training, breakdown, video review, or program
  analysis rather than a slide deck.
- The training should make people believe the founder knows what he is talking about.
- The CTA should sell personalized application of the free material.
- When stacking features, explain the resources and painful mistakes the founder spent to
  build them and what the buyer saves by getting them directly.
- The offer should wrap its components in a named method or process.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you want me to personalize this stuff for you, you should join this."

> "Talk about all the pain that you had that you're helping them avoid."

> "You want to have some sort of wrapper that buckets like your method."
