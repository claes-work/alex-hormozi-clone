# 17 Life-Changing Conversations I Wish I Had Earlier

## Summary

A rapid compilation of 17 lessons Hormozi got from mentors, peers, and events
during his 20s, each tied to the conversation that delivered it. Themes cluster
around decisiveness, volume/compounding, using others' success as fuel, buying
talent to scale past a plateau, content volume, investing within your circle of
competence, and product/brand as leverage.

## Key claims

- (#1) The Latin root of *decision* means "to cut off / kill off"; by not deciding
  he was actively killing the future that held his dreams. You rarely get more
  information — at some point you just have to jump.
- (#2) "The secret to sales is making an offer so good people feel stupid saying
  no" — became the subtitle of $100M Offers. Start from "how do I get the most
  people to say yes" and work backwards, rather than learning manipulation/scripts.
- (#3–#4, #14) Go slow to go fast; compounding beats erratic restarts. Under-doing
  volume (300 flyers vs. a 5,000-unit test) makes the right activity look like it
  failed. More content volume on every platform = faster growth (he 10x'd content,
  grew 10x).
- (#12) To break a multi-year plateau (~$30–40M), stop hoarding: build "a stable of
  stallions," give talented people a real slice, and buy decades of departmental
  expertise instead of being jack-of-all-trades. One brain = the ceiling of the org.
- (#15–#17) Investing: set capital-allocation rules by circle of competence
  (invest only in what you know — for him, businesses). Product is the strongest
  leverage (exceptional product = quadratic, marketing = linear). Brand is
  quantifiable: the price premium above the commoditized version is the power of
  brand (learned from "Uncle Warren" / Buffett).

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "The secret to sales is making an offer so good people feel stupid saying no."
- "What feels slow in the micro looks fast in the macro... what looks really fast
  in the micro is usually really slow in the macro because people can never get
  that compounding start harnessing."
- "When it gets easy is when you go hard, because you don't have that many fat
  pitches in a lifetime — you might get two or three and you just gotta crack the
  [expletive] out of it when it comes."
- "You need to have a stable of stallions... let them get rich too."
- "The amount that you can charge above the commoditized version of your product,
  the Delta there, is the power of brand."
- "It's actually less work in the long run to build a better thing than it is to
  build a shitty thing and then have to spend the rest of your time marketing and
  selling it."
