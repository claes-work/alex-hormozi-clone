# The Single Greatest Skill Entrepreneurs Need to Learn

## Summary

In this short interview clip, Alex Hormozi is asked what he's currently working on and what he's seeking clarity on. He uses the question to make his case for **focus** as the defining entrepreneurial skill — framing his own recent evolution not as adding things but as *eliminating* them and making his criteria stricter. He argues that all the entrepreneurial "greats" (Jobs, Bezos, Musk) obsess over focus precisely because it is so hard: the bigger you get, the more tantalizing outside opportunities become. His core mental model is that a new opportunity looks "fast and easy" only because you don't know enough about it — the attraction itself is evidence of your ignorance of its real pains. He reports that 2024 was the first year of his career with no FOMO, which he counts as a major personal win, and illustrates it with a crypto-trader friend who made ~$50M in a quarter that Hormozi felt no urge to chase. He says acquisition.com is the longest-duration single business he's ever stuck with. The clip closes with his own promo for the free acquisition.com/roadmap resource.

## Key claims (2026-02-19, paraphrased unless quoted)

- ★ **The single greatest skill is focus** — the discipline to keep doing the one hard thing you know, and to say no to attractive alternatives. He cites Jobs, Bezos, and Musk all talking about focus "because it's so hard to do." *(L3-candidate: durable, named-skill claim.)*
- Progress often comes from **eliminating**, not adding — usually eliminating activities or making his criteria more strict, rather than adding new things.
- Much of his attention goes toward deciding **what not to do**.
- Focus gets harder as you scale: "the bigger you get, the more juicy and tantalizing the opportunities become."
- **The "grass is greener" trap**: a new opportunity looks fast and easy only because you don't know enough about it. The fact that he finds something attractive is itself a signal that he doesn't understand its real difficulties — he's "been burned enough times jumping over that bridge."
- **2024 was the first time in his career with no FOMO** — no moment of feeling he should be doing something else instead. He only recognized it in retrospect and counts it as a huge win.
- Crypto-friend anecdote: a close friend made ~$50M in net earnings in Q4 trading crypto solo with no employees; Hormozi's reaction was "good for you" and a resolve to keep doing his own thing, whereas earlier in his career he'd have chased it and "taken his eye off the ball."
- Sticking with one thing compounds — a "snowball that's finally started to roll" — and he keeps getting rewarded for staying with it.
- **acquisition.com is the hardest / longest-duration single business he's run** — the longest he's ever done just one thing.
- He's currently thinking about the timing of how to launch his next book. *(self-reported; timing, not content)*
