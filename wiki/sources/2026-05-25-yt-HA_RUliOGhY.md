# How to Think About Competition at Every Goal Size

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says competition matters differently by goal size and market structure. A
local lawn-care operator trying to make $1M/year can mostly ignore competitors and find
enough customers. In AI models, network effects may create winner-take-most dynamics.
In media, attention is finite, but one viewer can watch multiple videos, so the real
question is how likely a desired outcome becomes.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Competition matters less when the goal is small relative to the market.
- A $1M/year lawn-care business does not need a monopoly; it needs enough paying houses.
- Network-effect markets, such as major AI models, may become winner-take-all or
  winner-take-few.
- Attention is finite because people have finite waking hours, and technology has moved
  more attention into places that can be advertised against.
- Watching one business video does not always preclude watching another, but marginal
  attention can still be contested.
- Alex thinks in likelihoods: what increases the probability that the desired thing occurs.
- Strategy is placing bets that improve those likelihoods.
