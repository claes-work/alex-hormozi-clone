# Just do MORE

## Summary

Alex Hormozi deepens his "more, better, new" hierarchy. When a business has something
that already works, truly new moves become statistically risky. He says the highest
risk-adjusted move is usually to do more of what works, then solve the hard constraint
that prevents more, even if that solution takes months or years to bear fruit.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- "New" is incredibly risky once a business already has a working stack of variables.
- The more evolved a business is, the more likely a random change makes it worse.
- A control that has won many split tests becomes hard to beat.
- If a business has something that works, the highest risk-adjusted move is usually to do
  more of that thing.
- Something truly new rarely works and often consumes the most resources.
- Alex allows himself one major new thing per year; in this year, he identifies the book
  launch as that one new thing.
- When nothing works, a business should try many new things; once something works, it
  should reduce deviation.
- Business owners often solve imaginary future problems while ignoring real current
  constraints.
- The key question is why the business cannot do more of what is already working.
- The second, third, and fourth most profitable things can distract from the most valuable
  thing.
- Some correct solutions take long enough that the business must give time time.
- Alex says early Gym Launch required turning 11 out of 11 directors over 12 months
  because the leadership bar was too low.
- He says Gym Launch sat around $30 million to $50 million per year for about 3 years
  before he concluded he needed to build a brand.
- Building the brand reduced his income for the first 2 years but was the correct
  long-term move, according to Alex.
- Winners who already know how to delay gratification must also learn when to reap.
- Alex calls indefinite delay the "third marshmallow fallacy."
- He says a $4,000 per month personal-training client helped him eat while building his
  first gym, even though that revenue was not scalable.
- Creators who build goodwill must eventually ask for money if making money was the goal.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Sometimes the most productive thing you can do in the business is give time time."
