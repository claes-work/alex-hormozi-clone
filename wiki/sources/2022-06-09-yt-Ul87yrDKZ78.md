# How to CLOSE THE SALE when the prospect says "I left my wallet at home"

## Summary

A tactical solo sales clip walking through a four-to-five-step process to prevent and overcome the "I forgot my wallet / I don't have my card" objection at the close, in person or over Zoom. The core move is asking for ID first (lower-friction than asking for payment), which exposes that the prospect does in fact have payment methods on them, then guiding them to alternative payment paths.

## Key claims

- The "I forgot my wallet" objection is essentially never literally true — nobody leaves home without a way to pay; if it comes up, you likely mishandled something earlier in the sale (paraphrase).
- Ask "Do you have your ID on you?" before asking for payment — it's easier to ask for ID than a credit card, prospects reveal their cards while retrieving it, and taking/returning the ID builds trust (paraphrase).
- After they claim they lack the specific card they want, use the bank-statement method: have them open their banking app, go to statements, and read the full account number (top right) to run an ACH transaction (paraphrase).
- Fallback options: Apple Wallet, or put down a card they "don't want to use," delay the charge ~24 hours, and text/call them to swap it — either way the deal closes on a card with a signed contract on file (paraphrase).
- Distinguish objection types by rapport: hesitation over money (ask what day they get paid, schedule the charge then) vs. hesitation about wanting the program (re-anchor on why they came in). This process handles ~80% of scenarios (paraphrase).
