# Niche Down and 5x Your Price

## Summary

Alex advises a YouTube-organic service helping Skool/coaching businesses to narrow to
YouTubers with 10k-100k subscribers if that is where results are fastest. He says narrow
avatars allow premium pricing because the deliverable is more specific, buyers perceive a
higher likelihood of success, and the operator can protect outcomes and reputation. He
contrasts wide markets with volume and narrow markets with price, success rates, and
word of mouth.

## Key claims

- If the provider already has credibility, narrowing to 10k-100k subscriber YouTubers can
  create faster value.
- Niche-based pricing can allow a provider to charge much more, which Alex frames as
  potentially 5x.
- A narrower avatar can perceive and actually have a higher likelihood of achieving the
  result.
- Serving too broad a market can damage reputation because many buyers are not the ideal
  fit.
- Alex prefers selling "from the back foot," where only qualified prospects ascend to
  the paid offer.
- He argues that 10k-plus YouTube channels are still a large enough market to build a
  major business.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "When you narrow down the avatar with niche-based pricing, you can 5x your price."

> "When you go narrow, the advantage that you have is premium pricing."

> "You'll sell from your back foot, which is my preferred way to sell."
