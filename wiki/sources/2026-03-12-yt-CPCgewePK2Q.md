# How to Negotiate

## Summary
Solo clip, framed as the first of "five or six" negotiation principles. Alex opens with what he calls the single greatest source of psychological power in any negotiation: **BATNA — Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement** — a term he credits to learning from Sharon Sarvatha via Harvard Business School. His thesis: "Negotiation's all about leverage," and your strongest leverage is a strong alternative you already hold before you ever sit down. He cites research (London Business School) that negotiators who know their alternatives set higher aspirations, make more aggressive first offers, and get better outcomes. The BATNA works as a **counter-anchor** in your mind and a decision standard: only accept deals better than your best alternative. He generalizes it across contexts — dating ("if you can only date 10s, a seven doesn't move you"), buyers ("edge them up from $9 to $10.50, $11"), and personal example: he's currently negotiating to buy a home Leila wants, but his best alternative is doing nothing and keeping the home he already loves, which puts the aggressively-priced, un-bid-on sellers in a weak spot — "it's who wants it less." The practical instruction is to **manufacture your BATNA before the table**: get multiple job offers, multiple vendor bids, multiple buyers/sellers for a business — echoing his own Gym Launch/Prestige Labs sale ("you only want to sell when you don't want to sell… buy when you don't want to buy"). He warns that going in needing the deal with no alternatives is "trying to win at poker only on bluffing" — he'd rather hold pocket aces. Finally, two ways to deploy a competing offer: **overtly** ("beat it or no worries, we won't waste time") or **held privately** so you can swing bigger — because revealing a $10 offer only invites a marginal beat, whereas confidence in your plan B lets you shoot for 11, 12, 15. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
