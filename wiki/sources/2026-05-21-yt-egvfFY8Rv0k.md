# Ads Have Changed Forever

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says paid media and organic content have merged. His paid and media teams
are now one, and the current play is to let the algorithm identify strong content, then
run the best content as ads with only a simple overlay or platform-native call to action.
He emphasizes saves, follows, and middle/bottom-funnel content over raw views.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex believes paid media and content have fully merged.
- His paid team and media team are now one team.
- Because his team publishes about 450 pieces of content per week, the algorithm helps
  identify which pieces are most interesting.
- The team takes winning content and runs it as ads.
- They used to add calls to action at the end of content, but now often use an overlay or
  banner with a simple click action.
- The same content can be used for different offers if it is relevant to the action.
- Middle- and bottom-funnel content with many saves performs especially well.
- Creators should look at content that produces follows and saves, not only views.
- Small teams can run all available good posts as ads and let the ad platform sort them.
- Ads that look like content align with platform incentives.
- Organic winners can work as ads; taking ads and posting them as organic content does not
  necessarily work in reverse.
- The best ads often come from short-form creators.
