# Why $1M Feels Harder Than $10M

## Summary

In this short teaching answer, Alex Hormozi advises an ads operator with one strong case study to get more proof before scaling client acquisition. He says some proof is better than none, but the first 10 testimonials are disproportionately valuable for conversion, sales, outreach, pricing power, and experience. He recommends working for free or at a discount to generate those testimonials faster than trying to sell 10 customers from scratch.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - sole substantive speaker. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- One compelling example is useful, but the operator still needs more volume and more proof.
- Getting 10 testimonials can shortcut the path to paid clients.
- The first 10 testimonials matter much more than incremental testimonials after a large proof base.
- Testimonials improve conversion pages, VSLs, sales processes, outreach response, and pricing power.
- It can be faster to get 10 customers by first serving 10 free or discounted customers, converting some to paid, and using their testimonials to win the next 10.
