# "I help web developers get jobs. How do I monetize?"

★ L3-candidate — introduces the "logical sale" concept for analytical buyers (engineers/developers) and the audience-survey step for choosing a paid follow-on offer; both are reusable sales/offer-design principles worth promoting to topics/persona.

## Summary
A guest running a free community of ~300 web developers asks Alex how to monetize — specifically whether to offer high-ticket one-on-one monthly support or a paid group with more courses than the free community. Alex first validates the audience: developers earn well and have big job/earning opportunities, so they carry both spending power and earning power. He suggests a low-priced monthly community (around $9.99/mo) as one option and points to "Farhoons" as a model for anything in the job-creation-for-a-niche space. His core prescription is to get avatar-clarity before building the paid offer: because "developers" is a broad audience splitting into subniches, he'd survey the group to learn whether members are there for love of coding, skill-upleveling, more money, or a job, then build the follow-on community around whatever they mostly want. A colleague notes they helped a ~100k-subscriber engineering YouTuber ("Life Engineered") monetize a "speedrun to promotion" offer that did $130k in 5 days purely because the audience has money. Alex closes with a sales tactic tailored to analytical buyers: run a "logical sale" — stick to facts, spell out the income delta and graduation rates in black-and-white terms — because engineers who make a logical purchase both buy and refund less.
