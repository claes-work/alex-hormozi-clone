# Charge 6K Upfront, Then $1K Forever (Here's How)

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a Ukraine-based founder who trains and places Ukrainian content partners into businesses. Alex recommends changing the offer from a simple placement/setup fee into a $6,000 upfront fee plus $1,000/month continuity, with the founder owning the relationship and paying the content partner about $500/month. He also simplifies the offer language and recommends affiliate/community-owner outreach with a repeated promotion cadence and two levels of testimonials.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - content-partner placement founder. His revenue, profit, Ukrainian talent model, audience, team, and partner experience are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The owner should charge more upfront while also owning the backend relationship.
- A suggested structure is roughly $6,000 upfront plus $1,000/month continuity, paying the content partner about $500/month.
- The best initial customers are people who already have working businesses and need more growth, not zero-to-one beginners.
- When the offer is confusing, describe the outcome in the simplest possible terms.
- Ad copy should say the company will hire, train, place, and manage a content person, and replace that person if they do not work out.
- Community owners can be a primary acquisition channel because they already aggregate the target customers.
- Affiliate / community partnerships should use a repeat promotion cadence, such as twice per year.
- Testimonials should be layered: proof for community owners that other owners made money, and proof for end customers that the service works.
- Warm introductions can be more effective than cold outreach in a small market.
- The owner should finish delivery automation, quantify capacity, then pursue volume without changing the offer again.
