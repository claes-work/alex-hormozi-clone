# How to Become Your Future Self Faster

## Summary

Alex asks listeners to imagine the focused, relentless competitor they would least want
to face and then treat that person as their future self. Becoming that person requires
repeatedly testing activities and relationships against the goal, including accepting
that some current relationships may need to end.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says retained distractions pose a larger risk to one's future
  than rivals do.
- He defines the feared competitor as focused, persistent, learning, improving, and
  rejecting anything that does not strengthen them.
- He repeatedly asks whether each person, activity, and behavior makes the goal more or
  less likely.
- Alex personally places his goals above friendships because he believes generalized
  goal achievement opens future relationships and opportunities.
- He says acquiring what one wants can require burning parts of what one currently has.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "That's future you. Now become him."

> "You have to kill your current self for your new self to have room to grow."

> "You do have to burn sometimes what you have in order to have what you want."

## Notes

Reinforces ruthless goal filtering and relationship tradeoffs; no new business framework.
