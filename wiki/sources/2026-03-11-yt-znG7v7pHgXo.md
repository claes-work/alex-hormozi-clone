# Advice for Employees Who Want to Start a Business

## Summary
Interview clip framed by a host who describes posting a "creator life" Instagram reel from a Greek island and getting comments like "I'd love to live this lifestyle, but I don't know where to start." The host asks Alex to break down the zero-to-six-figure jump for someone in a corporate/consulting job who hates their work and wants to make a living doing what they love. Alex first attacks the **employee mindset**: employees see top line as income. He notes he has employees who make $1M+/year, and that a $10M business owner might only take $1M in income depending on margins — so if you genuinely want six figures of *income*, you'll probably need ~$500k in *revenue*, otherwise "you just bought yourself another job." His central reframe: when you switch, **you're still an employee — just of a business you happen to own**, and you are basically 100% of the expenses, so the business itself makes no money; you wear all the hats and you've merely removed the person who gave you direction, making yourself responsible. He illustrates with consulting: quit your six-figure consulting job, consult on the side through your own LLC, and you have the same job minus a boss. The build sequence he lays out: you must **go get money → sell stuff → to sell you need people showing interest → so make your products known through advertising** (pick one of the "five ways to advertise" and do it consistently to bring people in), figure out **what to sell** (he points to the $100M Offers book), then sell it and deliver it. Once advertising + selling + an offer are consistent, start delegating **delivery by breaking it into chunks and specializing labor** — not "find someone to do what I do" (silly), but "how do I get five people to each do 20% of what I do, consistently?" Then increase advertising. He stresses **sales is very high leverage early**: if you spent all day just selling, you'd generate enough work for five people — and then you'd have a business. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
