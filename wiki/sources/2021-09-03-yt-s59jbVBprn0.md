# Entrepreneurs, stop starting new businesses. Fix the one you have (2021-09-03)

## Summary

Using three real conversations (a $550k/mo telehealth owner, a $4M/yr weight-loss
info-product seller, and a $150k/mo owner), Hormozi argues that entrepreneurs stuck at a
plateau wrongly reach for a *new* business when the real move is to remove the constraint
on the one they have. He frames this through the Theory of Constraints — a system grows
until something constrains it; find and fix the single true constraint, then the next —
and warns that a new business will only rise to the founder's existing level of
competence.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- **The stop-starting-businesses thesis:** when a business plateaus, entrepreneurs
  instinctively start a *new* business/product line because it feels good — but that's
  bad reasoning. The fix is almost always to grow the existing business, not add a new
  one. Full statement of the **focus / one-business thesis** proto-versioned in
  [[2021-08-16-yt-MLkFOaxIdxM]].
- The *only* rational reason a business can't grow that isn't your fault is reaching the
  total addressable market (market saturation) or a shrinking market (his newspaper-ad
  friend, whose industry shrank ~25%/yr). Otherwise "you just don't know how" — a
  solvable problem, not a reason to start something new.
- **Theory of Constraints** (his explicit mental model): a system grows until it hits a
  constraint; remove it and it grows to the next constraint. There is typically only
  *one* true constraint at a time. This is a foundational proto-**constraint framework**.
- Skill = prioritizing which problems to solve. Fast growth from the outside feels
  "manageable and clear" from the inside because everyone focuses on the one current
  constraint (e.g. scale sales team → then onboarding/training → then lead volume).
- Reframe deficiencies as questions: not "why am I not growing?" but "what is stopping us
  from doing an extra \$1M/month?" — better questions yield solvable problems. His opening
  question to any entrepreneur: *"What's stopping us from being at 5 million a month?"*
- **Businesses grow to the level of incompetence of the entrepreneur:** a \$4M entrepreneur
  starting a new business gets a \$4M business, because output is capped by their beliefs,
  traits, and skills. Unless you change one of those, the next venture is capped the same.
  Directly extends the "same ladder limit" idea from [[2021-08-16-yt-MLkFOaxIdxM]].
- You can't be CEO of two things; running one business is hard enough. No CEO (not
  founder) runs two public companies — cites Elon Musk as founder, not operator, the lone
  apparent exception.
- "We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught. Do the boring work, repeat
  successful actions." (Recurring tenet, also in [[2021-08-16-yt-MLkFOaxIdxM]].)

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Why don't you just take the thing that's making you 4 million and make it make you 10
> million dollars?"

> "I'm a big believer in the theory of constraints, which means that a system will grow
> until it is constrained... once you remove the constraint it'll continue to grow until
> it reaches another constraint."

> "There's really only typically one or two things that are constraining our growth, and
> by definition there'd really only be one thing that's the constraint."

> "The businesses will grow to the level of incompetence of the entrepreneur. So if you're
> a 4 million entrepreneur, the next business will be a 4 million business — doesn't matter
> what it is, because that is your level."

> "You can't be CEO of two things. It's hard enough to be in one business."

> "Whatever the run rate is... I'm like, what's stopping us from being at 5 million a
> month? And until we can clearly answer that question, there's nothing else we need to
> do."

> "We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught. Do the boring work, repeat
> successful actions."
