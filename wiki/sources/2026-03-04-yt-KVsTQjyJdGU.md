# If I Died Tomorrow...

## Summary
Short interview clip on mortality. Asked how he'd feel if told he'd die tomorrow, Alex says he's "good with it": he'd be bummed, not depressed or sad, because he's "lived life the way I want to live life" and is trying to "leave it all on the field." He accepts that "no one will remember me on a long enough time horizon" and is at peace with it. The core argument is that grief over death is a function of **expectations vs. reality**, not the death itself. He contrasts Betty White dying at 99 ("she lived a good life") with Kobe Bryant dying "before his time," and illustrates with a personal story: his cat died at 2 years old and he was very bummed — until he realized the sadness came only from the belief the cat *should* have lived longer. Reframing ("what if cats only lived 6 months and I got to have him for 2 years?") made him feel grateful instead of grieving. He applies the same lens to human lifespans: people expect to live to 100, but average life expectancy is ~74 (so 36 is genuinely middle-aged), and 500 years ago it was ~35. If he'd expected to live 20 years and made it to 33, he'd be "stoked" — noting Jesus lived to 33 and "did a lot more than I have." The only reason he'd be upset to die is "if I demanded from the universe that I live longer." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
