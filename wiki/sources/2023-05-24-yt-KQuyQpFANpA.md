# 3 Simple Fixes that Grow Any Business

## Summary

Using three real business owners from an event Q&A — an appointment-setting business, an
HVAC marketing agency, and a 30–40M-subscriber influencer — Hormozi shows that all three
shared the same misdiagnosed constraint and that the real leverage was to own more of the
result rather than just sell a service. He argues via the theory of constraints that a
business grows only up to its true constraint, and closes with the four-question framework
he uses to pick which problem to solve.

## Key claims

- Theory of constraints: a business grows up to its constraint and then grows no longer;
  most entrepreneurs work on what they enjoy instead of the actual constraint because they
  haven't identified it correctly.
- "More customers" is usually not the real problem — it's that existing customers keep
  leaving (retention/quality), so improving the lowest-converting step yields far more than
  incrementally improving every step.
- The real leverage across all three cases is to own more of the economic result (equity,
  partnership, or building/buying the underlying business) rather than only capturing a
  service fee — "at a certain point, you are the business."
- The fastest way to build a $10M business is often not the fastest way to build a $100M
  business — you must lay a different foundation, like a 10-story vs 100-story building.
- It's the size of the slice of the pie that matters, not its shape (he notes the average
  IPO founder owns ~12.5% of the company they founded).
- Problem-selection framework: (1) what problem are we solving, (2) over what time horizon,
  (3) which problem would we prefer / feel more equipped to solve.
