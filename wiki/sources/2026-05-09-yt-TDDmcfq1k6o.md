# Acceptance vs Good Enough: The Biggest Embodiment of Failure

## Summary

Alex distinguishes accepting circumstances from accepting current performance as the
final standard. He says one should accept the hand dealt and the outcomes created by
past work, but not treat that as "good enough" or as the end state. He connects hard
times to success as necessary context and argues that examples from more successful
people can help listeners recognize what the right path feels like while they are in it.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says people should accept outcomes, circumstances, and the hand they were dealt.
- He says rebelling against fixed circumstances is fighting the universe.
- He rejects defining acceptance as comfort with who one is and refusal to become better.
- He distinguishes "I am good enough" from "I am good."
- He says one can accept that past work created the current outcome without accepting
  the current outcome as final.
- He says the only acceptance he uses is accepting the gap between who he is and who he
  wants to become.
- He says big goals require hard times, and hard times can be the feeling of being on
  the right path.
- He says experience from others gives context for recognizing new business constraints,
  such as a missing finance function.
- He says high agency means making decisions independent from other people's opinions.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You accept outcomes and you accept circumstances."

> "I am good enough and I am good."

> "The hard times are a requisite for success."
