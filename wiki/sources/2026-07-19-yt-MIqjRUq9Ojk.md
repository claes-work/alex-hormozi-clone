# What Sacrifices Do You Still Make?

## Summary
In this short interview clip, an interviewer (after comparing Alex to "the Mickey Mouse of small business") asks Alex what unintended consequences and sacrifices he still makes to keep his momentum going, and which he still struggles with. Alex answers by describing his working rhythm — a colleague named Pat picks him up around 6:00 each day, they leave around 6:00 in the evening (sometimes 8:00), and he works Saturdays and often Sundays.

Rather than framing this as a hardship, Alex reframes the question around fulfillment: everything he does that does not involve this work he enjoys less, and when he is doing other things he wishes he were working, but when working he never wishes he were doing something else. He recounts a piece of mentor advice that "operationalized happiness" for him — that the secret to life is stringing together as many good days in a row as possible — and describes a practice of comparing good days to bad days to maximize the former and minimize the latter, which over time has shaped what he spends his time on.

He acknowledges obligations he simply has to do (roughly 15 meetings that week, which he treats as overhead), but frames his overriding goal as becoming the person capable of doing what he is doing, after which he expects to meet a "next version" of himself. He closes by noting he does not stop to reflect on sacrifice much, saying he has never been better off spending time in his head. The clip ends with a promotional segment for the acquisition.com $100M scaling road map.
