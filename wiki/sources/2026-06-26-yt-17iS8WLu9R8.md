# The Minor League/Major League Model for Scaling a Team

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a real-estate team leader doing about $6M-$6.5M who wants to add brokerage services outside the core team model and expand geographically. Alex recommends a minor league/major league ascension model for agents, adds a future JV layer, warns against letting Zillow Flex become too much of the business, and says geographic expansion needs a local market leader who can duplicate the model.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Brad** - real-estate team/brokerage leader. His revenue, Zillow Flex reliance, agent count, and expansion plans are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If the infrastructure exists, a real-estate team can add a brokerage-style layer for agents who are not a fit for the core team model.
- Alex frames the brokerage layer as a minor league and the team model as the major league.
- Agents who perform well in the minor league can be promoted to the major league; agents who do not fit the major league can move back down while staying in the ecosystem.
- As the business grows, a JV model and new-agent development program can create a cleaner ascension path across JV, minor league, and major league.
- Zillow Flex or similar referral-fee sources should be kept to roughly one third or less of the business because the 40% referral fee crushes gross margin.
- The minor-league brokerage can use a cap so the business knows what each capped agent contributes.
- Geographic expansion breaks when the company assumes the mothership can duplicate the model in a new market without local leadership.
- A new market should be built top-down with a local leader who can train, lead, and duplicate the current model.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You want to think about it as a minor league, major league model."

> "If I... am doing well in the minor leagues, you promote me up to the majors."

> "You have a clean ascension among all three."

> "You want to get your Zillow portion to a third or under of your business and keep it there."

> "That 40% referral fee is not going down anytime soon."

> "If you just think that the mothership's going to serve the expansion, it's going to be difficult."

> "I would do top-down in that market, not bottom-up."
