# The Key to Success: Consistency Over Intensity

## Summary

Alex argues that consistency is usually harder than intensity. People often suffer
through excessive short-term intensity instead of sustaining a lower-intensity behavior
for longer. He extends the point to decision-making under uncertainty: beginning creates
the feedback needed to improve the decision, while waiting for perfect information keeps
people stuck at the starting line.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says information and intensity rarely make success hard; sticking with it does.
- He says quitting breaks consistency.
- He says the intensity required for success is often lower than people expect.
- He says people often suffer too much over a short period instead of doing a lower
  intensity behavior consistently.
- He says personal development is befriending uncertainty.
- He says early decisions usually require incomplete data.
- He recommends making the best bad guess and being directionally correct rather than
  searching for perfect information.
- He says making a decision creates the feedback needed to improve later decisions.
- He says beginners with nothing to lose are in good conditions for risk because the
  downside is often baseline.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's the sticking with it that makes it hard."

> "The path of personal development is befriending uncertainty."

> "Best bad guess."
