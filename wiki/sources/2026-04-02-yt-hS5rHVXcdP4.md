# Helping a Marketing Agency Stuck at $2.5M Scale to $10M

## Summary

Alex makes an atypical recommendation for an MSP marketing agency: pivot toward its
lower-priced website subscription because its reported margins and retention outperform
the core agency. The standardized service is largely handed to a partner after setup,
making acquisition capacity the primary scaling requirement.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports about $300,000 monthly recurring revenue, 2.5% monthly agency
  churn, 1% churn on the $500 subscription, 75% subscription gross margin, and 60
  subscription customers; none is independently verified.
- As of April 2026, Alex says the low-ticket offer appears to be the better business
  because it combines lower churn, higher gross margins, a larger market, and lighter delivery.
- Since a partner handles much of the ongoing service, he identifies a repeatable sales
  machine and setup process, rather than additional service staff, as the main requirement.
- At 500 annual additions, he estimates the company would add $3 million of annual
  recurring revenue; this is illustrative arithmetic and not a verified forecast.
- He considers roughly 1% monthly churn unusually strong for the very-small-business or
  prosumer avatar and sees that stickiness as favorable to enterprise value.
- His recommendation is explicitly case-specific: low ticket is not automatically better,
  but the owner's reported unit economics support concentrating on it here.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I actually like the low ticket thing better."

> "You really just need to have a sales machine."

> "I actually do think it's a better business."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: judge low-versus-high ticket through retention, margin, market size, and delivery leverage rather than price prestige.
