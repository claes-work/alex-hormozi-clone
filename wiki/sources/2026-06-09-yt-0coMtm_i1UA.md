# How to Scale a White Label Agency Past $1.5M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a white-label media-services business for PR firms that has been stuck around $1.5M for five years. The agencies come organically but churn because they do not use the service consistently. Alex says the business has not treated agencies as customers with their own journey. He recommends thinking like an affiliate/distribution-layer business, pricing lower with usage components for volatile SMB agencies, and solving for 80% of agencies still sending business every month next year.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The business should treat PR agencies as the customer/distribution layer, not merely as pass-through buyers.
- High agency churn suggests there is no proper customer journey for the agencies.
- Alex points to the affiliate chapter in `$100M Leads` as the relevant model for building a distribution layer.
- A launch can create immediate time-to-value for distributors before the long-term journey matures.
- If agencies churn because they are not using the articles, a lower base fee with a usage component may fit better.
- SMB volatility becomes the vendor's volatility if the price is high enough to cancel in bad months.
- Serving SMBs in marketing/agency capacity often fails to stack because the clients are poor operators.
- The business is likely priced too high for most customers and too low for the customers that could maximize profit.
- The core constraint is getting 80% of agencies next year to still refer business every month.
