# Helping an Agency Owner Build a Sales Team

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client owns an agency selling growth/profitability services to real estate agents (~$17–18M/year revenue, targeting $100M); the bottleneck is that he and his COO are the two best closers for external growth and prospects keep pulling them into closing meetings (~20 hours/week for the owner), capping the founders' combined external revenue at ~$5M/year. He's realized he "needs to find more of me." Alex reframes the real question as **how to systematize the sales process so a new hire has an increased likelihood of success**, and delivers his core sales-team-building framework. First, an **identity shift**: the owner and COO must go from "sales god" to "sales training god" and treat every deal *they* close as a failure, not a success. Second, a **comp lever**: stop paying commissions on deals the founder helps close, which quickly kills reps' incentive to keep asking him in. The diagnostic: the client almost certainly runs a **founder-driven sales process** — consultative, reliant on heavy industry knowledge and authority — which does not scale (a 22-year-old rep can't lean on the same authority when talking to an $18M/year business owner). A sales process that scales is **based on the prospect, not the salesman**: the rep asks the right questions in the right order and provides *no answers*, leading the prospect to their own decision. Alex lays out the structure — a good intro frame → discovery (prospect states pain points) → match pains to the solution set → pitch moment ("here's how we solve these: thing one, two, three") → tie each stated problem to a solution. He argues ~97% of businesses don't know how to train sales (they hire reps and "see who sticks"); real training is a 100%-trainable reinforcement loop of getting someone to say the right thing at the right time, and most people don't train nearly enough (should be daily). On scripting: the script is probably too long and could be cut by half or more — give the buyer the *absolute minimum* information to make a purchasing decision, then answer only questions **related to the sale, not the product**. His "secretary / heart surgery" analogy: the rep is the secretary confirming you have a "heart that needs surgery," not the surgeon explaining the operation. Consultative closing makes the founder the hero instead of the prospect, which is exactly what undermines the reps. Ends with the $100M scaling roadmap promo (acquisitions.com/roadmap).
