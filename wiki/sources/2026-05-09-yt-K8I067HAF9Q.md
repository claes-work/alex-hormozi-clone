# What Makes Business So Difficult, and How to Win Anyways

## Summary

Alex explains business difficulty as the pain of confronting the gap between current
ability and desired outcomes. He says many entrepreneurs quit during the early years
because they feel dumb, compare themselves, and expect faster progress. His answer is
to treat business as an infinite game: keep rolling the die, learn through the losses,
and stay in the game.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says business becomes emotionally hard because growth requires admitting one's
  current self is not good enough for the desired outcome.
- He says the painful parts include not knowing, feeling dumb, comparison, ego, and
  plateauing.
- He says a plateau often means the entrepreneur has not changed behavior.
- He says it commonly takes about five all-in years to begin grasping business.
- He distinguishes all-in work from weekends, reading, or side-business dabbling.
- He describes business as an infinite game where the players change but the game
  continues.
- He references the many-sided-die frame from his Offers and Leads books: more primary
  actions create more chances for a green outcome.
- He says quitting is the only true loss in an infinite game.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I'm not good enough."

> "You need to stay in the game."

> "In infinite games, there are no winners and losers. There is only players and
> quitters."
