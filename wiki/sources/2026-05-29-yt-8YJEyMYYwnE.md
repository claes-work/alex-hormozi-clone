# How to See Reality More Clearly Than 99% of People

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains his project of defining abstract personal-development words in observable terms. Learning is "same condition, new behavior"; intelligence is rate of learning; excuses are statements to avoid punishment; patience is figuring out what to do in the meantime; trauma is permanent behavior change after an aversive stimulus. He argues that defining traits behaviorally improves persuasion, training, management, relationships, and self-change.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Learning means the same condition produces new behavior.
- Intelligence, in this behavioral frame, is rate of learning.
- An excuse is a statement made to avoid punishment.
- Patience can be operationalized as figuring out what to do in the meantime.
- Resilience is returning to baseline behavior; trauma is permanent behavior change after a negative stimulus.
- Terms should be defined in the observable universe so people can agree on what occurred.
- Good communicators transfer ideas efficiently by using observable language.
- Bundled labels like "confident," "lazy," or "acting like a dick" should be broken into specific behaviors.
- Training improves when abstract traits are translated into visible actions a person can repeat.
- Alex says many communication failures happen because people use words neither side has defined.
- Traits are usually continuums and context-specific, not binaries.
