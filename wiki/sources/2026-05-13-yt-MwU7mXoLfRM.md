# Helping a $2M Coaching Biz Hit $20M (The Supply Problem)

## Summary

Alex coaches a TikTok Shop affiliate mentor on scaling from about $2M toward $20M. Alex
identifies the true constraint as brand-deal supply, not coaching capacity. He reframes
the business as a mini marketplace: the hard side is brands with money, while demand
from influencers is easier. He recommends platform partnerships, recruiting brand
acquirers, outbound to brands, and eventually AI workflows to automate delivery before
scaling too fast.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The business is supply constrained if it cannot source enough brand deals.
- In a marketplace, start with the hard side.
- For this business, brands with money are the hard side; influencers wanting money are
  easier to find.
- The founder should first try to partner with platforms that already aggregate brand
  deals.
- If that fails, he should recruit someone who acquires brands from such a platform.
- A third option is AI/outbound to brands over a defined follower or sales threshold.
- The founder should measure LTV-to-CAC on brand acquisition.
- The business must decide whether to be high-end/talent-agency style or high-volume
  marketplace style.
- Revenue retention is what makes the company valuable, provided gross margins are good.
- Alex says the founder should document daily actions into workflows and automate them
  with AI before scaling headcount.
- The founder should go slow to go fast: fix ops until time approaches zero, then scale.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Whenever you start a marketplace, you always want to start with the hard side."

> "The thing that makes a company valuable is revenue retention, period."

> "Go slow to go fast."
