# How To Scale Yourself: The What-Why-How Framework

## Summary

In this solo video Hormozi gives a simple three-part diagnostic for why an employee isn't
delivering the outcome you want. Crediting Andy Grove's *High Output Management* for the
first two levels and his own experience for the third, he argues every performance gap
reduces to one of three missing things — the person doesn't know **what** you want (a
communication problem), doesn't know **how** to do it (a training problem), or isn't
**motivated** to do it (a why/motivation problem) — and that scaling yourself through others
means diagnosing and closing whichever gap is present, always assuming the fault is your own.

## The What-Why-How framework (as presented, 2021-07-05)

When someone is not doing what you want, ask in order:

1. **WHAT — do they know what I want them to do?** If not, it's a **communication** problem.
   Rule of thumb: *"if it's not written, it never happened"* — every verbal directive must be
   followed up in Slack / Asana / email so there is a digital record.
2. **HOW — do they know how to do it?** If not, it's a **training** problem. Solve by
   providing training, buying a course, hopping on a call, or assigning a manager/peer to
   train them.
3. **WHY / MOTIVATION — are they motivated to do it?** If they know what and how but still
   aren't doing it, it's a **motivation** problem, driven mainly by two things:
   - They don't understand **why the role matters** within the larger company (they feel like
     a cog / that their work doesn't matter → creates busy-work). Fix: tie every function to
     how it contributes to the bottom line.
   - They aren't **bought into the vision/mission** — the "soft, woo-woo culture stuff" that
     becomes critical at scale.

He notes people usually have two of the three and are missing one; diagnosing which is how
you "scale decision-making," which he says comes down to two levers: **culture and training.**

## Relation to the Talent Grid (2021-05-31)

This framework shares the *What / How* vocabulary with the earlier **Talent Grid**
([[2021-05-31-yt-gpKz22P84iM]]) but is a **distinct tool**. The Talent Grid grades **skill
level** (What→How→Who ≙ doing→teaching→systematizing, IC→Manager→Leader). The What-Why-How
framework is a **diagnostic for a single underperforming employee** (communication →
training → motivation). Note the third axis differs: the Talent Grid's third level is
**"Who" (systematizing)**, whereas here the third level is **"Why" (motivation)** — despite
the video's title being "What-Why-How," the spoken order is What → How → Why/motivation.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021-07-05)

- (2021) Business "expands based on multiplication" — repetition and scale are fundamental,
  so you must learn to scale yourself **through others**.
- (2021) Every failure to get an outcome from an employee is one of exactly three things:
  they don't know what, don't know how, or aren't motivated. "Simple scales, fancy fails."
- (2021) First two levels are from **Andy Grove's *High Output Management*** ("the GOAT of
  operations"); the motivation layer is Hormozi's own addition.
- (2021) Always **assume it's your fault** first when someone isn't executing.
- (2021) *"If it's not written it never happened"* — directives need a digital paper trail.
- (2021) The two things you actually scale are **culture and training**; that's how you scale
  decision-making.
- (2021) Common two-of-three patterns: motivated + knows-what but can't do it → hides being
  busy out of shame; motivated + trained but doesn't know what → paralyzed or works on
  non-priorities; knows-what + trained but unmotivated → checks out, avoids confrontation,
  probably job-hunting (the worst case).
