# 'What Is the Most Valuable Social Media Channel?'

## Summary
Panel/Q&A clip. Asked which social channels give the highest ROI, Alex and others converge on **YouTube and Instagram** as the top two for most creators — for monetization specifically. Alex references a big video he made breaking down all of Acquisition.com's lead flow by source, and says he ranks platforms "S-tier or whatever" on two axes: **how much discoverability** a platform offers, and **how easy it is to generate purchase intent** (cost-to-action). His key data point, drawn from Skool: **Instagram generates significantly more traffic than YouTube, but YouTube traffic converts at ~3–4x the rate** — so with attribution visible, YouTube makes community owners more money even though Instagram sends more volume. He stresses you don't need a big following to see this ("some people have like a thousand followers — you don't need a lot, you just need something"). A tactical hack: people monetize **TikTok** poorly on-platform, but do far better if the TikTok CTA routes traffic to Instagram ("DM me on IG") — for whatever reason people transact more readily on Instagram, especially for **more expensive** offers. He classifies **podcasts and email as middle-of-funnel / follow-up**, not discovery: nobody discovers a newsletter, and podcasts are now very hard to get discovered through — more realistically you repurpose YouTube content into podcast material and people find the podcast via YouTube. Bottom line: if he had to bet on two platforms for monetization, it's YouTube and Instagram. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
