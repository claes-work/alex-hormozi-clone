# How to Scale a Low-Ticket Digital Product Business to $1.2M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a founder with two low-ticket digital-video-product brands: a recurring church media product and one-time video-editor assets. Alex says the church subscription is simple and elegant enough to keep scaling rather than adding a high-touch consulting offer that would become a different business. He recommends reinvesting profit into talent and brand, using AI to reduce headcount needs, and hiring for taste/strategy where AI output alone is not good enough.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - digital-video-product founder. His revenue, customer pool, church/editor brands, margins, and content bottleneck are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The recurring church product appears to be the core business, while the editor assets are a low-time offshoot.
- A high-touch process-consulting offer for churches would likely be a different business.
- There is nothing wrong with one simple product if it can keep scaling and does not limit LTV.
- The company should focus on increasing demand for the existing product instead of inventing a new offer.
- In low-CapEx digital/service-like businesses, profit should be reinvested into talent and brand, not fully distributed.
- Brand is the reputation investment that lets the business keep growing next year.
- AI should do much of the execution work, so the team should be fewer and better.
- Taste and strategic thinking remain important where generic AI output feels wrong.
- Recruiting media talent without a brand is difficult because strong media talent has other earning options.
