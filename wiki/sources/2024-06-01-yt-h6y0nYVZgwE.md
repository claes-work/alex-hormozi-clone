# If you're not unbelievably rich yet, this is why

## Summary

A recorded talk Alex Hormozi gave to a room of gym owners at a fitness-business
conference ("Jim Con"), aimed at businesses doing roughly $10k–$150k/month. Part one
diagnoses why a business isn't growing using an inputs → efficiency → output model:
advertise enough (content, paid ads, outreach, referral partners), then work the
conversion chain (schedule → show → close → price → churn). Part two is about
reverse-engineering a goal into daily activity counts and removing the personal
constraints that stop owners from doing the work — buying back 96 hours/month of "human
stuff" for ~$1,500, spending the first 4 hours of every day on the business's single
constraint, and firing the underperformer ("Susan").

## Key claims

- Every business is a system of **inputs → efficiencies → output** (output = money).
  Most owners can't name their inputs, so they can't grow.
- **Step one is always: people have to know you exist.** The only ways to generate
  awareness are content, paid ads, outreach, and referral/affiliate partners.
- For a **local business, the purpose of content is not to get customers but to convert
  a higher percentage** — platform algorithms are global, so out-of-area views don't
  help. Content lets referred/reached-out/ad-clicked prospects verify you look legit.
- If you make zero content, one piece a week is a high-ROI "heartbeat." If content is
  your primary channel, drive volume and quality (his own operation posts ~400 pieces/week).
- **Rule of thumb: spend 4 hours a day, 6 days a week on your primary advertising
  activity.** Whatever the biggest problem in the business is, that's what the first 4
  hours of every day go to, until it's solved.
- **Brand is built three ways: what you say, what others say, and what people experience.**
  The first two are advertising (approximations/promises); the third is the product. A
  local brand is won or lost on delivery matching the promise.
- Spend ad money **before you eat** — it's how the business "eats" and distributes its
  message.
- His personal ad-making hack: he keeps a screenshot album of ads he likes, re-drinks in
  his all-time greatest ads before making new ones, merges new elements with proven
  ones, and simply **re-runs winning ads verbatim**.
- **In a service business there are almost no real emergencies** — owners get distracted
  by perceived emergencies; the real emergency is not doing the growth work.
- **The rule of 100** (100 outreach activities/primary actions per day) is "consistent
  as gravity." To get a referral partner, ask, give gifts, and send them business first.
- The only two levers of a business: **get more customers** (more traffic + better
  conversion) or **make them worth more** (raise price + decrease churn). Decreasing
  churn = getting them to buy for 10 months instead of 5 or 3.
- Conversion breaks into **schedule rate → show rate → close rate**; fix whichever is
  below benchmark. "All leads suck until they become customers."
- **Sales pro tip: whoever answers questions in the sale is the one losing.** Sidestep
  every objection by asking what the prospect is really trying to get from the question.
- **Most gyms are mispriced too low** — at that price they can't profit; a full gym that
  makes no money is proof. Raising price is a bet you must then deliver on.
- **Churn tactics:** exit interviews (save ~half of leavers), biweekly pulse-check
  touches, attendance tracking with proactive "I missed you" outreach, and heavy
  investment in the first 24–72 hours of onboarding.
- **Buy back ~96 hours/month** (food ~24h, cleaning ~20h, laundry ~19h, per his recalled
  stats) for ~$1,500/month so you can reinvest those hours into the business. Worth it if
  you can earn more than ~$15/hour actively working ($1,500 ÷ 96).
- **Every business has exactly one constraint;** de-constrain it and it grows to the next
  one. Focus on a "to-don't list," not the to-do list.
- Reverse-engineer goals: measure activities per customer (e.g., 6 posts → 1 customer,
  or 100 outreaches → 1 customer), then multiply the daily activity count to hit the goal.
- **Volume compounds skill** — "the rich keep getting richer... they do more and by doing
  more they get more from their more." Low volume keeps you sucking.
- The **"Susan" mindset**: owners who buy education/help only to prove it won't work, so
  they can be self-affirmed that failure isn't their fault. Fitness people should learn
  to love business the way they love their body.
- **Fire the underperformer ("Susan"/"Todd").** A bad employee sets your standard,
  repels good hires, hurts customers, and drains the owner's time. Most owners won't do
  it because they can't have the hard conversation.
- **"Double time":** spend half the day on tomorrow's job and half on today's; taking
  over a bad employee's 20-hour job often reveals you can do it in 5 and clean up systems.
- Management: **"give people a cookie when a plane lands on time," not only when planes
  crash** — praise good behavior or it stops.
- You can train anyone to do anything; the question is whether it's worth the cost.
  All business is reallocation of time, money, and energy to the best returns.
- **Scaling brick-and-mortar service chains requires an operator with profit share**
  (typically 10–20%, earning ~$75k–$100k/year). If you can't leave the business for 6
  months and have it still grow, it's not an asset yet.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

  makes you money. And everything that you're doing that's not this is not making you as
  much money as doing more of this."
- "Brand can only be built in three ways. One is what you say. The second is what other
  people say. And the third is what people experience themselves."
- "The fires are going to happen and they will get put out. You'll also find that if you
  ignore them, they tend to also go away."
- "No one can oppose you if you make no statements. So whoever answers questions in the
  sale is the one who's losing."
- "That's why the rich keep getting richer — they do more, and by doing more, they get
  more from their more. They get better, too."
- "It doesn't make you tough because you like it. What makes you tough is doing shit you
  don't like."
- "Everything you want is on the other side of you getting the business that works, that
  generates a profit, that keeps your customers happy, that provides for your team."
- "If you treated your business the way you treated your body, you'd make a shitload more
  money."
- "It's easy to have a hard conversation with someone you hate... Hard if you like the
  person. But this is what being a grown-up is."
- "If you can't leave the business for 6 months and it still grows, you are not ready,
  the business model isn't ready... Welcome to growing a legitimate business."
