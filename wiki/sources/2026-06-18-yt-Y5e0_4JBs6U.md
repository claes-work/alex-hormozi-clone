# You're Not Overexpanded, You're Undertalented

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a vertically integrated coffee company with three retail locations and a roasting operation. Alex says the wholesale roasting side is acceptable if it consumes no founder attention, but the retail business should not scale from 15% margins. He recommends fixing retail profitability first through pricing, menu engineering, staffing mix, compensation, register scripts, marketing, attach rates, inventory, and cash-conversion-cycle improvements before opening more stores.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - coffee retail and roasting owner. His revenue, location count, wholesale customers, margins, integration structure, and expansion goals are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The roasting and retail pieces are aligned enough that the company is not necessarily overexpanded.
- The business is "undertalented" rather than overexpanded if the founder lacks people to improve the model.
- Retail margins should be fixed before scaling to many locations.
- A store model should produce so much profit that opening another store feels inevitable.
- Wholesale roasting is acceptable if it takes zero founder attention and supports vertical integration.
- The retail margin problem likely requires many small operational fixes rather than one big change.
- Fixes include staffing mix, compensation, pricing, menu layout, high-margin item mix, register scripts, attach-rate incentives, local marketing, inventory, and cash conversion.
- Alex strongly discourages opening more stores before tripling profit from the current stores.
