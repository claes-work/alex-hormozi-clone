# Selling Outcomes Beats Selling Activity Every Time

## Summary
In a short coaching exchange, a business owner who sells lead-generation/listing services connecting personal-injury attorneys to physicians describes a bottleneck: his physician clients fixate on the raw count of referrals per month, which is hard to guarantee because accidents are unpredictable. Alex advises reframing the offer from lead count to revenue volume — the outcome clients actually care about — which reduces the pressure on any single lead number. He also advises against bolting on a branding/marketing service, recommending the owner stay focused on the core offer that already works.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that the bottleneck is largely how the offer is framed to clients, not the underlying delivery: physicians anchoring on lead count is a framing problem.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends reframing the promise from lead count to revenue volume (e.g. "usually around $20,000 worth of revenue volume"), since leads translate to revenue and revenue is what the client actually cares about — this makes any specific lead number "no longer matter nearly as much."
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that framing on revenue volume also absorbs natural variability (sometimes two or three cases, sometimes five or six).
- As of March 2026, Alex treats branding/marketing as a different business: either sell it separately with its own value proposition, or — his stated preference — skip it and keep doing the main thing that is working.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "But I think if you just reframe instead of lead count to revenue volume, which is also what you care about anyways, like leads translate to revenue."

> "And if you're saying that there's some variability there, I think the lead number will no longer matter nearly as much based on how you're just framing it."

> "In terms of the whole branding and all the other stuff, I think that's a different business. And so either you would start that as an additional service that you charge separately for based on its own value proposition, or my preferred method, don't do that and just continue to do the main thing that's working."
