# Most Businesses Do Lots of Small Things That Don't Matter

## Summary

Alex defines focus as the quantity and quality of things a founder says no to. He argues
most business communication and data are useless unless they change behavior. His strategic
test is the "big domino": if making one thing true would make everything else irrelevant,
then the other things do not deserve attention.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-18, Alex says focus is learning to say no and playing out the consequences of
  saying no.
- He says most business "emergencies" are not emergencies; unless people are dying or the
  company cannot transact, most interruptions can wait.
- He trains nearby team members to ask what the information changes about his behavior; if
  it changes nothing, it is irrelevant.
- He says data only matters if it changes what the team does.
- He says the measure of focus is the quantity and quality of things a person says no to,
  citing Steve Jobs's executive-meeting question about what people had recently rejected.
- He uses a "big domino" test: if one thing would solve all the problems, everything else
  becomes lower-return effort.
- Self-reported: around 2022, he chose building the biggest business brand as the one big
  thing because it would create more at-bats, improve deal flow, reduce negotiation burden,
  and make him better at business content.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If it's not going to change my behavior, it's irrelevant."

> "The only point of data is to change what you do."

> "The quantity and quality of things that you say no to is the measure of your focus."

> "Most businesses don't know what that one big thing is, so they do lots of small things that don't matter as much."
