# I Ran This Test, Here's What Happened

## Summary

Alex explains how to market inside Skool or any community platform from zero. He dislikes
the vague instruction to "provide value" because it often produces listicles from people
without experience. Instead, value means doing something that saves others time, then
reporting what happened. He points to high-value community contributors who post results
from work they actually did and says comments can create micro-value the same way.

## Key claims

- Alex says the same community-marketing strategy works on Skool, Reddit, Instagram, or
  Facebook.
- "Provide value" should be unpacked into doing work that saves others time or effort.
- Instead of telling people what to do, a creator should report an experiment, results,
  findings, and lessons.
- Alex summarizes his own content as "something I did, and this is how it worked."
- Posting real tests and findings is more credible than listicles from someone without
  results.
- Thoughtful comments that add context create goodwill with the original poster and
  audience.
