# The Only Thing That Will Protect Your Business from AI

## Summary
Short monologue. Alex's thesis for 2026: "in the world of AI, your brand is your moat." Because products and services get commoditized and content is easy to reproduce, the durable defense is *who and what you associate with* — that's what branding actually is. For a local business the brand is mostly word-of-mouth plus online reviews; for almost everyone else it's that plus the public content you make. His to-the-point prescription: "give away the secrets, sell the implementation" — people won't do the work themselves (or won't do it alone), and even DIY-capable buyers (himself included) will pay to go faster and de-risk rather than "pay the tax of ignorance." He says building a 2026 brand means choosing *curated associations* that reinforce the position you want in prospects' minds, and that his own 2026 plan is to do far more public "business fixing" as a demonstration of skill — do it "until your eyes bleed" because the market won't even know you exist by the time you're tired. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex's headline claim: "in the world of AI, your brand is your moat" — products/services become commoditized and content is easy to reproduce, so brand is the defensible asset.
- As of March 2026, he defines branding as "who and what you associate with" — the act of curating associations, not a logo or aesthetic.
- As of March 2026, he says a local business's brand is "mostly word of mouth and the reviews about you online"; for nearly every other business it's that *plus all the content you make publicly.*
- As of March 2026, he restates "give away the secrets, sell the implementation" as the sharpest version of the strategy: people don't want to do the work, or don't want to do it alone (plumbing, relationships, business — they'll still call you).
- As of March 2026, he includes himself: "I will always pay to go faster and decrease risk… I don't want to pay the tax of ignorance."
- As of March 2026, he says building a brand in 2026 means asking "what are the curated associations… that will reinforce the position I want to have in my prospects' minds" — link yourself to things they already like.
- As of March 2026 (forward-looking / personal plan): he intends to do "a lot more business fixing next year," building infrastructure to demonstrate a skill "very few people can do" as much as possible, because "the market won't even know you exist by the time you're getting tired."
