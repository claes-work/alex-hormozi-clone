# How to Start Advertising as a Beginner

## Summary

Alex Hormozi applies the Rule of 100 to advertising and beginner attention building:
reach out, make and post content, or make paid ads every day. He adds a beginner tactic
from the Skool Games: leave the best thoughtful comment on posts already attracting the
target avatar, then use the founder's asymmetric position against bigger competitors.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Beginners need to let more people know about their stuff.
- The Rule of 100 can mean 100 reach-outs per day, 100 minutes making and posting content,
  or 100 minutes making paid ads.
- Making content without posting does not count.
- Paid-ad practice includes research, hook analysis, recording, editing, cutting, and
  spending money.
- Monthly ad spend should be relative to what the business is willing to pay per customer.
- Comments on other people's posts are less limited than content volume or DMs.
- A strong comment on a post relevant to the avatar can hitchhike on that post's views.
- Alex recommends thoughtful, additive comments rather than dunking or tearing people
  down.
- New or smaller players should write down their current-position advantage against the
  market leader.
- Marketing should state factual differences that the leader cannot argue with.
- Going one level deeper makes marketing more compelling.
- A small entrant does not compete with the whole giant; it competes with a specific
  weak point or edge.
