# Brutally Honest Advice For Men to Win In Their 20s

## Summary

In this monologue, Alex Hormozi lays out a road map for young men (roughly 16–28) to build skills and wealth in their 20s. He argues you are valued on your utility, that in your youth you have energy but no skills, and that you should trade that energy for experience by working extreme hours, ruthlessly cutting spending to build a savings buffer, and out-working everyone in the room. He frames wealth-building as delayed-outcome decision-making ("patient with your outputs, impatient with your inputs") and stresses that high-volume sales experience plus self-feedback is how transferable skills compound, closing with a self-deprecating story of his own first, botched sales conversation.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- You are valued based on your **utility**; when you're young you have no utility but lots of energy, so trade energy for "experience points" and become useful.
- The **first rule of entrepreneurship is "use what you've got"** — it's about resourcefulness, not resources; people fail to start because they blame missing capital/connections.
- You only get good at a skill by **repeating it, failing, and making incremental improvements** over many repetitions.
- Advice framed as "not nice but kind" — it may hurt because it's honest.
- For the "super broke" with no skill set, go **completely on defense** to build a savings buffer so you can start taking bets (on education, opportunities, inventory, software).
- A **big savings stockpile buys patience** — the more money you have, the longer-term you can think, because you're out of survival mode and into "thrive mode."
- Survival vs. thrive is **relative to spend**: make $40k/month and spend $40k and you're still in survival; make $40k and spend $4k and you bank ~10 months of income each month.
- Five moves to spend way less when broke: (1) stop eating out, discount groceries only; (2) stop buying new clothes, Goodwill only; (3) only attend **free** friend events; (4) apply to higher-paying jobs / side hustles / learn tradeable skills; (5) find the hardest worker in the room and **double their input**.
- **"If they don't support your goals, they are not your friends"** — some people you call friends are actually enemies.
- **Caveat to the frugality:** if you already have money-making skills, don't waste time cooking or grocery shopping — outsource anything below your hourly value so you can keep working.
- After doing all the steps, **wait 36 months** — this is what "patient with outputs, impatient with inputs" means.
- You can **change your life in one decision**; a bad decision (e.g., drunk driving) ruins it instantly, a good decision changes direction too — the only difference is the outcome is delayed ("you can poison the fruit in a second... you just can't grow it real fast").
- Many male problems can be solved by **getting in shape and making money** — you'll still have problems, but smaller ones with more resources to handle them.
- Skills like **sales generalize across domains** — the same conversational skill works talking to girls or negotiating a car/house; "real life skills actually do generalize."
- All of his friends who became **millionaires and billionaires started in a high-volume sales environment** (door knocking, cold calling, in-person) for years where no one knew their name.
- **Skill development = number of repetitions × improvement between repetitions**; brute-forcing volume works only if paired with a **feedback loop**.
- The content-creation feedback method: review your **top 10 and bottom 10 videos**, pick one thing you did well, and do it in every video going forward — building a checklist that develops the skill.
