# How to Turn Anxiety and Tough Times into Growth

## Summary

A mindset clip on reframing anxiety and pain. Alex Hormozi argues champions interpret
pre-performance anxiety as excitement, that lingering anxiety is a signal you haven't
practiced enough, and that pain is a constant feature of every business condition —
growth, plateau, or decline — so it should be read as a signal of being alive rather than
a problem. He ends on "hypertrophying" resilience until acting despite bad feelings
becomes automatic.

## Key claims

- A reframe he credits to a therapist friend: when something bad happens, ask "what would
  it take for this to be amazing?" — which shifts the whole reality of the situation
  (paraphrase).
- Champions interpret anxiety as excitement; the physiological feeling is the same, but
  the framing changes how you feel stepping on stage (paraphrase).
- If you feel anxious before shipping (a meeting, presentation, email, book), you didn't
  work on it enough; you should rehearse until you're bored/sick of it and have no adrenal
  response (paraphrase).
- Every condition of business is painful — growing, stagnating, and declining all hurt —
  so pain is a prerequisite for reality and merely a signal that you're alive (paraphrase).
- Gym research (cited re: Olympic weightlifting teams) that "feeling bad" has zero
  correlation with performance led him to treat that discomfort as irrelevant (paraphrase).
- The ultimate form of resilience is "hypertrophy" past the point of weighing how you feel
  — you no longer consider it, you just do the thing (paraphrase).

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "champions just interpret anxiety as excitement."
- "if you feel nervous before you release it then you probably didn't work on it enough."
