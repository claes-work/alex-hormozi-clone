# Why You Don't Do What You Said You'd Do

## Summary

Alex explains inconsistency as a reinforcement problem. He says people often choose the
short-term behavior that gives the strongest immediate payoff, even when they verbally
endorse a long-term goal. His remedy is behavioral before verbal: take the action first,
then label the behavior into identity so that the person becomes, for example, someone
who is organized or consistent.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-13)

- Alex says people fail to do what they said they would do because short-term
  reinforcement often beats the long-term plan.
- He argues that long-term outcomes require repeatedly choosing actions whose payoff is
  delayed.
- He says identity should be built from observed behavior, not declared before the
  behavior exists.
- He describes the adult version of identity as self-created through action and
  self-labeling rather than passively inherited from earlier life.
- He warns that short-term behaviors compound into short-term outcomes.
