# Cut the Bottom 10%

## Summary

Alex advises a private-equity-owned cybersecurity company to improve its existing
enterprise sales force before adding a new small-customer motion. He recommends an
intensive diagnostic and coaching team, performance-banding the representatives, and
regularly replacing the bottom performers.

## Key claims

- The founder self-reports $304 million in revenue, 88% gross margin, 204 salespeople,
  about 1,000 customers, a $536,000 average deal, a nine-month sales cycle, and a recent
  EBITDA improvement from negative to $37.6 million; none is independently verified.
- As of April 2026, Alex initially says a small-customer motion should not take capacity
  from the existing enterprise team; acquiring a company with good small-customer sales
  and a weaker product could be faster than building the motion internally.
- Applying his more-better-new sequence to the stated 18-month goal, he ultimately
  prioritizes making the current sales organization better before adding a new segment.
- He recommends a temporary entrepreneurial intervention team that observes calls,
  runs intensive role-play, classifies representatives red/yellow/green, retrains the
  middle, and removes persistent low performers.
- He endorses cutting the bottom 10% quarterly, invoking Jack Welch, but gives no study
  or company-specific evidence that this cadence will produce the claimed result.
- He argues that a founder may recognize an existential market threat that financial
  owners discount because the founder has specialized operating knowledge.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I would first do better."

> "Cut the bottom 10% every quarter."

> "You parachute in a SWAT team of entrepreneurs."
