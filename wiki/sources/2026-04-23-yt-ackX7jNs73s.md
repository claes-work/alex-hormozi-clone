# Brand Is Just Persuasion at Scale

## Summary

Alex says brand is persuasion, suggestion, or influence at scale: a set of learned
associations that changes behavior. He frames his personal brand as context for
delivering business education at scale, says his mission is making real business
education accessible to everyone, and argues that free information can create more total
impact than paid information because far more people consume it.

## Key claims

- Alex says brand is the most valuable thing a business can build because it changes
  behavior at scale.
- He explains branding as taught association: people connect a symbol or object with
  values, identity, and expected behavior.
- He says his mission is making real business education accessible to everyone, with
  accessibility including understandability, entertainment, and distribution.
- He says personal brand supplies the context in which a message is consumed; the same
  message changes depending on who says it.
- He says free information has a lower follow-through percentage but a larger absolute
  impact because consumption is much higher.
- He says Acquisition.com gets about 2,400 inbound company requests per month and had
  done 22 deals over two years, highlighting how high the bar is.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Brand is basically persuasion... at scale."

> "The context is... a larger percentage of the message than the message itself."

> "You make a greater impact giving away things for free."
