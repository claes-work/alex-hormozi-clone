# You need to upsell

## Summary

Alex Hormozi advises a fitness or coaching business to upsell clients halfway through the
initial program, not at the end. He recommends a rollover-credit upsell where the initial
purchase is credited toward a longer six- or twelve-month commitment, followed by a
continuity downsell if the client declines.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A business doing about $60,000 per month with $20,000 profit may need a centralized
  salesperson for essentials.
- Selling at the end of the program often fails because people do not sign up again when
  they feel done.
- The upsell should happen at the halfway point.
- A rollover-credit upsell credits the original program payment toward the next longer
  program.
- The offer can present either-or options, such as another amount for 6 months or 12
  months.
- The owner should frame the extension as continuing progress rather than restarting.
- Trainers can sell if they are coached and incentivized, but if they hate sales, a
  salesperson may be needed.
- In the short term, the owner may be the highest-leverage person to conduct those calls.
- The sales conversation should confirm progress, position the work as a lifestyle change,
  and offer to credit everything paid so far toward staying on.
- If the customer says no to the main extension, downsell to continuity.

## Notable verbatim quotes
