# Don't Abandon Your Old Content

## Summary

Alex explains how he thinks about personal-brand content versus business marketing. If
the only objective were advertising Acquisition.com, he says business-only content would
be sufficient. But because he also has a personal brand, he wants content to become an
accurate reflection of who he is, while managing topic reach so fitness or relationship
content does not visually dominate the brand out of proportion to his real life.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex distinguishes marketing for the business from personal branding.
- A personal brand should become an accurate reflection of the person.
- He imagines content as a flashlight gradually illuminating the whole room behind a
  glass wall.
- When someone meets him in person, Alex wants them to find him exactly as expected.
- Relationship and fitness content can get far more reach than business content, so even
  a small amount can distort perceived brand composition.
- He tries to represent topics roughly in proportion to how much of his life they occupy.
- Brand curation is like managing a garden where some topics grow faster than others.
- He prefers "how I" framing for subjective areas like relationships and fitness, and
  adds conditions when giving "how to" advice.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I want the personal brand to be as accurate a reflection of me as humanly possible."

> "I think about the content as like a flashlight."

> "How I, not how-to."
