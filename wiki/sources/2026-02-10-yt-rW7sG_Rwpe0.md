# 'I Do $20M/Year as an AI Agency. How Do I Build a Personal Brand?'

## Summary
In this short Q&A clip, a business owner who runs an AI agency doing ~$20M/year in revenue (working in the SEO and AEO/AI-optimization space) asks Alex Hormozi how to stand out and build a personal brand in a market he describes as crowded and saturated with educational content. Alex first pushes to clarify the objective — the guest's goal is to get on more stages, speak more, and thereby win more clients (i.e., make more money). Alex's core advice is to make content about the topics that attract the specific type of buyer you want, disregarding views and likes, and to maximize the "highest value per second" for that audience. He treats packaging (hooks, visual hooks, pacing) as the mandatory ticket of entry, but insists the substance stay tightly focused on what your buyers want, suggesting roughly an 80/20 split: 80% straight down-the-barrel on-topic content, with occasional personal or philosophical "sprinkles." He uses his own practice as the example — nearly everything he makes, even philosophy or fitness, is anchored back through a business lens — and confirms he would do exactly the same if starting from scratch. The clip closes with a promo for his free 10-stage roadmap from zero to $100M+ at acquisition.com/roadmap.
