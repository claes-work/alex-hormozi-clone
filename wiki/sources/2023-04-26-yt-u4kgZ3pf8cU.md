# Alex Hormozi: The University of Texas at Austin (FULL LECTURE + Q&A)

## Summary

Alex joins a University of Texas at Austin entrepreneurship class for a lecture and Q&A. The class is validating businesses by getting strangers to pay, and Alex uses that frame to teach action-based learning, risk-adjusted decision-making, ignorance debt, the fallacy of the perfect pick, his "use what you got" motivation rule, basic customer acquisition, and the Rule of 100.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2023-04-26 - Validation**: Alex endorses the class definition that real validation means getting a stranger to give money, not simply liking an idea.
- **2023-04-26 - Learning and intelligence**: Alex defines learning behaviorally: same circumstance, different behavior. He defines intelligence as the rate at which behavior changes under the same condition.
- **2023-04-26 - Decisions**: Alex says he makes big decisions by risk-adjusted return, but notes that one-person life/career bets differ from portfolio bets because the person may not have unlimited tries.
- **2023-04-26 - Opportunity choice**: Alex warns students against the "perfect pick" fallacy. Early businesses are usually 3- to 5-year skill seasons, so the better target is directionally correct skill-stacking rather than a perfect lifelong business.
- **2023-04-26 - Motivation**: Alex says his early motivation was not passion but anger, shame, pride, ego, and fear of being judged a failure. He frames this as "use what you got," not as a universal prescription.
- **2023-04-26 - Debt**: Alex distinguishes financial debt from ignorance debt. He says beginners should avoid taking both at once because lack of skill is already a debt they must pay down.
- **2023-04-26 - Customer acquisition**: Alex restates the eight ways to get customers and tells students to start with simple warm outreach, free work, referrals, testimonials, and direct asks before high-leverage paid channels.
- **2023-04-26 - Rule of 100**: Alex tells students to do 100 primary actions every day for 100 days: reachouts, content minutes, ad spend, or another primary acquisition action.
- **2023-04-26 - Audience asset**: In a student party-promotion example, Alex says the audience list is the asset and warns against letting sponsors capture it permanently.
- **2023-04-26 - Work model**: Alex says students should model how he got to his current position, not his current schedule. He describes sleeping at his business for the first nine months and paying down ignorance debt through volume.

## Notable verbatim quotes
