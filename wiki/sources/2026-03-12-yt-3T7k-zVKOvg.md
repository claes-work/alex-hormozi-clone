# How I Scaled My First Gyms

## Summary

In this short interview clip, Alex Hormozi walks an unnamed host through the origin
arc of his first gyms and how it snowballed into Gym Launch. He gives month-by-month
figures for his first gym (grew ~$5k/month for seven months up to ~$35k/month, fully
outsourced by month nine, second gym opened by month 15), then describes pre-launching
subsequent locations "the right way," reaching five gyms, feeling stagnant, joining an
internet mastermind, and pivoting to a done-for-you "turnkey" model before switching to
a licensing model in 2017 — the point where the business "took off like a rocket." He
argues a gym is a low-ceiling but high-education "vehicle" that produces excellent
operators and salespeople because it forces you to do everything and get enormous
customer-facing reps. The clip closes with his standard acquisition.com/roadmap CTA.

## Key claims (Alex's account only)

- A gym can make money **only if you love small-business ownership and the product/
  experience** itself — it is not a passive or high-leverage play.
- A gym is a **"limited opportunity vehicle"** but a **great vehicle to learn to do
  everything**, because you personally have to do everything in it.
- Fitness is an exceptional training ground for **salespeople** specifically, because
  the sheer volume of daily reps (talking to 10–20 people a day) makes you very good
  very fast.
- Because it is an all-service business, a gym carries **high head count for very low
  revenue** — which teaches people management under hard constraints.
- Learning to run a **seven-figure or multi-seven-figure gym gives you the skill set to
  run a $10M business** in another category.
- The **turnkey model** (he flies out, does the marketing/spending/lead-work/sales for a
  gym owner) let him run **1/3 the head count at 4x the revenue** vs. owning gyms, and
  he **kept 100% of the cash collected** while charging the owner nothing up front.
- The **licensing model** was even more leverage — bigger head count but far bigger
  output.
- At sticking points ("stagnating"), the unlock came from **outside input** (the
  mastermind reframing him from gym owner to gym-marketing teacher), not from grinding
  harder inside the existing model.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

> "It grows by $5,000 a month for the first 7 months. Goes up to $35,000 a month. By month nine, I had the whole gym outsourced so I was just managing it. By month 15, I took the profit from that gym to open my second gym."

> "Now this one, I learned a lot at that point, so I pre-launched it at full capacity. Did it the right way."

> "I felt like I was stagnating. I felt like I didn't know what to do next. I joined an internet mastermind."

> "Then I transitioned to the turnkey — like I fly out and do the thing — which I make a hundred grand a month on with no employees. I was like, this is cool."

> "There's a million series of unfortunate events that happened during this period of time. Lose all the money like twice. I sell the gyms. I go all in on the new concept."

> "We switch from doing the turnkey system to doing a licensing model. And then that was 2017, and that's when everything just took off like a rocket."

> "You can make money owning a gym if you love — you have to love small business ownership, and it has to be about really being in love with the product and the experience."

> "It's just a limited opportunity vehicle. It's a great vehicle to learn how to do everything, because you have to do everything in a gym."

> "Some of the best entrepreneurs, some of the best salespeople especially, learn how to sell in fitness because you get so many reps. You talk to 10, 20 people a day. Just rep after rep after rep."

> "When I had the same head count with the turnkey sales model, I had a third of employees and I was making four times the revenue."

> "They didn't have to do anything. I'd fly out, we'd do the marketing, we'd spend the money, we'd work the leads, we'd do the sales. They didn't have to do anything. I just hand them customers."

> "They didn't have to pay me anything. I just took 100% of the cash I collected."
