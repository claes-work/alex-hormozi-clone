# How to Deal With the Stress of Entrepreneurship

## Summary

Alex says uncertainty, more than tactics, makes entrepreneurship stressful. Retrospect
removes uncertainty and therefore makes long periods look smoother than they felt. He
credits a Morgan Housel post for the observation and uses it to argue that present crises
will either resolve or cease to matter to the person experiencing them.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says historical periods seem better in retrospect because their
  outcomes are known, even though investors experienced crashes, war, and uncertainty.
- He credits Morgan Housel for prompting this retrospective-versus-lived distinction.
- Alex says tactical knowledge gaps are usually straightforward to solve; simultaneous
  personnel, payment, lead, and payroll crises create entrepreneurial stress.
- His coping frame is that a crisis eventually resolves or the person dies, in either
  case ending the need to worry about it.
- He expects the future to be better and the remembered past to have been less pleasant
  than memory suggests.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Everything seems better in retrospect because there is no uncertainty."

> "The actual tactics of business are relatively straightforward."

> "The future is likely going to be better."
