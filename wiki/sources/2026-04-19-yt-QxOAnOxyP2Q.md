# How to Win Every Negotiation Before It Starts

## Summary

Alex explains anchoring in negotiation: the first number shapes the rest of the
conversation, including the increments people move in. He recommends getting the gasp,
moving in small increments, trading on terms, and making the other side name the cost of
fixing a problem before asking for that amount as a discount.

## Key claims

- Alex says the first number said in a negotiation is an anchor, even if someone claims
  not to be anchoring.
- He says counter-anchoring is possible but usually less effective than anchoring first.
- He recommends putting the anchor as high as possible to shift the negotiation's
  reference range.
- He says people can anchor not only price but the increments by which price moves.
- He describes a house negotiation where he moved from $15M to $15.25M after the seller
  moved from $20M to $16.9M, signaling limited movement.
- He says negotiators are often too fixated on price and not enough on terms.
- He recommends asking what it would cost the vendor to fix a mistake, then using that
  number as the starting discount.

> "You can also anchor increments."
