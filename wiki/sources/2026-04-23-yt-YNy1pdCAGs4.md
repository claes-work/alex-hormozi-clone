# What Is the Best Advice You've Ever Heard or Received?

## Summary

Alex answers a rapid advice sequence. He names extreme work volume as the best advice,
"follow your passion" as the worst, seeking better people by helping them first as the
advice he wishes he had earlier, and focus as the lesson that took too long to learn. He
also argues for a law requiring wealth to be given away at death rather than inherited,
because he believes strong capital allocators would eventually become strong givers and
that inherited wealth harms opportunity and heirs.

## Key claims

- Alex says the best advice he has heard is to do enough work that failure becomes
  unreasonable.
- He says the worst advice is to follow your passion.
- He wishes he had earlier sought people better than him and helped them first without
  expectation.
- He says focus, expressed as one thing all in, took too many repetitions to learn.
- He says he would create a law requiring everyone to give away all wealth at death and
  not pass it to their children.
- He argues this would move capital from the best accumulators into giving and reduce
  nepotism while preserving equal opportunity rather than equal outcomes.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Do so much work it would be unreasonable for you not to be successful."

> "Follow your passion."

> "Focus. One thing, all in."
