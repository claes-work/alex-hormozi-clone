# Stop Wishing Your Story Was Easier

## Summary

Alex reframes suffering as an opportunity to create proof of the person someone wants to
be. A person cannot claim to withstand hard things without having gone through hard
things, so unwanted difficulty can still serve the identity-building goal. He also frames
hardship as story material: a story without villains, stakes, twists, and depth is less
interesting than one with a monster big enough to make the hero epic.

## Key claims

- Suffering can create evidence for the identity a person wants to hold.
- Hard things provide proof that someone can withstand hard things.
- Alex says telling himself "this is going to be an amazing story" helped him during
  early Gym Launch years when evidence was scarce.
- Easier stories are often less relatable and less compelling.
- He agrees with the idea that a story is only as good as its villains or stakes.
- The bigger the monster, the more epic the hero.
