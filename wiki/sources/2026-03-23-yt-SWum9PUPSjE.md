# Helping an In-Person Service Business Fix Their Offer

## Summary
In this short clip, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a fitness/health-club business (four locations, roughly 6,000–8,000 sq ft each, reportedly doing $2.4M and targeting $3M) who feels they need to reposition their offer. The owner runs an ecosystem model (ad-driven large-group as highest margin, one-on-one for deconditioned clients, plus a 24/7 open gym meant for engagement) and struggles to market its nuance. Hormozi's core guidance is to market the simple thing prospects want and deliver the fuller ecosystem once they arrive, and he advises against bolting a personal brand onto a business that doesn't need one.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex advises marketing the outcome the prospect already wants and only delivering "the thing they need" once they come in — not trying to explain the sophisticated ecosystem/business model up front.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that a business which already works without a personal brand should not have one tacked on, especially when the owner's goal is to build value and exit rather than own long-term.
- Alex frames the owner's aspiration to lead with personal transformation as secondary to first clarifying the exit-vs-own goal before deciding how much personal story to leverage.
- Caller claims (unverified): the business does $2.4M across four locations, wants to reach $3M, runs group as its highest-margin stream, and the owner was indicted at 22 and served 63 months in federal prison.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You market the thing they want. And then when they come in, you give them the thing that they need. Don't try to explain the thing that they need before they — you give them the thing they want."

> "You already have a business that doesn't require a personal brand. I wouldn't tack one on."

> "Do you want to sell the business? Do you want to own it long term? Like what do you want to do?"
