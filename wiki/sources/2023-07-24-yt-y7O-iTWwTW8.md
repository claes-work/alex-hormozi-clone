# Interviewing a Billionaire + Revealing My Workout Routine [Ep 005]

## Summary

This vlog follows Alex Hormozi through a Dave Ramsey visit, a workout, and a content/thumbnail session. Alex presents several small but useful operating principles: convert dead time into content, ask an older-future-self for advice when stuck, let tactical content serve people who are still "sick of being poor," and train with stronger partners because they raise effort. He also discusses why Ramsey's investing comments helped him and Leila keep Acquisition.com focused on buying and growing businesses rather than trying to become general investors.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2023-07-24 - Future-self journaling**: Alex says that when he feels emotionally stuck, he writes a dialogue with his 85-year-old self and answers his own questions from that older perspective.
- **2023-07-24 - Content volume**: He treats constraints as chances to create. When the plane Wi-Fi is too slow for normal work, he records instead.
- **2023-07-24 - Audience targeting**: He says content for people who are "sick of being poor" should be tactical, because the broadest audience still needs concrete first moves.
- **2023-07-24 - Investing focus**: Alex says Dave Ramsey and Graham Stephan helped him see that his own knowledge base was business, so he and Leila decided to keep investing through Acquisition.com by buying and growing businesses rather than trying to manage unfamiliar asset classes.
- **2023-07-24 - Training philosophy**: Alex says he trains harder with stronger partners, starts workouts with weak body parts such as calves, and uses full-body training because irregular travel makes perfect programming unrealistic.
- **2023-07-24 - Brand feedback**: During the photoshoot and thumbnail section, he says audience feedback can inform packaging, but it should not make him abandon the brand he actually is.
