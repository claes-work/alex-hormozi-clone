# Why You Shouldn't Switch to English Content

## Summary

Alex advises a creator with an existing native-language business not to switch to
English content unless their English is genuinely as strong as their native language.
The lowest-risk move is to keep compounding where the business already works and wait
for AI translation to make global reach easier.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-06)

- Alex says if a creator is already doing well in their existing language, the
  lowest-risk move is to do more there.
- He says English-speaking markets may offer more discretionary income, but also much
  stronger native-English competition.
- He asks how many people speaking their second language become number one in a field.
- He says a truly bilingual creator should choose the language with the highest
  opportunity.
- He says a creator whose English is weaker should probably crush their own market.
- He predicts AI translation capabilities will become exceptional and allow native
  language content to travel globally.
- He recommends building where the risk of failure is lowest.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Do more of what you're already doing."

> "Crush your own market."

> "Build where you think you have the lowest risk of it not working."
