# You should start a boring business

## Summary

Alex Hormozi advises a young founder selling digital business cards to switch vehicles.
He says the founder has sales skill but is using it on a hard pitch that requires creating
the problem and solving it in the same presentation. He recommends selling products people
already know they need, ideally in categories with durable retention and existing demand.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A salesperson can make far more money by applying the same sales skill to a better
  opportunity vehicle.
- Inventing a novel senior-project-style product is often worse than copying a boring
  existing business with proven demand.
- A hard pitch requires creating the problem, explaining the category, and then solving the
  problem in one presentation.
- It is easier to sell a product where the prospect already recognizes the problem.
- Look for products people do not cancel.
- If speed to money matters, sell to an avatar already understood from current experience.
- A good way to learn a new industry is to sell inside it, learn the ropes, then figure out
  lead generation and delivery.
- Entrepreneurs are fundamentally promoters/salespeople at heart.
- Some businesses are demand-constrained with sticky delivery handled by larger providers,
  such as insurance-like products.

## Notable verbatim quotes
