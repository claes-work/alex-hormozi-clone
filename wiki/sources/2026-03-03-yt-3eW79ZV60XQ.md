# The Mission for My Life

## Summary
Interview clip (two off-camera hosts asking; only Alex's answers are voice data). Alex states his life mission plainly: through Acquisition.com he wants to **document and share the best practices for building wonderful businesses** — that is the point of his life, and the businesses exist mainly to lend credence to the books, topics, and lessons and to give him real stories that drive fundamental truths home (and to discover those truths as he goes). He calls it "a travesty" that Elon, Bezos, and Buffett never wrote books, arguing accumulated wealth is irrelevant because "I will die." He frames his own writing as provisional — "as least wrong as I can think of it currently" — and is "horrified" at things he claimed five years ago, but sees saying something imperfect as better than saying nothing. On work and life: he loves business, there is "nothing that really stimulates me like this," so he does as much as he can; he and his wife are true 50/50 partners (she runs the operator half — infrastructure, recruiting, HR, culture — and has "more output than I do"), they are single with no kids and work roughly 5-ish a.m. to 4-ish p.m. with a gym block midday. On psychology: he traces his original drive to "crippling insecurity and needing approval," reinforced by positive feedback (behavioral conditioning); he still does the actions without the original catalyst, and reckons he's maybe "30% better" on the insecurity than at the start, half-joking that a "big pile of money" is an emotional crutch. He describes taking last year mostly off (dinners out nightly for seven months, moved to Vegas temporarily, traveled Cabo/Scottsdale/Sedona/Flagstaff), found it "pretty empty," valued the rest, and is now "ready for war again." He closes on the idea of **entrepreneurial "seasons"** — roughly five-year chunks, this being his fourth; ~3 years to see something through, ~2 to transition/monetize — and rejects others' arbitrary definitions of "balance." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
