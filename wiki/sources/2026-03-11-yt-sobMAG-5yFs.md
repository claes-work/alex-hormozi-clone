# How to Increase Show-Up Rates

## Summary
Interview clip (an off-camera host poses the questions; all substantive answers are Alex's). Alex argues that in most companies he "can make more money by fixing their show-up rate than by increasing their close rate," so when a business complains their closing sucks with a 25% show rate, he starts with show-up first. His data credibility comes from owning **Allen**, a scheduling software that lets him see ~4,000 appointments a day across industries with two data scientists testing every variable. The finding: the **number one lever on throughput is availability** — total time slots per day, seven days a week. He mocks gym owners who take sales calls only 7–9am Tuesdays/Thursdays and dismiss no-shows as "not serious"; the truth is buyers optimize for convenience (his Leila-eyebrow-appointment story: she booked whoever could see her *now*). Second lever: the **increment/granularity of time slots** — offering 15-minute increments rather than 60-minute ones yields higher schedule rates on internet bookings (the appointment itself can still run 45–60 min); odd start times like 3:05 or 3:15 also signal detail and make people more likely to arrive on time. Third: **proximity** — the closer the appointment is to the moment you spoke to the lead, the higher the show rate, so **dragging appointments forward into the present** ("I just had my 4:00 cancel, I can pull you up") sharply boosts show-ups because same-day/next-day rates far exceed 2–4 days out. His rule: try to book same-day or next-day for all appointments if at all possible. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
