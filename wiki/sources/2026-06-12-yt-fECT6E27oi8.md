# Why You're Still the Only Closer in Your Business

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a real-estate-agent services company doing about $17-18M. The owner and COO remain the best closers and spend too much time closing. Alex says the needed identity shift is from "sales god" to "sales training god." He diagnoses a founder-driven, authority-based, consultative sales process and says scalable sales must be based on the prospect, a tight script, and questions in the right order rather than the salesperson's expertise.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Founders who want sales to scale must shift identity from being the best closer to being the best sales trainer.
- Every sale the founder has to close should be treated as a failure of the system, not a win.
- One tactical lever is to stop paying commissions on deals the founder has to help close, so the team stops pulling the founder in.
- Founder-driven sales processes are often too consultative and rely on authority, heavy industry knowledge, and expertise.
- A scalable sales process is based on the prospect, not the salesperson.
- Salespeople should ask questions in the correct order so the prospect reaches the decision; they should not need to demonstrate deep expertise.
- Most businesses do not truly train sales; they hire a pile of salespeople and see who survives.
- If the script is successful, sales training becomes a reinforcement loop of saying the right thing at the right time.
- The script is probably too long if it gives more than the minimum information needed for a purchasing decision.
- The salesperson should answer questions about the sale, not detailed product-delivery questions.
