# How to Get Good at Sales

## Summary

Alex Hormozi gives a sales mini-lesson in an interview setting. He says the best salespeople often show strong early signal, sales is helping someone make a decision that helps them, and the two core ingredients are conviction and trust. Conviction is not binary; it can be strengthened by seeing customer results. He then outlines a basic sales conversation and emphasizes childlike curiosity as a way to handle objections without confrontation.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Many great sales trainers first sold well, then reverse-engineered what they were doing.
- Sales teams should have a short fail window because Alex says he has not seen killer salespeople start terribly.
- Selling is helping someone make a decision that benefits them.
- The strongest predictor of good sales is conviction.
- Conviction transfers through trust, which comes from expertise and rapport.
- Sales-team conviction rises when reps see testimonials and real customer outcomes.
- A sales conversation should uncover the problem, what the prospect tried, where they failed, why the offer differs, and then explain outcomes rather than features.
- Objections can be handled without confrontation through childlike curiosity.
- Slowing down under sales pressure is like learning to breathe under fight pressure.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're not selling, you're helping someone make a decision."
