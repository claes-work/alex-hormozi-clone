# If It's Competitive, It Means People Are Buying

## Summary

Alex tells a prospective cooking-community founder not to be discouraged by existing
competitors. Competition proves demand exists. He argues that business owners should
channel existing demand rather than try to create it, and that basic competence already
beats most operators: keep promises, show up, prepare, and do good work.

## Key claims

- Existing communities in a niche do not mean the niche should be avoided.
- Alex treats competition as evidence that people are buying.
- He says demand should be channeled, not created from scratch.
- A founder can enter a competitive market by taking part of an existing demand stream.
- Keeping promises, showing up on time, preparing, and doing good work can already put an
  operator above most competitors.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If it's competitive, it means people are buying."

> "You cannot create demand. You channel demand."

> "Make promises and keep your promises."
