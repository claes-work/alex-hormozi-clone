# What Should I Sell

## Summary

Alex advises a new entrepreneur to pick from knowledge already available through
parents, jobs, or interests, then sell a service that solves a problem someone else
wants solved. He frames service work as the lowest-risk first business because it
turns time into money while paying down "ignorance debt."

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says a person usually knows something from family background, past jobs, or
  current interests.
- He says Y Combinator looks for founders with industry experience because it reduces
  ignorance debt.
- He recommends choosing a problem that someone else wants solved.
- He says business-to-business offers can be priced as a share of the money or value
  created.
- He says business-to-consumer value depends more on perceived pain and the customer's
  desire to solve it.
- He says a first business does not have to be the entrepreneur's forever business.
- He recommends starting with a service because it is low risk and converts time into
  learning plus cash.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Everybody knows something."

> "Do stuff for money."

> "You're paying down ignorance debt."
