# From $5M to $3.6M: How Do I Get My Cybersecurity Firm Back?

## Summary

A live coaching Q&A on the @MoreMozi channel in which **Alex Hormozi** advises the **owner of a cybersecurity-compliance consulting firm** (mid-market/enterprise) whose revenue fell from ~$5M (2023) to $3.6M and net margin from 35% to 18%, largely because the owner stopped doing the solo outbound that drove growth while shifting focus to becoming a "real CEO," hiring, and building a people function. Alex diagnoses the business as **demand-constrained** (it can handle more customers) and prescribes a two-track go-to-market: run conferences to "fish with a net" and fill the pipeline fast, while concurrently hiring an SDR to feed steady outbound leads, plus building thought-leadership on LinkedIn by publicizing private client work. **Alex is the coach and primary voice throughout**; the owner's situation is context, not persona data.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- **Diagnose the constraint first.** Because the firm can handle more customers, it is **demand-constrained** right now — so the work is generating more demand, not adding capacity.
- **Lead with what you're good at.** The owner's strength is outbound; he can "either go one-on-one the way you were, or go hunt where there's fish in a barrel" (i.e., events/conferences where prospects are concentrated).
- **Run the conference playbook.** For this type of business, go to conferences, run the sales motion through them, collect as many leads as possible, and structure an offer with urgency so people sign up on-site. It "can happen fast" and he has the cash flow to do it — start with ~two conferences a month.
- **Expect a crisscross lead pattern while it's just you.** Event leads spike then taper; ramp outbound in the gaps until the next event — the two motions crisscross until you add help.
- **Hire an SDR next.** Once cash flow allows (which he's near), bring on a sales development representative to do the outbound/qualifying and feed the owner, who can close in a couple of calls. The SDR is "probably the largest time constraint for you right now and also not that expensive."
- **Run the two tracks concurrently.** Fill the pipeline via conferences immediately, and at the same time put out ads for the SDR — event leads stabilize into steady SDR leads that "continue to stack over time."
- **A recruiter/outbound background maps cleanly to SDR work** — recruiters do outbound to source candidates, so it's "the closest comparable role" for someone moving from recruiting into technical sales.
- **Thought leadership is simple:** "to be a thought leader, you just take the stuff you're doing privately and then just talk about it publicly" — talk about your client work on LinkedIn.
- **Cut "keep the lights on" marketing.** Marketing is binary — either it's getting you business or it isn't; a nice-guy marketer who isn't producing pipeline may need to go.
