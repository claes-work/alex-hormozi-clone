# Having ADD Doesn't Have to Destroy Your Business

## Summary

Alex says some founders need side quests to avoid breaking the main business while large
initiatives take time. For him, books serve that role: they are additive to
Acquisition.com, give him thinking space, and let the team improve without him blowing
things up. He warns that a side quest only helps if it does not drain resources from the
main thing.

## Key claims

- Alex says this advice applies to larger businesses more than small ones; small
  companies should generally ignore side quests.
- He says visionary or ADD-prone founders may need something productive to occupy them
  while the main business moves slowly.
- He says books are his side quest because they are additive to Acquisition.com and give
  him thinking space.
- He says side quests must not drain money, attention, or team resources from the main
  business.
- He says a side quest can increase the chance that a long, painful main-business
  initiative succeeds if it prevents destructive founder interference.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Side quests can make the main quest."

> "They cannot drain resources from the main thing."

> "For me writing books is that thing."

Synthesis candidate: side quests for visionary/ADD-prone founders; books as Alex's additive side quest; no resource drain from main business.
