# I Tried This Simple Business Strategy for 60 Days. This is what happened

## Summary

A case-study video (sequel to an earlier "three wise men" episode) in which Hormozi walks through how acquisition.com added $6M in Enterprise Value to one of his 13 portfolio companies in 60 days by fixing the back end after sales had already doubled. He teaches three tracked metrics — time to first value, customer satisfaction, and ascension/backend revenue — and the specific solutions applied (choreographed onboarding, CRM + KPI tracking, commissioning the customer-success team, and building a paid backend behind a wall).

## Key claims

- The company had doubled sales (56 → 93 units/month in 60 days) in the prior video; this created the next problem — fulfillment/retention couldn't keep up (the "problem-solution cycle": solving one problem creates the next).
- Before-state on three metrics: time to first value 47 days; customer satisfaction non-existent (untracked); ascension/backend revenue $0 (no backend existed).
- "Time to first value" is the metric he cares most about in any service — pull the activation/retention moment as far forward as possible and break the outcome into many small reinforced chunks (uses the Breaking Bad "third episode → 87% finish" retention-point analogy).
- Retaining a customer is ~17x more profitable than acquiring one ($60 in staff labor to keep vs. ~$1,000 ad spend to acquire the same value); allocate effort to the highest-return bucket first.
- Distinguish leading vs. lagging indicators: churn is a lagging result; you must act on leading indicators (e.g., no gym visits for a month, no Netflix logins) before the bad result happens.
- Never give a free forever backend — it creates "an endless liability for a one-time income" and reduces urgency; instead build a paid backend ("you have to pay to stay").
- Results after 60 days: time to first value 47 → 34 days; customer satisfaction 98% (measured post-onboarding, which he flags as likely inflated); ascension 0 → 27% of customers (10% at first); $0 → ~$140,000 in backend over 60 days, which annualized (~$70k/mo profit ≈ ~$840k/yr) at a ~6x multiple ≈ ~$6M in added Enterprise Value.
