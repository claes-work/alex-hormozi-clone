# How to Make Hard Decisions

## Summary

Alex presents regret as unavoidable because opportunity cost is unavoidable. The decision
frame is not "what do I want?" but "which regret can I live with?" That lets the person use
loss aversion and fear of the worse regret as fuel for action.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-15, Alex says regret is an unavoidable part of being human because opportunity
  cost cannot be eliminated.
- He says hard decisions can be reframed as choosing which regret to live with.
- He contrasts the short regret of a painful conversation with the long regret of wasting
  a decade in a hated job.
- He says the regret frame activates the "run away from" engine rather than only the
  "go toward" engine.
- The rat/cheese/cat example is discussed by the host and attributed to Jordan Peterson;
  do not treat it as Alex's original example.
- Alex says if someone cannot get themselves to act, they should think about what they
  have to lose rather than only what they have to gain.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "In life you have to choose your regrets."

> "Which regret could I live with?"

> "If you can't get yourself to do something, think about it from the perspective of what you have to lose rather than what you have to gain."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: choose-your-regret decision frame; opportunity cost makes regret
inevitable; use loss aversion when upside motivation fails.
