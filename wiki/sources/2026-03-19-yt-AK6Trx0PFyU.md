# My Standards for Conversion Rates Across Different Sources

## Summary
Q&A-style monologue giving rule-of-thumb conversion benchmarks by traffic source. Alex walks through: Meta leads for in-person service (~10% close), cold webinar leads (2–3% opt-in→sale for broad markets, up to 5% if niched), in-person/in-home salespeople (~35%, "one out of three," with a proper sales process), and web pages (typically 1–2%). He adds two "trust platform" outliers: a Skool "about" page converting ~4%, and — the wild one — his Amazon book page converting ~25% ("one out of four clicks buys"), explaining the trade-off: on a platform like Amazon you convert ~10x higher but keep maybe one-third the gross profit (Amazon takes the rest), so at ~2.5% margin-equivalent you're still "making three times as much money." He also states his pricing/process heuristic tied to salesperson close rates: above ~35% he *raises price*; below it he *fixes the process before considering lowering price*. Ends with the roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex's rule-of-thumb conversion benchmarks (leads → sale unless noted): Meta leads, in-person service ≈ **10%**; cold webinar leads (broad market) ≈ **2–3%** of opt-ins, up to **5%** if more niched (measured on opt-ins, not shows); in-person/in-home salesperson with a proper process ≈ **35%** ("at least one out of three"); web pages ≈ **1–2%**.
- As of March 2026, his pricing/process heuristic: if a salesperson closes **above ~35%**, "I will raise price"; if **below**, "I will fix the process before I even consider lowering the price."
- As of March 2026, he cites "trust platform" conversion lifts: Skool "about" pages "at like 4%… really good," because "when you have trust in a platform that starts to increase your conversion overall."
- As of March 2026 (self-reported, "the last time I looked"): his Amazon book page converts "roughly a quarter of the traffic… one out of four clicks buys."
- As of March 2026, he frames the platform trade-off explicitly: on Amazon you might get "10x the conversion per click" but "one-third the gross profit" (Amazon keeps most of the money and pays a small commission) — still "making three times as much money," so it can be worth it if you understand the numbers.

## Notable verbatim quotes
