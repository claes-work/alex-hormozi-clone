# You'll find this video when you need it

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that entrepreneurs should use whatever emotional fuel they currently have rather than moralizing whether the fuel is reward, punishment, shame, or fear. He says bad things do not "come in threes"; rather, people fail to recover from one bad thing and let it contaminate other parts of life. The practical lesson is resilience: isolate the bad event, recover before interacting with others, and avoid using one bad day as permission to misbehave.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The first rule of entrepreneurship is to use what you have.
- Whether motivation comes from a positive goal or an aversive stimulus matters less than whether it produces the desired action.
- People often create narratives about why they acted after the fact, without knowing the true root cause.
- Bad things do not inherently come in threes; people often let one bad thing snowball into more bad things.
- A bad event should be isolated rather than allowed to damage marriage, parenting, health, or future work.
- "I had a bad day" is often used as an excuse for lacking self-control.
- Resilience is the ability to recover before spreading the bad event into other domains.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Use whatever you've got to get whatever you want."
