# Be Resilient

## Summary
Solo motivational clip. Alex argues that the single trait that beats connections, money, looks, and intelligence is "an insatiable desire to improve" — someone who improves every second without giving up is nearly unbeatable. From there he develops his **resources-are-zero-sum / priorities** thesis: whatever you spend time or money on is taken from something else, so "most people don't have a resources issue, they have a priorities problem." The fix is to shift the priority list to invest time and money into what you actually want and to "starve out failure." He gives the double-benefit example of working more hours (you earn more *and* spend less because you have no time to spend), and urges betting on yourself rather than get-rich-quick plays (Dogecoin, meme coins): "Bet on you. Cuz you'll never sell you. You're a long-term hold." Skills compound — every skill stacks (Steve Jobs' calligraphy → Mac fonts; "you can only connect the dots looking back"). He recounts spending $10k of his $50k life savings at age 23 to join a **mastermind for gym owners despite not owning a gym**, buying five years of others' mistakes and getting his first location profitable and outsourced by month nine — his "ignorance tax": the price of not achieving your dreams is ignorance, and the debt is the distance between where you are and where you'd be if you knew how. He also frames **geographic relocation as a bet** — he moved to Southern California ("the capital of fitness") knowing no one, willing to risk existing relationships to gain the ones he needed. Closes urging the viewer to consider leaving where they're at, owning less stuff ("stuff only deteriorates… you will only get better"), then the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
