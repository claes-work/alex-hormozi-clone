# You Need to do 100x MORE WORK

## Summary
Short solo clip on the scale of effort real growth requires and the trade-offs it demands. Alex opens with a story: a friend who is a public-company CEO grew his business from a ~$200M to a ~$1.2B market cap in under two years, and when asked what he did "primarily," the CEO said he was "shaking hands, kissing babies, going to events all the time" — **66 in-person events in a single quarter, traveling to them himself**, despite having a wife and kids. Alex's point isn't that everyone should do this; it's that the CEO **made a trade** and paid the price for the outcome. This sets up his core theme: **trades / price tags**. People want the outcome "their way" — the six-pack without changing how they eat, hypergrowth without changing their work-life schedule — but you can't pick the price of the shoes; you pay or you don't. He then turns the lens on himself: when he was younger he *thought* he worked a lot and did spend a lot of time working, but made "so little progress" because much of that time he "wasn't actually creating output." The implicit multiplier: the gap between busy time and real output is why the required work is "a 100X, not 2X." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (free $100M scaling roadmap + quiz across the business functions).

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, the effort delta for outlier growth is "a 100X, not 2X" — an order-of-magnitude more, not incremental more.
- As of March 2026 (example), a public-CEO friend grew his company from ~$200M to ~$1.2B market cap in under two years primarily by relentless in-person presence — ~66 events in one quarter, traveling to them himself.
- As of March 2026, outcomes are **trades / price tags**: you cannot have the results without paying the associated cost (time, lifestyle, family trade-offs). "You can't pick the price for the shoes. You pay or you don't."
- As of March 2026, the big misconception (in sales, fitness, business alike) is that "people want your outcome their way" — the six-pack without changing their eating, hypergrowth without changing their schedule.
- As of March 2026, it is legitimate to decline the trade — but then you cannot complain about not having what someone who *did* make the trade achieved.
- As of March 2026 (self-reflection), when younger he thought he worked a lot and did put in a lot of time, but made little progress because much of that time "wasn't actually creating output" — time worked ≠ output produced.
