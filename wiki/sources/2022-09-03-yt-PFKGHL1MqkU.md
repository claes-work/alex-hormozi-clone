# Investor 3min Morning Routine (2022-09-03)

## Summary

Alex debunks the "millionaire morning routine" genre, arguing that elaborate routines (cold plunges, gratitude journals, making your bed) are non-work activities people add to avoid confronting that they simply aren't working. He redefines productivity as output-per-unit-time multiplied by leverage, and reveals his own anti-routine: wake up, cup of coffee, and immediately start work ordered from highest- to lowest-leverage tasks.

## Key claims

- Productivity = getting more done per unit of time; adding non-work rituals to a calendar cannot increase work-per-unit-time. (paraphrase)
- No universal "millionaire routine" exists — any claimed requirement (wake early, seven income streams, real estate) is disproved by a single counterexample (e.g., a friend doing 70M/year in janitorial services). (paraphrase)
- Superstitious habits like making your bed only "work" via the meaning ascribed to them (commitment/discipline); the habit is interchangeable and you can just tell yourself you're disciplined without proving it. (paraphrase)
- The worst routine would be longest possible, many energy-draining steps, and a dependency that blocks work if missing — so the best routine is the inverse: minimal, and trainable to shrink. (paraphrase)
- Leverage = more output per unit of input; entrepreneurship is consistently trading time for money (e.g., recruiting a salesperson costs 2–3 hrs/week support but buys back ~20 hrs/week of prospecting). (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "productivity is getting more done per unit of time ... people who move faster in life don't actually move faster they get more done per unit of time"
- "it's not the making the bed that's the thing that's important it's the fact that i'm committed to doing the things that i said i'm going to do"
- "there's work to be done and the question is how quickly can i begin that work that's it"
- "the perfect routine would be you wake up and you immediately work because you've trained yourself to be able to do it"
- "the fundamental equation of productivity how much you do multiply by how much leverage you have on the stuff you do"
- "for me that looks like waking up having a cup of coffee and getting to work"
