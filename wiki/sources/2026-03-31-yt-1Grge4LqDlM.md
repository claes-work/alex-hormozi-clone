# Helping a $4.3M Creator Platform Business Scale to $100M

## Summary
Live coaching Q&A: the founder of a creator-economy business ($4.3M/yr across a creator agency, a music label, and a brand-collaboration platform) has 40,000 creators but only ~1,000 making money, and wants to switch from taking a cut of creator earnings to a paid subscription (SaaS) while charging brands commission. Hormozi validates moving to a subscription as the path of least resistance to $100M, but warns that winning at SaaS demands going all-in, whereas the founder is splitting attention across four businesses.

## Key advice (Alex)
- The best marketplaces run a SaaS first and layer marketplace revenue on later once at scale (the Shopify pattern): you make more charging $29/mo across everyone than 4% of zero, because most users keep paying "for the hope that someday they will make money."
- To win at SaaS you must go all-in on software — it's one of the most competitive, best-funded, smartest-competitor spaces. Splitting attention across four businesses ("chasing multiple rabbits") makes beating them a tall order.
- Two viable structures: charge creators a subscription (freemium, low barrier to build the network), or go high-ticket — charge $5–10K, do premium onboarding, and select only the best creators.
- Push back on the "creators can't convert brands" premise: brands pay for impressions, and dropping that revenue guts the network's value to sponsors. Consider a second ad/media-side product or tiered offer.
- Get clarity so you can get the chosen business running fast, then shut off the other things and go all in.
