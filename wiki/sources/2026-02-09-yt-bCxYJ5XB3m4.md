# How to Make Money as a Coach in the Finance Niche

## Summary
In this @MoreMozi Q&A, a questioner running a free "Aussie Dave Ramsey" financial-freedom community asks whether giving everything away for free (vs. paid) is wise. Alex Hormozi endorses free-first as long as there is a plan to sell afterward, then uses Dave Ramsey as a case study: the personal-savings niche makes claims uniquely easy to substantiate (spend less and the money left over is undeniable, unlike income-generation claims that carry delivery risk), and Ramsey monetizes a ~$300M topline brand across roughly four-to-five revenue streams (media ~$120M, an app, coaching, and courses/events). His core recommendation is to give away all the information and sell the implementation — favoring remote semi-private coaching (e.g. one-to-six) as the most profitable model because it multiplies income while keeping clients accountable — then optionally an in-person experience. He closes on his key monetization insight: the same information sold in a different delivery medium commands a different price (PDF/free → 8-week remote livestream → in-person classroom), largely because a richer medium raises the perceived likelihood that the buyer actually consumes and applies it and can get questions answered in real time.

## Key claims
_All dated 2026-02-09. Paraphrased unless quoted. Alex-attributed unless marked._

- (2026-02-09) [guest-attributed] The questioner has started a free financial-freedom community around "no nonsense financial management," pitched as an "Aussie Dave Ramsey," and asks whether to give it all away for free instead of charging.
- (2026-02-09) Giving it all away for free is fine — as long as you have a plan for what you will sell afterward.
- (2026-02-09) Dave Ramsey has one of the most fascinating businesses because he managed to be a guru without ever being called a guru.
- (2026-02-09) The reason is unique to the personal-savings / personal-finance space: savings claims don't have to be substantiated with math — if someone earning and spending $5k starts spending $3k, they undeniably have $2k left over, and no one will argue that.
- (2026-02-09) By contrast, an income claim ("I'll help you make $2,000 more") carries real risk because the person might not actually do it; savings claims carry no such delivery risk, which makes claims much easier in that space.
- (2026-02-09) Dave Ramsey has four revenue streams; media is his biggest, at roughly $120M/year (stated by Ramsey publicly on Alex's podcast).
- (2026-02-09) Ramsey's topline is ~$300M (also stated publicly), leaving ~$180M across his other streams: an app that helps people save money, coaching, and courses/events (which could be counted as a fifth stream).
- (2026-02-09) Alex's "thousand IQ move" recommendation: give away all the info, then sell the implementation — i.e., sell helping people implement it.
- (2026-02-09) Implementation can be sold at a low level via remote one-on-one, remote one-to-many, or remote semi-private.
- (2026-02-09) Remote semi-private (e.g. one-to-six) is, in his opinion, the most profitable model: you can bill nearly as much as one-on-one, clients stay accountable, and you 6x your income.
- (2026-02-09) After that, you can sell an in-person experience.
- (2026-02-09) A surprising insight: you can sell the same information in a different medium for a different price.
- (2026-02-09) The pricing ladder by medium: information as a PDF/giveaway is cheap or free; delivered as an 8-week remote livestream it becomes coaching/implementation and is worth more; delivered as an in-person classroom it becomes even more valuable — even when the information is identical.
- (2026-02-09) A big driver of the higher value is the perceived likelihood that the buyer actually consumes and does the material; if someone shows up in person they will watch and consume all of it.
- (2026-02-09) Most importantly, a richer medium lets buyers get their questions answered in real time — very valuable, because "will I actually get the thing I want from this information?" is one of people's biggest risks/concerns.
- (2026-02-09) Recommendation: think deliberately about the medium you deliver information in; you can put paywalls on even the same concepts delivered through a different mechanism.
