# Why Nobody Wants Your Free Service

## Summary

Alex coaches a new agency owner who is offering diagnostics, proposals, and fulfillment
for free but still getting ghosted. Alex says the real issue is hidden cost and small
sample size, not necessarily a bad strategy. The useful question is "why not?" because the
price charged is often not the most expensive part of buying.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-16, Alex says when people reject a free service, the issue is often hidden
  cost: perceived work, friction, risk, or misunderstanding.
- He says asking "why not?" reveals the pile of gold because it identifies the next
  problems the offer must solve.
- Self-reported: a content agency got Alex to start making shorts by taking existing
  content, sending shorts weekly, requiring only yes/no, doing it free for a month, then
  asking to be paid.
- He says five leads is too small a sample to judge the strategy; a 20% close rate would
  be good, so the founder needs many more at-bats.
- He says the first objective for a small business owner is letting people know the
  business exists; the first four hours of the day should be advertising.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The price that you charge is not the most expensive thing about making a purchase with you."

> "If you can then solve the next three, four, five problems, then you have a very valuable service."

> "First objective when you're a small business owner is let people know you exist because no one does."
