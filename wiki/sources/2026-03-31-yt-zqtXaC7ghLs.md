# Life Doesn't Fight Fair

## Summary

Alex rejects a simple motivation narrative and describes work as a behavior reinforced
by past rewards and present stimulation. He operationalizes well-being as repeating a
currently preferred day, while treating suffering as feedback that accompanies growth,
stagnation, and decline rather than proof that life is malfunctioning.

## Key claims

- Alex self-reports taking most of 2021 off and experiencing it as one of his most
  depressed years, although later memory softened the punishment relative to its rewards.
- As of March 2026, he says he works largely because work is highly stimulating and has
  been reinforced, while tidy childhood or mission stories may be retrospective narratives.
- His operational happiness exercise is to define the components of a good current day
  and maximize how often that day repeats, allowing the definition to change with age.
- Growth, plateau, and decline each produce different stress; pain alone therefore does
  not establish that something is wrong.
- He self-reports ranking 2024 his best adult year and 2025 a local low, though still
  better than several earlier years; the comparison helps him expect survival and recovery.
- He reports beginning 2025 with eight lawsuits, resolving five in February, dealing
  with unspecified health issues, and facing talent constraints at Acquisition.com.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Pain in and of itself is not an indication that there's something wrong."

> "One of the greatest sources of suffering is thinking that suffering is a problem."

> "Life doesn't fight fair."
