# Helping a Real Estate Developer Doing $2.1M/Year Get to $30M

## Summary
In a live workshop exchange, a real-estate developer who converts motels and hotels to apartments (adaptive reuse) across the Sun Belt states describes wanting to grow from $2.1M net (on track for $14M revenue) toward ~$30M by becoming a thought leader to generate off-market deal flow. Alex reframes the real problem as deal flow, not thought leadership specifically, and argues the best path is whichever has the highest overlap with the owner's existing skill set. He offers his "fallacy of the perfect pick" mental model against analysis paralysis and advises sticking to what one has a track record for when making content in an interest-graph-driven algorithm.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the developer's actual problem as deal flow / pipeline, not thought leadership; thought leadership is one path up the mountain, not the path.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the highest-reward, lowest-risk strategy is the one with the most overlap with the operator's existing skill set (face-to-face → conferences; media affinity → content; ads background → paid lead gen), maximizing likelihood of success.
- As of March 2026, Alex says a simple paid ad (e.g. "motel owner looking to sell? let's talk") can generate deal flow cheaply; he cites his own payment-processing test that got 13 qualified companies on the phone for ~$6,000 in ad spend.
- As of March 2026, Alex teaches the "fallacy of the perfect pick": there is no perfect path, only tradeoffs; the two costly traps are stalling at the base deciding, and path-switching mid-climb.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises committing 18 months before judging whether thought leadership is working, tying it to his own 10-year YouTube commitment framing.
- As of March 2026, Alex says social media has shifted to interest-based / interest-graph media, so to attract a specific lead type you must make that specific content and stick to your track record.
- Caller claims (unverified): converts motels/hotels to apartments across the Sun Belt, on track for $14M revenue and $2.1M net, runs a Reg D 506 fund, previously partnered with (now key-man risk) Richard Wilson of Family Office Club, and now raises the capital himself.
