# Helping a Law Firm Solve a Marketing Constraint

## Summary

A law firm owner tells Alex he wants to scale but faces two constraints: all his marketing is outsourced to agencies he doesn't understand, and he needs to recruit higher-level talent; he also flags cash-flow intensity. Asked which to tackle first, Alex advises solving marketing first — but rather than immediately building an in-house team, he recommends hiring an excellent, referral-sourced agency and paying it a premium to teach the owner everything it does over an extended period, then retaining it as a consultant afterward. Alex frames this "pay to learn" approach as how he personally learned every marketing platform.

## Key claims

- As of March 2026, Alex advises tackling the marketing constraint before the talent/recruiting constraint for this owner.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims the law firms that make the most money are "exceptional promoters," especially in the transactional legal space.
- As of March 2026, Alex prefers learning marketing from agencies over the coaching world because agencies must produce results or get fired.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends sourcing agencies via referrals and hiring one with a proven track record.
- Alex's method: tell the agency you want to absorb everything they do, but over a longer period than a normal client; compensate them more for the extra time, then pay a consulting period afterward for troubleshooting — so they earn more lifetime value than a normal customer in exchange for teaching you.
- As of March 2026, Alex says this "just pay someone more" approach is how he has learned every platform.
- Alex references a script for approaching agencies in the agencies chapter of his "Leads" book, and advises re-using the same script with the next agency if one declines.
- The owner states his current agency offers no transparency (unverified caller claim); he also self-describes his team as roughly six to seven people (unverified).
- The clip closes with a standard invitation to Alex's Las Vegas headquarters for a two-day scaling workshop.
