# How to Get in Shape

## Summary

Alex answers a question about getting six-pack abs by separating knowledge from
motivation. He says the mechanics are simple: calorie deficit for visible abs,
progressive overload and protein for muscle, and enough time. The harder problem is
accountability and willingness to pay the price. He frames behavior change as carrots
and sticks, convenience, identity stories, and deciding whether a body outcome is worth
spending one's limited daily time.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex says he has counted macros for about 20 years.
- He says visible abs are primarily a body-fat-percentage issue, which requires eating
  less than one burns.
- He says being lean and muscular requires progressive overload, protein, and time.
- He recommends hitting every muscle group about three times per week, adding weight or
  reps over time, and maintaining a calorie deficit.
- He says the issue for many people is not a knowledge gap but an accountability gap.
- He frames human failure as unwillingness to endure short-term discomfort for long-term
  achievement.
- He says behavior can be changed with reinforcement or punishment, carrots or sticks.
- He tells the caller to identify what not having a six-pack currently does for him,
  such as convenience or a story that justifies late-night food.
- He says not every fitness outcome is necessary; the relevant question is what problem
  the person is solving.
- He describes his current body as a "passive income body," meaning maintaining it is
  much easier than building it was.
