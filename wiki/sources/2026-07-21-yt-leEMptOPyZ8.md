# Volume Negates Luck. Violence Is the Answer

## Summary
A short direct-to-camera clip (with brief off-camera interjections from an unidentified
second voice affirming/agreeing — attribution uncertain, not Alex) in which Alex Hormozi
unpacks two credos printed in the first few pages of his sales team's handbook (hosted at
"turnedlateacquisition.com" per the caption, likely a mis-transcription of an
acquisition.com-family URL): "volume negates luck" and "violence is the answer." He frames
"violence" not literally but as exhaustive, controlled-controllable effort, then pivots to a
personal philosophy he and Leila share — "a man must have a quest" — arguing that aimlessness
(not adversity) is what leaves people feeling lost and hopeless. He closes distinguishing
hopelessness (a perceived lack of options) from anxiety (many options, no priorities), quotes
a line from the film *300* about dying trying, and ends with a to-camera pitch for a free
scaling roadmap.

## Key claims
_All dated 2026-07-21. Paraphrased unless quoted. Alex-attributed unless marked._

- (2026-07-21) Two credos open his sales team's handbook: "volume negates luck" and "violence
  is the answer" — described as credos the team lives by.
- (2026-07-21) "Violence" is reframed as controlling the controllable — doing everything
  possible to win so that if an uncontrollable failure happens anyway (he gives the example of
  a lightning strike or a Vegas power outage during a book launch), he can still say he left
  nothing in the tank.
- (2026-07-21) Shared belief with Leila: "a man must have a quest" — the object of the quest is
  interchangeable (best father, best musician, best businessman, "best tire replacer," "best
  sweeper"); what matters is having one and pursuing it fully.
- (2026-07-21) Diagnosis of being lost: being questless/aimless, not adversity itself, is what
  produces hopelessness — defined as a perceived lack of options — while anxiety is defined
  as having many options but no priorities.
- (2026-07-21) Values a partner who "rewards the good fight" rather than only rewarding winning,
  illustrated via a quote from the film *300* ("Come back with your shield or on it").
- (2026-07-21) Closing pitch: a free scaling roadmap distilled from lessons scaling ten
  businesses past $10M and three past $100M, framed as covering zero-to-one, zero-to-ten, and
  zero-to-$100M-plus stages.
