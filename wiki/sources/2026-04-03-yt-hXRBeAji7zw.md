# Helping a Financial Services Business Scale

## Summary

Alex recommends that a Quebec financial-services company test English-language demand
across Canada before assuming geography is the constraint. He proposes comparing a
broad national campaign with localized campaigns, while making licensing a prerequisite
for expansion.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports two recent $100,000 months, 60:1 lifetime-value-to-acquisition-
  cost economics, 70% profit, and leads from an offer promoted on Meta; all are unverified.
- As of April 2026, Alex believes the company may still have room to reach $5-$10 million
  inside Quebec, but treats English-language creative as the main incremental requirement
  for testing the rest of Canada.
- He recommends hiring an English-speaking presenter and changing the targeting and
  headline before making larger operational changes.
- The company can compare one generic Canada campaign with roughly 20 localized
  campaigns; he expects local messaging may convert better while broad targeting may cost less.
- Licensing must be confirmed before national expansion; Alex does not offer legal or
  regulatory advice in the source.
- Alex self-reports that an Acquisition.com portfolio company sells insurance nationwide,
  but the company and operating details are not verified here.
