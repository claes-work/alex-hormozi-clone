# Why Are 40% of My Customers Canceling Before Install?

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Jacob, whose sales agency sells electricity plans and backup power but loses about 40% of customers before install because fulfillment takes roughly 120 days. Alex says the issue is probably not fulfillment capacity alone but expectation-setting in the sale. If customers already have electricity and the value proposition is a cheaper bill later, the sales script should explicitly say the savings take four months and require no action after signing.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Jacob** - the coachee. His electricity / backup-power sales agency, revenue, fulfillment partner, wait time, and cancellation rate are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A 40% pre-install cancellation rate means something is wrong with the sale.
- If the delivery delay is known, the sales team should sell the delay clearly instead of implying faster activation and pushing the timeline later.
- Expectation management can turn a fulfillment delay into an accepted condition of the offer.
- The correct framing: if the customer wants a lower electric bill, they can sign now, wait about four months, and be first in line for the lower rate.
- For this case, Alex's entire fix is a sales-script / expectation-setting change rather than a major operational overhaul.
