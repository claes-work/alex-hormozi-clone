# Uncertainty Is the Point

## Summary

Alex recounts the prolonged, unglamorous beginning of his first gym and argues that
uncertainty is what makes pursuit meaningful. A later success story compresses years of
isolation and repetition into a short montage, but the capability being built during the
uncertain period is already the real-time result.

## Key claims

- Alex self-reports arriving in California without lodging, renting a $400 room, then
  sleeping on the first gym's turf beneath a noisy parking structure for about six months.
- He reports earning exactly $4,972 in the first month, equal to the gym's rent, after
  enrolling 29 members in two weeks before opening; all figures are self-reported.
- He says the prospect of returning to his father as a failure felt worse than continuing,
  because it would restore his father's authority over his choices.
- As of March 2026, he calls "it must be easy" a victim frame because it relocates one's
  lack of action or capability into an external explanation and removes agency.
- A guaranteed outcome would remove mystery and excitement; uncertainty is therefore part
  of what makes difficult work worth doing.
- He argues that becoming more capable during pursuit is already an outcome, even if the
  external objective never materializes.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The Rocky cut scene lasts 30 seconds in the movie, but it can last five years in your life."

> "The lack of certainty is what actually makes it worth it."

> "Who you are becoming is the thing that you are amounting to in real time every day."
