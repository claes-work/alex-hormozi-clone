# Pain Is Not a Problem. It's Proof Your Alive

## Summary

Alex argues that all business conditions contain pain: growth, stagnation, and decline
are all painful in different ways. If pain is built into reality, he treats it as a signal
of being alive rather than a decision factor. He rejects judging pain as good or bad and
uses gym performance as an analogy: feeling bad has no necessary correlation with
performance, so the advanced form of resilience is not considering feelings when deciding
whether to do the necessary thing.

## Key claims

- Growth, plateau, and decline are all painful, so pain is not evidence that something is
  wrong.
- Alex reframes pain as a signal of being alive rather than a problem to solve.
- He rejects over-weighting whether pain feels good or bad.
- In the gym analogy, ordinary discomfort does not necessarily correlate with performance.
- The mature form of resilience is doing the required action without even considering how
  one feels.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "All conditions of reality are painful."

> "Pain is a prerequisite for reality."

> "You don't even consider how you feel."
