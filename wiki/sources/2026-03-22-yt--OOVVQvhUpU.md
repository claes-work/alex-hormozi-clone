# Focus on Winning, Not the People Beating You

## Summary
Alex Hormozi argues that winners keep their eyes on their goal while losers focus on
winners, and that criticism always comes from below, never above. He frames indifference
to others' opinions as a "cheat code" borrowed from one's imagined 85-year-old self, and
ties long-term success to holding a personal standard high enough to ignore lower bars set
by friends and doubters. The talk closes with a promotion for the free acquisition.com
$100M scaling road map.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex claims winners focus on winning while losers focus on winners, and
  that "hate" only ever comes from below, never from above, because winners keep their eyes
  on the goal rather than on other people.
- As of March 2026, he claims progress runs in seasons: first you look like an idiot while
  figuring it out, then others look like idiots for having doubted you.
- As of March 2026, he advises "cheat coding" the indifference of an imagined 85-year-old
  self into the present rather than looking back at 85 wishing you had known it sooner.
- As of March 2026, he claims the rich get richer because they see reality more accurately,
  and the poor see through "the lens of poverty"; his stated life goal is to see reality
  more accurately.
- As of March 2026, he says one must build "armor" against snide comments (working too
  hard, being obsessed, "toxic work capacity") until it fuses with you and you no longer
  hear it — and that he escaped his old critics because his reality changed drastically and
  because they are no longer in his world.
- As of March 2026, he claims how far you go is predicated on one thing — your actual
  standard — and that whoever holds the highest standard for a company should run it,
  including running acquisition.com over him.
- As of March 2026, he claims the biggest thing rich kids inherit from rich parents is a
  standard/expectation of life that makes them refuse opportunities below that bar.
- As of March 2026, he advises those without successful role models to "borrow someone
  else's measuring stick" — asking whether Elon, Bezos, or Jobs would pursue a given
  standard.
- As of March 2026, he frames the practical work as elimination: cutting bad friends (and
  their opinions) and removing interference on your time, attention, and environment.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Winners focus on winning. Losers focus on winners. And you never receive hate from above, always below, because winners have their eyes on the goal, not on other people."

> "First, you look like an idiot to everyone else while you try to figure it out. Then, everyone else looks like an idiot for ever doubting you. Seasons."

> "The difference between them and you is that what they consider work, you consider life."

> "The reason that the rich can get richer is because they see the world differently than the poor. And so it makes sense that if your poor friends don't see what you see, that they see through the lens of poverty. And all my goal in life is to be able to see reality more accurately."

> "I was only able to get here because what they deemed acceptable, I deemed intolerable."

> "How far you go in life, I truly believe this, is based and predicated only on one thing, which is your actual standard."

> "If there's ever a day that someone has a higher standard for acquisition.com than me, they should run this instead of me."

> "If you want to be like them, then act like them. If you want to be like your friends, then you can act like your friends, but don't be surprised that you have what they have or lack what they lack."
