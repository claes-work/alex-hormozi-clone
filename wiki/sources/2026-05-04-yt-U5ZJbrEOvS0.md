# Stop Letting Influencers Price Your Product Too Cheap

## Summary

Alex rejects an influencer partner's desire to price a community at $1 per month instead
of $14. His argument is simple unit economics: the $1 offer would need a conversion rate
14 times higher than the $14 offer to produce the same revenue, which he says will not
happen. He warns creators against pricing from their own fear or from their audience's
wallet, and argues that the loudest offended followers are usually not the buyers.

## Key claims

- A $1 community price usually cannot make enough money unless conversion increases by
  the same multiple as the price reduction.
- Alex argues that a $1 community would need roughly 10-14x the conversion of a $10-$14
  community, which he says is unrealistic.
- Creators should not price from fear of their audience or from their own wallet.
- Alex expects some followers to object to paid offers, but says offended non-buyers are
  not the customer base.
- He considers $14 per month low for many communities and says many creators have room to
  charge $99, $199, or $299 depending on the community.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's just math."

> "Stop selling out of your wallet."

> "The people that you will piss off will never be the buyers."
