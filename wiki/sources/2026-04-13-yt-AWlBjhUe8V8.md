# How to Get Distracted Employees to Actually Focus

## Summary

Alex reframes distracted employees as a culture-and-training problem rather than damaged
brains. He defines organizational culture as the rules governing reinforcement: leaders
must notice and immediately reward desired behavior, use negative reinforcement within
a much larger positive balance, and make values visible through public praise and
private criticism.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says leaders cannot "untrain" behavior; they can train better
  or additional behaviors.
- He defines organizational culture as the rules governing what gets reinforced as good
  or bad across hundreds of small actions.
- He recommends immediately rewarding correct behavior so the leader becomes a reliable
  source of reinforcement.
- Negative reinforcement can work when the leader has earned permission through a much
  higher volume of positive reinforcement.
- Alex says retention decisions and public praise or punishment communicate the real
  values of an organization.
- In a self-reported Slack example, a leader named Caleb volunteered for low-status work
  after 17 others waited; Alex publicly linked that initiative to why Caleb was the boss.
- He recommends praise in public and criticism in private.
- Because new hires bring their own behavioral rules, he says culture dilutes during
  growth unless leaders reinforce company rules more strongly.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Culture in an organization is the rules that govern reinforcement."

> "More public on the praise, more private on the criticism."

> "This is why he has moved up."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: culture as rules of reinforcement; leaders as reinforcement totems; scale requires stronger cultural signaling.
