# Why I Work Triple Shifts

## Summary

Alex explains why he sometimes works "triple shifts" while preparing a presentation. He
usually works two concentrated shifts, but a triple shift means returning after dinner
and working late into the night. His refrains are that there are no shortcuts for him,
he will do what is required, the work needs doing, and even if the outcome looks good to
others, he will know whether he could have done better.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice)

- Alex says he likes David Goggins' idea that there are no shortcuts "for you."
- He wrote the referenced work quote during his 11th run of a presentation.
- His normal workday is roughly 10 to 12 concentrated hours, six and a half days a week.
- A triple shift means working again after dinner, often from about 7:30 p.m. until
  11:30 p.m. or 1 a.m.
- He associates triple shifts with 16 to 17 hours of actual productive work.
- Alex hears the same "good enough" thoughts as other people, but hates them.
- His internal refrains are "I will do what is required" and "this work needs doing."
- "But I'll know" means that even public praise will not feel good if he knows he could
  have done better.
- He says he has never regretted working harder.
- A former boss's advice, that happiness is stringing together as many good days as
  possible, became a blueprint for how he lives.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "There's no shortcuts for you."

> "I will do what is required."

> "But I'll know."
