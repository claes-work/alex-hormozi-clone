# Stop Letting Other People's Preferences Run Your Life

## Summary

Alex reframes criticism as a preference mismatch: the critic is effectively saying Alex
lives in a way they would not prefer. He applies the same frame to complaints about the
world, other people, or oneself. Circumstances and other people are usually unproductive
targets for blame; self-blame can be useful only if it leads to action.

## Key claims

- Negative comments can be translated into "Alex lives his life in a way I would not
  prefer."
- That reframe reduces the emotional weight of criticism because other people's
  preferences do not have to govern one's life.
- Alex says complaints often mean the universe exists in a way the complainer does not
  prefer.
- He groups blame into circumstances, other people, and self.
- Blaming circumstances or others does not serve action; self-blame can serve only if it
  changes behavior.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Alex lives his life in a way that I would not prefer."

> "Why are you not bending to my will?"

> "Am I blaming circumstances? Am I blaming other people? Am I blaming me?"
