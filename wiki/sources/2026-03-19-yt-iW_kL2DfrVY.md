# Helping a Nonprofit Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The founder runs a **nonprofit** ($3.9M revenue, two years old) that closes the gap on "care, cost, and coverage" of health benefits for hard-working Americans. Its model: it takes ~20% of the healthcare savings it generates for employer clients (companies with 50+ employees), positioned as a fix for the "most broken model in America" — where brokers and insurers earn 5–10% commissions to keep clients overpaying. The founder gets nearly all traffic from ~170 affiliate/referral partners (CPAs, financial-advisory groups) rather than paid ads, because the pitch ("we'll save you ~$2,000/employee using US tax codes") sounds "too good to be true" and needs borrowed authority/trust. Two problems he raises: (1) a weak, scammy-sounding pitch, and (2) clients won't give testimonials because reducing their tax burden is "cancel culture" exposure.

Alex reframes the whole thing using for-profit frameworks. **On the pitch:** he offers a more believable angle rooted in the nonprofit structure itself — "we save you 15% because we're a nonprofit: the ~20% industry-average profit margin that a normal business would keep, we make zero, and that zero goes to you." The *mechanism* (nonprofit = no profit margin taken) becomes the believable reason. **On the core reframe:** he tells the founder to "chunk up one level" — the real customers aren't the employers, they're the **centers of influence** (the CPAs/referral partners). All acquisition metrics and marketing messaging should be rebuilt around cost-to-acquire-a-center-of-influence, not cost-to-acquire-an-employer. The warm handoff from partner to nonprofit is the mechanical piece Acquisition can help wire up. **On the affiliate layer:** he cites the affiliates chapter of *The Leads Book* — every affiliate needs a **launch** (fast burst: "refer me 10 people so you get an internal case study," or work for free in exchange for using their story) and an **integration** (the more valuable, durable piece: embed the offer into the partner's own onboarding so you capture ~100% of their new customers, not just whoever they choose to refer). With 170 partners each sending one qualified client per month, the business becomes a $100M business. **On priorities:** with a 10% close rate and only ~1 account closed per month, the offer is fine but "your sales process sucks" — fixing sales alone could triple the business "doing nothing else." His two-step plan: (1) fix the sales process first, (2) then build affiliate top-of-funnel via launch-and-integrate. He declines the founder's ask to be handed his content library/partnership until the founder actually executes the fundamentals first. (Closes with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo — not a claim.)

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex reframes a nonprofit's donors/clients using standard for-profit lenses: fundraising and referral partners are treated as sales and marketing, and the entities that send business (CPAs, centers of influence) are treated as the actual customers to be acquired with real acquisition metrics.

- As of March 2026, Alex says to "chunk up one level" — rebuild the value proposition and all acquisition metrics around acquiring **centers of influence** (referral partners), not around acquiring end-employer clients directly, because that reframe gives significantly more leverage.
