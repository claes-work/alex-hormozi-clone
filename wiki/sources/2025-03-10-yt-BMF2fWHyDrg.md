# Building a $3,500,000 Business for a Stranger in 51 Mins

## Summary

Live-build coaching session where Alex Hormozi diagnoses "Trophy Outlet," a third-generation
custom trophy/plaque e-commerce business doing ~$1.72M revenue at a thin 6.4% net margin, and
maps a 9-point plan to double it. His core diagnosis: the business is under-monetized because
of cost-plus pricing, a break-even second retail location, and no email/return-customer engine.
His growth thesis is to force a strategic decision (gag-gift/media direct-to-consumer vs.
B2B/recurring leagues), then consolidate costs, run pricing tests, max out proven channels (SEO),
and go all-in on TikTok organic + TikTok Shop to scale from ~$1M toward $10M.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- **Cost-plus pricing loses money; switch to value-based pricing.** "Every business I know that
  does a cost-plus model doesn't make a lot of money." Price should be divorced from your input
  costs and set purely on the customer's willingness to pay.
- **On thin-margin businesses, small price increases multiply profit.** A ~6% price raise can
  double net profit; compounding monthly raises (6% × 6%...) compounds profit. Not testing your
  price is "the most expensive thing you can do in a business."
- **Optimize price for gross profit × units sold, not for max conversion.** You usually "make more
  money before you make less money" as sales velocity drops — the profit sweet spot sits at a
  higher price than most entrepreneurs think (price elasticity).
- **Let the customer set the price via split tests.** Don't take anyone's advice (including your
  own) on price — run a test on your highest-sales-velocity items and 80/20 it. If it goes too
  high, just lower it again.
- **Price sensitivity differs by avatar/channel.** Car-show/B2B buyers are very price-sensitive;
  gag-gift buyers are impulse buyers with far more pricing power ($8 vs $16 barely matters to them)
  — a reason to lean toward the gag-gift path.
- **Consolidate to focus; kill the break-even location.** Closing the second retail location saves
  ~$50k/year that drops straight to the bottom line (~+50% profit) at near-zero revenue loss.
  "The amount of return you get on focus as an entrepreneur is higher than any resource."
- **Decide, meaning cut off a path.** "Decide" comes from Latin "to cut off"; pursuing two masters
  (gag-gift media vs. B2B recurring) serves neither. Frame the choice by which problem you'd rather
  solve, not which upside you'd rather have (Leila's downside-framing).
- **Don't buy the acquisition target.** Two red flags in the retiring competitor's business: keyman
  risk (undocumented owner knowledge) and 70% of revenue from a single customer. (Alex's Value
  Acceleration Method: maximize value, minimize risk.)
- **Post far more than "recommended."** Posting caps are mythology; the platform is incentivized to
  distribute good content. Only 1–2% of your audience sees a given post, so more posts reach
  different people. Quality is the real ceiling, not frequency.
- **Go where the fish are — go all-in on TikTok Shop while it's hot.** At "blood in the water" early
  success, go ham. His price point ($20–50) crushes on TikTok Shop; gag gifts are inherently
  shareable/viral. Hit affiliates/influencers hard; their winning videos feed your content and ads.
- **The content → ads → influencer loop.** Make TikTok content; ~10% goes viral; put ad money behind
  the winners; send winners to influencers ("make stuff like this"); model their winners back into
  your content. Be hook-centric — reuse a winning hook over and over.
- **Hire someone younger for native content.** If you're an older millennial, hire a 17-year-old to
  track trends/memes so content is contextually native and gets more shares.
- **Own your own branded search terms with PPC.** "I've rarely seen anyone in any business just own
  their own terms and not make money off of it." You won't always get the organic click, so pay for
  the intent-based ones (names + derivatives), then expand to long-tail associated terms.
- **Max out what already returns 13:1.** If a channel (his SEO) returns $13 per $1 and doesn't cost
  your time, pour in as many dollars as possible — ask the agency to "max me out."
- **Email is a "big bag of money."** Mature e-commerce gets 30–50% of revenue from email, nearly all
  profit (no ad spend). Run two types: behavior-based flows (abandoned cart, product-view sequences)
  and long-term nurture. Keep emails all-text with a link to an image (curiosity drives clicks;
  image-heavy emails hurt deliverability). Use a double opt-in "reply for the funniest trophy" to
  boost deliverability.
- **Put an offer in every box.** Replace a weak "10% off" insert with a compelling BOGO ("buy one get
  two/three free") featuring your top 3 gag gifts + a QR code, since you already own fulfillment.
- **"We make sales to get customers, we don't get customers to make sales."** The first (gag-gift)
  transaction is just the entry point; everything after — the box offer, email remarketing, social
  remarketing — exists to convert it into a repeat/lifelong customer.
- **Prioritize with ICE.** Order moves by Impact, Confidence, Ease — do the high-impact, high-
  confidence, easy ones first, then work down.
