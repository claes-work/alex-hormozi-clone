# How to Make Your Goal the Only Thing That Matters

## Summary

Alex frames life as trade-offs. People have what they have and what they want; the only
question is which current things they are willing to trade for the desired future. He says
time spent on anything not serving the goal reveals, behaviorally, that the other thing is
more important. He repeats his compressed life advice: figure out what you want, ignore
others' opinions, and do unreasonable volume.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-16, Alex says everyone trades current possessions, comforts, and time for
  what they want.
- He says there is no perfect way to live one's 20s or 30s: one can become under-skilled
  or under-lived, but not avoid trade-offs.
- He says every hour not spent pursuing the goal is behaviorally being ranked above the
  goal.
- He cites Seneca's time-wasting idea as external attribution, not his own coinage.
- He says giving up low-value time sinks is not really sacrifice if the desired goal is
  more valuable.
- He repeats the 60-second compressed advice: decide what one wants, ignore opinions, and
  do so much volume that failure would be unreasonable.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "All we have are trade-offs."

> "Everything that you spend your time on that is not you pursuing your goal, you are determining is more important than your goal."

> "Figure out what you want, ignore the opinions of others, and do so much volume that it would be unreasonable to not be successful."
