# Helping a $3M Balloon Arch Business Fix Their Offer

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a national balloon-arch and event-decor business doing about $2.2M trailing twelve months and aiming for $3M. The owner thinks the issues are avatar, offer, positioning, and messaging. Alex points him toward corporate nationwide events, productized packages, value-based pricing, and a global business-value frame that sells reduced coordination headache and better total return rather than cheaper balloons.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Bespoke services should be mapped into a small product suite when a few setups cover most use cases.
- A business at $1M-$3M should double down on the highest LTV:CAC avatar before broadening.
- Cost-plus pricing is usually the weakest pricing model; willingness to pay and value delivered should set price.
- Being the most expensive means not selling to everyone, and losing price-sensitive buyers is part of the trade.
- Corporate buyers may try to commoditize the vendor; the seller has to reset the frame to total return, time saved, and switching-cost reduction.
- A nationwide events buyer can pay more for consistency and reduced coordination across many local vendors.
