# The 4-Part Framework to Build Influence (SPCL)

## Summary

In this teaching segment, Alex Hormozi presents SPCL, a four-part framework for building influence: status, power, credibility, and likeness. He defines influence as high likelihood of compliance with requests and argues that content should build influence rather than merely views. Status means controlling scarce reinforcers; power comes from say-do correspondence; credibility comes from observable proof; likeness comes from similarity, shared values, or perceived fit.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The content problem is often not only too little content, but the wrong type of content for the customers the business wants.
- SPCL stands for status, power, credibility, and likeness.
- Status is controlling reinforcers or scarce resources in a given environment.
- Power comes from say-do correspondence: the audience does what the person says, gets a good result, and becomes more likely to comply again.
- Credibility is built through observable proof that is hard to fake.
- Likeness includes relatability, shared values, behavioral similarity, or literal resemblance.
- The four elements can work independently, but stacked together they create stronger influence.
- Influence means a high likelihood that an audience complies with the creator's requests.
