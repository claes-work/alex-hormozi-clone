# You Don't Have to Be an Expert, Just Be a Facilitator

## Summary

Alex advises a no-code web design seller who wants to use a group for roofing-company
customers without teaching. First, Alex says to sell outside the founder's own local
market and target markets such as the US, UK, or Australia where the same work can earn
more. For the community itself, he says the founder does not need to fake expertise; he
can facilitate a roundtable where roofers share what is working and where they are stuck.

## Key claims

- Alex tells the founder in India to sell outside his own zip code and target customers
  with more ability to pay.
- He warns founders to price from the customer's wallet, not their own.
- A non-expert community owner can position himself as a facilitator rather than a
  teacher.
- Alex says the original meaning of mastermind was a peer roundtable where members share
  what is working.
- A simple offer can be enough: a group, two facilitated calls per month, and done-for-you
  page setup.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Sell out of your own zip code."

> "Sell out of their wallet, not yours."

> "If you're not an expert, don't pretend to be an expert. Just be a facilitator."
