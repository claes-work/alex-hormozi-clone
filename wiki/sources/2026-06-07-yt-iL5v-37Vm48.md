# The Real Problem With Done-For-You Businesses

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the same high-ticket sales and marketing business covered in a nearby done-for-you/franchise clip. The company serves coaches and done-for-you / business-opportunity-style clients, wants to scale past $5M, and worries about operational bottlenecks and sales-team churn. Alex argues that the real problem is the business's underlying client quality and fulfillment risk: selling non-legitimate or weakly fulfilled offers attracts mercenary salespeople, creates liability, and produces a harder business than partnering with legitimate franchises or stronger products.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The operational problem in a done-for-you sales agency may actually be the quality of the things being sold.
- A company selling dreams it cannot control fulfillment for can create liability and weak long-term enterprise value.
- A better path is to replace weak clients with legitimate ones rather than simply grow the same client count.
- It may be easier to grow one legitimate product or service to $100M than to grow many weak offers to small scale.
- Franchises are attractive examples because regulation, disclosures, and existing operations can protect both sides.
- Direct-response offer-world businesses may weaken as CAC rises and trust falls.
- Sales churn should be interpreted through cohorts; high initial attrition can still work if retained reps stay for years.
- Mercenary reps are often a symptom of the underlying product and mission, not only a compensation problem.
