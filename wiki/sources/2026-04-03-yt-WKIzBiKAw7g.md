# How Do I Make My Investment Fund Actually Look and Sound Legitimate?

## Summary

Alex tells a real-estate fund owner that educational language is attracting people who
want to learn underwriting rather than invest. He recommends presenting the actual fund,
returns assumptions, and professional materials more clearly, benchmarking established
fund managers, and favoring channels where accredited investors congregate.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports $3.1 million in revenue, a $10 million goal, both fund and
  syndication structures, and a focus on accredited investors; these details are unverified.
- As of April 2026, Alex diagnoses messaging and presentation as the constraint because
  the owner's description initially sounded like an investment-education product.
- Free education can remain part of acquisition, but the core message should foreground
  the real fund, expected returns, pro formas, and the language used by fund managers.
- He recommends auditing the website and social profiles against established managers'
  materials so the visual and verbal signals match the intended investor audience.
- He recommends X and LinkedIn for reaching accredited investors, while explicitly
  warning that investment promotion faces substantial compliance scrutiny.
- Building a more credible fund brand may sacrifice near-term education revenue for
  larger, more aligned checks over a longer horizon.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You just need to level up your language so that you'll attract a higher level person."

> "If you're going to build the fund, then build the fund. And just do it right."

> "You'll have to be really careful with compliance."
