# The 3 Things I'd Put My Future Self Through to Be Bulletproof

## Summary

Alex describes motivational frames for becoming "bulletproof" and feeling gratitude. He
imagines the person he wants to become as the destination and asks what he would put that
person through to create him; the answer is hard struggle, not easy wins. His strongest
gratitude frame is imagining his 85-year-old self waking up in his current body and
noticing youth, health, energy, Leila, and the mundane details he would later miss.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex thinks of the person he wants to become as the destination.
- He wants to be bulletproof in marriage, business, and other domains.
- To create that future person, he would put himself through difficulty just above his
  threshold, not easy times.
- Hard times can be framed as making the future person.
- His strongest gratitude frame is imagining his 85-year-old self waking up as his
  current self.
- That frame makes ordinary health, energy, youth, and time with Leila feel vivid.
- The gratitude frame lasts only a few minutes, but those minutes can become the part of
  the day he remembers.
- Presence can function as a proxy for gratitude by opening up to what is happening.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I'm making you."

> "My 85-year-old self waking up as my 30-year-old self."

> "It allows me to enjoy the mundane."
