# Just Post More

## Summary
In this workshop-style clip, Alex Hormozi advises a caller who runs a short-form-video sports-betting affiliate business (roughly $1M revenue, $850K profit in 10 months, driven by TikTok/Instagram picks driving betting-app sign-ups on a CPA basis). Hormozi tells him his growth constraint is not LTV but simply posting volume: more posts mean more distribution, not cannibalized views. He also addresses whether the caller needs to own a product, and argues the real leverage play is becoming a "whale" affiliate big enough that a betting platform would want to acquire the media asset.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the caller's binding constraint is content volume, not LTV — the fix is "just do more of what's working."
- As of March 2026, Alex claims posting more does not get you penalized by the algorithm, citing that Instagram's Adam Mosseri has publicly stated they do not penalize you for posting frequency.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames each post as another "dot" of distribution: posting five times just means more dots (more people) see your content, rather than splitting a fixed pool of views.
- As of March 2026, Alex says perceived view "cannibalization" from posting more on one account is usually a content-quality issue, not a frequency penalty.
- As of March 2026, Alex says it makes no difference whether content is evergreen or topical/news-based — what matters is impressions per month.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends concentrating on one account rather than spinning up multiple accounts to post more about the same events.
- As of March 2026, Alex says a pure affiliate business (no owned product/customer/LTV) is fine if the goal is just to make money, but hard to sell someday unless you own a betting platform.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the path to being acquirable is scale: reach ~$10M/year in revenue first, because an acquirer paying you $10M/year implies they make $30–50M/year off your channel, making the acquisition worthwhile.
- As of March 2026, Alex sketches an enterprise-value logic: $50M/year in revenue at ~40% net margins is ~$20M EBITDA, trading at roughly a 12x multiple, implying ~$240M in enterprise value — so an acquirer would pay a large multiple to own that channel.
- As of March 2026, Alex's stated leverage principle: become such a big ("whale") acquisition channel that the acquirer is afraid of losing you — that fear is where your negotiating leverage comes from.
- (Caller-reported, unverified) The caller says he did ~$1M revenue / ~$850K profit in 10 months, mostly from short-form driving affiliate sign-ups, and wants to reach $5M; he previously ran content for Cody Sanchez. He also cites a similar competitor doing ~$4M/year via a Skool community plus affiliate commissions.
