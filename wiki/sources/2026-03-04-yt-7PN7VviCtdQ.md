# Go Out and Fail...

## Summary
Short solo motivational clip on **failure as the prerequisite to winning**. Alex opens with the line that "losers stay losers by never being willing to lose," and argues everyone loses before they win. He uses the Matrix "first jump" scene — Neo falls, and Cypher says "everybody falls the first time" — to frame failure as universal, even for "the one." Your first business won't be your last; your first product will embarrass you later; what matters is building, launching, and "shooting the shot." He offers a mechanic: your **second attempt after failing takes twice the emotional effort but half the actual effort**, because failing moves you from "unknown optimism to known pessimism" — you now have realistic expectations and can quantify what winning requires. His core reframe: "the best entrepreneurs in the world have the biggest failure resumes" — he doesn't want you to win, he wants you to **build your failure resume**. He lists his own losses as proof (money on his first two real estate deals, crypto, millions in bad hires, six failed businesses, nine failed partnerships) and stresses that you "only need to win once." He then reads the Jeff Bezos long-tail-distribution quote that opened *$100M Offers* (baseball caps outcomes at four runs; business can score 1,000 runs on one swing, so "big winners pay for so many experiments" — and they pay in failures/scars). He shares that he just found a 12-year-old note to himself about his old $10,000/month income goal, which choked him up, and closes: believe in yourself with all your heart because no one will believe for you, "you can never expect someone to invest in you more than you invest in yourself," and some people "say they'd kill for a shot… when in reality they won't even wake up 60 minutes earlier for it." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap ($100M scaling roadmap) promo.
