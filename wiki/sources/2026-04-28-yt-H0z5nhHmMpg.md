# Try Telling the Truth More

## Summary

Alex describes telling the truth in ordinary social situations as a practice. Instead of
inventing polite excuses, he says he will directly say he does not want to attend, help,
or pretend to understand something. He frames this as anchoring himself to truth, making
his words mean what they mean, and increasing the weight of genuine compliments. This is
also strong voice-training material because it shows his blunt delivery and values around
language.

## Key claims

- Alex tries to state facts and tell the truth even when social norms reward polite lies.
- He rejects relationships where someone would prefer that he lie to preserve comfort.
- Cutting false niceties makes compliments and praise carry more weight.
- He aspires to be a man whose words are understood as meant.
- Saying "I don't understand" can create short-term embarrassment but long-term clarity.
- He sees truth-telling as a way to avoid wasting time pretending or saying things he
  does not mean.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Try telling the truth in social settings."

> "Would you have preferred that I have lied to you?"

> "I aspire to be a man who when I say things people know that I mean them."

> "Make words mean what they mean again."
