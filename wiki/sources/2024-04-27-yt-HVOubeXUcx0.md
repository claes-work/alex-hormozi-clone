# How I Gained 7.8 Million Followers In 40 Months (6 Key Lessons)

## Summary

Solo presentation in which Alex Hormozi lays out six strategic shifts he made after
40 months, ~35,000 pieces of content, ~1,000 hours on camera, and ~$4M in team,
equipment and software — the changes that most grew his brand and drove business
outcomes. The through-line is that he is deliberately narrowing from broad
edutainment toward pure business education aimed at business owners, and measuring
success by ad revenue (RPM) rather than raw views. It is a distilled version of his
own content/audience-growth playbook, offered so the audience can "get the lessons
without the scars."

## Key claims

Self-reported growth stats over ~40 months (his figures, unverified):
- YouTube: 0 → 2.23M subscribers.
- Instagram: 7,000 → 2.5M followers.
- LinkedIn: 0 → 324,000 followers.
- TikTok: 0 → 856,000 followers.
- Twitter/X: 0 → 617,000.
- Facebook (recently started): 0 → 25,000.
- Podcast: 3,000 → 25M downloads.
- Email subscribers: 0 → 700,000.
- Totals: ~2 billion impressions, 7.8M new subscribers, ~1M copies of his book sold.
- Inputs cited: ~$4M spent on team/equipment/vendors/studios/software, ~1,000 hours on
  camera, ~35,000 pieces of content.

The six changes (his framework):
1. **Edutainment → Education.** Content produces entertainment or education as an
   outcome; he defines a piece by its most common outcome. Going all-in on education
   because (a) "all views are not created equal," (b) he likes making it, (c) he likes
   serving people who want to learn. Rejects the funnel theory that entertainment
   viewers convert into education viewers — claims data shows entertainment viewers
   want more entertainment and education viewers want more education; only the latter
   buy books, opt in, and apply to acquisition.com.
2. **For us → For you.** Make content for the audience (business owners), not for the
   media team. Five tactical changes: different packaging (vague → clear thumbnails
   and headlines), different introductions (confirming the thumbnail → **proof,
   promise, plan**; lead with proof/edification for education), different meat
   ("Razzle"/production → language, steps and stories — claims 75% of top videos
   emphasized language over production), different visuals (over-production →
   effective production; visual effects → visualizing data), and different pre-work
   (post-production → pre-search; a quarter of the old post time spent up front
   eliminates ~90–95% of editing, yielding ~4x output at higher quality).
3. **Wide → Narrow.** Drop relationships, college, food, lifestyle topics; double down
   on business — business models, business leverage, selling in a business. Viewers of
   a topic want more of that topic, so off-topic viewers aren't the business owners he
   serves.
4. **Views → Revenue.** Switch primary metric from views to **ad revenue / RPM**
   (views × revenue-per-view) as a fast, real-time, paired quantity+quality metric.
   High-RPM months also produced the most book sales, opt-ins, and applications,
   independent of view count. Cites a 90-day experiment: more edutainment/entertainment
   spiked views 2–3x but ad revenue dropped by half — "the wrong people."
5. **Shorts → Longs.** Rejects the shorts→longs→customer funnel; claims shorts viewers
   watch more shorts, long viewers watch more longs, customers buy more — different
   audiences. Longs drive more conversions (book sales, opt-ins, applications). Will
   keep shorts volume the same but skew toward business, emphasize longs overall. Notes
   cross-platform magic: found on TikTok (shorts) → watched on YouTube (longs).
6. **Assume more → Assume nothing.** Stop assuming viewers already know him. Tactically:
   introduce yourself every time, say why they should listen, fully explain inside
   jokes/references, and always mentally address a stranger. Counters retention fears;
   uses TV theme songs and The Simpsons' varying chalkboard intro as models for a
   consistent-but-varied intro that serves both cold and warm audiences.

Closing principles: "anything works better than nothing," "some things work better than
others," "nothing works forever." Business context stated: he and his wife exited their
first big company (Gym Launch + Prestige Labs) to American Pacific Group for $46.2M in
December 2021; portfolio companies now do over $200M/year; he does not directly monetize
the media. CTAs to Skool Games (school.com/games; claims 33.44% of people who finish
their first month make their first dollar online) and acquisition.com/training.
