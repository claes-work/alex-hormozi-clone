# `I Feel Stupid': Apartment Broker Stuck at $250K

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches an apartment broker whose revenue fell from a prior $2M year to roughly $250K amid a worse market and a still-founder-led sales motion. Alex separates market headwind from controllable bottleneck: the owner is still doing too much low-leverage production work, has not installed a strong enough hire, and has not created enough training/management leverage to climb out of the swamp.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A worse market can explain some decline, but it does not remove the founder's need to identify the next highest-leverage action.
- When the founder is in the swamp, the practical trade is not "delegate everything"; it is to trade about 40 hours of doing for a small amount of deliberate management time.
- If the bottleneck is sales capacity, the founder should hire or poach a legitimately strong broker/agent even if revenue dips during training.
- The recruiting pitch must answer why a productive broker would join: leads, brand, systems, training, faster learning, or some other concrete advantage.
- The founder should peel away low-value work and reserve the highest-value hours for the activity that most directly grows the company.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have a lot of stuff you don't need to do."
