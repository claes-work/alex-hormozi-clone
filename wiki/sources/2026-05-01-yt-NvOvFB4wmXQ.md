# What My Highlights Channel Taught Me About Views

## Summary

Alex explains that his highlights channel posts roughly 20 "mid" videos per day, run by
one person plus AI workflows. The channel's videos can have low view counts because they
serve narrow avatars, yet Alex says the channel has more attributed revenue to
Acquisition.com than his main channel. He uses that and Q1 main-channel data to argue
that business content should optimize for avatar value and revenue, not ego-driven view
counts.

## Key claims

- Alex says his highlights channel posts about 20 times per day with one operator and AI
  workflows.
- He calls the format "mids": more than a short and less than a long, often around four
  to eight minutes.
- Narrowly useful videos may have low views because the relevant avatar pool is small.
- Alex says the highlights channel has more attributed revenue to Acquisition.com than
  his main channel despite lower view counts.
- In Q1, Alex says none of the six most-viewed videos on his main channel were the
  highest revenue-producing videos.
- Business creators should care about value per second for the right avatar, not general
  entertainment-style reach.
- He says newer AI-driven recommendation and ad targeting make follower count and broad
  audience fatigue less relevant because platforms classify content and serve it to the
  most relevant people.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "That channel has more attributed revenue to ACQ than my main channel."

> "Our views are down should mean nothing to you."

> "If you are making more content and it's serving a specific avatar, then it will get shown to them and if it's good, they will buy."
