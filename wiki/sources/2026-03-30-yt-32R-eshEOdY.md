# Should I Build a Coaching Business or an Educational Platform?

## Summary
In this coaching-call clip, Alex Hormozi advises a caller who runs a business-coaching company for the wedding industry (roughly $1M run rate, targeting $5M in three years) and wants to build the "Ramsey Solutions of the wedding industry" — a multi-personality educational platform rather than a solo coaching business. Hormozi reframes the caller's assumed sales-conversion problem, distinguishes cold-traffic from warm/organic selling, and — because the caller intends to sell the business long term — recommends bringing in additional personalities, and specifically pitches building a team of AI avatars to deliver the niche coaching instead of hiring human coaches.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the caller is not sales-constrained; he sides with the caller's own sales team over the caller's self-diagnosis that low conversion is the core problem.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames moving from warm/organic/referral traffic to cold paid traffic as "an entirely different funnel" and a different game — you may only close ~1 in 10 cold leads, but that is fine "as long as the math makes sense" because cold volume vastly exceeds warm volume.
- As of March 2026, Alex says that if you want to sell the business long term, bringing in other personalities (beyond just the founder) is essentially the required path to build a saleable, non-founder-dependent platform.
- As of March 2026, Alex strongly recommends considering building out a team of AI avatars — a capability he notes "wasn't available 12 months ago" — instead of hiring focused human coaches for each niche, calling the technology "very good" now.
- As of March 2026, Alex's pitch for AI avatars is that you build the education/curriculum once and feed it into the avatars, yielding an entirely AI-driven business with staff that "won't cheat, won't sleep with each other," won't quit for a competitor.
- As of March 2026, Alex notes AI avatars can be built either from scratch or off of real people by training on them and licensing their likeness.
- Caller claim (unverified): the caller reports a 38% close rate and a wedding-industry coaching business at about a $1M run rate, having brought on a dedicated sales team in February.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "They don't know who you are. But as long as the math makes sense, who cares? Because there's way more of them than there are warm."

> "Well then if you do want to sell long term, then bringing in other kind of personalities, to your point, is pretty much the way that you're going to probably have to do it."

> "The really cool news that wasn't like available 12 months ago even is the AI avatars. And I think I would like really strongly consider just building out a team of AI avatars."

> "Now you have an entirely AI driven business with avatars that won't cheat, won't sleep with each other, won't go work for a competitor. Like all of that is just done."

> "You can train the avatars and just license their likeness if you want to. Or you can just do it from complete scratch. Either way it works."
