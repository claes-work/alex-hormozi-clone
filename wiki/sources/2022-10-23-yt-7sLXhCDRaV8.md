# 10 Different Ways To Break Down Making $1 Million

## Summary

Solo talk in which Hormozi enumerates ten ways to make $1M "for little or zero money down," attacking the myth that you need money to make money. The mechanisms scale to any amount ($100, $1M, $100M) by adjusting the number of zeros, and most rely on selling value, arbitrage, or acquiring/controlling assets rather than owning capital.

## Key claims

- You do not need money to make money; you make $1M by selling someone else $5–10M of value (e.g. 1% of a $5M conversion lift), and the constraint is skill, not capital. (paraphrase)
- Real estate is the archetype of "borrow to buy an asset, tenants pay it back"; his friend scaled from flipping houses to flipping 30–40 commercial buildings/year (e.g. buy at $10M, flip at $13M) with just himself and a couple of VAs. (paraphrase)
- Other zero-down mechanisms: refinance/seller-finance for more than you paid, buy-and-sell contracts/options, high-commission sales of expensive assets (skyscrapers, $20M homes), buying early from someone with an audience (NFT/pre-IPO dynamics), affiliate promotion, and acquiring businesses for no money down. (paraphrase)
- "Selling units" math: ~$20K/week × 52 ≈ $1M/year; a $100/mo membership held 10 months = $1,000 LTV, so 20 sales/week compounds to $1M. Grow only by selling more units or making the thing worth more. (paraphrase)
- People who make money don't constrain themselves by time (dollars per hour/year); they think in terms of how much value they can provide for a percentage of it. (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "You make a million dollars by selling someone else five to ten million dollars of value."
- "You can't say that you don't have the money and that's why you can't make money."
- "Real estate is you borrow money to buy an asset, you put tenants into the asset, they pay off the money that you never had."
- "Understand that the people who are making money don't constrain themselves by time. They're not thinking how do I make X dollars per hour, X dollars per year. They're thinking how do I provide the most value to somebody so they'll give me a percentage of the value I provide."
- "Arbitrage... just means that you sell the same thing between two different markets because they're priced differently... same thing, two markets, two different prices, you make the difference."
