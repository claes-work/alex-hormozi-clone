# How to Build a Community With Insanely High Engagement

## Summary

Alex advises a community for remote-working global adventurers to build engagement by
facilitating member-to-member connections, not just creator-to-member broadcast. He
recommends granular prompts around the everyday problems the audience shares, such as
workstations, visas, Airbnbs, Wi-Fi, and travel logistics.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-07)

- Alex says a "picture of your work desk" prompt would fit a global-adventurer
  community because members' workstations change.
- He recommends identifying the recurring problems unique to the community.
- He lists visas, Airbnbs, Wi-Fi, international connectivity, and Starlink as possible
  problem themes.
- He says the host should solve problems for multiple people at a time or create
  threads where members solve them for each other.
- He says community is stronger when it builds a network between members rather than
  only one-to-many lines from the creator.
- He compares an audience to a party where everyone only watches the host, which is not
  a good party.
- He says member-to-member reinforcement loops persist even if the host is less
  central.
- He recommends getting granular because everyday problem-solving is where community
  value appears.
