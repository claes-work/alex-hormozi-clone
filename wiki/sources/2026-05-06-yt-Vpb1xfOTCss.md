# The 2 Ways to Use a Community Inside an Existing Business

## Summary

Answering how he would approach Gym Launch with the Skool Games in mind, Alex explains
that a community can be the whole business, a long-term nurture vehicle, or part of
delivery for another business. For Gym Launch specifically, he says Skool is useful for
nurture and delivery, but trying to win the Skool Games would not be core because Gym
Launch is service-heavy and more like a white-label franchise in function, though not
legally a franchise.

## Key claims

- If community is the whole business, the model is straightforward: drive traffic to a
  page, sell access, and deliver inside the community.
- For an existing non-community business, the community can serve as long-term lead
  nurture, either free or low-ticket, to ascend people to higher service levels.
- A community can also be part of delivery, adding interaction with the company and other
  customers without replacing the actual service work.
- Gym Launch would not likely compete to win Skool Games because community is not the core
  business; it is a feature of nurture and delivery.
- He explicitly says Gym Launch should not be described as a franchise legally, although
  functionally it provides marketing, sales, creative, training, and vendor-discount
  support in a white-label-franchise-like way.
- Do not lose a bigger game to win a smaller game.
