# Helping an Accounting College in Brazil Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A Brazilian entrepreneur owns a certified accounting college serving accountants (bought the college; ~30,000 active students, ~100,000 total over 6 years, ~1.2 million social followers, ~$12M USD revenue this year — clarified from ~7M reals). His goal is to **sell the company**, and he's weighing whether to keep scaling education or move into AI/accounting software, since every Brazilian accountant needs a software system to work and he has ~1M+ accountants as a distribution base. Alex's core teaching is a **warning against the "add software to become sellable" fallacy**: software does not inherently make a business valuable — what makes it valuable are specific *economics* software can have when done right (viral growth, high gross margins, strong revenue retention). He explains **net negative churn / net revenue retention**: even losing 20 of 100 CRM customers, the remaining customers can spend enough more (upgrades, their business growing) that you retain >100% of revenue. Bolting software onto an education company does not earn a "software multiple" — only building a genuinely *exceptional* software product does, and Alex stresses "I have not seen anyone do it well… likelihood is low." If the client does build, he prescribes a **strong technical co-founder given a meaningful equity slug**, reserving equity for other key engineers. Alex then offers two lower-risk paths to the same goal: (1) a **continued-education / annual recertification** model — common in professional services (he cites attorney CE at ~$3,000/year via his general counsel), with monthly expert webinars on new laws and a Q&A forum — sold as a sticky renewable product without building software; and (2) instead of building software, **partner with the best existing accounting software in Brazil on a highly profitable deal**, collecting a sticky revenue stream tied into the continued education — "without having to build another company." Both client and Alex prefer option 2. Non-US context: Brazilian market, ~1M+ accountants, native-language distribution, reals-vs-USD revenue clarified. Ends with the roadmap promo (excluded from claims).

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues software does not inherently make a business valuable; value comes from economics software *can* have when done right — viral/growth, high gross margins (extra customers cost little at high profit), and strong revenue retention.
- As of March 2026, Alex describes **net negative churn**: if customers get fully integrated on a CRM they tend to stay long, and even keeping only 80 of 100 customers you can retain >100% of revenue because the remainder upgrade and grow.
- As of March 2026, Alex warns that simply adding software to an education business will not make it a software company, earn a software multiple, or make it more sellable — only building genuinely exceptional software does.
- As of March 2026, Alex says building the software well is hard: "I have not seen anyone do it well," so likelihood of success is low, even though the plan is sound.
- As of March 2026, if building software Alex prescribes a strong technical co-founder given a meaningful ("slug") equity stake, while reserving equity for other key engineers to be hired.
- As of March 2026, Alex offers a software-free path to sellability: a **continued-education / annual recertification** product — common in professional services (he cites ~$3,000/year attorney CE), delivered via monthly expert webinars on new laws plus a Q&A forum, at roughly $1,000–$3,000/year.
- As of March 2026, Alex's preferred second idea: rather than build software, **partner with the best existing accounting software in Brazil** on a deal that is very profitable for the client, collecting a sticky revenue stream tied into continued education — avoiding building another company.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the client's built-in accountant distribution as the key asset: with distribution baked in, a software partnership becomes mainly a negotiation ("you just need to make this not suck").
