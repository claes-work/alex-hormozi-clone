# If I Wanted to Get Rich and Famous, This Is What I'd Do

## Summary

Solo long-form video in which Hormozi reverse-engineers the personal brands of five
mega-influencers — Taylor Swift, Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson, MrBeast, Joe Rogan, and
Kylie Jenner — and extracts the principles he applied to build his own brand. He closes
with a Munger-style inverted-thinking section (how to *guarantee* you never build a
brand, then flip it) and a market-sizing case for why the creator economy is worth
entering. Core throughline: longevity plus staying true to yourself, monetizing only in
ways that reinforce who you already are.

## Key claims (the rich + famous playbook)

**From Taylor Swift:**
- **Longevity / compounding.** Everyone underrates time. Posting once/week for 10 years
  = 520 videos vs. 52 in one year — the reps compound and each old video keeps pulling
  people in. Don't compare your year 1 to someone's year 10.
- **Scale the unscalable.** She crashes fans' weddings; he can't, so he does sporadic
  Q&A lives and gives away all his books/courses/content free — put energy into one
  thing, then distribute to everyone.
- **Vulnerability.** Showing flaws (losing all his money twice, whiffed sales, stolen
  money, shutting 8 of 9 businesses) makes a multimillion-dollar life relatable and
  builds a fan base that defends you because they share the experience.
- **Ownership.** She fought to own her music; he refuses publisher advances so he can
  own his books forever and give them away as he sees fit. Conditions change — don't
  sign away long-term rights for a short-term check.
- **Shifting identity.** Her brand evolved (country → heartbreak → powerful woman).
  Let your brand change as you change rather than becoming a fraud to a fixed image.

**From The Rock:**
- **Longevity** (started WWE 1996) and **work ethic** (shows up early, never misses a
  workout). Hormozi's version: work 4am–10am on the thing he wants before doing the day
  job; currently writes/edits books ~6 hours/day.
- **Expanding niches.** Rock went football → WWE → physique-showcase movies → Disney
  (broadest base). Hormozi went in-shape guy → training clients → gym owner → gym
  licensing → acquisitions; each transition was *forced* by someone handing him money.
- **Monetize tastefully.** Monetize in ways that reinforce what people already love
  about you (Rock + tequila made sense). A luxury watch or suit line would be off-brand
  for Hormozi even if profitable. Reputation can't be rebuilt like bankruptcy — if
  forced to choose between reputation and money, choose reputation.

**From MrBeast:**
- **Longevity** — 11 years in; shrink the gap between when you *start* and when you
  *get serious*.
- **Make the clickbait true** — make the insane claim, then back it with an even more
  insane video. Reframed Hormozi's whole approach to marketing.
- **Doubling down** — reinvest everything back into the compounding vehicle; be willing
  to burn the boats and make big bets others don't understand.
- **Packaging** — thumbnail must match the first 5 seconds; they split-test thumbnails
  measuring both click-rate and watch-time; timely/trending framing (LVMH becoming
  richest man) dramatically widened reach of a luxury-goods pricing video.
- **Resourcefulness** — copy what he did when he had nothing (counting to 100k cost
  only imagination), not what he does now. Use what you've got; learn resourcefulness
  while you have little.

**From Joe Rogan:**
- **Longevity** (stand-up since 1988) and willingness to **restart at zero** and suck
  publicly (stand-up → Fear Factor → UFC commentary). Hormozi's biggest humble pie:
  going from white-collar consultant Harvard-bound to minimum-wage frontline trainer —
  a "season of learning, not earning."
- **You are the niche.** A personal brand should equal the whole person — the unique
  *thumbprint* is the mix and proportion of topics (Rogan = biohacking + aliens +
  politics + fighting + comedy). Speak to what you know; teach, don't preach.
- **Only promote what you believe in** — sponsorships that require zero change in
  behavior; limit your "brand bullets" to the 2–5 things you actually care about.
- **No hacks** — Rogan is anti-algorithm ("post and ghost"). Align with what the
  platform wants (engaging content people find interesting) instead of exploiting a
  flaw that will get patched. You are interesting because you are interested.

**From Kylie Jenner:**
- **Longevity** (on TV 20 years), **leverage** (famous family, media, scalable
  products — a lip kit scales, a tax service wouldn't), **outsource weaknesses** (owns
  product + marketing, gives away pie on logistics/manufacturing — better to own 20% of
  a $20B company than 100% of a $1M one; cites Charlie Munger on not chiseling), plus
  **embracing controversy** (which Hormozi admits he mostly doesn't), **adaptability**
  across platforms, and **accessibility** (showing the unglamorous behind-the-scenes).

**Why enter (market case) + inverted playbook:**
- These people are billionaires because **direct distribution** cut out middlemen
  (studios/theaters), letting creators capture the economics. Creator economy projected
  ~$500B/year by 2027 (Goldman Sachs); the real hidden value of an audience is *access*
  to other people.
- Inverted (Munger): to guarantee no brand — talk about what you don't know, be
  inconsistent/quit, sell too early, sell the wrong stuff, associate with the wrong
  crowd, wait for perfect conditions, and never change. Flip all seven to build one.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "Don't compare your first year to someone's 10th year."
- "You have to work double time. You got to work the job that you have today, but in the mornings or the late evenings you build for the job you want to have tomorrow."
- "You can file bankruptcy when you go way too far into debt and then you can start over, but you can't file bankruptcy on a reputation. Once it's ruined, it's ruined for life."

- "One is preaching, one is teaching, and you want to be the teacher, not the preacher."
- "He is interesting because he is interested."
- "I'd rather own 20% of a $20 billion company than 100% of a million dollar company."
- "I will be so much more proud of the failures that I risked than the successes that I didn't."
