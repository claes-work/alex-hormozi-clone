# Having a Clue is Overrated - The #1 Lie That Holds You Back

## Summary

Alex says being able to say "I don't know" is a green flag for intellect. He argues that
school and social punishment train people to hide ignorance, but that basic lie prevents
truth and learning. Not knowing is powerful because it makes learning the obvious next
directive and allows people to decide which things are not worth learning.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says having a clue is overrated.
- He says a green flag for intellect is the ability to say "I don't know."
- He says school can punish not knowing, training people to avoid admitting ignorance.
- He frames pretending to know as a basic lie that trains people not to say what they
  actually think.
- He says admitting ignorance is one of the easiest ways to start telling the truth.
- He compares trust in "no" with trust in "yes": if someone cannot be trusted to say no,
  they cannot be trusted to say yes.
- He says not knowing creates a clear directive to learn.
- He defines learning as changing behavior under the same conditions.
- He says admitting not knowing also lets someone decide many things are not worth
  learning because they will not affect life.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "One of my green flags for intellect is someone being able to say that they don't know."
