# A BILLIONAIRE taught me how to think like a BILLIONAIRE

## Summary
Solo talking-head video in which Hormozi shares mental-model "thinking frameworks" he says have served him well. Despite the title, no billionaire is named or quoted as a teacher; the video delivers two of a promised four frameworks: (1) distinguishing a dichotomy to be managed from a problem to be solved, and (2) distinguishing a continuum ("to what extent") from a psychological binary (yes/no). He credits the mental-models approach to Charlie Munger and the continuum idea to his biochemist friend Dr. Cashey.

## Key claims
- (2021) Framework 1: many recurring tensions are dichotomies to be managed, not problems to be solved — e.g. micromanagement vs. abdication (delegation), fairness vs. equality in a tax code, justice vs. mercy, variety vs. consistency in a marriage. Both poles are valid ideals in permanent tension.
- (2021) His diagnostic habit: when someone floods him with context, he pauses them with "what problem are we solving?" — and sometimes the prior question is "is that even a problem?" Many "problems" are just the ordinary course of doing business to be improved over time, not existential threats.
- (2021) People mis-prioritize issues by perceiving them as threats to existence when they are things that can be continuously improved through systems and process.
- (2021) Framework 2: reality exists on continuums, but the brain stores binary shorthand (on/off my diet, it works / it doesn't) for easy decision-making; stacking binary placeholders makes decisions progressively less accurate.
- (2021) Thinking in "to what extent" is harder but more accurate — e.g. not "am I diabetic" but "how diabetic am I"; not "Facebook doesn't work" but "to what extent has it not worked / where is the fall-off."
- (2021) The continuum frame also relieves the moral self-judgment of binary thinking (being "off" the diet → "might as well eat 19 pizzas"); "I was 10 over yesterday so I can go 10 under today."
- (2021) Precise language yields precise thinking: "if you ask stupid questions you get stupid answers; if you play stupid games you win stupid prizes."
