# How to Get More Leads in a Local Service Business

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches an outdoor-lighting business with seasonal Christmas-light revenue.
He says the owner can either stay full-time seasonal lighting and sell year-round, or add
complementary off-season services. For permanent lighting leads, he suspects the creative
and offer are weak, and suggests a stronger front-end lead magnet such as installing a
small element for free or at break-even to create sales conversations.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A seasonal lighting business has an existential choice: stay focused on lighting or add
  complementary home services for off-season utilization.
- Alex prefers keeping a simple holiday-season business and selling all year if the
  economics work.
- Selling all year and delivering in a short season can create a strong cash-flow model.
- If the business has lower-skilled people who need year-round work, complementary
  services such as lawn care may make sense.
- For permanent lighting, Alex suspects the ad creative may be the problem because the
  product is visual.
- Before-and-after dark-to-lit house creative should be straightforward to sell.
- Local markets are offer-driven.
- A free demo may feel like a free sales pitch rather than a compelling lead magnet.
- A lead magnet can be a trial of the solution or one step of a multi-step process.
- Alex prefers starting with a strong offer and adding friction later.
- The business can qualify houses by value or other criteria before giving away the
  strongest offer.
- The goal is to get the prospect to say they want lighting and provide contact
  information; the sales process handles the rest.
- A possible offer is free install on a small lighting section while the customer covers
  the lights, with margin embedded in the product.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I just need them to say they want lighting."
