# Stop Explaining Why You Suck

## Summary

Alex rejects identity labels and causal stories attached to behavior. Because the true
cause is unknowable and the past cannot change, he recommends stating only the observed
behavior, then rewarding a different behavior in the present. He describes this as the
third stage of his own thinking after first blaming trauma and then rewriting its story.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex is highly skeptical of "I am" identity labels followed by a
  "because" explanation.
- Causal stories grant control to an unchangeable past while the present behavior itself
  remains the only observable fact.
- He recommends describing past behavior without an identity or speculative cause, then
  identifying and reinforcing the desired present action.
- Different plausible causes can be attached to the same behavior, but none necessarily
  helps change it.
- Alex says potential and trauma can become ego protection for a lack of results.
- He self-reports three stages: attributing behavior to trauma, rewriting the trauma
  story, and finally treating the causal story as irrelevant to present action.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "All we know is that you do a certain type of behavior. That's it."

> "I behave this way, period."

> "The because statement in the second half is completely irrelevant."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: three-stage evolution from trauma attribution to causal-story irrelevance.
