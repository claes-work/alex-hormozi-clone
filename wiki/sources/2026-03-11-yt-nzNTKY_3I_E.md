# How to Think About Competitors Who Copy You

## Summary
Solo talk on how to handle competitors who copy you. Alex's central argument: copycats can duplicate almost every surface artifact — your promises, your services, your logo, your name — but they **cannot copy your proof**, i.e. what you've actually done. Since people building brands ultimately want reputation, status, and to be *known* for things, the durable moat is a track record, not the copyable trappings. He extends this into advice for young entrepreneurs who want to build a brand through content: only make content if you're **truly documenting** real work, and don't slip into "teacher mode," because it's the stuff you *do* that builds the brand. His evidence is the business titans (Elon, Bezos, Sam Altman, Jensen Huang, Steve Jobs, Bill Gates, Warren Buffett) — they didn't get there by building personal brands, they built big businesses, and the brand followed by extension once people wanted to know who was behind them. They "built their credibility before they built their likeability." He frames the general error as putting the cart before the horse — wanting "to eat the pudding before they have the proof," when in the real world **proof comes before the pudding**. In a world where anyone can say anything and get instant reach, **trust is the scarce commodity**, so a big brand must index hard on trust — and you earn trust by having done what you say. MrBeast is his content example: his videos *worked as proof* (doing something no one else was willing to do, like counting to a million) rather than claims of expertise — but that still requires epic amounts of work. He closes with his "overnight success" model: the public only sees the viral *moment* (the output), not the lifetime of inputs behind it; people mistakenly model **what they see, not what occurred** — copying what successful people do *after* they get rich (the plateau) rather than what they did to get there (the rise). "You don't get rich by flying on private jets. You get rich, then you can fly on private jets." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
