# "How Do I Attract Serious Money?"

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Alexis Morgan, who runs a real estate lending business (both a fund and syndications) at $3.1M in revenue with a goal of $10M. Her problem: her education-heavy marketing attracts leads who want to buy an education product from her rather than invest passively in the fund.

Alex diagnoses it immediately as a branding and messaging problem, not a lead-generation problem. From the way she introduced herself, he assumed she sold an "investment opportunity education thing" — her language "sounded less legit," so she attracts less legit people. His prescription: talk like a real fund manager — fund one, fund two, expected returns, pro formas — and level up the language, look, and feel so the marketing attracts a higher-level person who writes bigger checks. He tells her to stop selling education (free educational content is fine) and to lean fully into the legitimate fund she already has, framing it as a delayed-gratification move: sacrifice education revenue today for a bigger fund tomorrow. Tactically, he advises auditing her site, Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube against what other fund managers publish, warns that investment marketing carries heavy compliance scrutiny, and strongly recommends adding X and LinkedIn — "money Twitter is packed" — as the goldmine channels for accredited investors. The video closes with his standard $100M scaling roadmap CTA (acquisition.com/roadmap).
