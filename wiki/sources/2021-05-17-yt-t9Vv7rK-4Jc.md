# I Broke Down Every High Margin Service Into 6 Buckets (2021-05-17)

## Summary

Hormozi (citing $120M in sales across four companies over four years) presents his **service
delivery cube** — a six-dimension framework for repackaging an existing service into more
valuable offerings using excess capacity ("sawdust") without adding cost. He argues you make
customers more valuable by moving any current service one notch left or right on each of the
six axes, unearthing hidden value. Applies to service/consulting businesses, not physical
products.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- Making more money isn't only acquisition — the other half is making existing customers more
  valuable, largely by delivering more valuable services.
- The **service delivery cube** has six components; you evaluate any current or potential
  offering against all six and ask "can I move it one to the left or one to the right?"
- Core idea: monetize excess capacity / "sawdust" (like a lumber mill selling sawdust as
  sandpaper or stove pellets) — repackage what you already have, at little to no added cost.
- On the effort axis: the easier something is to fulfill, the harder it is to sell.
  Do-it-yourself is hardest to sell (lowest value, all effort on the client); done-for-you is
  easiest to sell but most operationally complex. Billion-dollar companies exist at each level.
- "Fast beats free" — speed alone is something people pay for (Uber vs. walking, FedEx
  overnight vs. postal, Spotify vs. free-but-painful downloads, Amazon Prime two-day shipping).
- Simple lever: take any service category and try to deliver the result in half the time; add
  tiered speed (e.g. normal 24-hr support vs. VIP 60-minute support) from the same resources.
- Goal of the exercise: find hidden value / excess capacity to drive more revenue and profit
  without new expenses or liability.

## The 6 buckets (service delivery cube)

1. **Ratio** — client-to-service ratio: one-on-one, small group, one-to-many, or one-to-unlimited.
2. **Effort / involvement level** — do-it-yourself, done-with-you, or done-for-you.
3. **Support level (channels)** — how clients reach you and your team: text, chat, email,
   expert representative, phone, Zoom (and tiering by channel).
4. **Consumption** — how they consume the information: live vs. recorded, and written, audio,
   or video.
5. **Speed & convenience** — how fast / how convenient ("fast beats free"; cut time to result,
   speed up support, VIP tiers).
6. **10x to 1/10th** — a creative exercise: if you charged 10x, what would you add? If you had
   to deliver at 1/10th the price (while adding value), how would you do it?
