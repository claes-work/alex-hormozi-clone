# Watch this if you're working hard, but not making progress

## Summary

Alex uses a caller with a salon, barber shop, Airbnb, and a new ring product for single
people to explain focus and resource concentration. He says the ring concept would
require changing the perception of a population, large capital, repeat monetization, and
word-of-mouth spread. Given the caller's limited resources and existing salon cash flow,
he recommends focusing on the salon or, if pursuing the ring, shrinking scope to a tiny
market such as a few colleges and iterating until it spreads virally.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex says the caller is pursuing too many enterprises with too few resources.
- He says even with his current resources, he would not want to pursue three things at
  once.
- He says selling a one-time physical product for $129 is difficult because it lacks
  repeat monetization.
- He says a product like a drink has a clearer repeat-purchase path than a one-time ring.
- He questions whether people want to publicly announce being single through a ring.
- He recommends focusing on the existing salon and asking how to grow it to $4 million
  per year.
- He says if the caller insists on the ring, she should dramatically narrow scope to
  saturate a tiny market such as three to five colleges.
- He says the ring would need a viral coefficient because the caller lacks enough money
  to buy attention at scale.
- He says the product should be fixed until it grows by word of mouth unless the founder
  raises a large amount of money.
