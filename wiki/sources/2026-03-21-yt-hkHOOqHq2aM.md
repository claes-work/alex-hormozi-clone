# Stop Calling a Payment Plan a Membership

## Summary
In a live Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a Serbian mastermind operator (2.4M revenue, 1.2M profit, wanting to reach 5M profit) on how to add continuity and raise LTV. Hormozi's central distinction: billing recurringly for a one-time deliverable is "calling a payment plan a membership," and it produces false churn anxiety. He recommends building a light-touch back-end alumni network (community plus a few in-person meetups a year) priced at about a third of the core program, rather than raising front-end prices or building a 4x-priced upsell the team can't deliver on.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames continuity around two categories: **consumable** things (used monthly) versus **one-time** deliverables — and says the big mistake is billing recurringly for a one-time deliverable, which is effectively "calling a payment plan a membership."
- As of March 2026, Alex argues this mislabeling is what drives founders' churn anxiety: once a customer finishes paying for a one-time thing, they got the thing — that is not churn, any more than a paid-off car loan is lost LTV.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends a **light-touch back-end alumni network** — community access plus two or three in-person meetups a year — priced at roughly a third of the core program, and suggests the Q&A component can be dropped since the meetups carry most of the value.
- As of March 2026, Alex says he optimizes for **gross margin and revenue retention**: he prefers selling more of a high-margin offer (e.g. a $10K product costing $100 to deliver) over building a $100K product costing $30K to deliver, even though the latter nets more per unit.
- As of March 2026, Alex advised against raising front-end prices when close rate is already ~20% (calls it "at KPI"), recommending instead increasing event frequency from twice a year to quarterly — unless the goal is deliberately to serve fewer customers or supply is constrained.
- As of March 2026, Alex advised against a 4x-priced upsell here: you could sell it, but likely couldn't deliver on it without a robust team, and under-delivery at 4x price makes customers "hate you." He prefers a "downsell the upsell" into a cheap, high-margin alumni tier.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The big mistake that a lot of business owners make is that they will try and bill on a recurring basis for something that is a one-time deliverable, right? Which is fundamentally calling a payment plan a membership."

> "It's like a car payment person being like, 'I have five-year LTV on my car buyers.' It's like, 'No, they bought a car and they financed it.' Like, calm down."

> "I look at gross margins and I look at revenue retention. If I can find something that people will just continue to pay for and it doesn't cost me a lot and it makes me a good amount of money, I'm just like, 'Cool.'"

> "If I could sell something that cost $10,000 to a customer that cost me $100 and everyone keeps buying it, I don't necessarily want to build a $100,000 thing. I just want to sell more of that."

> "If you're going to charge four times as much money, you better have a really robust team that's going to deliver that value and if you don't, you're going to have people hate you."

> "I want to get you into the alumni network which is a third of the price and you get to be only with the ballers."
