# You Need To Focus

## Summary
In a short live consulting exchange, Alex Hormozi advises a business owner (Preston) who runs a marketing agency (~$450K top line, $200K EBITDA) while his wife runs a real estate law firm (~$3.5M revenue, ~$500–750K bottom line) that is also a client of the agency. Hormozi's core recommendation is to focus: wind down the smaller agency and redirect its 30–35 weekly hours into the far larger, faster-growing law firm, whose constraint is simply getting more clients ("butts in seats"). He then walks through a clean, reputation-preserving way to shut down or offload the agency's book of business.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that redirecting the 30–35 hours/week spent on a business earning ~$200K into a business already making roughly seven times as much is the higher-leverage move, and that doing so would likely more than recover the $200K.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the choice as a focus problem: the caller is spreading effort across two businesses when the constraint on the bigger one (law firm) is straightforward — more client acquisition.
- As of March 2026, Alex's recommended shut-down method is to give clients ample notice (~6 months), stop accepting new customers, maintain service levels through the wind-down, and refer existing clients to 2–3 trusted providers.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims the owner can negotiate referral kickbacks from those trusted providers and could plausibly earn ~$100K/year of nearly 100% passive income just from referring out the existing book of business.
- As of March 2026, Alex endorses the alternative of hiring an operator to run the marketing company as a "nice" option if the owner does not want to shut it down.
- Caller-reported (unverified): the agency does ~$450K top line / $200K EBITDA; the law firm did $2.5M last year, is on pace for $3.5M, with a bottom line around $500K (possibly ~$750K this year); the law firm's constraint is getting more clients.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "If you took 30 to 35 hours a week and put it towards the thing that's already making, you know, seven times as much money, do you think you'd be able to find a way to get the extra 200?"

> "I just give people ample time. So if I think that something's going to disrupt someone's business, you know, for your own reputation, I'd say, 'Listen, it's been an amazing time.'"

> "Number one is we stopped accepting new customers. And number two, we're going to be phasing this out over the next 6 months. And so I wanted to give you guys 6 months. We'll continue levels of service for this period of time."

> "You'll probably be able to make 100 grand a year just from referring the business out to those guys, which they'll happily do."

> "That's a very easy way for you to keep the money, get it to 100% passive, and then move on."
