# How to Hire the Right Leadership Team as You Scale

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a large skincare direct-response business doing about $4.5M/month that has solved lower-level sales-team hiring but now lacks the leadership layer. Alex says the company has built the bottom layer but is missing top-layer leaders who remove founders from day-to-day fires. He recommends separating one-time executive hires from recurring hires, using founder-led outreach for sales/marketing leaders where the founders have pattern recognition, using headhunters or outside experts for technical roles, and attacking the most on-fire role first rather than hiring every leader at once.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - skincare direct-response founder/operator. His revenue, sales-team manpower issue, leadership needs, compensation ranges, and recruiter-cost concerns are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The company has built the bottom operating layer but is missing the top leadership layer.
- One-time senior leadership hires require a different approach than recurring frontline hires.
- Founders with sales and marketing skill may be able to source and evaluate sales/marketing leaders through their network and founder-led outreach.
- Technical roles like finance, tech/data, and people/HR may justify outside search firms or outside expert vetting.
- For C-suite hires, founders should be involved in the process.
- CEO outreach gets higher response rates than generic recruiter outreach.
- A useful headhunt message asks whether the recipient knows anyone like themselves for a great role, which creates a low-pressure opening.
- The company should recruit one constraint role at a time, starting with the most on-fire function.
- Hiring fees should be viewed as investments if the hire creates far more value than the fee.
- Scaling keeps creating new problems; onboarding senior people is work, not a reason to avoid the hire.
