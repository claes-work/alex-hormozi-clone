# This Lesson Cost me $5,000,000...

## Summary
In this short clip, Hormozi recounts a pricing mistake that he estimates cost him $5 million. Roughly two years earlier, his employees complained that the offer was too expensive and blamed price for ~15% month-over-month churn, so he dropped the price from $800/week to $600/week and simultaneously added coaching services — "more for less." The result: existing clients complained they'd paid more, he cut $400/month in profit, and churn stayed exactly the same. His takeaway is that a decrease in price never produces a corresponding increase in sales (or retention).

## Key claims
- Lowering price does not produce a corresponding increase in sales — the two do not equate. (2021-01-01)
- ~Two years before filming, his team blamed a too-high price for 15% month-over-month churn and pushed for a price cut. (relayed 2021-01-01; events ~2018-2019)
- He dropped the price from $800/week to $600/week and stacked additional coaching services on top ("give more for less"), announced as a big discount + over-delivery. (2021-01-01)
- Outcomes: existing clients were upset they'd paid more; profit fell ~$400/month; churn remained unchanged. (2021-01-01)
- He estimates that this decision — driven by the complaining employees — ultimately cost him ~$5 million. (2021-01-01)
