# There Are 2 Ways to Learn Something

## Summary
Solo clip on the two distinct kinds of learning and why naming which one you're after makes you learn it faster. Alex splits learning into **declarative knowledge** ("knowledge of existence" — learning *about* something that exists you didn't know existed) and **procedural knowledge** ("knowing how to do it"). Declarative knowledge is a prerequisite for procedural: you can't learn to do a thing you don't know exists. He frames this as "paying down ignorance" — before you invest (time, trade, or money) you should be clear on which of the two you're buying. High-level rooms and networks mostly sell **declarative** knowledge: they expose you to tax, investment, marketing, sales strategies and business models you'd never heard of — the "unknown unknowns," which is why sitting with people making 10–100x more can be a breakthrough (they see the world differently). **How-to** learning is more predictable: you already know the skill exists and just need the steps clarified. He illustrates with his own story: in 2013 he learned that Facebook ads *existed* (declarative) and taught himself just enough to make them work in one weekend (procedural), then rode that skill for years. When he needed national-scale ads for Gym Launch and had exhausted his relational capital, agencies quoted ~$5,000/month he couldn't afford, so he talked one operator into $7.50/hour. He prepped for those calls "like it was Harvard on steroids" — notes out, recorded, two people watching so he'd miss nothing — and ran his **document → demonstrate → duplicate** method: had the expert document his ad process as if he were Alex, demonstrate it click-by-click while explaining each step, then Alex duplicated it in front of him until he did it right multiple times unaided. Eight sessions (eight hours × $7.50) to master the skill. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, there are two types of things you can learn: **declarative knowledge** (knowledge that something exists) and **procedural knowledge** (knowing how to do it).
- As of March 2026, declarative knowledge is a prerequisite for procedural knowledge — you cannot learn to do something you don't yet know exists.
- As of March 2026, learning is "paying down ignorance"; before any investment of time, trade, or money you should know which of the two kinds of knowledge you're buying.

- As of March 2026, how-to (procedural) learning is more predictable in what you get, because you already know the skill exists and only need the steps clarified.
- As of March 2026, simply delineating which kind of knowledge you're trying to learn helps you actually learn it.
- As of March 2026 (biography): his first declarative-knowledge moment was learning Facebook ads existed in 2013; he then learned how they worked and enough to make them work in a single weekend, and rode that skill for a couple of years.
