# How I find MILLION DOLLAR opportunities...

## Summary

Alex explains strategy as choosing the right opportunity vehicle. He contrasts generic persistence with the harder judgment of when to change vehicles, then gives a three-part filter for opportunities: total possible units sold, profit per unit at scale, and supply-demand dynamics in the market.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2021-11-29)

- Alex says changes in opportunity vehicle added roughly a zero per month to his income across stages.
- He adapts Stephen Schwarzman's opportunity filter into a smaller-operator lens: market size, profit per unit, and favorable supply-demand economics.
- He says total addressable market alone is not enough if entrenched supply makes the market hard to enter.
- He says short-term niching can create a favorable supply-demand wedge inside a larger market.
- He warns small business owners not to confuse persistence with staying in the wrong vehicle forever.
- He says changing one's mind when better data arrives is a marker of intelligence.
- He says focus still matters: do not start building two bridges at once; decide in time whether to cut bait.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Strategy is one of the most amorphous terms."

> "The single hardest decision as an entrepreneur... is understanding when to quit."

> "If you are unable to change your mind... you will not be better."
