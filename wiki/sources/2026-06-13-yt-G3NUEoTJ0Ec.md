# I'm the bottleneck in my $1.6M practice

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a functional-medicine practitioner doing about $1.6M who is trying to hand clients to another practitioner and move into paid acquisition. Alex says clients resisting the transition is a feature of being good, but keeping all fulfillment personally means staying stuck forever. He warns that moving from warm word-of-mouth to cold paid ads requires a real cold-lead sales motion, not the same warm consult process, and recommends a partial service-delivery-ratio shift before handing off 100% of care.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - functional-medicine practitioner. Her revenue, telehealth model, client-transition issue, margins, ads/webinar plans, and avatar details are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If clients love the founder because she is good, they will be upset when they cannot get her.
- Losing some revenue during delegation may be a short-term cost, while not delegating keeps the founder stuck forever.
- The founder needs confidence that customer acquisition can refill the practice without her personally fulfilling.
- Warm-lead sales processes fail when applied to cold leads.
- A cold acquisition process can still convert warm leads, but a warm process will not reliably convert cold leads.
- Paid traffic likely needs more friction, such as an application, VSL, webinar, or other warming steps before a consult.
- Switching clients 100% to a new practitioner can risk churn, margin compression, and even practitioner dependency.
- A safer transition may increase leverage by reducing founder touch frequency while keeping the founder visibly in charge.
- The new practitioner can handle components while the founder remains the perceived authority and internal reviewer.
