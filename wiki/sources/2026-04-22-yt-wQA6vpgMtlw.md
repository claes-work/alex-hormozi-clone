# How to Sell Without Competing on Price

## Summary

Alex coaches a product seller to dispensaries to reframe the offer away from selling
SKUs and toward bringing more dollars through the retailer's door. He recommends using a
competitor-based curiosity opener and proving that the product acts as lead generation
for the store, so the price premium becomes a marketing budget rather than a product
cost.

## Key claims

- Alex says the seller should frame the product as creating customer traffic for the
  retailer, not merely as inventory.
- He recommends an outbound opener that references a nearby competitor to create
  curiosity and a callback.
- He says the sales pitch needs proof of how many customers come in for the product and
  what else those customers buy.
- He says the product premium can be framed as the store's customer-acquisition budget.
- He says the product should make the retailer feel that the seller is generating leads
  for the store, rather than the store generating leads for the product.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You want to be lead gen for them."

> "The premium that I charge is your marketing budget."

> "Name their competitor."
