# The 4 Ways Fortunes Are Made (and how I stumbled into one)

Source id: `yt-ZfaZYFx89UU` · Published 2021-06-11 · Channel [[../entities/channel-alexhormozi]] · Speaker: Alex Hormozi (sole speaker)

## Summary

Hormozi lays out his framework distinguishing money (a medium for transferring wealth)
from wealth (assets that earn while you sleep), then argues fortunes over history have
been built through four types of leverage: labor, capital, code, and media. He frames
labor and capital as older, permission-based leverage and code and media as newer,
permissionless leverage, and recounts that he "accidentally stumbled into" the media type
when he shifted his own company from a done-for-you service to a licensing/media model.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- (2021) Money is not valuable in itself; it is simply a medium for transferring wealth
  and acquiring assets. Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep — no time traded.
- (2021) Framing he attributes to Taylor Welsh: poor people do the wrong things, rich
  people do the right things, wealthy people own the right things. *(attribution: Alex
  citing a mentor)*
- (2021) Lawyers, doctors and accountants can't get super wealthy while trading time for
  dollars; they must transition from practitioner to owner of a practice (an asset).
- (2021) Fortunes over 500,000+ years have been built through four types of leverage:
  1. **Labor** — the oldest; buying other people's time (pyramids, kings/queens,
     Rockefeller's oil). Hardest leverage to actually employ. Permission-based.
  2. **Capital** — other people's money; financing. The Buffetts, Mungers, Carl Icahns.
     Permission-based.
  3. **Code** — software; zero incremental cost per additional user. Permissionless.
  4. **Media** — content/eyeballs sold at ~100% gross margin, zero incremental cost.
     Permissionless.
- (2021) Labor and capital require others' permission; code and media do not — and the
  newest fortunes are permissionless.
- (2021) He stumbled into media by switching his company from selling other people's time
  (a done-for-you service) to a media/licensing model, which sent margins "through the
  roof."
- (2021) Combining two or three leverage types juices returns — "winner takes all" games
  use other people's money to get other people's labor to write code sold as media.
- (2021) Now is the best time to be alive to build a fortune: the ways to build one have
  increased and the permission required has decreased.

## Biographical note

Self-reported figures (unverified): At age 27, Hormozi says he sold all of his gyms, put
all his money into a new venture, and lost it, leaving him with **$1,036**. He states that
two years later he had **over $17 million** in the bank, attributing the turnaround to
stumbling into the media form of leverage — pivoting his company from flying out a sales
team to fill brick-and-mortar locations (a done-for-you service) to **licensing media**.
This maps to the Gym Launch → licensing transition. Treat all dollar figures as
self-reported.
