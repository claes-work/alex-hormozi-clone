# If You're at Zero, Here's What You Need

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says people at zero think they need a tactic, but often need courage to be
wrong, fail publicly, and tolerate shame around people whose opinions matter. He gives a
detailed account of leaving his consulting path, disappointing his father, driving away
before calling him, and accepting that his father's dream had to die for his own to live.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Beginners often think they need a tactic to start a business, but may actually need
  courage to be wrong and fail in front of people whose opinions they care about.
- The harder it is to break out of a mental prison, the more compelling the story can be
  after the breakthrough.
- Alex says he was not a childhood entrepreneur; he was good at school, worked jobs, and
  had a clear white-collar path.
- He had a GMAT score above Harvard's minimum score, according to him.
- He expected the conventional path to be top business school, consulting or investment
  banking, and eventually private equity.
- Looking at people 20 years ahead on that path made him realize he did not want their
  lives.
- He says his major life decisions have often come near the doorstep of a parent death.
- At about 22 as a consultant, he owned a paid-for condo overlooking the city and often
  hoped he would not wake up.
- His father was most proud of him when he was following the conventional plan.
- Alex realized that living out his father's dream made him feel like he did not want to
  be alive.
- He crystallized the line that his father's dream had to die for his own to live.
- He left the state and drove halfway across the country before calling his father to say
  he was gone.
- He took a minimum-wage personal-training job to learn the gym business.
- His relationship with his father struggled for years afterward.
- Alex says he had to accept the possibility that his father might never talk to him again.
- He read many business books then, but fear prevented him from using them.
- He thinks fear is often the real blocker at zero, even when people name missing tactics
  or resources.
- He would rather be a failure than a coward.

## Notable verbatim quotes
