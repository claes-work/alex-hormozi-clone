# TikTok Shop Has a Hidden $200M Loophole

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a first-year TikTok Shop creator community and brand-matchmaking business doing about $3.6M, with revenue split between memberships and transaction fees. The founder considers raising capital for a platform build. Alex argues against raising because the company already has cash flow and because the largest growth lever is not capital; it is acquiring more high-leverage creator faces who already hold trust and attention.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A cash-flowing business should not raise capital by default, especially when growth does not clearly require money.
- AI and modern tooling reduce the software headcount needed to build early platforms.
- More money can make founders lazy by making them frame every constraint as a spending problem.
- If one thing could 10x the company in a year, the founder should remove everything that is not that constraint.
- In the current media environment, creators hold attention and trust; influencer relationships may be the highest-leverage growth asset.
- A blended model of membership plus transaction fees can be acceptable; the model does not need to be simplified before the true constraint is solved.
