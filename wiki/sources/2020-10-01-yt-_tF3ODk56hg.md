# My Address To The Gym Industry At The Onset of COVID (2020)

## Summary

In this 2020 Gym Launch address to his gym-owner community, Hormozi argues that
COVID has permanently shrunk the brick-and-mortar gym market (estimating 25-30%+ of
independent gyms already permanently closed, possibly climbing to 40-50%) and
declares that Gym Launch will pivot its focus to a remote/online coaching model. He
frames the online model as offering effectively unlimited lead generation, lower lead
costs, higher margins, and geographic/temporal freedom, and lays out why the specific
GLX free-trial model succeeds where three common online models fail.

## Key claims

- (2020) Hormozi estimates 25-30% of all independent gyms had permanently closed at
  the onset of COVID, and predicts the number could climb to 40-50% over the next six
  months. He states he originally guessed ~30% and thinks he underestimated.
- (2020) He invokes the finance maxim that "the market wins" — a strong operator in a
  weak/shrinking market loses; the gym industry is a shrinking market for the
  foreseeable future.
- (2020) COVID has "permanently trained consumers" to fill the fitness need in a
  different way; any fraction of clients introduced to a new solution greater than
  zero will stick with it, so the market only shrinks.
- (2020) Gym Launch is "loyal to gym owners, not to gyms" — the gym was just the
  vehicle for the owner's skills; the company will pivot to whatever vehicle makes
  owners the most money, which he now judges to be remote.
- (2020) Online lead generation is effectively unlimited ("you won't even fatigue a
  50 mile radius for weight loss"), whereas a 5-mile radius fatigues fast. In-person
  copy/creative burns out at 3-4x the rate of remote.
- (2020) Average in-person lead cost is ~$30 even for a free offer; COVID friction
  roughly doubles the drop-off of people who already had excuses not to go to a gym.
- (2020) The remote model is more profitable: no forced rent, fixed margins,
  one-on-one online fulfillment produces superior client results, and the owner gains
  location and time freedom.
- (2020) He explains he had no online model before COVID because (1) he didn't have to
  and (2) no existing model could be run at scale by anyone without a superior skill
  set, authority, or following. He critiques three common models: outbound/cold
  messaging (works but too effort-heavy, doesn't scale), webinars (fatigued, ~$100
  applications, only ~1-2x return even for experts), and 5-day online group challenges
  (effort-based, "clumpy," hard to scale).
- (2020) The GLX model works because it uses a free-trial front end in a semi-regional
  (same-state) market with existing trust, low-skill sales, fixed lead cost (via a
  system he credits to "Allen"), and a supplement upsell plus continuity that lets
  owners break even on acquisition and profit by day 30. Continuity is priced at
  ~$39/$49/$59 a week and retains clients at roughly a $2,000 price point.
- (2020) He claims the in-person and online models run in "parallel" — same sales
  choreography, same skill set — the only change is fulfillment via app instead of
  in-person workout. Spending the same $5,000 marketing budget on online clients earns
  more; it is currently easier to get 50 online clients than 50 in-person.
- (2020) On the sunk-cost fallacy: don't let prior gym investment cloud the current
  decision. Options for the existing gym: (1) walk away / break the lease — he argues
  this is the easiest time ever to exit a lease "scot-free" given the circumstances,
  or (2) earn enough online to cover it.
- (2020) He frames the moment as a "golden opportunity era" — high demand for online
  services, low supply, and a shortage of experienced professionals (gym owners are
  more experienced than Instagram fitness personalities or their own trainers).

## Biographical/context note

Confirms Hormozi's role running Gym Launch (a B2B licensing/education company serving
gym owners) during COVID in 2020, and that this address marks the deliberate strategic
pivot of the company toward remote/online coaching. He references his own gym-owner
past directly: "I didn't take a vacation for five years. I never even left because I
was so afraid. I went back for Christmas... every single year for three straight years
came back after two days because I couldn't handle the stress of being gone." He notes
that many of his community's seven-figure gym owners had already sold their gyms and
pivoted 100% online, and that half the people in the industry are "ex-clients of mine
[or] ex-employees of mine." He references his own $37 cold-messaging course (built from
two or three $10,000 courses he bought), the "traineriz[e]" fulfillment app, and a
lead-nurture system he attributes to "Allen."
