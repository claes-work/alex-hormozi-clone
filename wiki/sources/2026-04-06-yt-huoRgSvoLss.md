# Normalize 72-Hour Work Weeks for Young Men

## Summary

Alex advocates a six-day, 12-hour workweek for men aged 16-28. His rationale is that
young people have little experience or market utility but substantial energy, so they
should trade that resource for repetitions, skill, and proof. He frames resourcefulness
as using the assets currently available rather than delaying for capital or connections.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says men aged 16-28 should normalize 72-hour workweeks; this is
  his prescriptive opinion, not a universal health or labor standard.
- He says market value follows utility and that inexperienced young people can build
  utility by trading energy for experience.
- Alex calls using what one has the first rule of entrepreneurship.
- Lack of capital or connections should prompt resourcefulness rather than inaction.
- Skill develops through repeated failure, incremental adjustment, and many repetitions.
- He distinguishes kindness from niceness: advice intended to help may feel painful.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Trade that energy for experience points and become useful."

> "It's about resourcefulness, not resources."

> "I'm not here to be nice to you. I'm trying to be kind."
