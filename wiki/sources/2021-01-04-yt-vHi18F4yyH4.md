# The Mind-Blowing Reason A Competitor Made Twice As Much

## Summary

A short story from Hormozi's gym-era (age 24): despite converting leads at 2.5x the rate
of a competitor, that competitor made twice as much money because he spent 5x as much on
ads at a lower efficiency. The lesson: efficiency metrics don't matter — total profit
does, and declining efficiency at higher spend is a normal part of scaling.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated 2021-01-04)

- At 24, running ads locally, Hormozi got 15–20:1 returns and converted ~25% of leads; a
  new competitor converted only ~10% but spent ~$500/day to Hormozi's ~$100/day.
  (self-reported)
- The competitor spent ~$15,000 to make ~$60,000 (keeping ~$45,000); Hormozi spent ~$3,000
  to make ~$25,000 (keeping ~$22,000) — so the competitor made roughly twice as much
  despite far worse efficiency. (self-reported, illustrative figures)
- **Core lesson:** conversion rate and efficiency don't matter — "it's just about who
  makes the most money." (paraphrase)
- **Scaling seed:** as you scale, all your marketing gets *less efficient* per dollar
  spent, and that loss of efficiency is a normal, expected part of scaling. (paraphrase)

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "He was making twice as much money as me even though he was spending five times as much."

> "That's when I was like, wait, none of this [stuff] matters — it's just about who makes
> the most money."

> "At some point… all your [stuff] will get less effective… less efficient in terms of
> spend, but that's part of scaling."
