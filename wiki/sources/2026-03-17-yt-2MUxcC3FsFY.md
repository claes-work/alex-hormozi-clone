# Helping a Real Estate Consultant Get More Leads

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A commercial real-estate advisor selling advisory services to C-suite execs and business owners (~$1.2M revenue, ~60% margins, one employee setting appointments) plans to move from a Seattle suburb to a much larger Phoenix market next year, effectively restarting from zero, and wants Alex's customer-acquisition framework for reaching $10M in a new market where he knows no one. His current growth came from "brute force calling and emailing one-to-one." Alex first grounds the strategy in **channel selection by customer type**: high-level buyers get everything from referrals and specific "arenas," so acquisition there is outreach-driven, not ad-driven. He lays out a **channel ladder** for entering a new market — V1: face-to-face networking (conferences, meetups, "pounding the pavement"); V2: digital outreach (organic following if you have one, else true cold outreach on LinkedIn) — both governed by volume. Then the key twist: because the consultant has a **unique niche angle** (specializing in the *tenant/buyer* side of industrial and office leases, ~80-90% tenant-side in a high-interest-rate market — a decommoditized position that doesn't get "beat on" by commercial brokers), Alex is unusually willing to endorse **paid ads (Meta as well as LinkedIn)** for him, where he'd normally reject ads for a generic realtor. His core advice: normally run only one channel, but for local, hammer the market; ads don't take much time, so spend **$100-200/day in local ads as the "fast/guaranteed path"** whose real purpose is to build the sales motion, while running the normal networking/outreach playbook in parallel "as though ads don't exist." On offer/messaging he pushes **niche-down messaging** ("I am a specialist at negotiating leases for commercial buildings under these conditions"), **before/after proof** (landlord's original offer vs. what was negotiated), and a **rebate-style guarantee** — but advises anchoring to **dollar amounts, not percentages** (percentages "get eaten up real fast"; a big absolute number is more impressive), and only taking projects over a size threshold so the anchor number stays large. Ends with the roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames acquisition-channel choice by customer type: for high-level buyers (C-suite, business owners), acquisition is outreach- and referral-driven because "most high-level people are going to get everything from referrals or from meeting people in specific arenas" — such buyers won't respond to a generic Meta ad.
- As of March 2026, his channel ladder for entering a brand-new local market: V1 = face-to-face networking (conferences, meetups, "pounding the pavement"); V2 = digital outreach (leverage an organic following if you have one, otherwise true cold outreach on LinkedIn); both are volume games.
- As of March 2026, a **unique/decommoditized niche angle changes the calculus on ads** — for a generic "realtor number six" he says paid ads have "no chance," but for a specialist (e.g. tenant-side commercial lease negotiation) niche messaging "could pull someone who otherwise" already has multiple brokers, so he's willing to endorse Meta as well as LinkedIn.
- As of March 2026, he "[doesn't] normally recommend doing more than one thing" for acquisition — the exception is **local**, where the market is smaller and "you just have to hammer a market."
- As of March 2026, ads "don't take a tremendous amount of time," so he'd have the client spend ~**$100-200/day in local ads** treated as the "fast path / guaranteed path" — the primary goal being to work those leads and **get the sales motion in place** — while running the normal networking playbook in parallel "as though ads don't exist."
- As of March 2026, plenty of realtors are "super successful running ads in local markets," so local paid acquisition for real estate is viable.
- As of March 2026, for messaging he recommends **niching down the claim** ("I am a specialist at negotiating leases for commercial buildings under these conditions") because narrow specialist messaging pulls buyers who otherwise already have brokers (a "Red Ocean").
- As of March 2026, he recommends **before/after proof** in the ads: show what the tenant was originally offered, what was negotiated, and the difference — plus a **rebate-style guarantee** (e.g. rebate the fee if savings fall short of a target).
- As of March 2026, on proof/guarantee framing he advises **anchoring to dollar amounts rather than percentages** — "percentages get eaten up real fast," a small-percentage or back-loaded multi-year saving reads as unimpressive, so anchor to the biggest absolute number you can and **only take projects over a size threshold** to keep that number large.
