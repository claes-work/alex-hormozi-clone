# Hard work is the goal...

## Summary

A short solo clip in which Alex Hormozi distinguishes being **happy** from being
**satisfied**, and argues that the reward of hard work is the work itself. He draws a
line between contentment (fine) and complacency (taking no more action), invokes the
Haitian proverb "behind mountains are more mountains," and recounts a roughly year-long
"retirement" experiment whose conclusion was that **hard work is the goal** — any
outcome it produces is a secondary, consequential effect, not the point. He frames this
as a personal (not universal) foundational truth: there is "misery on both sides" —
boredom and depression when he doesn't work, stress when he does — so he'd rather be
productive and useful. The clip ends with a promo for his free 10-stage roadmap from
zero to $100M+ at acquisition.com/roadmap.

## Key claims

_All paraphrased and Alex-attributed (solo clip), dated 2026-02-23 unless quoted._

- You're allowed to be **happy** before you hit your goal — just not **satisfied**. (paraphrase)
- There's a big difference between being **content** with your life/work and being
  **complacent** (complacent = not going to take any more action). (paraphrase)
- Haitian proverb he cites: "behind mountains are more mountains" — after every peak you
  just see more peaks ahead. (paraphrase of his framing)
- "The work works on you more than you work on it." (near-verbatim aphorism)
- He once took roughly a year of "retirement" to figure out what he wanted to do; the
  thesis he came out with was that **hard work is the goal**, and any result of the work
  is only a secondary/consequential effect. (paraphrase; biographical — self-reported)
- A friend asked why he still works; his answer was that it's the thing he enjoys doing
  most. (paraphrase; anecdote)
- Looking back on his life: when he didn't work he was bored and depressed; when he
  works he's stressed but also has moments he really enjoys. (paraphrase; self-reported)
- He treats "there is misery on both sides" as a personal foundational truth (explicitly
  not for everyone), so he'd rather be productive and useful. (paraphrase)
- The only way to be productive and useful is to be happy about the process but not
  satisfied, so you can keep providing value to the world. (paraphrase)
- Promo: a free 10-stage roadmap from $0 to $100M+ that "less than 1% of companies
  finish," broken down across eight business functions, drawn from doing it multiple
  times across software, physical products, service, and brick-and-mortar; available at
  acquisition.com/roadmap. (paraphrase; marketing claim)
