# The 8 Laws of Business I Learned From Charlie Munger (2021-05-14)

## Summary

In this early (2021) video Alex Hormozi relays eight principles he attributes to
Charlie Munger — whom he calls a hero and mentor despite never having met him —
consolidated from Munger's speeches and writings. Seven of the eight are framed as
Munger-style **inversions** (i.e. things guaranteed to make you miserable or fail,
so that avoiding them points toward success). Hormozi illustrates each with his own
stories and commentary, then closes by adding a ninth principle of his own —
handling pressure — supported by three Stoic/mental-model strategies. As of this
date he describes himself as running a portfolio (Gym Launch, Prestige Labs, and
others) that has "done 120 plus million in the last four years."

## The 8 laws (as presented)

All eight below are attributed by Hormozi to Charlie Munger. He deliberately reorders
them and leads with "Invert." The heavy elaboration/examples on each are Hormozi's own.

1. **Invert. Always invert.** — *Munger-origin* (Hormozi notes Munger references
   Johnny Carson on solving problems in reverse). Hormozi's elaboration: start from
   what you don't want and solve backward; the remaining seven are framed as inversions.
2. **Don't ingest chemicals to alter your state.** — *Munger-origin* (avoid getting
   addicted to drugs/alcohol as a coping mechanism). Hormozi adds a non-judgmental caveat.
3. **Don't envy.** — *Munger/Buffett-origin* (their line that "every single year
   someone is doing better than us"). Hormozi's elaboration: the "measuring stick"
   for wealth changes over time (his went from Chipotle burritos → $100k → $1M units);
   envy is "an imaginary way to make yourself suffer."
4. **Don't resent people.** — *Munger-origin* (Hormozi retells Munger's story of a
   king who wrote resented names on slips of paper in a drawer and later saw how life
   had dealt with them). Hormozi's elaboration: people who do bad things already suffer
   from being who they are.
5. **Be reliable / don't be unreliable.** — *Munger-origin* ("if you just get this one
   right... it's very difficult to be miserable"). Hormozi's elaboration: his high-school
   story of a hardworking, reliable kid he mocked who out-consistented him (Hormozi went
   to Vanderbilt; the kid got into Duke) — reliability as the trait that "opens things up."
6. **Don't overspend your income.** — *Munger-origin.* Hormozi's elaboration: wealth is
   a **ratio** between income and expenses, not a number; he aims to live on ~10%, and
   at one point lived on $30k/year (~3%) while earning over $1M/year.
7. **Don't learn only from your own mistakes** (learn from others' mistakes and
   successes). — *Munger-origin.* Hormozi's extended elaboration: a "learning ladder"
   that evolves with your level — trade time for free apprenticeship → paid coaches →
   masterminds (peers) → networking + advisers (equity-incentivized) → heroes (people
   you learn from at a distance, as he does with Munger).
8. **Don't quit early** (don't quit at your third or fourth big failure). — *Munger-origin*
   ("you will fail many times"; counting your failures means nothing). Quitting early
   guarantees long-term misery by removing hope and purpose.

**Hormozi's own added 9th law — "Handle pressure / don't crack under pressure."**
*Alex-original* (he credits the seed to a speech by a past U.S. president, not Munger).
Core idea: most pressure comes from arbitrary, self-created expectations. Three
strategies he offers:
- **Negative visualization** (from Epictetus, via a friend named Trevor): imagine a
  negative event happening 1,000 times, then "skip to the end" so expectations match
  reality (his shrunk-shirt example).
- **Expect the worst going in** (e.g. assume every workout you'll be tired/unmotivated;
  anything better is a bonus).
- **The "fine mist" / meaning-making frame** (from hearing Elon Musk): over a long
  enough horizon everything becomes a fine mist and disappears, so nothing matters —
  which frees you to choose what to assign meaning to. "Our brains are simply
  meaning-making machines."
