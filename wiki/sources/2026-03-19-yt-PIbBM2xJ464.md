# How to get your first customer

## Summary
Monologue on the Rule of 100 and the inputs-over-outputs philosophy. Alex defines the Rule of 100 (which he credits to others, not himself) as 100 *primary actions* per day in advertising — 100 minutes of content, 100 outreaches, $100/day on ads, or (if already spending) 100 minutes/day writing ad copy and studying others' hooks — about 4 hours/day of pure inputs. He introduces "rule of 100 on steroids," a concept from *$100M Leads* he learned from a gym owner (13–14 gyms) called **"open to goal"**: managers work from open until they hit the goal — sometimes done by noon, sometimes 5am to midnight. His core method: figure out the single input that most closely correlates with the output you want, then "jam as much as you possibly can into inputs," because telling someone "go get me 10 customers" makes them freeze and scatter. He credits his own outsized success across domains to "ruthlessly focusing on one input" (with a childhood video-game grinding analogy). He closes on his definition of work as **output = volume × leverage** (reps × skill), where the only way to get skilled is more reps — a virtuous cycle — quoting Andy Grove ("so many people working so hard and achieving so little"). Ends with the roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex defines the **Rule of 100** (explicitly "not the one who invented this") as 100 primary advertising actions/day: 100 minutes of content, 100 outreaches, $100/day on ads, or 100 minutes/day writing/studying ad copy and hooks — "about 4 hours a day," measured as pure *inputs*, not outputs.
- As of March 2026, he introduces **"open to goal"** ("rule of 100 on steroids," from *$100M Leads*), learned from a gym owner with 13–14 gyms: work "open until they hit their goal" — could finish by noon or run 5am–midnight.
- As of March 2026, his method: "first figure out what the input is that most closely correlates or tracks with the output you want, and then you jam as much as you possibly can into inputs."
- As of March 2026, he argues vague output goals ("go get me 10 customers") cause people to freeze and scatter ("shotguns, sprays"); once they find the one thing that works, "you just jam that button as hard as you possibly can."
- As of March 2026 (self-reported): he attributes being "disproportionately successful in different domains" to "ruthlessly focusing on one input" (illustrated with grinding a zombie spawn point in a childhood video game for hours).
- As of March 2026, his definition of work: **output = volume × leverage** ("how many times you do the thing times how much you get for each time"); "work smart or work hard? … you do both."
- As of March 2026, he frames a virtuous cycle: the only way to get skilled (more leverage) is more inputs (more reps), so "doing more and getting better" compounds "and then you get more for what you do."
- As of March 2026, he cites Andy Grove: "there are so many people working so hard and achieving so little" — the lack of correlation between inputs and outputs; most business owners "work a fair amount. They just work on the wrong stuff."
