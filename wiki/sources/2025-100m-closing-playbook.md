# $100M Closing Playbook (2025)

**What it is.** A ~58-page Acquisition.com playbook by Alex Hormozi, subtitled *"How to Turn Nos into Sales"*, teaching a complete closing system — the psychology of buying decisions plus a named library of scripted "closes" for each source of objection. Raw source: `raw/books/2025-100m-closing-playbook.md`. NOTE: the raw was extracted via Drive text export from an image/graphic PDF, so the OCR is **noisy** — many passages are garbled and word-scrambled, and there are no reliable page markers. Verbatim quoting here is deliberately conservative: only clean, coherent lines are quoted; scrambled fragments are paraphrased or omitted, and uncertain content is flagged.

## Thesis

Closing — the part of selling that begins *after* you ask the prospect to buy — is where money is actually made, and it is the highest-leverage skill in a business because teams "routinely double close rates" once trained (Closing Playbook). Advertising splits an audience into three buckets: YESes (qualified, ready), NOs (unqualified, wrong fit), and MAYBEs (on the fence). "The money is in the maybes." People on the fence lack the **power** to decide, so they avoid deciding and disguise avoidance as objections. The salesperson's job is not to force a purchase but to return decision-making power to the prospect so they can genuinely choose either way. Avoidance is expressed as **blame**, which falls into three categories (the "onion of blame"): circumstances, other people, and self.

## Structure and content

Opening narrative frames the origin: January 2017, at rock bottom (grandfather's death, Leila's father's house, a DUI, a business partner who drained the accounts), Hormozi's new Gym Launch sales team was closing almost nothing ("0 for 18"). He realized they simply didn't know how to sell, wrote them a closing playbook, drilled it daily, and went from red to black — the book reproduces 2017 payment-processor records showing the climb (see Key claims).

- **Why Is Closing So Important?** — advertising vs. selling vs. closing; the three-bucket model (YES / NO / MAYBE); closing decides who captures bucket three.
- **Power: The Missing Ingredient** — the goal of closing is to give the prospect enough power to make a real decision, not to extract a yes; "you win either way" if they decide genuinely. Includes an "Ethical Assumptions" author note (the product must actually get them to their goal; the difference between help and manipulation is intention).
- **Blame: What Robs People Of Their Power** — the "onion of blame" (external circumstances → other people → self); objections are "obstacles"; expect people to swap objections and peel layers.
- **My Rules of Closing** — ~28 numbered rules (repeat back, acknowledge/agree never disagree, get permission before "getting real," stack closes, volume negates luck, never change price on the spot, be kind not nice, etc.).
- **All-Purpose Closes** — the "80/20" closes usable against any non-yes: *What's Your Main Concern*, *Reason*, *Hypothetical*, *Zoom Out*, *1 to 10*, *Best Case Worst Case*, and *Card Not On Me* (plus an ACH author note).
- **When They Blame Circumstances** — Time closes (*Better To Start When You're Busy*, *You're Gonna Get Busy Again*, *It's Not About Timing It's About Priorities*, *Smartphone*, *When/Then*) and Money closes across four "flavors" (not-enough-value, can't-afford, others-do-it-for-less, haggling), including *It's Good That It's A Lot*, *Good Things Aren't Cheap*, *Resourcefulness Not Resources*, *You Pay The Price Either Way*, *Some Now Or More Later*, *We Could Do It For More*, and *It's Not, Not The Money*.
- **When They Blame Other People** — decision-maker / permission sequence ("support not permission"), *Three-Day No-Sweat Guarantee* downsell, and *Don't Let It Burn You Twice* for bad past experiences.
- **When They Take The Blame (Self)** — preferences (*Isolate And Solve*, *We Vote With Our Dollars*, *Pain Of Change*, *You Gotta Change To Change*, *Mechanic / Surgeon-Secretary*) and rushed decisions (*What Would Make It A No?*, *Another Year Of Almost*, *The Gameplan*, *This Isn't A Fast Decision*).
- **Guarantee Closes** — *Both Options Are Risk Free*, *Decadere* (decide = Latin *de-cadere*, "to cut off"), *Closer Or Further*.
- **Why You Should Train Closing** and **Closes Consolidated** — a benefits list and a full index of every named close.

## Key claims

- Closing is defined as the part of selling that begins *after* the prospect is asked to buy; "if you don't close, you don't make money" (Closing Playbook, *Why Is Closing So Important?*). Paraphrase.
- "Sales teams routinely double close rates with effective closing" — framed as the single best use of training time (Closing Playbook). Paraphrase of a stated claim; no study cited.
- Advertising splits an audience into three buckets — qualified YESes, unqualified NOs, on-the-fence MAYBEs — and "most people interested in what you sell fall into" the MAYBE bucket, which is where trained closing pays off (Closing Playbook, *Why Is Closing So Important?*). Paraphrase.
- Origin story dated **January 2017**: after building and selling his first five gyms, his business partner drained their accounts; Gym Launch launches cost ~$3,300/day and he was "going into debt at a rate of $412 dollars per working hour" before the sales team learned to close (Closing Playbook, *Start Here*). Paraphrase; some surrounding figures appear in badly scrambled OCR and are treated cautiously.
- The book reproduces 2017 payment-processor records: revenue climbs from ~$100K (Jan 2017) toward >$1M/month by late 2017–Jan 2018, totaling on the order of **$6.8M collected** across the period at ~86% approval (Closing Playbook, *Start Here*, processing-records table). ⚠️ These exact figures are OCR-degraded and should be cited as approximate.
- "Power is the ability to direct or influence people" — to be powerful in a sale you must understand how to close; the goal of closing is to hand the prospect enough power to decide, not to force a yes (Closing Playbook, *Power* + Rule 28). Paraphrase.
- "The difference between help and manipulation is intention" — the ethical guardrail for the whole method (Closing Playbook, *Power*, Author Note). Paraphrase.
- All objections reduce to three sources of blame — **circumstances, other people, self** ("onion of blame") — and prospects commonly present several and swap between them; swapping is a sign closing is working (Closing Playbook, *Blame*). Paraphrase.
- "Price too high" almost always really means "value too low"; the fix is making the value more real, and Hormozi's rule is to **never change the price on the spot** to win a sale (Closing Playbook, *Money* section + Rules). Paraphrase.
- Qualify prospects with **STAR** — Situation (problem to solve), Timing (time to solve it), Authority (power to decide), Resources (money to spend); unqualified prospects "are not a sales problem — they're an advertising problem" (Closing Playbook, Author Note: Qualifying Prospects). Paraphrase. (Note: elsewhere STAR is also used for the interviewing/practice framework Situation-Task-Action-Result — the OCR shows both usages.)
- On decision-makers/partners the frame is "**support, not permission**": there are "no social obligations, only social consequences," and prospects should ask their partner for support rather than cede the decision (Closing Playbook, *When They Blame Other People*). Paraphrase.
- Guarantee-backed closes work by making *not* buying the riskier option; pair with a downsell like the **Three-Day No-Sweat Guarantee** (card down, payment delayed 24–72 hrs to "lock in the spot") (Closing Playbook, *Guarantee Closes* / *Other People*). Paraphrase.
- The stated purpose of the whole system: give people real decision-making power so "you win either way" — a genuine no avoids "the cancerous and costly bullet of unqualified purchases," while leaving someone on the "avoidance treadmill" is the real failure (Closing Playbook, *Power*). Paraphrase.
- Business benefits of closing well (dated 2025): lower customer-acquisition cost, higher ROAS, more paid-in-fulls / fewer payment plans, better client results because buyers are "fully bought in," longer closer retention (Closing Playbook, *Why You Should Take The Time To Train Closing*). Paraphrase.
