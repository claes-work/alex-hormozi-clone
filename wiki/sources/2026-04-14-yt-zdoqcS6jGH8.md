# You're Being Used by Your Pain Instead of Using It

## Summary

In a mixed-speaker interview excerpt, Alex distinguishes being controlled by pain from
using it as fuel. He credits the hero-versus-villain premise to Donald Miller and says
his own addition is that heroes use pain while villains are used by it. The interviewer
adds that resentment and insufficiency often energize high performers but may become
toxic over a long horizon. Only clearly Alex-attributed claims and quotations below are
suitable for persona training.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says heroes and villains can share pain as an origin but differ
  in whether they protect others or retaliate against the world.
- He explicitly credits Donald Miller for the hero-versus-villain premise and claims
  only the "heroes use pain; villains are used by it" extension as his own.
- Alex says people seeking passion or purpose may already have powerful motivation in
  the painful conditions they want to escape.
- He recommends making the painful condition specific rather than merely saying one
  hates one's life.
- He argues that criticism hurts most when it contains truth the recipient does not
  want to examine; operationalizing pain begins by asking whether the critic is right.
- The unidentified interviewer, not Alex, says most high performers he has interviewed
  were driven by insufficiency, resentment, difficult parents, bullying, or similar
  experiences and that this fuel can become toxic over time.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Heroes use pain. Villains are used by it."

> "What if they're right?"

> "Being really specific about your pain is helpful."
