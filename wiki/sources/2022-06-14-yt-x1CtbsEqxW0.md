# I lived on $0 income for 3 years growing my business...

## Summary

Solo YouTube video in which Alex Hormozi walks through the personal living
expenses at each "season" of his career, from sleeping on the gym floor at
$0 income to running Acquisition.com at over $150M/year. The through-line is
that he kept personal expenses near-zero and reinvested (or saved) almost
everything, driven by a strong "security" instinct. He reframes wealth as the
ratio between income and expenses plus the peace of mind of having "enough,"
and argues status purchases rarely convert value.

## Key claims

(All self-reported by Alex; solo video.)

- The reinvest-everything philosophy: for years he took no dividends/distributions
  and poured every dollar of cash flow back into growing the business — but he
  later regarded this as partly a *mistake*, because reinvesting every dollar
  became an excuse to avoid focusing on profitability, and because it left him
  exposed to losing everything.
- He says he has "lost everything in between here multiple times," which is why
  he became a strong advocate for taking distributions and de-risking as you grow.
- Wealth is defined as the ratio between income and expenses, plus the peace of
  mind of knowing you have "enough"; his goal was to reach "enough" as fast as
  possible.
- If material wealth accumulation is the goal, the lower your personal expenses,
  the more you accumulate — chase your "FU number" as fast as possible by widening
  the income-to-expense gap.
- Lifestyle should not scale in proportion to income; he and Leila kept expenses
  low relative to income the whole way up because both are "very security driven."
- Spend money on what *you* value (equity growth of his businesses) rather than
  what society tells you to value (a Lambo, a Bentley); before buying, pause and
  ask whether the thing actually confers value equal to its price.
- Advocates spending "extra dollars" on acquiring more skills to increase earning
  capacity, accelerating the income-vs-expense gap.
- "You become wealthy the moment you decide you are"; money buys freedom, not
  happiness, but it can decrease pain and inconvenience.

## Biography facts (dated, self-reported — the $0-income / reinvestment period)

Timeline of "seasons" as narrated (self-reported; approximate, no exact years given
in the video beyond 2021 and the 2022 present):

- **Season 1 — quit job, slept on the gym floor (Gym Launch origin era).** No
  personal rent (only business rent), car owned in cash (no car payment). Lived
  exclusively off the gym's cash sales; ate Chipotle constantly — his accountant
  told him he'd done 500+ Chipotle transactions in the first year. Grew income to
  ~$15,000/month during this period. Location context: Southern California.
- **Two partners come in; stop taking dividends, reinvest in growth.** Went from
  1 to 6 gyms. Had saved ~$70,000 after taxes from the first ~6–9 months of
  business; that cash lasted him **three years**. Upgraded his lifestyle to renting
  a shared room in a 7-person house in Costa Mesa, CA — rent ~$400–$700/month, a
  real roommate sharing the room. Personal expenses "virtually zero"; the only
  extra spend was going out to Ebola/EDM(?) [auto-caption garble — likely a club
  night]. This shared-room, near-zero-expense phase lasted three years.
- **Bought a condo, then rented it out.** Bought a condo he liked, lived there
  briefly, then rented it for ~$3,500/month. He and Leila started doing gym
  turnarounds — flying around to do launches. Business revenue ~$200–300K/month;
  his personal income still very low because he still wasn't taking distributions.
  Lived in an extended-stay at $1,800/month; owned a Prius in cash (#MozyMobiles).
- **Moved to Albuquerque** (to learn from friends there); switched to sending sales
  teams to fly out and do turnarounds so they could stay central. Rent $200/month
  (later stated $1,200/month for this Albuquerque phase — the transcript gives both
  figures). Prius in cash. Leila started cooking (real kitchen); they ate out once
  a week for date night. During this phase the turnaround business went from
  $300K/month to $1.5M/month; he was adding ~$1M/month to his bank account.
- **Bought a $1.8M house in Austin in cash** (after accumulating ~$4–6M saved
  after taxes). Still shared one car (owned in cash) — a BMW i8 gifted by
  ClickFunnels for being an affiliate (got the car essentially free). Ate out even
  less due to intense work mode. Later bought a Bentley (on friends Brad/Berkustio's[?]
  urging to "spend some money"), realized they didn't care, and sold it within six
  months at a loss. During this period revenue went from $1.5M to $4M+/month;
  accumulated ~$30M after taxes in cash + non-public equities/assets.
- **2021 — sold everything.** Sold the three businesses, the Austin house (bought
  $1.8M, sold ~$4M ~2.5 years later), and one car (the Bentley). Kept business
  assets and investment assets; no personal assets after the sale.
- **2022 (present) — Acquisition.com.** Renting the current condo at $15K/month;
  ~$15K/month on food/eating out; ~$15K/month on travel. Flew private more this
  season, then switched to JSX semi-private ($200–400/ticket vs. ~$50K for a
  private flight) for West Coast travel. Acquisition.com portfolio companies grew
  from ~$7M/month to ~$13M/month; company doing "over $150M/year" at time of
  recording.
