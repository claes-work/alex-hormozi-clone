# The Reason Most Agencies Never Break $10M

## Summary

Alex argues that a highly specialized agency's larger opportunity may be a rollup of the
customers it already serves. Standardized marketing, pricing, packages, and operations
perform much of the integration work; ownership in a consolidated group can capture
more of the value than stopping at agency fees.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports about $5 million in agency revenue, 160 neuropathy-clinic
  customers out of an estimated 2,000, and a desire for cash or an eventual sale; these
  figures and market estimates are unverified.
- As of April 2026, Alex says niche B2B agencies and information businesses often do the
  operational standardization required for a rollup but stop before consolidating ownership.
- He proposes offering clinic owners a potentially higher group exit value in exchange
  for a 20%-30% slice, but all example valuations and exit figures are hypothetical.
- A first successful rollup could change the operator's reputation, pricing power, and
  long-term retention because the operator can demonstrate an ownership outcome.
- Alex says do-it-yourself education fit gym owners because learning sales and marketing
  could exceed their earnings, whereas high-earning physicians can pay specialists instead.
- He self-reports that Gym Launch licensed tested ads rather than providing full service
  and says he would have considered a rollup had a buyer market existed for group fitness.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The ultimate version of those businesses is rollups of the customers you serve."

> "They do all the heavy work of integration for what a rollup would do."

> "The value of learning the skill exceeds their current skill."

## Notes

This is strategic commentary, not medical, investment, valuation, or transaction advice. Synthesis candidate: agency-to-customer-rollup path and the income-based boundary between teaching a capability and doing it for the client.
