# How to Know Who Your Real Friends Actually Are

## Summary

Alex says real friends are revealed by how they react when someone wins. He advises
taking advice only from people whose dreams for the person's life are larger than their
own, names Leila as the closest tactically aligned person in his life, and frames
optimism as accepting many small losses for uncapped upside.

## Key claims

- Alex says a friend's reaction to a win is a useful test of whether they truly want the
  person to succeed.
- He says advice should come from people whose dreams for one's life are bigger than
  one's own.
- He says, self-reported on 2026-04-23, that Leila's dreams for him have often been
  bigger or ahead of his own and that listening to her has produced strong returns.
- He says doubters are often right most of the time until the person succeeds enough
  that earlier misses no longer matter.
- He says downside losses are more frequent and smaller, while upside is uncapped.
- He contrasts pessimists being right often with optimists becoming rich because they
  account for intensity and outlier returns.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "How they react when you win."

> "Upside is uncapped."

> "The pessimists get to be right and the optimists get to be rich."
