# 8 Entrepreneurs Compete For $100,000 - Episode 1

## Summary

This first "Scale or Fail" episode has Alex diagnose Flex 100 Moving and Delivery, a San Diego moving company. The contestant's biography and operating facts are his own; Alex's contribution is a detailed repositioning plan: stop competing on hourly price, create VIP/standard tiers, sell risk/ease/speed differences, use realtors and brokerages as higher-leverage acquisition nodes, and build local organic proof through before/after/review content. Alex ultimately gives the plan but marks the company "fail" for the competition because it is too early for the multi-location scale prize.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2026-06-26 - Back-to-front diagnosis**: Alex says he fixes businesses from back to front: what is sold, how it is sold, who it is sold to, and then how customers find out.
- **2026-06-26 - Commodity escape**: A moving company selling by the same hourly unit as competitors becomes a commodity; Alex recommends moving toward job/package pricing and a differentiated white-glove offer.
- **2026-06-26 - Richer avatar**: Alex points out that realtor-sourced jobs are larger because they are a taste of selling to customers with more money.
- **2026-06-26 - Offer tiering**: He recommends a VIP tier with packing, master movers, included materials, insurance/risk reduction, and eventually same-day premiums, with standard as the fallback.
- **2026-06-26 - Sales education**: Alex says a good sales process moves the buyer from local price comparison to global evaluation by teaching variables they had not considered.
- **2026-06-26 - Realtor leverage**: He recommends going beyond individual realtor outreach to brokerage-level integration, because one brokerage can represent many agents and recurring move referrals.
- **2026-06-26 - Local proof content**: He recommends before/after content from each move, ending with reviews/testimonials, especially realtor testimonials.
- **2026-06-26 - Competition decision**: Alex says the plan can work short term, but the company is too early and too far from multi-location scale to receive the competition's "scale" decision.

## Context / participant-attributed claims
