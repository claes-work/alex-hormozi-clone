# Don't Worry About What You're Earning, Worry About What You're Lear...

## Summary

Alex Hormozi answers a short question about what kind of sales experience to pursue. His recommendation is high-volume sales because it produces the fastest feedback loops. He cites cold calling, door-to-door, car-wash sales, and fitness sales as examples where a beginner can get many repetitions quickly. The priority should be learning volume, not immediate earnings.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A beginner trying to build sales skill should prioritize volume.
- High-volume sales positions create more feedback loops in less time.
- Many strong marketers Alex knows started in high-volume sales roles.
- Fitness sales are useful because the market for weight loss creates many conversations.
- Early on, the person should worry less about earnings and more about what they are learning.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Volume, brother. Volume."
