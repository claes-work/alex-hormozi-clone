# Leila Hormozi reveals how her and Alex started their business together

> (first person: "he's now my husband"). **Alex does not speak on camera here.**

## Summary

In this ACQ clip, Leila Hormozi recounts how she and Alex met on Bumble and how
he recruited her — over roughly five weeks of dating — to co-found Gym Launch
instead of opening her own gym. The pivotal moment: after Alex flew out to run a
gym for two weeks, they sat down and processed a backlog of member contracts
worth over $100,000, which convinced Leila the business was real. Within two
weeks she quit her job, kept only her online clients, and flew to Baltimore for
the first-ever "Gym Launch."

## Key claims (Alex-attributed only)

All items are Alex's stance/actions **as reported by Leila** (self-reported via
partner recollection; not registry-verified):

- Alex pitched Leila that she should not open her own gym but instead learn from
  him, framing it as "leapfrogging" because he was ~3 years ahead of her on the
  same path.
- Alex was starting a new business called **Gym Launch**; he had already filed
  the LLC formation documents (kept in a binder) and wanted Leila to co-run it.
- At the time Alex was already running, per Leila, **six gyms plus about two
  agencies** — roughly eight businesses at once.
- Alex personally flew out (~2 weeks) to launch a client's gym and, per Leila,
  effectively set aside his other businesses to do it — which read to her as real
  commitment to Gym Launch.
- On that trip Alex signed up gym members but had **not processed any of the
  payments**; he said "somebody else does that." The unprocessed contracts
  totaled over $100,000 once processed.

## Biography facts (dated; how they met / started)

Source date 2024-03-22 (retrospective account; events ~2017). Marked by speaker.

- **[Leila]** They met on **Bumble**; Alex is now her husband.
- **[Leila]** On the first date Leila described a career "fork in the road";
  Alex told her he didn't think she should do either option and that she should
  "work for me" — she declined ("we're on our first date").
- **[Leila]** Over several more dates Alex pitched the "leapfrog" idea (skip
  opening a gym, learn from him).
- **[Leila]** At Alex's house she found a binder on the table containing the
  **Gym Launch LLC formation documents**; Alex said he was "waiting for you to
  come join me."
- **[Leila]** While Alex was away ~2 weeks launching a gym, he asked Leila to
  handle owner tasks (e.g. pick up cash from his gyms, deposit at the bank).
- **[Leila]** On his return they processed the backlog of signed member
  contracts together — **over $100,000** — which convinced her it was "for real."
- **[Leila]** Within 2 weeks she quit her job, told her clients, kept only her
  **online clients**, and flew to **Baltimore, Maryland** for the **first Gym
  Launch**. They had known each other **5 weeks**; she was **23**.
- **[Leila]** After she quit, Alex — by his own later admission to her — was "so
  wrapped up in my own stuff" and "barely said a word," instead suggesting they
  go to Chipotle. She calls this her second-biggest pivotal moment.
