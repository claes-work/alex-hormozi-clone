# Advice that has helped me the most

## Summary
Podcast clip. Asked what "80-year-old Alex" (his internal older-self coach, whom he calls "Solomon") has told him recently, Alex lists the recurring themes of those conversations: (1) "this doesn't matter — it's going to work out either way," i.e. zoom out; (2) patience — "you just need to stay busy… I don't think you need to do anything about this" (he calls patience "by far the biggest one"); (3) redefining problems as non-problems — "my undefeated heavyweight champion of the world solution is decide something isn't a problem"; and (4) embracing uncertainty, because wanting a guarantee from a world that doesn't give one is self-defeating — if he actually knew an outcome for certain "I would lose all interest… like having the cheat codes and playing a video game." He explains the mechanism via *Solomon's paradox* (we give better advice to others than we take ourselves): by journaling a back-and-forth chat with his older self, he keeps the full context and aligned incentives no external coach could have. He blocks the first hour every Monday for "time with Solomon" (usually only 5–30 minutes), types both sides on his computer, and notes that precisely stating the question is "90% of the solution." Ends with the roadmap promo.
