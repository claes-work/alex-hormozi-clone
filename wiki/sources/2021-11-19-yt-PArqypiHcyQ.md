# How to get more sex and increase your revenue

## Summary
A "walk-and-talk" video (originally posted to a private community, later made public) whose provocative title is a hook for a single argument about retention and taking relationships for granted. Hormozi argues that both customers and spouses should be treated as "week-to-week" — never secured by a contract or a wedding vow — because people act in their own self-interest and will leave the moment you stop over-delivering value. He explicitly maps his near-zero churn on Gym Launch's highest-tier group onto how he treats his marriage to Leila. Despite the title, the "sex" framing is a metaphor for relationship retention, not sexual advice.

## Key claims (2021)
- One of Gym Lynch's / Gym Launch's five core tenants is **"own it"**; core tenants exist as a decision-making reference for the founder and employees ("this is who we are") (2021).
- People complaining about customers who quit a 12-month membership after three months (or who try to escape 5-year franchise agreements) are in a **victim mindset**; the churn is the market telling them they stopped being worth the money (2021).
- **If you rely on contracts, you lose.** Contracts and vows don't keep people; people do what they want. You should be discerning and protect yourself with agreements, but a business (or marriage) that *relies* on the contract is "living hell" (2021).
- He attributes his near-zero churn on the most expensive group to a deep **insecurity and fear of rejection** — he doesn't want anyone to feel he failed to deliver what he promised (self-reported, 2021).
- Reframe: shift from "we *got* the contract, we're done" to "we *got* the contract — now we have to fulfill on this"; treat the relationship as **week-to-week / day-to-day**, resold every single day (2021).
- A mentor's rule he repeats: **predict that people will always act in their own self-interest** and "you'll always be right" — so *align* relationships so the other person's best interest is to stay and keep paying you (2021).
- **Value discrepancy retains people.** People go to Chipotle because they pay $7 and get a "$20 meal"; he aims to be 10× more valuable than a price that is itself 3× more expensive than competitors — and never take the customer for granted (2021).
- The same logic applies to **employees**: complaints that employees don't care are a leadership failure ("how are you showing up as a leader, how invested are you in them"); a trainer who leaves with clients left because it was bad working for you (2021).
- Humans remember what *they* did and forget what others did for them; therefore you can't coast on past effort — you "have to do more again today." You can lament how people are, or "react and win" (2021).
