# You're Trapped In Your Own Business Because It's Too Local

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Naveed, a Fayetteville, North Carolina real-estate entrepreneur with a local ecosystem of home flipping, contracting, coaching, and vertically integrated services. Naveed wants national impact, but Alex argues that he has not yet dominated the local market. Alex recommends ignoring the national move for now, fortifying the local base, increasing sales volume and price architecture, using content to satisfy the impact goal, and avoiding overextension before the city is conquered.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Naveed** - the coachee. His real-estate ecosystem, local market, companies, revenue, pricing, and impact goal are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A founder can be trapped by a local business, but that does not mean the next move is national expansion.
- The coachee's model was built to dominate a local market, and Alex argues he is barely getting started locally.
- Before conquering the world, a company has to conquer a country; before conquering a country, it has to conquer a city.
- Overextended armies collapse; a business should fortify its base before expanding.
- The coachee's current volume, about 10 students every three months, is tiny relative to local opportunity; Alex sees an 8x sales increase within existing infrastructure as plausible.
- If the impact goal is to reach millions, content is a more leveraged path than personally helping every American become a home flipper.
- The COO should free the founder to do higher-leverage work such as content and sales, not to abandon the local base.
- The offer could support a higher-ticket structure: a done-with-you tier around $15K-$25K and a turnkey tier around $100K.
- Alex rejects the belief that the local market cannot afford a higher-ticket offer, pointing to the share of Americans with million-dollar net worth and the founder's limited current reach.
