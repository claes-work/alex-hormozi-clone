# Do More Than What Is Required

## Summary
Solo reflection framed around going "above and beyond" — specifically, treating 2026 as a "legal year" in which Alex proactively educated himself on the compliance and legal risks that grow with company size. His thesis: the bigger you are, the bigger the target on your back, and success brings its own painful problems ("champagne problems" are still real problems). He argues you must become educated on the laws that apply to you because "ignorance is not a defense" — not knowing a law exists does not exempt you from its consequences. He says he almost earned "a law degree this year" going through public/private law, and concludes most laws are written with good intent and make sense when you follow the letter of them. He wants to "crush everyone and play by the rules": once you know the rules of the game, you can operate with more freedom because you know what to avoid. He extends this to rereading your own contracts (employment, partnership agreements) to understand what tools you have at your disposal and what expectations others have of you — which let him "sleep so much better." Finally he frames the emotional cost of legal battles: legal stress "eats up your shower time," dinner time, and mental cycles, but if you understand where the conflict is, know your next steps, and have documentation ready, you can circumvent most of the pain and gain a strategic advantage in extended conflicts. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
