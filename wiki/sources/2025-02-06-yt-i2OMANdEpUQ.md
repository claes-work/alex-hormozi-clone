# Building a Brand: This Strategy will help you Make more Money!

## Summary

A clipped segment from an 8-hour Alex Hormozi workshop with a room of business owners, focused on organic marketing strategy. Alex argues that the biggest money comes from marrying two worlds — audiences with traffic but no product, and businesses with a product but no traffic — and lays out how to run organic content as pure "give" while reserving all CTAs for paid ads. He also covers hiring an agency to build accountability, quality-over-quantity power laws in content, his "promise, proof, path" retention framework for the first 30 seconds of a video, and a working definition of a brand.

## Key claims

- Two worlds exist: audiences with traffic and no way to make money, and businesses that make money but need traffic; merging them produces outsized returns (examples given: Logan Paul + Prime, MrBeast + chocolate, and Alex himself needing a product for the Skool distribution base).
- Starting organic content immediately makes existing paid ads more profitable — people see the ad, check the organic content, are more likely to show up to booked calls and close at a higher rate.
- In the early days, treat content as lead nurture; as the channel grows it shifts toward acquisition.
- Alex starts every new platform/medium by hiring an agency, framed as buying lessons to shrink timelines; his early agency (~$5k/month) mostly kept him accountable to making three videos a week and posting them.
- Recommends not promoting inside the content: CTAs in content kill reach and feel off; put links in the description and link-in-bio instead. Let content be "give only" and let ads carry the "ask," which sidesteps the give-to-ask ratio and protects the brand.
- The 3-to-1 give/ask ratio still exists but plays out across a newsfeed: three pieces of free content, then one ad — the ad is "the money one."
- Niche vs. wide: keep serving your avatar's niche, but sprinkle in wider top-of-funnel content (~3:1 or 4:1 niche-to-wide); wider content gets fewer views but introduces the right people who then go deeper.
- Content quality follows power laws — spending 10x more time on a video can yield 100x the views; bigger creators post less frequently for this reason. Quantity in the beginning exists to find the quality.
- His "promise, proof, path" framework for the first 30 seconds: promise (what's in it for me) and proof (why should I believe you) drive whether viewers stay; path (structure/expectations) drives watch time. For educators, "proof" is the equivalent of the entertainer's spectacle ("the Lamborghini").
- Packaging must be congruent — match the headline words and confirm the thumbnail early in the video.
- Working definition offered: a brand is "an association between two things"; branding is also a word for teaching.
- Alex says he will probably write a book on branding at some point.
