# The Price of Building Something Big

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a real-estate law-firm owner who also runs a smaller marketing agency. The law firm is already much larger and more profitable, but the founder spends 30-35 hours per week on the agency because it personally pays him about $200K/year. Alex says the opportunity cost is obvious: phase out the agency, refer clients to trusted providers for kickbacks, keep some passive referral income, and put the time into the law firm instead of hiring an operator for the smaller distraction.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If one business is much larger and more profitable, spending 30-35 hours per week on a smaller business can be a poor use of founder time.
- The right question is whether the founder could replace the smaller business's profit by putting that time into the larger, higher-leverage business.
- To shut down a service business responsibly, give clients ample notice, stop accepting new clients, maintain service during a phase-out period, and recommend trusted alternatives.
- The founder can negotiate referral/kickback deals with trusted providers to make the old business partly passive.
- Hiring an operator to preserve a much smaller distraction is not the smart move in this case.
