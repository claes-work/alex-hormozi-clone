# My Actual Social Media Strategy For 2026

## Summary

Solo livestream in which Alex Hormozi lays out his personal content playbook for 2026, built on a four-part influence framework he calls **SPCL** ("special") — **S**tatus, **P**ower, **C**redibility, **L**ikeness — and argues creators should reverse-engineer all four into their content rather than chase raw views. He announces his forward media strategy is two words, **"live, interactive"** (Acquisition.com / Mozi Media 3.0), justified by a MrBeast anecdote about an applause meter where live streamers drew the loudest reaction, and by the logic that longer, rawer formats deliver far more "reinforcement cycles" per prospect than shorts. He closes with the shift from "social media" to "interest media" (the content is the targeting) and a data-driven case for judging content by fit-to-avatar, not view counts.

## Key claims (his 2026 platform/format strategy)

- **Influence = likelihood of compliance with a request**, and it is built by stacking four elements — **SPCL: Status, Power, Credibility, Likeness**. Each works independently, but stacking all four maximizes influence ("min-max your influence").
  - **Status** = you control a scarce resource / reinforcers others want (his bar-bartender example; inherited money still confers some status). Demonstrate it in content via proof (e.g. "32.7M views this month," "$100M+ book launch in 72 hours").
  - **Power** = "say-do correspondence" — you tell people to do something, they do it, a good thing follows, so they're more likely to comply next time. If he had to pick only one of the four, he'd pick power. In content this is: give directions the viewer can follow and get a result.
  - **Credibility** = third-party validation you can see (he paid Guinness judges on-site to verify the book-launch records; the $10M building behind him in ads is "hard to fake").
  - **Likeness** = relatability, shared values, or literally looking like the audience. His prescription: "just be you." (Notes his audience is ~89% male vs. Leila's ~54% female on similar topics.)
- **Parents are the archetype** of maxed-out SPCL — they control scarce resources (shelter, food, money), run thousands of reinforcement cycles, often share your looks/values — which is why they hold so much influence over us.
- **Forward media strategy = "live, interactive"** — the Acquisition.com / "Mozi Media 3.0" vision. He is going all-in on live streams despite saying he "honestly hate[s] making YouTube videos" (staring at a camera with prompts).
- **Format hierarchy by influence** (from a MrBeast-recounted stadium applause meter): A-listers < shorts creators < long-form (podcasters/YouTubers) < **live streamers** (loudest). Live and long-form win because they maximize reinforcement cycles per prospect.
- **Shorts are a funnel into longs**, not the destination — viewers watch a few shorts to decide "this person seems legit," then risk their time on long-form.
- **Reinforcement-cycle math**: a 15-second short = 4 per minute; to match two 1-hour videos (120 min) a viewer would need to watch **~480 shorts**. Cites the "podcast election" (Trump on Rogan 3+ hrs, Elon on Tucker Carlson) as proof long-form time nudges behavior.
- **The internet always moves toward truth** — audiences reward the rawest, least-scripted approximation of "us hanging out." A-lister content is curated/photoshopped/scripted; podcasts and especially live streams are raw. Marry that rawness with SPCL elements.
- **Volume is near-linear.** He posted ~35,000 pieces of content this year vs. a typical $1-2M business's ~365 (one/day). ~100x the volume ≈ ~100x the prospects. Roughly 1 in 10 shorts becomes a 2-3x outlier; the game is to "jam as much into that input-output machine as I possibly can."
- **"Social media" is becoming "interest media."** The algorithm is now so good that **"the content is the targeting"** — it reads your topic, background, and clothing and serves it to people with matching interest history. So make content *for your avatar*, not for views.
- **Don't judge by views; judge by fit.** A "grandma slap" video gets humor-interested viewers, not customers. If you sell services you're an **educator, not an entertainer**. He cares more about **IRL responses** — texts from respected business owners ("that was fire") and in-person Vegas guests naming a format (e.g. the "cash cows" format) as their favorite.
- **Market-size realism (US Census-based math):** ~9% of people own a business (~32-33M); 95% of those are under $1M revenue; ~5% over $1M (~1.5M people); ~4% over $10M (1 in 250); ~1 in 3,000 businesses reach $100M/yr. So 100K views on avatar-fit content can be "crushing it" — don't compare to MrBeast's 100M; "we have different games." He's seen two businesses doing $1M+/yr with under 5,000 followers.
