# Dentist Asks for Advice, Gets Brutal Reality Check

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a dentist whose three practices produce about $20M and who wants to become a Gym Launch-style growth investor for other dental practices. Alex strongly pushes against minority-style growth investments in practices the dentist does not control. He says the simpler, higher-return move is to keep expanding or acquiring locally and own the market rather than creating a new, harder business with low control.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - dentist and dental-practice owner. His revenue, office count, proposed investment model, and licensing concerns are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Investing in other dental practices without majority control is a different business from operating the dentist's current practices.
- Alex predicts the minority/growth-help model would be miserable because the operators will not execute like the founder.
- If the other dentists were as capable as the founder, they would already have the founder's results.
- The founder is at the point where the business is getting easier and more scalable; Alex says that is when to go harder, not switch games.
- Opening or acquiring more local practices is more straightforward and likely more valuable than small growth investments.
- Buying older dentists' local practices outright may make more sense than helping them grow and hoping to sell back a stake.
- Owning a whole local market can create a cleaner and more valuable exit.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What stops you from just opening two more practices?"

> "This is an entirely new business."

> "You will want control and they will not execute and you will hate life."

> "If they were you, they would be you and they're not you."

> "When it got easy is when you need to go hard rather than when you need to do different."

> "I would probably continue to expand locally."

> "A very clean exit is like I own this whole market."
