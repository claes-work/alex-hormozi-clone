# My Writing Process

## Summary
Interview clip (an off-camera host with `>>` turns; only Alex-attributed lines are used here as voice data) in which Alex walks through how he writes his short-form posts — the one-liner "ad headline" style tweets/aphorisms he's known for. The mechanics are deliberately low-tech: for tweets he **just rewrites**, works one sentence at a time, and uses **no AI / ChatGPT at all** for them, only "a generic automation tool" to publish. The bulk of the clip is the host reading a string of Alex's aphorisms back to him ("Either sell extremely expensive to a select few or sell something super cheap to everyone — the middle is where people die"; "Poor people stay poor because they want a fast way to get rich"; "The fastest way to become confident is to build evidence… do so much volume that you don't have to doubt"; "You must first become misunderstood before you can become great"; "It's very hard to have a vision when you have bills to pay"), which lets Alex reveal his sourcing and craft. Two writing inputs stand out. First, **audience-sourced lines**: listeners on livestreams tag him in a quote they heard him say, and he then "quote[s] them quoting me" as a tweet — using the audience's own selection as pre-validation of what landed. Some lines are freestyle riffs ("raw dogged"); others are pre-tested — the "vision/bills" line drew an audible gasp at a workshop, so his team flagged it as known-good before posting. Second, the **rhetoric layer**: Alex separates the *idea* from *how you communicate it*, and consciously engineers rhythm — opposites/contrast (good/bad, high/low, give/get), the power of threes, repetition, and alliteration — asking himself what "permutation" of these devices makes a plain point ("do hard stuff") sound better and easier to remember. Ends with the standard $100M scaling-roadmap promo (acquisition.com/roadmap).
