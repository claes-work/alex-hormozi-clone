# Why You're Not Winning: You're Distracted

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a Fayetteville real-estate education and vertically integrated
services founder who wants national impact. Alex says the founder has not yet dominated
the local market and should fortify the base before expanding nationally. He recommends
doubling down locally, using content for impact, learning paid ads and sales, and
considering higher-ticket tiers.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex reframes the founder's goal as expanding a local real-estate education and service
  ecosystem from local to national, not exiting the company.
- He says the founder is stressed because the business contains too many connected pieces.
- A $15,000 offer that fills without paid marketing suggests strong proof and room for
  more demand generation.
- Alex says the founder has not saturated Fayetteville or nearby Raleigh and may be able
  to reach much larger local revenue before going national.
- To conquer a country, a business must first conquer a city; overextended armies
  collapse.
- The founder should ignore the national plan for now and fortify the local base.
- Content can satisfy the founder's impact goal more easily than trying to make everyone
  in America a home flipper.
- The COO should free the founder to do high-leverage work such as content, paid ads, and
  selling.
- Alex says a local offer like this may support both a done-with-you tier and a higher
  turnkey tier, potentially around $100,000.
- A small workshop sample does not prove the market cannot afford a higher ticket.
- Alex says about 9% of Americans have a million dollars in net worth, including assets
  such as home equity.
