# The Best Offer You Can Add to Your Business

## Summary
Solo teaching clip (opens on an audience question, "What is a good rule of thumb for how many people prepay?") in which Alex lays out his toolkit for **pulling cash forward** — getting customers to pay sooner rather than in trickled-out monthly installments. His core logic: a prepaid year eliminates churn ("no one can churn if you prepay") and hands you all the cash today, which you can redeploy to acquire more customers, so everyone "should at least give a 10% discount for getting paid in full today" — because money today is worth at least ~10% more than the same money a year out (you could park it in the stock market for the same return). He gives rule-of-thumb take-rates: a simple "buy 10 get 2" prepay deal (pay for 10 months, get 2 free) converts ~15–20%; a moderate discount **plus** one or more ancillary sweeteners lifts that to 30–40%. The sweeteners come from three levers — deliver faster (skip the line), make it less risky (add or double the guarantee), or make it easier (a dedicated concierge vs. group). He then covers third-party financing, citing a figure he attributes to knowing senior people at a financing firm: good financing can drive a **~35% increase in overall sales** because people who couldn't otherwise afford it now can — a "double whammy" of more buyers plus cash-now. Caveat: "financing will not save your business" — if the product is bad, financing just lets more people find out faster. On payment structuring he prefers to sell durations then push for prepay; failing that, split it (half now, half in 30 days) and keep asking for a third/fourth payment to compress the schedule, aligning payment dates to when the customer is most cash-flush (payday for wage-earners, deposit-heavy days for businesses) — and payments need not track delivery. His single favorite method is **layaway** (which he traces to the 1930s Depression): the customer pays first and receives the thing only once fully paid. Because faster payment = faster delivery, the customer self-incentivizes to pay it off quickly, collections get far easier (they've decided they want it and have nothing yet), it builds anticipation (his boyhood X-Men/Cyclops Oakley sunglasses saved for over a summer), and — the biggest benefit — **you risk nothing** since cash arrives before any delivery. Illustrated with an ALLCAPS-worthy anecdote from his software days (a ~$25k white-label enterprise onboarding with Allen, where two partners offered unlimited splits then voluntarily collapsed to half-now/half-next-month once they realized they had to pay before the onboarding). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
