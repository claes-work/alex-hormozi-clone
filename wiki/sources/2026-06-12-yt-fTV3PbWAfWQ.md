# I'm so confused. Walk me through how you make money

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the operator of Easy Dwell, a real-estate business doing about $3M top line and $500K profit. After clarifying the model, Alex identifies acquisition deal flow as the constraint. The owner thinks the wholesale market may already be tapped, but Alex strongly rejects that belief, argues the company is nowhere near exhausting Florida wholesaler deal flow, and recommends getting deeper into wholesaler networks, asking for exclusive deal flow, or bringing/acquiring wholesalers in-house to control more of the funnel.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Before giving advice, Alex insists on understanding how the business actually makes money.
- If a real-estate model produces about 50-60% cash-on-cash returns, more deal flow is highly valuable even if returns fall somewhat.
- A $3M/year real-estate buyer is nowhere near exhausting the Florida wholesale market.
- The owner should break the limiting belief that he has already tapped the available wholesale network.
- The fastest growth path may be to identify the biggest wholesalers and ask what it would take to receive all of their deals exclusively.
- If the company has a superior monetization vehicle, acquiring or bringing wholesalers in-house can create vertical integration from lead/click to close.
- Alex prefers controlling the whole funnel when it meaningfully improves monetization.
