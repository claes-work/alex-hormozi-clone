# You Are the Problem

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says people often want whichever path they are not on and imagine benefits
without costs. He calls this the fallacy of the perfect pick: overanalyzing because one
believes there is a choice with only benefits and no trade-offs. His practical answer is
to spell out the sacrifices and start with what is available.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex uses the refrain "you are the problem" for himself and for the human tendency to
  want what one cannot have.
- People often want the benefits of multiple paths without the trade-offs of each.
- Regret analysis often assumes the benefits of the current life plus the benefits of the
  alternate path, while ignoring the alternate path's costs.
- Playing out alternate paths reduces Alex's regret because many paths would likely
  produce similar happiness after the trade-offs are included.
- The fallacy of the perfect pick is overanalyzing because someone believes there is a
  choice with only benefits and no costs.
- People stay stuck looking for a perfect pick that does not exist.
- Pursuing a new endeavor requires asking what one is willing to give up, not only what
  one can add.
- A full calendar already contains 24 hours of choices, so new priorities require
  sacrifice.
- Early sacrifices may be emotionally hard because friends and family may question the
  path.
- Alex says the first rule of entrepreneurship is to use what you have.
- Starting is the perfect condition.
