# 30 Minutes of Sales Training That Will Explode Your Business (2022-01-21)

## Summary

A condensed recording of a sales presentation Hormozi (2022) delivered to the War Room
Mastermind (run by Ryan Deiss, Perry Belcher, Roland Frasier). He lays out three
frameworks he says he used to scale multiple high-ticket phone-sales teams: the
**CLOSER** question-based sales script, the **Conviction** framework (belief corrects
tonality), and the **six-C scaling** framework for duplicating closers across a team.
He frames it as a playbook for founders building a first sales script or scaling a team
past their first $1M–$3M/year, citing $110M sold over the phone across his portfolio.

## Sales frameworks / tactics

### 1. CLOSER framework (the sales-script skeleton)

Hormozi's claim: every "gangbusters" script he has studied is a **question-based
framework** following these steps. Works B2C and B2B, from $500 to $100,000 tickets.

- **C — Clarify why they are there.** Open by surfacing their goal/reason for the call:
  "What made you reach out today? What's your goal? Why is that number specifically?
  Why is that important to you?" Then reflect back ("What I'm hearing is X, does that
  sound about right?") — a milestone that must be hit.
- **L — Label them with a problem.** "We can't cure cancer unless they admit they have
  it." If a prospect says "just wanted more info," reframe: "You're not hopping on sales
  calls all day for fun — is there a problem you're trying to solve?"
- **O — Overview their past pain (the Pain Cycle).** A 4-step loop repeated until they
  feel they've tried everything: (1) "What have you tried so far to accomplish that?"
  (2) "How long did you do that for?" (3) "How long ago?" (4) "Did that work for you?"
  → "What else have you tried?" Repeat. Goal: they conclude "I've tried everything," and
  you validate ("It's not your fault, you're six inches away").
- **S — Sell them the vacation.** The pitch. Keep it **under 3 minutes (180 seconds)**.
  Sell **what** they'll experience, not **how**. Sell **three bullets/outcomes**, get a
  yes/commitment on each, and have a memorized **30-second anecdotal story** ready for
  each in case of hesitation. (See "Sell the vacation, not the plane flight" below.)
- **E — Explain away their concerns.** Handle price, decision-maker, and stall
  objections (detailed below).
- **R — Reinforce the decision.** Added later to the original framework. Post-sale
  reinforcement (e.g., personalized welcome video from the CEO) to raise LTV and cut
  churn/refunds/chargebacks — because customers decide if they like the business in the
  first 48 hours after purchase.

### Sell the vacation, not the plane flight

- Always sell the same final destination ("Maui" = the outcome). Different service
  tiers are just different ways to get there (swim / boat / normal flight / private
  jet). Variables are the speed and quality of the journey; **arrival is assumed**.
- Don't sell modules, macros, URLs, support team ("TSA") — that "sounds like work."
- Example (fitness): frame the program like binge-watching a favorite show — enjoyable
  enough that motivation/willpower aren't required (the reason they failed before).

### Objection-handling (the E in CLOSER)

- **Price ("I can't afford it") → sell value / break limiting beliefs.** Test question
  for the sales team: "If we were giving away Ferraris for $5,000, would they find a way
  to get the money?" If yes, then affordability = they don't yet grasp the value → it is
  **always the salesperson's fault** if a prospect "can't afford it."
- **Decision-maker ("I have to ask my spouse/partner") → 3-step:** (1) **Circumvent** —
  "What if they say no?" Roughly 1 in 3 say "I'd probably do it anyway" → close now.
  (2) **Divert** — "What do you think their biggest objection would be?" Then attack that
  actual obstacle even though the decision-maker isn't on the call. (3) **Past
  agreements** — "Does your partner know you're struggling with X? Does he approve of
  that? Then why would he be against you solving a problem?" Optionally tack on
  **ask-for-forgiveness/permission** at the end of any close.
- **Stall ("I need to think about it") → teach them to make a decision on the call.**
  Walk the variables explicitly: Can the product meet your needs? Greater or lower
  likelihood of the outcome if you work with us? Do you have access to funds (or know
  someone who does)? If still unclosed: "Let's take a card down, delay payment to Friday;
  go ask your partner." (Uses the exaggerated "if your husband says he wants you to live
  a shorter life… call me and I'll tear up the contract" close.)

### 2. Conviction framework (tonality)

- Two simultaneous dialogues: the **words = logic** (logical brain) and the **tone =
  emotion** (emotional brain, which actually decides). "How you say what you say is what
  you say." One word ("Alex") carries wildly different meaning by tone.
- You can **train** tonality (raise pitch at the end to make a statement a question;
  lower voice to signal importance) or **hack** it: **conviction corrects your tone.**
  If you truly believe in what you sell, you'll say it the right way unconsciously.
- Reps who "crush it then tank" often lost belief (bad reviews, angry customer, ethics
  doubt) → their tone goes off.
- **Diagnostic:** ask the rep "How good are the leads?" A believer answers with genuine
  excitement; a non-believer hedges. (John / mortgage-leads floor story.)
- **When a rep gets hot (on a streak), study THAT game footage** — best time to capture
  and replicate their skill.
- Team actions to build conviction: (1) **Re-read fresh testimonials aloud daily** to the
  team; (2) **fix everything you can about the product** — never blame the customer;
  (3) **keep improving it**; if sales dip, bring the head of customer support into the
  sales meeting to detail everything done for customers, re-inspiring the team.

### 3. Six-C scaling framework

How to duplicate the process across reps "in any niche in seven days or less." Educate
reps about the **prospect, not the product** — product-focused sales training is wrong.

1. **Closer sequence** — the question-based script above.
2. **Consistent daily training + conviction** — the #2 highest-leverage item. Team trains
   60 min/day, 5 days/week (his team does twice/day). **Talking:** read the script aloud
   with correct tonality ~25 min; if you mess up, start over. **Drilling** obstacle
   overcomes ~5 min. The only two things reps truly memorize: the 30-second anecdotal
   stories and the objection overcomes. **Listening:** review a 30-min call recording as
   a team — "what went right / what went wrong / what will you do next time."
3. **Call recordings** — record all calls (compliance + "game film"). Recommends **Gong**
   for Zoom (talk-time ratios, question counts, AI analysis; "expensive but worth it").
4. **Communication cycles + feedback** — give **one thing** to fix at a time (golf-swing
   analogy: overloading corrections = paralysis). Cadence: daily training, daily wrap-up
   (pump-up), weekly team meeting, 1-on-1s (weekly for new reps, biweekly for tenured).
5. **Cuts — cut the fat fast.** Sales is sink-or-swim; if a rep can't close in the first
   1–2 weeks they likely won't. Easier to take a "6 to a 9" than a "2 to a 5." Waiting
   six months for someone to "turn the corner" rarely pays off.
6. **Competition + career path.** Run competitions on **6-week cycles** (long enough to
   feel achievable, short enough to feel imminent). Team-based contests (mini-teams of 4,
   drafted to pair best with worst) preserve camaraderie. Career path: milestone bumps
   (50/100/150 deals → per-deal pay rises $500→$525→$550) plus role progression, to
   relieve the anxiety of a repetitive job.

- **Bonus structural tip:** keep **outbound and inbound separate**, with distinct
  **setters and closers** for each, because the prospects differ — mixing lead types to
  one person loses efficiency and conversion.
