# I Sell Branded T-Shirts and Do $6M/Year. How Do I Scale?

## Summary

A live coaching exchange in which Alex Hormozi advises "Liam," a 24-year-old founder of an apparel business projecting ~$6M revenue this year. The business licenses IP (revealed mid-conversation to be anime TV shows), makes branded t-shirts, and sells them via hyped, time-limited "drops" driven almost entirely by paid Meta/TikTok traffic to a waitlist. The founder is stuck at ~10% net profit with inconsistent cash flow because each IP converts wildly differently, and he wants to position for a 4–5x EBITDA exit (~$3–4M) within two years. Hormozi diagnoses three levers: (1) build predictive data so IP performance stops being a guess, (2) reframe the acquirer/exit expectations because multi-billion-dollar buyers don't write $4M checks and the model isn't defensibly complex, and (3) build an anime-creator ambassador program that both generates first-party predictive data and cheaper distribution than paid ads. The exchange is brief and diagnostic rather than a full framework teardown.

## Key claims

All claims are Hormozi's, paraphrased, dated 2026-02-26 unless marked otherwise. Business facts about the company are self-reported by the founder (audience), not verified.

- **Volatility shrinks with data volume.** The more drops you run and the more data you track, the less "wildly different" IP performance becomes — the missing lever is predictive data. (paraphrase)
- **You should be able to answer conversion questions quantitatively.** Just as two people with equal follower counts sell books at very different volumes, you should be able to predict, in advance and numerically, which IP/audience will convert — removing guesswork and volatility from which brands you partner with and strengthening negotiating power. (paraphrase)
- **Predictive data improves negotiation.** Some brand owners overvalue their IP and some undervalue it (the latter more often); quantifying true buyer willingness lets you win better licensing deals. (paraphrase)
- **The creator economy is an under-used, cheaper channel.** Premium TV shows often have smaller audiences than big creators; leveraging existing creator audiences reduces heavy ad spend. He notes a top creator (cites Mr. Beast as example) could sell a full year's revenue of shirts in a day. (paraphrase; example)
- **Creators are abundant and weak negotiators.** There are countless creators, many don't make money, they'll collaborate readily, and they won't be hardcore negotiators — the opposite of premium IP holders. (paraphrase)
- **Bad news on the exit thesis #1:** Multi-billion-dollar media companies don't write $4M checks — it's de minimis and won't move their needle, so they're unlikely acquirers at that price. (paraphrase)
- **Bad news on the exit thesis #2:** The business model isn't that complicated/defensible — "I can just have the show and make my own t-shirt" — which weakens acquisition value beyond the customer list and exclusive IP rights. (paraphrase)
- **Ambassador program as the high-leverage move.** Build an anime-creator ambassador program: each ambassador can promote the other products (functionally your own TikTok-shop of t-shirts), you get early data on which ambassadors have the most pull (feeding the predictive-data engine), and t-shirts are an easy, low-friction thing for creators to sell because they think the shirt is cool. (paraphrase)
- **Scale mechanic:** Recruit ~1,000 micro/nano/mega anime creators, make shirts for the top ~100, run two drops a week for those creators — their own drops pull far harder — while they also promote the premium-IP releases throughout the year (a "double dip"). (paraphrase)

### Founder-reported business facts (audience, self-reported — NOT persona data)
- ~$6M projected revenue this year; model = licensed IP apparel sold via own-channel hyped drops.
- Shirt sells for ~$50, fully-loaded cost ~$12 (~75% gross margin, ~$38/shirt).
- License fee to IP owner ranges 5%–25% of price depending on IP size (~$10/shirt at midpoint).
- Net profit ~$600K (~10%); waitlist-to-purchase conversion ranges ~5% to ~25% by IP.
- Wants a 4–5x EBITDA exit (~$10M+ range implied, ~$3–4M to founder) within two years; sees big Western-anime-IP media distributors as buyers.
