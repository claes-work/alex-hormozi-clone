# The "No Sale" Sale: How to Close 100% of Prospects

## Summary

In this teaching clip, Alex Hormozi reads from a "Lost Chapters" money-model section on "free with alternate revenue stream." The core strategy is to give one product or service away for free, then monetize through a paired or independent upsell that is naturally required to get the desired outcome. Alex traces the idea through Jim Wendler's gym example, storage, weight-loss clinics, physical therapy, software, free books, and Gym Launch/Prestige Labs reactivation campaigns.

## Key claims (Alex's teaching only)

- "Free with alternate revenue stream" can work as a downsell after a prospect says no or as a primary front-end offer.
- The offer gives one thing away for free and monetizes through another product, service, or recurring stream.
- A paired upsell requires the buyer to purchase B to use A, while an independent upsell encourages the purchase of B alongside A.
- The strategy works best when the free item has low incremental cost and the upsell has high enough margin to cover fulfillment.
- If a business advertises something as free, it must actually make the free item obtainable without requiring a purchase.
- The next upsell should be the natural next thing the prospect needs on the path to the outcome.
- Alex says businesses should think three decisions ahead because each solved problem creates the next problem to solve.
- High take rates require the upsell to feel essential and seamless.
- Gym Launch used free 28-day transformations to reactivate members and bring friends, then sold supplements as the alternate revenue stream; Alex says this helped build Prestige Labs.
- The key metric is conversion from free to first upsell; if someone will not buy the first small upsell, later conversion drops sharply.
