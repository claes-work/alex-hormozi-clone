# How to Decommoditize a Service Business

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an $18M employment medical-evaluation business that sells mostly through RFPs to large employers, cities, counties, defense contractors, and agencies. The owner wants more pricing power and broader awareness because buyers often compare providers as commodities. Alex frames the answer as decommoditization through value framing: pick the strongest competitive angle, prove the total-system savings, and add case studies directly into RFP responses so the buyer evaluates risk, speed, and total return rather than lowest line-item price.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- In an RFP-heavy business, decommoditization comes from reframing the buyer's decision around the whole system cost, not only the scoped line item.
- A seller can win RFPs without being the lowest price if the response proves superior risk reduction, speed, or total economic value.
- Case studies belong inside or alongside RFP responses because they make a similar customer do the selling.
- Anonymous or whitewashed proof can still work if it preserves enough meta-detail to feel believable.
- A seller should set the frame early in the document or conversation: buyers usually want the best return, not the cheapest component.
- Once a procurement relationship is open, scope adjustments and consulting add-ons may be easier than getting the initial budget approved.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Whenever you think RFP, think case study close."

> "You want the best return, not the lowest price."
