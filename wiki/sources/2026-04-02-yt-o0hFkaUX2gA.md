# Helping a $200K Service Business Double Revenue

## Summary

Alex recommends that a Hong Kong tutoring marketplace monetize both sides: extend the
student-side placement fee and add a small recurring or annual tutor fee. He also rejects
the claim that organic traffic is exhausted at the current scale and points out that the
owner's own paid-ad figures imply attractive acquisition economics.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports $200,000 annual revenue, 70% margins, 90% of traffic from
  organic search, $200 revenue per student, and $38 acquisition cost; all are unverified.
- As of April 2026, Alex recommends testing four weeks of student fees instead of two,
  potentially spreading the extra amount over time to soften the payment.
- Because tutors persist while students flow in and out, he reframes tutors as durable
  customers who can pay an annual membership or small fee per matched student.
- A tutor joining fee could be credited back after initial matches while still placing
  the tutor into an ongoing paid relationship.
- He says $200,000 in revenue is unlikely to represent the organic-search ceiling; the
  founder may have reached a personal SEO skill ceiling rather than a market ceiling.
- At the reported $38 acquisition cost and $200 student revenue, paid advertising is not
  negative-return on the face of the provided numbers; higher pricing would widen the ratio.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's a two-sided marketplace. You can charge both people."

> "The tutors are your customers."

> "It might just be that your skill set for SEO is capped."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: identify the durable side of a marketplace and monetize it; distinguish channel ceiling from operator skill ceiling; reconcile claimed ad performance with basic unit economics.
