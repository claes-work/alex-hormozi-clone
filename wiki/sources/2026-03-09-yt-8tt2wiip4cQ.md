# Starting a Gym at 23

## Summary
Interview clip (interviewer off-camera) in which Alex recounts opening his first gym at age 23 after quitting a management-consulting job. He frames the leap as driven by **ignorance and luck** rather than calculation — "I didn't know what I didn't know," and admits he "probably wouldn't have advised myself to do that now." The gym came with a **$5,000/month lease** and, going in, his entire customer-acquisition plan was to copy the man he had apprenticed under, who simply ran **Groupons**: that mentor had a 20-year-old, established gym and pulled ~120 people/month via Groupon as effectively free (even paid) customer acquisition. Alex ran the same Groupon and **got nobody** — he was in Huntington Beach / Orange County, the most competitive part of Southern California, saturated with gyms running the same play. What saved him was chance: **two weeks before opening** he attended a marketing workshop where he first learned Facebook ads existed (this was early enough that "no one even knew what they were"). Alex, a self-described anti-social-media "productive mindset" person, learned the tool by accident and it became the only reason the gym worked. The clip's emotional core is **quitting the job**: he stresses it was an *emotional*, not a *math*, decision — he was leaving the entire path (management consulting, business school) he'd spent his life building toward, against a **Middle Eastern father** who called it a terrible idea and stopped speaking to him for a while. The deciding logic: "I would rather accept myself and have him reject me than me reject myself and have his approval," combined with pure defiance — not wanting to give his father an "I told you so." He describes driving **halfway across the country to Ohio** before phoning his father, specifically so he couldn't be talked "off the cliff" over lunch as had happened before, telling him he'd sell or leave his condo — "I'm gone, I'm not coming back." He notes he only quit because he **hated the consulting job** enough ("I felt dead inside") to prefer death-to-his-father over death-to-himself, and speculates that if he'd liked the job he might never have become an entrepreneur. Closes noting he and his father are now good and his father is proud. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
