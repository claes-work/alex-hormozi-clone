# I Hit My First Million. What Now?

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A clip, Alex Hormozi advises a coaching-business owner who has hit their first million and wants to reach the $1–3M/year bucket. His core answer: coaching businesses are rarely supply-constrained, so the constraint is almost always leads — get the offer right (the "purple book," $100M Offers) then drive awareness (the "blue book," $100M Leads). He argues organic can carry a business to that level at very high margin, notes there are escalating "levels of organic" (one channel → all channels → all channels at high volume), and frames the paid-vs-organic camps as each needing what the other has.

## Key claims

- The right focus depends on the business and its constraint, but for almost all coaching businesses the constraint is leads, not supply — "they usually just need to advertise more."
- Sequence: nail the offer first ($100M Offers / purple book) so people are "buying it left and right," then scale awareness ($100M Leads / blue book).
- A small team (e.g. three people — founder plus a VA) can be ideal at the $1–3M bucket.
- Doing it all organic can be "amazing" because it's "almost all margin" — super profitable with CAC near zero.
- There are levels of organic: one channel, all channels, and all channels at high volume — usually far more room to grow than people assume.
- Content "costs less than you think, but more than you think at the same time"; Hormozi says his own content operation costs ~$3M/year, which is "a lot, but not a lot relative to what we make on it."
- Paid-ads operators and organic operators are each right for their own case, and each needs the opposite of what they've defaulted to; organic operators with CAC at zero would be "crazy" profitable if they actually charged money and ran a sales process.
- At the $1–3M coach level, leads are the lever: "if you doubled your leads, you double your business."
