# The First $100K Is the Hardest

## Summary
Solo monologue. Alex argues the first $100K is the hardest money you will ever make — it took him five years, and there is no clever shortcut for the beginner: "you just have to save it one dollar at a time." He concedes smarter, faster ways to make money exist (sell expensive things and "just move the decimals"), but insists the fast path is "factually true, spiritually false": you cannot realistically sell a $10M building for a $500K–$1M commission "when you are broke as [expletive]" — you'd "have to become a different person," which is why almost nobody's first sale is that. He attacks the audience that chases "complex money-making skills" from YouTube while not having "mastered showing up on time" — wanting calculus before simple addition, when the basics are the foundation and therefore arguably *more* important, just simpler to understand than to do. The core of the video is a mindset argument about poverty. A common trait of poor people is that it's "always tomorrow" — they delay pain instead of delaying gratification, flipping the equation (pleasure now, pain later) and then acting surprised when the bill comes due. The first lesson of getting out of poverty, he says, is two words: "It's my fault" — total ownership of everything up to this moment, refusing to blame parents, circumstances, country, zip code, race, gender, or political party. His mechanism: "whatever you cast blame to is where you also cast power to" — blame your mom and your mom controls you; blame Trump or "the radical left" and you hand that thing more power over your life than you keep for yourself. He warns the blame loop is self-fulfilling (more evidence → more confirmation; "the more your life sucks, the more you're confirmed you were right") and invokes Zig Ziglar's "you can either be right or you can be rich." Personally, realizing he was the "bitch" of the things he most hated made him angry, and through that anger he chose to succeed *despite* — "the chips are stacked against me, I will still win." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
