# How to Kill Bad Habits

## Summary

Alex argues that changing environment is often the simplest way to change behavior
because it removes or creates cues. He applies the same principle to work locations and
to his sunscreen habit: place the product where the behavior should happen and remove
the small punishment—oily hands—that discouraged compliance.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says people often overgeneralize a bad experience with a few
  places or people into a judgment about an entire city or environment.
- He reports, without citing a study in the excerpt, that Vietnam veterans' heroin
  relapse pattern reversed the high relapse rate seen when addicts returned to the same
  environment; treat the figures as unverified within this source.
- He says returning to an old environment restores the triggers and cues associated
  with an unwanted habit.
- Alex uses six or seven work locations for different tasks so that each place cues a
  particular mode of work.
- To build a habit, he recommends placing cues at existing "watering holes" and removing
  the friction that punishes the behavior.
- He self-reports keeping sunscreen at three frequently used locations and using a
  dispenser that prevents oily hands.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you want to change your actions, the easiest thing you can do is just change the environment."

> "You eliminate all the triggers and cues that are associated with the habit."

> "How can I cue myself on a more regular basis by changing my environment?"
