# Helping a Concierge Dentist Get Out of the Chair

## Summary
In this live coaching exchange, Alex Hormozi diagnoses the growth constraint for Carrie, a concierge dentist in Santa Monica doing $1.1M/year who wants to reach ~$3M. She names herself as the bottleneck: she is stuck operating as a "provider operator" instead of a "visionary and revenue architect." Alex quickly deprioritizes a mobile/house-call service that is only run one or two times a month, judging it non-core, and focuses on the actual economics — high-ticket veneers, implants, and full-mouth reconstructions ($50K tickets, 2-3 per month) that make up effectively 100% of revenue, sourced entirely by word of mouth.

Alex frames the fix as two moves. Step one: bring the new associate in to absorb ~80% of the lower-value work, which requires transferring patient trust from Carrie to the associate, freeing roughly 50% of Carrie's time. Step two: use that reclaimed time to build a reliable engine for generating leads and converting them, rather than relying on referrals — which he notes are harder to scale for a high-ticket, full-mouth-reconstruction offer.

His primary bet is content. Carrie makes no content today; Alex argues that integrating content capture into the existing customer flow (8-10 patients a day, each producing a visible before/after "reveal") could yield several posts per day at near-zero marginal cost. He points to Instagram accounts with strong "veneer game" as a structural template for reveal-style reels. The prescription is a concrete three-step system: (1) remove herself and insert the associate as provider, (2) capture the customer journey and post it as reels, and (3) connect ManyChat to DM every follower an offer to book. He recommends hiring one editor to handle the formulaic volume, notes content pairs well with paid ads, and tells her that since she is already good at closing, she can "take care of the rest."
