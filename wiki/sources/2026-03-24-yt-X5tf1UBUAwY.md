# Scaling a B2B Product Without Paid Ads

## Summary
In a short live Q&A, a business owner who sells a frozen bar-cocktail mix (pina colada, margarita, etc.) B2B to country clubs, resorts, and high-volume venues asks whether he should invest in brand awareness or chase more accounts. His company did $3.5M in revenue last year, all via word of mouth, with his father as the sole salesman drawing on 20+ years of industry relationships. Alex Hormozi advises against a brand play and instead recommends mechanizing the father's existing outbound sales motion — breaking it into discrete scriptable steps and hiring a team to run it — supplemented by trade shows where the product can be tasted.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that when choosing between doubling down on a proven revenue motion versus starting a brand-new unproven one, he would "definitely" mechanize the existing thing rather than pursue brand awareness.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames scaling outbound sales as decomposition: not "how do you do outbound" but breaking the motion into ~50 pieces — initial contact, first message, second message, the script, the opener, the enticing offer.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that outbound should increase the perceived pain between where the prospect is and where they want to go, so the seller can "insert" the solution into that gap.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends that a product with a strong taste-test advantage should use conferences and trade shows, where prospects can literally taste it, as a high-conversion channel.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises against a brand/awareness play for a company that built itself entirely on B2B, favoring "more of that for now to start."
- Caller claim (unverified): the business did $3.5M in revenue last year, sells a frozen mix that lasts ~2 years frozen and a week thawed, and grew entirely through word of mouth and referrals with the father as sole salesman.
- Caller claim (unverified): the hardest step is getting a prospect to actually taste the product, because buyers dismiss it as "just another mix" until they try it.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "So what stops you from mechanizing his existing outbound motion and then getting like 10 guys to do it?"

> "If I had to pick between more of that thing, right, and this brand new thing that you've never done, I definitely would mechanize the existing thing."

> "It's not like, 'Hey, how do you do outbound?' Like that doesn't work. It's like, 'Okay, what's initial contact look like? What's first message? What's second message? What's the script? What's the opener? What's the offer to get them kind of enticed?'"

> "What's the thing that we can increase the pain between where they're at and where they want to go so we can insert ourselves."

> "There's probably a combination of conference slash trade show that would work really well, too, especially cuz you are a taste — like literally taste it. And it would probably crush in that setting."

> "I just don't think you should go brand now, especially since you built the whole business off B2B. I would just do more of that for now to start."
