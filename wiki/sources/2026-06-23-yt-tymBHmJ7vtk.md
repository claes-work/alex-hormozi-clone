# $51M Doctor Can Cure Anything (And That's the Problem)

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a doctor selling genetic reports and supplements with a mostly word-of-mouth $51M business. The founder feels his message is too broad because he can help many conditions. Alex says the core message is clearer than the founder thinks: problems come from genetic vulnerabilities interacting with environment and lifestyle. He recommends a quiz funnel, influencer recruitment, genetic testing as a front-end offer, and giving affiliates the front-end profit to scale reach while monetizing through segmented follow-up.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - doctor/founder of a genetic reports and supplements company. His revenue, EBITDA, word-of-mouth growth, book, philosophy, and supplement stance are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The founder likely does not need to niche down; he needs a unified message.
- Alex sees the message as clear: problems come from genetic vulnerabilities plus environment/lifestyle, and the company helps interpret and address that.
- A quiz funnel is the natural funnel because the company handles many symptoms and needs to route people to the right educational path.
- The quiz can branch to videos for the most common issues and then recommend physical products or next steps.
- Alex recommends recruiting health/wellness influencers who can preach the founder's gospel after reading the book.
- A low-barrier genetic test can be the front-end offer that influencers drive traffic to.
- Giving affiliates 100% of front-end test profit can maximize distribution while the business makes money on segmented back-end sequences.
- Alex endorses lifestyle-first sequencing before supplements, then jokes about still selling supplements as the final 10%.
