# What Makes a Good Friend?

## Summary
In this short clip Alex Hormozi and an unnamed co-host discuss how to identify a true friend or good partner. They propose diagnostic questions — who you have the least filter around, and who you can sit in silence with — and argue that the best relationships make it safe to be fully yourself, which in turn builds confidence. Alex frames this through his behavioral definition of authenticity. The clip closes with a promo for his free acquisition.com scaling roadmap.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex (and co-host) suggest a better test of friendship than "who is your best friend" is asking who you have the least filter around, or who you can sit in silence with without needing to fill it.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues you should prioritize time with people around whom it feels safe to be truly yourself, because doing so builds the confidence to be that person every day.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that a good partner should help you become more of yourself, not dilute you (co-host offers the "salt brings out flavor" analogy).
- As of March 2026, Alex defines authenticity behaviorally: how you would behave if there were no risk of punishment; if you behave that way in front of someone, it means they either do not or have no desire to punish you.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "We all feel the compulsion to change ourselves to fit in. We adjust our behavior, words, nature, everything in an attempt to be liked, validated, and accepted."

> "The more people who make it feel safe for you to truly be yourself around them, the more confidence you'll have to be that person every day."

> "Authenticity, as I define it from a behavior perspective, is how you behave if you have no risk of punishment. And so what would you do if you could not get punished?"
