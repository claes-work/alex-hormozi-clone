# I Have a Challenge for You...

## Summary
In this clip, Alex Hormozi answers an interviewer's closing "challenge for the audience" by giving his own: define your "input output equation" — the specific input that moves you closer to your goal — and then do 10x more of it. He argues most people fail not by doing the wrong activities but by doing the right activities in far too small an amount, thinking in doubles rather than orders of magnitude (10x, 100x). He claims that to make 100x more money you need roughly 100x more input, and that he now has 10,000x the input he had running his gyms because of leverage. Using a cold-calling example, he lays out escalating levels of leverage — doing the calls yourself, hiring someone to do them, then recruiting a recruiter who compounds new callers weekly — and argues leverage is unlocked by relinquishing control. He frames the entrepreneurial journey as sequentially giving up production, admin, sales, marketing, management, leadership, and financing, and ultimately giving up the desire to be needed by the business, since the business exists for the customer, not the founder. The clip closes with a promotion of his free acquisition.com "0 to $100M+" 10-stage roadmap.

## Key claims
_All dated 2026-02-10. Paraphrased unless quoted. Alex-attributed unless marked._

- (2026-02-10) The first step toward any goal is to define your "input output equation": what you have to do that will get you closer to where you want to go; without it you are working for no goal.
- (2026-02-10) Once the input output equation is defined, do 10x more of that input.
- (2026-02-10) Most people are often doing the right activities but the wrong amount — far too little of it.
- (2026-02-10) People think in doubles, not in orders of magnitude (10x, 100x); to make 100x more money you need roughly 100x more input.
- (2026-02-10) In fitness people have some intuitive context for effort (e.g. working out five minutes a week won't cause weight loss), but in business they have no such calibration.
- (2026-02-10) Alex claims he currently has 10,000x the input he had when running his gyms, achieved through leverage on his input (self-reported).
- (2026-02-10) There are escalating levels of leverage in cold calling: (1) do 200 calls a day yourself; (2) hire someone to make the 200 calls for the same sales; (3) make calls until you land a recruiter who brings a new caller every week, compounding added calls and customers month over month.
- (2026-02-10) Leverage is unlocked through relinquishing control.
- (2026-02-10) The trait that gets someone to quit their job — wanting complete control — is the very thing they must stop doing to grow; the rest of the journey is unlearning that rewarded control.
- (2026-02-10) The founder must sequentially give up production/delivery, then admin, sales, marketing, management, leadership, and financing.
- (2026-02-10) Eventually the founder must give up the desire to be needed by the business.
- (2026-02-10) A business does not exist for the founder's personal fulfillment; it exists for the customer, and the founder is not required — believing otherwise just makes one feel more important.
- (2026-02-10) If you are trying to do 100x more, it is probably not just you — it requires other people.
- (2026-02-10) Alex offers a free 10-stage roadmap from $0 to $100M+ that fewer than 1% of companies finish, broken down by eight business functions (constraint, symptoms, and the steps taken to graduate each stage), applied across software, physical products, service, and brick-and-mortar businesses, available at acquisition.com/roadmap (promotional).
