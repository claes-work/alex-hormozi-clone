# The 5-to-9 Schedule That Builds Businesses

## Summary

Alex says someone trying to build a business while paying large bills should use the
bookends of the day: 5 a.m. to 9 a.m., 5 p.m. to 9 p.m., plus weekends. If that still
does not work, he says the person must examine expenses, lifestyle, and ego. He cites
his own move from a consulting job and condo into a cross-country move, minimum-wage gym
work, and a spare bedroom as an example of stepping back to buy the future.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex recommends a "5-to-9" schedule for building a business around a day job.
- He defines it as 5 a.m. to 9 a.m. and 5 p.m. to 9 p.m., plus Saturday and Sunday.
- He says if someone still cannot pay bills while using those hours, they need to examine
  expenses and lifestyle.
- He frames the question as where one is willing to give up ego to achieve dreams.
- He says he once had a condo, a consulting job, and a nicer lifestyle before choosing to
  get into fitness.
- He says he sold everything, drove across the country, worked minimum wage at a gym,
  and lived in someone's spare bedroom.
- He says unwillingness to step back often means the person does not want the goal enough.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "This is a 5:00 to 9:00 special."

> "Where am I willing to give up on my ego so that I can achieve my dreams?"

> "If you're not willing to do that, you just don't want what you want enough."
