# Helping a $4.5M Clothing Brand Scale to $100M

## Summary
Live coaching Q&A: the owner of a premium women's resort-wear brand ($4.5M/yr, ~50/50 B2B wholesale and B2C e-com, $200–$400 swimsuits) asks whether to keep her luxury line or drop prices to broaden the avatar. Hormozi says keep the premium price and instead obsess over lifting repeat-purchase rate from 30% to ~65%, which is what actually builds a brand and lowers blended CAC. He then details how to build the brand through curated influencer associations without her having to be the face.

## Key advice (Alex)
- Keep the premium price — a premium brand is what everyone wants; don't compete down-market on a $200 bikini that costs $30–40 to make.
- The single metric to solve is repeat purchase: take 30% returning to ~65%. Higher retention compounds via referrals and free sales, drives brand lift, and lowers blended CAC so you can expand into colder markets.
- Repeat purchases and brand are the "equally strong force" that keeps a business alive as you scale into progressively colder audiences (the Gymshark dynamic).
- You don't have to be the face — build the brand through the people you associate with. Pick 5–10 influencers with true followings (personality, not "generic hot chick #6") whose audience matches the luxury buyer, so the customer's associations with that person transfer to the brand.
- Match association to price tier: luxury/"Aspen" faces for a $200 bikini avoid the complaints a "Carnival cruise" audience would generate. White-list those creators for ads and run testimonial/giveaway contests through their base to get two layers of creative leverage.
