# Alex Hormozi KILLER LEG DAY (with Stan Efferding)

## Summary

A rare fitness-domain video in which Alex Hormozi and his wife Leila join pro
bodybuilder and businessman Stan Efferding for a leg-day workout at Sin City Iron.
Most of the training and nutrition content is voiced by Stan (his low-fatigue,
high-rep, muscle-focused training philosophy and his vertical-diet / meal-prep
business), not Alex. Alex's own substantive contribution is a self-reported
biographical note that he hasn't done any fitness content since selling his gyms
~six years earlier, plus an impromptu business-consulting segment where he advises
Stan on how to monetize the vertical-diet IP.

## Key claims

Alex-attributed:
- On monetizing existing IP, he would "start with looking at like what's the highest
  value I can provide to a person and then kind of working backwards from there."
- Recommends leaning toward **B2B certifications**: sell/license the vertical-diet IP
  to hundreds of certified coaches at near-zero incremental cost, who then become an
  **affiliate army** that trains clients and evangelizes the masses.
- Structure: e.g. first three months teaching how to coach, second three months adding
  business acumen so certified coaches can sell clients.
- Unit-economics rationale: "it's just LTV to CAC" — a certification/front-end product
  can have a fifth of the customer-acquisition cost, stacking a high-margin licensing
  product on the front end with recurring revenue (the meals) on the back end.
- Prefers "the simplest business model possible," where certified people evangelize the
  masses rather than the founder doing all the promotion.
- Content/attention tactic: you can change the description on every existing video with
  one edit because old videos still get views daily; the copy above the "see more" fold
  is "above the fold" attention — drive viewers to a webinar, qualify them, then sell the
  cert (he floats ~$10k as an appropriate price).
- Four promotional levers he names: one-on-one outreach, mass-blast lists, organic posts
  (YouTube / Instagram / TikTok), and paid ads.

> ⚠️ Note: The above are Alex's *business* views applied to a fitness product; the video
> contains very little of Alex's own *training/nutrition* philosophy. The fitness views
> below are Stan Efferding's, not persona data.

Stan Efferding-attributed (NOT persona data — context only):
- Now trains "low fatigue": high reps (15s–20s), shorter rest, muscle-focused, avoiding
  joint soreness and general fatigue; says he no longer trains heavy after years of
  overtraining that left him with brain fog and sore joints for days.
- Claims this maintains size and most strength with far less fatigue, protecting sleep,
  energy, and quality of life.
- Prefers greater knee-angle range of motion over hip loading; uses elevated heels and a
  forward-loaded (front-squat-feel) bar setup.
