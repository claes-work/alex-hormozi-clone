# My Thoughts on Legacy

## Summary
Podcast clip. Alex says he doesn't believe in *individual* legacy — your name won't be remembered ("do you know your great-great-great grandfather's name?"), and even a king becomes just another traceable historical figure to whom you're barely genetically related ("0.5 to the eighth… you're just another human"). At that scale, relation generalizes to all humanity, so he is "pro legacy" only as a *humanitarian*: the ultimate legacy is **education**, because we all live surrounded by inventions from people we'll never know. "The ultimate wasted life is one where you learn lessons and pass it on to no one and force someone to truly reinvent the wheel." The interviewer connects this to David Deutsch's *The Beginning of Infinity* / Naval's "wealth is knowledge." Alex adds the inverse (to guarantee someone loses money, keep them uneducated) and a Sanskrit-style proverb about the good vs. bad son (saving money is pointless either way). On his own future kids, he says he'd reverse-engineer the *experiences* that build good character, warning that lifelong ease "increases the likelihood that someone does not turn out good" — he'd rather have them "suffer to become the types of people that I'd want to spend the rest of my life with." Ends with the roadmap promo.
