# Why You Can't Run Two Businesses at Once

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a marketing-services owner serving restaurants and chauffeur companies. The chauffeur niche has much lower early CAC than restaurants, but the test is only two weeks old and LTV is unknown. Alex tells him not to change yet: observe the niche for about a month, measure LTV:CAC, and make sure the attractive CAC is not hiding bad customers. If the economics hold, the owner may need to sunset the restaurant side because a $1M/month business requires paying the price of focus.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Do not pivot solely on two weeks of CAC data; wait long enough to see LTV and customer quality.
- Cheap acquisition can hide a bad customer segment that is easy to acquire because nobody wants it.
- If the new avatar's LTV matches or beats the old business, sunsetting the old customer base may make sense.
- Running two entirely different avatars is unwise for a company trying to scale.
- The price of building the larger business may be giving up half of the current small business.
- Focus requires accepting the near-term loss if the long-term opportunity is meaningfully better.
