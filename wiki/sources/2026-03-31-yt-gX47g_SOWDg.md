# How to Become a Better Communicator

## Summary

Alex defines communication by behavior change rather than information transfer or verbal
style. Clear desired behavior determines what to say; memorable phrasing helps the listener
retain and internalize it; repeated useful instructions build influence through reinforcement.

## Key claims

- As of March 2026, Alex defines communication's purpose as changing behavior, including
  status-oriented speech that seeks different treatment from listeners.
- He filters meetings and corrections through the question of what should change in
  reality, which reduces words and discourages punishment that undermines the desired outcome.
- Alex self-reports retaining an employee who accidentally announced a large Gym Launch
  meal-company launch 24 hours early because the costly mistake made recurrence less likely.
- Alliteration, inversion, rhyme, and similar devices matter because memorability helps
  ideas become usable and eventually feel internally owned by the audience.
- Advice that produces a result reinforces future attention; he contrasts educators who
  prescribe behavior with entertainers whose primary output is continued consumption.
- A small expert audience may monetize better than a huge entertainment audience when
  its members repeatedly act on specific, valuable instructions.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The purpose of communication is solely to change behavior."

> "What is going to change about reality as a result of us talking?"

> "If it rhymes, it means it's true."
