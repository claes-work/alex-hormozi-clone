# You Need to Stop Being the Bottleneck in Your Business

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Winter, who sells fund-launch and financial-services work to technologists and is personally bottlenecking delivery and training. Alex broadens the answer into a 2026 operating thesis: businesses should shift from role-based thinking to workflow-based thinking. Instead of simply hiring another role, founders should decompose work into granular workflows, because AI agents can increasingly perform chunks of those workflows. For Winter's service, Alex recommends breaking training down into action-only steps and building AI agents around the administrative and instructional pieces.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Winter** - the coachee. His fund-launch services, CFO services, client type, revenue, and bottleneck are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- In 2026, organizations need to shift from role-based thinking to workflow-based thinking.
- The future of organizations is less about org charts and more about flows.
- Every function in a business is a series of granular actions; founders often skip the work of defining those actions clearly.
- The example of making one YouTube video can be broken into trend research, ranking, brand/expertise filtering, scripting, outline, intro, hook, roadmap, stories, proof, packaging, and more.
- AI agents are increasingly able to perform individual workflow steps; Alex says Acquisition.com recently tripled media output without increasing headcount because prompts absorbed 30-50% of the workload.
- For training clients, the founder should reduce theory and explanation and instead give the exact actions to take.
- Less explanation can improve comprehension and adherence because fewer words need to be translated into action.
- Alex encourages Winter to learn AI-agent building, comparing the moment to the internet.
