# Why Being New Has an Unfair Advantage

## Summary

Alex reframes being young or new as an asymmetric position. The beginner lacks
experience and repetitions, but has energy, little to lose, time, lower
responsibilities, and unlimited shots on goal.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says beginners have a large deficit in experience and repetitions.
- He says younger beginners often have more energy, can work longer hours, and can
  maintain high focus.
- He says having nothing going for you also means having little to lose.
- He says every board position has an advantage, including starting from zero.
- He says beginners often have more time and fewer responsibilities.
- He says even responsibilities such as children are not a total blocker because
  world-class businesspeople have also had children.
- He says people can usually find examples of others who achieved world-class success
  under worse conditions.
- He tells young listeners the world does not hate them; they are simply not good at
  anything yet.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have nothing to lose."

> "You have unlimited shots on goal."

> "You're just not good at anything yet."
