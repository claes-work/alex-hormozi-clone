# Why AI Agents Will Replace Your Next Hire

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says AI occupies a large share of his current business thinking. His main shift is from role-based hiring to workflow-based automation: instead of asking for an editor, break the editor's job into discrete actions and train agents for each workflow. He says the future belongs to operators who can map every action in the business, describe behaviors in observable black-and-white language, and automate the reducible steps. He also predicts that UX will shrink as agent interfaces replace much clicking and navigation.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Businesses need to shift from role-based growth to workflow-based automation.
- A role should be decomposed into the actual activities it performs across workflows.
- Every business can be mapped as a chain of actions, though the map may be very granular.
- AI should be trained like a human: clear task, examples, feedback, and increasingly specific prompts.
- Vague language fails with machines and humans; instructions must be observable and behavioral.
- Alex says Acquisition.com is working on AI workflows for content, including transcript segmentation, speaker detection, tension detection, edit starts, filler removal, and collapsing to required context.
- Agencies may become more lucrative for operators who can automate delivery workflows because customer demand remains high while operations improve.
- A high-leverage hire can be an AI automation person who watches teams and identifies automatable work.
- Administrative work, invoicing, receivables, and lead nurture are near-term automation opportunities.
- Over the next 12-24 months, Alex expects UX to shrink as people ask agents for answers instead of navigating interfaces.
- Humans plus better technology will beat humans with inferior technology, even though adoption will be uneven.
