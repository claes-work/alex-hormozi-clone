# Helping an Online Coaching Business Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The owner of an online piano-course business ($997 courses; a YouTube tutorial channel feeding an email chain → sales page → webinars; the whole funnel automated) is at $1.4M/year and wants to reach $3.6M. His constraint: he already ranks for almost all the "learn piano for beginners" keywords, so his long-form channel has plateaued — the last ~15 videos don't move total channel views (and therefore leads) even when an individual video does well. A second "piano prank / street performer" channel got views but was pure entertainment and converted nothing. He lays out four options he's debating: (A) make much better, deeper-in-the-weeds content that gets fewer views but wins trust with serious pianists; (B) go all-in on a new platform (Instagram + ManyChat); (C) learn to run ads; or (D) work on the conversion rate of his existing funnel. Alex's diagnosis: conversion-rate work is the **lowest-risk** lever but capped — a couple of 10–20% lifts can compound into a double, yet you can only ever get to 100% conversion, so it can't produce a 10x. If the goal is $3.6M, the single bet he'd make "bar none" to most likely double the business is **run ads**, precisely *because* the owner already has an audience that knows, likes, and trusts him. He explains this with his "big circle, little dot" model: your subscriber count is a big circle, but only a small slice sees any given video, and only a pinprick of those see (and act on) the call-to-action after retention curves thin the audience out — promotion-heavy videos tend to underperform. Ads take the effectiveness of that little dot and apply it across a pie ~10x bigger, because ads reach not just subscribers but every lurker who has watched a video or two and never subscribed — people the interest-based algorithm already knows are interested. That warm-audience effect makes his funnel conversion and metrics disproportionately better than a competitor running the same ads truly cold. On execution, Alex says to "nuclear bomb it": buy everything at once — a course, free content, and an agency — hire an agency but make the deal that he learns what they're doing (paying more if needed), and once ads are cracked he's "bought a money-printing machine." He notes that once you saturate the warm audience there's "an entirely different strategy," but for a $1.4M→$3.6M jump, ads are the play. (Closing acquisition.com/roadmap promo excluded.)

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, improving a funnel's conversion rate is the lowest-risk growth lever in a business, but it is capped — you can only reach 100% conversion — so it can produce a double via stacked 10–20% lifts but never a 10x.
- As of March 2026, for a creator-led business that already has an audience that knows, likes, and trusts them, the highest-probability, lowest-risk lever to double the business is to run paid ads.
- As of March 2026, subscriber/follower count no longer matters because the algorithm has shifted "from social media to interest media" — only a small percentage of subscribers see any given video, and only a tiny fraction of those see and act on the in-video call-to-action after retention curves take effect.
- As of March 2026, promotion-heavy videos tend to perform worse overall (though not always), further shrinking the audience that reaches the call-to-action.
- As of March 2026, ads outperform organic reach because they reach not only a slice of your subscribers but every lurker who watched a video or two and never subscribed — typically a ~10x-bigger audience the interest algorithm already flags as interested.
- As of March 2026, because that broader audience has already consumed your content and gotten value, your funnel conversion and metrics on ads will be disproportionately better than a competitor running the same ads truly cold.
- As of March 2026, once you saturate the warm audience, scaling requires "an entirely different strategy" — but for a jump from $1.4M to $3.6M, ads are the recommended move.
- As of March 2026, his execution advice for a must-do skill is to "nuclear bomb it": buy a course, consume free content, and hire an agency simultaneously — hiring the agency on the condition that you learn what they do (paying a premium if necessary), so that once it's cracked you own a "money-printing machine."
