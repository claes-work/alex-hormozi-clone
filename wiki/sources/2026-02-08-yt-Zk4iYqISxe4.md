# Scaling a Marketing Agency That Works With Martial Arts Schools

## Summary
A live-coaching Q&A. The member runs a lead-generation and appointment-setting agency for martial arts schools, at ~$420K revenue / ~$100K profit, but has been stuck at ~$35K/month with high churn on three-month contracts. He came from a marketing background, stumbled into the niche via a client win, built a repeatable system to fill first-class appointments, and is now torn between pushing the agency or pivoting (he's mused about selling his book of business and buying a brick-and-mortar kids-education business). The coaches (Alex plus a second coach — voice cues suggest Leila) diagnose the core problem as the market itself: martial-arts studios are small, volatile, high-churn businesses, so an agency serving them inherits that volatility and will not be sellable — fixing it would mean essentially starting over. Their prescription: the member has a genuinely valuable, transferable skill (customer acquisition for a specific avatar), but he's applying a "level-eight skill to a level-three opportunity." The fix is to solve for revenue retention / customer stick first — find an industry with a product people don't churn out of (payment processors, CRMs, insurance) — then apply his acquisition skill on top for a full stack. They push him to stop thinking linearly, pick an opportunity "worth the pain" (since building any business is ~50% suffering regardless), start before feeling ready, and learn by doing (even as an affiliate or in-house contractor) using his ~$100K savings as risk capital.
