# You Can't Beat Me at Business, So Play a Different Game

## Summary

Alex reviews a generic money-making landing page and pushes the creator to narrow the
avatar. Specificity makes lead magnets, copy, and content more relevant to the pains of a
particular buyer. He says a niche expert can beat a broader business expert by knowing
the trench-level examples and pains of that niche, even if the broader expert has more
business proof overall.

## Key claims

- Generic "make money" positioning is too broad to convert well.
- Narrowing the avatar is a major strategic decision because all messaging flows from
  the "who."
- The more specific the avatar, the more specific and valuable the lead magnets, copy,
  and content can be.
- A niche operator can counter-position against a broader authority by going deeper into
  trench-level examples.
- Niche content may get fewer views but can generate leads who are more likely to buy.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Everything flows from that who."

> "If I can't beat him at that game, I have to play a different game."

> "Beat me in the trenches."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: avatar-specificity as strategy; trench-level niche knowledge beats broad authority.
