# Why I don't go home for the holidays

## Summary
A walk-and-talk video (originally posted to a smaller private community, later released publicly) in which Hormozi explains, under the working title "success equals ingrained intolerance," why he stopped going home for the holidays. His argument: family and old acquaintances haven't grown the way an entrepreneur has, so they "meet you in your past" and try to speak old identities over you; he finds visits regressive and guards his mental space by limiting them. The back half generalizes into his signature mindset thesis — that limiting beliefs (things spoken over us by others and by ourselves), not tactics, are what hold entrepreneurs back. **Biographically sensitive**: he directly quotes his father criticizing him.

## Key claims (2021)
- Most people don't change; entrepreneurs do (he calls it "growth," not "change") because they have to — so non-entrepreneur family/friends struggle to reconcile the person in front of them with their memory and try to put him "back in the box" (2021).
- Going home requires being "on guard"; afterward he needs about **a week to detangle** and "re-speak" over himself the identity he chooses. He dislikes it and dislikes spending extended time there because "it feels like I'm going backwards" (2021).
- What "the community" (entrepreneurs) is trying to accomplish "is not normal," and normal people won't like it; the higher up in business he goes, the **more intolerant** he becomes of people speaking negatively over him — down to tone ("don't speak to me in that tone") (2021).
- You must **teach people how to treat you** now, because it's often very different from how they treated you when you first met — and it's hardest with family (2021).
- Identity is fluid and can change "in an instant" (cites Tony Robbins that the only moment we can live in is now); our past attributes are "only stories" we can choose to replay (positive) or ignore (negative) (2021).
- Favorite saying he repeats: **"we question all of our beliefs except for those that we truly believe, and those we never question at all"** (2021).
- **Necessity breaks beliefs**: a friend hired 30 people in 14 days because he had to; contrasts a team member comfortable onboarding only 4/month. The bottleneck for stuck entrepreneurs ($5–12M) is "not the business model, it's you" — an unidentified limiting belief; sometimes people are simply "choosing to make less money" (2021).
- Fixing beliefs (not tactics) is the leverage: he says he spent a full day with visiting winners (Chris, Rob, and Barnett) and "didn't tell them a single tactical thing," only surfaced the beliefs they were speaking over themselves (2021).
- Defines **wisdom** as vision — "if two people are looking at the same situation, the wiser person sees things that the other person doesn't"; limiting beliefs are "unseen chains that people have already chosen to tie themselves up with" (2021).
