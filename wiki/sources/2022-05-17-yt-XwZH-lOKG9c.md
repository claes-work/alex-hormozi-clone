# KEYNOTE: Small To BIG. The Big 4 Customer Acquisition Models.

## Summary
In this keynote (delivered solo to a live audience), Alex Hormozi walks through the four frameworks he says took him from $1,036 to over $112 million in revenue in 44 months. He frames all business growth as only two levers — get more customers and make each customer worth more — and organizes the talk around four checklist "frameworks": the math (Client Financed Acquisition, i.e. acquiring customers at a profit within 30 days), the offer (making offers so good people feel stupid saying no, via the value equation and seven offer components), the traffic (six customer-acquisition channels he calls "never go hungry again"), and the money (six ways to increase lifetime gross profit so you can outspend all competitors). He opens with a tactical five-video shortcut to set up Facebook lead-gen in under an hour, and repeatedly emphasizes generating cash in the first 30 days off a credit card so growth never requires outside capital. The material is essentially a condensed live version of the systems later published as $100M Offers and $100M Leads.

## Key claims
- Only two ways to grow any business exist: get more customers, or make customers worth more. Everything else is a subset of these two.
- The four frameworks (his checklist for scaling or acquiring a business): (1) the math / Client Financed Acquisition, (2) the offer, (3) the traffic (six sources), (4) the money / lifetime gross profit.
- Client Financed Acquisition core equation: in the first 30 days you must generate more gross profit from one customer than the cost to acquire two customers plus the cost to fulfill both (2×CAC + 2×COGS). If you can, growth becomes unlimited and constrained only by operations, not capital.
- "30-day cash" is his key tracked metric: gross profit extractable from a new customer in the first 30 days. He uses a credit card as free financing — as long as the money returns plus more within 30 days, he never uses his own capital to grow. (self-reported method)
- Fast lead-gen shortcut: five Google-able steps (run a Facebook lead ad; connect it to Google Sheets via Zapier; give something away free; target a local or lookalike audience; exchange free value for a phone number) replicate what third-party lead-gen agencies charge 5x for; their button-pushers are trained in under 7 days on ~2 hours of video at under $15/hour. (self-reported)
- An offer's job is to decommoditize — force a values-based rather than price-based decision, lifting click-through, opt-in, and close rates multiplicatively (more leads, more sales, higher prices).
- The value equation has four variables: increase dream outcome and perceived likelihood of achievement (top); decrease time delay and effort & sacrifice (bottom). He argues mature businesses (Netflix, Amazon) win by attacking the bottom (removing effort/time), and that selling speed — doing in half the time what others do — is the easiest way to add value.
- Seven offer components he audits: promotion (free/discount/premium), core offer, bonuses, scarcity (units), urgency (time), guarantees, and the "magic naming formula."
- MAGIC naming formula: Magnetic reason why, Announce the avatar, [time interval / container words like blueprint, jumpstart, sprint, accelerator] — lets the same program be reskinned (Fall 6-Week Challenge → Spring 28-Day Blueprint → Halloween 42-Day Detox) to keep marketing evergreen without changing operations.
- Guarantee stacking: pair an unconditional guarantee on one timeline with a conditional guarantee on another (e.g. "if you don't make $10k in 60 days I'll pay you $10k, provided you show up to every call and hit the activation points"). Credits Jason Fladlien's close: "I'm not asking you to say yes or no today, I'm just asking you to make an informed decision, which you can only do on the inside of the program."
- Six traffic sources ("never go hungry again" framework): paid media, earned media, owned media, outbound, affiliates & partners, and referrals. He self-reports having made over $20M from each except earned media (which he was just starting).
- Branded search is his cited highest-ROAS "hack": bidding on your own brand terms returns roughly 20:1 to 40:1 and takes ~10 minutes to set up. (self-reported)
- Referral "golden ratio": get the percentage of customers who refer to exceed the percentage who leave (churn), and growth becomes limitless with nothing else. He self-reports 30% of customers still come from referrals.
- Ask for referrals/testimonials at the customer's "activation point" — the first magical moment — because the favor-doer disproportionately feels goodwill (cites the Ben Franklin effect / airport-favor example).
- Six ways to increase lifetime gross profit (LTGP): increase price, reduce cost of fulfillment, increase number of purchases, upsells (quality or quantity), downsells (turn a no into a yes, or a leaver into a stayer), and cross-sells (the next natural adjacent need).
- "Business genie" exercise: doubling price yields ~6x profit; doubling purchases/lifespan and doubling new clients each double profit, but doubling lifespan is superior because it also doubles LTV and halves marketing cost. Pricing is "the number one lever on how much money you make."
- The strategic point of LTGP: whoever makes a customer more valuable to their business than competitors can outspend everyone, go deeper into each channel, and effectively "create a monopoly" by bidding CPMs so high competitors are priced out. He calls LTGP "the arms race of business."
- Upsell pricing tip: price the upsell ~5x the core offer, because ~20% of customers (Pareto) will buy it, doubling revenue off a single upsell.
- "Fast beats free" — the only thing that beats a free competitor is speed (Chipotle free-burrito line example).
- Self-reported company results cited as proof of the frameworks: Gym Launch $0 → $2.4M/month in 18 months at 74% net margins; Prestige Labs $0 → $1.7M in 6 months; ALAN $0 → $1.4M in 6 months; a recent acquisition $40k → $700k/month in 10 months; scaled his own gyms 0 → 6 locations in under 3 years off cash flow as a 23-year-old, then lost everything when a business partner was "indicted for fraud"; ~1,000+ success stories and 71 millionaires created; Prestige Labs runs 4,000 brick-and-mortar affiliates; ALAN has ~400 white-label agencies.
- Agency offer case study (self-reported): switching agencies to a pay-per-show model plus quadrupling price took upfront return from ~$5k on $10k ad spend to ~$112k (an 11.2:1 return before back-end), driven by a 2.5x response rate and 2.3x close rate from a better offer.
- Gym offer origin (self-reported): switching from a $99/month boot camp to a free 6-week challenge with a $600 deposit (rebated toward membership) plus a 48-hour $300 supplement upsell dropped CAC to a tenth and returned 30:1 upfront before membership dues — which funded opening six gyms.
