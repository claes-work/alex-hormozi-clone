# 'How do you get what you want out of a negotiation?'

## Summary

Alex answers that negotiation power comes mostly from the conditions before the table:
need nothing from the other person and have something they want. He places tactics like
anchoring and logrolling below the order-of-magnitude forces of supply, demand, and
leverage. The best negotiation is one where the person has enough demand and alternatives
to state terms plainly.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-18, Alex says the simplest negotiation rule is to need nothing from the other
  person while having something they want.
- He says negotiation tactics matter incrementally, but order-of-magnitude improvements
  come from supply and demand.
- He says employment, vendor, dating, and other negotiations are all games of who needs
  the other person less, given relative demand.
- He says negotiations are won before the table; if he has no cards, he would rather fix
  the conditions than sit down.
- He frames strong leverage as the point where the person can state the terms and walk to
  the next option if those terms are not accepted.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Don't need anything from the other person and have something that they want."

> "The order of magnitude improvements in negotiations happen from supply and demand."

> "All of the negotiations are won before you sit down at the table."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: negotiation power from pre-table supply/demand and leverage; tactics
as incremental; fix conditions before negotiating.
