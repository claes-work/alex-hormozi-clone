# How to Appear 50 IQ Points Smarter in Meetings with Just 20 Minutes

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that preparation produces a disproportionate appearance of intelligence
because the bar for excellence is low. He describes one of his few consulting days, where
he spent four hours reviewing a client's pages, landing pages, offers, and funnel, then
arrived with seven pages of money-making recommendations. He also gives a simple focus
tool: a kitchen timer that tracks actual time on task.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Twenty minutes of preparation can make someone appear dramatically smarter in a meeting.
- Alex says he has done only three consulting days and prepared four hours for one by
  reviewing the client's pages, offers, and funnel.
- The goal was to make sure the client received more value than Alex received.
- The bar for preparation and excellence is low enough that modest prep can feel shocking.
- A simple kitchen timer can reveal real time on task versus time merely sitting near work.
- Stopping the timer when checking phone or Slack makes the work measurement honest.
- Earlier in his career, Alex spent long hours at a computer with few real minutes on task.
- Starting work beats spending time getting in the mood to work.
- Define what evidence would make success unreasonable not to happen, then do those actions.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's amazing how much smarter you can appear with 20 minutes of preparation."

> "Starting is the perfect condition."
