# He Makes $4M Tax-Free but Can't Scale

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a UAE-based business selling to wealthy investors and entrepreneurs, with about $6M revenue and $4M profit, where the founder personally drives most revenue despite having seven agents. Alex reduces the constraint to manpower, office capacity, recruiting, and training. The founder must shift identity from being the top salesperson to being the person who recruits, trains, scripts, and brands the firm so many agents can sell.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When a founder personally produces most revenue, scaling requires identity shift from top performer to trainer/recruiter.
- If the economics are strong, a relatively small infrastructure cost should not delay scaling.
- The founder should document winning behaviors into scripts and a repeatable "black box" so agents can be put through the system.
- Great performers often do not know why they win, so codifying behavior is a separate skill from selling.
- Time should be hard-locked for reinvesting in the team even if it temporarily lowers the founder's personal production.
- Long term, the brand should attract already-strong agents who can be made better, not only beginners who need full training.
