# You Have 8 Seconds to Nail Your Intro

## Summary

Alex reviews short-form and long-form content and says creators must obsess over the
opening seconds. TikTok may require the first second, while YouTube can allow closer to
eight or ten seconds, but the introduction should be airtight, clear, and mapped to the
thumbnail and headline. He recommends reviewing top-performing clips, extracting their
hooks, and making several more tactical videos using the same hook structure.

## Key claims

- For short-form, long-form, and ads, Alex says the first seconds are critical.
- TikTok may require the first second; YouTube may allow about eight to ten seconds.
- The intro should have no extra words and should map to the thumbnail and headline.
- Creators should identify hooks from top-converting clips and make more versions with
  the same structure.
- Hook reuse should still deliver tactical value rather than merely copying the opening.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have to obsess about the first 5 seconds."

> "You've got 8 seconds to nail the introduction."

> "Airtight, no extra words, very clear."
