# Stop Selling Done-For-You, Sell Franchises Instead

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a $5M business that helps founders scale high-ticket offers by taking over sales and marketing while the founders fulfill. Most clients are done-for-you / business-opportunity-style offers. Alex warns that the model is hard to scale and potentially risky because the agency sells dreams it does not control fulfillment for. He recommends applying the same sales and marketing skill to legitimate franchise or turnkey businesses with real disclosures, stronger compliance, and better enterprise value.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Done-for-you business-opportunity offers can be gray, heavily regulated, and unattractive at scale.
- A marketing/sales agency that does not control delivery should be cautious about selling dreams the underlying product cannot fulfill.
- Scaling to 50 small clients may produce a worse business than finding a few legitimate partners with real product-market fit.
- Franchises can be a better application of high-ticket sales skill because they are regulated, have earnings disclosures, and can create enterprise value.
- Many franchisors have viable businesses but weak promotion; a skilled sales/marketing partner can become the missing distribution function.
- High-ticket direct-response / offer-world economics may become less durable as CAC rises and trust falls.
- Sales-team churn should be analyzed by cohort: if one out of three hires sticks for years, the model may still work.
- Mission/quality of the underlying product affects sales-team loyalty; mercenary reps leave for small compensation differences.
