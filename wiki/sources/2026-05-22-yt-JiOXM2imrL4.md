# Every Week 20 New Opportunities Try to Distract Me

## Summary

This clip overlaps heavily with the "woman in the red dress" opportunity-cost analogy,
then adds Alex's pain frame: heroes and villains can have the same pain, but heroes use
pain while villains are used by it. Alex credits Donald Miller for part of the
hero-villain setup and says his own addition is the "use pain" versus "used by it"
distinction.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- As success creates more opportunities, saying no becomes more important.
- Alex repeats the "woman in the red dress" analogy for distracting opportunities that
  pull attention from the main objective.
- Better operators get more attractive distractions.
- A post-Gym Launch-sale mentor's billion-dollar threshold helped Alex filter decisions.
- Acquisition.com receives about 3,000 company deal inquiries per month and had done 13
  deals in 2 years, according to Alex.
- Alex says the real no muscle is a yes muscle for the most important thing.
- Heroes and villains can start with the same pain; the difference is what they choose to
  do with it.
- Alex credits Donald Miller for the first part of the hero-villain pain idea and says he
  added the line that heroes use pain while villains are used by it.
