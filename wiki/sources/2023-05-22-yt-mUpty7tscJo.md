# Alex Hormozi's Content Strategy (REVEALED)

## Summary

A stage talk in which Alex Hormozi walks a room of business creators through the content
system that took him from ~600 to over a million total followers across platforms in six
months. He splits the talk into three parts: why he decided to build an organic audience,
the mechanical "how and how much" of his content-creation model, and — the part he
considers the real lesson — a set of observations on why content actually works. The
mechanical model is deliberately downplayed ("lip service"); Alex argues the true drivers
are frame ("why should I listen to you?"), doing before teaching ("how I" not "how to"),
giving away secrets while selling implementation, and patience — delaying the ask so
Goodwill compounds. He repeatedly grounds authority in evidence: he built real companies
before teaching how to build companies.

## Key claims (content-strategy frameworks)

- **Volume is the lever.** After Grant Cardone told him "it's volume," he went from 7
  pieces of content a week to 80. Result over six months: +300K YouTube subs, ~1M total
  audience, ~100K organic site clicks/month, ~400 applications/month to acquisition.com.
  "We 10x the inputs and we got 10x the outputs."
- **Volume × Time = Skill.** His stated "equation of life (or at least business)": you get
  better only by doing it a lot, for a long time.
- **The content-creation model** (five steps): **Test** (public brain-dump — post idea as
  tweets ~5x/day; winners graduate to content), **Record** (threads → long-form, tweets →
  shorts), **Inject** (add call-to-actions — the step that converts eyeballs into results),
  **Contextualize** (reformat per platform, don't cross-post identical), **Distribute**
  (increase volume across all channels).
- **Long-form format:** hook / a question he's answering / a relevant story / a framework /
  an explanation of why it works. **Shorts format:** hook-question / "hammer" (a proven
  tweet) / example / explanation.
- **Virality = CTR × watch time** (credited to a Mr. Beast interview): get the click, then
  fulfill the promise of the thumbnail. He learned by "going to the person who's the best
  at it and listening to them because they've already reorganized the information for you."
- **Frame beats content.** "Why should I listen to you?" is the unspoken question in every
  viewer's mind and the frame the content is consumed in. The same presentation from a
  1,000-follower nobody would land as "stupid, pointless, preachy."
- **"How I" not "how to."** Speak your truths ("here's what I did, if it works for you
  awesome") rather than commanding ("here's what you should do"), which reads as ego and
  preaching. "Everyone is unquestionably an expert on your own life."
- **Do, then talk — in sequence.** Build the thing first, then teach it. Niche down to a
  small pond where you can be king; broaden only after you earn it (his own path:
  fitness → gyms → gym launch → portfolio/frameworks).
- **Give away the secrets, sell the implementation.** Only ~1% act on free content; his
  model banks on that 1%. "If you're not afraid of what you're giving away... you're not
  giving away enough." Make your free material better than others' paid material (credited
  to Tony Robbins).
- **Play games where if you wait, you win.** "Goodwill compounds faster than money." Delay
  the ask; the longer the runway, the bigger the plane. A hard pitch interrupts the
  compounding. Sell without selling.
- **Damaging admissions build trust.** Admitting what you're bad at makes what you claim
  next more believable — "a sentence structure for persuasion," also useful in negotiation.
- **Confidence vs. evidence.** He's against the "believe in yourself" mantra; certainty
  should come from the path behind you (evidence), not bravado. "Confidence is
  self-delusion — it's trying to make up for what you don't have evidence for."
- **What's inside beats the wrapper (80/20).** A cheaply-shot video outperformed his fancy
  studio one — content matters more than production polish.
- **Cost/time to run it:** ~$20K/month per person (Twitter, YouTube, LinkedIn, podcast,
  IG/TikTok editors), ~two of them; his own input is two recording days/month plus ~4
  hours/week of Twitter review. He calls himself a business guy, not a full-time creator.
