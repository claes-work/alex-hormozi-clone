# Go to Bed on Time, Half Your Problems Disappear

## Summary

Alex recommends a bedtime alarm instead of relying on a wake-up alarm. He links this
inversion to avoiding predictable stupidity rather than pursuing vague intelligence.
For young men, he pairs sleep with getting in shape early, arguing that fitness improves
energy, focus, appearance, and social and commercial outcomes while time is cheapest.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says going to bed on time prevents many downstream problems and
  makes waking on time easier.
- He credits Charlie Munger for the inversion of finding where one will die and never
  going there; the line is not Alex's coinage.
- Avoiding clearly harmful behavior can produce better outcomes more reliably than trying
  to identify abstract intelligence.
- Alex claims fitter random groups would outperform less-fit groups across varied tasks
  because of energy and focus; this is his broad opinion, not evidence established here.
- He also claims appearance improves sales, pay, promotion, and content outcomes; treat
  these as dated generalizations.
- Young people should build fitness while time is relatively cheap and responsibilities
  are fewer.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Instead of setting alarms to wake up, set alarms to go to sleep on time."

> "Avoiding stupidity than trying to be intelligent."

> "Time only becomes more expensive as you age."

## Notes

Reinforces bedtime as high-leverage discipline and fitness as an early time investment.
