# Helping a Pet Care Coach Scale to $3M/Year

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the founder of a pet-care coaching business modeled on Gym Launch. The business has demand from organic content and industry conferences, but the founder is the only coach and has created a high-touch delivery model with six cohorts, long weekly calls, daily office hours, and rapid access. Alex diagnoses a supply/delivery constraint, not a demand constraint, and tells her to reduce or restructure delivery expectations before changing the selling model.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If a business cannot take more customers, the constraint is delivery/supply, so changing demand generation is the wrong first move.
- The founder should either find another person for delivery or change the delivery model.
- Over-delivery can come from insecurity about whether customers receive enough value.
- Customers learn to request fast access when the founder rewards fast access with immediate replies and answers.
- Accessibility can be a differentiator when a company is the underdog, but it becomes unsustainable once the business proves itself.
- Delivery decreases can be rolled out like price increases: position the change as a better, different way to fulfill the real promise.
- Alex recommends framing the change from a moral high ground: the current model conflicts with the broader mission, so the delivery model must change to keep integrity with that mission.
- Losing some old-expectation customers can create room for better customers who pay more and fit the new model.
