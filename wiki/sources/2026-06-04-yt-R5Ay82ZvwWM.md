# Hormozi Reveals the Marketing Lie Costing You Leads

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a business doing about $80K/month that wants more qualified leads. The owner is considering low-ticket products to educate beginners into qualified buyers. Alex argues that marketing usually filters for existing ideal buyers more than it creates them. He recommends attracting advanced buyers with advanced-buyer content and lead magnets, accepting higher lead costs if the LTV:CAC ratio improves, and repricing for the better avatar.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Marketing is usually more of a filtration system than an education system.
- Low-ticket offers can shift a viewer into being a customer, but they should not be relied on to turn beginners into advanced buyers quickly.
- The business should attract advanced buyers by speaking to advanced problems.
- Lead magnet, content, and advertising must be congruent with the target avatar's real problems.
- Moving to a better avatar may make cost per lead triple, but the better ratio can still be superior.
- When the avatar changes, the business must expect a re-baselining period for metrics.
- Pricing should adjust upward when the business targets a higher-value avatar.
