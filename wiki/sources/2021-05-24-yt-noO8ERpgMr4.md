# The 3 Evil Es That Almost Killed My Business (2021-05-24)

## Summary

Hormozi walks through what he calls the "three evil Es" of entrepreneurship —
**Expectations, Emotions, Expenses** — the recurring root causes of the biggest
mistakes he sees in the entrepreneurs and portfolio companies he works with. He
argues the three form a self-reinforcing loop (virtuous or vicious) and names
Expectations as the most deadly of the three, since beliefs about what "should"
happen drive everything downstream.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- **The three evils are Expectations, Emotions, and Expenses.** Expectations is
  "what you think should happen," Emotions "affect how you think it should happen,"
  and Expenses are the liabilities/risk incurred along the way.
- **Expectations (most deadly):** Entrepreneurs set arbitrary outcome goals with
  arbitrary timelines, then force themselves toward them — taking shortcuts, making
  hasty high-risk decisions, and often destroying the business. The fix is to shift
  from **outcome-based to process-based expectations**, while staying intolerant of
  anything but excellence in the process.
- **Emotions:** The biggest CEOs (he names Bezos, Munger, Buffett) stay even-handed.
  A tell for being emotional is flip-flopping on the same data day to day. His tactic:
  when he feels urgency (usually fictitious), he *extends the time horizon* before
  deciding — sleeping on it 24 hours — and only makes big decisions from a state of
  **contentment and gratitude**, not scarcity or deficiency.
- **Expenses:** Expenses are liabilities and risk. The core error is hiring/spending
  in *anticipation* of growth. Rule: **let the growth pay for the expenses** — get the
  growth first. Only exception noted: commission-based sales reps.
- **The three form a self-reinforcing loop** that starts at Expectations and spins
  around; it can be virtuous or vicious.
- **Reframe beliefs as "assumptions"** (credited to a different coach) to make them
  more malleable and open to new data.
