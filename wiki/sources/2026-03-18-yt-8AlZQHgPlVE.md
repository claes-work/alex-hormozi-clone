# Helping an SEO Agency Get to $14M/Year

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The owner of an SEO/PPC agency selling websites and paid ads to roofing, HVAC, and home-services companies wants to grow from $7M to $14M in revenue and sell the business for ~$20M; he thinks margin is his constraint (running only ~13% EBITDA at 50% gross margin). Alex first runs his **sales-velocity × LTV ceiling** calculation to size the business's hypothetical max: ~10 units/month at $5,000/month with 5% monthly churn = a ~$100k LTV, ×10 units = ~$1M of new revenue booked per month, projecting the business to a ~$12M run rate. His reframe: the client is currently at $7M but already *pacing* to ~$12M, so he can "change nothing" and get most of the way to his goal — the target of $14M is "in shooting distance," and the real job is not top-line growth but making the incremental ~$5M **drop to the bottom line**. On margin, Alex rejects the client's belief that 50% gross margin is a hard ceiling in SEO: his rule of thumb for service businesses is **80% gross margin** ("if I run service businesses, my rule of thumb is always 80. So I just don't start" below it). He dismisses "industry averages" as a benchmark entirely — American averages are "overweight, divorced twice, going to die at 70," most people are bad at business, and measuring against them "just anchors me to lower standards." On *how* to get the margin, he gives a "12-months-ago answer" and a "today answer": the old answer is **nearshore almost the whole team** to reclaim margin; the current answer is that the client must become an **"AIG" (AI-generated / AI-native)** operation, because ~80% of what an ops-heavy, tech-oriented SEO agency does is automatable *today* with agents and automation tooling (he name-checks getting up to speed on agents and make.com-style automations, the "2.0" successor to old Zapier flows) — everything except account management. He pushes the client hard to stop treating AI as a threat and instead treat it as the thing that threatens everyone else: "Be the big bad wolf, not the sheep." Bottom line: with no changes to pricing or sales motion, tightening operations to ~$6M of margin on a ~$12M run rate makes a ~$20M exit achievable — the client just needs to skill up on AI. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (excluded from claims).
