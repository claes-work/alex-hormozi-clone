# How I Became an Extreme Person

## Summary

Alex discusses intensity as partly natural temperament and partly widened by exposure
to people operating at higher levels. He says entrepreneurship is a path of personal
development because it gives real-time feedback about one's weaknesses, but also
requires deciding whether the next level is worth its tradeoffs.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says the origin of extreme standards is probably a nature-and-nurture question,
  and he does not know the full answer.
- He self-reports always being intense and hearing his father call him unbalanced.
- He says when he found something, he wanted to do almost nothing else until it was
  finished.
- He says observing people ahead of him, including Elon Musk, stretched his horizon of
  what was possible.
- He says entrepreneurship requires many skills and is therefore a strong path of
  personal development.
- He says entrepreneurs get real-time feedback that they are bad at something.
- He says some entrepreneurs decide a current level of success is enough because they
  are unwilling to make the next set of tradeoffs.
- He frames business as one game inside the larger game of life.
- He says one can see the price of a desired thing and still decide not to buy it.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I've always been a really intense person."

> "Entrepreneurship as the single greatest path of personal development."

> "It's okay to go into a store, see something you like, and then not buy it."
