# How to Get Through Hard Times Without Quitting

## Summary

Alex says bad times end and good times end, so "this too shall pass" applies to both.
Hard periods have three outcomes: the person quits, the situation gets easier, or the
person gets harder. He also says today's hard times can become tomorrow's good old days,
and that stress is a fact of life whether from growth, stagnation, or decline.

## Key claims

- Alex says bad times end, but so do good times.
- He says there are three outcomes in hard times: quit, it gets easier, or the person
  gets harder.
- He says two of those three outcomes make life easier, so the loss is quitting before
  seeing it through.
- He says past business problems that once seemed difficult often look trivial after the
  person grows.
- He says hard times become the good old days in hindsight.
- He says people consistently growing are consistently in pain because growth creates
  stress.
- He says stress and problems are facts of life; the biggest problem is deciding that
  having problems is a problem.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You either quit, it gets easier, or you get harder."

> "These hard times become the good old days."

> "Being stressed just means you're alive."

Synthesis candidate: three outcomes of hard times; hard times as future good old days; stress from growth/stagnation/decline; problems not a problem.
