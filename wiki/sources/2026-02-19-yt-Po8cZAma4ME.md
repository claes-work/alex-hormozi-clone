# If You Want Power Over Your Own Life, Do This

## Summary

Short interview clip (Alex plus an unnamed host asking the questions). Alex Hormozi
answers what "low-hanging fruit" people can implement today for long-term payoff:
**shrink the time between making a decision and acting on it.** He frames "power" here as
personal potency/agency — the theoretical maximum being an omnipotent god figure for whom
a thought instantly becomes reality; every step away from that instant thought-to-reality
loop is "less and less powerful," so shrinking the decision-to-action gap makes you more
potent and less "impotent." He then argues most business speed reduces to **speed of
decision-making**, offers a one-question filter for whether to decide now ("will I get more
information?"), and illustrates how making many rapid sequential decisions compounds into
operating orders of magnitude faster — citing Elon Musk as the example. The clip closes
with an ad read for his free acquisition.com/roadmap resource (not persona content).

This restates his standing **potency = minimizing the decision-to-action gap** idea and
the "will I get more information?" decision rule already captured under
[[../topics/mindset/discipline-and-decision-making]]. Note that the persona's other
sense of "power" (SPCL: say-do correspondence, doing what you said you would) is a
distinct construct from the agency/self-control "power over your own life" framing used
here. No genuinely new framing; the Elon rapid-sequential-decisions illustration is the
only fresh detail.

## Key claims (Alex's teaching only; paraphrased, 2026-02-19)

- Shrinking the time between making a decision and acting on it is one of the strongest correlates of high producers (2026-02-19).
- "Power"/potency is defined by proximity to an omnipotent ideal: for a god figure, thinking a thing makes it exist instantly; everything short of that instant thought-to-reality loop is less powerful, so closing the decision-to-action gap makes a person more potent and less "impotent" (2026-02-19).
- The vast majority of speed inside a business comes down to speed of decision-making, because so many decisions must be made every single day (2026-02-19).
- Decision-speed filter: ask "will I get more information to make this decision?" — if no, decide now, because waiting won't improve the decision (2026-02-19).
- People often take 7 days or a month to make one decision; making a decision now, another two minutes later, and a third two minutes after that lets you effectively operate ~1,000x the speed of someone else (2026-02-19).
- Alex attributes this rapid-sequential-decision style to how Elon Musk gets so much done, adding that Musk also distributes many decisions to intelligent people (2026-02-19).

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I think shrinking the time between when you make a decision and when you take action on the decision is probably one of the strongest cor[rel]ates with like high high producers."

> "If you define a god being as omnipotent then as they think things would exist... if I want to be a more potent person or less impotent then I should shrink the time between when I make a decision and when I act on that decision and try to make it into a reality."

> "One of the good frameworks that I have is just asking myself the question, will I get more information to make this decision? If the answer is no then make the decision... you might as well just make it now."

> "If I can make a decision now and then make a second decision 2 minutes later, make a third decision 2 minutes later... I could operate a thousand times the speed [of] somebody else's. And I think that's fundamentally like how Elon operates."
