# Easiest Way To Get Your First 5 Clients [if you have no budget]

## Summary

In this short 2021 solo talk, Hormozi argues that the fundamental client-acquisition
methods reduce to **outbound** (actively reaching out, or paying a platform to put your
message in front of eyeballs) and **inbound**, and that the number-one reason people
fail at either is **insufficient volume**. He introduces what he explicitly claims to be
coining here — the **"Rule of 100"**: do 100 units of an acquisition activity every day
(100 outreaches, or $100/day on ads) for 90 days. His load-bearing point is that
**volume builds the skill** (not the reverse), and that this compounds because getting
better at something makes you enjoy it more, which drives more volume.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated 2021-04-01)

- There are "six ways you can search for clients," but the two he focuses on here are
  **outbound** and **inbound** (2021-04-01).
- **Paid ads are fundamentally outbound** — you're paying a platform for the eyeball
  instead of doing the manual work to get it; the mechanics and the ~1% response/click
  rate are "more or less the same" (2021-04-01).
- The **number-one mistake** he sees is doing **insufficient volume** (2021-04-01).
- He introduces the **"Rule of 100"** and explicitly claims to be coining it — he says
  he hasn't heard it from anyone else: do **100 of something every day** and you'll get
  better at it and do enough to see an outcome (2021-04-01).
- Concretely: if you're not **spending ~$100/day on ads** or doing **~100 reach-outs per
  day**, you're not doing the Rule of 100. You can "add zeros or remove zeros"; the base
  unit is **per working person** (2021-04-01).
- **Skill comes from volume, not the other way around.** People want high skill + low
  volume to get their outcome, but you can only generate the skill by first doing the
  volume; you'll "suck in the beginning," hope to break even, and improve over time
  (2021-04-01).
- It's a **self-reinforcing circle**: volume → skill → more enjoyment → more volume
  (2021-04-01).
- Most people do outbound **once a week or once every two weeks when motivated** instead
  of every day — that inconsistency is why they don't grow (2021-04-01).
- For a solo operator, outbound is the **cheapest** channel because "it's just selling
  your time"; once you have the skill you can teach it to hire and scale it across
  people (2021-04-01).
- Hires won't individually be as good as you, but **10 of them will outperform you**;
  going from you-selling to someone-else-selling can be a "short step back," but you can
  quickly scale one → three → five sellers once you're no longer the bottleneck
  (2021-04-01).
- Prescription: if you're struggling for clients, pick one of the two methods and do the
  Rule of 100 **every day for 90 days** — he guarantees you'll have customers by the end
  (2021-04-01).
