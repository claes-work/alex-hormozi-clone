# Acquiring a Medical Practice as a Physician

## Summary

Alex Hormozi advises a final-year medical student to get into business as fast as
possible and use the medical license as an asset. He points to physician-required
ownership categories, telemedicine, HRT, peptides, medical-student lending, and retiring
doctors who may prefer a successor over a private-equity sale.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex would get into business as fast as possible if he were a final-year medical
  student.
- A medical license can create access to businesses that require physician ownership or
  supervision.
- He sees peptides, HRT, and telemedicine as interesting areas.
- He would look for businesses that require a physician to own them.
- Medical students may have lender access that helps with acquisitions.
- Some retiring doctors may be willing to hand over practices cheaply or creatively if a
  successor will care for their patients.
- Some doctors avoid selling to private equity because they do not want to work for years
  under a new boss after the sale.
- A semi-apprenticeship with the retiring doctor can be a path into the practice.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I would honestly get into business as fast as I possibly could."

> "Your medical license does afford you a bunch of benefits."
