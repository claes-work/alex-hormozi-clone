# How to Protect Your Assets (with Dave Ramsey)

## Summary
In this guest conversation, Alex Hormozi interviews Dave Ramsey about asset protection, entity structure, and estate planning for entrepreneurs whose wealth is concentrated inside the enterprise they run. Hormozi frames the questions from his own situation — he and Leila run almost everything (buildings, apartment complexes) inside acquisition.com, which began as a family office — and asks how Ramsey separates personal investing from the operating business and whether legal/M&A teams are shared. Ramsey (the primary source of the substantive answers) describes keeping all outside investing in separate legal entities, holding the debt-free campus in a children's trust so the growth avoids estate tax, isolating each >$10M property in its own standalone LLC for litigation containment, and using partial-interest LLC appraisal discounts to move assets into the estate plan tax-efficiently. Most tactical claims here are Ramsey's; Hormozi mainly supplies the framing and his own parallel practices.

## Key claims
- (Hormozi, 2026-03-24) For many entrepreneurs the biggest share of their wealth sits inside the enterprise they operate; he and Leila run almost everything — including owned buildings and apartment complexes — inside acquisition.com, which started as a family office and spun up other profit centers over time. (paraphrase)
- (Hormozi, 2026-03-24) He shares the same estate attorneys and M&A attorneys across "personal" and acquisition.com matters, and was probing Ramsey for mistakes to avoid in that shared-resource setup. (paraphrase)
- (Ramsey, 2026-03-24) He keeps all investing outside Ramsey Solutions in separate legal entities for risk management and estate-planning purposes. (paraphrase)
- (Ramsey, 2026-03-24) The Ramsey campus (~$600–700M, paid in cash) was dropped into a children's trust so he is not the owner; while the property has doubled in value, hundreds of millions in growth have avoided estate tax, and Ramsey Solutions is merely a tenant — so a lawsuit against Ramsey cannot reach the campus. (paraphrase)
- (Ramsey, 2026-03-24) Any property over $10M goes into its own single standalone LLC so all associated litigation risk is contained within that property. (paraphrase)
- (Ramsey, 2026-03-24) Partial-interest LLCs held by a child or trust can be greatly reduced in appraisal value, letting more assets slip into the estate plan without taxes; he has done this for ~20 years. (paraphrase)
- (Ramsey, 2026-03-24) Accounting is entirely in-house; taxes, estate planning, and nuanced litigation are outsourced; his real-estate team (not technically Ramsey employees) handles minor legal checks, and specialist work like re-zoning is given to outside specialist attorneys. (paraphrase)
- (Ramsey, 2026-03-24) At 64 he owns nothing personally — not even his cars; everything is in LLCs his wife holds. (paraphrase)
- (Ramsey, 2026-03-24) Putting big branded buildings on the interstate created a litigation "bull's eye"; he refuses to settle and has cases running six to seven years because he will not tolerate what he sees as thievery. (paraphrase)
