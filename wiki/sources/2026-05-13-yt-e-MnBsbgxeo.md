# Helping a $150K/Mo Coaching Business 100x to $15M/Mo

## Summary

Alex coaches a men's transformation business co-founded with Chris Bumstead, currently
around $150k MRR and aiming for $15M MRR. He identifies insufficient creative volume and
ad spend as immediate constraints. To get more at-bats, he recommends moving from about
five creatives per week to roughly 100, increasing daily spend toward CAC, improving the
front-end offer, and optimizing the pixel around qualified applications rather than
closed sales.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Insufficient volume can feel like volatility in a high-ticket business.
- If CAC is $3,500 and daily spend is $1,300, sales will naturally feel lumpy.
- High-ticket offers with high CAC require higher daily spend to generate enough signal.
- Scaling spend creates the next problem: needing more creative.
- Alex says the company should move from about five creatives per week to roughly 100
  per week as a baseline.
- Walking iPhone videos and teleprompter hooks can generate many new ads quickly.
- Winning hooks can be reused with changed backgrounds, creative meat, and static
  variants.
- Increasing spend toward one CAC per day changes the learning perspective.
- The "six-pack in a year" offer is more believable than the old generic "work with us"
  offer.
- For pixel optimization, qualified application thank-you pages may provide a better
  signal than closed sales because closes are too low-volume.
- If trying to grow in half the time, Alex would focus on creative volume and spend.
