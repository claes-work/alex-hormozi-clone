# Helping a Course Business Scale

## Summary

Alex advises a course business moving from a $2,500 eight-week course to a $5,000 annual
subscription with a community. He says to launch the simple offer first rather than adding
downsell/upsell complexity immediately. Then work on retention. If the owner wants an
enduring network brand, Alex says the hard part is quality control and exclusivity: turn
people down. Price helps, but exclusivity ultimately comes from choosing whose money not to
take.

## Key claims

- Alex says to launch the $5,000 annual offer simply and test before adding downsells or
  upsells.
- He says the next focus is retention once people are in.
- He compares enduring networks to YPO and Harvard, where reputation comes from standards,
  selectivity, and who is admitted.
- He says a network's quality depends on the quality of the network.
- He says making the community aspirational over time can create a credential-like brand.
- He says exclusivity comes from turning people down and choosing whose money not to take.
- He says raising price alone may raise the bar somewhat, but it does not replace actual
  selection.
- He distinguishes two paths: short-term marketing/sales to make money versus a decade-long
  brand/network build.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I would just launch with that. Keep it simple."

> "It's all about the quality of the network."

> "Everything of exclusivity comes down to who you choose to not take money from."

> "You can't be exclusive and also accept all forms of payment."
