# Why You're Not Winning (You're Distracted)

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A home-flipping entrepreneur from Fayetteville, North Carolina (near Fort Bragg, ~300-400k population) has built a vertically integrated local empire: ~30-40 flips/year at ~$4M revenue, plus owned HVAC/roofing/dumpster/contracting companies, plus a coaching program (~$15k ticket, ~10 students every 3 months, filled purely by word of mouth via quarterly seminars in a 55-seat venue). He's stressed, "lost count" of his businesses, hired a CEO (Amy) to pull himself out of operations, and wants to take the coaching "national" to fulfill a promise to impact 1 million lives before he dies. Alex's diagnosis is that the owner is **distracted by the grand vision while sitting on an unconquered local market**. His core framing: "in order to conquer the world you have to conquer a country first… before you conquer a country you got to conquer a city. And right now you haven't conquered a city." He warns that "when armies overextend, they collapse" and tells the owner to **fortify his base** — 10 students a month is "nothing," and within his existing infrastructure he could plausibly do an 8x increase in sales (from $4M to ~$32M), and between Fayetteville and Raleigh realistically reach $100M/year. Alex reframes it as "a revenue problem, not the impact problem," and dissolves the local/national tension two ways: (1) **raise prices and tier the offer** — a typical offer like this is a $100k turnkey done-for-you tier plus a $15-25k done-with-you tier, and "9% of people in America have a million dollars" in net worth, so the "no one in my market can afford it" objection is false; and (2) **make content** — the leveraged, free way to impact millions and "scratch your impact itch" without trying to turn every American into a home flipper. The owner's real bottleneck (no content, no paid ads, too buried in operations) is exactly what the new CEO should free him from. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex's central diagnosis: the owner is distracted by a national/global vision while his local market is nowhere near saturated — "in order to conquer the world you have to conquer a country first… before you conquer a country you got to conquer a city. And right now you haven't conquered a city."
- As of March 2026, overextension kills focus: "when armies overextend, they collapse" — so you should fortify your base (dominate locally) before expanding, rather than abandoning a business you just built to chase the next thing.
- As of March 2026, doing ~10 coaching students a month is "nothing" relative to the market; the owner could plausibly get an 8x increase in sales within his current infrastructure (from ~$4M to ~$32M), and between Fayetteville and Raleigh realistically reach ~$100M/year.
- As of March 2026, this is framed as "a revenue problem, not the impact problem" — the impact goal (1M lives) is better served by leverage than by trying to make every American a home flipper.
- As of March 2026, the leveraged path to impact is **content**: making content about what you already do, distributed for free, impacts millions and lets you "scratch your impact itch"; a legitimate business gives you the credibility to talk about it.
- As of March 2026, the CEO hire's real purpose is to take the operational work off the owner so he can do the high-leverage work (content, sales) he knows he should be doing but isn't.
- As of March 2026 (pricing): a typical offer like this is priced at ~$100k as a done-for-you / turnkey tier, with a done-with-you tier around $15-25k; a tiered structure (done-with-you + turnkey) is the right design.
- As of March 2026, the "no one in my market can afford $100k" objection is false: "9% of people in America have a million dollars" in net worth (including home value) — "significantly higher than you think… they just look older."
- As of March 2026, the standard high-ticket local play is to fill a room (~100 people), run it a few times a day, pitch, and close a handful at the ~$100k tier plus more at the $15-25k tier — and the owner should learn paid ads to fill it rather than relying only on word of mouth.
