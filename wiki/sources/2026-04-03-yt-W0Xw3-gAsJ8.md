# Helping a $24M Law Firm Get to $100M

## Summary
Live coaching Q&A: the owner of a US immigration law firm (doing $24M last year, now on a ~$60M run rate) is tempted to spin the internal tech/software he built into a separate product, or to license his "factory" to other lawyers. Hormozi tells him to ignore both shiny objects and simply open the ~5 more offices that would mechanically get him to $100M — a pure focus problem, not a capital or strategy one.

## Key advice (Alex)
- Do not start a software company off a tech-enabled service; you'll never get true SaaS multiples and you'd risk the real business. Keep the tools proprietary to widen your competitive moat and drive higher EBITDA than rivals — tech-enabled service can still fetch ~13–18x bottom line.
- The path to $100M is the thing he already knows how to do: open ~5 more offices (each ~$15M/yr). At $150K to open a location, capital isn't the constraint — focus is.
- Beware disguising avoidance as strategy: "do more so I do new so I could do more." Starting a new business to dodge the friction of the current constraint is a near-universal trap.
- Ask "what's the one thing that, if true, would make all my worries go away?" — then just do that. At every level the opportunity you get to say no to looks juicier because you already have a base.
