# The Grand Slam Offer for Employees

## Summary

Alex applies the Grand Slam Offer idea to recruiting. He says Acquisition.com competes
for talent with growth, impact, meritocracy, demand, mission, and strong pay, not just
cash. Recruiting becomes a reverse funnel: acquiring talent uses many of the same
value-proposition principles as acquiring customers.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says companies competing with tech or AI firms may not match every equity-style
  perk, so they need a distinct employee value proposition.
- He says Acquisition.com's number-one draw for employees is growth.
- He says fast company growth creates career advancement and opportunity.
- He describes Acquisition.com as meritocratic, with movement possible independent of
  age and tenure.
- He says growth can become a virtuous cycle: faster growth attracts stronger people,
  who help the company grow faster.
- He says guaranteed demand can make a role less risky because the question becomes
  best use of demand, not whether demand exists.
- He says mission-driven candidates who have consumed the content may be excited when
  recruited.
- He frames talent acquisition as a reverse funnel and asks what the employee version
  of a Grand Slam Offer is.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The number one thing that they come for, it's growth."

> "You have to grow fast."

> "What's the grand slam offer from an employee perspective?"
