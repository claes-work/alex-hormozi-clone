# What Makes Something Valuable?

## Summary
Short Q&A clip. An interviewer asks how to find out what is valuable to your marketplace. Alex gives a two-part answer. First, the pragmatic market test: **you find out what people value by asking them to buy it — if they pay, they found value in it.** Second, the theoretical model behind it — he restates the **Value Equation** from his book ($100M Offers). Value has four components: the **dream outcome** (what people ultimately want), reduced by three detractors that "pull away" from it — **time delay**, **effort/sacrifice**, and **(low) perceived likelihood of achievement.** He illustrates with fitness. Selling personal training to get a six-pack is a hard sell because it maximizes every detractor: slow (takes years), demands daily time, forces you to start things you don't want to do (workout) and stop things you do want (tacos, weekend drinking), and even after all the sacrifice you might miss the outcome due to bad genetics — "that's why you have to spend an hour arm wrestling somebody to sign up for personal training." The contrast case: a GLP-1 diet drug paired with a (hypothetical/emerging) muscle-preserving drug delivers the same physique outcome but is **fast, virtually guaranteed, and requires no willpower** — far easier to sell despite arguably being less "earned." This, he says, is "fundamentally how you differentiate value."

- As of February 2026, comparing these two offers is "fundamentally how you differentiate value" — the more valuable offer is the one that maximizes dream outcome while minimizing time, effort, and uncertainty.
