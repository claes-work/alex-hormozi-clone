# Are You Trying or Are You Moving Mountains? | Hormozi Hotline

## Summary
Hormozi Hotline clip. A Dutch medical-practice owner (a podiatry practice) opens by asking what AI he should implement first. Alex reframes it as a strategy question, not an AI question, and walks the caller down his **constraint-chain** questioning: What's stopping you doing more? → Capacity. → What stops you increasing capacity? → Can't find/hire podiatrists in the Netherlands. The caller says he's "tried for a couple of years" — hiring a different recruitment bureau each quarter for a few thousand each, offering 30–40% higher pay — and every bureau reported that podiatrists won't switch. Alex pushes back hard: he doesn't buy that *zero* candidates would even take an interview at 30–40% more pay (especially given Dutch tax rates), suspects the recruiters were mediocre, and insists the real issue is effort and incentives. His prescription: add a **relocation bonus** and more incentives, run **LinkedIn ads and Indeed ads**, do direct outreach, and pursue many attack vectors (internship programs, fresh graduates before they sign elsewhere, podiatrists already relocating to the Netherlands). The core lesson, borrowing **Leila's line** — "are you trying or are you moving mountains?" — is that when one single thing constrains your business you must hit it with "an atomic bomb," not 10% effort; you blow a hole in the wall, you don't knock on the door. Alex grounds it with his own recent example: to unlock big upcoming plans he ran the identical questioning chain on himself, found the constraint was six missing C-suite executives, and he, Leila, and Sheron spent 8 weeks of all their free time and filled all six roles — and almost none of those hires knew who he was (so it wasn't brand). He refuses to let the caller accept his excuse as valid: others in his market have bigger businesses, "they're not made of different flesh and blood," so it's doable — "how bad do you want it?" Two follow-up AI questions: (2) dedicate **4 hours a week minimum, one block, one task** to automating/amplifying a single task — lock in with zero distractions (a Sunday), the first attempt is hardest then it compounds ("people much dumber than you have figured this out"); (3) to get better AI ideation, **ask the AI to write the prompt** ("what prompt should I give you to help me ideate…"), then fill the gaps and run it. Ends with the 10-stage zero-to-$100M roadmap promo.
