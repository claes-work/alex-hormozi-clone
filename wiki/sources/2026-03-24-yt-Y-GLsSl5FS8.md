# How Do I Know If My Offer Is the Problem or My People Are?

## Summary
In a live Q&A, a ~$3M/year deck builder in East Tennessee (aiming for $10M+) tells Alex he suspects his "ambassador program" offer has become too "slimy" because his salespeople get feedback that it's not a good fit — even though the same offer worked well when he ran it himself. Alex argues the offer is almost certainly not the problem; the reps are simply presenting it poorly, and the business owner has no visibility because he isn't recording sales calls. Alex's fix is to record the in-person calls (with legal disclosure) and listen back before changing the offer, pricing, or marketing.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that when an offer previously worked well for the owner but now underperforms with hired reps, the problem is almost always execution/delivery ("phrasing it wrong or presenting it wrong"), not the offer itself.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the root cause here is a lack of data: the owner had no sales-call recordings, so he had no visibility into what was actually going wrong.
- As of March 2026, Alex urges owners to record every sales call and listen before changing anything about the business, warning that people too often overhaul offer, pricing, and marketing over a problem they haven't actually diagnosed.
- For in-person, at-home sales, Alex's practical method is to have the rep put a phone in their pocket to record, disclosing the recording per local law.
- Caller's claim (unverified): his deck business does ~$3M/year, he learned one-call closing at a prior event and "over-complicated" it so he can't train reps on it, and his "ambassador program" offer discounts the build in exchange for a Google review, signage, and referrals/spokesperson use.

## Notes
Synthesis candidate: Diagnostic heuristic — when a previously-working offer underperforms under hired reps, treat it as a data/visibility gap (record and listen to calls) before touching offer/pricing/marketing; the problem is usually rep execution, not the offer.
