# How to Hire 20 Lawyers and Add $12M in Revenue

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a $15M law firm whose main constraint is hiring enough high-caliber attorneys. The owner says each attorney can produce about $600K in gross profit and recruiting one costs roughly $60K. Alex translates the gap into economics: hiring 20 attorneys costs about $1.2M, can add about $12M in gross profit, and can create large enterprise value. He says the constraint is not economics but recruiting volume, metrics, and resource intensity.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When talent produces far more gross profit than it costs to recruit, the business should scale recruiting aggressively.
- If the firm needs 20 attorneys and currently hires about eight per year, it is years behind demand at the current pace.
- The first recruiting bottleneck is metrics if the owner does not know outbound volume.
- The owner should think in multiples of recruiting capacity: more recruiters, more firms, more outbound, and higher-end search resources.
- Alex frames this as a pure resource issue, not a complex model issue, because the firm already has profit and clear economics.
- He cites a portfolio-company example where the team hired 90 salespeople in 12 weeks by using many recruiting firms and bulk terms.
- For high-touch talent, the company may need to go upmarket to better recruiting firms and effectively buy access to better candidate networks.
