# You're Not Winning (You're Just Getting Used to Pain)

## Summary

Alex Hormozi frames painful growth as exposure training. Every new hardship shows a person a new territory they feared but survived, creating evidence that they can handle more. He compares it to a training partner in a hard set who wants to hear "you're fine" rather than motivational praise. Alex also relays a frame from his father before fraternity hazing: nothing they could do was worse than what he had already endured.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Fast growth periods are often miserable.
- Progress requires getting used to pain.
- Each new challenge proves the person can survive more than they previously knew.
- Hardship can function like exposure therapy for workload and fear.
- "You're fine" can be more useful than hype because it normalizes the discomfort.
- A person is living proof they have survived everything they have already endured.
- If a new hardship is worse and the person survives, it becomes a new evidence bar.
