# Business is now on 'Easy Mode' in 2026 (here's why)

## Summary

Solo Alex Hormozi video laying out a four-step (plus a "step 4.5")
monetization funnel he claims puts business "on easy mode": (1) get the ad,
(2) capture the opt-in via a good lead magnet, (3) warm the prospect with a
video sales letter (VSL), (4) qualify hard (BANT) before a sales call, and
(4.5) long-term nurture for everyone who doesn't buy immediately. The through-
line is his "high information vs. low information" model of buyers: most people
need 1–3 hours of information before buying, so the funnel's job is to deliver
that information up front — "microwave" a cold prospect to hot in days instead
of over a year. He frames building a brand as scaling the base of that same
process. Despite the "2026" title, this is a classic funnel breakdown, not an
AI/leverage-era thesis.

## Key claims

- The four-step process: **get the ad → opt-in (lead magnet) → VSL → qualify
  before the sale**, with a "step 4.5" of long-term nurture for non-buyers.
- As of "last year," the acquisition.com portfolio did over **$250M in
  aggregate revenue** (stated as proof the process works, not a promise of
  outcomes).
- Among customer-acquisition channels, **paid ads scale fastest** ("one guy
  with buttons and good creative could scale it to the moon"); it's skill-based.
- **"Organic + CTA"**: take your best-performing organic clips (proven by the
  algorithm), add a CTA at the beginning, then pay to boost. Free "test shots on
  goal." His current favorite tactic ("absolutely murdering it for us").
- Alternative ad framework: **hook + meat + CTA**. The **hook is ~80% of the
  ad**; have at least **50+ hooks**; steal top hooks across all industries.
- **Four-step persuasion** (the "meat"): more good stuff / less bad stuff if you
  act; more bad stuff / less good stuff if you don't. Four angles on any action.
- **"Think in moments"** — don't say "you'll feel better"; describe the specific
  moment that proves it (picking up a grandkid, tying shoes standing up).
  Specificity is what makes copy potent; research Amazon reviews for language.
- Levels of persuasion: L1 = the copy framework; L2 = **who and when** (prospect
  vs. spouse, kids, rivals; today vs. future). CTA must be clear AND
  demonstrated in the ad (congruence → fewer bounces + more perceived
  authority: "when that guy says something's gonna happen, that's what happens").
- **Lead magnets**: "bad lead magnets are dead," good ones work; most suck.
  Solve the **tiniest problem completely** and blow them away (his favorite
  example: "how to know if a girl wants you to kiss her"). Types: (1) one step of
  a multi-step process, (2) reveal a problem, (3) give a free trial/step. Must
  fit the **right avatar** — the customer you actually want, not beginners.
- **High information vs. low information buyers** replaces "emotional vs.
  logical." ~97% need more time/information; only a minority buy off one ad.
  Providing more information to everyone closes more overall.
- **VSL** is one of the highest-leverage sales assets. Two structures: **5 Ps**
  (proof, promise, pain, plan, picture) or **5 Ws** (who/what/where/when/why) for
  better-understood offers — both plus FAQs, proof stack, and CTAs. The VSL just
  pre-answers every question/objection so the sales call is just a decision.
- At Gym Launch, **78% of customers had consumed at least two long-form pieces**
  (~an hour). Insight: force 100% of prospects to consume what the 78% of buyers
  naturally consumed.
- **Add friction, not less** — questions, qualifications, required viewing.
  "It's not that ads don't work for you. It's that you haven't put enough
  friction in your process."
- **Qualify with BANT** (budget, authority, need, timing) in the application/
  booking form, and only take calls with people who pass all four.
- **The "runway/plane" and "microwave" models**: a bigger plane (offer) needs a
  longer runway (information) to take off; compress a year of consumption into a
  few days.
- On **price in the VSL**: "it depends" — he puts it in when demand exceeds
  capacity (adds friction, protects the sales team). Over time he tends to put
  **all information up front** ("they're going to find out eventually").
- **Brand = giving lots of information over a long time.** The pure funnel makes
  money but "caps"; growing the base of the pyramid (brand) is what makes the top
  (buyers) bigger. Non-buyers go into long-term nurture (e.g., a Skool group)
  and re-enter the funnel when ready.
- Repeated book/resource plugs: "Run Paid Ads" chapter (p. 129), Leads book
  (problem-solution cycle, p. 32), free "Your First Avatar" chapter on
  acquisition.com, and a $100M Scaling Grow Map lead magnet.
