# How To Turn Organic Content Into Paid Ads

## Summary
In a MoreMozi mentorship clip, Alex Hormozi advises a caller who runs "Small Threads," a kids' clothing/thrift business with four locations (three new, one operated for 16 years). The caller had dabbled in organic content — landing videos with up to ~2.5 million views but attracting audiences outside their local market — and asks what content to make and how much time to invest. Hormozi reframes the question from content *type* to the *audience's problems*, and lays out a workflow for testing organic content cheaply and promoting the winners into targeted local paid ads.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex advises creators to stop asking "what type of content" and instead ask what will be valuable to the person they want to attract — for this caller, the specific moms they serve, whose problems become the content topics, with the product tied in afterward.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends making many pieces of targeted organic content, treating posting as free testing to see which pieces outperform relative to the others.
- As of March 2026, Alex's core workflow: take the organic winners and turn them into ads; if you don't want a CTA in the organic piece, stitch ~5 seconds of a CTA onto the end of the winner and run that as the ad.
- As of March 2026, Alex says adding organic content to a predominantly-paid business improves conversions via lead nurture — prospects who get on the phone look up the account, see the person is "alive"/real, and convert better.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames good content simply as providing value and solving problems (e.g., ways to save on repairs for kids' torn clothes, how to get stains out), with a soft close for people unwilling to do it themselves.
- Caller-reported (unverified): the caller works ~90 hours/week; raising modeled revenue to $5M instead of $3M and removing a data-quality issue would make the company worth ~$6M; one video reached ~2.5M views but drew people outside their market.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Instead of thinking about what type of content, think about what will be valuable for the person that I'm trying to attract."

> "The ones that take off, you take those, make them into ads. And if you don't want to even put a CTA in the organic, you can just make the organic content. When you have the winner, you take that one and then stitch on five seconds of a CTA at the end and then run that as the ad."

> "It's a really good strategy for anybody... you'll get more conversions on a lead nurture perspective, 'cause people get on the phone, they're like, 'What is this person, who's this person?' Then they'll look at your account. They'll be like, 'Oh, okay, they're alive.'"

> "Basically it's just free testing. So you post as many times you want. You can just see relative to the other pieces that you have. These ones outperformed, great. Now I'll make these into ads. So just provide value. That's easy. Yeah, just solve problems."
