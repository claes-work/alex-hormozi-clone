# Helping a consulting firm doing $15M/year scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The managing partner of a boutique NYC consulting firm (data management, risk, and regulatory-change advisory to highly regulated, data-intensive businesses — historically banking, with Credit Suisse and Silicon Valley Bank as major clients) explains that the 2023 banking crisis forced a pivot toward asset management, insurance, and private equity. The firm was over $30M after three years, fell back to ~$15M this year, and wants to reach $100M by 2029. Operations, people, and finance are strong (~8/10 on Alex's nine-stage scale); **sales and marketing are the lagging "last frontier."** Nearly all leads today are founder-led "warm outbound" — the partner texting people in his network on a whim. Alex diagnoses the lead-gen constraint and prescribes an **enterprise acquisition system built on three channels: trade shows/conferences, affiliate (JV) partners, and cold outbound as the front end that feeds the other two.** Cold outbound isn't used to close deals directly — it's used to book conference appearances and land affiliate/JV agreements; those then become repeatable playbooks. He illustrates the conference play with **Sharran** (former president of Real Brokerage), who 6x'd the company from $200M to $1.2B largely by doing ~220 speaking events a year and selling attendees from stage plus staying two days after. Alex tells the partner he's under-squeezing his ~10–20 events/year — both more volume and more conversion points per event are available if the event is set up properly. On the firm's affiliate problem ("too many alliance partners," poor activation), Alex reframes **affiliates as a second customer base** requiring their own customer-success function and regular communication cadence, tiered by leverage (his GoHighLevel/"Alan" example: SMBs → agencies → super-affiliates with 500–1,000 agencies who could push hundreds with one email). Activation is engineered by reverse-engineering the demographics and behaviors of the partners who actually refer, then building those actions into a recurring activation cadence. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the firm's binding constraint as lead flow into the funnel — operations, people, and finance are already solid (~8/10 on his nine-stage model); sales and marketing lag.
- As of March 2026, an enterprise-level services firm should acquire through a combination of three things: dominating trade shows/conferences, affiliate/JV partners (centers of influence who share deal flow), and warm/cold outbound.
- As of March 2026, "founder-led sales" for this firm is really warm outbound — texting known contacts — which works but is only being applied to ~30 people.
- As of March 2026, cold outbound should be used as the **front end** to secure conference appearances and affiliate deals, not primarily to close end-clients; conferences and affiliates are where the outbound effort is aimed.
- As of March 2026, conferences are attractive because "that's where all the fish are in one barrel," and a properly set-up event has multiple conversion points, so more can be squeezed per event beyond direct deals.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites Sharran (ex-president of Real Brokerage) 6x'ing the company from $200M to $1.2B mainly by doing ~220 speaking events per year — selling from stage and staying two days after to sell everyone who approached.
- As of March 2026, events feed acquisition in multiple ways: direct deals, plus triaging out potential affiliate partners who become the ongoing between-events lead-gen engine — a self-reinforcing cycle (outbound → events → partners + deals).
- As of March 2026, Alex treats affiliates/alliance partners as a **second customer base**; having many partners is worthless without activation.
- As of March 2026, affiliate bases should be tiered by leverage — his software company ("Alan"/GoHighLevel) served SMBs, agencies, then agency-influencer super-affiliates with 500–1,000 agencies who could push 100–500 agencies with a single email — and a customer-success function must sit on top of the affiliate base.
- As of March 2026, partner activation is engineered by identifying the demographics and behaviors of the partners who actually refer, contrasting them with those who don't, and reverse-engineering those actions into a regular activation cadence.
- As of March 2026, Alex notes that "adding more zeros to the thing you sell" (enterprise services vs. $5/mo gym memberships) lets the same warm-outbound motion go much further.
