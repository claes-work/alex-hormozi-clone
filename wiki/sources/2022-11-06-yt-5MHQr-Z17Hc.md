# This ONE Equation Will Make You RICH

## Summary

A recorded AdWorld talk in which Hormozi teaches the Value Equation from *$100M Offers* — value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice) — and then covers guarantees as one of the four/five ways to enhance a valuable offer. The fortune, he argues, is on the bottom (denominator) of the equation: speed and reduced effort.

## Key claims

- Value has four variables written as a fraction: top = dream outcome + perceived likelihood of achievement (create value); bottom = time delay + effort & sacrifice (destroy value). Goal: maximize top, minimize bottom. (paraphrase)
- Dream outcome differentiates whole industries/categories on price; perceived likelihood explains why a surgeon with 10,000 procedures charges more than one with a single procedure for the same operation. (paraphrase)
- The real competitive moats live on the bottom of the equation: Netflix beat Blockbuster and Amazon wins by collapsing time-to-value and effort/sacrifice; "speed is one of the best all-time competitive advantages." (paraphrase)
- The four variables double as copy/headlines (dream outcome, a proof number, time to achieve, stipulations) — persuasion and value should be "married." (paraphrase)
- Offer enhancers: scarcity, urgency, bonuses, guarantees, plus naming. Four guarantee types — unconditional, conditional, anti-guarantee, implied — and you can stack them and tie conditions to the behaviors that actually drive client success. (paraphrase)
- You're selling against inaction/doubt/risk, not competitors; lower the action threshold. Guarantees are a math equation, not an emotional one (more sales can outweigh more refunds). (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)
