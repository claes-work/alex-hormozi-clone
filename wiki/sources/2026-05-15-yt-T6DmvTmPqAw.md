# How I Tell if a Job Candidate is Actually Smart

## Summary

Alex says he evaluates smart candidates partly by the quality of the questions they ask.
A high-quality question shows research, links multiple parts of the business, and probes
an important underlying business issue. Generic questions are not necessarily bad, but
when information is already public, Alex expects better than generic.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The quality of a candidate's questions is one signal of intelligence.
- Strong questions demonstrate research and connect complex parts of the business.
- A candidate asking about revenue retention between media, advisory, and other possible
  vehicles would impress Alex because it shows synthesis.
- Generic questions such as the five-year vision are weaker when the answer is already
  available publicly.
- Public-facing companies should expect candidates to do enough research to ask more
  specific questions.
