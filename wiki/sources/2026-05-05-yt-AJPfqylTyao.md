# How I'd Build a Business in 90 Days With $0

## Summary

Alex says the underused zero-capital move is not a hidden tactic but sustained work.
He frames consistency as hard to observe because only another consistent person sees
the daily repetition. A single workout or work session is unimpressive; the compounding
edge is doing the same basic work for years without immediate reward.

## Key claims

- With no audience, no capital, and 90 days, the main edge is work and consistency.
- Consistency is difficult for outsiders to witness because they only see isolated
  sessions, not the repeated standard over years.
- Longevity is one of the remaining places where people can still beat competitors.
- The reason consistency is rare is that each day presents many reasons not to do the
  work and only one delayed reason to do it.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "No one's willing to work."

> "The only way to witness consistency is to also be consistent."

> "The one workout isn't impressive. It's just that you do it for 20 straight years."
