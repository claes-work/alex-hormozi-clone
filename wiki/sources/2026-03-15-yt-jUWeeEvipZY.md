# Never Underestimate Your Competition

## Summary
Short clip (mostly Alex, with a brief interviewer prompt) on competitive humility. The seed is a portfolio-CEO meeting where someone said "we're way better than those guys," which Alex calls "a stupid thing to say" — not because it's necessarily false, but because you *gain nothing* from the statement. What you actually gain is downside: **complacency**, plus increased odds of looking stupid if the competitor crushes you. His deeper point is about who loses and why: the person "on top of the mountain" has the most resources, highest perspective, most vision — there's "really no reason" they should ever lose, yet history shows they do, and the cause is **hubris/ego**. We lose because it hurts to ask "what if that person is better than me?" He links this to a parallel tweet — "people underestimate how much smarter you can seem if you have 20 minutes of preparation" — and the fighter analogy: fighters who show up unprepared do it purely as an ego play, to *appear* more naturally gifted; but you still win by less than if you'd prepped, so you gain nothing. Alex adds an aside that he'd rather be known for work ethic than natural gift. He frames his Twitter feed as "notes to self" reminders because he falls into the same trap ("we're going to outperform this guy"), and his corrective rule: **assume you're always the underdog** and that the competitor has "a trick up their sleeve you don't know about" — "only the paranoid survive." Ends with the $100M scaling roadmap promo (acquisition.com/roadmap).
