# We hit $700K then crashed to $200K. Why?

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a real-estate wholesaler whose revenue spikes and crashes despite similar lead quality. Alex diagnoses the issue as sales operations and sales culture: script adherence, daily training, management cadence, consequences, and leadership standards are not strong enough to produce consistent outcomes. He also outlines a recruiting process that screens for preparation, coachability, and adherence before a salesperson ever reaches the team.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If lead quality is stable but sales performance swings widely, the constraint is likely sales discipline and sales operations.
- Sales culture is visible quickly because sales has fast feedback loops.
- Script adherence should not be optional; reps who cannot follow the script should not take the calls.
- Daily training, memorization, management cadence, and consequences create consistency.
- If the team will not adhere after the standards are clear, the leader is reinforcing the wrong behaviors or failing to enforce consequences.
- In a sales-and-marketing business, the process must sit above the player.
- The recruiting arbitrage is building a process that turns raw, coachable people into consistent salespeople.
- Screening can start before interviews: send the script, request a short video, test feedback response on a group call, then make offers only to prepared and coachable candidates.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "No one is above the process."
