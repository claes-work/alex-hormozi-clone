# I Baited the Hook With the Wrong Bait

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a life-coach certification training business doing about $3M. The owner has built a 250K Instagram following with relationship content, but the audience mostly wants relationship advice, not certification training. Alex says the business baited the hook with the wrong bait. Because platforms are now interest-based, he says the owner can attract different people by changing the content frame: teach life-coaching frameworks as if speaking to coaches handling clients, not to consumers seeking coaching.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The content is working, but for the wrong audience.
- Interest-based media makes audience shifts easier than older social-graph-based distribution.
- The owner should publish the frameworks and philosophies taught inside the certification, because giving away "secrets" usually does not prevent sales.
- If people implement free material and it works, they often want more.
- Victim-oriented framing attracts people who want to stay in victim identity; changing the frame changes the audience.
- The owner can keep the same "meat" but change the wrapper: frame stories as what a coach should do when a client presents a problem.
- Current relationship content markets the owner as a life coach, so it attracts life-coaching customers rather than people who want to become life coaches.
- A hotline-style format should have coaches call in about their clients, not consumers call in with life problems.
- A two-tier model could sell life coaching first and then certifications later, but if the business only wants to sell certifications, it should frame content for coaches.
