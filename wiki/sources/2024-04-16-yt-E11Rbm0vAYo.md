# Alex Hormozi's secret to reaching his goals 10x faster

## Summary

In this short clip, Alex Hormozi argues that reaching a 5–10 year goal in a single
year is primarily about the opportunity vehicle you choose, because different vehicles
offer vastly different leverage (the ratio of what you put in versus what you get out).
He illustrates with a local restaurant versus a platform that aggregates buying power
for many restaurants — similar hours, radically different upside. He adds that as you
level up, shiny-object syndrome never disappears; the opportunities simply look better,
so the hardest discipline is knowing when to push and when to pivot.

## Key claims

- The way to accomplish a 5–10 year goal in one year is choosing the right opportunity,
  because opportunity is the biggest source of leverage.
- Leverage = the difference between what you put in and what you get out.
- Two ventures can demand the same effort (e.g. ~80 hours/week) yet have wildly
  different potential upside — the local restaurant vs. the restaurant-buying platform.
- When starting out, his main disadvantage was ignorance — he simply didn't understand
  how much leverage opportunity choice provides.
- Stating a hard fact outright (e.g. "you'll never make $100M on one gym") shifts
  behavior toward asking what opportunity could absorb the same effort for a far larger
  return.
- Success then requires being "ruthlessly selective" about which opportunities to pursue.
- Part of why the rich get richer is what they choose to operate in, not only that they
  get smarter.
- Shiny-object syndrome never truly disappears; as you level up, the opportunities you're
  tempted by just get bigger and better.
- One of the hardest parts of entrepreneurship is knowing when to push and when to pivot.
