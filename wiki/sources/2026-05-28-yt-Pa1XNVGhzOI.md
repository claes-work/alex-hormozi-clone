# Your Business Needs Consumable Media

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains why memberships and education businesses need "consumable media" or recurring inputs, not only one-time curriculum. Education loses value after the student learns the thing, so the business needs monthly consumables that feed the system the customer already bought. He uses Gym Launch ads, wholesaling property lists, print-on-demand trend lists, accountability, and community as examples of recurring value that saves work, reduces risk, and lowers churn.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Education has a natural business constraint: once a student learns the thing, the specific lesson's value falls.
- The solution is consumable media or recurring inputs into the customer's system.
- Gym Launch's most valuable product was tested winning ads: Alex says they produced, tested, and distributed ad winners to gym clients.
- Recurring consumables should do work for the customer and/or take risk off the customer's plate.
- Valuable consumables often come from research, analysis, distillation, and packaging into something immediately usable.
- A good membership should provide something customers use monthly because monthly use lowers churn.
- Community and accountability are consumable because people continue using them rather than graduating from them.
- Adding unused bonuses does not make an offer more valuable; it can make it less valuable.
- The business should "pre-chew" complexity and create a magical experience where work happens in the background.
- It is more work to find new customers than to retain the ones already acquired.
