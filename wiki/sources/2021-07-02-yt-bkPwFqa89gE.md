# Unconventional Productivity Habits That Made Me Successful (2021-07-02)

## Summary

In this early (2021) YouTube video, Alex Hormozi describes the small set of
"abnormal routines" he credits for his productivity, framing them explicitly as
a rejection of elaborate biohacking and "productivity hack" culture, which he
argues is often just "procrastination hacks." His core message is that the
habits that stuck for him were the simple ones: plan 1–3 tasks the night before,
wake early and start working immediately, minimize repeated low-stakes decisions,
and prioritize a consistent bedtime over a fixed wake time. He positions
resilience and adaptability against dependence on elaborate rituals, which he
sees as crutches. At the time of recording he cites Gym Launch / Prestige Labs /
ALAN having done "120 plus million" and being "on pace for over 85 million this
year."

## Habits

- **Plan the night before (1–3 tasks max).** He can't sleep until he has decided
  what he'll focus on the next day; he never gives himself more than three things
  to do, usually one to three, and refuses to make rigid rules about the exact
  number.
- **Wake early and start working immediately.** For the first ~8 years of his
  business career he woke around 4:00 a.m. almost every day and the first thing he
  did was start working — no elaborate morning routine besides a cup of coffee.
- **Protect uninterrupted morning deep-work.** Nobody needed to talk to him until
  ~10:00 a.m., giving him roughly six hours (he also phrases it as "first four,
  six hours") of uninterrupted time to move projects forward.
- **Minimize repeated decisions.** He eats the same thing every day for breakfast,
  lunch, and dinner to eliminate a recurring low-stakes decision.
- **Declutter / clear "open loops."** Get rid of accumulated belongings and
  trinkets to reclaim mental bandwidth, which he says yields higher-quality
  decisions.
- **Set an alarm for your bedtime, not your wake time.** He treats a consistent
  bedtime as more important than a fixed wake time; he'd rather sleep earlier and
  wake naturally than force himself up exhausted.
- **Avoid rituals that become crutches.** He deliberately keeps his routine minimal
  to stay resilient and adaptable rather than dependent on superstitions.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021-07-02)

- He does not subscribe to biohacking — cold showers, red/infrared saunas, orange
  glasses, toe shoes, etc. [2021]
- Many so-called productivity hacks are really "procrastination hacks" — people
  spend all their time making lists instead of doing the work. [2021]
- He deliberately keeps a minimal routine because he'd rather spend the time
  working. [2021]
- For roughly the first 8 years of his business career he woke around 4:00 a.m.
  nearly every day, a habit partly carried over from starting in the fitness
  industry. [2021]
- The morning "starts the night before" — clearing mental bandwidth by choosing
  the next day's focus is what lets him sleep. [2021]
- He limits himself to 1–3 focus tasks per day and refuses to make rigid rules
  about the exact number. [2021]
- Repeated decisions with roughly neutral outcomes (e.g. food) are "wasted" ones;
  eating the same meals daily removes that cost. [2021]
- Decluttering possessions returns mental "attention"/bandwidth and improves
  decision quality. [2021]
- A consistent bedtime matters more than a fixed wake time; his 4 a.m. wake time
  actually ranges (3:10 to 5:30 on different days). [2021]
- He can work on four hours of sleep for a long time, but makes better,
  higher-quality decisions when well rested; very wealthy people he knows value
  sleep quality. [2021]
- Successful people keep "wildly different" sleeping hours — he cites a billionaire
  friend who does his best work from ~10 p.m. to 4 a.m. and can't work before
  10 a.m. What matters is finding the schedule where you do your best work. [2021]
- Elaborate rituals create superstition and become crutches — anything giving a
  short-term boost often becomes a dependency; he is "very sensitive" to that. [2021]
- He has personally tried gratitude journals, affirmations, and saying things out
  loud, and the only things that stuck were the simple ones. [2021]
- The higher up an entrepreneur goes, the more success is about the decisions made
  rather than the actions taken. [2021]
- At the time of recording, Gym Launch / Prestige Labs / ALAN had done "120 plus
  million" and were "on pace for over 85 million this year." [2021]
