# How to Learn Anything Faster Than 99% of People

## Summary
Interview clip. Alex is in conversation with a creator-entrepreneur who co-owns Prime and Lunchly (context — Prime, Lunchly, "Jimmy's" idea — strongly indicates Logan Paul); the host asks Alex how he built his business sense and whether he had a mentor. Only Alex's speech is used as voice data here. Alex's "learn faster" angle in this clip is **buy your way up the learning curve and get proximity to people above you**: he calls himself "a product of the alternative education scene" — he "paid everybody," bought "everything… courses, masterminds, coaching," and says that shortcut "a lot" for him (with the caveat that the self-help/guru space has "a lot of sewage" alongside genuinely good material). He notes YouTube wasn't the free firehose it is now when he started, so paid access mattered more then; regardless, the durable lever is "getting around other entrepreneurs who are at your level or above and just learning from them." He reframes another person's *taste* as **pattern recognition** — good taste is having enough stored "this is good / this is bad" patterns that a mismatch registers as a feeling. He closes with a stair-step framing of ambition: growing up in Baltimore (doctor father, not wealthy, "no ballers that I knew"), his first goal was simply to make $100k/year ("you're doing good"), and it's been "leveling up from there" — he's now neighbors with someone who did ~$3.7B in sales and ~$1B in *personal income* in a single year, which taught him "there's levels."
