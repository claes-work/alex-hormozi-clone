# 81 Minutes of Money Making Advice You Needed to Know Yesterday

## Summary

A long-form **compilation** of Alex answering audience/community Q&A (stitched from
what he calls "10 hours" of questions, including a session hosted by a Hungarian
community member named Kristoff). It ranges across what business to start today, the
core skills of money-making, a live brand-theory riff, unit economics (CAC/LTV, churn),
teaching-as-training, market positioning, and how to scale service/agency businesses.
Because it is a compilation, lessons are captured below as distinct nuggets rather than
a single argument.

## Key claims

- **What to build with no money in 2023:** a "boring" local **services** business,
  ideally "around the body" (dentistry, nail salon, lawn care) because those resist AI
  disruption and people value the human exchange. Cites Coco Fit failing by trying to
  automate personal training — the real reason people hire trainers is accountability
  and human contact, not because "they don't know what to do."
- **The "$100k in a month from nothing" playbook:** find a local business selling
  something expensive (e.g. a chiropractor), negotiate a bulk zero-risk rate ("if I
  sent you 100 customers, what's the lowest rate?"), front the marketing capital, work
  the leads and sell at the front desk, and keep the spread. He says he ran exactly this
  after losing everything.
- **Three core skills to make money:** learn to **build**, **sell**, and **lead.** He
  prefers to solve a business "back to front" — build a product people actually keep
  using first, because marketing is a commoditized skill and most businesses are leaky
  buckets with mediocre products.
- **Unit economics over income goals:** the one metric he'd judge a business on is the
  **CAC:LTV ratio**; fix churn before spending on acquisition. For a subscription app he
  targets churn "under 3% monthly" / "80% annually at least," and warns that
  marketing-driven income disappears the moment ad spend drops.
- **Brand = changing behavior via association.** Origin is branding cattle to change how
  observers behave. A brand is a "bouquet" of associations (pair the unknown — logo,
  tagline — with known positives); one rotten flower (a Bud Light / R. Kelly move) taints
  the whole bouquet. Plotted on a 2x2 of direction (toward/away) x strength. A strong
  brand is "the ultimate cheat code" — everyone shows up, no price objections — but it
  takes patience.
- **Four market positions:** luxury (a Veblen good — price *is* the value), premium
  (utility + a bit better, e.g. BMW), commoditized mid-tier ("sucks"), and low-cost
  leader. Most "high ticket" is actually premium; he optimizes the price-to-value
  discrepancy (the Chipotle example) so products go viral and CAC trends toward zero.
- **Teaching is training:** you've only taught someone if their behavior changes in the
  same context. Levers are **reward, punish, extinguish.** He almost exclusively rewards
  because "punishment changes behavior faster, reward changes behavior longer"
  (illustrated by Dr. Cashy teaching an autistic child to read with Skittles).
- **Meta-skills** (learning how to learn) predetermine who succeeds with the same free
  course; he writes books at a low grade level deliberately to raise the activation rate.
- **Theory of constraints** governs where he focuses: find the single weakest link,
  deconstrain it, move to the next. "If we solve that problem and the business doesn't
  grow, we picked the wrong constraint."
- **Scaling services/agencies:** be ruthlessly narrow about the problem and the avatar
  (Gym Launch served only micro-gyms with 30+ members, a lease, and 1+ employee);
  productize so anyone can deliver; price for retention on the client's *worst* month,
  not the best; learn to say no ("that's why most people stay poor").
