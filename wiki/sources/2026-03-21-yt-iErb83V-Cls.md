# You Need to Focus

## Summary
In a short clip, an interviewer asks Alex Hormozi how bad the lack of focus was in his early years when he ran many businesses at once. Hormozi reframes his current 13 "portfolio companies" as clients of a single holding company that he owns, each run by its own CEO and leadership team, so that the holding company itself is his one focused business. He contrasts this with his earlier mistake of personally becoming CEO of multiple companies, which he calls a nightmare, and says he only learned to run multiple things after selling Gym Launch and getting "above the business." The clip closes with a promo for his free $100M scaling road map.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex distinguishes owning a holding company from being CEO of multiple companies: he owns one holding company with ~13 portfolio companies, each staffed by its own CEO and leadership team, so his "one thing" is the hold co, not the individual businesses.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the holding-company model as analogous to owning a marketing agency with 13 clients — except he owns the clients rather than serving them.
- As of March 2026, Alex identifies his earlier mistake as starting a new company every time an existing one made money, ending up as CEO of three companies at once, which he calls a nightmare.
- As of March 2026, Alex says he only understood how to do multiple things after selling Gym Launch, because that was the first time he was "above the business."
- As of March 2026, Alex argues a holding company is a business in its own right that should niche down on a specific type of "customer" (the portfolio companies it serves), just like any other business.
- (Caller, unverified) The interviewer states he previously ran a senior-living business plus an education venture and others simultaneously, and that the guest had run nine different businesses.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "people are like, 'Alex, you talk about focus now, but you have 13 portfolio companies.' But like, I have one holding company. And if you were to reframe it as like, 'I own a marketing agency and I have 13 clients.' It feels that way, except I own them."

> "where I made the huge mistake when I was earlier on is like, I have one company that makes money, I'll start another. And so then you end up becoming CEO of three companies and it's a nightmare."

> "I was only really able to understand how to do multiple things once I sold Gym Launch. Because then I think it was the first time I was really above the business."
