# Helping a $5M Electrical Contractor Scale

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an electrical contractor doing about $1.6M/year who wants to reach $5M but is pulled back into the van when team members are absent. Alex diagnoses the constraint as manpower and redundancy, then reverse-engineers the solution: increase price and strengthen the offer so the company has cash flow to hire backups, then add outbound to create more "wells" beyond the current six or seven HVAC-company relationships. He tells the owner to watch customer behavior more than price complaints.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - electrical contractor. His revenue, profit, customer base, and hiring bottleneck are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If the owner can get more business but cannot handle it, the constraint is people, not leads.
- The business needs enough cash flow to hire redundancy so the founder does not re-enter delivery whenever someone is absent.
- Premium pricing should be supported by an offer that is faster, more reliable, easier, or more guaranteed.
- A 20-40% price bump can be paired with a guarantee that returns profit if promised standards are not met.
- A guarantee with "teeth" differentiates the company from competitors who make promises without consequences.
- Customers will always ask for lower price, faster service, and guarantees; the owner should care more about what they do than what they say.
- Longtime customers who keep buying despite complaints are showing behaviorally that the offer is valuable.
- The contractor has no real marketing function if all business comes from a few HVAC companies.
- Outreach to more HVAC companies can create more "wells" and increase leverage with existing partners.
- Many business fixes are second- or third-order: price creates cash flow, cash flow creates hiring capacity, and hiring capacity solves delivery constraints.
