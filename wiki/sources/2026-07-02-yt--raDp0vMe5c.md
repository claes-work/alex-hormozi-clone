# You're Closing 2% Because You Have No Sales Motion

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises George, whose wildfire-sprinkler company may receive 16,000 insurance-mandated leads. The founder is unsure whether to treat those as one commercial relationship or thousands of B2C customers. Alex reframes the decision around whether the opportunity materially changes day-to-day activity. Since delivery and product are similar, the real constraint is the 2% close rate; installing a sales motion could turn the same lead source into a 7x, 10x, or 20x outcome.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **George** - the coachee. His wildfire-sprinkler business, insurance relationship, close rates, revenue, and goal are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When choosing between customer avatars, Alex asks what the opportunity changes about day-to-day activity.
- If a new avatar requires starting a different business, the founder needs a clear cost-benefit case; if it does not materially change operations, the opportunity is easier to accept.
- A 16,000-lead insurance mandate is still lead generation; the business must close and install individual customers.
- A 2% close rate on high-value leads means the business does not have a sales motion.
- At a $40K-$60K sale, even small close-rate improvements are worth millions.
- If sales motion takes close rate from 2% to 5%, or higher, the same lead source can materially change the company without changing delivery much.
