# Helping a Dentistry Practice Scale

## Summary
Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a cosmetic dentistry practice (custom porcelain veneers / "smile makeovers") who wants to grow from $2.4M last year to $5M per location nationally and is considering opening a third location. Hormozi reframes the problem: rather than adding locations, the owner should saturate existing demand, arguing the two current clinics have ~$25M of latent capacity. He prescribes a local ad strategy plus better monetization of the owner's Instagram following through collaboration posts, regular story CTAs, and staff DM-triage of new followers.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that opening a new location to fix profitability problems is usually the wrong move; the answer is to increase demand and fully utilize existing capacity before expanding. He mocks the reflex to "add a third" when a second is already struggling.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames a visual, high-margin service business as a "mousetrap" model where being the face of the brand is fine, but revenue growth comes from getting more customers, not more locations.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends regular collaboration posts with prominent or semi-prominent people showing off results as the primary brand-building lever for such a business, optionally offering a discount to secure them.
- As of March 2026, Alex prescribes a capacity math check: at ~4 hours per case and two cases per day, each location could do ~10 cases/week, implying ~$1M/month per location and ~$25M combined — roughly a 10x on current revenue with no added capacity.
- As of March 2026, Alex's follower-monetization play: know your net monthly follower gain, have the front desk DM every new follower a quick triage question, expect ~1 in 4 to be interested, converting a following into ~25 qualified consults/month.
- As of March 2026, Alex says collaborations can run across multiple accounts at once — personal page, business/location page, and individual doctors' pages (Instagram allows up to five collaborators).
- Caller claims (unverified): the practice did $2.4M revenue last year across two locations; average case is $15K–$50K (~$20K); cases are all cash; a full case takes ~4 hours; the second location is struggling; the owner has ~6,700 Instagram followers.

## Notable verbatim quotes
