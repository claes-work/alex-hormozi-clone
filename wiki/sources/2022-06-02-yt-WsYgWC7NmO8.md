# Top 10 Fast Business Lessons From My Last 10 Years

## Summary

An early solo compilation video in which Hormozi runs through eleven (numbered "1–11") lessons distilled from a decade of building and observing his portfolio companies. The lessons center on focus, ownership dilution, patience/compounding, brand, simplicity, emotional discipline, talent, risk respect, and customer-centricity — many of which later become recurring pillars of his content and books.

## Key claims

- Most things don't matter and a few matter a lot; you can win by ruthlessly focusing on the few high-leverage levers and getting everything else "wrong" (paraphrase). He credits this to a private-equity managing partner who acquired two of his companies.
- The size of your slice of the pie matters more than the shape — the richest people (Bezos ~10% of Amazon, Musk ~20%/~17% of Tesla/SpaceX) don't own the majority of their companies; making more people wealthy makes you wealthier (paraphrase).
- Big things take time; compounding at ~10% per quarter is a ~40x return over a decade. Cites Andrew Cherng (Panda Express, ~2,600 locations, all company-owned) taking ~45 years, with most growth recent (paraphrase).
- Brand = making and over-delivering on promises; it grants premium pricing power (Starbucks coffee, Nike/Gucci logo examples) and a competitive moat, while negative word of mouth is a stronger "invisible hand" than positive (paraphrase).
- "Simple scales, fancy fails"; "emotions are the enemy"; only take a company-sized risk when inaction risks the company more; sell what the customer wants, not what you want to build (survey example: 85% wanted more of what they had, 15% wanted the shiny new thing) (paraphrase).
