# Helping a Business Consultant Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A UK business-training consultant (Adam) runs a proven in-person model: 12–15 free live events per month (~100 business owners each), a low-ticket offer at the events filtering to ~250 new clients/month, then a three-day boot camp with offers from ~£5K to £30K and ~£5,000 average revenue per attendee — £14.8M last year, targeting £25M. He frames his constraint as **acquisition method, speaker skill, and geography**, and says his focus this year is replicating the pitching skill set across the six-to-eight speakers he's trained (they don't come up to speed quickly). Alex reframes the whole problem: the reason training speakers takes so long is that the pitch **isn't standardized**. His fix is to make the pitch **word-for-word scripted on the slides** — one phrase/sentence max per slide, roughly one slide per 6–10 seconds, visuals paired to each line for comprehension and to hold attention. Once words and visuals are fixed, the only things left to coach are delivery mechanics (pace, volume, when to raise/lower the voice, what to do with the hands). Method to build the master script: take the best pitches ever done, transcribe them, run through AI to assemble a "master transcript" of the most compelling pieces, put it one sentence per slide (words first, then visuals). Training loop: reps at home — a speaker runs the ~90-minute deck 25–50 times, records, sends their best take, gets async feedback (watched at 2x), and only presents in person once good enough — keeping the owner's time low and cutting the biggest cost of the role (a botched pitch). Alex strongly advises against pivoting the model online to chase the constraint: the business already has ~50% margins and knows how to double, so going all-in on standardizing the pitch and training speakers can take profit from ~£7M to ~£14M "with one move" you can execute in a week — whereas competing online would be significantly harder and off their core game. He references his own Money Models book-launch pitch (1,700 slides for a 90-minute presentation, every word on screen paired with a visual) and a company that did ~$70M last year using the same approach. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
