# Helping an Online Service Business Scale

## Summary

In a short live Q&A, Milly, who runs a small online math-tutoring company with her partner Christian, asks Alex how he instills a "get-it-done" attitude across a team. Alex reframes scaling a service business as scaling culture and training: you fire people who lack the values, keep interviewing until the remaining team embodies them, and accept that culture is built through repeated hiring mistakes done fast. He argues anyone can be taught any skill, but the owner's real question is whether the training time is worth the return versus finding someone with higher base skills.

## Key claims

- As of March 2026, Alex holds that scaling a service business is fundamentally scaling culture and training.
- Culture is enforced by removing people who don't fit the values and continuing to interview (often mis-hiring) until the remaining team shares them.
- Alex defines culture as "what you choose to tolerate internally."
- As of March 2026, Alex maintains you can teach anyone anything; the business decision is whether the training time is worth it versus getting better returns on your time from other people.
- The vagueness you can afford in giving directives is directly proportional to how skilled a person already is in that domain — low base skills require highly explicit instruction.
- For a teaching business, the relevant skill is not just competence in the subject but the ability to teach people to teach it, plus the adjacent people-skills; many people decent at math are poor at teaching it and worse at talking to people.
- Basic interpersonal skills (greeting, smiling) sometimes must be explicitly trained, invoking Dale Carnegie and Chick-fil-A as examples of teaching people "how to people."
- Unverified caller claim: Milly states her offer and sales process are good and that the business just needs to do more of it.
