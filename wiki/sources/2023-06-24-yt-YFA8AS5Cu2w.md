# watch this if you're tired of being broke

## Summary

A direct-address video answering "Avante" — a young man Hormozi says stopped him at a
grocery store, working two jobs with a one-year-old, asking how to get ahead. It's a
step-by-step escape plan from poverty: cut all costs to food and shelter, increase income
by out-hustling every job applicant, learn to sell, then flip increasingly expensive
things. The emotional core is "volume negates luck" and reframing rejection and sadness as
information rather than defeat.

## Key claims

- **Three-step escape plan:** (1) cut ALL costs — no eating out, no new clothes for 2
  years, live packed with family/others splitting rent; (2) increase income — apply to
  better jobs (at-home phone sales pays ~$40k with zero experience), drive Uber; (3) sell
  someone else's stuff, then buy cheap and flip, scaling up (cards → furniture → homes).
- **"Volume negates luck"** — the three words he gave his ~16-year-old neighbor. Assume you
  are never going to get lucky and win anyway by sheer volume of attempts.
- **Be undeniable, out-apply everyone:** don't just apply — be the best candidate ever;
  personalize a message to multiple people at each company (HR, head of talent, director),
  add a custom cover letter, then drive over after your shift to ask for a shot in person.
- **Sadness = ignorance, not hopelessness:** "sadness comes from a lack of options... this
  is ignorance not sadness. It means I don't know enough, and that I can control."
- **Curate your information diet / cut poor influences:** "the best way to stay poor is to
  listen to poor people about how to get rich"; if people in your life don't have bigger
  dreams for you than you do, ignore their advice.
- **The Kobe standard:** three practices a day while everyone did one or two — match the
  top guy's effort then add your "get ahead" effort on top.
