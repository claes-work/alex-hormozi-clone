# If You Sell SaaS to B2B, Sell Like This

## Summary
In a short clip, Alex Hormozi answers a founder struggling to sell SaaS to chiropractors by reframing the problem: nobody buys software, they buy an outcome. He argues sellers should sell to the prospect's problems (more time, higher prices, better margins, more leads) and position the product's value relative to those problems. The clip closes with a promotion for his free acquisition.com 10-stage "zero to $100M+" roadmap.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that you never really sell SaaS, products, or services — you sell an outcome; the product is only wanted because a prospect's current problem (e.g., their existing CRM's limitations) blocks the outcome they want.
- As of March 2026, he says the sales move is to sell to the problem and ascribe the product's value relative to how much the prospect suffers from that problem.
- As of March 2026, he frames chiropractor buyers' underlying desires as: more time, more efficiency with patients, higher prices, better margins, a better team, and more leads.
- As of March 2026, Alex promotes a free acquisition.com 10-stage roadmap from zero to $100M+ that he says fewer than 1% of companies finish, broken down across eight business functions by constraint symptoms and the steps taken to graduate each stage.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You're not selling SaaS. You're not selling products. You're not selling service in general. We're selling an outcome."

> "No one wants to buy software. But everyone wants the problems to go away. And so we have to sell to the problem and then we ascribe our value relative to the problem as the chiropractor suffers from it."

> "The problem is that their CRM doesn't allow them to do those things. Yours does. That's why they want to buy yours."
