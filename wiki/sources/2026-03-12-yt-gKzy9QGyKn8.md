# How Local Businesses Can Make More Money

## Summary
Short solo clip in which Alex lays out three levers a single-location local business can pull to make more money. **First, expand the existing location** — get more out of the same four walls by knocking down walls, adding seats, extending hours, or increasing throughput. He cites his own first gym: when it got too crowded he cut class times from 45 to 30 minutes and removed the breaks between classes, fitting roughly 1.5x more classes (and people) into the same space. **Second, add more channels** — if you post only on Instagram, add X, YouTube, Facebook, TikTok. This is his stated exception to his usual "one avatar, one product, one channel until $1M/year" rule: local businesses in a thin market may need multiple channels. In an A/B market you can hit $1M/year on one channel; in a C/D market (he estimates fewer than ~50,000 people within a ~10-mile radius) there simply aren't enough people, so you must saturate the small market by getting in front of people in more places. **Third, open another location** — eventually you max out the four walls, so you duplicate the model. He closes by reframing the "is this a $100M opportunity?" doubt: one store isn't a $100M opportunity, but nailing and then duplicating/scaling the model can be.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, a single-location local business has three ways to make more money: expand the existing location, add more marketing channels, and open additional locations.
- As of March 2026, you can get more out of existing "four walls" by knocking down walls, adding seats, extending open hours, and increasing efficiency/throughput.
- As of March 2026 (personal example), when his first gym got too crowded he cut class length from 45 to 30 minutes and cut the breaks between classes, fitting about 1.5x more classes — and more people — into the same space.
- As of March 2026, his usual rule is "one avatar, one product, one channel until you get to a million dollars a year," and local businesses are sometimes the exception to it.
- As of March 2026, a business in an A or B market can reach ~$1M/year on a single channel; a C or D market (he estimates under ~50,000 people within a ~10-mile radius) will likely need more than one channel because there are not enough people, so you must saturate the small market by appearing in more places.
- As of March 2026, at some point a location maxes out its capacity, so opening a new location is "the eventuality of most of these businesses."
- As of March 2026, a single store is not a $100M opportunity, but duplicating and scaling a proven model can be.
