# The Lonely Chapter Every Successful Person Goes Through

## Summary

Alex frames social pushback as an indicator that someone may be doing the right thing.
He says peers often attack a person who is doing what they know they should be doing,
because that person becomes a reminder of their own unacted-on ambitions. The other
speaker expands this into the "lonely chapter": no longer fitting the old group, while
not yet having the outcomes to fit a new group.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed and speaker-separated, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex says pushback can be an indicator that someone is doing the right thing.
- Alex says other people may project their own avoided obligations onto the person who
  is actually taking action.
- Alex repeats the frame that people root for someone on the way up, then try to tear
  them down when that person reaches the top of their mountain.
- Alex says quitting drinking, staying in to work, launching a podcast, or building a
  business may put someone at the top of another person's mountain.
- The other speaker describes the period between old belonging and new outcomes as a
  lonely chapter.
- The other speaker says knowledge removes the excuse of ignorance, making missed
  actions feel more personally accountable.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The push back you are getting is the indicator that you are doing the right thing."

> "You're actually at the top of their mountain."

> "Ignorance in some regards is bliss."
