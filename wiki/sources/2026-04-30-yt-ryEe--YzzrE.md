# Having ADD Doesn't Have to Destroy Your Business

## Summary

Alex says "side quests" can help visionary or ADD-prone founders protect the main
business, but only after the business is large enough that initiatives take time. A
founder can scratch the novelty itch with projects that do not drain resources from the
main thing. He uses writing books as his own example: they take time and create thinking
space while allowing the Acquisition.com team to keep improving without him making
disruptive changes.

## Key claims

- Alex says small founders should ignore this advice; it applies more once the business
  is larger and initiatives take longer.
- Visionary or ADD-prone founders may need a side project so they do not break the main
  business out of boredom.
- A side quest only helps if it makes the main quest more likely to succeed and does not
  drain resources from it.
- Alex says writing books serves this function for him while still being additive to
  Acquisition.com.
- He frames side projects as a way to pass time while larger business changes mature.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Side quests can make the main quest."

> "They cannot drain resources from the main thing."

> "The side quest only helps if it allows the main quest to succeed even more."
