# How to Learn Faster Than Everyone Around You

## Summary

Alex compares human first-party learning with AI training. After modeling stronger
performers, a learner should create substantial volume, compare the top 10% of their own
repetitions with the rest, apply one or two observed differences, and repeat. Each pass
adds observation density like another coat of paint.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says real-world feedback accelerates learning because action
  produces immediate first-party data.
- After initial modeling, the learner should generate enough repetitions to identify a
  meaningful top-performing subset.
- Compare the top 10% with the rest, infer one or two observable differences, apply them
  to the next batch, and repeat.
- Observation density accumulates in layers; each cycle reveals distinctions the learner
  could not see before.
- Alex says his reputation for simplifying ideas comes from analyzing observable
  differences without attaching hidden meaning.
- Social-influence examples include nodding, interjecting, and stepping back during a
  request; these are his behavioral hypotheses, not independently validated rules here.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Your idea and observation density will increase like coats of paint."

> "The key to learning is observation."

> "What is different about these things?"
