# How to Make Every Decision With Zero Regret

## Summary

Alex says his strongest decision heuristic is choosing the option that creates the more
epic story. Both success and failure can become meaningful stories, whereas the untaken
path produces regret. He extends the frame to failure: repeated high-stakes practice
creates evidence, and starting disadvantages can make eventual success more compelling.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says the prospect of a more epic story is the strongest
  predictor of his decisions.
- He argues that older people regret stories they never created more than epic attempts
  that ended in either success or failure.
- Because people create meaning through associations, he says they can deliberately
  redefine what an event means.
- He recommends practicing repeatedly under conditions close to the high-stakes event
  so past successes create evidence for confidence.
- Alex says willingness to attempt again after misses is what eventually produces the
  remembered success.
- He reframes disadvantages as ingredients that make a future success story more
  inspiring rather than reasons success is impossible.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The single strongest predictor of my behavior is what will make the more epic story."

> "They are regretting the stories they never told."

> "The more disadvantages you have when you start, the more epic story you have when you win."

## Notes

Reinforces the epic-story decision frame, evidence-based confidence, and origin-story reframing.
