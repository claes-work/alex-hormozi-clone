# Helping a Wellness Dentistry Practice Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The owner runs a "wellness dentistry" practice (a biological/holistic, more medically-oriented niche within dentistry) doing $4M revenue, plus a coaching/education platform on Kajabi doing ~$1M. She has sketched a plan to reach a **$100M valuation in ~3 years** by consolidating the practice owners she already coaches into a **DSO** (dental support organization) to gain buying power and a bigger valuation multiple, and asks Alex how to approach those owners to come together, acquire them, or partner. Alex's core answer is his roll-up / "make an offer so good they feel stupid saying no" playbook: a practice selling *alone* might fetch a ~4x multiple, but selling *together* as a group could reach **10–12x**, so the arbitrage is real. He offers a sample structure — "you'll get [what] four [is worth] on your own, you'll get seven with me, and everything above seven I keep" — noting there are a million ways to structure it but it comes down to either outlaying a lot of capital or giving up some of the pie; for a first deal he'd give up pie to get the first ones in. He advises not even chasing the full $100M initially: **rope 4–5 practices together for a reasonable exit as proof of concept, then rope 25 together.** The strategic insight he flags as the "magic" of her position: her coaching business is **"the goose — you want to sell the eggs, keep the goose."** Because she already gives coaching clients her systems, price points, and training, she is *already* consolidating and unifying operations the way a post-acquisition integration would — "three quarters of the work a roll-up would do" is already done, which is why he likes the business (he notes he once bid on a similar business and lost a bidding war to private equity). On mechanics he warns to lock in **an option for 100% of the business plus drag-along rights** (sellable above a set threshold) and to get **all rights agreed up front**, because otherwise at close "the person who's most greedy will hold out the longest" and squeeze everyone — "I only know this out of pure experience." On keeping the education platform / AI / IP out of the practice sale, he frames it as a straightforward negotiation with the acquirer: grant an unlimited license at no cost for a restricted period/territory. He confirms acquisition.com can help structure such a deal ("I literally wrote this entire deal"). Ends with the standard roadmap promo.
