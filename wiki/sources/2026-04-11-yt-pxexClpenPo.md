# Ignorance

## Summary

Alex defines ignorance debt as the gap between current performance and a desired result.
He says paying for education, trading skills, building reciprocal relationships, and
entering relevant rooms can reduce that gap faster. His own early pattern was to spend
saved money on rooms before owning the corresponding business, then contribute complete
sales help to participants in exchange for future access to their expertise.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex calls ignorance the largest cost and values education by the
  gap between a goal and current results.
- He prioritizes paying down ignorance through money, relationships, favors, or skills.
- Alex self-reports saving $50,000 over two years, spending his first $10,000 on
  information, and later paying $25,000-$35,000 for learning rooms.
- He says he joined a gym-owner room before owning a gym and an internet-marketing room
  before operating that kind of business so he could start correctly.
- When money was exhausted, he gave participants complete sales training, scripts, and
  objection help rather than partial favors.
- Alex says full value-in-advance creates reciprocity and a network of specialists who
  can later exchange expertise.
- He describes skill barter as efficient trade; the tax remark in the excerpt is his
  opinion, not legal or tax guidance.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The biggest cost that you have is ignorance."

> "The number one priority in my life has always been to pay down ignorance as fast as I possibly could."

> "If you're going to pay for help from someone, pay in full."
