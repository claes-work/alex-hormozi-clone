# You need to advertise more

## Summary

Alex Hormozi applies "do more" to the Core Four acquisition channels, especially paid ads.
For paid acquisition, he orders "more" by risk: make more creative first, then spend more,
then expand platforms after the machine can produce winners. He uses the Money Models
launch as a self-reported volume example: thousands of ads made before and during launch,
more editors contracted, and ad spend scaled only after input volume existed.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- "More" in paid ads can mean more creative, more spend, or more platforms.
- Alex usually sequences paid-ad expansion in reverse order of risk: more creative first,
  more money second, more platforms third.
- More creative increases the chance of winners; winners improve ROAS and make larger spend
  safer.
- Before expanding platforms, build the machine that can create enough volume on one
  platform.
- For the Money Models launch, Alex says the team banked 2,000+ ads before the six-week
  window and ultimately made about 2,800 ads.
- He says the launch required contracting more editors; the cost was worth it relative to
  the launch target.
- The pain of high volume forces optimization because fixed large inputs make inefficiency
  too expensive to ignore.
- Erratic sales often reflect erratic advertising volume; to move from one sale/week to
  one sale/day may require 7x or more advertising activity.
- Founders often over-focus on margin and relative returns when the needed answer is a
  violent, unreasonable amount of consistent input volume.
