# How to Get Taken Seriously When Nobody Thinks You're Ready

## Summary

Alex says people become exceptional by following the rule for long enough, not only by
being a young lottery-ticket outlier. A 30-year-old who works for a decade and becomes
excellent is still exceptional, even if the path was predictable. He also warns young
operators that if people do not take them seriously, age may not be the real reason; it may
be that their work is not good yet.

## Key claims

- Alex says "become the exception by being the rule": doing the normal hard thing for long
  enough can still produce exceptional results.
- He says young outliers are highlighted more than older operators who worked for a decade,
  but both can be rare.
- He uses MrBeast as an example of someone young who nevertheless had many years in one
  game.
- He says lower startup costs, abundant information, and AI make it easier for young people
  with fewer resources to start real things.
- He says customers do not automatically value an app less because it was built by a
  14-year-old instead of a 40-year-old.
- He warns that if people keep dismissing a young person, the issue may be quality rather
  than age.
- He says self-assessment is hard, and especially hard when young.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You become the exception by being the rule."

> "If you work for a decade and they get you there, you are exceptional."

> "It might be not because of your age. It might be because you suck."
