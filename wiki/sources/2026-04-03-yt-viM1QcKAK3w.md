# Helping an $88M Watch Business Double Revenue

## Summary

Alex helps a luxury-watch wholesaler decompose founder-dependent deal sourcing. He
contrasts scaling through established partners with building a more durable outbound
system of offshore assistants, documented rules, and a real-time pricing rubric.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports $88 million in annual revenue and roughly 3% margins; these
  numbers and the described partner economics are not independently verified.
- As of April 2026, Alex recommends splitting proprietary deal flow into smaller tasks,
  then documenting, demonstrating, and duplicating each task.
- Offshore assistants can scale outbound sourcing because they do not need the capital
  or buyer relationships required to complete the transaction.
- A volatile market requires a current price sheet, rubric, or decision tree so buyers
  can act without routing every judgment through the founder.
- He says partner profit should not be treated as a loss when the deal still works for
  the company; richer partner economics can motivate more volume, although partners may
  eventually accumulate enough capital to transact independently.
- Existing partners may be the faster path if the company already has the necessary
  management skill, while the assistant-led process is the more owned and durable route.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Chop up the process into pieces."

> "Document, demonstrate, duplicate."

> "Never count the other guy's money. If the deal works for you, it works for you."

## Notes

Alex explicitly credits the last principle to Leila Hormozi. Synthesis candidate: encode volatile founder judgment into a live rubric; partner-speed versus owned-process durability.
