# HOW TO GET WHAT YOU WANT (6 Proven Methods)

## Summary

Hormozi walks through Robert Cialdini's six principles of persuasion — reciprocity,
consistency, social proof, liking, authority, scarcity/urgency — and shows how he
applied each in his own businesses (which he says now do over $200M/yr combined).
He frames persuasion as one of the highest-leverage skills because "you can get
anything you want if you can get other people to get it for you."

## Key claims

- **Reciprocity**: gifts create obligation, proportional to the giver's stature.
  He and Leila donated $1M to Arnold Schwarzenegger's charity to get repeated
  meetings. Small "$5 mint bags" (free water, waiter mints → +3%/+14%/+23% tips)
  are cheap, high-ROI persuasion levers everywhere in business.
- **Consistency**: get a small public commitment first, then ask people to stay
  consistent with the identity they claimed ("since you're the type of person
  who..."). Cites the drive-slowly-sign study; explains why lead magnets /
  front-end offers work.
- **Social proof**: people copy others' behavior (laugh tracks, Amazon 5-star
  reviews). He papered his gym lobby floor-to-ceiling with framed 5-star reviews
  and before/after photos, then challenged doubters on their proof threshold.
- **Liking / Authority**: people are persuaded by those they like and want to be
  like; he uses an ACA framework (Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask) and kept his mood
  up with a mini-trampoline / cartwheel gag to close more. Authority lowers a
  listener's filters independent of the message — show credentials and track
  record; he printed certifications for himself and every trainer (US certs aren't
  accredited).
- **Scarcity/urgency**: urgency = a function of time, scarcity = a function of
  units. Loss aversion beats desire to gain (use loss language). The key is that
  scarcity must be *real* — state your true limits (like Amazon's "23 left") and
  you'll sell more.
