# Empty Motels Are a $14M Goldmine (Here's the Catch)

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a real-estate operator converting hotels/motels into apartments and extended stays. The owner's stated constraint is deal flow, and he is considering thought leadership to attract owners and brokers. Alex says thought leadership is a path, not the path: the best channel depends on the operator's existing skills and the highest-reward, lowest-risk route to deal flow. If the owner chooses content, he should commit to a long enough time horizon and make narrow, expertise-specific content for the exact motel/hotel audience.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The problem to solve is deal flow; thought leadership is only one possible path to that outcome.
- Channel choice should start from existing skill set and overlap: conferences if face-to-face is strongest, paid ads if acquisition is strongest, content if the owner truly wants and understands media.
- Analysis paralysis often comes from the false belief that there is a perfect path; every channel has trade-offs.
- Waiting at the base of the mountain can cost more than picking a reasonable path and climbing it.
- Thought leadership should be judged on an 18-month or longer horizon, not a 90-day lead expectation.
- Content should stay narrow to the operator's real track record; do not try to copy broad personality creators.
- Interest-based algorithms can serve niche content to tiny but valuable audiences, so low view counts are not automatically failure.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "There just isn't one. There's trade-offs on all of them."
