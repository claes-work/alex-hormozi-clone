# Why Skool is the best next thing

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains why he chose to promote and invest in Skool, framing it as
the company most likely to "disrupt" the still-fragmented education industry. He
walks through his "golden ratio" — the percentage of customers who refer vs. the
percentage who churn — as the litmus test for passive, self-sustaining growth, and
says Skool's metrics were the best he had ever seen across the thousands of
businesses that pitch Acquisition.com monthly. He argues that a good product grows
on its own, and that once referrals exceed churn you "pour gas on the flame" with
marketing.

## Key claims

- Most industries have been disrupted by technology (hotels by Airbnb, taxis by
  Uber), but **education has not** — there is no single place everyone goes to learn,
  it remains "completely fragmented." Hormozi bets **Skool will be the company that
  takes it**. (paraphrase)
- His litmus test for any product he promotes: it must **grow on its own**. If a
  company only grows while advertising and shrinks when ads stop, he doesn't like it;
  he wants **passive growth**. (paraphrase)
- The **"golden ratio"**: passive growth rate = (% of customers who refer a new
  customer per month) ÷ (% of customers who churn/exit per month). (paraphrase)
- Worked example: if 10% refer and 10% leave monthly, the business stays flat; if
  20% refer and 10% leave, the business **doubles every interval** regardless of
  size. (paraphrase)
- The ratio holds identically whether you sell **physical products, software, or
  services**. (paraphrase)
- Most businesses run ~2% referring vs. ~10% leaving, so they must constantly refill
  the top of the bucket with ads/marketing/outreach — a symptom of **not knowing how
  to build a good product**. (paraphrase)
- Growth sequence: first build a product where more people refer than leave, *then*
  pour marketing on it so the doubling compounds off a bigger customer base ("increase
  the n as fast as you can"). (paraphrase)
- Skool's metrics **"blew him away"** — the best passive growth rate he had seen in
  his history of evaluating businesses. (paraphrase, self-reported)
- Skool's mission — to make **"business online easy and fun,"** starting in the
  education space — was a key reason it fit him. (paraphrase)
