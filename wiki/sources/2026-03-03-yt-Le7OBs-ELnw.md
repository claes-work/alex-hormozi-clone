# The Greatest Risk to Your Business

## Summary
Solo monologue. Alex argues the single greatest risk to a business is **not** running out of money — it's the **founder running out of will**. "Most businesses don't actually run out of money. The founder runs out of will." Businesses die when the entrepreneur decides it's no longer worth it and switches to something else. His diagnostic for whether it's actually worth quitting: ask "if this thing made 10 times the money, would you keep doing it?" — if yes, the business isn't the problem, so fix the business rather than switch. He warns that more money is like "electroshock therapy": it helps in the short term but not forever. He maps the emotional terrain by growth state: fast-growers are in lots of pain but eventually stop wanting it because they no longer need the money; shrinking/volatile businesses are the most painful (same volatility as growth, but no money); and **plateaus** are the trap people stay in for years — "not painful enough to quit, but still very painful." His mitigation is to anchor the business to **something outside yourself** that the business is a *vehicle* toward — a societal impact, a problem to solve, or (for him) "the person that I want to become." Once you've made all the money you want, you need another reason to keep going, and that reason has to be real (actions must align with it). He says he must keep this front of mind and be willing to let acquisition.com do things that serve *his* interest (keeping him engaged) even when not strictly in the business's best interest — because if it's in his interest, he keeps doing the business. Ends with the $100M scaling roadmap promo.
