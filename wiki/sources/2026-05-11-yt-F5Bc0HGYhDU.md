# I'm in my 20s and want to make millions  What should I do

## Summary

Alex tells a 22-year-old that starting now and building skills for a year are not truly
separate paths, because skills are built by trying and failing. He still recommends that
many young people work one to three years in the industry they want to enter, because it
provides models, defaults, and practical directions that are hard to invent from scratch.
He cites his own path from college to consulting to an unpaid/minimum-wage apprenticeship
under a gym owner before starting in fitness.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex says building skills and failing forward are largely the same because failure
  builds skill.
- He advises young people to do things, learn from them, and make money if possible.
- He says working one to three years in the target industry can be a major advantage.
- He compares industry experience to having directions for an IKEA desk or a model for a
  building.
- He says working in an industry teaches defaults and unknown unknowns.
- He recommends an "earn and learn" path for many people.
- He says he did college for three years, worked for two years, and then apprenticed at a
  gym before opening his own fitness business.
- He says he worked for free or minimum wage under a successful gym owner to learn the
  business.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You will build skills by failing."

> "Earn and learn."

> "Work 1 to 3 years in the industry that you want to get into."
