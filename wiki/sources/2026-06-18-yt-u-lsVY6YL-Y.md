# His Ads Are Fine. His Offer Is Killing Him.

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a real-estate-agent coaching solopreneur doing all-organic acquisition. Alex says the short-term growth lever is paid ads because delivery is not constrained, while the long-term brand lever is more and better organic content. He recommends poaching an in-house brand/media leader from brands the founder admires, warns against outsourcing the core brand asset, and says ads plus sales motion must be improved together because cold traffic will not convert like the current warm challenge funnel.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - real-estate-agent coaching founder. His revenue, solopreneur structure, organic-only acquisition, challenge funnel, and hiring question are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If delivery can handle more customers, the business is a pure advertising play.
- Paid ads can create a one-time 3x to 5x lift from an organic baseline, depending on execution.
- Organic content grows the total audience base over time; ads move the monetized line deeper into the existing audience.
- The business should do both organic brand building and ads in parallel.
- A brand manager should be brought in-house because the brand is a core asset, not an ancillary function.
- Recruiting a brand manager can be done by identifying admired brands and poaching the people behind them.
- Core value-creation functions are attraction, conversion, and delivery; brand is central to this caller's attraction.
- Turning on ads will likely break the existing warm-audience checkout conversion rate because cold traffic behaves differently.
- Ads and sales motion have to improve together; either one failing can break the funnel.
