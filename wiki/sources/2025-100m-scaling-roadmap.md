# $100M Scaling Roadmap (2025)

**What it is.** A free Acquisition.com "personalized guide" that maps any business across
**10 sequential stages, indexed by employee headcount** (Stage 0 through Stage 9). Each
stage assigns the founder a **role** (Researcher → Chairman), a **headcount band**, a
single **binding constraint** ("bottom line"), and a **graduation goal**, with a per-department
breakdown of the problems that surface at that size. It is a lead magnet that funnels to the
paid **Scaling Workshop** and cross-promotes **Skool**. Extracted from the per-stage guide
PDFs (each guide covers its target stage plus immediate neighbors); the 128 MB master PDF
and the Journal are image-only, so this page relies on the four per-stage guides. The
`source_date` is uncertain — the guides carry no explicit publication date, so 2025-01-01
is a best-guess placeholder for the 2025 training release.

## The 10 stages

| # | Name | Role | Headcount | Bottom-line constraint | Graduate by |
|---|------|------|-----------|------------------------|-------------|
| 0 | Improvise | Researcher | N/A | Nothing is happening | Get people to try your stuff for free |
| 1 | Monetize | Starter | 1 | Your business makes no money | Make your first sale |
| 2 | Advertise | Doer | 1 | New customers are inconsistent | Let more people know about your stuff |
| 3 | Stabilize | Trainer | 1 to 4 | There's too much for one person to do | Get help |
| 4 | Prioritize | Manager | 5 to 9 | Trying to be everything to everyone | Niche down to serve only people like your best customers |
| 5 | Productize | Director | 10 to 19 | Not making enough per customer. Low LTV:CAC. | Make and sell your second premium product to customers |
| 6 | Optimize | Leader | 20 to 49 | Everything is inefficient | Doing better rather than doing more |
| 7 | Categorize | Executive | 50 to 99 | Every system is overwhelmed; the business is disorganized | Triage appropriately |
| 8 | Specialize | President | 100 to 249 | No one is good enough to know everything | Create dedicated people and teams for specific tasks |
| 9 | Capitalize | Chairman | 250 to 500 | Not sure where the next big growth comes from | Make a big bet on the future |

Management depth scales with the stage: Stage 4 adds one layer between founder and
front-line; Stage 7 (Executive) has three layers; Stage 8 (President) four; Stage 9
(Chairman) five layers, including CEOs.

## Key claims

- The roadmap's core method is **one binding constraint per stage**: at each headcount
  band exactly one problem is the bottleneck, and the founder's job is to solve that one
  and only then graduate — solving downstream problems early is wasted motion (all
  stages, 2025).
- **Stage 0 → 1 is the free-to-paid shift**: give the product away to get feedback and
  demand (Stage 0), then charge for a "good enough" **V1** product to make the first sale
  (Stage 1) (2025).
- At **Stage 2 (Advertise)** the fix for inconsistent lead flow is the **Rule of 100** —
  spend 100 minutes daily on marketing — plus "double down on what works, don't diversify
  early" (Stage 2, 2025).
- **Stage 4 (Prioritize) = niche down**: stop saying yes to every paying customer, pick
  the ideal-customer avatar, reshape the product for them, raise prices, and learn to say
  "no" even though it means turning down short-term money (Stage 4, 2025).
- **Stage 5 (Productize) = extend LTV**: a single product caps growth and drives churn;
  add a **second, premium product** to existing customers to raise revenue per customer
  and improve the **LTV:CAC** ratio, funding more ad spend and better hires (Stage 5,
  2025).
- **Stage 6 (Optimize): "better beats more."** More headcount lowers efficiency; the
  explicit rule is train current people / improve current products / serve current
  customers / use current tools better — rather than hiring, adding products, chasing new
  customers, or buying tools (Stage 6, 2025).
- **Stage 7 (Categorize)** de-risks the single acquisition channel ("a boat with only one
  engine") by adding a **second channel such as a referral program**, and sorts the chaos
  into categories: leads, customers, applicants, employees, money, data (Stage 7, 2025).
- **Stage 8 (Specialize): brand before selling at scale.** Direct-response ads decay and
  CAC rises ("shouting in a crowded room"); the fix is to **build brand** — clear message,
  give away valuable free content, and associate with other trusted brands/people — and to
  route best leads to best closers (Stage 8, 2025).
- **Stage 9 (Capitalize) = the big bet / M&A.** Cash-cow products degrade if altered, so
  the next leg of growth comes from a big bet — **M&A or R&D** — that takes 1+ year to pay
  off; the founder personally recruits top talent and gets the books audited to raise
  capital (Stage 9, 2025).
- **US business-count funnel (self-reported by Acquisition.com).** ~30M reach Stage 1
  (only ~9% of Americans own a business); ~7M (~24% of businesses) reach Stage 3; ~3M
  Stage 4; ~2.4M Stage 5; ~2.1M Stage 6; ~1.2M Stage 7; ~900K Stage 8; ~300K Stage 9 — a
  steep survivorship narrowing used to frame each stage as rarer air (all stages, 2025).
- The roadmap **cross-references Hormozi's named playbooks**: the **Rule of 100** (Stage
  2), the **CLOSER** sales method (Stage 2), the **Angry Boat** complaint-handling method
  and the **Epiphany Script** for testimonials (Stage 2) (2025).
- It is a **funnel asset**: every stage ends with a call to a paid **Scaling Workshop**
  ("we have 4 portfolio companies each worth in excess of $100M") and the early stages
  cross-promote **Skool** ("1 out of 2 people who start a paid community on Skool make
  money") (2025).
- **Stage 9 is the terminal stage** of the model; beyond ~500 employees "companies enter
  a whole different league" and each path becomes industry-specific (Stage 9, 2025).

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You've graduated from giving stuff away for free, and now it's time to start making
> actual money." (Stage 1)

> "The key here is to fix things in an 'unscalable way.' ... Think: driving to the
> convenience store to drop off ice cream to a customer's house at 2AM. The point is to be
> valuable, not scalable yet." (Stage 2)

> "The key to Stage 4 is learning to say 'no.' No to customers who aren't right for you.
> No to projects that don't fit. No to doing everything yourself." (Stage 4)

> "You need to extend LTV - aka - you need to make more per customer. This gives you the
> cash to scale advertising and recruit even higher level people to your team." (Stage 5)

> "The real secret to Stage 6 is realizing that 'better' beats 'more.'" (Stage 6)

> "Your business is like a boat with only one engine - if that engine breaks, you're
> stuck." (Stage 7)

> "This is why it's called 'Capitalize'—you need to take advantage of your success to make
> big moves. ... Small bets won't move the needle anymore!" (Stage 9)
