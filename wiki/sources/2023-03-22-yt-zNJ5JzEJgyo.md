# Why You Shouldn't Copy Me

## Summary

Hormozi argues that copying another person's brand, identity, or content makes you
generic and pre-emptively concedes second place — you can't beat someone at being
them. Differentiation comes from being authentically, weirdly yourself (stating
beliefs that don't fit your "uniform") and from doing real work that gives you a
true story. He distinguishes *replicating a skill* (legitimate) from *replicating
an identity/story* (a losing game).

## Key claims

- Trying to "build a personal brand" by mimicking an Instagram-influencer
  "starter kit" just reinforces a known identity nobody finds interesting because
  you're the same as everyone else.
- People pay attention when you don't fit the mold — stating two or three beliefs
  that clash with your assumed "uniform" makes you unique (he cites Joe Rogan's
  mix of UFC, aliens, comedy, biohacking as un-categorizable).
- Copying = accepting second place: you literally declare the other person is
  inherently better, so you'll never win at being them. If others copy *you*,
  that's a good sign it's working.
- He stopped consuming competitors (turned off ads/feeds) so his content stayed
  original; consuming others' stuff makes their thoughts top-of-mind and turns you
  into "another Mickey Mouse." Twice, competitors copied *his* landing page/emails
  and performed worse.
- "Replicate before you iterate" applies to *skills* (shadow the best caller /
  door-knocker) — not to stories or identities. Fix the story and the brand takes
  care of itself; if you don't have a story, go do work worth doing that no one
  else will.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "You're not building anyone's [expletive] brand. You're just reinforcing an

  you're the same as everybody else."

- "If you copy them, what it means is that you accept the position of second

- "What they can't copy is the story behind it. Fix the story and the brand will
  take care of itself, if you tell the story that's true."
- "If you don't have the story to tell, then do the [expletive] that's worth doing
  that no one else is willing to do."
- "You've heard me say replicate before you iterate... you duplicate the skill so
  you know you can do it. That's different than replicating a story and
  replicating an identity."
