## Key claims (attributed)

Launch numbers (all self-reported by the Acquisition.com speakers in this clip):

- **$106 million made in 72 hours** on the *$100M Money Models* book launch. — *male exec & Leila*
- Broke the **Guinness World Record for fastest-selling non-fiction book of all time**; described as **"top four" book launches of all time**. — *male exec*
- The team spent **~33 hours live-streaming** across the 72 hours and **never once used the word "buy"** on-stream (by design). — *male exec*
- Sold an **$18,000 back-end offer as a one-click upsell with no sales call** — cited as the single most-remarked-upon thing online. — *male exec*
- The most they had previously seen anyone "transact in that amount of days was like 40 million." — *Leila*

Operational lessons (attributed):

- **Every important initiative needs a single owner** who "wakes up every morning and owns it"; recurring pains in a business are usually a symptom of being under-resourced (no owner). — *male exec*
- **"Nothing great ever happened without it being written down"** — the launch produced two-to-three dozen memos on offer design alone; the sales-support manual for the weekend "had more in it than most people will do in a year." — *male exec*
- The launch worked because Acquisition.com had spent 3+ years building a full company (sales, client success, media, events, AV, design, legal, tech, finance) that could be **pointed at the launch**; this is why it's hard to replicate — "you couldn't just go out and hire this team." — *male exec*
- **Obsess over risk / redundancy**: they asked "what could kill this launch" and layered backups on backups (second generator, second Wi-Fi, then Starlink — "something not even attached to Earth"). Success came from eliminating every failure mode. — *Leila*
- **Big, meaningful goals out-motivate financial incentives**; "you can't fix a comp plan if you have no goals." The record-breaking goal, not the money, drove the team. — *Leila*
- **Three reasons objections exist**: (1) value prop not good enough, (2) sales process not clear enough, (3) brand not big enough — solve all three and you can sell anything anywhere without a sales call. — *male exec*
- **"Fresh eyes"**: new hires have ~2 weeks of fresh eyes before indoctrination — capture their observations in a shared doc; you can also "manufacture" fresh eyes after a holiday break or right after a big event. They surveyed the team the day after the launch. — *male exec*
- **Difficult conversations dissolve when anchored to the other person's goal** — lead in service of the person, framed around their long-term goals, not around complaints. — *male exec & Leila*
- **Buy-in framework**: three camps (believers, non-believers, and the movable middle); you don't need everyone to believe it will happen, only to **"disagree and commit"** and execute 10/10. — *Leila*
- **Goal → Plan → Behavior**: a goal stated without a plan makes you "sound like a quack"; the plan gives the team safety, and your behavior aligning with the plan is what shifts belief. Codified internally as **"WAM — write a freaking memo"** using a **why / what / how / now** structure. — *male exec*
- **"Nothing fails like success"** / "when it feels easy is when you step on the gas" — dissect success the way you'd dissect failure; "success leaves clues." — *Leila*
- **Deploying the windfall**: normalize the number to your own scale; the money must be redeployed (tax-efficiently) to rebuild the engine and generate the next $100M without repeating the launch — not spent on "a couple houses." — *male exec*
- **"Hilo to Humvey"** (from a Navy SEAL friend's story): SEALs drilled only the helicopter-to-Humvee transition for three days; isolate the single behavior a big goal depends on and drill it to precision. — *male exec*
- **Play to win vs. play not to lose**; know whether you're an over- or under-shooter; pressure-test goals against 3x/10x multiples; the male exec attributes to Alex a rule of thumb of taking a shot at ~a **35% probability of success**. — *Leila*
