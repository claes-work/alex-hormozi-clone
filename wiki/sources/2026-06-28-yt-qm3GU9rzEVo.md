# Mr Beast Showed Me Who Actually Has Fans

## Summary

In this teaching clip, Alex Hormozi explains why Mosey Media's next strategic direction is "live interactive" content. He uses a MrBeast soccer-game anecdote to compare celebrity, short-form, long-form, and livestreamer audience intensity, then argues that the internet keeps moving toward rawness and truth. Alex treats short-form as useful mainly when it moves viewers into longer, higher-trust formats.

## Key claims (Alex's teaching only)

- Alex says the next media direction for his team is "live interactive" because live interaction is closer to raw reality than polished content.
- In his interpretation of the MrBeast soccer-game crowd reaction, live streamers generated the strongest fan response, followed by long-form creators, then short-form creators, then traditional A-list celebrities.
- Short-form content can be useful as a path to long-form, but long-form gives the audience far more time and reinforcement cycles with the creator.
- Alex argues that marketers should optimize for prospects who are more likely to comply with a future request, not just for views.
- He uses long podcast consumption during the 2024 election cycle as an example of long-form media's persuasion power, without making the clip primarily political.
- The internet moves toward truth: scripted and curated formats lose relative trust as audiences get access to rawer formats.
- Alex wants to combine SPCL-style content with live and interactive delivery so the audience can feel closer to "hanging out."
