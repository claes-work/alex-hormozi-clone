# The Stoic Response to Hate

## Summary
Podcast clip (Alex plus an interviewer/co-host whose brief interjections are marked `>>`; only Alex's words are voice data). Alex lays out his mental model for handling hate, insults, and criticism. Two threads run through it. First, a **mortality frame**: he "puts almost everything immediately to death" — insults, enemies, and vendettas all dissolve once you remember the person "is going to die," everyone forgets them, and they become irrelevant; a hater's ability to influence your trajectory when they'll "disappear and become irrelevant" is, to him, absurd ("whispers in the wind"). He half-jokingly frames murder as "just picking when someone dies" — you could murder every enemy in your mind, you just don't pick the time — and finds the thought "incredibly peace-giving": why hold a vendetta when life takes care of them. Second, the titular **stoic response to hate is to "agree and one-up."** Level one is to agree ("Alex, you're an idiot" → "You're right. Now what?"); agreement is "the ultimate nullifier for insults" — an extinguishing event, like a slot machine paying out null. He raises it: "you think that's good? Let me tell you how to really get me." Underneath is a diagnostic: **the only insults that hurt are the ones we believe.** If an insult stings, ask whether it stings because it's *true* (then agree and fix it) or merely because of ego and how you want to be perceived. He ties this to **authenticity** — a life goal to "die with no secrets," to have as few "back rooms" as possible so everyone meets the same person; the payoff is that those who hate you hate the real you, not a performed idea of you. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex says the only insults that hurt are the ones you believe; an insult that lands does so because you already accept some part of it.
- As of March 2026, his primary coping/perspective mechanism is putting things "immediately to death" — remembering that any hater will die, be forgotten, and become irrelevant, which strips their power over your trajectory.
- As of March 2026, he reframes retribution as unnecessary: you don't need revenge because "they're going to die already," so a vendetta is pointless — "life will take care of them for me."
- As of March 2026, the criticism/hate people cast on you is just "whispers in the wind"; it's absurd that someone who will disappear could influence the trajectory of a life you consider meaningful.
- As of March 2026, his named "stoic response to hate is to agree and one-up": level one is to agree, then optionally raise the insult ("let me tell you how to really get me").
- As of March 2026, he calls agreement an "extinguishing event" and "the ultimate nullifier for insults" — the frame of agreement evaporates conflict because most people are "more interested in winning than being right."
- As of March 2026, his self-diagnostic when insulted: ask whether it's true (if there's an element of truth, agree and correct it) or whether it only stings because of ego and a desire to be perceived a certain way.
- As of March 2026, he treats daily hate as practice/reps, so that in-person insults barely register ("I've so many reps on this").
- As of March 2026, one of his life goals is "to die with no secrets"; authenticity means being seen as you truly are, which requires holding nothing back (content or otherwise).
- As of March 2026, he wants "the fewer back rooms" possible so he isn't a different person to different audiences ("100 faces") — the tradeoff is that people then genuinely like or hate the real him, not a performed idea of him.
- As of March 2026 (illustrative aside), on a holiday-family video that opened by telling people who love their family to go home, he notes critics don't actually watch that far, so his reply to a tirade was simply to agree ("God, I suck").
