# How to Find the Customers Who Value What You Do the Most

## Summary

Alex frames ignorance as the largest debt people pay, then explains that the same work can
create radically different value depending on the customer. A conversion-rate improvement
for a $1M e-commerce company creates far less value than the same improvement for a $100M
company. The skill is knowing where to fish: find customers who value the work most.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-18, Alex says the biggest debt people pay is ignorance.
- He says the most expensive thing people pay for is the information they do not know.
- He says customer selection can create 100x value differences from the same work.
- His example: a 20% conversion-rate lift creates $200K of value for a $1M store, but
  $20M of value for a $100M store; taking 10% would mean $20K vs $2M for the same work.
- He restates his "solve rich people problems, they pay better" idea as "find the people
  who value what you have the most."
- He uses the father/son old-car story as a market-selection metaphor: the same asset is
  worth little to the wrong buyer and much more to the right one.
- He says selling $10K, $100K, and multi-million-dollar packages is often the same thing
  and sometimes easier at the high end.
