# Helping a $5M Coaching Business Build an Affiliate Program

## Summary

Alex coaches a tech boot camp doing about $5M/year with a $12K product and roughly $1,200
CAC. Instead of improving a generic 20% affiliate payout, he recommends giving affiliates a
small front-end lead magnet they can sell or bundle while keeping 100% of the money. The
lead magnet then becomes qualified lead generation for the main boot camp.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-17, Alex says he is always willing to pay CAC to an affiliate because he would
  rather pay a known partner than pay Zuckerberg if the economics are the same.
- He says a strong affiliate system often peels off one narrow portion of the core product
  as a lead magnet.
- He recommends allowing affiliates to charge whatever they want for the lead magnet and
  keep all of that money, while it feeds leads to the main product.
- He says the generic 20% affiliate model is weak compared with this lead-gen structure.
- He gives a massage/gym analogy: two free massages may cost $50 in labor but create a
  showed referral that would otherwise cost more through paid media.
- He cautions smaller businesses that many large businesses drive less revenue from
  affiliates than outsiders assume and drive more themselves.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I'm always willing to pay CAC to an affiliate straight up."

> "How can I give my affiliates a lead magnet that they can get a 100% commission on?"

> "That is how I prefer to create affiliate systems."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: affiliate lead magnet with 100% partner commission; pay CAC to
affiliate instead of ads; caution that big businesses often self-drive more revenue.
