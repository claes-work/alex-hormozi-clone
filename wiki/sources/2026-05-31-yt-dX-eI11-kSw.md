# Affiliate Offer: 10x the Kickback to Boost Referrals

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches an accounting/CFO-services business for e-commerce brands that has implemented an affiliate offer but struggles to activate partners. Alex says the affiliate offer is likely under-compensated relative to LTV and gross profit. He recommends launching harder: larger upfront or ongoing payouts, urgency for activating in the first 30 days, done-for-them launch assets, reduced risk through testimonials, and a future content/ad funnel that proves ROI before the sales call.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If LTV and gross profit are high, the business can afford a much richer affiliate payout.
- Underpaying affiliates can make money look "not motivating" even when the real issue is that the amount is too small.
- Activation can improve through urgency: a larger payout is available only if the partner launches within a defined early window.
- Ongoing payments may motivate partners more than a one-time spiff because they reduce the social-capital risk of referring clients.
- The launch should be made easier with prewritten emails, custom pages, CRM help, and other done-for-them assets.
- Risk should be reduced with testimonials and clear proof that referred customers are helped.
- The fastest new channel is still often improving the existing channel that is already close to working.
- A future ad funnel should be content-driven for authority: show client work, specific savings/tactics, then drive inbound through ManyChat/VSL/close and promote winning content as ads.
- Cold affiliate acquisition can be done by advertising to B2B agencies with an offer that makes them money without costing them money.
