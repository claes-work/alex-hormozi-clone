# I'm Stuck at $7.5M and Can't Break $15M

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a door-to-door sales / recruiting / coaching business capped around $7.5M. The business serves reps, managers, and owners with multiple products. Alex diagnoses a model issue: three avatars are effectively three companies. Since owners generate about 80% of revenue and have the stickiest business model, Alex tells the founder to cut the rep/manager appendages, focus on owners, simplify the front door and ascension path, and then ask how to put more owners into the front door.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Door-to-door training/recruiting founder** - the coachee. His revenue, products, avatars, prices, and verticals are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Three avatars can function like three companies, especially when each has different fulfillment and offer paths.
- Organic-led businesses often add products to monetize a fixed pond instead of choosing the highest-leverage, highest-retention line and expanding the pond.
- Alex prefers straight-line businesses: front door, second door, third door, then scale the front door.
- The issue here is a business-model issue, not an inherently bad set of logical decisions.
- Small businesses have limited resources, so doing many product lines poorly is worse than focusing on the strongest one.
- If owners produce about 80% of revenue, cutting other products may remove 80% of work while sacrificing only 20% of revenue and improving margins.
- The owner-facing offer can include training and recruiting for teams, but the business should primarily sell to owners.
- Sticky per-user recurring training is likely the best business line; low-ticket rep and manager offers are less sticky.
- The founder should prune bad branches so nutrition and resources flow to the central trunk.
- Once the model is cleaned up, the next question is why the business cannot sell 100 owners per week.
