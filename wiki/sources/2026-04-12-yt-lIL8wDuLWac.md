# The Shiny Object Trap Killing $100M Companies

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a solar-company owner who scaled his sales team from 20 to 400 reps in two years and built internal software from scratch that has become one of his strongest recruiting tools. The owner is torn about whether to white-label / license the software as a separate SaaS product to industry peers who won't join his company. Alex tells him firmly not to bother: the tech is already captured in his company's multiple as a "tech-enabled" business, spinning it into SaaS would require inventing a revenue model while distracting from a business that is already crushing it, and no sophisticated acquirer would pay SaaS multiples on solar revenue. Keep the edge, keep the moat.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- White-labeling the internal software is a "big shiny object" — a **missed opportunity, not a problem** — and passing on it "takes you zero effort," so declining is the low-risk correct move.
- The tech already benefits the owner: **you capture its value in your company's multiple because the business is tech-enabled** — you do not need to sell it as SaaS to be rewarded for building it.
- To sell it as a "quote SaaS software" you would have to **invent a revenue model from scratch** (it currently makes no revenue) while your real business — the solar company — keeps running and "obviously crushing it."
- Building internal tools past a certain company size is **standard and normal** — it streamlines processes and creates a competitive moat; the "we built this for ourselves like Slack did" comparison fails because Slack "was making no money" at the time, whereas his solar business is doing serious revenue with 400 reps.
- **You will not get SaaS multiples on your existing (solar) revenue.** At best you'd have a small standalone SaaS company earning a SaaS multiple bolted onto a solar business that gets a solar multiple.
- A sophisticated buyer ("the guy who's writing you a check for 500 million") is **not an idiot** and knows the business isn't SaaS — to actually earn SaaS multiples you'd have to **transform into a different business**, which makes no sense given the current trajectory.
- Net prescription: **keep doing what you're doing, keep the edge, keep the advantage** — the software is a competitive moat and recruiting tool; leave it internal.
