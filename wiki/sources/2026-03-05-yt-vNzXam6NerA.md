# You Need to Understand Leverage

## Summary
Interview clip (an off-camera host asks the opening question via `>>` turns; all substantive content is Alex's). Alex frames leverage as one of his favorite topics and defines it plainly: **leverage is the ratio between what you put in and what you get out.** Low-leverage people put in a lot of input and get little out; high-leverage people put in a little and get a lot — and most people who aren't earning what they want simply hold "low leverage opportunities." He narrates his own income as a **staircase of leverage**, where each order-of-magnitude jump in monthly income came from adding a *type* of leverage: employee → self-employed (four figures → five figures/month, "I'm now in control"); hiring other people to work for him (a realization he says he didn't even know was possible until his gym members pointed it out — a bottleneck removed) got him to six figures; **licensing** a digital product with near-zero cost of goods — "I made it once and could license it out infinity" — got him to seven figures; **capital** ("I don't have to sacrifice time in order to get something for it") got him to eight figures/month, where he is now; and he speculates nine figures/month will come from **technology/code or more media**. He maps these to the **four types of leverage** popularized by Naval Ravikant — **labor, media, capital, and code** — and argues you should **stack as many types as you can and maximize each**. Degree matters, not just presence: Joe Rogan and a random podcaster both use media, but Rogan has vastly more of it; Facebook and Amazon "maxed out" every element (other people's money, other people's work, media, code). He adds that capital itself has degrees — friends-and-family → institutional → public markets — citing an IPO where public markets simply have "significantly more capital": "same work, more zeros." His closing thesis: "the people who move faster in life don't actually move faster — they get more for every step." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
