# Helping a $30M Online Education Business Scale

## Summary

Alex diagnoses a women's personal-finance education company's constraint as a 7%
ascension rate, not its $1,500 front-end CAC. He recommends bringing sales in-house,
integrating goal-setting and upsell calls into onboarding, adding buy-now-pay-later
options, and testing webinar openings. He says the hiring transition can happen quickly
by using several specialized recruiting firms in parallel.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says the company's low LTV is primarily a back-end ascension
  problem rather than a front-end CAC problem.
- He recommends placing an onboarding call after purchase, requiring the customer to
  book a goal-setting call before leaving, and routing qualified buyers to a closer.
- He says group onboarding is a practical starting point, while one-to-one onboarding
  is operationally heavier but more profitable.
- For the $10,000 back-end offer, he says a 7% ascension rate should rise to at least 25%,
  with 50% as the target.
- He recommends third-party financing and several buy-now-pay-later options on both the
  front and back ends.
- He advises testing two or three versions of the webinar's first five minutes and
  comparing resulting LTV.
- To build an internal sales team quickly, he recommends hiring one strong sales leader
  and using multiple sales-recruiting firms in parallel, paying for speed and negotiating
  volume pricing.
- The owner-reported $30 million revenue and funnel metrics, plus Alex's account of
  hiring 40 salespeople in two weeks, are self-reported and unverified in the excerpt.
