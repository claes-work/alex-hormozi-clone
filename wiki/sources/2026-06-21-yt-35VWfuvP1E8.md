# How to Scale an Online Service Business to $5M/Year

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an online golf-coaching company whose subscription revenue has shrunk while brand/media revenue has grown. Alex challenges the founders' attachment to the membership business and argues that the media side appears more attractive: it has high margins, sticky brand buyers, growth, attention-as-both-marketing-and-delivery, and a larger path to enterprise value. He frames the call as an example of outside eyes pointing out the obvious business that the operator is too close to see.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - online golf-coaching/media business owner. His revenue, prior peak, team size, brand revenue, subscription decline, and exit goals are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A sellable business is strengthened by high gross margins, fast growth, and sticky revenue.
- Media revenue can be stickier than consumer community revenue when brand buyers keep buying access to attention.
- In a media business, the way the company advertises is also the way it delivers: get attention, sell attention, then get more attention.
- The company should consider making more golfer-facing content free and monetizing the attention through brand/media revenue.
- Golf is an attractive niche because its audience contains high-spending male consumers.
- Alex questions the probability of building a $50 million golf-coaching subscription business versus a $50 million media business.
- Outside perspective is valuable because operators can miss the growing, high-margin, high-value business directly in front of them.
