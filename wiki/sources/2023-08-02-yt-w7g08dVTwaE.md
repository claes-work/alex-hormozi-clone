# Watch this to get your first 5 customers

## Summary
A solo lecture in which Alex Hormozi lays out a 10-step playbook for a brand-new
business to land its first five customers, presented as a sneak peek from his second
book *$100M Leads* (out 8/19). The core move is warm outreach: build one combined list
from your email, social, and phone contacts, personalize a message, reach out to 100
people a day, warm respondents with an "ACA" script, ask for referrals, and give the
first customers a free service in exchange for usage, feedback, and a review — then
ratchet price up from free to full retail as demand and reputation build. The video is
solo throughout, so all teaching content is Alex-attributed persona data (a mid-roll
book-launch ad interrupts the lesson).

## Key claims
- **Step 1 — Get a list.** Build three lists: (1) all email contacts (anyone you've ever
  messaged/emailed), (2) everyone you can direct-message across your social profiles, and
  (3) all phone/personal contacts (export them). Combined, this is far more leads than you
  think you have.
- **Step 2 — Pick a platform.** Start with the single platform where you have the most
  contacts (Instagram, email, or phone) and begin messaging there.
- **Step 3 — Personalize your message.** Spend ~30 seconds on someone's profile, find
  something in common or a recent life event ("saw you had a baby / switched jobs / moved"),
  and lead with that so it's clearly not a blast. "This is also just called being a human."
- **Step 4 — Reach out to 100 people every single day.** The first DM is the hardest; the
  second takes two minutes. "Everything must be hard before it can be easy."
- **Step 5 — When they reply, warm them up with ACA:** Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask. Tie
  the ask to the compliment and transition to a question that leads toward the service you
  sell. It's "not selling, it's talking to humans."
- **Step 6 — Invite their friends (ask for referrals).** Don't solicit the contact; ask if
  they know anyone looking for [outcome] because "I'm opening up a few slots." You're asking
  a favor of a friend, not asking them to buy. A reply of "I'd like to find out more" turns a
  *lead* into an *engaged lead* (a contact who has shown interest in what you sell).
- **Step 7 — Give the easiest offer to say yes to: free.** Do it free in exchange for three
  terms: they actually use the service, give feedback, and leave a killer review if deserved.
  Rationale: more reps, you probably suck at first, and lowering the barrier to free gets more
  people working with you. "It is monetarily free" but customers still exchange time/effort —
  so they are customers. If someone won't work with you even for free, ask why: the reason
  reveals *hidden costs* you can then eliminate and later charge a premium for.
- **Step 8 — Start back at the top of the list.** Referrals give you free leads; reaching out
  to ~3,000 people with a free thing yields ~5 who take you up on it.
- **Step 9 — Start charging once people start referring** (the signal you're good enough).
  Reuse the same script, changing "free" to "80% off for the next five," then 60, 40, 20, then
  full price — and beyond full retail once you deliver 10x the value. As free/discounted slots
  fill you hit true capacity → genuine scarcity → you can ethically raise prices. Ask free
  clients to either match the paying rate or release the slot (keeping the review either way).
- **Step 10 — Keep them warm.** Check in like a human and provide value to the whole list on a
  regular basis, depositing goodwill until people start soliciting *you*. "Don't think short,
  think long, and you'll make long money."
- **Referral scripts.** Long form: "Do you know anybody who is [struggle] looking to [dream
  outcome] in [time]? I'm taking on five case studies for free... I just want testimonials."
  If they say no one comes to mind: "Does anyone you *hate* come to mind?" Short form: "I help
  [customer] get [dream outcome] in [time period] without [effort/sacrifice], and I guarantee
  [XYZ] to decrease risk."
