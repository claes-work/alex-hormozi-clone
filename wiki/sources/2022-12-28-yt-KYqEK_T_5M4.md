# Get RICH in the A.I. Revolution (2023)

## Summary

An early solo monologue (published 2022-12-28, ~four weeks after ChatGPT's public
release) in which Hormozi lays out a sweeping, speculative thesis on where AI is headed —
across work, business, the economy, romance, and even space/aliens — before turning to
practical advice on how individuals can use AI today to multiply their output. His central
practical claim is that AI is a leverage tool that lets a person consume ~10x the
information and produce ~10x the output (a "100x" increase), and that the correct posture
is "AI is here to supercharge me, not replace me." This is his **December-2022 take**,
notably more futurist/philosophical and less operator-focused than his 2026 positions.

## Key claims (his Dec-2022 AI position)

- **ChatGPT is a watershed.** Its Nov 30 2022 release "democratized access to AI for the
  first time" via a usable chat interface; it hit 1M users in 5 days (fastest ever).
- **AI is the biggest shift of our lifetime** — "the equivalent... of the moon landing,"
  but unlike prior tech (internet, phones, TV) that merely *reshuffled* the workforce, AI
  "has the ability to remove or eliminate the workforce altogether."
- **Five domains AI will upend:** the nature of work, how businesses operate, the economy,
  romance/mating, and the nature of life itself (incl. space exploration).
- **Escalation ladder:** current narrow tools → merged multimodal input/output → robotics
  in the physical world (Tesla bot, Boston Dynamics) → super-intelligence → the singularity.
- **Jobs fall in reverse of intuition:** creative/white-collar knowledge work (copywriters,
  logo designers, coders, physicians, finance, attorneys) is hit *first*, physical labor
  later. He cites AI writing a malpractice-law argument nearly identical to a 40-year
  attorney's (a friend's father's) work.
- **Capitalism may be strained/overturned:** costs collapse but the wages that would have
  circulated disappear; he points to Sam Altman's concurrent UBI study and floats universal
  basic income as part of a societal "reset."
- **Short-term optimism — a "golden age":** he expects scientific acceleration, specifically
  nuclear fusion ("renewable clean energy for everyone"), a bigger change than the
  Industrial Revolution.
- **AI sales agents are coming:** an AI trained on 10M+ sales calls will out-experience the
  best human rep, run 24/7, and eventually be *preferred* by prospects. (He references the
  4-year-old Google Duplex haircut-booking demo as the baseline.)
- **Practical how-to (two levels):**
  - **Level 1 (everyone):** use AI as a filter/"home screen" — it distills inputs down and
    predicts your outputs — to consume 10x and produce 10x ("100x").
  - **Level 2 (experts):** shift from spending ~90% of time generating to spending most
    time on QA/validation/tweaks of AI-generated work, becoming *more* valuable, not less.
- **Mindset:** "AI isn't here to replace me, it's here to supercharge me." Embrace change
  or "throw in the towel." He also argues AI regulation is likely futile ("I have a hard
  time believing they will be able to stop it").
