# $1.7M Welding Brand With 300K TikTok Followers Stuck

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a welding-accessories founder with 300K TikTok followers and about $1.7M revenue. The founder is burned out from daily content and considering Amazon. Alex says not to jump to a random new channel simply because content got hard. Instead, reverse-engineer the founder's own sustainable content style, remember that audiences need reminders more than brand-new lessons, and keep making visual, educational welding content while also increasing ad spend if paid channels are working.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - welding-accessories founder. His revenue, TikTok audience, Shopify store, burnout, ads, and Amazon consideration are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The founder should not switch to another channel for no clear reason.
- The long-term play is finding content the founder enjoys enough to keep making.
- Sustainable content either comes from making things the founder likes or capturing work the founder already likes doing.
- The rest of content creation is pain tolerance.
- Creators can change content format across seasons because there are no content police.
- Audiences often need reminders more than brand-new teaching.
- The creator does not need content to feel as novel to himself as it feels to the audience.
- Visual businesses like welding have a content advantage because the work can be shown.
- If ads are already contributing meaningful revenue, spend should likely increase.
