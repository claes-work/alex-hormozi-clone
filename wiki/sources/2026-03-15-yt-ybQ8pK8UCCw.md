# Helping a Real Estate Wholesaler Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A real-estate wholesaler doing ~$450k/month gross (40% spread, ~$240–250k), with $700k months already this year, wants to reach $1M/month but keeps "tanking" back to $200–250k every time he tries to ramp. His team is 17 salespeople on a $42k base plus 10% of assignment fees, closing inbound leads from Google Ads. Alex diagnoses it not as a lead or spread problem but as a **sales-ops / culture problem**: the volatility comes from low script adherence and an every-man-for-himself "mercenary" team with no consistency. His core reframe: **the process must always sit above the player — "no one is above the process."** Because lead quality is constant month to month, the swings are a discipline issue rooted in the sales manager (the leader), who is tolerating off-script "cowboy" selling with no consequences. He gives a two-step plan: **Step 1**, do all the fundamentals not currently being done — one shared script everyone memorizes and "breathes," daily training, no calls if you won't say the script word-for-word. **Step 2**, if adherence still fails, it's a leadership/culture problem and you may have to gut and rebuild the team. He reframes the client's "where do I find good salespeople?" question as **"it's not about where, it's about how"**: the company's real IP is the *box* that turns a raw, work-ethic-only hire into someone closing wholesale deals in 14 days — that repeatable training system is the arbitrage and the trade-secret value of the business. For sourcing he suggests Indeed/Indeed ads (this caliber is "everywhere") and a screening funnel that doubles as frame-setting: send applicants the script before they show up, have them submit a 60-second video reciting it, run group (not one-on-one) drill calls giving one piece of feedback to test coachability, then job offers to the prepared/coachable few (~10 → pick 2–3). The whole hiring process itself sets the militant "this is how we do things here — get in or get out, but we win" frame that attracts winners. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames a wholesaling operation as a **sales-and-marketing business with functionally no delivery** ("you buy media, you sell") — so being world-class at sales is the core constraint on scaling.
- As of March 2026, when lead quality and volume are constant month to month, revenue volatility is a **sales-discipline/ops problem**, not a lead problem — high variance in close rates points to low script adherence.
- As of March 2026, the root of a volatile sales team is the **leader**: the sales manager is reinforcing off-script behavior with no consequences, so the fix starts top-down.
- As of March 2026, "**no one is above the process**" — for a consistent, world-class team the standardized process must always take precedence over any individual "player."
- As of March 2026, he estimates 95–98% of sales teams "have no idea how sales training or sales culture works" — they just hire many people, keep whoever closes, fire the rest, and end up with mercenaries and no team, which is what creates volatility (and long-term trouble as reps over-promise deals "you can't cash").
- As of March 2026, scripts used as "suggestions" or "guidelines" are the failure mode; everyone must read and memorize the same script and there must be daily training to enforce it.
- As of March 2026, on hiring the reframe is "**it's not about where, it's about how**" — the better you get at training and standardizing sales, the less inherent skill a hire needs to still produce a consistent outcome, and that becomes the business's arbitrage.
- As of March 2026, the company's real IP / trade secret is the **box that turns a raw, work-ethic-only hire into a deal-closer in ~14 days** — that repeatable system is what makes the company valuable.
- As of March 2026, this caliber of salesperson is "everywhere" (find them via Indeed/Indeed ads); high-volume teams get stuck because churn from people quitting roughly equals hiring throughput, making it a talent demand-gen/process problem.
- As of March 2026, his screening funnel: send the script pre-interview → require a 60-second video reciting it → group drill call giving one piece of feedback to test coachability and preparation → job offers to the prepared/coachable (screen ~10 down to 2–3); unprepared or attitude-on-coaching applicants are out.
- As of March 2026, the hiring/screening process itself sets the operating frame — how disciplined and "militant" you are in selling candidates on selling for you signals "this is how we do things here," and setting that frame ("get in or get out, but we win") is what attracts winners.
