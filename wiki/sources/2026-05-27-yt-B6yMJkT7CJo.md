# How Long Can You Go Through Hardship Before You Crack

## Summary

Alex Hormozi defines tolerance as the first component of his mental-toughness model: how much hardship or how long a person can endure hardship before behavior changes. He emphasizes that tolerance is not ignoring pain; it is maintaining intended behavior despite pain before disruption occurs.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Tolerance measures how much hardship a person can endure before their behavior changes.
- A long fuse means high tolerance; a short fuse means low tolerance.
- The label matters less than the thing being measured.
- Measuring the components of mental toughness makes improvement possible.
- Tolerance is not the absence of pain.
- The practical question is how long the person can act normally before they stop acting normally.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Tolerance is not about ignoring pain."
