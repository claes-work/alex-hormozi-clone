# Netflix's Recurring Revenue Tactics Can Scale Any Business (2021-09-10)

## Summary

In this 2021 video Hormozi shares a framework he built for his own portfolio companies: the "eight C's" of recurring revenue. His core reframe is that recurring revenue is not binary (you either are or aren't a recurring business) but a continuum — every business, given a long enough time horizon, can be made *more* recurring. He cites Netflix and Spotify as consumption-driven continuity businesses that retain purely because people keep consuming, with no contracts required.

## Key claims

- (2021) Adding recurring revenue is, for most businesses, the single greatest way to increase enterprise value; he says doing so 2.5x'd the lifetime value of his physical-products business.
- (2021) Recurring revenue is a continuum, not a binary — the question is not "am I a recurring business?" but "*to what extent* is my business recurring?" Each of the eight C's is itself a sub-continuum to push further.
- (2021) The eight C's of recurring/sticky revenue:
  1. **Consumption** — get people to consume more (better onboarding, more value, more "addictive" product). Netflix and Spotify retain purely on consumption, no contracts.
  2. **Collateral** — secure the revenue against something the customer needs back: physical storage holds your stuff; Dropbox/Google Drive hold your files; a CRM holds your contacts.
  3. **Cost of switching** — raise time/money/effort to leave; amplify sunk-cost bias with a big upfront fee ("the bigger the head, the longer the tail" — a big upfront makes the recurring back-end stickier).
  4. **Choice** — reduce the customer's alternatives via patents, trade secrets, niching down, or being the best; differentiation exists to remove choices.
  5. **Control of the money flow** — the more covert/automatic the charge (taken off the top before the customer sees it, like Uber's fee or payroll tax withholding), the stickier, because the fee is less noticed.
  6. **Cause** — align customers with a cause so they pay from identity (churches/tithing, political donations, brands people believe in).
  7. **Community** — people stay because leaving would mean sacrificing an identity they've committed to; commitment-and-consistency keeps them paying.
  8. **Contracts** (enforceable) — packaging month-to-month as a term commitment (e.g. a $12,000 program as six payments of $2,000 vs. $2,000/month) increases stick; enforceability comes partly from collateral like creditworthiness.
- (2021) Reframing an offer from month-to-month to a fixed-term agreement (that then continues month-to-month) can lift LTV / lifetime gross profit by 2-3x with no change to the underlying product — though it may be harder to close.
- (2021) He explicitly disclaims the ethics of tactics like covert fees or reducing choice, saying the channel's point is the economics of the business model, not the ethics.
- (2021, self-reported) At time of recording his acquisition.com portfolio did $85M/year.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It is not... do I get my people to consume the product, but it is how well do I get people to consume the product."
