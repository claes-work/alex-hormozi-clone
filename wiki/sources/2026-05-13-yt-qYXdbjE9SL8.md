# I Caught My Employee Stealing What Should I Do

## Summary

A caller says he is selling a luxury resale and pawn-related business while discovering
that a key employee stole from the company. Alex treats the issue as a values and
reputation decision, not a clever transaction problem. He tells the caller to disclose
the facts to the buyer, remove the employee, and treat full truth-telling as the best
way to protect trust.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-13)

- Alex says the caller should disclose the employee theft issue to the buyer rather than
  hide it during a sale.
- He frames the disclosure as a liability, representation, and warranty issue as well as
  a trust issue.
- He says the employee should be removed because stealing is sufficient cause.
- He argues that the buyer may trust the seller more after hearing the full facts than
  after receiving a sanitized version.
- He says reputation is worth defending at extreme cost.
- He frames the decision as a values question more than a business tactics question.
