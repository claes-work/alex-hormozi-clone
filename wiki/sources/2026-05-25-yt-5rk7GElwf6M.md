# I Build Websites For Clients - Is AI Going to Put Me Out of Business?

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a $20M website-as-a-service company worried that AI will erode its
product. He says the AI threat is currently a narrative, not a metric, unless churn is
meaningfully worsening. Instead of rebuilding the product around fear, he recommends
doubling down on inbound acquisition, using quarterly prepay to offset CAC, and using AI
internally to reorganize workflows, reduce headcount, and increase EBITDA.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If AI disruption is not showing up in churn or other business metrics, do not solve a
  problem that does not yet exist.
- Laggard SMB customers may still value done-for-you websites and may not adopt AI tools
  quickly.
- The customers most excited about building with AI likely were not buying the service in
  the first place.
- If average customer life is around 29 months, the game can still work.
- The better focus is likely acquisition, especially paid inbound, rather than product
  reinvention.
- New customers can be asked to prepay for a quarter to offset higher CAC from paid media.
- Existing customers do not have to be forced into new billing terms.
- If EBITDA is low because the company is heavy on people, AI should be used internally to
  reorganize workflows and reduce headcount.
- Higher margin can fund going negative on front-end acquisition while monetizing 29 months
  on the back end.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "All I hear is that you have customers and your job just got way easier."

> "You're worried about them doing it, you're not even doing it."

## Notes

- **Speaker attribution**: The owner supplies revenue, churn, and customer context; Alex
