# You Need to Focus

## Summary

Alex defines commitment as the elimination of alternatives and uses his own earlier
business sprawl as the cautionary example. He says that around age 26 he had several
million dollars of revenue spread across roughly nine businesses, but the fragmentation
made him stressed and unfocused. After a serious car accident and advice from Leila, he
says he exited the other ventures, focused on the one thing that was working best, and
made more from that focus within six months than he would have made selling all the
businesses.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-13)

- Alex says commitment means eliminating alternatives.
- He compares scattered business effort to trying to start multiple parties at once:
  none develops enough concentrated energy.
- He says strategy is prioritization of scarce resources.
- He says that around age 26 he was CEO or operator across roughly nine businesses with
  a few million dollars in revenue.
- He says a DUI/head-on collision and outside warnings made the stress impossible to
  ignore.
- He credits Leila with pointing out that one business was working much better despite
  receiving only about two hours a week.
- He says he called partners within about 48 hours, gave up the other businesses, and
  focused on the best one.
- He says that within about six months, focus created more value than selling all the
  scattered businesses would have.
- He frames focus as a daily battle, not a one-time decision.

## Notable verbatim quotes
