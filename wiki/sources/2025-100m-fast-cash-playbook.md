# $100M Fast Cash Playbook (2025)

**What it is.** A short (~21-page) Acquisition.com playbook by Alex Hormozi, titled on the cover "$100M Playbook: Fast Cash — The Fastest Way to Rip Cash Out of a Business" (PDF p. 1). It teaches "Fast Cash Plays": limited-time premium offers run to a business's warmest audiences to generate large amounts of cash quickly. Raw path: `raw/books/2025-100m-fast-cash-playbook.md` (cite `PDF p. N`).

## Thesis

The book's governing maxim is "Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Cash is reality." (PDF p. 3, p. 6). Because unexpected cash drains are inevitable, every owner should keep several fast ways to generate lots of cash quickly "in their back pocket" (PDF p. 9). The mechanism is selling a small number of ultra-premium, unscalable offers (priced 10x–50x the normal ticket) to existing/past customers and engaged leads over a seven-day, scarcity-and-urgency-driven promotion (PDF p. 7, p. 11, p. 15). Because those leads are already acquired and the price carries ~90% gross margin, the extra revenue drops almost entirely to profit — a small slice of annual revenue that becomes a large slice of annual profit (PDF p. 9, p. 15, p. 18).

## Structure and content

- **Start Here (PDF p. 4–6):** Origin story — April 2019, Gym Launch clients hit with unexpected tax bills invent a grand-opening/high-ticket promo that netted an average extra ~$30,000 per gym; states the "Revenue is vanity / Profit is sanity / Cash is reality" lesson.
- **Fast Cash Plays — What/How/Why (PDF p. 7–9):** Definition (limited-time offers to warmest audiences), why they are pure profit, the "10x the 10%" rule, and why plays matter (unexpected cash drains).
- **The Fast Cash Playbook (PDF p. 10–14):** Full mechanics — who to advertise to, what to sell (unscalable value categories), how many to sell, how/what to charge, and two promotion sequences (Push to Consult, Push to Automated Checkout), ultra-premium examples, important points, and a summary.
- **5 Reasons To Run Fast Cash Offers Every 90 Days (PDF p. 18–19):** Cadence rationale and ROI benchmarks with a worked gym example.
- **What To Do Next / Wrap Up (PDF p. 19–20):** Call to action to book at acquisition.com/scale.
- **Fast Cash Checklist (PDF p. 21):** One-page tear sheet.
- **Sample Email and Text Sequence (PDF p. 22–29):** A full copy-paste seven-day campaign schedule plus six sample emails with bracketed placeholders.

## Key claims

- (2019, PDF p. 5) In the origin story, gyms that ran the promo collected an average extra ~$30,000 in cash; the few who sold a $5,000 offer "all raked in $50k or more," and previously-canceled members were deliberately re-invited.
- (2025, PDF p. 8) "10x the 10%" rule: if 10% of your customers pay 10x the price, you double your revenue — and because acquisition is already paid for, the extra revenue (minus delivery) drops straight to the bottom line.
- (2025, PDF p. 9) In Hormozi's early days, Cash Plays were only ~15% of annual revenue but "nearly half my profit" — doubling his take-home income.
- (2025, PDF p. 8, p. 10) Fast Cash offers are built on scarcity (limited spots), exclusivity (high touch), big ticket, and urgency (limited time), sold to existing customers, past customers, and engaged leads — the lowest-cost, likeliest-to-buy audiences.
- (2025, PDF p. 11) What to charge: take your average cart/transaction value and "add a zero" (10x), or multiply by five (up to 50x); the price selects for buyers with purchasing power.
- (2025, PDF p. 10–11) Offer construction: one-to-three core components plus three bonuses drawn from unscalable value (attention, personalization, convenience, status, duration, speed, experience, access, network, secrets); layer bonuses in one at a time across the sequence.
- (2025, PDF p. 10) How many to sell: cap at a number that sells out — roughly 5–10% of customers (e.g., 100 customers → limit 5–10); selling out fast makes the next play more compelling.
- (2025, PDF p. 11, p. 22) Promotion runs seven days or less (or until spots run out); a well-built offer should sell out by day three or four.
- (2025, PDF p. 12) Push-to-Consult sequence: staged texts/emails at 7, 6, 5, 3, 2, 1 days and 12 hours out, each adding a new bonus and a countdown, ending with a "sold out" notice and an optional "one spot left" back-out message.
- (2025, PDF p. 13–14) Push-to-Automated-Checkout sequence: build tension launch-style (referencing $100M Leads), open the cart to everyone at once, first-come-first-serve stampede, adding the most immediate bonus at cart open.
- (2025, PDF p. 15) Financing/prepay angle — "Recurring Max Cash Offer" priced at monthly price × 12: offer existing customers extra value for prepaying a year; typically needs "little else beyond the bonuses to get 10% to say yes."
- (2025, PDF p. 18) Run cadence: every 90 days (once a quarter) is the sweet spot — it cleans the pipeline, raises customer value, keeps things fresh, allows delivery/reset time, and (if break-even or better) drops added revenue straight to profit.
- (2025, PDF p. 18) ROI benchmark: Fast Cash Plays run "at least 90% gross margins." Worked example — a gym at $25,000/mo recurring (166 members × $150), 25% margins, $75,000/yr net.
- (2025, PDF p. 18–19) That gym's four quarterly plays add $229,000 extra cash and 22 new customers: revenue rises from $300,000 to $529,000 (+76%) and net income from $75,000 to $281,000 (roughly a quadrupling).
- (2025, PDF p. 21) Close every play by collecting the "4Rs": Reviews, Results, Referrals, and Resells, then cool down the list and set the next date.
- (2025, PDF p. 12, p. 19) This playbook is presented as one component of a forthcoming "Cash Calendar" playbook covering Reactivation, Referral, and Easy Cash plays.
