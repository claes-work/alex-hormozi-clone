# Should I Take 5% to Help Grow His Business?

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an 18-year-old considering earning 5% of a friend's $200K pest-control business if he brings in $2M of revenue. Alex reframes the question away from partnership excitement and toward goal clarity, skill acquisition, and what a partner actually contributes. He says 5% for performance is a stake, not a real partnership, and recommends learning inside a more experienced, growing company if the goal is large wealth and skill development.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Figuring out what one wants is most of the problem; getting it becomes easier once the target is clear.
- Early in a career, "tasting mode" is acceptable, and the person should not overjudge exploratory choices.
- A partnership makes sense when the partner contributes money, time, experience, skill, network, or distribution that the other person lacks.
- If two partners are essentially the same, one of them is unnecessary.
- Great friendships rarely produce great businesses, while great businesses more commonly produce great friendships.
- Owning 5% for bringing revenue is not really being a partner; it is having a stake.
- For a young person with large goals, maximizing skill acquisition may matter more than maximizing immediate earnings.
- A company scaling from roughly 15 to 100 people can be an undervalued place to learn how business is done.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

> "Figuring out what you want is 99% of it. Getting what you want is the easy part."

> "If you want to do a partnership, they got to have money you don't have, they got to have skills you don't have, they got to have time you don't have or they got to have network or distribution you don't have."

> "If we're both the same, then one of us is unnecessary."

> "It's rare for great businesses to come out of great friendships. But it's common for a great friendship to come out of great businesses."

> "The goal is for you to learn not earn."

> "You're in maximizing skill acquisition mode right now."
