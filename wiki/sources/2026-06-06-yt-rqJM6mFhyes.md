# $3M Med Spa Owner Wants to Scale to $20M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a one-location wellness, weight-loss, and med-spa owner doing about $3M revenue and roughly $700K-$900K seller discretionary earnings. The owner names sales as the problem, but Alex traces the real constraint to the founder's time in the chair as a physician. Because the business has enough profit, Alex recommends hiring another physician to buy back the owner's time before trying to fix sales or add more initiatives.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The first diagnostic step is to separate accounting EBITDA, cash flow, owner compensation, and seller discretionary earnings.
- A profitable service business may not have a sales constraint if extra profit would not change the owner's life or unlock the bottleneck.
- If the founder is spending 80% of time in delivery, the constraint is likely manpower and founder time.
- A doctor-owner can often buy back time by hiring a physician, then use recovered time to improve sales, operations, and focus.
- Culture does not require the founder to keep doing the clinical work personally.
- More sales initiatives can become half-built bridges if the founder has not first removed the time constraint.
