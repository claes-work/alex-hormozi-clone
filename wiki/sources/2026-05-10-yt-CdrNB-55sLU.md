# Shrink the Gap Between Thought and Action

## Summary

Alex says entrepreneurs and wealthy people act with urgency. He defines personal power
as the shrinking gap between thought, decision, and action, and he ties decision-making
to cutting off alternatives. He also attacks the "you're young, you have time" story,
arguing that time is uncertain, accelerates subjectively, and getting good takes
repetitions.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says successful entrepreneurs act with urgency.
- He says one can predict a person's likely success partly by how quickly they do
  things.
- He defines power as shrinking the gap between thought and action.
- He says when there is no more information to collect, one should make the decision.
- He says decision means eliminating alternatives.
- He recommends asking whether a task promised by the end of the week can be done by the
  end of the day.
- He attributes to Leila the rule: if it is a five-minute task and one has five minutes,
  do it now.
- He rejects the idea that youth means one has plenty of time.
- He says tomorrow is not guaranteed and gives an example of someone his age dying of
  stomach cancer.
- He says the subjective speed of time increases with age, making the effective midpoint
  of life feel much earlier than the calendar midpoint.
- He says it takes repetitions to get good, so the sooner someone starts, the sooner they
  can become good.
