# 3 Landing Page Tests To Skyrocket Conversions & Optins (2021-05-10)

## Summary

In this short tactical video, Hormozi shares one "simple hack" his teams used to
consistently improve marketing: run exactly one split test per week in a standing
marketing meeting, and focus almost all of those tests on the landing page area
above the fold. He argues that conversion rate optimization (CRO) is where marketing
leverage increasingly lives because ad-platform auction prices only rise over time,
so whoever converts eyeballs best wins the auction.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021-05-10)

- (2021) The future of marketing leverage sits in CRO, because most ad platforms are
  auctions where multiple businesses compete for the same eyeballs and prices only
  climb; the better converter out-competes. (He credits Neil Patel for the CRO-future
  point.)
- (2021) Run **one** split test per week — just one — in a weekly marketing meeting.
  Forcing a single test keeps the team focused on the highest-leverage variable and,
  compounded over 52 weeks a year for years, conversion gains stack up.
- (2021) The **3 tests** that matter, all above the fold: (1) **headline**,
  (2) **image beneath the headline**, and (3) **sub-headline** — with headline and
  image sometimes tested together as a paired unit when a new headline makes the old
  image no longer fit.
- (2021) ~90% of their landing-page tests are headline or image tests; almost all the
  "juice" (biggest conversion jumps) comes from above the fold — everything visible
  before scrolling on desktop or on a loaded phone screen.
- (2021) Weekly cadence: review last week's test, identify the winner, ask whether it
  beat the control; if not, try another test. Continuous testing continuously improves
  the business.
- (2021) The return on this process is disproportionate to the effort, yet many
  experienced marketers and business owners still don't do it — a focus discipline
  point (fewest variables, biggest result).
