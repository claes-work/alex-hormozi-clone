# I've met Arnold 4 times. Here's what I learned. (2020-10-01)

## Summary

In this short 2020 monologue video (titled by Alex "violence is the answer"), Alex
Hormozi recounts what he took away from four meetings with Arnold Schwarzenegger,
including a two-hour breakfast at Arnold's house. His central takeaway is that hatred
and negativity are a *condition* of success: the more impact and scale you have, the
more haters you attract by simple law of large numbers, and the winning response is to
ignore the noise and stay focused on the fundamental business numbers. He frames Arnold
(and, controversially, Trump) as examples of near-"delusional optimism" — people who
operate on their own plane and refuse to acknowledge haters.

## Lessons (Alex's takeaways from Arnold)

- **Hatred is a condition of success.** The higher your success, the more noise and
  hate you receive; if you have zero haters, you stand for nothing and have no impact.
- **Delusional optimism / operating above the noise.** The single biggest thing Alex
  says he witnessed in Arnold: Arnold exists on his own playing field, plays his own
  game, and lets everyone else react — he doesn't even acknowledge the presence of
  haters.
- **Law of large numbers on haters.** The more people you impact, the larger the
  absolute number of "batshit crazy" people you must deal with — at least ~2.5% of any
  clientele. Put every complaint in the context of total numbers.
- **Negativity only affects you, not your business.** A departed client/trainer/
  competitor talking trash reaches maybe 20 people; your ads reach 50,000/day. Their
  impact on the business is essentially zero unless you let it into your head space.
- **Focus on the four numbers.** The only things that drive the business: more
  applications/leads, higher close rate, lower churn, higher profitability. Everything
  else (including his own Instagram posts) doesn't move the needle.
- **Scale = fundamentals at larger numbers.** "All scale is is doing the fundamentals
  in larger numbers."
- **Competitive strategy (small player vs. incumbent).** As the small player, attack
  the *methodology* (never the character) of the bigger player — but you must be right
  and have a unique angle. The incumbent should never lose except through pride/ego;
  the Amazon move is to let others do the R&D, then copy and distribute it.
