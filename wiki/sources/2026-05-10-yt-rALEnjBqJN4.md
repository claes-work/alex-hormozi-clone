# Achieving Goals Is Impossible Until You Change This

## Summary

Alex reframes pain and hardship as the price of valued goals. He says a bigger goal
requires more boredom and pain, but the pain feels different when it represents progress
toward something meaningful. He uses several self-reported hardship stories, including
living in crowded conditions while taking home about $20,000 per month, losing
everything twice, and using past hardship as proof of resilience.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says achieving a goal requires accepting boredom or pain, and bigger goals bring
  more of both.
- He says he once lived with six other people and several dogs while building his gym.
- He says he was taking home about $20,000 per month while choosing that living
  situation so he could invest in the business.
- He says later losing everything does not make the earlier suffering worthless because
  the skills and experience remain.
- He says the pain becomes more bearable when it is framed as the price of the thing one
  wants.
- He says work changes the person even if the project, relationship, or business later
  fails.
- He says he lost everything five years into entrepreneurship, made some back, and lost
  it again.
- He says a person can only start from scratch once; after that, they start with
  experience.
- He says hateful comments can often be reduced to: the critic would not prefer to live
  the same life.
- He says hardship reveals rather than defines the person, and tested traits become
  experience rather than opinion.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The work works on you more than you work on it."

> "You can only start from scratch once."

> "A man with an experience is never at the mercy of a man with an opinion."
