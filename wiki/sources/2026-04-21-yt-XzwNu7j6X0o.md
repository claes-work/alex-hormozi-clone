# Why You're Afraid of Money

## Summary

Alex distinguishes loving money from loving making money. He says money is neither good
nor bad, but potential energy or bottled time that can be used for harm or for hospitals,
wells, schools, and education. If someone has a mission to help the world, he says fear
of money is often fear of power and should be removed as an obstacle.

## Key claims

- Alex says people should not love money itself, but can learn to love making money.
- He says the common Bible framing is often simplified; the danger is desiring money
  above everything else, which can lead to unethical behavior.
- He says money is potential energy and bottled time, not inherently good or bad.
- He says fear of making money can be fear of having power.
- He says power can amplify either misuse or positive contribution.
- He says someone with a mission to help should remove obstacles to making money because
  money amplifies that mission.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You don't want to love money."

> "Money is... bottled time."

> "You're afraid of having power."

Synthesis candidate: money as bottled time/potential energy; love making money not money; fear of money as fear of power; money amplifies mission.
