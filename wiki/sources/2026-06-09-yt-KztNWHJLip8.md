# Why Most Agency Founders Never Break $1M

## Summary

In this short answer, Alex Hormozi responds to a LinkedIn agency founder whose service sends content and DMs from founder accounts. The concern is that larger founders do not want DMs sent from their personal account because of brand risk. Alex gives three options: serve smaller founders, make the outreach work from the company brand instead, or sell larger founders on why the risk is worth it because growth improves the CEO brand.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If larger founders resist personal-account DMs, one option is to target founders below the size where that concern appears.
- Another option is to solve the operational problem and make outreach openable from the company brand account.
- A third option is to sell the founder on why the brand risk is acceptable in service of business growth.
- The objection can be reframed: a CEO's brand may improve if the company grows.
- Not everyone needs to say yes; if half accept the argument, the agency can continue growing.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

> "You've got two options."

> "These are features not bugs."

> "What is your objective as a CEO? Is it to grow the business?"

> "Do you think that your brand as a CEO would be better if your business were bigger?"

> "I think you just need to sell them on it."
