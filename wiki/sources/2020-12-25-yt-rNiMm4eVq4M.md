# You Can Turn Any Income Goal Into Action Steps in 3 minutes

## Summary

In this short 2020 clip, Hormozi walks through how he reverse-engineers a
revenue goal down to daily activity, framing it as the same breakdown he uses in
paid "cash flow consulting." He reduces the problem to two variables — how much a
client is worth (LTV) and how many clients you need to sell — then chains
conversion ratios (close rate, show rate, cost per call) backward until he
arrives at a daily ad spend and a monthly ad-production cadence.

> ⚠️ This is an early (2020) proto-version of his later inflow/reverse-engineering
> diagnostic: an income target decomposed into unit economics and activity math.
> Worth flagging when tracing the evolution of that framework.

## Key claims

- (2020) A revenue goal breaks down to two core pieces: how much a client is
  worth (LTV) and how many clients you need to sell. Paraphrase.
- (2020) Be specific about gross vs. net before you start. Example given: for
  $1M gross at a $10k LTV, you need to sell 100 clients — i.e. ~2 clients/week
  over 52 weeks (with a small buffer). Paraphrase of his worked example.
- (2020) Reverse-engineer from the sale backward through each conversion ratio.
  If you close 1 of 5 calls, 2 sales/week means 10 calls/week; at a 75% show
  rate he rounds up to ~15 booked calls/week to build in slack. Paraphrase.
- (2020) At each step he adds a deliberate buffer (e.g. budgeting $75 cost/call
  when actual is ~$50) so the plan stays realistic and achievable. Paraphrase.
- (2020) 15 calls/week at ~$75/call ≈ $2,250/week ≈ ~$300/day in ad spend in his
  worked example. Paraphrase.
- (2020) Supporting production cadence: ~10 good ads/month, block one full
  calendar day per month to produce ads and copy, plus 2–4 hours/week to review
  and update creative. Paraphrase.
- (2020) The full chain: $300/day yields 15 calls → 10 shows → 2 sales, and the
  back-end system makes each sale worth a $10k LTV. Paraphrase.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "When people pay me 50 grand for six hours I literally break it down to these
> two core pieces, which is how much is a client worth, how many do I need to
> sell."

> "So and then I just reverse engineer from there."

> "At each level I give myself a tiny buffer so that it makes it realistic that
> I'm going to achieve it."

> "Three hundred dollars a day yields me my 15 calls, 15 calls gives me 10 shows,
> 10 shows gives me two sales, and then because of the back end system that I
> built I know that my LTV is 10 grand. And that's the game."
