# 12 Hours, No Phone: How I Actually Get Work Done

## Summary

Alex defines productivity as doing nothing except the task one set out to achieve. He
says productivity gains come from elimination, not addition: remove outside stimulus,
control the work environment, and reduce alternatives until the task is the only
available action. He describes his own environment as sleep, prior-day task clarity,
caffeine, double ear protection, and a windowless room.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The highest-value productivity hack is to do nothing except the task at hand.
- Anything done to increase productivity that is not working on the task is, by
  definition, reducing productivity.
- Action can create the mood faster than waiting for the mood to arrive.
- Alex says everything that made him more productive came through elimination rather
  than addition.
- His preferred work setup removes outside stimulus and approximates doing only one
  thing.
- Controlling conditions can control outcomes more than relying on motivation.
- People who work with notifications, interruptions, and distracting environments may
  not have given themselves a fair chance.
- Commitment is the elimination of alternatives.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Do nothing except for the task you set out to achieve."

> "All of the things that have made me more productive in life have been through elimination, not addition."

> "Commitment as the elimination of alternatives."
