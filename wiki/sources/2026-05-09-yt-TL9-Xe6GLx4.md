# How to Fix 75% Churn in a Subscription Business

## Summary

A founder selling premium meat boxes says new customers churn heavily after the first
month, while customers who stay longer become more stable. Alex immediately identifies
manual re-ordering as a core problem: subscriptions should default to automatic boxes
with easy edits. He also recommends premium avatar selection, text-based customization,
skip or portion-adjust options, quarterly billing tests, and continued price testing
because higher prices may indicate a better customer segment.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says a subscription box should not require customers to manually place a new
  order every month.
- He recommends a default box that customers can edit if desired.
- He says the business should position against groceries and daily food sourcing, not a
  one-time meat discount.
- He says meat boxes are price elastic and can run thin margins.
- He recommends texting customers before renewal so they can skip a month, reduce
  portions, or edit the order conveniently.
- He says customization should be easier, potentially through an AI agent that edits
  orders on the back end.
- He recommends testing quarterly shipments or quarterly billing to improve cash flow and
  offset acquisition cost.
- He says if higher-priced customers churn less, the business should lean into the higher
  avatar and test even higher prices.
- He recommends copy that explicitly says the product is not the cheapest meat, but the
  most premium.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It has to be automatic."

> "You want to just have a product that people want to keep subscribing, not that they forget about it."

> "Keep going until they tell me no."
