# Get Over Yourself

## Summary

Alex argues that difficult past experiences can become either excuses or origin stories.
He does not deny disadvantages, but says the only useful question is what action the person
will take now. He uses a video-game "spawn point" frame, credited to Joe Rogan, as a way to
act from today without emotional attachment to the past.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-15, Alex says childhood or past hardship can explain difficulty but does not
  replace the need for action.
- He says no one cares about what happened then, only what someone can make happen now.
- He frames disadvantages as potential fuel because they can make the success story more
  powerful.
- He says the same reason one person uses as an excuse can become another person's origin
  story.
- He contrasts losers defining themselves by what happened to them with winners defining
  themselves by what they can make happen despite it.
- He credits the video-game spawn-point frame to Joe Rogan and says it is not his own.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "No one cares about what happened to you then, only what you can make happen now."

> "Where some person sees an excuse, another person sees an origin story."

> "Losers define themselves by what has happened to them. Winners define themselves by what they can make happen, despite what's happened to them."
