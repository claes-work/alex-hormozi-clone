# How to go ALL IN on your side hustle...

## Summary

Hormozi gives a three-step, risk-minimizing process for turning a side hustle into a
full-time main hustle: (1) secure your family financially with ~6 months of savings while
living well below your means, (2) get the side hustle to at least match your job income,
and (3) prove that income is reliable over ~6 months before quitting. He illustrates each
step with his own early transition from a $50k consulting job into fitness, and argues the
job-to-entrepreneur leap is the single hardest jump in entrepreneurship — so stack the odds.

## Key claims

- A side hustle is just a main hustle that hasn't yet grown big enough to support you
  financially; if you're only chasing "side money," that's a distraction from your real
  main hustle (usually your career).
- Early in a career (first decade) a job should be geared more toward learning than earning.
- Three orders of risk-reduction before quitting: (1) have savings (~6 months living
  expenses) living below your means, (2) match your current income with the side hustle,
  (3) sustain that matched income for ~6 months to prove reliability.
- Going from a job to "eating what you kill" is the single hardest jump in all of
  entrepreneurship — it was not easy for him, so minimize risk however you can.
- Your first business is very rarely your last; you don't need to be perfect, only
  "directionally correct" — the first correct direction is simply becoming an entrepreneur.
- Massively cut your overhead so you get as much time as possible back to invest in the
  thing you actually want to do long-term.
