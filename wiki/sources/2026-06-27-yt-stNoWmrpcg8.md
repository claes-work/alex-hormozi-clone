# The 3 Roles Every Company Needs to Scale

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi answers a founder who asks which three roles every business needs. Alex rejects the premise and reframes the issue around functions: promotion/conversion, product or service quality, and day-to-day operation/culture/recruiting. He says hiring should start from the company's current constraint, especially whether it is supply constrained or demand constrained.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - founder/operator of a group of businesses under a holding-company-like umbrella. His company names and revenue context are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The better question is not which three people are required, but which functions must occur in every business.
- The first essential function is promotion and conversion: somebody must get customers.
- The second essential function is building or delivering the product/service and keeping it exceptional.
- The third essential function is operating the day-to-day business, leading the team, recruiting people, and setting standards/culture.
- One person can cover pieces of multiple functions, but finding one person who covers all three well is hard.
- Hiring should depend on where the deficiency ties to the current constraint, not on a generic org chart.
- Alex says the first diagnostic is still whether the company is supply constrained or demand constrained.
- Businesses often alternate between fixing demand and fixing supply; when one side is constrained, the other side is temporarily ignored.
