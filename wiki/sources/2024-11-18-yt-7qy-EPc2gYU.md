# My Full Workout with Chris Bumstead [6x Mr. Olympia Champion]

## Summary

A filmed leg/calf workout at Alex Hormozi's Las Vegas gym with guest Chris Bumstead
(then 6x Classic Physique Mr. Olympia). Between sets, Alex talks candidly and at length
about his own lifting past — competitive bodybuilding as a teen, his gym-owner years,
his eating and training methods, and specific personal-record numbers — making this one
of the richest single sources for Alex's fitness background and physique. The
conversation also drifts into his wealth/champion philosophy, his content cadence, and
biographical notes (Vegas move, the Kylie Jenner "failure" moment). Alex is the primary
speaker; Bumstead's competitive-bodybuilding remarks are guest context only.

## Fitness & training (Alex's own routine and physique — self-reported)

- **Training philosophy:** stick to a handful of lifts, avoid a lot of variation, and
  slowly progress reps or weight week over week. He trains largely **by feel/intuition**,
  knowing his top numbers per movement from memory and adding a little each session.
- **Programming:** 2024 is the **first year he has ever followed a written program**; for
  years before that he just "knew in his brain" what he did last time and progressed it.
- **Warm-ups:** recently started doing **3 proper warm-up sets**, which "helped a lot";
  before that he went straight into working sets and it felt like his "muscles were
  tearing off." Notes the nervous-system/activation science behind warming up.
- **Now vs. then:** he has decreased loading and increased warm-up ("more proper") yet is
  in **more pain** now than when he was young and just piled on volume.
- **Calves (his signature body part):** trained calves **every single day for 10+ years**;
  went from 3 days/week at the end of a workout to **7 sets of 20 at the beginning**. The
  two movements that built his calves: the **donkey calf raise** and **standing single-leg
  calf raise off a box** (bodyweight, deep stretch). His growth technique: 20–30 bodyweight
  reps off a box under a pull-up bar to failure, then assisted reps and slow controlled
  negatives emphasizing the **deep stretch and full flex** — "controlled negatives are
  such a secret to making anything grow." He once gave clients such bad calf DOMS they
  couldn't wear heels / could barely walk. Has a preferred **Nebula** calf machine and used
  up to a **100 lb plate on top** at his peak. Has **not trained calves in ~a year** due to
  a foot problem (pain at the base of the foot under the big toe; describes it vaguely as
  "10-to-9s in my toe" — caption unclear).
- **Deadlift:** originally pulled **conventional**, switched to **Sumo** and hurt his hips;
  was ~100 lb weaker on Sumo but could do more volume. His deadlift "didn't move for 3
  years" on low-rep 5/3/1-style training; then he used a high-rep **Juggernaut method**
  (Chad Wesley Smith / JTS) doing **sets of 20**, adding ~10 lb/week for **16 weeks**, and
  **added ~160 lb to his 20-rep** — has video of **465 lb for 20 reps** before stopping.
- **Squat:** did **3 sets of 20** on barbell back squats, working up to **~405–455 for 20**.
- **Bench (his best natural lift):** "every time I touch the bench it blows up." In a **6-week,
  7-sets-of-20/high-set cycle** he went from **245 for 7×5** to **395 for 1** and put on
  ~35 lb. His all-time one-rep bench max was **415 lb** (powerlifting-style / paused bench).
  He otherwise avoids benching.
- **Chest:** "I don't train chest" much — says he always had a "decent enough chest."
- **Walking lunges:** did **225 lb** walking lunges "when I was strong and young"; rates
  walking lunges as incredible for leg growth. Leila does heavy walking lunges too.
- **Eating (peak-bulk era, ~age 25):** ate **~6,000 calories/day** after reading that
  "insulin is the most anabolic hormone." Sample day: a box of pancakes with half a bottle
  of syrup for breakfast (then "pass out"), a **1,200-calorie shake** post-workout, a
  double-meat/double-rice burrito with sour cream/chips/guac, and **a full box of pasta +
  a jar of tomato sauce** for dinner ("a box of pasta is ~70g of protein"). Digests pasta
  and basmati rice very well; can no longer eat at that volume.
- **Music:** only listens to music when he trains — **alternative rock, Eminem, Kanye West,
  90s hip hop**.
- **Bodyweight:** was **235 lb natural** in his heaviest lifting years.
- Cites Milos Sarcev: **"there's no such thing as overtraining, only undereating"** — and
  says that as a natural lifter he can "kind of attest to it."

## Biographical facts (dated; self-reported unless noted)

- **Turning 35 in August** (as of Nov 2024) — consistent with a **birth date of August 1989**.
- **Competitive bodybuilding / lifting:** "obsessed from 15" (says he started around **14**)
  and competed until **~22–23**. Won a **state record (not national)** in competition.
- **Dropped out of university at 22** to pursue lifting/training **full-time**; had been "there
  for four years but on my second year of school." As a **gym owner** in that era he "lived
  at the gym" — spent ~4 hours in the weight room then "in the cafeteria all day"; "8 hours
  a day of food and training."
- **Moved from Austin to Las Vegas:** previously had "about half these [gym] pieces" at his
  **house in Austin**; now based in **Vegas**, planning to stay **3–5 years**. Praises Vegas
  as non-judgmental, non-political, tax-free, dry, with direct flights and the UFC.
- **Sold Gym Launch:** afterward his monthly cash flow (as owner) "went to zero" and he "felt
  poorer" despite cash in the bank — this triggered his year of content about "the meaning of
  money" and redefining where he grows.
- **The Kylie Jenner moment (~age 26–27):** seeing "her" on the cover of Forbes as the
  youngest self-made billionaire at 19 made him feel like "a failure" even though he'd
  "taken home $17 million in income that year"; he went into a **7-day depression**. He calls
  it a **career-defining moment** that got him onto **brand-building**. (He says "Kendall
  Jenner" but the youngest self-made billionaire on that Forbes cover was **Kylie Jenner** —
  likely a slip.)
- **Content cadence:** "we do **450 posts a week**." Filming evolved from **once every 90 days**
  (~100 clips in one full day), to every other week, to Fridays, to now filming **fresher /
  fewer-but-more** whenever "lightning strikes" in the morning. Tried filming seminars to a
  near-empty room (did it ~4 times, disliked it), then switched to **Instagram Lives** (a
  room of ~2,000).
