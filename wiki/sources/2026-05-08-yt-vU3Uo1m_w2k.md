# Helping a Landscaping Business Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches a landscaping design/construction owner with a $50M enterprise-value goal.
He translates the goal into an EBITDA and revenue target, then recommends investing in
the current constraint: better sales motion through two VSLs and hiring a crew leader
to free the founder's time. He also supports recurring maintenance revenue after the
founder is out of the weeds.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says a $50M enterprise-value target implies roughly $8M EBITDA at about a 6x to
  6.5x multiple.
- He says the best short-term allocation is always the current constraint.
- He diagnoses the owner as time-constrained in the $1M-$3M "swamp."
- He recommends adding two VSLs: one between appointment set and site visit, and one
  between first and second appointment.
- He says the VSLs may improve close rate and allow higher pricing.
- He recommends hiring a crew leader to free the current operations lead and give the
  owner more time for marketing and sales.
- He says current cash should be saved for trucks and equipment when that becomes the
  next constraint.
- He says recurring maintenance revenue can improve enterprise value, but should come
  after the owner regains time.
- He says each lead source has a different sales motion; Google/search intent and Meta
  interruption traffic should not be treated the same.
