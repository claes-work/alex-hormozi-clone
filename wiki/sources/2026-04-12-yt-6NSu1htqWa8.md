# Helping a Tax Firm Get More Leads

## Summary

Alex advises a nine-month-old cost-segregation firm to keep its saturated-looking Google
channel, continue improving Meta rather than abandoning six months of learning, and
invest in content for the long term. He also suggests partnerships with real-estate
educators, but rejects a one-time list of 100 prospects as a real test of outbound.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says a failed Meta result means the audience, ad, funnel, or
  offer did not work—not that the channel itself cannot work.
- His highest-likelihood alternative is affiliate partnerships with real-estate
  influencers whose audiences buy properties and need cost-segregation studies.
- He applies the Rule of 100 to outbound as 100 contacts per day, not 100 total.
- For a nine-month-old company, he treats content as a long-term channel that needs
  continued investment.
- Given a reported $5,000 Meta CAC and $5,500 initial LTV with repeat potential, Alex
  says CRO improvements could bring acquisition near break-even and create a compounding
  customer asset; the figures are owner-reported and unverified.
- He recommends retaining working PPC while exploring additional keywords and spending
  another six months improving Meta because the team already has media-buying expertise.
- Channel choice should follow accumulated skill and evidence rather than restarting a
  completely different acquisition game.

## Notable verbatim quotes
