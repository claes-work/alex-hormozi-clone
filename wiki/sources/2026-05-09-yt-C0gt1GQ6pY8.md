# The Problem with Comparison in Business & How to Use It Correctly

## Summary

Alex says comparison is an exceptional measurement tool but a harmful judgment tool. In
business, he treats higher revenue as evidence that someone is better at some part of
the game. He says seeing Kylie Jenner's billionaire outcome in Forbes forced him to
question his transactional, quantitative view of business and pushed him toward studying
brand.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says comparison itself is useful; judgment attached to comparison is what hurts.
- He says comparing another person's superior results can reveal what they do better.
- He says one of his business rules is that if someone makes more money than him, they
  are better at the game of business in some way.
- He says business is objective enough that revenue can reveal performance differences.
- He says Kylie Jenner appearing on the cover of Forbes as a billionaire when he was
  about 27 challenged his business fundamentals.
- He says he had made just under $20 million personally that year and thought he was good
  at business.
- He says Kylie Jenner, The Rock, George Clooney, Conor McGregor, and Huda Beauty pushed
  him to study brand.
- He says he had previously been very transactional and focused on metrics such as
  conversion rates, CPMs, spend, and upsells.
- He says comparison launched him into brand because he did not protect his ego by
  dismissing the comparison.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If someone's making more money than me, they're better at the game of business in some way."
