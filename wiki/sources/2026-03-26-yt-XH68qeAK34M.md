# Helping a Business Solve Delegation Issues

## Summary
In this clip, a business owner running a bleacher-rental company (self-reported ~$4.5M revenue, ~$1M EBITDA) asks Alex Hormozi how to train leaders so they can fully delegate, given a lack of followed processes. Hormozi reframes the problem around operationalizing vague terms into concrete behaviors, hiring for the smallest skill deficiency, and using a "document, demonstrate, duplicate" training framework. He argues most training failures stem from leaders being insufficiently specific about the behavior they want changed.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that traits are not real as distinct things — a "trait" is just a series of skills, so any trait can be broken down into trainable sub-skills.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises hiring for the "smallest skill deficiency": if a candidate has a large valuable skill set and one small fixable gap, hire them and be transparent up front about the gap.
- As of March 2026, Alex weighs training time when choosing between candidates — e.g. if sales takes 2 days to train and kindness takes 2 months, hire the kind person and train them on sales.
- As of March 2026, Alex says vague behavioral feedback ("stop being lazy," "change your behavior") is unactionable; leaders must operationalize terms like patience, courage, humility, or kindness into observable behaviors.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames patience as "figuring out something to do in the meantime" — a behavioral definition rather than a character label.
- As of March 2026, Alex's training framework is "document, demonstrate, duplicate": write the checklist, show them how you do it, then have them do it in front of you.
- As of March 2026, Alex prefers checklists over SOPs (stated as a personal preference).
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends structuring behavior feedback as "stop doing this / start doing this / keep doing this" for granularity.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites Chick-fil-A's head of people to argue recruiting matters more than process: teams often "lost the championship in the draft" and try to fix a playbook for a team that will never win.
- Caller claim (unverified): his bleacher-rental company does ~$4.5M revenue and ~$1M EBITDA, and his blocker is his leadership team not fully delegating responsibilities.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Based on Alex's worldview, this is not a thing. It's just that a trait is just a series of skills."

> "If it's 2 days to train sales and 2 months to train kindness, take this person who's kind and then train them on sales."

> "It's telling someone what they actually have to do, not who they have to be. 'Stop being lazy' is very hard for someone to solve."

> "You have to break the term down into what behaviors you describe as lazy."

> "A lot of people are trying to fix process when they really lost the championship in the draft."

> "The big framework that we use for training in general is document, demonstrate, duplicate."
