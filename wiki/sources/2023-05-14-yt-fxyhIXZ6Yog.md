# The Alex Hormozi Diet (REVEALED)

## Summary

Solo video in which Alex lays out the exact daily eating system he says he has used for
~10 years: a simple body-weight-times-a-coefficient formula that sets calories, a
body-weight-in-grams protein target, and full flexibility for the remaining calories
(including dessert every night). He frames it explicitly as "what works for me," not a
scientifically optimal protocol, and offers a training follow-up if commenters want it.

## Key claims

- **Two-step goal choice.** First decide whether you're "trying to get bigger" or
  "trying to get smaller," then pick a coefficient on a scale from **7 to 21**.
- **Coefficient bands (each 3-point block).** 7–9 = extreme weight loss; 10–12 =
  moderate weight loss; 13–15 = maintenance; 16–18 = moderate weight gain; 19–21 =
  extreme weight gain.
- **Calories = body weight × coefficient.** His worked example: a 200 lb person picking a
  moderate cut (coefficient 10) → 2,000 calories/day. Adjust up (e.g. 10 → 11) if you
  lose weight too fast; exercise level and physical job "doesn't really matter" to the
  formula.
- **Protein = body weight × 1 (grams).** 200 lb → 200 g protein/day. He dismisses the
  "you can only absorb ~7 g at a time" objection.
- **1 lb of lean meat ≈ 100 g protein** (lean white fish, ground turkey breast, ground
  beef, shrimp). So 200 g protein ≈ 2 lb of meat "if you exclusively ate meat."
- **Protein first, then spend the remainder however you want.** In the 200 lb / 2,000 cal
  cut example, two 1-lb turkey meals at 560 cal each = 1,120 cal, leaving ~880 cal to
  spend on drinks, a business dinner, a pint of ice cream, or cookies — "it doesn't
  matter."
- **He personally eats over 1 g/lb** because he likes protein, it fills him up, and he
  feels good on it — not because it's required.
- **Dessert every night is compatible with a six-pack** because protein and total
  calories are controlled; if he "just eats cookies" he isn't full, so he eats protein to
  stay full.
- **Greens covered by a multivitamin** ("I have a multivitamin and I continue to stay
  alive").
- He has tried many diets over the years — keto, high carb, low carb, high fat,
  intermittent fasting, alternate-day fasting, carnivore — and for the last ~10 years has
  eaten "pretty much the exact same way."
- Reports **social proof**: entrepreneurs who eat around him adopt it and "never go back";
  a couple of guys told him they lost ~50 lb using the earlier version of this method.

## Appearance/diet facts (dated, self-reported — 2023-05-14)

- **Same daily breakfast + lunch; dinner is the variable "flex" meal.** (self-reported)
- **Breakfast ≈ 360 cal / ~50 g protein:** a ~200 cal protein bar/"brownie" (or a Quest
  Bar — "200 calories and 20 g of protein" — when traveling/at the airport) plus a shake
  at **30 g protein / 160 cal**. (self-reported)
- **Lunch:** ~1 lb turkey (**560 cal**) plus one cup of rice; puts him at ~150 g protein
  and ~1,140 cal cumulative for the day. (self-reported)
- **Dinner ("the big flex meal"):** he *also* tends to eat ~1 lb of protein at dinner —
  by choice, not necessity — because he loves eating protein and it keeps him full.
  (self-reported)
- **His own maintenance ≈ 3,000 cal/day** (states he's "more than 200 [lb]" but uses 200 ×
  15 = 3,000 for the math). (self-reported)
- **20-year dessert streak:** claims he has eaten dessert "virtually every night for 20
  years… never skipped dessert" while maintaining a six-pack "for that entire period of
  time." (self-reported)
- Staple protein sources he names for himself: **turkey** (eats it for both meals in the
  cut example), plus generic lean meats; protein bars and shakes for breakfast.
  (self-reported)
