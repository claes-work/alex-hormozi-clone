# Why The Best Business to Start in 2026 is a Skool Community

> ⚠️ FORMAT NOTE: Despite the title, this is **not** a teaching video. It is a ~37-second
> parody advertisement for [Skool](https://www.skool.com) — a shot-for-shot spoof of the
> Old Spice "The Man Your Man Could Smell Like" / "I'm on a horse" commercial. Alex delivers
> it in-character as a comedic pitchman. It contains marketing claims and platform stats, not
> a step-by-step playbook. Treat the "playbook" content below as the *promise/positioning*
> Skool markets, not an operational how-to.

## Summary

A short comedic Skool promo in which Alex parodies the famous Old Spice ad to pitch starting a
paid (or free) online community on Skool. The core sell: running a community is like hosting
your own party where you decide who's invited, it can be free or paid, and you talk about
whatever you want. He cites a "real leaderboard from last month" — top earners (Bill at
$62K/month teaching bookkeeping; Jamie at $41K/month teaching women to invest) and an average
paid community earning $1,360/month — and dangles a trip to his Las Vegas headquarters for
top-10 communities. Closes with the tagline framing that "one out of two businesses on Skool
create money online" and a call to start for free. All figures are self-reported Skool
marketing stats for a platform Alex promotes.

## Why a Skool community (the business-model case)

As pitched in the ad (positioning, not a proof):

- **It's your "party."** You decide who's invited, set the topic, and set the vibe — full
  control over membership, content, and tone.
- **Free or paid — your choice.** The model flexes: monetize with a paid membership or run it
  free to build audience/goodwill.
- **Low barrier, upside potential.** "Click the link to start for free" — framed as an easy,
  low-cost business to start, with a leaderboard showing a few members earning tens of
  thousands per month.
- **Teach what you already know.** The example earners monetize a single competency
  (bookkeeping, investing for women) — i.e., package existing knowledge into a community.
- **Aspirational ceiling.** Becoming a top-10 community earns a trip to Skool HQ in Las Vegas —
  gamified status as a retention/motivation hook.

## Community-building playbook (detailed bullets)

The ad implies, rather than teaches, the following. Captured as the *marketed model*:

- **Build:** Pick one thing you know and can teach; the community is the container ("your
  party") for that knowledge and its members.
- **Launch:** Start free on Skool ("start for free") to remove friction; the platform is the
  distribution/hosting layer.
- **Monetize:** Convert to (or start as) a **paid** community — Alex explicitly says it "can be
  free or it can be paid, it's up to you." Leaderboard examples anchor the income upside.
- **Retain / motivate:** Gamified leaderboard + a real-world reward (fly-out to the Vegas HQ for
  top-10 communities) create status incentives to keep creators engaged and growing.
- **Realistic expectation set:** Average paid community ≈ $1,360/month (self-reported) — the top
  numbers are outliers, not the norm.

> Note: the ad does **not** cover engagement mechanics, content cadence, onboarding, pricing
> strategy, churn tactics, or acquisition — the operational "how." Those gaps should be filled
> from longer-form community-building sources during any L3 synthesis.
