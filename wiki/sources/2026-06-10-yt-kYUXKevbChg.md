# They Pass My Exam and Triple Their Income (Then Ghost Me)

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a test-prep business for future board-certified behavior analysts making about $80K/month with high margins. The owner worries that students leave after passing the exam, making continuity hard, and is exploring university collaborations. Alex says that is fine because the product is close to money: if students triple income after certification, the business should document before/after earnings, use that data in pricing and university sales, add an alumni/job-board incentive to collect outcomes, and eventually think one level up by selling partnerships to universities.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A test-prep product that directly increases income can price and sell against documented economic outcomes.
- The company should collect current earnings at entry and post-outcome earnings after students graduate or get jobs.
- A rebate, alumni membership, or job-board access can incentivize students to submit earnings data.
- Published median outcomes let the company frame value like universities do with graduate earnings.
- University partnerships mean chunking the business up one level: the real customer can become the university, not only the student.
- The owner should ask "what would it take" to make university partnerships exclusive or multi-year.
- If certified graduates are scarce, the company can eventually monetize placement by hoarding the scarce resource and earning on both customer and placement sides.
- For university sales, trade shows and conferences are useful because the buyers are there.
- A higher-end strategy is to hire someone who has already sold into academia and is effectively bringing a relationship "black book."
