# Helping an Entrepreneur With Three Businesses Pick the One

## Summary

Alex coaches a financial-services entrepreneur who is exiting a partner conflict and has
three possible paths: rebuild the same business, scale a real-estate portfolio, or keep a
junk-removal company that feeds real-estate leads. Alex's advice is to choose the main
thing he is already exceptional at. He treats the other businesses as hedges against risk
that actually increase risk by reducing focus on the one sure path.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-18, Alex says a founder who was number two out of roughly 3,000 reps should
  strongly favor rebuilding that known game, even if a non-compete forces a new local
  network.
- He says the founder can likely rebuild faster the second time because he already has
  the lessons from the first run.
- He distinguishes side assets from the "main game"; he personally owns apartment
  buildings but does not treat real estate as his main wealth-building game.
- He says private equity owners are far wealthier on average than real-estate owners:
  real estate makes more millionaires, while private equity makes more billionaires.
- He says side hustles can become hedges against one's own risk, but they increase risk
  by lowering the chance that the sure thing becomes the sure thing.
- He tells the founder to ask what he would do with a gun to his head if he had to make
  money, then do that thing.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Don't you think you could do it faster the second time?"

> "Don't try to make your side hustle your main hustle."

> "Do the thing that you would do when you need to make money and you will make money way more."
