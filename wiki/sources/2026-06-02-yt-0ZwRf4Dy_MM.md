# How to Sell More Franchises (Without Selling Them)

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a barbecue restaurant group that wants to grow systemwide franchise revenue. The owner believes franchises are bought rather than sold because buying one changes the franchisee's lifestyle. Alex agrees that bad-fit buyers should be filtered, but says the core constraint is not sales technique: every franchise problem ultimately points back to unit economics. If the restaurant-level economics were compelling enough, selling franchises would become much easier.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The North Star metric for franchising is unit economics.
- Difficulty selling franchises often means the franchise offer is not compelling enough.
- The company should fix the restaurant economics before looking for franchise-sales tactics.
- The same economics that make corporate expansion unattractive also make the franchise less attractive to buyers.
- A smaller number of highly profitable corporate locations may create more enterprise value than a larger but low-royalty franchise system.
- The operator should spend a long focused period improving profitability down to the penny before chasing 100-location systemwide revenue.

## Notable verbatim quotes
