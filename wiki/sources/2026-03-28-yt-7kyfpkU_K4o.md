# What Do High-Level Buyers Actually Want?

## Summary
In a live Q&A, a caller named Josh — who runs an Australian business helping universities retain students — asks how to sell to senior executives (his buyers' bosses' boss) rather than the front-line student-engagement staff he's used to. Alex advises that higher-level enterprise buyers care about ROI math, not narrative, and that the deliverable (training vs. data tool) barely matters — only the likelihood of getting customers to stay, pay, and renew. Alex also coaches making data-sharing a contractual requirement of working together, so results can be tracked and proven.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that in enterprise sales, the more money the buyer makes, the less they care about the narrative and the more they want the ROI math ("what is your return on this?").
- As of March 2026, Alex maintains that stories still sell and a meta-narrative can help, but senior buyers only act once shown the math behind the return.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises making the client's data-sharing contingent/mandatory as a condition of the engagement, framed as necessary for a proprietary technology to tailor and improve the solution over time.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the specific components of an offer (a data tool vs. training) barely matter to the buyer; the only thing that matters is the likelihood their customers stay, pay longer, and renew year after year — "how we get there matters just really little."
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the entrepreneur's fixation on their own deliverables as being "in the weeds" with the "entrepreneur hat on," versus what the customer actually wants (the outcome), using a fitness analogy: the customer doesn't care about keto vs. cutting calories, only fitting into their high-school jeans.
- Caller claim (unverified): Josh reports the Australian government introduced 2026 policies requiring universities to increase low-socioeconomic enrollment and to direct roughly $60 million of student funding to clubs/societies to support students.
- Caller claim (unverified): Josh's data tool is a $30,000/year subscription; his prior training LTV was $34,000; annual churn was ~20%, largely because clients wouldn't share retention data to prove training worked.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "For enterprise sales, the more the person makes, the more they don't care about the narrative."

> "Of course stories sell ... but like until you show them the math behind it, which is like what is your return on this? And that's what they want."

> "The only thing that matters to them is the likelihood that they get their customers to stay and pay longer and renew year after year. And so how we get there matters just really little."

> "If you're a customer I don't care if we're going to be doing keto or cutting my calories, like I just want to be able to fit in my high school jeans. So just tell me how much there."

> "You have to get this data so we can implement it in real time ... So you have to do it. So these are the three things that we'll do to make it easier for you to actually get this data back to us so we can help you make more money."
