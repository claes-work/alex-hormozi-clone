# Helping a $5.5M Auto Hail Repair Company Scale to $15M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of an auto hail repair company doing about $5.5M and targeting $15M. The owner asks about building a senior leadership bench and compensation. Alex reframes the role away from true C-suite toward VP/director/general-manager-level operations, recommends targeted outreach for an operator with relevant auto or repair experience, and explains profit-share and phantom-equity-like structures that align leaders without giving up control.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- At roughly $5M, the needed hire is probably not true C-suite; it is more likely a VP, director, GM, or right-hand operator.
- For this level, useful recruiting channels include LinkedIn outreach and sometimes Indeed, with targeted outreach to people already doing similar work in comparable auto businesses.
- A serious targeted search may require reaching out to hundreds of candidates and, if geography is a constraint, using relocation/signing incentives.
- The operator should be a leader people admire and go to when problems happen, not merely a systems/process person who avoids people.
- Good operators are usually "fixing people," not the technical work itself, which makes leadership transferable across businesses.
- Compensation depends on the candidate's leverage and track record; someone who has already scaled similar businesses can justify more.
- A 10-20% leadership profit pool can align leaders with owner-like thinking as the business grows.
- Employees often say they want equity but do not want the risk or control that comes with actual equity; profit share and sale-bonus/phantom structures can provide cash-flow and exit upside without giving control.
- Store/location leaders in private expansion often need a tie to the bottom line to truly decentralize operations.
- Profit-share percentages should be reverse-engineered from target OTE, not chosen abstractly.
