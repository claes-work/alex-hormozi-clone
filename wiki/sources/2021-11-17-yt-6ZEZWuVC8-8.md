# Millionaire explains why time is NOT the most precious resource

## Summary

In this 2021 re-share of an older recorded talk (originally made for a Facebook
group), Hormozi argues that the entrepreneur's single most valuable resource is
**not time but attention** — "the new entrepreneurial golden resource." He claims
high performers and billionaires scale by ruthlessly protecting and consolidating
their attention: quieting their environments, cutting toxic relationships and open
"loops," saying no to good opportunities, and doing fewer things rather than more.
His prescription is to close the "leaks" that drain attention so it can be
concentrated on the one thing being grown.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- (2021) Most people mistakenly believe **time** is the number one resource ("time
  is money"); Hormozi argues **attention is the golden resource** — the true single
  most valuable resource an entrepreneur has.
- (2021) You have a finite amount of attention; time is useless if you are not
  actually present ("potent") to spend attention on it. Being "always everywhere and
  at the same time nowhere" is the failure mode.
- (2021) The traits that make someone an entrepreneur (hustle, out-working everyone,
  being a builder) get you "from zero to one" but not further; "the things that got
  you here are not the things that get you there." Identity must stay fluid and change
  with each stage of growth.
- (2021) Scaling requires growing other people, which requires free attention and
  bandwidth — so you must remove attention drains and delegate.
- (2021) "In order to grow you need to do less" — via both delegation and choosing
  what NOT to do. The better you get, the better the opportunities you must say no to;
  this requires discipline, which is why higher-level entrepreneurship is harder.
- (2021) Illustrates with gym owners: an owner with four gyms can make less than they
  did with one gym, because attention per facility drops so far that fixed costs eat
  the margin (grossing $1-3M but taking nothing home).
- (2021) He paid a coach for nine months, 90 minutes every day, purely to learn to
  manage his attention / "how to think" — talking about what he was thinking about.
  His business "blew up" as a result.
- (2021) "Open loops / open cycles" — unresolved issues (toxic relationships with
  parents, friends, health) — silently consume units of attention. Uses a "bowl of
  marbles" metaphor: each marble is a unit of attention; quieting the small drains
  frees potency for the one thing you're growing.
- (2021) The "Tony Stark" image of the entrepreneur is a farce; real billionaires are
  quiet, keep clean/pristine spaces (office, home, phone, computer), are hard to reach
  (his billionaire friend's phone only rings for whitelisted contacts), and have low
  tolerance for negativity and drama.
- (2021) Attention is what grants the ability to "see" — he equates wisdom with seeing
  what others cannot, which comes from having attention/"horsepower."
- (2021) Focus on **one thing**: kill side hustles and side businesses; "one fat
  paycheck is better than two." You can build a $100M single-service business (e.g. a
  roofing business that only repairs roofs). Having more businesses is a farce.
- (2021) He quit drinking — not for religious reasons — because evening drinking masked
  unresolved anxieties (attention drains); when he stopped, "everything got really
  loud" and he had to confront and resolve those things (~6 months).
- (2021) Claims that after telling 2-3 entrepreneurs to consolidate and kill
  businesses, two of them 3x'd their total revenue within 30 days "just by getting
  their attention back."
- (2021) Context / self-reported scale at time of re-share: he identifies himself as
  the owner of Acquisition.com, a "portfolio companies" group doing "about 85 million
  a year"; notes the numbers inside the older video reflect an earlier time.
