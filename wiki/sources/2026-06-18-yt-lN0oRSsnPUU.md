# Why Your Leads Are Feast or Famine (And How to Fix It)

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a medical billing company whose lead flow depends almost entirely on a podcast. He recommends 5x-ing the existing channel by using outbound to invite ideal customers and whales onto the podcast, then adding conferences as a second channel. His conference tactics include paying for a booth, choosing a high-traffic right-side entrance spot, getting on stage if possible, collecting leads through giveaways, trading a speaking fee for list access, and using slide opt-ins plus qualifying questions.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - physician and medical billing company owner. Her revenue, margins, team size, podcast history, avatar, and lead-flow issue are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The least operationally disruptive move is to do more of a channel that already works.
- A podcast can become outbound by inviting potential customers or whales as guests.
- Increasing from one podcast per week to one per day is a straightforward 5x on that activity.
- Conferences are likely a strong channel for this medical billing avatar.
- Booth placement matters; Alex says many attendees turn right when entering, so a booth on the right near the entrance can produce more ROI.
- A giveaway revealed later in the event can collect contact information over multiple days.
- A speaker can trade a stated speaking fee for list access or an email send.
- Slide opt-ins with a few qualifying questions can identify engaged prospects from the audience.
- Conferences can be monetized through list access, survey/slide opt-in, and booth giveaways.
