# Why You Aren't Getting as Many Leads as You Want

## Summary

Solo Alex Hormozi clip (single speaker throughout). Alex argues the primary reason
most businesses don't get enough leads is simply that **not enough people know about
them** — the market is vastly larger, and personal "awareness" vastly smaller, than
founders assume. He uses two anecdotes from his own book launch (the *Leads* book /
"last launch") to dismantle the "I've saturated my market" belief: even after spending
$4–5M advertising, most of his own followers didn't know he had a book coming, and an
accidental 24-hour Amazon leak that sold 72 copies caused no damage because awareness
requires active, sustained promotion — nobody promotes your thing for you. His takeaway:
founders wildly underestimate how much effort it takes to become known.

## Key claims (2026-02-23, paraphrased unless quoted)

- A fundamental cause of insufficient leads is simply that not enough people know your
  business/product exists — the market is enormous and individual attempts at being
  known are small and short-lived ("ephemeral"). (paraphrase)
- At the previous launch (~two years prior), roughly **19 of 20 people** who approached
  Alex on the street didn't know he had written a book. (paraphrase, specific ratio)
- At this launch, awareness had improved only slightly: about **1 in 4** had heard a
  book launch was coming; **3 of 4 still hadn't** — and this is among people who already
  knew who he was, after spending ~**$4–5M**. (paraphrase, specific figures)
- The "I've saturated my market / no one new could find out" belief is almost always
  wrong; Alex wants to give as many examples as possible to disprove it. (paraphrase)
- ~A month before the *Leads* launch, a team member prepping the book on Amazon
  accidentally set it **live**; for ~24 hours it was purchasable and **72 people bought
  it in ~60 minutes**. (paraphrase, anecdote)
- Despite Alex's initial panic that "the launch is ruined," **nothing happened** — the
  leak caused no real damage. (paraphrase)
- ★ Core mechanism (why the leak didn't matter): a leaked product only spreads if
  **someone actively takes it and promotes/advertises it**. Awareness doesn't propagate
  on its own — even Alex, "shouting from the rooftops," still had 19/20 followers unaware.
  72 random strangers buying quietly created no momentum. (paraphrase — the load-bearing
  distribution insight of the clip)
- Signal of how quiet it was: one buyer publicly asked on LinkedIn how the book was
  "somehow already in my Kindle," and no one connected the dots. (paraphrase, anecdote)
- Lesson: no one knows you or your business exists; everyone is absorbed in their own
  lives, so becoming known takes far more sustained effort than founders estimate.
  (paraphrase)
- Building the brand he has now was even more work than he anticipated it would be, and
  he already expected it to be a lot. (paraphrase)
