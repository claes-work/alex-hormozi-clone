# Helping an Agency Owner Stuck at $90K Scale to $900K

## Summary

Alex tells a four-month-old cannabis advertising business that hiring is premature. The
owner's reported true revenue after pass-through ad spend leaves too little room for
payroll, so the immediate problem is the model: charge much more or make delivery far
more efficient before scaling.

## Key claims

- The owner initially reports $600,000 revenue but clarifies that most is collected ad
  spend and estimates roughly $90,000-$100,000 annualized gross profit; all are unverified.
- As of March 2026, Alex says the owner does not yet have a people problem because the
  economics cannot support replacing the founder's labor.
- The founder is normally the most valuable early employee, so a model that barely pays
  that person will be still harder to staff with hired labor.
- He diagnoses the service as too much work for too little money and gives two stark
  options: raise revenue per customer approximately tenfold or make delivery approximately
  ten times more efficient.
- Attempting to scale the current structure would multiply its weakness; the model should
  be repaired before additional customers or employees are added.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You don't have a people problem. You have a model problem."

> "You're offering too much for too little."

> "You need to fix what you're currently doing so that you can scale it."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: separate pass-through billings from economic revenue; fix price or delivery leverage before hiring.
