# How do you hire A-players with limited cash?

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a corporate-training company doing just under $2M/year that wants to reach $10M but has limited cash for a senior sales hire. Alex explains that every business incurs some form of debt: financial debt, talent debt, operational debt, data debt, or tech debt. For a bootstrapped founder, he recommends first building a reliable acquisition channel and backfilling the founder's existing work, because true rainmakers are expensive and often do not need the company unless the vision is unusually compelling.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - corporate-training owner. His revenue, business model, cash-flow constraint, SEO/word-of-mouth acquisition, and B2B/B2C mix are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Founders are disproportionately compensated because they do more jobs than most people for a long period.
- Every business incurs debt; the choice is which debt is easiest to pay back.
- Taking capital can avoid some talent, infrastructure, data, and tech debt, but creates financial debt.
- Bootstrapping avoids financial debt while creating talent, infrastructure, and tech debt.
- Equity can attract a stronger person when cash compensation is limited, but the hire is partly betting on the founder.
- A $2M business with about 20% margin may have enough cash flow for one strong hire, but that is a large founder-risk bet.
- Cash flow is the oxygen of the business and can be improved by changing money-model variables.
- The deeper problem is likely the lack of a reliable acquisition channel, especially if SEO is threatened by AI search.
- A cheaper first hire may be someone who backfills the founder's current work so the founder can build the growth channel.
- True rainmakers are expensive because people who can reliably grow often can do it on their own.
- B2B corporate training likely has more operating leverage than B2C because the same sale motion can add zeros.
