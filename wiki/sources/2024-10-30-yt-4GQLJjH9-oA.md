# This Business Was Stuck. Here's How I Fixed It.

## Summary

Alex Hormozi walks through how one of his portfolio companies broke a 6-month plateau (~$570K/month) and reached a $10M/year run rate in 4 months, using only marketing and sales optimizations rather than anything new. He details the fixes to a webinar-based funnel across three stages — show rate, booking rate, and close rate — each of which he lifted ~33% or more, and closes with generalizable takeaways (measure what matters, execution over plan, use what you've got, better beats new). The company is a coaching client (context); the transferable content is Alex's tactical advice.

## Key claims (Alex's advice)

- **Diagnose the pipeline as a chain of constraints**: CTR → opt-in → schedule → show → close. Actually look at the data (attendance rates, drop-off points, engagement spikes) instead of "shooting from the hip."
- **Raise webinar show rates with multi-channel reminders**: he added a 9-email sequence, SMS, ringless voicemail, and behavior-triggered video/"behind the scenes" content so you reach people who prefer text, email, or voicemail. SMS has a ~95-99% open rate and was likely the single biggest lever.
- **The three most important reminders** are 24 hours before, the morning of, and 1 hour prior — these should be SMS. One reminder a day before an event is fine; "no one remembers what they signed up for this morning, let alone five days ago."
- **Emails written benefit-driven and hitting pain from multiple angles** (pain, speed, ease, being stuck/plateaued) drive attendance, because pain drives motivation. Alternate pain with proof; use the value equation (dream outcome, decrease risk with proof, decrease time, decrease effort).
- **CTAs inside emails that get opens/clicks/actions improve deliverability** and keep you out of the promo/spam tab.
- Result: show rate 18% → 24% (a 33% increase). A 33% show-rate lift produces the same result as a 33% close-rate lift but is usually easier and automatable — one-time work for permanent revenue gains.
- **Obsess over the first 5 minutes (and first 60 seconds)** of any webinar/call/content — rescripting the first 5 minutes of a 3-hour presentation at another portfolio company added a 20% close-rate lift. Beginners meander for 1-2 minutes and lose ~40% of the audience before getting to the point.
- **Open with proof-promise-plan**: proof (someone just like them succeeded so they identify), promise (exactly what they get from and after the webinar), plan (set expectations for the next 60-90 minutes).
- **Lean into "live"**: state the date, time, weather, current events. Live converts better ("no idea why, I just know it works"). If automated content struggles to convert, go live frequently for a while to tighten it.
- **Use takeaway selling**: clearly state who you do and don't work with (qualification magnetizes the right people, wards off the wrong ones), then apply ethical scarcity by stating your real constraint (only X slots because only X people on the team).
- **Ethical, compliant scarcity/urgency**: say "typically we book out" (fine if true more than half the time), first-come-first-serve, and call out "sold out" times in real time ("Wednesday morning is sold out"). Have attendees announce bookings in the chat to create proof, open loops, and a booking frenzy.
- Result: booking rate +33%; total calls booked 58 → 102 per webinar (a 77% increase) from the same ad spend.
- **Fix sales close rate by working front-to-back**: replace weak rapport-building intros with a framed intro — introduce credentials, edify the webinar host, ask "what resonated most," then reframe the call as qualifying them for a guarantee/bonus (not selling), which flips them into earning your favor.
- **Congruence / "baton pass"**: ad → webinar headline → webinar open → sales call → onboarding must all match. Simple to understand, hard to do, and makes a lot more money.
- **Stay chunked up in Discovery**: avoid "traps" (getting into the weeds); the salesperson asks questions and stays high-level. Reps who "get cute" and play consultant/expert after they ramp start slipping — they aren't the ones doing delivery.
- **Match pitch complexity to the offer**: DIY / done-with-you → keep it simple (1-2-3, three-pillar pitch); done-for-you → show many steps (11, 27, 38) so they believe it works but want you to do the work. Chunk up extra steps into buckets. If unsure, default to the three-pillar pitch.
- **Pre-pitch invitation**: summarize their situation, say you can likely help, then ask "where do you want to go from here?" so they invite you to pitch.
- **Execution beats plan**: role-play/practice with the team (the founder had never role-played Discovery), track granular KPIs (not just close rate — show %, offer-made %, obstacle reasons) to personalize coaching.
- **Ruthlessly prioritize your best closers**: route the best leads to the best closers (best-to-best), give them the best times/calls, cut under-KPI reps' calendars in half and manage them out if they don't recover. "If your best closers get the best leads you'll sell more; if your worst closers get the worst leads you'll lose the least."
- **Pay closers on cash collected**, weighted to paid-in-full, so a PIF sale pays ~5x a payment-plan close — aligns closer incentive with owner, produces stronger closes, and better long-term retention.
- Result: close rate 14% → 35% (~2.4x).
- **Abdication vs. delegation**: if stats drop when you hand something off, you abdicated, not delegated. Founders leave revenue generation too early; "know where the bodies are buried."
- **Takeaways**: (1) keep the main thing the main thing — revenue increases drop disproportionately to profit; (2) execution over plan; (3) measurement as intervention — just measuring show rate/profit/weight improves it; look twice at stats you hate; (4) "better" is lower-risk than "new" because what you do already works; (5) use what you've got — everything in business is perceived benefit/scarcity/urgency, so communicate the advantages you already have.
- Overall results: CAC $5,763 → $2,868 (roughly halved); ROAS 1.62 → 3.96; revenue ~$569K → $860K/month (passed $10M run rate, sustained); ~30.7% profit margin → $250-300K/month profit.
