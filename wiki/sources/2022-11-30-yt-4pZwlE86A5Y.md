# A YouTuber Asked Me "How To Business". Here's My Answer

## Summary

A recorded advisory session (mini-series pilot) in which Hormozi helps YouTuber "Unspeakable" (Nathan Graham, ~200M monthly views) choose which monetization vehicle to pursue across his main channel, a car shop/parts business (0–60), and a possible consumable/energy-drink brand. Hormozi's answer: double down on the media asset he's already best at, add co-hosts to scale beyond himself, and drop the lower-likelihood plays.

## Key claims

- Opportunity should be scored as: (number of units sold × gross profit per unit) ÷ (competitive mode); Hormozi's own model is few clients but very high $/deal, still worth it. (paraphrase)
- A creator's existing audience is a "launch pad," but growth requires new distribution plus a product good enough that the first ring tells their friends — most people fail by just slapping their name on a white-label product. (paraphrase)
- Media is one of the most profitable businesses on the planet (Warren Buffett bought newspapers); uniquely, the marketing IS the fulfillment. Sponsorships go straight to the bottom line — Unspeakable could ~5x income tomorrow just by building out the sponsorship function. (paraphrase)
- The recommended play is "do more of what you're already doing": recruit 1–3 co-hosts, rotate them in, transition the brand toward a person-independent identity (like Red Bull) so the founder can phase out. (paraphrase)
- Lower-likelihood or cash-eating vehicles (an energy drink, a self-built software game, the car-parts marketplace) should be dropped or deferred; keep the car shop only because it's fully integrated into content and already has an operating partner. (paraphrase)
