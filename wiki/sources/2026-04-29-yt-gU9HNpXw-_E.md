# You Will Never Be Sharper Than You Are Right Now

## Summary

Alex says he thinks frequently about the end of his life and inevitable cognitive decline.
Because fluid intelligence peaks earlier in adulthood, he frames the present as likely
the sharpest he will ever be. That thought, plus the fact that younger people will
replace everyone, lowers the emotional stakes of enormous ambitions: the universe does
not respond to ultimatums about what must happen.

## Key claims

- Alex says thinking about the final years of his life is close to an obsession.
- He believes cognitive decline is inevitable in the back half of life.
- He frames the present as the sharpest he will ever be.
- The replaceability of people lowers the stakes of personal ambitions.
- Alex says threatening the universe with "or else" does not change reality.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I will actually never be sharper than I am today for the rest of my life."

> "Humans are all replaceable."

> "The universe is undefeated."
