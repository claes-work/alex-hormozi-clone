## Summary

In this short clip, Alex Hormozi coaches a business owner (running a paid Skool community that grew from ~20 to 100,000 members in four months, strong in Latin America) who has just started a free trial and is anxious that it is dragging his MRR down and attracting people who cancel immediately. Hormozi reframes the decision as a pure math problem rather than an emotional one: a free trial adds a second conversion point and changes churn behavior, so the operator must model earnings-per-click across both the trial funnel and the paid-upfront funnel and compare which total is higher. He gives specific cancel-rate breakevens and urges the owner to let the test run its full window before judging it.

## Key claims (paraphrase unless quoted; all Alex-attributed, dated 2026-02-25)

- **Free trials create two conversion points instead of one.** You get a higher conversion on the landing page (more people start), but then a second conversion — trial-to-paid — that reduces the count. (paraphrase)
- **Post-trial churn is lower.** Hormozi says he is "pretty sure we have the data on this": customers who came through a free trial churn less because they already tried the product, whereas customers who pay upfront churn higher in that first month. (paraphrase)
- **Decide it with an earnings-per-click spreadsheet.** For each funnel, model: per 100 clicks to the page → % who take the trial → % who convert to paying → LTV of that customer given the (lower) churn. Compare against the paid-upfront funnel (100 clicks → converts → churn → LTV). Whichever top-line number is higher wins. "It's just math." (paraphrase; "it's just math" verbatim)
- **Cancel-rate breakevens (given a 4x lift in front-end conversion).** If half the trial-takers cancel, you have still doubled sales; if two-thirds cancel, you are still up 50%; if three-quarters cancel, you are net even. (paraphrase — precise mechanics)
- **A "start for free" CTA also raises clicks, not just conversion.** Offering "start my group for free" versus "go buy my group" gets more people to click over in the first place, so cost-per-click (or, for organic content, clicks-per-piece-of-content) is a second lever the free offer pulls beyond back-end conversion. (paraphrase)
- **Don't quit the test early.** "If you back out now, you'll never know" and will keep wondering; the change is reversible ("you can always go back"), so run it the full window (he suggests waiting the remaining seven days / the first month) to get clean data, then decide once and never revisit. (paraphrase; short phrases verbatim)
- **Lower emotional reaction to immediate cancellers.** The owner admitted manually kicking out people who join the trial and cancel right away; Hormozi's answer is to ignore the feeling and "just do the math." (paraphrase)
- Context (operator, not Alex): the member reports going from ~20 to 100,000 in a paid Skool community in four months after adding Instagram to a YouTube-only strategy, and that his clients prefer Skool ("like Facebook") over a Facebook group. (context only — not persona training data)
