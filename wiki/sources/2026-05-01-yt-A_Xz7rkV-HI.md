# How to Charge $250 an Hour as a Videographer

## Summary

Alex roasts a videographer community page and says the strongest promise is buried:
helping people charge $250 for a one-hour video. He recommends leading with that promise,
using a five-step mechanism as the plan, and adding proof that the result is real. He
also gives a broader B2B persuasion rule: once the monetary value is obvious, the page's
job is to reduce perceived risk and friction so buyers believe the outcome can happen
for them.

## Key claims

- A vague "take videography to the next level" promise should be replaced by a concrete
  economic result such as charging $250 for a one-hour video within four weeks.
- Alex recommends structuring the page around promise, proof, and plan.
- The plan should explain the mechanism: brand positioning, pricing/packages, sales
  process, client acquisition, and delivery under an hour.
- Vague feature labels such as "exclusive access" or "resources" should be made concrete.
- In B2B offers, the buyer often already sees the ROI; persuasion focuses on making them
  believe the result will happen for them.
- Offer features or bonuses should be framed by what they do, why they matter, and what
  time or money they save.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Promise proof plan, very simple."

> "Just tell them what it is."

> "All of the effort is decreasing the risk associated with the purchase."
