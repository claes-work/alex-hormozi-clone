# Alex Hormozi Reveals The MOST Powerful Marketing Strategy in 2023

## Summary
In this conversation clip (Alex Hormozi with a co-speaker addressed as "Andy"), Alex argues that word of mouth is the single most powerful and durable marketing force because it is the only channel that is exponential rather than linear: paid ads, cold outreach, and other tactics return a fixed ratio for every dollar or effort, while word of mouth compounds (one tells two, two tell four). He frames it as the counterforce every scaling business needs, since ad costs inevitably rise and colder audiences convert worse over time. He distills enduring businesses into two paths — sell things people never stop buying, or build a company people never stop selling for — and shares a "marketing gods" thought experiment he runs on his portfolio CEOs to force referral-first thinking.

## Key claims
- Word of mouth matters more every year and is the only marketing force that is still genuinely "viral"; everything else (ads, cold outreach) is linear — a fixed return ratio per unit of spend or effort.
- Word of mouth is the only channel that is exponential in nature: one customer tells two, two tell four, four tell eight.
- To have an enduring business you must have "the monster of word of mouth" working for you rather than against you — building your reputation rather than eroding it.
- There are only two ways to build an enduring company: sell stuff people never stop buying from you, or build a company where people never stop selling for you.
- As you scale ad spend you reach colder, lower-trust, lower-fit audiences, so cost to acquire a customer rises and conversion on colder audiences falls — two forces working against you inevitably.
- Ad costs only move one direction over time (up); conversion on progressively colder audiences only moves one direction (down). Word of mouth is the non-linear force that counteracts both by lowering effective cost of acquisition when one customer brings five.
- You can become a millionaire (even a "deca-millionaire") on transactional selling alone if you learn to sell, even selling low-quality products — but real scale requires strong word of mouth.
- His scaling advice: operate at a scale "way below your friends" until you figure out how to get everyone who buys to tell a friend; then the returns on remaining advertising compound in the right direction instead of degrading linearly.
- A mental exercise he runs on his portfolio CEOs: the "marketing gods" ban all marketing and delete every customer except one, and every future customer must come from that one — forcing you to rethink how you treat customers.
- Self-reported biography: he came from the direct-response world and started at 18 writing classified newspaper ads for a credit-repair booklet he wrote (pre-internet).
- Generosity/reputation framing: small acts of goodwill (e.g., stopping to answer a stranger's questions at an airport) return as the "invisible hand" of word of mouth; the times you fail to help become "splinters" that, in aggregate, form either a strong stick you can wield or one wielded against you.
