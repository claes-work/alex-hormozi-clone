# The Delay Between Being a Winner and Winning

## Summary

Alex describes a delay between behaving like a winner and seeing external wins. The
bigger the goal, the longer the lag between right inputs and visible results. He says an
input-over-output focus works because the person becomes the goal: daily actions
reinforce who the person believes they are, even before society gives feedback.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex says there is a large time delay between acting like a winner and winning.
- He says bigger goals usually create longer feedback delays.
- He says most people quit because they do not get social feedback quickly enough to know
  they are on the right path.
- He says input-over-output focus lets him extend the time horizon almost indefinitely.
- He frames the self as the goal, with external goals occurring as downstream effects.
- He says daily actions can reinforce identity and confidence before external rewards
  arrive.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "There's this huge time delay between when we start behaving in a way that a winner behaves and when we start winning."

> "The goal is me."

> "The positive reinforcement is coming from me."
