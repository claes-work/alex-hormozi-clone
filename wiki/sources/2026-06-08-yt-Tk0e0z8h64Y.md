# Stop Using a Warm Sales Process on Cold Leads

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a $6M video-testimonial agency selling to mid-market and enterprise B2B software companies. The company has grown through inbound and word of mouth, but outbound and LinkedIn ads produce much weaker close rates. Alex diagnoses the issue as a warm sales process being applied to cold leads. He prescribes a cold-first sales machine: VSL/pre-frame, qualification, training cadence, and a split offer with premium upfront testimonial production plus ongoing quantity-oriented proof refresh.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A sales process built for warm introductions will underperform on cold traffic.
- Building for cold usually still works for warm audiences; building only for warm does not travel in the other direction.
- Cold traffic needs pre-framing before the close call, not just better reps.
- A VSL can move belief-building earlier, shorten the sales cycle, and improve sales utilization.
- A training cadence is required so salespeople follow the new process rather than improvising.
- A testimonial agency can add continuity by separating high-resonance "quality" testimonials from recurring "quantity" proof.
- Older testimonials lose relevance; ongoing proof keeps the company visibly current.
