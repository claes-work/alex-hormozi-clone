# The Winner's Mindset Is a Contradiction

## Summary

This excerpt discusses the tension between pessimism, cynicism, and ambition. Alex says
critics are often right in frequency but not necessarily right in magnitude: family and
local peers may correctly reject many ideas, yet miss the one idea that matters. He
frames local status games as noise when someone is playing a wider game with more
players and a longer time horizon.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex frames a winner's mindset as a tension between paranoia in the present and faith
  in the future.
- Critics close to someone can be right often while still being wrong about the outcome
  that matters most.
- Local peers may try to pull someone into their own status game, where they know the
  rules and can win.
- A person pursuing a bigger outcome should evaluate criticism against the wider game
  they are actually playing.
- Information from people outside the relevant game is often disproportionate noise.
- A long enough time horizon changes the weight of local criticism.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "They are right more times but they are not more right."

> "They're playing a different game than you."

> "You're playing a much wider game that includes more players."
