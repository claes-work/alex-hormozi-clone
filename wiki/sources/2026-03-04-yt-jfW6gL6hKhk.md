# If I Wanted to Make My First $100K in 2026, I'd Do This

## Summary

Solo Alex Hormozi video laying out a six-step roadmap to making and banking a first
$100,000 in the bank (not revenue). He frames the goal emotionally: the moment he felt
wealthiest in his life was hitting $100K in savings, because it bought freedom from
worrying about rent, food, and bills. The plan: cut all living costs, reclaim time,
research a skill people already pay for, learn it through high-volume iteration, spend
the freed money on tools/help/attempts, and — critically — never inflate your lifestyle
so the money actually banks.

## Key claims (his first-$100K playbook)

- The target is **$100K in the bank, not $100K in revenue.** "Everything minus food and
  shelter is your profit." This is about being rich, not looking rich.
- **Step 1 — Cut all costs so you can take more risk.** No eating out; only discount
  grocery food; wear what you already own for the next 2 years; cheapest possible
  housing (live with family, or roommates); ideally drive a paid-off clunker. Goal: create
  cash-flow "fluff" to reinvest in skills.
- **Step 2 — Save time.** A 9-to-5 isn't killing your dreams; you're wasting the two
  four-hour chunks you already have (5–9am and 5–9pm). Wake earlier if needed. Focus is
  achieved "not through addition, but subtraction" — remove everything that doesn't matter.
- **4-4-4 split** (for those without a day job): 4 hours promotion (let people know about
  your stuff first thing), 4 hours delivery (give buyers what you promised), 4 hours
  building (curate opportunities, then prioritize the highest-return one). "You promote,
  you deliver, and you build."
- **Maker vs. manager.** Maker = build mode, ideal day is an empty calendar. Manager =
  people/interaction mode, ideal day is 5-minute chunks maximizing touchpoints/decisions.
  Block them separately because "the biggest killer of productivity is task switching."
  His own setup: Mondays are manager days, rest of the week he protects maker time.
- **Life hack:** keep the first four-to-six hours of each day to yourself.
- **Step 3 — Research a skill people already pay for.** B2B: pick one function a business
  already buys (content, outreach, funnel building) — any one can be a $1M+ business. B2C
  hack: print your bank/credit-card statement and see what you actually spend on. Selling
  to consumers = giving them time back; selling to businesses = giving them money.
- **1-1-1 rule:** sell one product/service to one avatar on one channel until you make
  $1 million.
- **Step 4 — Spend time learning.** Definition of learning = "same condition, new
  behavior." If your daily behavior isn't changing, you're not learning. Reframes the
  10,000 hours idea as **10,000 iterations** — a proxy for feedback loops; no feedback,
  no learning. Fastest way to learn a skill: hire someone great one-on-one.
- **First-party-data learning loop:** (1) gather lots of volume, (2) analyze the top 10%,
  (3) find what the top 10% share that the other 90% don't, (4) isolate the most important
  details and test them one at a time, (5) avoid the 90%'s mistakes, do more of the top
  10%, run another 100 reps, repeat.
- **Step 5 — Spend money in the right places:** tools (software, CRM, Skool at ~$9/mo),
  implementation help (courses, communities, tutoring — which he strongly advocates), and
  trial attempts (ad spend, editing software — minor investments with big leverage).
- **Step 6 — Do not increase your lifestyle.** Knows great sales guys making $40K/month
  who spent every dollar and didn't start saving until their 40s. Increasing income alone
  never gets you to $100K banked — you also have to not let lifestyle creep take over.
- Most self-made billionaires made their money from **monster active income** they could
  deploy, not passive income. You get rewarded for the risk you take; you can't take risk
  with no time and no money.
- Framed as Maslow's hierarchy: you can't think long-term while you're worrying about
  rent tomorrow. $100K banked is the checkpoint that unlocks bigger dreams.
