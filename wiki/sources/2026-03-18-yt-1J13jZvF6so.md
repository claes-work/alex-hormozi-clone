# Helping a VA Agency Scale | Hormozi Hotline

## Summary
Hormozi Hotline clip. The caller (JP) owns a virtual-assistant agency supplying high-quality VAs to US and Canadian companies (~30 customers across industries, no single industry over 30% of the base). He asks whether Acquisition.com's AI tool (ACQ AI / "ATK AI") can help research and pick a niche, since a peer (Will from Virtual Rockstars) urged him to niche down but the caller feels he lacks subject-matter expertise in any one industry. Alex's diagnosis: you don't need customer concentration to niche — you've already *sampled* many industries, and the goal is to pick the avatar you can deliver the **most value to for the lowest cost to you** ("that's where the arbitrage really occurs"). He walks a Goldilocks value-vs-cost calculation: given three candidate niches (10x value / 5x training cost; 8x value / 1.5x training cost; 2x value / no extra training), take the **middle door** — the one with disproportionate value for modest added training. Crucially, he tells the caller to niche by **VA *type* first (marketing VAs, media-buying VAs, customer-service VAs, healthcare VAs, SDR VAs), not by industry** — because a narrow type lets you **standardize training**, produce higher-quality output, charge a premium, and get better margins; the industry to crush will reveal itself inside the type. He affirms any of these VA specializations could reach $100M/year given the industry size. On using AI for the decision, Alex prescribes a **prompt chain**: first ask the model for a *prompt to give it* ("what questions would you ask me to get better insights?"), answer those, reload with the added context, then ask it to weight the top-five niches by likelihood of outsized returns per unit effort — deeper iterations yield better output. Final Q on go-to-market: the caller plans to skip saturated paid ads for the virtual space and instead use **affiliate/channel partners** (consultants, B2B coaches, marketing agencies). Alex's rule: **if affiliates are your primary acquisition channel, your marketing is for the affiliates — the affiliates are your customers**, citing the white-label software they ran where advertising targeted only agency owners, never the SMB end-users. Ends with the 10-stage 0→$100M roadmap promo (ignored for claims).
