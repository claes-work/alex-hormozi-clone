# $100M Lead Nurture Playbook (2025)

**What it is.** One of the 12 companion playbooks to the *$100M Money Models* 2025 launch, subtitled "How to get more leads to respond, schedule, and show" (PDF p. 1). A ~40-page (main content pp. 1–41, plus a 2-page checklist pp. 39–40) tactical guide by Alex Hormozi, sole author, on medium-term lead nurture — maximizing 30-day show rates in the window after advertising and before sales (PDF p. 10). Raw path: `raw/books/2025-100m-lead-nurture-playbook.md` (cite by `PDF p. N`).

## Thesis

- The single job of the playbook: maximize 30-day show rates, because "If they do not show, they cannot buy" and "the more people who show up to buy your thing, the more of your thing you'll sell" (PDF p. 1, 9).
- Getting leads to show is not one thing but many — "There are no silver bullets, only golden BBs" (PDF p. 9). Four pillars drive show-ups: **Availability, Speed, Personalization, Volume**, unified by a fifth imperative — **Execution** (PDF p. 11, 36).
- Origin: the pillars come from data mined out of Hormozi's software ALAN (Artificial Lead Automation & Nurture) during COVID-2020, analyzed by a data hire ("Vlad") across ~1M rows of appointment data (PDF p. 2–3). A 20–40% revenue lift from show-rate work, at 20% margins, is 2x–3x the profit of the business in year one (PDF p. 4).

## Structure and content

- **Lead Nurture intro (pp. 1–4):** COVID/ALAN origin story; sale of the software to a larger competitor in 2021; definitions of schedule rate, show rate, throughput; worked 100→50→25 example (PDF p. 1–4).
- **Four Pillars overview (pp. 5–6):** the four show-up drivers, framed against a slow competitor (PDF p. 5).
- **Pillar I — Availability (pp. 7–12):** open more days, more hours, more slots per hour; inbound/outbound/self-scheduling; online scheduler tips; adding friction to filter bad appointments (PDF p. 7–12).
- **Pillar II — Speed (pp. 13–18):** speed to first contact, speed to first appointment, speed of response; the pull-forward call script and hot handoff (PDF p. 13–18).
- **Pillar III — Personalization (pp. 19–26):** six tactics — preferred channel, qualify, best-leads-to-best-closers, segment messaging, incentives (push/pull), proof (PDF p. 19–26).
- **Pillar IV — Volume (pp. 27–32):** 7+ reach-out cadence, automated + manual reminders, BAMFAM (PDF p. 27–32).
- **Execution (pp. 33–37):** the "Jacob" culture story; ranking reps by show/close/lead-to-close; compensation and culture (PDF p. 33–37).
- **Lead Nurture Checklist (pp. 39–40):** one-page action list per pillar (PDF p. 39–40).

## Key claims

- **The four data-verified show-rate drivers** (from ALAN, ~1M rows): more time slots available, fewer days out leads schedule, more follow-ups, and more often leads respond to follow-ups — each raises show rate (2020, PDF p. 2–3).
- **Availability is the single biggest lever** on throughput — bigger than speed; the aggregate of days, hours per day, and time slots per hour is the strongest predictor of show-ups, with diminishing but still-large returns at the upper quartiles (June 2021 anecdote, PDF p. 8).
- **Availability tactics:** take appointments 7 days/week (booking the "40% of the year people don't" yields ~40% more availability); extend hours (6am–6pm PST covers the highest share of US nationwide sales; checklist target 9am–9pm EST); offer 4 slots/hour (every 15 min) rather than 1–2; and run inbound + outbound + self-scheduling simultaneously (PDF p. 9–10, 39).
- **Speed statistics (cited third-party):** 391% lift in conversions when contacting within 60 seconds (Velocify); contacting within an hour = 7x more likely to qualify vs one hour later, 60x vs 24 hours (Harvard Business Review); 78% of customers buy from whoever responds first (Lead Connect) (PDF p. 13).
- **Speed to first contact standard:** contact all leads in 5 minutes or less (extra credit: under 60 seconds); faster contact means fewer follow-ups needed before they buy — a rare "work less to make more" case (PDF p. 13–14, 39).
- **Speed to first appointment:** cap scheduling at 3 days out (72 hours; 5 days max if forced) because closer appointments show higher; pull appointments forward, ideally same-day — "show rates for I'm talking to you right now are 100%" (PDF p. 14–15).
- **Personalization = usefulness, relevance, pleasantness:** figure out what the lead wants, get it for them, and tell them they can have it if they show up — lowering their risk and raising their opportunity cost of missing (PDF p. 19–20).
- **Six personalization tactics:** use the lead's preferred channel (start everywhere, continue where they respond); qualify via application (a weight-loss firm actively cancels bad-fit appointments); route best leads to best closers (score 1–5 or red/yellow/green); segment messaging (HubSpot got 7x email ROI after segmenting); incentives; and matched proof (PDF p. 19–26).
- **Push vs pull incentives:** "push" = a no-strings gift before arrival (e.g. a $5 gift card, free products) to trigger reciprocity — works even when the lead knows the tactic; "pull" = an A/B choice that assumes attendance (e.g. "black or pink?") and signals you incurred a cost for them (PDF p. 24–25).
- **Volume — the persistence gap:** nearly half of salespeople quit after the first attempt (avg ~1.3 attempts, 44% stop after the first — InsideSales.com & RAIN Group); the fix is reaching out 5+/7+ times because most single attempts fail for benign reasons, and opted-in leads asked to be contacted (PDF p. 5, 27).
- **Volume cadence:** call within 5 min; double-dial (many phones block the first call, pass the second); voicemail then immediate text; double-dial + text 3x day one; 2x days two and three; 1x/day for the next four days; then transition to long-term nurture (PDF p. 27–28, 40).
- **Reminders:** automated (labeled as automated) at 24h, 12h, 3h out — include name, date, time, and the area code you'll call from (adding area code ~2x pick-up rate; use the lead's local time zone); plus manual reminders from a real cell the night before, morning of, and 60 min prior (PDF p. 28, 31, 40).
- **BAMFAM ("Book-A-Meeting-From-A-Meeting")** — learned from Sharran Srivatsaa: never end a call with "we'll circle back"; schedule the next appointment live, in the current appointment. Applied to an acquired insurance company it "skyrocketed" second-call show rates and raised sales (PDF p. 31).
- **Execution / culture:** the hardest part of working leads is actually working them; build a culture that rewards the boring work. Rank reps by show rate, close rate, and total call-to-close (lead-to-close) percentage — not close rate alone; add a small show-rate commission bump (~5–30% of a salesman's per-sale earnings) (PDF p. 35–36).
- **Company scale reference:** Acquisition.com portfolio companies generate "upwards of 20,000 leads a day" (PDF p. 5); ALAN was "managing" 4,000+ appointments per day at its problem peak (PDF p. 2).
