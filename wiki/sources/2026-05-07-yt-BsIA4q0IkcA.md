# Stop Charging Hourly (Do This Instead)

## Summary

Alex coaches a data-consulting owner to stop selling hours and sell ROI. He recommends
ROI-focused LinkedIn content, a high-value "five one-on-one calls" lead magnet for
qualified companies, describing outcomes instead of explaining technical steps, and a
calculator close that charges a percentage of expected savings.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-07)

- Alex says charging hourly is the obvious constraint in the business.
- He says content should talk about the benefit of data, not data itself.
- He says the content should demonstrate what happens as a result of the service.
- He recommends a CTA offering five one-on-one calls, limited to qualified companies.
- He says this offer works because everyone understands the value of several expert
  calls.
- He recommends setting the expectation that after the fifth call he will explain what
  working together could look like.
- He says the calls should describe what will happen, not explain the technical how.
- He recommends a calculator close: quantify expected savings or efficiency gain, then
  charge a percentage of that outcome.
- He says the maximum charge for a result-based service is about 30% of the result.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Let's just get you to 1.2 million and get you no more customers and just change how
> you bill."

> "We're going to describe it, not explain it."

> "You're charging for outcomes, not hours."
