# Is a 6-Month Sacrifice Worth a Lifetime of Higher Income?

## Summary

Alex answers a finance professional considering CFA Level II while balancing family, a
side business, gym, and a second child. He frames the decision as a straightforward ROI
and trade-off question: if six months of study unlocks a permanent income increase in the
person's chosen career path, the sacrifice may be fair; if it is mostly ego, the case is
weaker.

## Key claims

- A professional credential is worth the trade-off when it has a clear income ROI.
- Short-term sacrifice can be rational when it creates a long-term or lifetime payoff.
- The trade should be made explicit to family: six months now for higher income and
  optionality for the rest of life.
- If the credential is mostly ego, the decision is much less compelling.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I love short-term investment for long-term forever payoff."

> "I just want to make sure that it's tied to some level of higher income."

Synthesis candidate: short-term sacrifice for permanent income lift; credential ROI filter.
