# From Rock Bottom to Breakthrough (2022-08-23)

## Summary

Alex narrates the "worst six months of my life" (starting October, during his gym-turnaround era) as a chained sequence of disasters — his mom hospitalized, a DUI, and money seized by payment processors — and how a desperation pivot from launching gyms to *licensing his system* pulled him out. He frames the episode as the origin of Gym Launch's licensing model and of his belief that desperation forges the best businesses, then traces the subsequent revenue run (400k → 780k → 1M+ per month) with Leila from a kitchen table.

## Key claims

- The worst stretch began in October: mom got sick and was hospitalized, he got a DUI, and money from a new venture was taken by processors after he'd sold his gyms to go all-in on it. (paraphrase)
- A first "selling my secrets" launch brought ~100k that covered refunds; repeated launches kept getting clawed back by refunds and processor holds, at one point leaving him ~150k negative. (paraphrase)
- The pivot to *licensing / selling the membership system* to gym owners was born when he told his clients he was quitting the gym business and they asked him to teach them instead — he sold the first one for 6k, then raised the price to 8k then 10k the same day, doing 60k that day / ~100k in ~four days. (paraphrase)
- Revenue then scaled monthly (self-reported): 400k → 780k → 1M, then 1.2M / 1.5M / 1.8M, run by him, Leila, and one assistant from a kitchen table on 1,200/month rent. (self-reported)
- He explicitly framed the motivation as escaping pain, not building a company: "business became my feel better drug," and he cut vacations, weekends, friends, and drinking to avoid ruining it. (paraphrase)
- He came "up for air" past a ~20 wealth number to find he had ~120 employees and a company later valued (by someone else) at 200 million. (self-reported)
