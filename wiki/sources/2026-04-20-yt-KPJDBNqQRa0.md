# Everyone Is Jealous of What You've Got. No One Is Jealous of How You Got It.

## Summary

Alex responds to the idea that people envy outcomes but not the path. He says people
mistake his description of his life for a prescription, and that others should take in
the view if they want to. For him, freedom means choosing what to do after he can say no
to everything else, and he happens to choose work.

## Key claims

- Alex says many people hear his description of his life as if he were prescribing it to
  them, which he says is not the intent.
- He says people can take breaks and enjoy the view if they want because no one will
  remember most people in four generations.
- He says he enjoys seeing how much he can do and feels most present when working.
- He says people often struggle to accept that someone can work all the time and truly
  love it.
- He defines his own choice as exercising absolute freedom.
- He says freedom is strongly reinforcing, but after someone says no to constraints,
  what they choose next reveals what they actually want.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "My description as prescription."

> "There's nothing else I would rather do."

> "Freedom is reinforcing."
