# Do 100 Reps, Then Find the Top 10%

## Summary

Alex describes a practical learning loop: do a high volume of reps, identify the top
10%, isolate what they have in common, apply those differences to the next hundred, and
repeat. He uses content, sales calls, outreach, customer success, writing, and Chris
Rock's comedy process as examples. Learning, in this frame, is many small improvements
accumulating into outsized returns.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The fastest non-course way Alex learns a skill is tremendous volume of activity.
- After 100 reps, look at the top 10 and compare them with the other 90.
- Turn the differences into a checklist for the next 100 reps.
- New variables will appear in later batches, expanding the checklist.
- This process applies to sales calls, outreach, content, customer success, and writing.
- Alex uses Chris Rock's club-testing process as an example of accumulating tested
  material into a polished performance.
- Outsized returns usually come from many small things added together, not one magic
  move.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Do a hundred of those and then you see what are the top 10%."

> "That fundamentally is the process of learning anything."

> "It's usually not one thing, it's many small things."
