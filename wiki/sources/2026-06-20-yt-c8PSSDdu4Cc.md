# Helping an Online Service Business Fix Their Broken Offer

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a live-poker coaching business with two front-end offers, one 28-day higher-ticket offer and one 7-day lower-priced offer. Alex diagnoses the immediate constraint as operations rather than offer architecture: before cutting either product or turning on paid ads, the company needs to redesign delivery so it can create three to five times more capacity. He also suggests that poker players may respond positively to higher pricing, but says a proper price analysis should precede a specific recommendation.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - live-poker coaching business owner. His revenue, offer prices, monthly sales volume, delivery structure, and warm-inbound demand are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When two products are highly similar, Alex tends to prefer simplification, but only after the business can replace demand and capacity.
- Cutting either offer immediately would likely reduce revenue because the company does not yet have scalable customer acquisition.
- The first fix is operational redesign that increases delivery capacity by three to five times.
- Once capacity improves, paid ads can become useful because the company can absorb more demand.
- After paid demand and capacity are working, the company can decide whether to drop the lower-ticket offer and focus on the higher-ticket one.
- The poker avatar may treat price as a credibility signal; in some cases, higher price can raise conversion when the original price is too low to be believed.
- Alex identifies that phenomenon as related to a Veblen good, but says specific pricing should be researched before recommending a move.
