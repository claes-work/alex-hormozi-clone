# Helping a Med Spa Owner Build a Membership Model

## Summary

Alex adapts the Gym Launch challenge model to a day spa membership business. He suggests
a six-week stress-release challenge, a week-three or week-four conversion into annual
or continuity membership, and a product upsell 24 to 48 hours later. He also describes
the "Green Play": advertise a valuable experience for free when someone becomes a
member.

## Key claims

- Alex says a float-tank business successfully used a six-week stress-release challenge
  modeled after Gym Launch.
- He says the key mechanic is converting the front-end challenge into a year or
  continuity offer around week three or four.
- He says spas can offset customer acquisition by upselling home-use products after the
  initial sale.
- He says the expensive experience can become a free gift when the prospect becomes a
  member.
- He says the price disparity between a high-ticket experience and a lower monthly
  membership can make the membership feel obvious.
- He says the goal is to break even on the front end so traffic can be scaled.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Flip it at week three or week four into the year."

> "We used to call this the Green Play."

> "Break even on the front end."
