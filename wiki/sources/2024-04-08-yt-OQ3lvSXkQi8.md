# How Leila and Alex Hormozi scaled their first business

## Summary
A short clip (Acquired podcast) in which **Leila Hormozi** narrates, in the first person, how she and Alex scaled their first business together (the gym-licensing business, i.e. Gym Launch). She recounts being invited to a meetup of eight- and nine-figure entrepreneurs while their own company was still tiny (three people), a mentor warning them to "hit the gas" while it felt easy, and hiring consultants who forced a hard split of roles — Alex onto marketing, Leila onto building the company/team and everything else — with the counterintuitive instruction that Alex should *not* help Leila so she would develop on her own. She caps it with self-reported revenue figures for the run-up. **Attribution note: this clip is almost entirely Leila speaking; there is essentially no Alex-attributed speech, so it yields biography context but no persona/voice data.**

## Biography facts (dated; which business, scaling story)
> All facts below are **self-reported by Leila Hormozi** in this clip — CONTEXT for Alex's biography, not Alex-attributed. Corresponds to the early **Gym Launch** period (their first business together; the gym-licensing model built on marketing Alex "had learned" to acquire gyms). No explicit calendar years are stated in the source.

- Early on, they were invited to a meetup of ~8- and 9-figure entrepreneurs and felt their company was "barely a business compared to" those attendees — the others had ~100-person teams, while "there's three of us."
- At that meetup, an entrepreneur/mentor told them: when the business feels easy is exactly when to "hit the gas," warning that if they didn't capitalize on the opportunity, someone bigger and faster would come and take it all — leaving them "with nothing again."
- **They got married during that same ~four-month window** ("we got married in those four months").
- They hired multiple consultants; one diagnosed that Alex and Leila could no longer both run sales (the business's primary function) and told them to split the company: **Alex → marketing** (the channel through which they had acquired their gyms), **Leila → "everything else," building the business and the team.**
- Leila had a background in sales and some marketing and had "never done anything else" — the consultant put all business functions on a board, circled every one except marketing, and assigned them to her; she calls this "the next most terrifying moment."
- The consultant also instructed Alex **not to help Leila** — reasoning that if Alex tried to help her, "she won't be as good as she can... she is completely capable on her own." Leila frames this as the true start of their separation of roles and of "Alex and Lea as we are known today."
- Self-reported revenue trajectory after the role split: **first six months ~$7 million; year two ~$27 million; then ~$34–36 million** (Leila is unsure of the exact later figure), "and then it just... continued to explode."
