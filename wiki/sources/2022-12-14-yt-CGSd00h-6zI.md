# The Best Student Side Hustle in 2026

## Summary

Alex Hormozi lays out a step-by-step blueprint for a test-prep / tutoring business he says students (or anyone) can run in ~4 hours a day for top-1% income. The core mechanic: sell the *outcome* (a higher test score / better school / higher lifetime earnings), not the tutoring, and use a semi-private one-to-eight group model instead of one-on-one to multiply revenue per hour. He escalates from a solo operator earning ~$1,100/day to a "God mode" version hiring part-time teachers at $50/hour to clear ~$3,600/day (a $1M/year income).

## Key claims

- Test prep is the highest-value tutoring niche because standardized-test scores have an outsized, measurable effect on which school you get into, and thus on network, recruiting, and lifetime earnings — "College was the original Biz op." (paraphrase)
- Semi-private (one-to-eight) is one of the most profitable service models: charge ~2/3 of a one-on-one rate ($35 vs $60) but serve 8x the people — $280/hour vs $240 for four hours of one-on-one. (paraphrase)
- Test scores are directly proportional to the number of practice problems solved beforehand; he cites a research study on SAT/GMAT scores as a "clear input-output equation." (paraphrase)
- The business needs no capital, rental space, or build-out; sessions can run at free public libraries. Client acquisition = DM your network, offer 5-10 free sessions, then ask for feedback, a testimonial, and a referral. (paraphrase)
- Scaling: keep service margins at 80%+ (cost per unit under ~$56), pay teachers $50/hour, keep the spread, and run parallel pipelines for recruiting both students and teachers. (paraphrase)
