# The brutal truth behind passive income (2021-11-03)

## Summary

In a solo video prompted by a dinner rant, Hormozi argues (2021) that "passive
income" is largely a myth and an overrated goal, and that people should "make active
income cool again." Having reached a near-passive state himself, he reports regretting
it — describing his life over the prior ~9-12 months as boring and empty — and frames
work as something humans are meant to do rather than escape. He opens with a
self-reported figure of $37.9M in dividends taken over the prior 36 months.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- (2021) Passive income is a clickbait headline; 99.9% of people will never have true
  passive income in any material amount. — self-reported framing
- (2021) There is no true binary of active vs. passive income — everyone trades time
  for money; the only difference is leverage. "Passive" just means you trade a smaller
  percentage of your time. The real question is *how* passive or active an income
  stream is (a 0-10 scale), not whether it is one or the other.
- (2021) Humans are "meant to work"; pursuing a pleasure-seeking life after winning is
  one of the saddest outcomes he observes among the ultra-wealthy around him.
- (2021, self-reported) He lives in a building of eight-, nine-, and ten-figure
  entrepreneurs and watches older wealthy men (40s-60s) with no "off switch" — likening
  a retired driven entrepreneur to a "defanged tiger."
- (2021, self-reported) He and his wife make roughly $6M/year "passively" and rate
  their own involvement ~8.5 out of 10 toward fully passive; he says it was more fun
  when they were actively involved.
- (2021) He rejects the common advice that being actively involved in your business
  means it is broken or that you are a lesser entrepreneur — he calls that "horseshit."
- (2021) Building a business that merely *maintains* without you is different from — and
  lesser than — building one that *grows* without you; the latter is when you truly
  have a business. He claims to have proven he can do the latter and now chooses to
  stay in the game anyway.
- (2021) Freedom means "the freedom to choose, not the freedom to do nothing." Having
  tasted the passive-income fruit, he prefers the challenge and the game.
- (2021, self-reported) Acquisition.com does about $85M/year; the three weekly YouTube
  videos are the most engaging work he does. He signals big, aggressive moves for 2022.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> We trade time for money. The only difference is that some of us have more leverage
> that we with which we trade that time for more money. But at the end of the day, we
> are always trading time for money. Period.

> We are not meant to not work. We are meant to work.

> There is no off switch on a tiger. When you turn it off, it's not pretty.

> If you love the game, then why would you want to get yourself out of it?

> I've given myself permission to work for the love of working because I choose to.

> We're meant to work. We have the freedom to choose, not the freedom to do nothing.

> You eventually win the game, right? And then you look back and now you have all this
> time... And eventually you have all your time back, but you have nothing to do.
