# This ONE Thing Will Make You A Better Entrepreneur (2022-09-20)

## Summary

Hormozi argues the sole limiter of any business is the entrepreneur, not the
vehicle or opportunity — and that entrepreneurs are constrained by exactly three
things: skills, character traits, and beliefs (what they hold true about the
world). He details the order in which he personally bought learning (free
interviews → masterminds → apprenticeship → workshops → events → coaching
programs), argues you pay experts for *time saved*, not information, and closes
with his theory that character traits are the "wellspring" and are built by
acting as the trait-holder would act despite discomfort — not by feeling the
trait.

## Key claims

- The one thing that limits every business is the entrepreneur, reducible to
  three levers: **skills, character traits, and beliefs**. He uses a ladder
  analogy — skills and beliefs are the two rails, character traits the rungs.
  "Overnight success" usually means someone had two of the three very high and
  was only missing one broken belief.
- **You pay for time, not information.** Information is free but finding it costs
  time; paying an expert buys the sorting they already did. Highest-leverage
  spend was one-on-one mentorship from people far ahead of him.
- **Skills compress with experience:** the gap between wanting a skill and
  acquiring it shrinks as you stop avoiding "the feeling of sucking." Landing
  page took 3 years (avoidance) then 4 hours (once he decided).
- **Beliefs break by witnessing**, not argument — the Roger Bannister
  4-minute-mile effect. Communities' biggest benefit is broken beliefs, not the
  coaching.
- **Character traits are not binary** — it's a matter of degree, and you build
  one by acting in accordance with it despite internal discomfort. Your feelings
  about the action are irrelevant to whether you own the trait.
