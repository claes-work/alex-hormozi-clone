# Go 100% All In on Your Goals

## Summary
Solo motivational monologue arguing that people under 30 (and, more broadly, anyone with little to lose) have no reason not to go 100% all in on their goals, because "the downside is nothing and the upside is everything." Alex frames life as scratch-off lottery tickets: when you have nothing to lose, a failed attempt costs only the ticket, so the rational move is to take as many shots as possible while your downside is zero — the one window in a career where you can out-attempt everyone. He diagnoses what actually holds people back (himself included when younger): fear of being judged a "failure" by people you don't even talk to anymore. He reframes failure as non-destructive because "you never actually start from scratch ever again" — after the first attempt you always start with experience, so every shot nets positive; you either get the big win or, more likely, you learn something and that learning compounds. His core model of how success works: it's **stacking** many things on top of each other, not "nothing nothing nothing and then something works." He uses a bridge-over-a-chasm metaphor: rather than repeatedly trying to jump the gap in one leap, you lay bricks on the path, fall, fix, keep walking, and eventually cross. The "overnight success" experience feels like it happens all at once because the *outcome* of a system working end-to-end is binary, but the *process* of building the system is linear. He grounds this in his own record — **nine failed businesses before one that really worked** — and tells the viewer they have more failures in them than they think. Finally he invokes the **infinite game**: unlike finite games with a fixed end and a final score, infinite games exist only to keep playing. The point of marriage is to stay married, of health to stay healthy, of business to stay in business — so all you must do to stay in business is choose to keep fighting. You become a success the moment you decide you are, because success is about being in the arena. In your 20s, what you lack in experience you make up for in effort and hours, and believing otherwise "leads to a life of honestly well-deserved mediocrity." Ends with the $100M scaling roadmap promo (acquisition.com/roadmap).
