# How I Met My Wife

## Summary
Interview clip (an off-camera host asks the questions; all substantive answers are Alex's). Alex recounts how he met Leila and the arc of their relationship. He met her while pitching her to come work for him — "I liked her mind first" — offering to pay her twice what she was then making and telling her to judge the offer on its own merits, independent of him. She said no for the first 30 days because he asked her every single day; she reversed only after he ran a gym turnaround, launched three gyms, and came back "with a big stack of money," at which point she asked "Oh, so this works?" On the day she joined they processed roughly **$120k in about 45 minutes** (he was 27, she was 23); her reaction was "Is this legal?" Their early "dates" were just working together — he "just liked her around" — and there was no romantic/chemical love until much later. He got her to quit her job and fly out with him within about **six weeks**. Notably, they were still not "in love love" even when they married; that shift came only in **December 2019**, roughly 2 to 2.5 years after the wedding, after a deep conversation in which both admitted they were unhappy. Alex's original stated premise had been "business and then us — we feed the business and the business feeds us"; in that December 2019 talk they consciously **flipped the priority to marriage first**, and he says it has been great since. He frames love as an active decision, not a passive feeling, and says his relationship with Leila is founded above all in **respect and pure acceptance**: as an "extreme person," the thing that made him truly love her was that she never tried to change him ("You do you and I will do me next to you"). On kids, he says it's possible, that Leila is still young and he is "not the limiter" for procreation, and that they are "cool either way." The clip ends with the $100M scaling-roadmap promo.
