# Should You Do More or Better?

## Summary

Short solo clip in which Alex Hormozi describes the relationship between doing **more** and doing **better** as an "accordion" — a normal, expected back-and-forth that a business (or a creator) oscillates through over time rather than a one-time choice. He illustrates with a YouTube-cadence example: go from 1 → 5 videos a week and you get more views, but you also notice one of the five (or ten) dramatically outperforms the rest; that pulls you toward *better* (drop to ~3 videos that are each significantly better, which nets more views overall); once you're good at those three, you swing back to *more* (10 at that quality) — and again one outperforms the rest, restarting the cycle. His practical takeaway: you must always be pushing on one lever or the other, and **when you're unsure which, start with more — because more will always make you better.**

## Key claims (2026-02-21, paraphrased unless quoted)

- Over a career of sustained high-volume action, Hormozi has noticed an "accordion" oscillation between doing **more** and doing **better** — the two trade off and swing back and forth. (paraphrase) ★ (fresh framing — the more-vs-better toggle framed explicitly as an accordion/pendulum)
- Increasing volume surfaces information: when you make more (e.g. 5–10 videos), one will dramatically outperform the other nine, which reveals what "better" looks like and pulls you toward quality. (paraphrase)
- Swinging to *better* (fewer, higher-quality videos) can raise the average enough that total output (e.g. views) still goes up even at lower volume. (paraphrase)
- Once you master the higher-quality version at low volume, you swing back to *more* at that new quality bar — and the cycle repeats, because again one output beats the rest. (paraphrase)
- The oscillation is "normal and to be expected"; where you should focus depends on where you currently sit in the pendulum swing. (paraphrase)
- Non-negotiable: you must always be doing one or the other (more *or* better) — never neither. (paraphrase)
- Tie-breaker heuristic: if you don't know whether to do more or better, **start with more, because more will always make you better.** (near-verbatim)
