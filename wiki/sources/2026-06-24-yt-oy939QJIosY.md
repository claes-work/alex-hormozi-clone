# He Sends 10 Million Emails a Month for $10K

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a high-volume cold-email agency moving from flat retainers to performance pricing. Alex says the current fee sounds underpriced for the volume, but the real issue is collection risk. Performance models are best on paper but only work when the provider controls the money flow, has absolute tracking transparency, or works with enterprise customers that honor contracts. Otherwise the model incentivizes clients to underreport or avoid payment.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - cold-email agency founder. His revenue, client types, email volume, pricing, performance model, and churn context are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Sending 10 million emails for $10K per month sounds absurdly underpriced.
- Performance pricing is often the best model on paper but not always the best in practice.
- Performance models depend heavily on prospect quality; enterprise customers with assets and obligations are more likely to honor contracts.
- Smaller or medium customers may avoid payment if the system gives them an incentive to cheat.
- Performance pricing works better when the provider controls the money flow or has transparent access to the revenue system.
- If the provider cannot control money flow, a custom fee tied to a North Star metric such as emails sent may be safer.
- Alex suggests a two-part structure: a higher one-time fee to find message-market fit, then an ongoing execution tier after the message works.
- One-time offers can be much easier to sell than ongoing offers.
- If payment certainty is solved, Alex is willing to do performance with no upfront fee.
