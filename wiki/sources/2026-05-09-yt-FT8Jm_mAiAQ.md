# The Power of Absolute Acceptance

## Summary

Alex describes "absolute acceptance" as disarming criticism by accepting the label or
premise without letting it define him. When someone says "must be nice" or claims his
father gave him everything, the response "you're right" removes the fight. He connects
this to agency: doing what he truly wants without fear, especially fear of judgment.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says absolute acceptance can melt criticism because it denies the critic a fight.
- He gives examples such as agreeing with "must be nice" or "your dad gave you
  everything."
- He says much of the fear comes from being labeled by someone else.
- He says he does not have to adopt another person's label, but can allow them to hold
  it.
- He connects the frame to mortality: if everyone dies eventually, many labels matter
  less.
- He describes his ideal expression of himself as absolute agency.
- He defines that agency as doing what he truly wants without fear, while not hurting
  people.
- He says the fear is usually judgment or perceived judgment from people who are not
  thinking about him.
- He says his work is to discount others' judgment and increase the value of his own
  ideal-self judgment.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Must be nice. You're like, it is."

> "You're right."

> "I do what I truly want to do."
