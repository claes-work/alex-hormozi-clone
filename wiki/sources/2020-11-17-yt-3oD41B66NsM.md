# The Sweet Spot (2020-11-17)

## Summary

A short management video in which Hormozi shares "paired metrics," a concept he
credits to Andy Grove's *High Output Management*: every role should be measured by
two counterbalanced metrics — one for speed/volume and one for quality — rather
than a single number. He calls the balance point between them "the sweet spot" of
optimal performance and argues that defining both metrics per role is what lets a
service business scale.

## Key claims (paraphrase)

- (2020) **The core idea — paired metrics:** each role should be governed by *two*
  parallel metrics, not one, so that pushing volume/speed doesn't silently degrade
  quality. Credited to Andy Grove (Intel founder), *High Output Management*.
- (2020) **"The sweet spot"** is the balanced middle path between the two paired
  metrics — the point of optimal performance for a role. Hormozi coins the label in
  the video and says he'll formally name the concept later.
- (2020) Business is usually about managing a *dichotomy*, not choosing an extreme
  (e.g. micromanaging vs. over-delegating); you walk the middle and lean one way or
  the other as conditions change.
- (2020) Paired-metric examples by role:
  - Sales: total sales / closing % **vs.** refunds & backouts.
  - Marketing: total applications **vs.** qualified applications.
  - Customer service: tickets handled / speed of resolution **vs.** CSAT/NPS.
  - Physical products / inventory: inventory level **vs.** stockouts.
  - Finance: (tax) aggressiveness **vs.** audits/IRS issues.
  - Image editing (the photography business he'd just invested in): images edited
    **vs.** callbacks/corrections.
- (2020) For every role there's a metric you push and a conversely-negative outcome
  you want to avoid; define both, explain them to the person, and you get clarity
  plus more profit.
- (2020) In service businesses, scaling comes first from the ability to train and
  onboard people: "training gets them up and going, culture keeps them going," and
  monitoring the right (paired) metrics keeps performance high.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's not just number of sales or just closing percentage or just insert one
> thing here... it tends to be two that are in parallel."

> "A lot of times in business it's not about either extreme. It's about managing a
> dichotomy."

> "I want to call this the sweet spot, which clearly I didn't and I'll name it
> later... managing the dichotomy in management using parallel metrics will get you
> so much closer to the sweet spot of optimal performance for each of these roles."

> "You train them, you give them the two metrics, you walk the middle and then that
> is how you maintain high level performance over an organization."

> "Shout out to Andy Grove for being smarter than me."
