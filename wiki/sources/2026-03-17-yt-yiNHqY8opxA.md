# Helping a Home Services Company Get to $200M/Year

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A founder sells wildfire sprinklers B2C and B2B ($2.2M revenue in year one) and wants $200M/year within five years. They recently narrowed their avatar from "the whole market" to high-net-worth private clients and commercial clients, which lifted their close rate ~400%. Now they're on the verge of an insurer deal that would mandate their product to 16,000 of that insurer's residential clients — pulling them back toward the residential market they'd left. Their question: treat that as one commercial account or as 16,000 individual B2C prospects? Alex reframes it as an **avatar / day-to-day-activity decision**: pursue a new customer type when it doesn't force you to materially change your daily delivery or spin up a second business; otherwise weigh it as a cost-benefit against the size of the opportunity. He notes the mandate is really **lead gen, not guaranteed revenue** — you still have to close each install. Then he runs the math: the founder closes only ~2% of cold/paid leads (vs. 45–60% on referral) and admits they "don't have a sales motion." At 2% of 16,000 that's ~320 deals; at ~$50k revenue per sale that's roughly $15M. Alex's punchline: the constraint isn't the opportunity, it's the missing **sales machine** — installing a sales motion could "7x the business instantly," and a strong one might 10–20x it, all without changing daily delivery. He gently ribs the founder for not yet working with acquisition.com given millions in leads are incoming. Diagnosis: they went up-market to escape sales inefficiency, but they're "literally just missing a sales machine." Ends with the roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex evaluates whether to chase a new customer type by asking how much it changes your **day-to-day activities**: if delivery barely changes you should likely do it; if it requires starting another business, weigh it as a cost-benefit against the opportunity's size.
- As of March 2026, a mandate/endorsement that routes customers to you is **lead gen, not guaranteed revenue** — "it's lead gen and then you have to close it."
- As of March 2026, the client's close rate is ~2% on cold/paid leads versus 45–60% on referral, which Alex reads as the diagnostic sign that they "don't have a sales motion."
- As of March 2026, Alex's quick math on the 16,000-client mandate: ~2% close ≈ 320 deals × ~$50k revenue per sale ≈ ~$15M in revenue.
- As of March 2026, Alex's core recommendation: installing a sales motion could "7x the business instantly," and a good sales machine might make it a "10 or 20x," making it clearly worth it as long as daily delivery doesn't materially change.
- As of March 2026, Alex infers the client moved up-market because of **sales inefficiency** (higher close rate on premium clients masks the missing sales system rather than solving it).
- As of March 2026, the client's own data point: narrowing their avatar to high-net-worth and commercial clients ~3 months prior raised their close rate roughly 400%.
