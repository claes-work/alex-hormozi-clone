# 5 Steps to Live the Life You Want

## Summary
Short clip in which Alex lays out what he calls his "master life plan" — a five-step framework for designing a life you want: (1) **Figure out what you want**, (2) **ignore the opinions of others**, (3) **do so much work it would be unreasonable that you fail**, (4) **realize it never mattered to begin with**, and (5) **help others once you get there**. Only steps 1 and 4 are elaborated in the clip (an off-camera host reads the list and prompts him). On step 1, he credits his first (bad) post-college boss, who told him "figuring out what you want is 99% of it — once you know what you want, getting it's the easy part." He frames clarity as the mechanism that tells you what you're *willing to give up*: once you know what you want, "everything that's not that is what I'm willing to give up to get it." He adds a crucial permission: you're allowed to change what you want at any moment, and refusing the "sunk-cost / this-is-forever" weight (invoking Ed Mylett's addiction-recovery "one more day at a time" frame) has let him make dramatic, faster changes in life and business than most people, because he isn't apologizing to anyone or extrapolating a single choice into forever. On step 4 ("realize it never mattered"), which the host labels **"cosmic irrelevance,"** he attacks the human condition of wanting it all: we sacrifice everything for the thing we want, then want back what we sacrificed. He dismisses most deathbed regrets as biased — people romanticize the path *not* taken without counting its cost; they say they'd "give it all up for family," but they didn't, because they chose their actual path. His conclusion: "if you want it all, life will give you nothing" — everything has a price, and you can't reach critical mass in any domain unless you're willing to trade from another. Accepting trades, he says, has significantly minimized his regret. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
