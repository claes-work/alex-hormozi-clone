# You're Killing Conversions With This One Mistake

## Summary

Alex reviews a YouTube/channel funnel for a waves/hair-care creator and identifies a
conversion problem: the content and CTA seem direct-to-consumer, while the Skool offer
appears to teach people how to do waves for others. He says the CTA, page, and offer must
be congruent. He also recommends clearer benefit-of-benefit framing, fewer CTAs, mobile
thumbnail checks, and reverse-engineering successful videos by packaging before filming.

## Key claims

- A CTA must match what the offer actually sells; direct-to-consumer content should not
  send people to a B2B offer without clarity.
- Alex calls congruency one of the top conversion factors people miss.
- The offer should explain the benefit of the benefit, such as higher rates, referrals,
  social content, or audience growth for barbers.
- Multiple CTAs can dilute conversion; Alex prefers one or two primary actions.
- Thumbnails should be checked on mobile before posting because small details disappear.
- For YouTube, Alex recommends studying outlier videos in the niche, then deciding the
  title/thumbnail/package before making the video.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have to have congruency."

> "They click for something, you want to give them what they clicked for."

> "You want to do it reverse."
