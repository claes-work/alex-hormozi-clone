# How to Hire A-Players When You're Low on Cash

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a corporate-training company doing just under $2M/year that wants to reach $10M but has limited cash for a senior sales hire. Alex explains that bootstrapped companies avoid financial debt by taking on other forms of debt: talent, operational, infrastructure, data, and tech. He says the founder likely needs a reliable acquisition channel more than a rainmaker hire, and should consider backfilling current founder work so the founder can build that channel.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - corporate-training owner. His revenue, cash-flow constraint, SEO/word-of-mouth acquisition, and B2B/B2C mix are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Founders earn disproportionate upside because they do more jobs better than most people for a long time.
- Every business incurs debt; the question is which type of debt it takes.
- Financial debt can fund all-stars and reduce operational, talent, data, and tech debt.
- Bootstrapping avoids financial debt but creates other debts the founder must pay down later.
- Equity can help attract better talent when cash compensation is low, but that person must believe in the founder and upside.
- With about $400,000 in free cash flow, hiring one strong person is possible but risky.
- Cash flow can be accelerated by recombining money-making variables: cheaper customers, faster payment, more payment, or better model structure.
- The real missing piece is probably a reliable acquisition channel, especially if SEO is eroding due to AI search.
- It may be cheaper to hire someone to do the founder's current work than to hire a person who can grow the business.
- B2B corporate sales may have more leverage than B2C open enrollments because the same motion can sell larger tickets.
