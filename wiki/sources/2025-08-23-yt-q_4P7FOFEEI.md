# If you're watching this, we did it.

## Summary

A behind-the-scenes / vlog-style conversation filmed during the run-up to the
$100M Money Models book launch (published Aug 2025). Alex explains the strategic
ambition — a "$100 million launch," which he claims no one has done before — and
walks through the extraordinary volume of writing and preparation he personally
did for it (multiple books, all copy, all scripts). He also discusses the
personal toll: minimal sleep, the mountain of work, and the "physiological
depression" he expects after the climactic launch event. Leila and team members
appear as interlocutors; only Alex-attributed content is persona data.

## Key claims

- The title "$100 million" (Money Models) would be "perfectly demonstrated" by a
  launch that generates $100M — something he says "no one has ever done before."
- A big first-day launch (analogized to Harry Potter book 7 outselling books 1–6)
  only happens if you "continue to overdeliver on the promises you make"; he wrote
  multiple good books to give the brand "surface area" for people to come into
  contact with.
- $100M Money Models is framed as the third book in his series and the one
  "about making money" — chosen deliberately as the "elegant" vehicle to
  demonstrate the $100M thesis: anyone can do it "if they have the skills and they
  have the patience."
- Selling ~3.3 million copies would make it roughly the third- or fourth-biggest
  book of all time, "more than double the nonfiction record," unseating two Harry
  Potter books. (Figures stated in loose back-and-forth; treat as aspirational.)
- His stated mental model ("I'm an objectivist"): preparation should scale with
  audience size. He'd prep ~a week for a 5,000-person stage; with an estimated
  300,000–500,000 people live, that implies ~100x, i.e. "100 weeks or more" — which
  he says is about how much he prepared for this event.
- Everything written/consumed in relation to the launch was written by him — see
  Biographical facts for the full inventory.
- Post-launch "depression" is predictable and physiological (cortisol dump after a
  climactic event); this year's three-day event spreads the down out vs. last
  year's single moment.
- Dismisses people who complain about lost sleep ("I only slept five hours") —
  points to people like the interviewer (a one-year-old at home) as having it
  harder; he says his three kids don't wake him because he wakes at 3–4am first.
