# How did you learn how to sell?

## Summary
Podcast clip, heavily biographical — Alex's sales origin story from his first gym (2013). He recounts that he initially thought sales was "beneath me" (self-styled "academic / cerebral thinker / management consultant") and even turned down a sales job from his dad's financial advisor. Sales became a *forcing function* when he had to pay $5,000/month rent on an empty gym ("literally turf and a table"). He'd spent $3,000 on a weekend Facebook-ads workshop in 2013, ran ads for his gym, and signed up 27 people in the first two weeks at $300/head (~$1,000 ad spend → ~$8,700), which paid his first month's rent. His "selling" was just earnest promise-making to skeptical prospects. He details the offer — a 6-week challenge with a money-back guarantee if you lose 20 lbs (skin in the game), ~80% success rate, three buckets of outcomes — and how the real sale was reframing the challenge as the *start of a lifelong habit* and crediting the $300 toward a year. He explains the enduring strategic lesson: defined front-end programs give a specific outcome on a specific timeline, function as onboarding, and let you charge 3–4x more one-time to liquidate acquisition costs. He confesses he never felt weird selling because "I was begging for money," and that he'd done "4,000 sales consults before I ever consumed a piece of sales training." Ends with the roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026 (biographical, ~2013): Alex initially viewed sales as beneath him ("I'm an academic… a management consultant… not a salesman"), and had turned down a sales job offered by his dad's financial advisor.
- As of March 2026 (biographical): sales became a forcing function when he signed a ~$5,000/month lease on an empty gym ("turf and a table") and had to figure out "how do I get people to give me money?"
- As of March 2026 (biographical): he paid ~$3,000 for a weekend Facebook-ads workshop in 2013, ran ads locally, and signed up 27 people in the first ~2 weeks at $300 each (~$1,000 ad spend → ~$8,700), paying his first month's rent.
- As of March 2026 (biographical): his front-end offer was a 6-week challenge — lose 20 lbs in 6 weeks and get your money back ($300) — designed for skin in the game; ~"just under 80% success rate."
- As of March 2026, he explains the real sale: reframe the challenge as the start of a lifelong habit, not a 6-week event, and credit the $300 toward a year of membership ("do you want to do it on your own… or with me?"); he'd get people to commit to the year *before* finishing the challenge.
- As of March 2026, the transformations were "all food" — custom meal plans, grocery lists, food-prep instructions, eating-out guides — plus training (originally 5 days/week, later switched to 3).
- As of March 2026 (enduring strategic lesson): defined end-programs on the front end give a specific outcome on a specific timeline and become the onboarding process; you can typically charge 3–4x more one-time than recurring, which helps "liquidate acquisition costs" — he's "almost done that in every business I have."
- As of March 2026 (biographical): during the first gym he taught six sessions/day, ran ads, took sales appointments, cleaned the gym, and did billing at night — "the hardest I've ever worked… I've still to this day never worked that hard."
- As of March 2026 (self-reported): "I'd done 4,000 sales consults before I ever consumed a piece of sales training" — he learned most of it by doing.
