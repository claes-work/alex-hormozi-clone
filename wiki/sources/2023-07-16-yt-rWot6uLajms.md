# Alex Hormozi's Weird Views on Death

## Summary

In this short interview clip, Alex Hormozi lays out his personal philosophy on
death, legacy, and mortality: he believes there is no such thing as legacy because
everything he builds will eventually become dust over a long enough time horizon. He
frames this realization as ego-quieting and presence-inducing, and describes using a
"fictitious 85-year-old self" as his primary mentor. He ties the idea to a viral tweet
about wanting to be a millionaire in his 20s and wanting his 20s back once he was a
millionaire, concluding that his future self would trade all his money to be young
again — which reframes how he values the present moment.

## Key claims

His views on death, mortality, and legacy (all Alex-attributed):

- There is no such thing as legacy: when he dies, everything he had will become dust,
  and anything, expanded over a long enough time horizon, disappears. He notes this is
  the theme of the Book of Ecclesiastes for Christians. (paraphrase)
- Realizing this quieted his ego and let him be more present — this moment only exists
  in his mind, and nothing he does here will last. (paraphrase)
- His "number one mentor" is his fictitious 85-year-old self, because it's the only
  person he believes truly has his best interest at heart with no ulterior motive.
  (paraphrase)
- Mortality reframes ambition: his future self would trade all the money he has to be
  young again, so chasing achievement over present presence is misaligned — even his
  future self knows the present moment is worth more. (paraphrase)
- Two frames follow from "we're all going to be dust": (1) there is no "better," so ego
  comparison is pointless, and (2) if we're all going to be dust, "we're all siblings."
  (paraphrase)
- He credits this shift with showing up better as a leader, husband, and content
  creator, and says he couldn't have made his current content five years ago because it
  would have been more about proving something / proving a fictitious foe wrong.
  (paraphrase)
