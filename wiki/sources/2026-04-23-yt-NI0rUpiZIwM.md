# How to Build Confidence Through Action

## Summary

Alex says confidence is produced by evidence, not affirmations. He treats confidence as
domain-specific prediction based on prior reps, and says people should build evidence
through action before speaking as if they already have the capability.

## Key claims

- Alex says the useful target is not confidence itself but evidence that supports
  confidence.
- He argues that confidence without evidence is delusion.
- He reframes "fake it till you make it" as acting and practicing before publicly
  claiming competence.
- He says confidence is domain-specific: confidence in one arena does not automatically
  transfer to sales, content, or another skill.
- He says rehearsing a sales script is more useful than mirror affirmations because it
  creates capability evidence.
- He recounts an early failed sales consult where a prospect left to get a card and his
  team laughed at him.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Don't build confidence, build evidence."

> "Confidence without evidence is delusion."

> "Walk it before you talk it."
