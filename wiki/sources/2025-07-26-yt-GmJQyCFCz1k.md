## Key claims (context-attributed; no Alex persona training)

- **2025-07-26 - Crisis communication**: The hosts argue that a leader caught in a public mistake should tell the truth quickly, accept responsibility, avoid compounding lies, and future-pace changed behavior.
- **2025-07-26 - Core values**: The hosts frame an apology as naming how the action violated the leader's stated values, not hiding behind generic PR language.
- **2025-07-26 - Reputation repair**: The hosts say reputation is rebuilt through consistent changed behavior over time; there is no immediate erasure of the public memory.
- **2025-07-26 - Market leadership**: The hosts argue that in an industry crisis, the leader should address the whole industry's problem and become associated with the first useful response.
- **2025-07-26 - Audience treatment**: One host says that if a company treats its audience like clients, some of that audience can eventually become clients.
- **2025-07-26 - Delegation and founder focus**: The hosts describe early hiring by removing low-dollar-per-hour work the founder dislikes or performs poorly, while protecting the founder's highest-value revenue-driving function.
- **2025-07-26 - One-on-ones**: The hosts say one-on-ones need preparation, running logs, end-of-week reports, clear agendas or memos, and structure adapted to the seniority and current focus of the direct report.
