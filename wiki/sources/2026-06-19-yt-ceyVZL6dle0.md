# Stuck at $2.4M for 5 Years. Here's What's Missing

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a chiropractor stuck around $2.4 million for five years. Once the caller clarifies that a new doctor shifted the practice from supply-constrained to demand-constrained, Alex recommends a sequence: fix pricing and packaging to free cash flow, install attribution tracking, scale ads, and then increase content cadence for long-term local thought leadership. He also says the hiring problem ladders up to cash flow because higher pricing creates the money needed to hire better doctors.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - chiropractor. His revenue, margin, space, family/exit thoughts, channel mix, recent doctor hire, and Wyoming hiring issue are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If the practice is no longer supply constrained, the constraint is demand.
- A demand-constrained local practice needs an input-output equation for growth.
- Attribution tracking is needed to know whether ad spend returns $5, $10, or $20 per dollar.
- Local ads are easier than broader funnels because local trust reduces the need for complex indoctrination.
- The downside of local is finite market size, so a non-expanding practice must dominate its market.
- Pricing and packaging should come first to free cash flow.
- Freed cash flow should fund attribution and paid ads.
- Content and thought leadership should be layered in as the long-term brand well.
- A strong local brand can expand the radius people will travel and the prices they will pay.
- The doctor-hiring problem ladders up to cash flow: better pricing funds better doctors and reduces owner reliance.
