# Helping a Marketing Agency Scale Past $5M/Year

## Summary

Live coaching Q&A. The questioner (self-identified as **Julian**, owner of "High Ticket
Solutions" — NOT Alex; treat all business figures as audience self-report) runs an agency
that takes over sales and marketing (~two-thirds of the business) for founders selling
high-ticket offers, mostly done-for-you "business-in-a-box" / bizop offers (e.g. an
e-commerce coach charging ~$50k to set up an Amazon store). He has ~6 clients, wants to
scale past $5M revenue and to 50+ clients, and cites fulfillment failures on the clients'
side and high salesperson turnover as constraints.

Alex reframes the whole ask: he argues the underlying bizop/done-for-you businesses Julian
sells for are gray, "arguably illegal, just not enforced," so scaling that model is a trap
— 50 clients would reveal ~4 driving 70% of revenue, and the equity in bizop businesses is
worthless. His counter-play: keep the same marketing/sales skill set but point it at
**selling legitimate franchises** for franchisors who have a good model but can't promote
— a large, common, legal opportunity where a sales org can earn big upfront fees and take
equity stakes (with real enterprise value) in the franchisor parent co. He advises Julian
to replace his six shady clients with six legit partners over 12 months rather than chase
volume, warns that the direct-response "offers" world is dying (rising CAC, falling trust),
and diagnoses the turnover problem as "mercenary" salespeople selling a product no one
believes in.

## Key claims (all 2026-02-16, paraphrased unless quoted; Alex-attributed only)

- ★ **Franchise-sales-org play**: instead of selling gray done-for-you/bizop offers, apply
  the same marketing-and-sales skill to selling *legitimate franchises*. Franchisors with a
  good business model who "don't know how to promote" are fairly common; a franchise sale
  can carry a large upfront fee (he references ~$150k on a 10-year franchise) and franchise
  sales orgs often take 10–30% equity **stakes in the franchisor parent company**, which —
  unlike bizop equity — has real enterprise value.
- ★ **Where franchisors stall**: only ~5% of franchisors ever reach 100 locations. Many
  spend ~$250k–$500k on legal setup, sell to friends and family, open 10–15 locations, then
  stall because they can't attract customers — yet won't pay the "arm and a leg" that good
  franchise salespeople cost. Being that sales arm is the opening.
- **Bizop / done-for-you critique**: selling done-for-you businesses to buyers who want to
  own a business without running it is heavily regulated and gray; legal only when it sits
  under regulated structures (which is exactly what franchising provides — regulation that
  "protects both sides").
- **Volume is a trap**: scaling to 50 clients is undesirable — you'd discover ~4 clients
  produce ~70% of revenue ("50 minnows"), and the value you create for shady partners can't
  be captured as equity.
- **Better partners over more customers**: growth comes from replacing weak clients with
  better partners, not going from 6 to 15. Proposed 12-month plan: go from six clients to
  six *legit* ones, find the one that can truly scale, and become a real partner in it.
- **One legit thing beats twenty small ones**: it's easier to grow a single legitimate
  product/service to $100M/yr than to grow 20 small offers.
- **Direct-response "offers" world is dying**: framing a business in terms of "offers" is
  hardcore direct-response; that world is not sustainable long-term because CAC is rising
  and trust is falling.
- **Skill vs. opportunity**: Julian has acquired the marketing/sales skill but not the
  delivery/fulfillment skill; the skill is valuable but "applied to the wrong opportunity."
- **Turnover / mercenaries**: high salesperson churn stems partly from selling something no
  one believes in — "mercenary" reps leave for 1% more pay; reps passionate about the
  mission stick. Sales is inherently higher-churn, so think in **cohorts** (hire three, keep
  one, but that one stays ~3 years).
- **Compliance framing**: sell a better thing and market compliantly (he references having
  an FTC attorney audit funnels) rather than trying to make a bad model work.
- Alex notes he "come[s] from a world that was very heavy in franchising" and has multiple
  friends who gave away ~30% of their business and are selling for nine figures to franchise
  sales orgs. (Self-reported anecdote.)
- Closing (host/promo): pitches the free 10-stage "zero to $100M+" roadmap at
  acquisition.com/roadmap.
