# How to Raise Capital Without Using Your Personal Network

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a micro-development housing business that wants to raise capital from high-net-worth investors without relying on the founders' personal network. Alex frames the problem like an enterprise sale: accredited investors can be reached through affiliates/audience owners, paid ads, content, outbound, or events. If he had to pick one path without a special media-buying advantage, he would start with conferences and events because face-to-face contact creates faster trust with investors.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Capital raising still reduces to getting the right people to find out about the offer.
- The available paths are limited: borrow another audience through affiliates/events, run paid ads, make content, or do outbound.
- Selling to high-net-worth investors behaves like an enterprise sale even if the product is an investment opportunity rather than software.
- Investor trust can move faster face-to-face, so conferences and events are high-leverage when the founder does not already have a superior acquisition skill.
- If the founder has a specific advantage in media buying or content, that advantage can change the best path.
