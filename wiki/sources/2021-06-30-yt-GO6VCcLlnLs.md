# How I RAISE PRICES without losing sales (2021-06-30)

## Summary

Hormozi lays out a tactical, repeatable script for raising prices without spooking a sales
team or losing deals. The core mechanic: introduce a **high price anchor** above the price
you actually want, keep the old price available as the buyer's/sales rep's **emotional
"home base," and offer the true target price as a same-day prepayment "discount"** —
structured as an uneven split that keeps more cash up front. He closes with a "clear the
pipe" tactic: pre-announce increases to create urgency and a cash-flow buffer.

> ⚠️ This is a **proto-version of his price-anchoring / prepayment-discount pricing
> psychology** — practical field mechanics that predate and feed the formal offer/value
> framework of *$100M Offers* (2021). Date all specifics to mid-2021.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- (2021) The fear of raising prices ("no one will buy," "my sales team won't have
  conviction," "the market won't bear it") is "99 times out of 100 purely in your head."
- (2021) To go from $2k to $3k, set the stated anchor price *above* the target — e.g. quote
  **$4k** as the new price on the phone, jumping over the $3k you actually want.
- (2021) Keep the old price as the buyer's **emotional anchor / "home base"**: let them
  still pay the familiar $2k (as a split payment), so they feel safe — while the sales team
  keeps conviction quoting a number they're comfortable with.
- (2021) Offer the real target price as a **same-day prepayment discount** (down-selling
  from the high anchor): "pay $4k over two payments, or just $3k today" — framing the true
  price as a saving.
- (2021) Prefer **uneven / disproportionate payment splits** (e.g. $4k + $2k rather than
  even halves) to keep more cash up front, covering higher onboarding costs early.
- (2021) The gap between the comfort price and the prepayment price should be **marginal**
  ("just a little bit extra") so buyers readily choose to prepay — e.g. $10k→$12k done as
  6 + 3 + 3.
- (2021) Four consistent moves: (1) give them their home-base number, (2) anchor above the
  target price, (3) present the target as a prepayment discount, (4) keep the comfort-vs-
  prepay difference small.
- (2021) You must **meet buyers where they are** emotionally; entrepreneurs are naturally
  more adaptable/growth-oriented, but customers cling to a familiar price.
- (2021) Pro tip — **"clear the pipe"**: always pre-announce price increases. Owning the
  increase creates genuine scarcity/urgency, motivates the team to close stalled deals
  before the change, and hands the owner a cash-flow boost to weather any post-increase dip.
- (2021) Pre-announcing also pre-empts "I heard a different price" objections: you already
  warned them, and the higher price reflects added features/benefits/successes.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "One of the fears of raising your prices is: what if I don't sell anyone, what if my
> sales team doesn't have conviction, what if my market won't bear that price? 99 times out
> of 100 it's purely in your head and in your sales team's head."

> "The price that we are going to be anchoring is not the price that we're actually trying
> to get to."

> "2k is their emotional anchor... so I'm gonna say, cool, you still have that in your back
> pocket, you're totally fine, it's safe."

> "So all of a sudden you're down-selling this upsell... compared to a price anchor which we
> introduced on the call. So it actually makes it easier for your sales team to sell."

> "If you're ever going to raise your prices, always always always clear the pipe... own
> your price increases. It's one of the easiest ways to create true scarcity and urgency."
