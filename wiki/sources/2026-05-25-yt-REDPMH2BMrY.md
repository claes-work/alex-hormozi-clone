# You Think You're Not Smart Enough

## Summary

Alex Hormozi defines learning as "same condition, new behavior" and intelligence as speed
of learning. He says slower learners can still win by compressing more iterations into less
calendar time. His Vanderbilt example: even if classmates needed fewer iterations, he could
do more iterations faster by studying 9 a.m. to 9 p.m.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Learning means changing behavior in the same condition.
- Intelligence is the rate at which someone changes behavior.
- One person may be "smarter" by needing fewer iterations, but another can beat them by
  doing more iterations faster.
- Calendar speed matters as much as iteration count.
- Alex says he felt below average intellectually at Vanderbilt and compensated by working
  from 9 a.m. to 9 p.m. around classes, meals, and training.
- A skill is a chain of adapted behaviors.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Learning is same condition, new behavior."

> "Intelligence is speed of learning."
