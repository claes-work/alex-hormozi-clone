# 14 Money Mistakes to AVOID in Your 20's

## Summary

A rapid list of 14 money mistakes Hormozi says people make in their 20s, unified by one thesis: your 20s are a "season of learning, not earning," so invest every spare dollar into your own earning capacity rather than status, savings, or lifestyle. He mixes personal-finance mechanics (automate investing, manualize spending, check your account daily) with identity/environment advice (who you live with, whose approval you chase).

## Key claims

- Your 20s are a season of *learning*, not *earning*; taking a job to earn (or holding out for a small raise) instead of to learn is one of the biggest mistakes because early-career skill compounding dwarfs a $10-25k salary bump. (paraphrase)
- "Save" money only in the sense of not blowing it — then invest it in yourself, not just dollar-cost-averaging the S&P; the number-one asset is you and your earning potential. (paraphrase)
- Decide your investment amount *first* (off the top) and let it stockpile until an opportunity to learn appears — prioritization literally means it comes "prior." (paraphrase)
- Automate investing and manualize spending: make investing frictionless and spending painful (cash over credit cards, à la Dave Ramsey). (paraphrase)
- Compete on what you *save*, not what you make; check your bank account every single morning — "what gets measured gets improved," same as weighing yourself to lose weight. (paraphrase)
- Contrarian takes: as soon as you earn >$15/hour, buying takeout can beat cooking because it buys back time; and it's worth spending on once-in-a-lifetime experiences (e.g. hosteling through Europe) that windows close on by age 40. (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "Underspending on education and overspending on status."

- "Never listen to people who have smaller goals for your life than you do."

- "Measure money in how long it took you to earn it."
