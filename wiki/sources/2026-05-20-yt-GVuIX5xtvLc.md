# Judge Yourself by Your Actions, Not Your Thoughts

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says he became less disappointed in himself when he judged himself by
actions rather than thoughts. He frames traits such as patience, loyalty, honesty, and
courage as behavioral continuums proven by repeated evidence, not binary identities or
felt emotions.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex judges himself by actions taken, not thoughts had.
- He says he used to speak over himself that he was impatient.
- A trait is only proven when the person acts that way under conditions that test it.
- Feeling impatient while acting patiently still counts as patience.
- Traits are not binary; the question is how patient, loyal, honest, or courageous someone
  is.
- Character traits can be treated as continuums where each action adds another piece of
  proof.
- Alex frames proof as his north star for personal development.
- He says he did not talk about running gyms until he had six gyms and other people were
  asking how he did it.
- He says he did not feel qualified to talk about fitness despite having a six-pack for a
  decade and multiple state records until people repeatedly asked him.
- Evidence changes how the world treats a person independent of how the person feels.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Judge yourself by your actions, not by your thoughts."

> "I can be patient if I act patient even if I don't feel patient."

> "Just give myself proof."
