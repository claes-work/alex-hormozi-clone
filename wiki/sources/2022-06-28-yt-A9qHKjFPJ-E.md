# 2 Types of Business Risk and the One I Choose EVERY TIME

## Summary

A solo video, framed around a conversation with a software-entrepreneur friend, distinguishing two business risks — idea risk (does the idea fundamentally work) and execution risk (can you execute it) — and arguing for eliminating idea risk by only pursuing boring, established-demand businesses, leaving yourself as the only variable. Hormozi contrasts the Bezos "swing for the fences / long-tail payoff" model with his own preference for high-probability, risk-adjusted, compounding bets.

## Key claims

- Two big risks in business: idea risk and execution risk. Hormozi only goes after ideas with virtually no risk that people want them, leaving execution (himself) as the only variable (paraphrase).
- New entrepreneurs systematically overestimate the payoff and likelihood of success and underestimate the cost and likelihood of failure (paraphrase).
- The Bezos casino/portfolio logic (10% chance of 100x, bet every time) only works with unlimited spins/capital; most people don't have unlimited spins. Model a dollar as a year of life (~$35 of life, ~5 years per "bet") and the math favors caution (paraphrase).
- The wealthiest people he knows eliminate/control risk and "only play games where if they wait, they win" — e.g., The Rock writing a guaranteed bestseller because he controls a massive audience, a variable you can build deliberately (paraphrase).
- His method: pick the highest-probability, higher-certainty bet even at lower upside; assume everything goes wrong and check if you still win; stack many "virtually guaranteed" 3x-ish wins so compounding reaches 100x quickly with limited downside (paraphrase).

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "There are two big risks that exist in business. The first is the execution risk. The second is the idea risk."
- "Most newer entrepreneurs I think would be better served going after boring businesses that have established needs."
- "The richest people that I know control risk and only play games where if they wait, they win."
- "I'm going to assume everything goes wrong. What then do I make?"
- "That's 100% what I do. That is I've always gone for the sure thing. And then I just continue to try and trade up the upside of my sure things."
- "If you double or triple or 5x things enough times, you get to the 100x very quickly and you can do it with limited downside."
