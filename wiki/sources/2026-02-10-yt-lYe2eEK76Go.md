# Helping a $3M/Year Business Owner Make Better Content

## Summary
In a live Q&A on @MoreMozi, a business owner named Luke — who sells life coaching certification training programs and does $3M/year in revenue (targeting high earners and bored business owners who want more meaning) — asks how to grow the right following on social media. His problem: he built 250k Instagram followers over years by posting relationship content (red flags, green flags, men vs. women fighting in comments), but none of that audience wants to become certified life coaches. Alex diagnoses it as "baiting the hook with the wrong bait" and reframes the fix as small: Luke doesn't need a new business or new topics, he needs to change the frame of his content. Because the algorithm is now interest-based (not "social") media, good content is served to people interested in that content's subject, so to attract aspiring life coaches Luke should make content about life coaching philosophies, frameworks, and getting clients — i.e., talk about what he sells. Alex works through Luke's objections: fear of giving away secrets (irrelevant — most people don't act, and those who do want more), attracting "victims" when he discusses trauma/healing (fix the pro-victim framing), and boredom with "how to get clients" business content (reframe it around the customer's experience rather than tactics). The core move is a "half step": keep the same material and hooks, but frame every video from the perspective of a business-owner buyer ("when you have a client who comes to you with this…") rather than marketing to end-customers. Alex notes Luke is currently doing a perfect job marketing to customers, not to business owners. He closes with a two-tier model a friend used to reach $50M/year (life coaching clients who then buy certification), noting it applies only if Luke wanted to rejigger the whole business rather than just sell certifications.
