# What Is Discipline?

## Summary

A short-form clip in which Alex Hormozi gives his operational definition of discipline. His core thesis: you are not disciplined if you like the thing you are doing. Being consistent with things you enjoy (lifting, boxing, hanging out with someone you love) requires no discipline; discipline is consistency with the things you *don't* like. He illustrates with fitness business owners who bragged about waking at 5 a.m. to train but neglected the unglamorous revenue work (working their leads) — proof, in his telling, that they were only consistent where it was easy. He reframes discipline as **frustration tolerance** — the ability to do things you don't want to do, without an immediate reward, for an extended period — and stresses that this is a *skill, not a trait*, so it can be trained and grown incrementally (10 → 20 → 30 → 50 → 100 outreaches). He ties it to his "rule of 100" (do 100 units of outreach/day for 100 straight days) as the concrete way to build the muscle and grow a business.

The clip contains a brief embedded interview segment where an unnamed interviewer asks "Do you consider yourself disciplined?" — all substantive content is Alex.

## Key claims (2026-02-18, paraphrased unless quoted)

- ★ **Definition of discipline** — You are not disciplined if you like the thing you're doing. Discipline is the ability to be *consistent with things you don't like*; consistency with things you enjoy doesn't count. (Durable, quotable definition — candidate for `beliefs.md`.)
- ★ **Discipline = frustration tolerance** — He operationally defines it as "your ability to do things that you don't want to do without an immediate reward for an extended period of time." This is the same eliminative/operational move he uses elsewhere (focus = elimination of alternatives, commitment = elimination of alternatives, mental toughness = probability); here discipline = frustration tolerance. (Durable — candidate for `beliefs.md`.)
- **Discipline is a skill, not a trait** — Because it's a skill you can learn it and strengthen it deliberately; it is not a fixed personality attribute you either have or lack.
- **The "I wake up at 5 a.m." trap** — People who claim discipline by pointing to early workouts or never skipping meals are often just doing things they already like (lifting, boxing) while neglecting the hard, low-reward work (e.g. working their leads / outreach). Doing what's easy for you is not evidence of discipline.
- **Enjoying something isn't virtue, and disliking it isn't discipline** — He notes it takes zero discipline for him to spend time with his wife because he likes it; someone who dislikes their spouse doesn't have more discipline, they just "have a shittier life."
- **Build it incrementally** — Train frustration tolerance by ratcheting volume: do 10 outreaches, then 20, then 30, 50, 100, and keep going even when early attempts feel like nothing happened.
- **Rule of 100 grows the business** — Committing to the rule of 100 (referenced as being "in that book") for 100 straight days will grow your business; many who fully commit see major change well before day 100, but only if they go all in.
