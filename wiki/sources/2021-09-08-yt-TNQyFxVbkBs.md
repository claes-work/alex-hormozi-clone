# How MILLIONAIRES become BILLIONAIRES

## Summary
Solo talking-head video in which Hormozi argues that the fundamental difference between his millionaire friends and his billionaire friends is a compounding view of product: build something so good that customers refer others on their own, rather than perpetually chasing new sales. He reframes underperformance as a product problem, offers three product-improvement questions, and identifies word-of-mouth as the only one of six customer-acquisition channels that compounds.

## Key claims
- (2021) If you are not making the money you want, the product is not good enough — hard to confront, but if you already have customers, people already know about your product and have chosen not to refer it; ask why.
- (2021, attributed to Dan Kennedy) Small entrepreneurs try to get customers to make sales; bigger entrepreneurs make sales to get customers — one races to the money, the other builds a compounding long-term customer relationship.
- (2021) The millionaire→billionaire mechanism: put one-time front-end effort into a product so remarkable it grows on its own over time (word of mouth), instead of consistently showing up to promote and sell; time becomes an asset, not a liability.
- (2021) Three product-reframing questions: (1) if it cost 10x more ($100k / $1M), what would the experience and delivery need to be to be worth it? — hits **value**; (2) if it cost one-tenth as much but had to be better, what one-time, scalable, profitable assets would you build? — hits **profit**; (3) if you could sell only one more customer and all further growth had to come from that customer's unprompted referrals, what experience would you create? — hits **experience**.
- (2021) The six ways to get customers: paid media, earned media (following), your contact list, manual outbound (DMs/cold calls/emails), affiliates, and word of mouth. The first five scale linearly (additive); word of mouth is the only one that compounds (multiplies).
- (2021) A product good enough to compound reduces churn, negative reviews, and badmouthing, and is where the profit and enduring enterprise value live.
- (2021) Promotion should exist only to get the product right; once it is right, "juice it and let it take care of itself." Ultra-wealthy people talk almost exclusively about product; small operators talk about promotion.
