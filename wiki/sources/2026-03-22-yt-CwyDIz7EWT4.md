# Am I Undercharging for My Service?

## Summary
In this call, a caller who owns a dental-implant practice (15 years, half of patients from Google Ads, half from referrals) asks whether to keep advertising his current price while highlighting extra value, and how to sequence building ad content versus general social content. Alex Hormozi's core answer is to simply raise the price: unless there is a demand or conversion problem, a price increase drops straight to the bottom line. He illustrates with a company his firm bought and repriced (+50% after negotiation), where close rates actually rose because the offer had been mispriced.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the simplest growth lever for the caller is to raise price outright, not to justify the current price with more added value. (Caller's practice details are self-reported and unverified.)
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that a price increase should be done even without justification — "because you want to" — since a business owner can choose what to charge and customers can choose to decline.
- As of March 2026, Alex says price increases are uniquely attractive because the added revenue is essentially all profit, flowing directly to the bottom line.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims that a company his firm acquired ~a year prior was repriced by +50% (after nine calls negotiating down from a proposed doubling with the founder), and close rates went UP because the offer had been underpriced.
- As of March 2026, Alex asserts most owners already have a sales "process" even if undocumented — repeatable jokes, discovery questions, and a close — and the work is to write it down and train the team.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises the caller he will either have to learn paid ads himself or keep iterating via trial-and-error with vendors; there is no shortcut around gaining the knowledge before delegating.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the "ultimate version" of the caller's business as acquiring other practices and installing his own systems and reputation on top of them.

> "We sold a higher percentage of people at 50% more money, and if you do more times more, it's way more, cuz all that goes to the bottom line. And so that's the beautiful thing with price increases is that it's all profit. One of my favorite ways to make more profit, just charge more."
