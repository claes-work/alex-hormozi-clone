# $2.1M Founder Working 70-Hour Weeks Asks for Help

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a construction-safety founder doing about $2.1M while working
60-70 hour weeks. Alex frames the constraint as supply generation: the business needs a
repeatable talent-acquisition system for field safety professionals, not just more
founder execution. He recommends making referral bonuses economically meaningful,
building a recruiting funnel like a customer-acquisition funnel, and using contingency
recruiters aggressively for one-off specialized roles.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex first checks profit, growth, and the cost of the person needed before advising.
- Referral bonuses should be sized against the gross profit an employee can produce.
- If a role can generate about $130,000 in gross profit, Alex says a $10,000 to $15,000
  referral bonus can be reasonable.
- Repeatable roles deserve a full hiring funnel; one-off roles can use recruiters.
- Service businesses need supply-generation funnels as much as demand-generation
  funnels.
- Recruiting should be measured like acquisition, with cost of acquiring talent and
  lifetime gross profit per employee.
- Recruiting funnels can use ads, opt-in pages, VSLs, and group calls.
- If one or two recruiters do not work, Alex says the answer may be to use ten or fifteen
  contingency recruiters, not to abandon the channel.
