# The 4 Things That Build a Powerful Brand

## Summary

In this teaching segment, Alex Hormozi outlines a brand framework based on four association types: status, power, credibility, and likeness. He explains that brand mistakes cannot be erased, only diluted by creating more and stronger positive associations. He also argues that product experience is the strongest influence layer, above what a company says about itself and what others say about it, and connects this to why interest-media content should attract the right customer rather than chase raw views.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A brand should deliberately build associations around status, power, credibility, and likeness.
- Status means control over scarce resources.
- Power means someone follows the brand's instruction and gets a good result.
- Credibility means third-party or real-world evidence that a claim is true.
- Likeness means similarity in appearance, behavior, or values.
- One event can create multiple associations; for example, selling a company can create both credibility and status.
- A negative brand association is permanent, but its influence can be diluted by a higher volume of stronger positive associations.
- Influence is weakest when it comes only from what the brand says about itself, stronger when respected others say it, and strongest when the customer experiences the product or service personally.
- The product must continue the brand loop because marketing can drive a first purchase, but product experience determines lasting belief.
- Current platforms act more like interest media than social media; content should target the right buyer's interests rather than maximize generic views.
