# Why Sales and Marketing Won't Save You

## Summary

Alex says his younger self misunderstood the relative power of sales and marketing because
they gave him fast feedback and fast money. The larger levers are product and brand: a
product good enough creates word of mouth, and a brand strong enough makes people want to
buy before the tactical copy, headline, landing page, media buying, or CRO matter. He uses
*$100M Offers* as his example of "too good to fail": a book written originally as an
internal document that kept selling more month after month with no paid marketing.

## Key claims

- Alex says his 20-year-old self did not understand brand or product.
- He says sales and marketing gave him fast feedback loops, which created a false belief
  that they were the right game.
- He frames obsession with sales and marketing as trying to get faster inside the wrong
  game rather than seeing the code.
- He says a product good enough causes people to do marketing and sales for you.
- He says a strong brand creates buying associations that reduce dependence on tactical
  copy, headlines, landing pages, CRO, and media buying.
- He says *$100M Offers* was originally made for himself/internal use and became an example
  of product leverage through word of mouth.
- He self-reports that the book sold out in minutes after one post, then sold more copies
  each month for two years, still number one in category, with zero paid marketing.
- He says he had never marketed well enough to sell a thousand customers a day, but the
  book did because product created leverage.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You don't understand brand and you don't understand product."

> "I was obsessive in my 20s on sales and marketing and what I would consider fast money."

> "If you make a product that's good enough, people will do the marketing and sales for you."

> "Too good to fail."
