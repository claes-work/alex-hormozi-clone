## Summary

Leila Hormozi delivers a ~66-minute talk to gym owners on what she calls the "five
principles of management" — the operational layer she argues most owners skip when they
chase more traffic, more platforms, and more headcount instead of first getting more out
of the team they have. The five principles are Expectations, Accountability,
Communication, Cadence, and Recognition, each defined, illustrated with gym/business
examples, and closed with a 1-10 self-rating prompt. She frames leadership impact with a
sports-coach analogy (a coach accounts for ~30% variation in team success, and she claims
a CEO/owner has almost the same influence) and grounds each principle in personal
experience running Gym Launch and building Acquisition.com. The throughline is that the
"soft stuff" — culture, accountability, people management — is what actually drives
profitability, retention, and hiring, and that owners should walk away implementing just
one principle rather than all five.

## Key claims (frameworks and numbers)

- **The five principles of management** (the talk's spine): 1) Expectations, 2)
  Accountability, 3) Communication, 4) Cadence, 5) Recognition.
- **Growth misconception**: adding traffic/platforms/headcount to a small business often
  yields lower conversions, higher churn, chaos, no data, and overwhelm — there is "a step
  in between" (management) before adding growth inputs.
- **Leadership leverage**: average sports coach drives ~30% variation in team success; she
  claims the average CEO/owner has "almost the same influence" on a business.
- **Expectations** = "the verbalization of how someone should act or something should be
  done." Four levels of expectations exist; she focuses on **core values** as the highest
  level. Lead with values, not rules — "if you lead with values, you can get people to
  adhere to 99% of what you need." Values are business-specific (Gym Launch value: "do the
  boring work"; a tech friend's value: "always innovate"; Acquisition.com value:
  "unimpeachable character"). "The average employee does not believe something until they
  hear it seven times."
- **Accountability** = "an expectation tied to a measurement, and the only thing that can
  amplify it is feedback." It is "the glue between the commitment someone makes and the
  result you get." She reframes CEO as "Chief Accountability Officer" — you don't delegate
  accountability, you disseminate it down through your direct reports.
- **Weight-loss study (accountability proof)**: over Christmas, daily weighers lost avg
  1.9 lb; non-weighers gained avg 4.9 lb — "You are the scale" for your team.
- **McKinsey stat (self-reported as cited)**: 2 of 3 (66%) business strategies fail;
  companies that focus on the "soft stuff" (culture, accountability, talent/people
  management) raise strategy success to 78%.
- **Communication** = "trading your assumptions for curiosity and actively listening";
  "the bridge between ignorance and understanding." She cites an MIT badge study (~30
  businesses) finding four keys: **Energy, Engagement, Emotional control, Exploration** —
  formula given as (Energy × Engagement) + (Emotional control × Exploration). Communication
  predicted success more than individual skill or intelligence.
- **Cadence** (meeting cadence) = "the heartbeat for your team." Three P's: **Pulse**
  (Activity × Size determines frequency), **Purpose** (tactical vs. strategic), and
  **Preparation** (right information + right people + right agenda). Contributors and
  decision-makers are the two attendee types.
- **Recognition** = acknowledgment of behavior/effort/result that supports the org's goals
  and values. Four dimensions: **What** (formal vs. spontaneous), **Who** (individual <
  social < peer-to-peer), **How** (written/verbal/reward), **When** (goal/effort/milestone/
  value). "Recency beats intensity" — an immediate high-five beats delayed praise or
  compensation. Quotes Mary Kay Ash: people want recognition and praise more than sex and
  money. The "extinction curve": unrecognized behavior fades.
- **Recognition ROI stats (self-reported)**: reduces turnover 18%, increases positive
  culture 66%, profitability 23%, engagement 14%, ability to recruit talent 56%.
- **Self-reported company data points**: Gym Launch had **120 full-time employees in
  2018**; a peak month of **~$4.5M** (Leila age 24); after she began holding people
  accountable, headcount dropped from **120 to 75** while revenue held.
