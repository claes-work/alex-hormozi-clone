# "Getting rich isn't fun" [Ep 014]

## Summary

A day-in-the-life vlog interspersed with reflections, not a solo monologue: cameras follow Hormozi through calls, a portfolio-company pricing save, a haircut, and errands, while an off-camera interviewer/videographer asks him questions. Core theme: the glamorous outputs of business (jets, cars, watches) hide a day-to-day that is "predominantly communication" — meetings, calls, Zooms — and success is jagged, incremental, and unglamorous. Growth requires voluntarily choosing pain because comfort signals a plateau.

## Key claims

- Entrepreneurship as actually lived is mostly communication — "a lot of meetings, a lot of calls, a lot of zooms" — and those don't look good on Instagram; the visible luxuries are outputs people use for personal gain, not the work.
- Growth is jagged and incremental: "the closer you zoom into it the more jagged it looks and the more jagged it feels." There is no home-run that changes everything; it's 1% improvements that only feel big in retrospect, so it looks like overnight success from outside.
- "Everyone wants recurring revenue but no one wants recurring work" — and early on it's recurring work with zero recurring revenue for a long time before any pays off.
- Hard is good: difficulty is the barrier that keeps competition out ("if it's hard, good — it means no one else will do it"); what makes you exceptional is stomaching the harder thing others won't.
- Beyond a certain wealth level you can't buy expertise with money — you trade influence, connections, introductions, and deals; paid communities' price tags act as qualifiers (e.g. a $100k/yr golf club filters for a certain caliber of person).
- Diet: most people already eat the same 5–7 meals, so changing your diet just means swapping those meals — that gets you 80–90% of the way; dieting doesn't require giving up variety.
