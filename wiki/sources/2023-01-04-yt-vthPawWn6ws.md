# Watch this if you're feeling burnt out

## Summary

Hormozi releases his own side of a phone call with an eight-figure-plus CEO who was contemplating shutting down his (recently tripled) business because he felt burnt out. The through-line: stress is a learned skill and a judgment about external conditions, not the conditions themselves — improving your circumstances won't change how you feel, so you must learn to put space between feeling and behavior. He reframes "burnout" and negative self-labels as the normal cost of extraordinary growth.

## Key claims

- "Burnout isn't real" as an external force; changing conditions doesn't change feelings — the CEO's business tripled yet his pain was unchanged, proving the feelings track judgment, not circumstance. (paraphrase)
- Extraordinary outcomes require being abnormal; traits others frame negatively (obsessive, paranoid, difficult — Andy Grove, Steve Jobs) are just "not the same as other people," and you can't want a different outcome while keeping average behaviors. (paraphrase)
- Thoughts and feelings aren't truth — they're "just chemicals in your brain meant to keep you alive, not to help you succeed"; taken to their root, stress spirals usually terminate in an irrational "I'm going to die." (paraphrase)
- Practice: notice the physical trigger (for him, tightness in the chest), identify the label you've assigned as good/bad, then insert space before acting; law of large numbers means ~1 in 10 days is a "bottom 10 day" and doesn't warrant changing a working plan. (paraphrase)
- Discomfort during growth is expected — "let your lungs adjust to the altitude"; the problem is purely "between your ears," which is good news because the fix is reframing, not new conditions. (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "Right now what feels unsustainable to you is unsustainable because you've defined it as unsustainable."
- "Do not listen to people whose dreams for your life are smaller than yours."
