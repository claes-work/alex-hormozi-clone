# 5 UNTAPPED Industries that could make you a BILLIONAIRE

## Summary
Answering the recurring "what market should I go after" question, Hormozi names five industries he'd pursue in 2021-2022 because they have strong tailwinds. He grounds it in Warren Buffett's point that the market matters more than the entrepreneur ("it is far more important to pick the right boat than how hard you row"), and closes with his four-part market-selection checklist.

## The 5 industries (exact list)
1. **Crypto / NFTs / DeFi / Web 3.0** — and all downstream ramifications of the technology; a massively growing industry where even being "okay" gives you a huge tailwind.
2. **Cannabis** — still relatively untapped, becoming mainstream, not yet decriminalized everywhere; framed as recession-proof (actually pro-recession, since alcohol and drug use rise in recessions).
3. **E-learning** — his personal favorite; formal education has "failed many of us," people want specific marketable skills, and the market is increasingly fragmenting into niche verticals with expert teachers.
4. **Remote living** — everything downstream of the remote-work shift: people moving out of cities for more space, home offices, mobile studios, home decoration/improvement; he cites Zoom's rise as an example.
5. **Warehousing / logistics** — growing need for shipping and storage as people stay home and want things delivered fast with the least effort ("the demand for people to be lazy is very very high").

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated 2021)
- Citing Buffett: a good entrepreneur in a bad market loses, and a bad entrepreneur in a good market wins — the market wins either way; picking the right boat matters more than how hard you row (2021).
- The market you pick largely determines your level of success (2021).
- Cannabis is recession-resistant because alcohol/drug consumption rises in downturns; the market is still huge and unconquered (2021).
- E-learning will keep fragmenting into more silos/verticals with excellent niche teachers; formal education is an antiquated, general-knowledge system while people want specific skills (2021).
- Remote-living demand is so high you barely have to market; home improvement boomed because people spend more time at home (2021).
- Warehousing/logistics rides the human wiring to expend the least effort; anything that delivers faster with less effort maps to his value equation (high perceived likelihood + dream outcome = "click something and it shows up") (2021).
- His four-part market checklist: (1) Is the market growing? (2) Are the people easy to target? (3) Is there a need / are people in pain? (4) Do they have purchasing power? Then niche one or two steps down inside a growing market (2021).

## Notable verbatim quotes
