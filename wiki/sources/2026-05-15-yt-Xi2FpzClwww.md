# How I Learned to Market and Sell

## Summary

Alex recounts learning from Sam Bakhtiar after moving to California and working minimum
wage at Sam's gym for three months before opening his own gym. The clip focuses on sales
framing: use humor and tone to ask hard questions without aggression, set the frame
early, avoid commission breath, sell from the back foot, and use curiosity rather than
combat to understand and overcome objections.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice)

- Alex says he worked minimum wage at Sam Bakhtiar's gym for three months after moving
  to California to learn gyms.
- He learned from Sam how to say hard things in a light, non-accusatory way.
- Humor and tone can make direct follow-up or hard questions feel human rather than
  aggressive.
- Alex says he does not take rejection personally because people are busy and have low
  attention.
- Early in a call, framing and tonality can make an internet lead feel like they are
  talking to a real local person.
- Objection handling should feel like a dance, not a fight.
- Salespeople should ask the hard question they know they need to ask.
- Alex wants prospects to make the decision in front of him, not after leaving.
- Childlike curiosity, question asking, and stories about similar customers are central
  to his sales style.
- He says if a sale does not close, the salesperson did not understand the prospect
  enough.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I got rejected so many times I just don't take it personally."

> "Overcoming the obstacles a dance, not a fight."

> "Sell from our back foot."
