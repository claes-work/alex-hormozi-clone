# Most Problems Can Be Solved With More Advertising

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a luxury-home builder who wants to hire an A-player salesperson. Alex generalizes the problem: every business is demand constrained, either for customers or for talent. The same acquisition logic applies to talent, especially outreach for high-level people, and the founder's likely issue is insufficient volume.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - luxury-home builder in Texas. His revenue, profit, operating capacity, and hiring target are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Businesses are demand constrained either for customers or for talent.
- Most problems can be solved with advertising or outreach once the founder identifies the target audience.
- A-level sales talent for luxury homebuilding is more likely to be found through outreach than through passive ads.
- The founder should reach out to other construction salespeople who already sell luxury products.
- Alex recommends expanding the region, offering relocation packages for strong candidates, and using LinkedIn/Sales Navigator DMs.
- Most founders suffer from insufficient volume; reaching out to a small number of people and stopping is not enough.
- Alex says the founder should add "two zeros" to the effort.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Every business is demand constrained."

> "You're either demand constrained for customers or you're demand constrained for talent."

> "Most problems can be solved with advertising. It's just who are you advertising to?"

> "You find them the exact same way you find customers."

> "Most people here suffer from insufficient volume."

> "You just need to add like two zeros to effort and you'll get what you want."
