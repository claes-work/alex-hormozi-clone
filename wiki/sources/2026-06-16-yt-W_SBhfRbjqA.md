# Why I Sold My Likeness (And Shaved the Mustache)

## Summary

In this audience Q&A, Alex Hormozi explains why he shaved the large mustache associated with his Gym Launch era. He says he sold his likeness in the Gym Launch deal, did not want to be permanently seen as "the gym guy," and changed the visual associations around his public persona. He also describes his current simple outfit as an intentionally practical uniform optimized for many settings and temperatures, while saying the beard itself was not a highly strategic choice.

## Key claims (Alex's statements only)

- Alex says he sold his likeness as part of the Gym Launch sale.
- He had a large "gunslinger" mustache for roughly four or five years while he was heavily featured in Gym Launch ads.
- He shaved it during the year the company was being sold because he did not want to be permanently associated with being "the gym guy."
- He frames the mustache, larger body, and gym identity as branding associations he wanted to move away from.
- He says the beard was not deeply strategic; he simply stopped shaving.
- He says Leila watched him experiment for about six months with different ways to dress.
- His outfit is optimized for not changing clothes across many situations: sockless shoes, shorts, tank top, and flannel.
- He says the clothes cover roughly 40-45F to 120F in Las Vegas and work for workouts, restaurants, the pool, and temperature regulation.
