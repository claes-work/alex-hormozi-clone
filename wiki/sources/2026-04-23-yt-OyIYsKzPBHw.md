# Helping a $250K Accounting Firm Get to $1M

## Summary

Alex coaches an accounting-services founder selling to real estate entrepreneurs. Since
LinkedIn and podcast appearances already work, he advises reclaiming time from
fulfillment, scaling the front-end acquisition motion, and recognizing the current stage
as a normal "swamp" where the founder pays talent and technical debt through overwork.

## Key claims

- Alex says the owner is primarily responsible for acquisition and needs more marketing
  time rather than a completely new channel.
- He says if LinkedIn already works, the owner should find a way to scale LinkedIn
  outbound or use LinkedIn ads that mimic the proven DM structure.
- He says the real meta-constraint is time and attention because the founder is still
  overloaded by back-end work.
- He says every business incurs some form of debt: talent debt, technical debt, or
  financial debt.
- He recommends mapping the calendar, delegating the lowest-leverage activities, and
  getting to roughly six hours per day of promotion.
- He says the $1M-ish stage can feel terrible because the founder either works extreme
  hours or hires expensive help and loses profit.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Every business incurs debt."

> "You focus on the constraint."

> "That's why we call it the swamp."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: swamp stage; talent/technical/financial debt framing; six hours/day promotion; LinkedIn outbound or ads from proven DM structure.
