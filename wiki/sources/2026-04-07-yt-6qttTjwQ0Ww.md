# Most Entrepreneurs Stay Distracted Because They Can't Say No to Bad Customers

## Summary

Alex advises an accounting firm to identify its best current customers rather than guess
at an ideal avatar. Rank the customer base by enjoyment, price, retention, and
profitability; trace the best cohort to its offer and channel; stop low-return channels;
and scale the highest-LTV:CAC source. The difficult step is saying no to lower-value
customers and channels.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex recommends examining roughly the top 10-20% of current customers
  to define the ideal avatar.
- Selection criteria include customers the team likes, who pay most, stay longest, and
  generate the strongest economics.
- He attributes the playbook to a former head of pricing and packaging at Vista Equity
  Partners; the exact person is unnamed in the excerpt.
- Alex describes the playbook as identifying the most profitable customer and acquisition
  source, stopping low-ROI channels, and scaling the highest-LTV:CAC channel fivefold.
- Because existing infrastructure can serve the same logo count, replacing weak customers
  with stronger ones can increase profit without equivalent overhead growth.
- He self-reports using this analysis early with Acquisition.com portfolio prospects.
- The owner's 80-client figure and the reported PE performance effects are unverified here.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Which of these do I like the most, that pay the most, that stay the longest?"

> "They 5x the channel that has the highest LTV to CAC ratio."

> "The problem is that most people can't say no."
