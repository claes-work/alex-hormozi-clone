# I STARTED AND SOLD 6 BUSINESSES BY AGE 32...my boring formula

## Summary
In this solo video Hormozi argues that ~99% of businesses close and only ~1% ever sell because most businesses are worthless — they are jobs, not sellable assets. Using Kiyosaki's employee → self-employed → owner → investor quadrant, he lays out his "boring formula" for making a business sellable: productize the service, build a predictable acquisition channel, then systematically take off every "hat" (fulfillment, sales, marketing, product, decision-making) by hiring people to replace yourself until you are merely a shareholder. He frames the payoff as an asset that "produces cash while you sleep" and stresses this takes years, not months.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated 2021)
- ~99% of businesses eventually close; over a long enough horizon only ~1% ever get sold — not because owners can't market the sale, but because most businesses are worthless (2021).
- A business is only sellable if it is an asset that can run without the owner; most "owners" are actually self-employed, even with employees (2021).
- The switch from employee to self-employed usually means a pay cut, because in a job you are one specialized part of a larger machine; alone you must do every part poorly at first (2021).
- The "boring formula" / sellability sequence: (1) stop doing the fulfillment; (2) build a reliable, single-channel acquisition process (referrals, affiliates, cold outbound, owned media, earned media, or paid); (3) productize the service so it is repeatable, not custom; (4) take off the remaining hats — VP of sales, director of marketing, chief product, and decision-maker — by hiring replacements (2021).
- Custom, sell-to-everyone service businesses are unscalable and get valued poorly; the seller ends up with a multi-year earn-out where the buyer takes the upside and the seller takes the risk (2021).
- As you replace yourself, margin often drops (those roles must now be paid for), but you gain 100% of your time back and become a true shareholder (2021).
- Business is fundamentally a people game once you have the model: you must learn to recruit, hire, manage, and lead; high-skill talent won't work for someone less skilled than they are (2021).
- Once a company crosses multiple eight figures, the people you hire are better than you at everything, and your only real role becomes recruiting and casting the vision (2021).
- Key negotiation leverage principle: the party who needs the deal least wins; make the business so sellable you don't even mind keeping it (the "FU position") (2021).
- Equity in your business is likely the biggest line on your net-worth balance sheet; making it a genuine sellable asset is how net worth "skyrockets" (2021).
