# The Types of Businesses I Like to Buy

## Summary
Podcast interview clip in which Alex lays out Acquisition.com's investing thesis and what businesses he likes to acquire. The core model: Acquisition.com takes a **minority interest** in a business and then **deploys its playbook** — i.e., "I just scaled a business exactly like yours; let me take a minority seat and help you go national." Asked which niches interest him, Alex says it's really "just services": he likes **high-cash-flow service businesses** because they're "super malleable" — you can change price points, product, and client experience *without* a lot of dev/engineering/UX work — which makes them simple and easy to get high margins in "if you know how to deliver value." He is deliberately dismissive of the software hype ("everybody's really hot on software and there's all the valuations... but I may just be a simpleton"). He names four current acquisition targets: (1) **e-learning businesses in a niche**; (2) **brick-and-mortar chains that already have multiple successful locations** and are deciding whether to go national; (3) **SaaS / tech-enabled services** (leaning tech-enabled services that *might* carry a small software component, but software isn't the main thing); and a fourth he blanks on mid-podcast. The bulk of the clip is a case study of his **children's photography** company ("A Chain of Fairies") — his fourth deal, which he now expects to be worth more than all his other businesses combined. Unusually, they own **all** the locations rather than a minority stake: ~13 locations, up from one a year earlier. When the founder came to him, he had one studio plus ~100 B2B customers in a hybrid agency/coaching model Alex "really hated"; the founder was charging $5k for that ("all right, we're never doing that again"). Alex saw a photography studio made far more than it cost to open, so Acquisition.com **fronted the capital and built the studios out** directly. The business is mission-driven (donated $1M last year to kids' causes), rooted in the founders' story of their daughter regaining her voice through a personalized storybook experience — the "take Disney and put it in other areas" positioning. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
