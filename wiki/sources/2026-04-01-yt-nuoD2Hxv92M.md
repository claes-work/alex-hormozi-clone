# How to Stop Caring About What People Think

## Summary

Alex and Chris Williamson discuss the social cost of growth, independent judgment, and
rejection tolerance. Alex grounds nonconformity in evidence rather than reflexive dissent:
one should value an independently checked conclusion enough to accept disagreement and
practice surviving small social rejections.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex interprets friends saying someone has changed as possible lack
  of experience with growth rather than necessarily malicious intent.
- He says normal behavior produces average results, while exceptional outcomes require
  actions that differ from the consensus.
- In investing, he recommends forming an isolated estimate of value, checking the math
  more carefully when it differs from the market, and accepting both opportunity and loss risk.
- Alex self-reports being more miserable trying to satisfy his old social circle than he
  is accepting that many of them may now dislike him; this is autobiography, not a general rule.
- He distinguishes evidence-backed independent belief from inventing a contrary position
  merely to appear different.
- He endorses repeated low-stakes rejection exercises as a way to learn that embarrassment
  is survivable; the named 100-day challenge is discussed but not independently evaluated.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Normal people get normal results. Exceptional people get exceptional results."

> "Truly valuing your opinion of yourself more than other people's opinion of you."

> "You actually have proof and evidence that you're not full of shit."
