# There's No Easy Business

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a CPA/CFO advisory owner who wants to grow from roughly $3M to $20M but has stopped selling because fulfillment is hard. Alex says the owner is supply-constrained, not demand-constrained, and should not start a course/book business just because the assets exist. The priority is to fix operating leverage in the valuable sticky service business, then use content/books/courses as marketing assets when supply can absorb demand.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A business growing organically at 30%+ is already marketing; the owner may just not recognize it.
- Closing new sales at zero will definitely stop growth.
- Courses and books are not easier businesses; they have their own churn, expectations, and fulfillment problems.
- The owner should fix the supply constraint before increasing demand.
- Operating leverage can come from offshore support, better process, a data layer, and eventually AI.
- An AI-first company must first become a data-first company because AI needs complete and usable data.
- Once operating capacity expands, the owner can restart selling and later use existing content assets for lead generation.
- "Hard" is not evidence that the current model is wrong; every business has hard parts.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're supply constrained."

## Notes

- **Speaker attribution**: Revenue, content assets, current sales pause, and fulfillment constraints are the owner's context. Alex's advice is the supply-constraint and operating-leverage diagnosis.
- **L3 recommendation**: Strong candidate for AI/data-first operations and "do not flee hard service work into info products."
