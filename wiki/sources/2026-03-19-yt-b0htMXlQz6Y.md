# How I come up with content ideas

## Summary
Podcast clip on Alex's content-ideation process. Today, his team brings everything — they're "inherently curious people who like my content," watch the podcasts, and pull threads worth going deeper on — so he hasn't been asked to generate topics in a long time. But he explains how he did it solo, using *time* as the anchor across four buckets: (1) **far past** — life-changing experiences/conversations that shaped his worldview (story material); (2) **recent past** — scroll his calendar/meetings from the last week and narrate what actually happened (e.g., a portfolio company's pricing change); (3) **present** — brain-dump whatever comes up in the moment, with X/Twitter as his "home base" for that; (4) **manufactured** — content that doesn't exist yet (e.g., "I lived on $100 for 30 days"), plus case studies and vlogs. The through-line: "most of it is just real stuff" — do the thing, then talk about it. On production cadence: they record ~twice a month, ~one 8-hour day each, with six-page pre-productions per video (themes, 4–5 stories pulled from his other pieces, stats from HoldCo subject-matter experts). He indoctrinates staff by "osmosis… proximity and exposure," notes Gym Launch scaled fully remote ("remote before it was cool"), and is very excited that the new physical HQ will be a game-changer. Ends with the roadmap promo.
