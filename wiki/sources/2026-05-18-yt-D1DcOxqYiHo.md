# Why I Stopped Trying to Outearn My Father

## Summary

Alex reflects on using his father as a scoreboard: first wanting to earn as much as him,
then more than him, then more than his father had ever earned. After achieving that, he
realized he had been trying to win a game whose rules he had not chosen. The excerpt
ends with Alex reconsidering his father's role: if Alex likes who he became, then his
father may have shaped him in exactly the way that produced that outcome.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report only)

- Alex says he once cared deeply about out-earning his father because money functioned
  as a status marker in his family context.
- He describes escalating the goal from matching his father to beating his father's
  lifetime earnings.
- After winning that money-status game, Alex says he realized he had not asked what game
  he actually wanted to win.
- He says many people may be trying to win games whose rules they did not choose.
- Alex says he later felt terrible about aiming his achievement at beating the father
  who raised him.
- He reframes his father's influence by asking whether liking who he became means his
  father may have been the right father for him.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I was trying to beat him at his game."

> "What game am I trying to win?"

> "I like who I am. He raised me."
