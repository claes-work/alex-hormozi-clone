# Hormozi's AI Just Out-Coached Him on an Invisalign Offer

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a dental practice that has dramatically increased Invisalign cases and wants to hire an appointment setter and improve the Invisalign front-end offer. Alex says AI appointment setting will likely make an SDR role short-lived, praises an AI-suggested $49 whitening offer over a $500 discount, and adds sales-process details: take payment by phone, use the card on file, use imaging, and present a menu-style smile outcome.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - dental practice owner/operator. His revenue, Invisalign case count, pricing, receptionist AI, and offer ideas are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A full-time appointment setter for a well-understood medical/dental offer may be a short-lived role because AI appointment setting is already emerging.
- Young, hungry 20-year-olds can work well as SDRs when a human setter is still needed.
- A paid low-ticket whitening offer is better than a pure $500 Invisalign discount because the prospect puts money down and stays in the game.
- Charging over the phone can reduce no-shows and enables no-show charging when appropriate.
- Having the customer's card on file makes the later Invisalign sale smoother.
- The in-office sales process should use imaging to show the patient possible smile outcomes.
- Alex recommends a menu-upsell-like presentation: show options and ask which look the patient prefers, so all options imply buying.
