# Pick One Sales Channel or Stay Broke

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a junk removal and demolition company doing about $1M that serves commercial property managers and wants to reach $10M. The owner asks how long to test a sales channel before choosing one. Alex rejects the premise that the owner must discover the one channel that works; most repeatable channels can work if the company commits. He recommends choosing the channel with the strongest overlap to the founder/team's current skill set, which in this case is in-person networking, and doing much more of it until the business has enough cash and pattern recognition to learn another channel.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Referrals are not a controllable sales channel beyond doing good work.
- Many channels can work; the owner should think "we will make one work" rather than passively search for one that works.
- Choose the first channel based on the highest overlap with the founder or team's existing skill set.
- If the founder is strongest in person, the obvious move is to attend far more small local events that contain the target avatar.
- For a business around $1M, the path to $3M may be doing much more of what already works rather than learning several new channels at once.
- Under roughly $3M, owners often cannot afford high-quality specialists, so they must learn many functions themselves.
- Later businesses grow faster because pattern recognition improves across roles and departments.
- Scaling eventually feels more like assembling businesses than building them from scratch.
