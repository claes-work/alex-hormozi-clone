# The Moment I Realized Private Equity Had a Better Game

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains the setup for Acquisition.com. Before selling Gym Launch, Prestige
Labs, and ALAN in 2021, he and Leila made two investments that went well. During the sale
process, he concluded that private equity had a better game: the buyer's team was more
talented, and the buyer could make more money on Alex's business than Alex did. His entry
strategy was to use marketing and content to create superior deal flow.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex and Leila made two investments in 2020 before selling the company at the end of
  2021.
- Those two investments went well and helped point toward Acquisition.com.
- In 2021, Alex says they sold ALAN, Prestige Labs, and Gym Launch.
- During the sale process, he dealt with many private equity firms.
- He concluded that the private-equity team across the table was more talented than his
  team and would make more money.
- He realized the buyer might make more money from his business than he did, despite not
  founding it or knowing gyms.
- Alex spent much of 2021 talking to investors and finance people to understand that game.
- He did not have the normal Harvard/banking network, so he asked what he could do that
  they could not.
- His answer was marketing: create more deal flow, then pick better.
- The Acquisition.com thesis was that an audience built through long-term value could
  create deal flow and trust.
- He treated the move as a probability bet with "need to believe" assumptions, not as a
  guaranteed outcome.

## Notable verbatim quotes
