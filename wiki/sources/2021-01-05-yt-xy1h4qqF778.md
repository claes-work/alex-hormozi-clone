# The "Assumed Close": How to Close Deals Without Resistance

> Source: [yt-xy1h4qqF778](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xy1h4qqF778) — @AlexHormozi, 2021-01-05. Single speaker (Alex Hormozi).

## Summary

Alex Hormozi recounts how, while running his gym roughly eight years earlier, he stumbled onto the "assumed close" while trying to sell supplements he had over-purchased. After repeated rejections, he sold his first supplement by asking a client *which* flavor she wanted rather than *whether* she wanted the product at all. He frames the assumed close as one of the strongest upsell mechanics in business, arguing it can lift close rates on an upsell into the 90%+ range because the prospect chooses between two options that both involve buying.

## Key claims

- (2021) The assumed close works by presenting an either/or choice where **both options involve purchasing**, so the prospect decides *which one* to buy rather than *whether* to buy. [[../topics/business/sales]]
- (2021) He made his first supplement sale by asking a client to pick a protein flavor (chocolate or vanilla) and a pre-workout flavor (kiwi or strawberry), then defaulting to the card on file — never pitching the product's benefits or how to use it.
- (2021) He deliberately avoided asking any open yes/no purchasing question, because he feared the prospect would "back out" if given the chance to reconsider.
- (2021) Upsells convert far higher than front-end sales because the customer already knows and trusts the seller and a purchase (with payment details on file) has already happened.
- (2021) Whereas a normal front-end close might land one in three or one in four (one in two if you're good), an assumed-close upsell can close roughly nine or nine-and-a-half out of ten, often at higher ticket.
- (2021) He claims a sales process generating heavy traffic and closing 80%+ is usually an upsell, not a front-end close.
- (2021) The supplement upsells eventually produced as much or more profit than the gym service itself, and covered his entire advertising/customer-acquisition cost — effectively turning service revenue into profit.
- (2021) He taught the mechanic across his gym locations, later applied to selling Prestige Labs supplements at high margin.
- (2021) To find something to upsell, predict the next natural thing a customer will buy on their journey (fitness clothes, shoes, at-home gear, apps, groceries/food prep, supplements) and weave it in as an either/or decision.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "And the key was I didn't ask her whether she wanted them. I asked her which one she wanted. And as soon as I realized that, that is when I was introduced to the assumed close."

> "The beauty of an assumed close is that the prospect is choosing between two options that both are buying from you rather than whether or not they're going to purchase."

> "With an assumed close as an upsell, you can sell nine out of 10, nine and a half out of ten of the people that you sit in front of and you can close them at higher tickets at higher percentages."

> "I was able to cover all of my advertising expense simply from the upsells. And so that took all of what used to be my service revenue and just turn that into profit because I could cover all my acquisition costs simply with the upsells."

> "You want to think about what's the next natural thing that they're going to want to need... and weave those into your sales process as an either/or decision."
