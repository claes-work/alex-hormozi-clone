# You Must Endure

## Summary

Alex discusses endurance when the finish line is unknown. He uses a breath-hold
analogy and a hope-study frame to argue that people can endure more when they believe
there is an end, then applies that to entrepreneurship: the entrepreneur must keep
fighting even without knowing when the exit comes.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says knowing there is an endpoint changes how much pain a person can endure.
- He compares entrepreneurship to holding one's breath without knowing when the clock
  ends.
- He says the entrepreneur must fight as if there is an exit even when the timeline is
  unclear.
- He says the best case is winning and the worst case is that the pain eventually will
  not matter.
- He repeats the absolute-acceptance frame: agreeing with "must be nice" accusations can
  remove the attack.
- He says accepting unfair advantages can reduce competitors because many people refuse
  to pay that social price.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You must endure."

> "Best case, you win. Worst case, it won't matter."

> "Must be nice. Yeah, it is."
