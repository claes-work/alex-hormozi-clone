# How To Stop Being Anxious About Your Goals

## Summary
In a conversation with a British host/podcaster (who shares stories about his own club-promoter 20s), Alex Hormozi argues that anxiety about goals comes from trying to balance too many priorities at once. He reframes "work-life balance" as something to periodize over years and decades rather than split within a single day, and defines emotional states — sadness as a perceived lack of options, anxiety as many options but no priorities. He closes with a promo for the acquisition.com $100M scaling roadmap.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex maintains that he is fine being "a beacon of relentless hard work," believing the pro-balance / mental-health side is "wildly over-represented" and that more people he has met regret a "live-it-up" 20s than a "work-it-up" 20s.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that in your 20s you usually don't know what you want, but you do know how to make progress through work — so you should work on the known, high-confidence path and gain perspective along the way.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames work-life balance as best split over a longer time horizon (season/year/decade), e.g. working hard for 3 years then a chill year, rather than balancing within each day.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims maintaining a skill costs roughly 1/10 the effort of growing it, so "effort arbitrage" favors going 10-or-13-out-of-10 on one priority while merely maintaining the rest; doing 4/10 on ten things makes progress on none.
- As of March 2026, Alex defines sadness as a perceived (not actual) lack of options, and anxiety as many options but no priorities — and says the escape from sadness is realizing the only task is to figure out what to do.
- As of March 2026, Alex endorses "hyperbolic discounting" / hedonic renormalizing as a frame: now is not forever, good things aren't as good and bad things aren't as bad as they feel.
- Alex attributes to a person named George the line "General ambition gives you anxiety. Specific ambition gives you direction," said (per Alex) while they were on mushrooms in Nashville around the 4th of July. (Unverified anecdote.)
- The host (a caller, not Alex) claims he spent roughly 5,000–10,000 hours working the front door of nightclubs, including ~3,000 hours at one club on Saturdays, and did not miss 202 Saturdays in a row. (Unverified caller claim; not persona data.)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "There's no perfect way to live your 20s. You either live them up and become an under-skilled 30-year-old or you work them up and become an under-lived 30-year-old."

> "I am okay being a beacon of relentless hard work. I'm okay being the guy who says, 'Fuck your mental health.' ... Because I think it will help more people."

> "Sadness is a perceived lack of options. It's why it feels like hopelessness because you don't know what to do."

> "Anxiety is many options but no priorities, which is why you feel scattered."

> "If you think about what priority means, it means prior. It comes before everything else."

> "If you do a four out of 10 on 10 things, you will make progress on none... You just don't regress. But the extra three points that you save on the nine, you could put on one other item and have a 10 out of 10 or a 13 out of 10 in effort."

> "The further you extend the time horizon, the more flexible you can be with your achievement of it as long as you only get the few things that matter most."

> "General ambition gives you anxiety. Specific ambition gives you direction."

> "A perceived lack of options is what causes sadness, not a lack of options... all I have to do is figure out what I need to do. And figuring it out becomes the option."
