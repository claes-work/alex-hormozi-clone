# You Can't Target Christians in Ads, So Do This Instead

## Summary

Alex advises a worship leader launching a mentorship program not to worry about being
unable to select religious targeting directly. The creative and message can do the
targeting: visuals, props, setting, language, and who appears in the ad will attract the
right audience. He sketches an ad filmed from the church stage, with the congregation in
the background, a short origin story, a lead magnet, and a CTA for personalized feedback
inside the Skool community.

## Key claims

- If platforms block direct religious targeting, the ad's message and creative can still
  attract the intended audience.
- The people, props, background imagery, clothing, and setting in the ad all influence who
  responds.
- Alex would film the worship leader on stage with the congregation visible to signal the
  target market.
- The ad should include a personal origin story and proof that the leader now trains
  others.
- A lead magnet can teach quick pre-stage actions, while the paid community sells
  personalized video feedback and weekly calls.
