# Do This And You Will NEVER Need A Marketing Budget For Your Gym EVER AGAIN

## Summary
Solo teaching video in which Hormozi walks gym owners through "Client Finance Acquisition," the model Gym Launch uses and teaches, framed as the reason the company scaled past $100M in revenue over six years and how it moves gyms to over $100K/month. He first dismantles two "broken" models — the organic/referral model (killed by ~16% industry-average churn eating all new members) and the seven-day free pass (which adds members but is cash-flow negative). He then argues that a high-ticket front-end offer ($599–$1,000 challenges) that earns more on the front-end sale than it costs in ad spend lets each new client's collected cash fund the next round of ads, so the gym profits immediately and never needs a separate marketing budget.

## Key claims
- The model taught is "Client Finance Acquisition"; Gym Launch has scaled past $100M in revenue over six years and teaches gym owners to scale past $100K/month with it.
- Over six years Gym Launch has talked to over 33,000 gym owners, collecting data and numbers on the industry.
- Broken model #1 — organic/referral: industry average is that at best ~10% of clients refer, and ~40% of referrals actually close. Even a great gym adding 10 members/month gets wiped out by churn.
- Industry-average monthly churn is ~16%; a 100-member gym at 10% churn loses 10 members in the same month it gains 10, leaving it flat on a "hamster wheel."
- Churn includes people who move away or stop paying, not just formal cancellations.
- Broken model #2 — seven-day free pass: even with generous assumptions ($1,000 ad spend, 100 leads, 30 redeem, 15 join at $150/mo), the gym nets a cash-flow loss (~$250) in the month because lost recurring revenue plus ad spend exceeds new EFT revenue.
- Boutique/smaller gyms cannot run cash-flow-negative acquisition because, unlike PE-backed chains (La Fitness, Planet Fitness), they lack reserves to wait 5–6 months for lifetime value to kick in.
- Client Finance Acquisition requires a high-ticket front-end product; he suggests $599–$1,000 depending on length, finding six-week challenges at $599 and eight-week challenges at $997 highest-performing.
- Gym owners close 50–70% of cold traffic into these offers, depending on the owner's skill.
- Core principle: make more money on front-end sales than on lead/ad costs, with enough left for profit — so you never need a marketing budget again.
- Worked example: $100/day ad spend → 10 leads → 5 show → close 2 at $600 = $1,200/day collected; credit-card processing delays cash ~4 days, so early days run negative on paper.
- Once day-one cash lands on day four, each day's ~$1,200 collection funds subsequent ad spend (e.g., $800 net covers ~8 more days of ads), and remaining money is pure profit — profiting immediately upon closing.
- Losing money one month forces a smaller ad budget the next month, fewer clients, and compounding churn — a "vicious cycle" that keeps gyms stuck.
- Offers a free download, "the seven money models," linked in the video description.
