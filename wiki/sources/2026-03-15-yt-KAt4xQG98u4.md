# Passive Income Is Overrated

## Summary
Short interview clip in which Alex argues that passive income is overrated: what people
chase as "freedom" — outsourcing every activity and buying all their time back — actually
leaves them with nothing to do, and what they really want is **optionality**: the option
to engage in meaningful activities on their own terms. He grounds it in his own
experience. In 2021, after selling his three majority-owned companies (ALAN, Prestige
Labs, and Gym Launch), he deliberately took a passive seat — his CFO ran the deals, and
Alex stepped back rather than go "hardcore" on scaling Acquisition.com before that process
finished. The result was not bliss but boredom: a near-"existential crisis," because
passive freedom was the exact thing he had always optimized toward, and having achieved it
he found "this sucks." Looking back, the periods of *building* each company were "some of
the happiest times of my life." So he chose to get back in the game — at the time of
filming, running two full days a week of interviews to build out Acquisition.com's core
team — and reports being genuinely happy doing it. His key distinction: the difference
between this time and last time is that he now has the *option* to work, and is choosing it
rather than being forced into it. Because he built the business, he created that option for
himself; without it, he "wouldn't have the option to work." The reframe he lands on is
**freedom → optionality**. Ends with the standard $100M scaling roadmap promo
(acquisition.com/roadmap).
