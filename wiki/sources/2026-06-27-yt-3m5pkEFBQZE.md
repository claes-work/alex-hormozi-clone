# Our Close Rate Is Insane But Nobody Shows Up

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a closer for a home-services scaling offer with very high close rates but poor show rates. Alex diagnoses a pre-call funnel issue rather than a closing issue. The fixes are more friction and proof before booking, a real five-to-seven-minute VSL, lead nurture, proof-promise-plan framing, belief-breaking, and personalized pre-call research that makes the prospect feel understood.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - closer for a plumber-scaling offer. His show-rate, close-rate, and funnel details are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If speed-to-lead and call-attempt fundamentals are already in place, poor show rate usually points to what happens before scheduling.
- Too little friction can create vanity scheduled-call volume while reducing actual shows.
- A higher cost per booking can be good if the show rate increases enough to improve the economics.
- The application should add qualification and friction before the prospect reaches the calendar.
- A 10-second owner video is not enough; Alex recommends a five-to-seven-minute VSL.
- The VSL should begin with proof, promise, and plan, then break the prospect's main beliefs by explaining what they believe, why they are wrong, what is right, and proof.
- Lead nurture and the proof checklist are the missing playbooks behind the low show rate.
- AI and automation can personalize pre-call research so the prospect feels the team has already studied their business.
