# Tell Them What Didn't Work — That's the Value

## Summary

Alex explains "build with me" positioning by describing Gym Launch R&D. His team would
run $50,000 tests every two weeks, and when half of them failed to beat the control, he
still considered the report valuable because clients no longer had to spend time or money
running the same failed test. Community value comes from compressing others' learning by
doing work and sharing the findings, including what did not work.

## Key claims

- A creator can sell a "build with me" value prop by doing real experiments and reporting
  what happened.
- At Gym Launch, Alex says an R&D team ran $50,000 tests every two weeks on tactics that
  might help gyms.
- Failed tests were still valuable because members learned what not to try.
- People join communities to save time, not merely to access information that already
  exists online.
- Community operators are curators who compress relevant information per second of
  consumption.
- Doing the test personally makes the lesson more believable and relevant.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "We're just going to tell them the exact thing that we did and just tell them it didn't work."

> "That's value."

> "People go into communities to save time."
