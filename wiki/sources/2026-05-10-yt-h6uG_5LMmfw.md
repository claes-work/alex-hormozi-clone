# How to Sell a $30K Offer With a 50% Close Rate

## Summary

A career-planning founder asks how to sell a one-year, white-glove job-placement program
for young adults at around $30,000 after selling a $4,000 planning offer. Alex says the
price can work because parents already pay similar amounts for college in hopes of job
outcomes. He recommends selling during the existing program before it ends, involving
parents in midpoint and final calls, testing qualifications by meeting everyone early,
using VSL preframes, and structuring payment around cash up front plus job-placement
milestones.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says a $30,000 career-year offer can sell because parents already pay roughly
  that range for a year of college to get job outcomes.
- He recommends selling the ascension offer between week two and week three, before the
  first program ends.
- He says selling before the initial program ends makes the second offer feel like the
  next phase rather than a separate buying decision.
- He recommends parent check-in calls at the halfway point and the end.
- He says early in a new sales motion, the company may need to meet with many prospects
  and reverse-engineer who buys.
- He recommends a VSL before the parent sales call.
- He suggests payment structures such as half now and half on job offer, or payment
  plans with as much cash up front as possible.
- He recommends crediting the $4,000 planning fee toward the larger offer to make it
  feel like phase one of a multi-step process.
- He says a $35,000 price may behave similarly to $30,000 in this context.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It'll totally sell at that price."

> "In the beginning on any sales motion, we kiss all the toads."

> "We want to make it feel like it's incomplete on a multi-step process, not a new sale."
