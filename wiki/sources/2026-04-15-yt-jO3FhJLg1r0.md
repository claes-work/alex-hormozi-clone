# How to Get Rich by Doing Less, Not More

## Summary

Alex says people stay poor by starting new things instead of getting good at one thing.
He uses his own history of multiple simultaneous agencies and gyms as the cautionary tale:
the complexity fed his ego, not his bank account. Leila pushed him to cut everything but
the one thing he was clearly good at.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-15, Alex says it is arrogant to think one can do multiple things at once and
  beat someone doing one thing with full effort.
- Self-reported: he says he had nine failed businesses and six at the same time; he lists
  a chiropractor agency, dental agency, Gym Launch turnarounds, and five gyms.
- He distinguishes that old complexity from current Acquisition.com portfolio work, where
  CEOs run their own P&Ls and he drives Acquisition.com.
- Self-reported: he says Leila told him he was clearly good at one thing and should cut
  everything else.
- He says the complexity generated revenue but not profit, and fed ego rather than the bank
  account.
- He says imposter syndrome often comes from misrepresentation or posturing; the solution
  is to state facts and tell the truth, then make the truth more compelling through work.
- He says determination is rewarded more than intelligence, but in the short term it looks
  like a long string of failures.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The only thing that it fed was my ego, not my bank account."

> "If you want to eliminate imposter syndrome, just stop lying."
