# One of My Favorite Negotiation Tactics and How to Use It

## Summary

Alex explains MESO negotiation: multiple equivalent simultaneous offers. Presenting
several acceptable options embeds reasonableness, reveals the other side's priorities,
and can turn negotiation into a positive-sum exchange. He also shares Sharran
Srivatsaa's counter-tactic: ask for several versions, then combine the best pieces into
an option D.

## Key claims

- Alex says MESO means presenting two or three offers with different prices and terms
  that all work for the seller.
- He says multiple acceptable offers embed reciprocity because they signal flexibility.
- He says the tactic helps reveal priorities when the other side is not willing to say
  what matters most.
- He says negotiation is not zero-sum because different parties value different terms.
- He says the best negotiation gives away what matters less to one side and more to the
  other, creating positive-sum value.
- He credits Sharran with the counter-tactic of asking for multiple versions and then
  combining the best pieces into a fourth offer.
