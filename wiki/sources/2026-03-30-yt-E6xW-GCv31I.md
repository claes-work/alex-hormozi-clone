# Stop Working Part Time on a Full Time Opportunity

## Summary
In this recorded Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a solo AI-sector headhunter who does ~$1.5M revenue (self-reported ~$1.35M net) on referrals alone and wants to reach $5M in 2-3 years. Alex reframes the caller's questions about branding and content as distractions, arguing the real constraint is simply that the caller runs a "lifestyle business" and isn't using his available time. His prescription: accept the inbound "whale" clients already coming in, restart outbound (the caller's existing skill), and add a minimal "pulse" of content to look credible — not make content the strategy.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that when someone with an unused capacity wants more revenue, the answer is often simply to "work more" — the constraint is not time but the failure to use it.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that the way you are accustomed to acquiring customers is often the most efficient way to acquire talent (and vice versa), because the skill set already exists — so a recruiter used to outbound should get customers via outbound too.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames content as bifurcated: a large volume needed to use content as a top-of-funnel channel, versus a much smaller amount (roughly one or two LinkedIn posts a week) needed just to look like a real business and support the primary acquisition channel.
- As of March 2026, Alex says content should be the "base level" supporting a primary channel; customers who arrive via content are "bonus or gravy," while outbound is the main channel for this business early on.
- As of March 2026, Alex names acquisition and delivery as the two core components of a business that should never be outsourced; bookkeeping, recruiting, and media buying can be outsourced, but creative strategy, offers, and pricing stay in-house.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises the caller not to hire yet — the constraint is unused time, not a lack of it — and predicts that reaching $5M will generate cash flow to later renegotiate better/non-exclusive rates and hire outbound help.
- Caller claim (self-reported, unverified): runs a solo AI-sector headhunting business for ~7 years, ~$1.5M top-line revenue, ~$1.35M net profit, doing zero paid customer acquisition, with large "whale" clients arriving inbound.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You want 5 million. You're not working very much. So, the solution is work more?"

> "The way that you were accustomed to acquiring customers is often the most efficient way for you to acquire talent. Not always, but often, because you already have the skill set."

> "And so like the highest likelihood path is just to do more what you're doing. Just do it on the other side."

> "I think having the outbound spin up and then having what I would consider a pulse of content rather than trying to make content your strategy."

> "There's the level of content that you have to create in order to use it as top of funnel and then there's a significantly smaller amount of content that you need to look like you're an actual business."

> "Anybody who comes in from the content I just see as like bonus or gravy, but the main acquisition channel is probably going to be outbound for you in the beginning."

> "You're making one and a half as a lifestyle business. You could just not be a lifestyle business and probably get to five."

> "So, like we're talking about something that's just like not the constraint. Maybe it's something that you're annoyed about, but it's not the problem."

> "More. Just more of it. I'm being dead serious. It's what it is."

> "You're currently not doing outbound and when you did do it it started to get you customers and then you stopped working it. So, don't stop once it starts working."

> "Acquisition and delivery are two of the core components of a business... I see those as the last things are basically things that you never outsource."

> "TLDR, make more content, do more outbound, stop working part-time, and you'll probably get to your 5 million."
