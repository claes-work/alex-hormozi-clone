# How Do We Protect Info Product Businesses From AI?

## Summary
In a live Q&A, a Brazilian portfolio operator describes a group of businesses (110M reais in 2025, targeting 200M reais in 2026) in which he holds minority stakes, mostly info products (online courses, mentorships). He asks Alex two things: whether centralizing shared services (sales, HR) across these small companies makes sense, and how to protect info-product businesses against AI. Alex advises against building a central services team for stakes that small, and — notably — says he would not try to protect an info-product business from AI at all, instead extracting maximum cash while pivoting to a different vehicle.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex would NOT build a centralized shared-services team (e.g. sales, HR specialists) for small minority-held portfolio companies, because the businesses are not big enough to afford that talent; the team's cost would eat up 100% or more of the economics, turning it into a money-loser, and you would have to bank on exiting positions that are small and highly liquid — a bet he would not make.
- As of March 2026, when asked how to protect an info-product business (online courses, mentorships) against AI, Alex's answer is blunt: he would not protect it. He would try to make as much money as possible from it and look for a different vehicle.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the AI threat to info products around a shift in buyer behavior: people increasingly do not want to learn — they want the answer — which AI supplies directly.
- (Caller claim, unverified) The caller's portfolio did 110M reais in 2025, is targeting 200M reais in 2026, holds roughly 20–30% minority stakes with aligned founders, and is concentrated in info products.
