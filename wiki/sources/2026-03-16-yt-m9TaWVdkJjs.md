# Helping an Orthopedic Surgeon Doing $2.1M/Year Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. An orthopedic surgeon specializing in orthobiologics and regenerative medicine does ~$2.1M/year, having shifted over the past year from an insurance-based practice toward more cash-based services. Much of that cash-based growth rode a "credibility tailwind" from a Joe Rogan podcast appearance. He wants to scale to $10M and diagnoses his own bottleneck as **top of funnel** — his close rate is very high (~80%), so he doesn't need better selling, he needs more qualified people who know and understand what he does. The complication: healthcare marketing is constrained by FDA/FTC limits on what he's allowed to claim. Alex's core answer is to **turn the existing Rogan asset into a video sales letter**: identify the highest-likelihood candidates for the service, build an audience of them, and drive them to watch (a possibly truncated version of) the Rogan episode — which they'll want to watch anyway because it's Rogan and on-topic. "That podcast does the selling for you," sidestepping the compliance problem because Rogan makes the claims, not the practice. To keep the relationship with Rogan clean, Alex prefers driving paid traffic to Rogan's own episode/asset rather than re-cutting it — and notes that running ads to a YouTube video "you're promoting my show, so that's clean." Because the surgeon's patients are mostly **local** (with some flying in), Alex refines the play: drop a **50-mile-radius pin** and target locally — proximity to "the guy Rogan talked about" makes conversion likely; that local audience becomes the lead magnet. The last missing piece: the practice had **no sales function** (common in healthcare) — it needs at least one person to proactively work the leads ("Did you get a chance to watch the podcast? What was the most interesting part?") and book appointments. Alex frames the whole thing as his "60 seconds, hands tied behind my back" first move; the deeper second move is diagnosing where the centers of influence are and whether the best channel is affiliate, content, or paid ads, choosing the lowest-risk path for the owner's skill set. Ends with the roadmap promo.
