# Learn Email Marketing in 39 Minutes!

## Summary

Solo masterclass in which Alex Hormozi breaks down the email-marketing system behind
his own newsletter ("Mozi Money Minute" / acquisition.com newsletter), which he only
launched "not that many months ago" after avoiding email for the vast majority of his
career. He argues email is one of the highest-ROI, most-ignored channels (cites a
35–45x average ROI) and walks through ~10 tactics covering list building, opt-ins,
deliverability, cadence, copy structure, and segmentation. He treats email as just
another form of content — every send must earn its keep — and repeatedly frames the
whole system around behavioral reward loops. He admits emailing late was one of the
biggest mistakes of his career (e.g. didn't email Gym Launch until 2–3 years in).

## Key claims

- **Why email**: average email ROI is 35–45x ("for every dollar you spend... you can
  get like $45 back"). The contact is already acquired, so email is essentially free
  follow-up — only real cost is software plus whoever writes the emails. One writer can
  send to 10 million people = huge leverage.
- **Email is durable and universal**: 61% check email multiple times/day, 20% multiple
  times/hour; consumption is roughly uniform across age brackets (15–24 up through 65+).
- **Email = content**: same objective as content (make them buy now or more likely to
  buy later), but with guaranteed delivery — you own the list; the algorithm doesn't
  gatekeep who sees it, past performance does.
- **Make unsubscribing easy**: don't keep an uninterested segment captive. Emailing
  dormant/uninterested people for vanity volume lowers domain deliverability long-term
  and hurts the people who do want your emails. Unsubscribes aren't an insult — many
  workshop attendees had unsubscribed.
- **"Shaking the tree"**: the first email after a long gap pulls forward churn — you get
  this month's and next month's churn at once. His highest-ever unsubscribe was on his
  first email (just over 1%); now steady ~0.42% per email, which is fine as long as the
  list grows faster than that.
- **Segmentation** is the biggest lever: cites HubSpot ~791% ROI increase from
  segmenting. Send the right email to the right people (by revenue tier / has-a-business
  qualifiers captured at opt-in) — "way less about how good the email is and so much
  more about who's getting it." Urges people to be honest in opt-in fields.
- **Email structure/template** (Mozi Money Minute): consistent subject brand → a
  quote/tweet up top (immediate reward for opening) → the "meat" kept under ~200 words
  (~one minute) → CTA → PS statement. Reuse the template ("think about McDonald's...
  I don't want any surprises").
- **Reward loops**: behavior is driven by what happens *after* the action, not before.
  Reward every step — reward the open (top-of-email quote), reward the read (valuable
  nugget), reward clicking so the audience is trained to click again.
- **PS statement in every email** — after the headline it's the most-read part ("not
  having a PS statement is PS stupid").
- **Drive clicks (don't hoard them)**: too many links is bad, but you should regularly
  get people clicking. Don't dump a full blog in the email — put an interesting nugget
  and link out, training the audience to click.
- **Mobile optimization**: ~58–60% of traffic is mobile; check emails in both desktop
  and mobile views (especially with images) or click-through plummets.
- **Deliverability / avoiding the promo tab**: keep emails looking like plain personal
  emails (mostly text, ~1–2 links max, minimal/no images); strip "money language" from
  the body/title (more money talk = higher promo-tab rate); get replies (a reply signals
  engagement to the server). B2B stat: ~16.99% of marketing emails never even reach the
  inbox. No silver bullet — "100 golden BBs": consistently send emails people open,
  read, click. Align with the platform's objective (deliver high-quality email
  experience) instead of chasing hacks.
- **Google-native links** (a "2011 advanced tactic"): links to Google-owned/understood
  assets (YouTube, Google Drive-hosted sales letters/VSLs) can get higher deliverability
  and click-through — sometimes "duct-taped" assets convert better.
- **A/B test subject lines**: split-test the subject on a small percentage, then send the
  winner to the rest (same logic as YouTube thumbnail/title testing).
- **Opt-in strategy**: give a platform-specific bonus (e.g. a strong "secret chapter"
  peeled out of a book) that only list subscribers get. On opt-in, sell them on the
  *ongoing communication*, not just the lead magnet — deliver the promised reward near
  the end after reselling why to stay. Have them reply "yes" to get the bonus (reply =
  deliverability boost + engagement).
- **Cadence**: best marketers told him ~3x/week is the sweet spot ("the more often you
  email the more you will convert," up to an annoyance ceiling that quality offsets). If
  starting from zero, begin once a month. He personally refuses to commit to a fixed
  cadence — a quality threshold instead ("if I've got two good ones in me, two go out...
  if I've got zero it's zero"). He batch-wrote 24 emails in half a day.
- **Annual renewal fee tactic** (from one of the emails): add a one-time annual fee
  (~1–3 months' price) on top of monthly for recurring businesses; doesn't hurt front-end
  conversion (happens a year later), adds ~8–25% revenue at no added cost, can double
  profit on a low-margin business. Example: $39/mo + $99 annual → effective ~$47/mo,
  ~21% revenue lift, while keeping the advertised $39 that drives conversions.
- **Speed-to-contact** (an in-email tactic): call leads within 60 seconds of opt-in;
  cites Harvard Business Review ~391% increase in sales.
- **Preview text**: optimize it (~24% of people check preview text before opening);
  otherwise the client grabs the first 150 characters ("hey John...") — pull curiosity-
  inducing nuggets forward. Takes ~10 seconds.
- **Metrics**: market has shifted from open rate to CTR (Apple privacy laws broke open-
  rate tracking). His last-3-months benchmark: open rate ~35.7%, CTR ~8.5%. Optimize CTR
  over a longer window, not per-email ("depositing before we withdraw").
- **Send-timing golden BBs**: B2C best days Mon/Tue (~18% open lift); B2B best day
  Wednesday (comparable lift). Best times ~10am–noon and ~1–3pm (before/after lunch);
  first-thing-morning is too competitive with real business email. (Day-of-week data
  credited to Neil Patel.) Timing matters most for single-time-zone/local businesses.
- **Testimonial hooks** (from a top-performing email): reviewed 2,500+ Gym Launch
  testimonials; the top-4 hooks all had a pain-based hook and were about a *moment*.
  Don't ask "how was life before" — ask "what was your worst moment." Moments make hooks;
  details make them powerful.
- **Contextual CTAs**: don't copy-paste the same CTA everywhere or slice in generic mini-
  ads — make the CTA contextual to the content (this video's CTA was about email;
  beginner content → Skool, high-level → scaling workshops/acquisition.com).
- **Just start**: waiting to email was one of his biggest career mistakes; adding email
  follow-up on Skool produced a ~40% lift in conversion.
