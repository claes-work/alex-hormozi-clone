# What Makes Goals Worth Pursuing? | Dave Ramsey

## Summary
Interview clip in which **Alex Hormozi interviews Dave Ramsey** (founder of Ramsey Solutions / EntreLeadership) about how a mature, multi-unit company decides which new ideas to pursue, who runs them, how leaders are compensated, and why the company holds no outside equity. **The bulk of the substantive content is Dave Ramsey's** — his org, his frameworks — and is context only, NOT persona training data. Alex's role is the interviewer; his own contributions (the shiny-object/whiplash warning and the "goose vs. eggs" framing of acquisition.com) are the only material that may inform persona.

Dave Ramsey's account: in the early days they "just did it," but now new ideas go through a beta with ROAS, CAC, margin, and opportunity-cost checks — most things "don't make it out of the test tube." Anyone can throw an idea "on the table" but must defend it and its resource usage, because "ideas are dime-a-dozen; people who can implement them are the problem." Ideas usually bubble up through the VP of the relevant business unit (e.g. EntreLeadership); orphan ideas with no home go to a **special-projects "win team"** — an internal incubator/"shark tank"/"NICU" — that also fixes broken units. New leaders are ~80–90% in-house (they run on "bench depth"), ideally the person in whose "heart" the idea was born, because they'll "fight all the enemies" for their "baby." Structurally the company runs a **matrix**: each business unit is job-costed with its own P&L, and its VP is paid off that bottom line ("I want them to be very wealthy because that means they made me a lot of money"). The operating board is half C-suite, half business-unit heads. On equity: Ramsey decided ~25 years ago **not** to go public, sell, or take a liquidity event — Simon Sinek's "infinite game," perpetual generational operation. Therefore **no one holds equity**; leaders instead earn "lights-out scary good money" off the bottom line. He owns 100% (now held in his grown kids' names for estate reasons, with 1% voting stock retained). Alex closes with the roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, new ideas at Ramsey are now vetted via a beta measuring ROAS, customer acquisition cost, margin, and — decisively — **opportunity cost**; a low-return idea "we fell in love with" must be let die because the real cost is the missed return on something already working "10x" better. (context, not persona)
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, his innovation posture: test many ideas quickly, iterate before killing, and double down on what hits — but "when we lose hope" a thing will scale at a margin commensurate with resources committed, "we've got to be grown-ups" and kill it. (context)
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, "ideas are dime-a-dozen; people who can implement them are the problem" — anyone may propose an idea but must defend both the idea and its resource usage. (context)
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, ideas normally bubble up through the VP of the relevant business unit (front-line coaches surface customer needs); orphan ideas go to a **special-projects "win team"** incubator that also rehabilitates broken/underperforming units — Dave personally sits on that team because he "love[s] working on broken things." (context)
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, ~80–90% of new leaders are promoted in-house from "bench depth," preferably the originator of the idea, because someone for whom it "was born in their heart" will champion and defend it internally and externally. (context)
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, ~20 years ago Ramsey moved from siloed departments (the "web department") to a **matrix**: each business unit is job-costed with its own P&L and rent/overhead allocation, and its leader is paid off that unit's bottom line. (context)
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, the operating board is half C-suite and half business-unit heads; accounting/budgeting support is rolled under bootstrapped operators so they can keep doing what they're good at while the numbers get counted. (context)
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, Ramsey holds **no equity for anyone**: ~25 years ago they decided against IPO, sale, or any liquidity event — Simon Sinek's "infinite game," perpetual generational operation. Leaders instead make "lights-out scary good money" off the bottom line "like they're a partner." (context)
- **[Dave Ramsey]** As of March 2026, he owns 100% (stock now in his grown children's names for estate purposes, retaining 1% voting stock); reasoning: people sometimes leave, and "I cry a lot if they took stock with them." (context)
- **[Alex Hormozi]** As of March 2026, Alex's warning on expansion: pursuing new stuff "happens at all levels," but with more resources you can "distract the team, they feel whiplash, and then sometimes the core business starts to falter because everyone's looking at the new shiny toy rather than the main game."
- **[Alex Hormozi]** As of March 2026, Alex frames acquisition.com as a **"forever business"** he and Leila plan to keep owning and operating — the **"goose versus the eggs"**: the 14 profit centers are the eggs, acquisition.com is the goose / "the mothership."
