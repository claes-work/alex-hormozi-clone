# Is Everyone Teachable? A Controversial View on Skills and Behavior

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says everyone is theoretically teachable, but the real managerial question
is whether teaching a person is worth the time, cost, and uncertainty. He collapses hard
and soft skills into behavior change: communication, leadership, and sales should be
broken into observable micro-behaviors rather than treated as vague personality labels.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Everyone is teachable in principle; the economic question is whether teaching that
  person is worth it.
- Older people tend to have less behavioral plasticity, so changing them can require more
  effort.
- Buy-versus-build hiring is an ROI question, not a moral question.
- Soft skills are still behavior skills; broad labels must be broken into observable
  sub-skills.
- Managers should identify the smallest skill deficiency that would create the needed
  business result.
- An entrepreneur's job is allocation of resources.
- Strategy means prioritization: deciding where limited resources go.
- If someone has massive ego and is not receptive, the current behavior may be what the
  company gets unless the manager accepts a high training cost.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Everyone is teachable. The question is, is it worth it?"

> "Strategy is prioritization."
