# Social Media Is Dead. Interest Media Is Here

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that creators should think less in terms of social media and more in
terms of interest media: the content itself is the targeting. He says business creators
should judge content by whether it reaches the right avatar and creates real-world
responses, not by comparing raw views to mass entertainment.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Judging business content only by views is a mistake.
- A humorous broad video may get many views without attracting the buyer or increasing
  belief in the business.
- Business content is usually educational rather than entertainment-first because it is
  meant to change behavior and move the viewer toward a purchase.
- To attract the right avatar, make content for that avatar.
- The content is the targeting because modern algorithms infer topics, audience, visuals,
  and viewer interest.
- A small number of highly relevant views can be valuable if the target market is small.
- Alex uses real-world responses from respected business owners as a stronger signal than
  broad vanity metrics.
- His "cash cow" business-breakdown format is favored by business owners who visit
  Acquisition.com in person, according to Alex.
- Only about 9% of Americans own businesses, and most are below $1 million in revenue.
- He gives rough market math: about 32 to 33 million U.S. business owners, about 1.5
  million over $1 million, about 0.4% over $10 million, and roughly 1 in 3,000 businesses
  reaching $100 million.
- A creator should set expectations from the size of the target market, not from mass
  entertainment benchmarks.
- Small followings can still produce large businesses if the content is directly valuable
  to the right audience.
