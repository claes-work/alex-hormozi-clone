# Should I Add a Second Offer to My Webinar?

## Summary

A personal-branding and content-education founder asks whether to add a second offer to
a virtual event whose primary goal is selling tickets to a later in-person event. Alex
warns that the second offer may be a made-up opportunity rather than a real problem,
borrowing a frame he attributes to Leila. He recommends protecting the primary sale,
using automated upsells on the way in, and only making the second offer after the ticket
goal is reached, positioned as a bridge to the live event.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says an in-person selling environment usually converts better than a virtual one.
- He says if the caller will pitch high-ticket offers at the in-person event, he should
  not worry too much about money left on the virtual table.
- He attributes to Leila the frame: this may be a missed opportunity, not a problem to
  solve.
- He says the risk of damaging the main virtual-event sale is relatively high.
- He approves automated upsells such as replays or assets on the way into the purchase.
- He says a higher ticket tier could work if it adds a real benefit.
- He suggests that if the ticket goal is already reached, the caller could offer a
  recurring community as a bridge between the webinar and the event.
- He suggests crediting the event ticket toward the community to make the second offer
  easier.
- He warns the only major risk is cannibalizing sales at the live event.
- He repeats that young people choosing between agency work and starting should consider
  learn-or-earn opportunities and value roles that teach a lot quickly.
