# You Need to Call Your Leads Faster ★

★ Adds concrete speed-to-lead data/mechanics (60-second call window, 4–5x conversion multiple, a staffed-caller cost/ROI worked example).

## Summary

Short solo clip (@MoreMozi) in which Alex Hormozi argues that speed-to-lead — calling opted-in leads within 60 seconds — is one of the highest-leverage, lowest-cost moves most companies overlook. He frames it as a prioritization test: among all the initiatives a company is weighing, few if any have as high a chance of 4–5x-ing the business immediately at such low cost. He preempts the common objection ("we don't have enough people to call leads that fast") by reframing it as a hiring math problem — the cost of one dedicated caller versus the value of 4x-ing company revenue — and stresses the hire is paid out of the new revenue it generates, not the current budget. He illustrates with a water-damage restoration company whose owner converts 55% of leads by paying a single dedicated person to drop everything and call each lead the instant it arrives. Clip ends with a promo for his free acquisition.com/roadmap resource.

Solo speaker throughout (Alex Hormozi). The restoration-company owner's words are relayed second-hand by Alex, not a second on-camera speaker.

## Key claims (2026-02-18, paraphrased unless quoted)

- Research suggests you can 4x–5x your conversion of leads by calling them within 60 seconds of them opting in for any product. (paraphrase)
- Speed-to-lead is a prioritization filter: if none of a company's current priorities has a higher likelihood of 4–5x-ing the business immediately at lower cost than calling leads within 60 seconds, then leads should be called within 60 seconds. (paraphrase)
- Common objection ("fob question"): "we don't have enough people to call our leads within 60 seconds." Hormozi's counter — weigh the cost of hiring one person whose only job is to call leads against the value of 4x-ing company revenue. (paraphrase)
- The dedicated caller is funded out of the *new* revenue the practice generates, not out of current dollars — "you're going to pay for it out of the dollars that you're going to make that you're not making yet." (paraphrase of a near-verbatim line)
- Restoration-company case study (water-damage repair for storm-hit homes): the owner converts **55% of leads** — leads, not calls. (paraphrase)
- That company gets only **2–3 leads per day**. (paraphrase)
- The owner pays his aunt **$60,000/year** for a single job with no other company responsibility: the instant a lead comes in, she stops everything and immediately calls it — roughly three calls a day. (paraphrase)
- That $60k dedicated-caller role reportedly brings in "millions of dollars of revenue." (paraphrase; figure is the owner's/Alex's claim, unverified)
- Promo: Hormozi offers a free "10-stage roadmap from zero to $100 million+" that fewer than 1% of companies finish, broken down across eight business functions (constraint symptoms + steps to graduate each stage), tested across software, physical products, service, and brick-and-mortar businesses — at acquisition.com/roadmap. (paraphrase)
