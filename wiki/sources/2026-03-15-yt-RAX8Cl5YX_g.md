# Helping a Marketing Agency Fix Their Pricing

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client runs a white-label media/PR services agency: he sells (mainly to other PR firms who then resell/white-label his work) at ~$1,000/month, ~10 articles for that price, and has been stuck at $1.5M revenue for five years, wanting to reach $5M in three. All his lead generation is organic — agencies come directly to him — but he suffers **high churn** among the reselling agencies. Alex reframes the whole problem as **pricing and retention, not lead volume** ("ads obviously don't work" for this; "I think I'm the problem" — "yeah, probably"). He diagnoses two core issues. First, the client treats his reselling agencies as a **distribution layer** but doesn't run them like customers with a customer journey; Alex points to the affiliates chapter in *$100M Leads* and his two-pronged distribution playbook (a **launch** to get the affiliate an immediate win before time-to-value, then ongoing), citing Prestige Labs, Alan (software), and Skool as prior distribution builds. Second, and central: the agencies churn because **they don't use the articles** (not because the articles are bad), so the fix is a **lower entry tier with a usage component** — e.g. ~$199/month plus ~$100 per article — priced low enough that SMB-adjacent buyers "don't churn on their bad months," because the client's volatility is really his customers' volatility passed through. Alex's broader thesis: SMBs "suck at being businesses" and thus suck as customers; in any agency/marketing capacity serving them you must **either price so low they won't cancel, or price high enough they can't afford you** (selling to a fundamentally different customer) — the muddled middle is why the SMMA space can grind to $1-3M revenue (not profit) but never *stack*. He also flags a **pricing barbell**: the client is priced too high for the majority of his customers and too low for the ones he'll maximize profit on (the high-volume agency publishing 900 articles/month vs. the one publishing 10). The real constraint to solve: get ~80% of agencies to keep referring business every month — do that and revenue stacks toward $3M, $4.5M, $6M. Ends with the roadmap promo.
