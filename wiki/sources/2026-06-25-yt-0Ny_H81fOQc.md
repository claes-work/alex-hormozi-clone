# Scaling a $3M CFO Firm to $20M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a CPA/CFO advisory founder doing about $2.9M who wants to reach $20M and is tempted to market books and courses. Alex says the source of growth is not the unused courses, because the founder has stopped taking new monthly clients. The business is supply constrained, so the correct sequence is operating leverage, offshoring, data architecture, AI, and then more demand generation.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - CPA/CFO advisory founder. Her revenue, growth rate, books, courses, organic content, and service capacity are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A company that has stopped taking new customers cannot solve its growth problem by marketing more.
- Books and courses already recorded are not the active constraint and should be treated as future marketing assets.
- A sticky monthly service business is more valuable than a new course business that will have its own hard problems.
- The founder is supply constrained, so the business should redesign service delivery and increase operating leverage before creating more demand.
- Offshoring can create capacity before AI automation is ready.
- AI-first requires data-first; without complete business data and architecture, AI cannot do much useful work.
- Once supply capacity increases, the business can start selling again and use the saved books/courses for lead generation.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Well, that will not grow the business."

> "It just never stops being hard."

> "The course thing will suck, too. You just don't know it yet."

> "The first thing that you have to do in order to have an AI first company is you have to be a data first company."

> "You'll make more money if you sell people."
