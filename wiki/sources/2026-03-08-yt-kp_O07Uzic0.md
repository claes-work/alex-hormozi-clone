# The Most Expensive Debt You Pay

## Summary
Short solo monologue. Alex reframes "debt" beyond the financial: the biggest and most expensive debt anyone carries is **ignorance debt** — "the cost of what you do not know, but should." He stresses this is not a beginner's problem he has outgrown: he says he *still* pays down ignorance debt every day, because it is the debt in skills and knowledge he would need to move faster and further toward his goals but does not yet have. The only way to pay it off, in his framing, is to **fail** — to make bets and accept the price in looking bad, money lost, time lost, and being judged; if he refuses to fail, he never acquires the missing knowledge at all. He recasts expertise on the same terms: "an expert is just somebody who's made all the mistakes you can make in a very narrow field," so you have to learn how to lose before you can learn how to win. His "first rule of losing" is a mindset flip — "you didn't lose, you just learned. You paid down the most expensive debt." He closes the argument by asking why, knowing this debt exists, people still don't pay it: because they don't realize how expensive *not* paying it off really is (i.e. the compounding opportunity cost of staying ignorant). The back half of the clip is a promo for the free $100M scaling roadmap (acquisition.com/roadmap).
