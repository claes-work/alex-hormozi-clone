# Is Your GYM Making LESS Than $20,000 A Month? THIS is The REASON Why!

## Summary
A Gym Launch training video hosted by Kale Owen (identified on-screen as CEO of Gym Launch and Prestige Labs) walking gym owners through what Gym Launch calls a "level one" gym — under ~150 members, revenue under $20,000/month, little to no profit — and diagnosing the root cause of the ceiling. His central claim is that gyms stuck below $20K/month lack an acquisition system to consistently generate and convert leads into paying clients. The video is a playlist entry promoting a free "Seven Money Models" PDF download and previews later videos covering "level two" gyms. Alex Hormozi is named only in the third person as the founder of Gym Launch and a former gym owner; he does not speak in this clip.

## Key claims
- Gyms making less than $20,000/month are stuck primarily because they lack an "acquisition system" — a repeatable way to consistently find leads and convert them into high-paying, long-term clients.
- A "level one" gym typically has fewer than 150 members, charges roughly $125–$150/month for group training, and generates under $20,000/month in revenue.
- Such gyms typically make no profit up to at most ~$3,000/month in profit, because of the expenses of running a brick-and-mortar facility.
- The owner usually wears most of the hats, works ~50–80 hours/week, and (at the lower end, under $10K/month) worries about covering rent and payroll.
- The stated first priority is cash flow: getting new members in the door profitably on day one to generate cash.
- Pricing is a common secondary problem — the host claims 95% of gyms that come to Gym Launch have pricing that is "way too low," which they adjust.
- Capacity issues (from class setup) are another secondary problem, but acquisition is framed as the biggest one to solve first.
- Company scale cited: over 6 years Gym Launch has worked with 4,500+ gyms; the two companies (Gym Launch and Prestige Labs) have made a combined $100M+ in revenue.
- Positioning: "by gym owners, for gym owners" — the host and most of the team are former gym owners/operators.
