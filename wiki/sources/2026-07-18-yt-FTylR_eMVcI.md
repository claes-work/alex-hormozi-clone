# Helping a Content Agency Scale from $800K to $3M

## Summary

In this live coaching exchange, a caller named Ricky — who runs a content agency selling to real estate agents — asks Alex Hormozi how to grow from $800K to $3M/year. His original channel was cold outbound via Instagram (finding top agents on Zillow, then DMing them at scale), which periodically got his account restricted. To reduce that risk he shifted toward inbound: running paid ads against his good organic content (e.g. a "local celebrity" ad telling an agent they're a "top 1%" performer) to pull the same prospects in rather than chasing them out. The inbound channel is not converting yet, and he's unsure whether to double down on it or return to outbound.

Hormozi reframes the problem around a general marketing principle: the hardest and most valuable part of marketing is getting the right person in front of you; once you have a qualified person, the rest is just sales motion and calibrating friction. Using a football analogy, he says getting the right person to follow, DM, opt in, or book a call is "90 yards down the field" (90% of the job), and the remaining 10% is adjusting how much friction to add. If qualified people are showing up but not buying, the fix is more friction, not more traffic. He endorses continuing the inbound build because long-term content creation is necessary anyway and it also feeds the cold-outbound motion rather than cannibalizing it.

The bulk of Hormozi's advice addresses a staffing mistake Ricky admits to: he moved his best outbound salesperson onto the new inbound channel, and outbound is now declining while inbound isn't yet working. Hormozi affirms the logic — you want a proven salesperson who already knows the process on a new, unproven channel, because putting a new rep on a new process leaves you unable to diagnose what's broken ("I don't like two new [things]"). But the timing was wrong: "You never stop a flow." The correct sequence is to first train a replacement on outbound until that person's numbers match the top rep's, and only then move the top rep to the new channel. He confirms it's fine to keep the best rep on inbound while having him help train the new outbound hire.
