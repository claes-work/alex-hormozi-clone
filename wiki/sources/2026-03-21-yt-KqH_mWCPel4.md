# I Live the Same Day 7 Days a Week

## Summary
Hormozi describes his current daily routine, which he says is nearly identical seven days a week and built almost entirely around things he loves. He uses this to make a mindset point: aspiring people mistakenly model the lifestyle at the "top of the mountain" rather than the grind of the climb, because his present life of doing only what he loves is not how he got there.

## Key claims
- (self-reported, 2026-03-21) His days are more or less the same seven days a week, and he genuinely loves what he does.
- (self-reported, 2026-03-21) A typical day: wake up, go to his office and write his next book for ~6 hours, take ~2 hours of calls, lift in a gym he custom-designed with equipment he loves, eat with his wife, then sit in a recliner in his condo overlooking the city, read, and go to bed.
- (self-reported, 2026-03-21) The ~2 hours of calls is the only part he does not love; he treats it as an accessory to the rest of his day.
- (paraphrase, 2026-03-21) It is misleading for someone at his level to present their current enjoyable routine as the path, because that models the behavior at the top of the mountain rather than the climb — like flying private because rich people do, or playing basketball to become tall.
- (self-reported, 2026-03-21) He did not get where he is by doing what he loves; he did a large amount of work he hated for a very long time, without anyone cheering him on, and with many people actively trying to derail him and telling him it was a terrible idea.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "I live more or less the same day 7 days a week."

> "I wake up and then I go to my office here and I work for 6 hours writing my next book. And then I take 2 hours ish of calls typically. And then after that I lift in the gym that I custom designed with all the pieces that I love. And then I go and eat with my wife and then I go to my condo and I sit in my recliner that overlooks the entire city and I read and then I go to bed and I do that every day."

> "I love every aspect of the day with the exception of maybe the 2 hours of calls that I have to take, but I see those as an accessory thing for everything else that I have there."

> "modeling the top the behavior at the top of the mountain rather than the climb."

> "But I didn't get here doing everything that I love. I did a shitload of shit I hate and I did it for a very long period of time and I did not have anyone to root me on and in fact I had many people who were actively trying to destroy my path and tell me why it was a terrible idea."
