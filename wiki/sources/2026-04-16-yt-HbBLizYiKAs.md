# How to Build a Life You Actually Want

## Summary

Alex uses death and generational forgetting as a device for agency. The opinions that stop
someone from acting will disappear, along with everyone who held them. He says thinking
about death helps him act despite insecurity and judge from first principles instead of
societal expectations.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-16, Alex says in three generations everyone who knew a person will be dead,
  including the people whose opinions stopped them.
- He says he thinks about death constantly and calls it probably the central theme in his
  thinking.
- Self-reported: he says he may have more insecurity than others and has built mental
  devices to act despite it.
- He says the "do it for me" conclusion leaves only one voice to listen to, but requires
  first-principles thinking without relying on norms or role models.
- He says practicing agency and responsibility makes other people's autopilot decisions
  look increasingly insane.
- He says the freedom to fail repeatedly feels more real and fulfilling than living an
  okay life on autopilot.
- He repeats a perspective line he says to Leila: zoom out far enough and you cannot see
  the earth.

## Notable verbatim quotes
