# $15 Leads vs Medicare Part B: How to Fix the Funnel

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a chronic-wound medical consulting business doing about $5M revenue and $2M profit. The owner is buying low-cost Facebook leads but taking too many unqualified calls. Alex says the first task is to reduce confusion into unit math: lead cost, qualified rate, close rate, and average customer value. Then he recommends adding friction and filters to the funnel: target older patients, clarify copy, ask wound-size questions, score leads, and only show the scheduler to qualified prospects if needed.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - chronic-wound medical consulting owner. His revenue, profit, patient avatar, Medicare Part B constraint, ad funnel, and conversion data are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A seemingly special healthcare funnel can still be reduced to lead cost, qualified rate, conversion rate, and average value.
- The owner should determine whether unqualified leads are truly a problem or simply part of profitable math.
- Five treatments from 1,000 leads suggests the current funnel has a serious quality/filtering problem.
- If everyone sees the scheduler after downloading the PDF, the team will waste time on unqualified calls.
- The funnel needs filters when the team is sifting through too many unqualified leads.
- Targeting and messaging should focus on the group most likely to qualify.
- The opt-in/application should ask questions that identify wound size and qualification.
- Lead scoring can decide who gets immediate scheduler access.
- If the company is inefficient, adding friction is appropriate.
