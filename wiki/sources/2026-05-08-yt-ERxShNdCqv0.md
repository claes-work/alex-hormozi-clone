# Don't Conform, Find Your True Personal Brand

## Summary

Alex argues that personal brand is not created by copying what people expect, but by
removing conformity and asking what the person would choose if no one else existed.
He uses his post-Gym Launch clothing choices as an example of picking function and
comfort over role performance.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says everyone has a unique personal brand, but many people hide it by conforming
  to what they think others want.
- He says boring presentation can come from living as a reflection of others'
  expectations.
- He recommends starting from "what do I want?"
- He self-reports dressing a certain way at Gym Launch because he was leading a gym
  community and needed to look the part.
- He says after Gym Launch he asked what his 85-year-old self would wear and chose
  function, comfort, and versatility.
- He frames the broader question as what work, clothing, and relationships one would
  choose if everyone else disappeared.
- He says "stick-to-it" is a muscle built by making each diet, workout, or behavior
  attempt last longer than before.
- He says the bar is low for separating from the pack because many people are mediocre
  or financially behind.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What would 85-year-old me wear?"

> "The stick to it muscle itself is something that you work on."
