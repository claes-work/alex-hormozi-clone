# You Need to Hire Better Talent

## Summary
Live Q&A clip (audience of business owners; the exchange opens with a group of four who flew 14 hours from Sydney). The talent thread is short but sharp: an attendee marvels that "all my bloody questions have been answered by your team without you having been in the room." Alex reframes this as the lesson — the biggest belief-breaker is what you can **see and demonstrate**, not what he can say: meeting his team makes owners realize "I understand why they make a lot more money than my team does," and that realization *is* the point — if you had that caliber of team, you could scale. His headline claim: **"the best talent you have is in the future, not in the past"** — i.e., the people who will unlock your next stage of growth are people you haven't hired yet, so you should keep raising the bar rather than assuming your ceiling is your current roster. He notes it took him a long time to internalize this himself. The clip then pivots to a second, unrelated question — an ambitious owner who feels increasingly "numb" as he succeeds asks what Alex's "KPI for the soul" would be. Alex declines the mystical framing and instead offers his **observable-days framework**: on the days he looks back on and rates as good, three things tended to happen — he worked out with people he liked, ate with people he liked, and wrote something down; so his goal is simply to maximize the number of days in a row that contain those things. He defines things by the observable universe (rejecting undefinable terms like manifestation/energy/vibrations), defines creativity as "mistakes that happen while copying," and describes iteratively adding nuance to the framework over "100 days in a row of your best day." He closes with a Shervin (now Acquisition.com president) quote — 20s: the destination, 30s: the journey, 40s: the company — and the point that surrounding yourself with people you genuinely like takes the "shittiness" out of the days you hate your business. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
