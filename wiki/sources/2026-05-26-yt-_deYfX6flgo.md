# Are You Even Trying

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that most people do not take enough actions per unit of time to earn
the outcomes they want. He illustrates the volume gap with his first-gym flyer test: he
distributed 300 flyers, thought the method did not work, and then learned his mentor tested
5,000 at a time and distributed 5,000/day after finding a winner. He extends the same
volume mismatch to content and sales calls.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The main reason many people stay average is not talent or belief; it is too few actions
  per unit of time.
- People talk about doing more than they actually do.
- Alex's first-gym flyer story taught him that 300 flyers was not a real test when his
  mentor tested 5,000 per batch and then distributed 5,000/day.
- Many creators think 90 daily posts is a serious test, but Alex says his operation
  produces about 450 content pieces per week.
- Sales skill differences often reflect call volume; someone with 4,000 closed deals has
  far more reps than someone with a few hundred calls.
- Beginners delay by searching for an easy way; eventually they discover the hard way is
  the only way.
- Volume gaps are often 100x, 1,000x, or more, not merely 2x or 3x.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I think people don't do very much."

> "The hard way is the only way."
