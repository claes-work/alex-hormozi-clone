# Why You Should Charge 5x More, Not 50% More

## Summary

In this short solo teaching clip, Alex Hormozi argues that low-budget mass markets are hard to serve without lots of capital and automation. If a business cannot automate delivery to the masses, it should go the opposite direction: serve the best customer at the highest possible price. He says many owners misprice higher tiers by moving from $1,000 to $1,500, when the true next buyer tier may be $5K or $10K. Disproportionate profit comes from gigantic jumps that only a small percentage of buyers accept.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Serving low-budget masses profitably usually requires large capital, time, automation, and volume.
- Large consumer companies often needed outside capital to sustain low-price mass-market models until they worked.
- Service businesses usually do not have an automated way to serve the masses.
- Without automation, the better path is to serve the best customer at the highest possible price.
- Owners misprice upsells by increasing 50% when the buyer's next tier may be 5x or 10x.
- Disproportionate profit sits at high price points even when only a small percentage of customers buy.
