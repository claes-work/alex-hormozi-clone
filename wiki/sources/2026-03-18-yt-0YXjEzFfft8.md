# Get RICH in the A.I. Revolution (2026)

## Summary
Live workshop (Alex teaching a room + live chat; the two `>>` lines are an audience aside/laughter, not an interviewer — all teaching is Alex's). His practical playbook for using AI to build wealth as a business owner, structured in three parts: **AI hygiene**, **workflows/SOPs**, and **AI assets/data sources**. The through-line: AI is **amplification, not automation** — a workhorse that outsources the *typing*, never the *thinking*. "The idea is still the alpha"; AI cannot live your life or have your experience, so the core value/idea must come from you, and AI merely remixes your language patterns, structure, and format to help you produce more, faster, at the same quality.

**AI hygiene:** start a new chat for every new topic (don't build one endless thread); the more iterations/feedback loops inside one chat, the better the output ("the big C-word is context"); verify facts/figures to catch hallucinations; outsource the typing, not the thinking — you're still rewarded for judgment and taste; the idea is the alpha, not the collateral.

**Workflows/SOPs:** reframe a prompt as an SOP — "the prompt is the SOP," so most people's prompts are far too short (trying to hand an employee a task in one sentence). Build a **prompt repository** of standardized prompts for repeated work (do a 15-minute-block time study to find repeated tasks). Store centrally — one master doc with tabs (by role), or paste the prompt into recurring calendar blocks so it's there when the work is scheduled. "One-time work, forever payoff." Prompts are "SOPs 2.0"; a business is functionally run by a series of prompts.

**AI assets / data sources:** your past work is data that trains AI to sound like *you*. "AI sounds like the internet if you give it no context… AI sounds like us if we had no personality." Export everything you've already produced — all-time tweets (one-button X export), sent emails/newsletters, top sales-call transcripts, lead-nurture sequences, ad copy, website copy, FAQs, support transcripts, pitch decks, transcripts of content you *like* — into a **data sources** folder. Three folders total inside "AI assets": **business context**, **SOP/prompt repository**, **data sources** — the "three wheels" that make AI work.

**Leverage numbers (his own, self-reported):** Mosey Minute email went 60–90 min → ~4 hrs for a whole month of 16 emails (~4x); the newsletter drives ~20% of the advisory practice's revenue. Tweets: ~1.5 hrs/day → ~90 min/week (~7x); tweets = 20–30% of all content. YouTube: 2 hrs/video → 15 min (~8x); YouTube = ~40% of lead flow. Memos ~4x; VSLs went from a full day to quick. He stresses these are amplification, not automation, and that his output is good *because* he did the volume first ("my tweets are good because I already wrote 6,000 tweets before this").

The back half is a walkthrough of ~14 named prompts (given away free via email) built on a consistent **role → task → rules → output** structure: mother sales script from 10 winning call transcripts; newsletter voice-cloner; objection-handling playbook; writing-style analyzer; VSL generator; sales-call feedback analyzer; YouTube long/short script generators; lead-magnet builder; lead-magnet follow-up system; and an **Offer Architect prompt chain (11–14)** where the output of one prompt feeds the next: offer → sales page → sales-call script → VSL, letting you "build out entire marketing universes." Key prompting law: "the longer the prompt, the stronger the output; the shorter the prompt, the longer the output" — more context narrows the answer.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames AI as **amplification, not automation** — "you will get amplification, not automation… more leverage, not infinite leverage"; true automation (infinite, self-improving, never checked) is rare, whereas realistically a 60–90-min task becomes a 15–20-min task.
- As of March 2026, the core rule is "outsource the typing, not the thinking" — you cannot outsource thinking; you're still rewarded by the quality of your judgment and taste; "the idea is still the alpha."
- As of March 2026, AI hygiene: start a new chat per new topic; more iterations/feedback loops within one chat produce progressively better outputs ("the big C-word is context"); always verify facts and figures to catch hallucinations.
- As of March 2026, "the prompt is the SOP" — a prompt is a standard operating procedure for producing an output, so most people's prompts are far too short; everything worth doing still takes up-front thinking work.
- As of March 2026, build a **prompt repository** of standardized prompts for repeated work (found via a 15-minute-block time study), stored centrally in a master doc with tabs by role, or pasted into recurring calendar blocks — "one-time work, forever payoff"; prompts are "SOPs 2.0."
- As of March 2026, your past work is your competitive edge with AI: "AI sounds like the internet… AI sounds like us if we had no personality" — feed it your own exported work (tweets, emails, sales transcripts, ad/website copy, FAQs, decks, liked content) so it pattern-matches your voice instead of sounding generic.
- As of March 2026, the "three wheels" that make AI work well: (1) complete business context, (2) great prompts / clear instructions, (3) high-quality data sources to pattern-match against — the three folders inside "AI assets."
- As of March 2026 (self-reported leverage): Mosey Minute email ~4x (whole month of 16 emails written in one ~4-hr half-day); newsletter drives ~20% of the advisory practice's revenue; tweets ~7x (1.5 hrs/day → 90 min/week, tweets = 20–30% of content); YouTube ~8x (2 hrs → 15 min/video, YouTube = ~40% of lead flow); memos ~4x; VSLs from a full day to quick.
- As of March 2026, "you are rewarded for the work that you have done" — his outputs are strong because he built the data bank first (he cites having written 6,000+ tweets before using AI); a beginner who churns out AI content still loses because they have no experience/idea for it to remix.
- As of March 2026, AI is a "remixer," not original ("AI is not original yet") — it replicates format, structure, sentence-level style, and language patterns; the essence/core value still comes from the human; it will replace people "eventually… not for a while" because it can't have real-life experience.
- As of March 2026, the prompting law: "the longer the prompt, the stronger the output; the shorter the prompt, the longer the output" — more context narrows the answer to what you actually want.
- As of March 2026, good prompts follow a **role → task → rules → output** structure; when uploading multiple data sources, clearly delineate each one (e.g. `*** number 1 ***`, `*** number 2 ***`).
- As of March 2026, prompt **chaining**: the output of one prompt becomes the input of the next — his Offer Architect chain (offer → sales-page copy → sales-call script → VSL) lets you build entire marketing systems from one offer.
- As of March 2026, on which tools he uses: ACQ AI most for offer, pricing, sales-flow, and copy (because it pulls from small-business/0–100M context rather than blogs/Wikipedia); GPT and Gemini for images; GPT for personal/health questions; Grok for edgy or non-political-agenda questions.
