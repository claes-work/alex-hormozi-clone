# Should I Fire My Marketing Agency and Bring It All In House?

## Summary

Alex advises a profitable pest-control company not to shut off a functioning agency while
building an internal team. His general rule is to add acquisition flows, measure them in
parallel, and retain every flow that still produces an acceptable return.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports $8 million current revenue, a $12 million target, strong margins,
  and about $100,000 annual agency fees; none is independently verified.
- As of March 2026, Alex says he typically adds marketing flows rather than stopping
  existing ones, provided the product has sound gross margins.
- The company can build internal marketing while the agency continues, avoiding a risky
  interruption to the flow that supports the business.
- Even if the internal team outperforms, the agency remains rational if its incremental
  return still exceeds its cost.
- Efficiency gains do not justify risking the whole acquisition engine before the
  replacement is proven.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I will typically never stop flows. I'll just add flows."

> "I will never sacrifice the thing that puts food on the table."

> "I would suggest adding, not canceling."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: parallelize acquisition transitions and judge each incremental flow by return rather than winner-take-all replacement.
