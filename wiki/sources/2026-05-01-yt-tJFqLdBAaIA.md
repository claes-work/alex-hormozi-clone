# Give Away the Information, Sell the Personalization

## Summary

Alex advises a community owner with a free 30-day course not to charge merely for
unlocking the whole course at once. He recommends keeping a shorter free course and
upselling a paid community built around personalization: weekly calls, direct help,
one-on-small-group guidance, and faster unsticking. The core monetization frame is to
give away generic information and sell customized application.

## Key claims

- Alex does not find "pay to unlock the same course faster" compelling enough as the
  main upsell.
- He recommends shortening a free course to five or seven videos and putting the CTA at
  the end.
- A stronger paid community offer adds personalization through weekly calls, direct
  troubleshooting, and small-group help.
- The value tradeoff is time: free information supports trial and error, while paid
  personalization helps the buyer get unstuck faster.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Give away the information, sell the personalization."

> "If you want me to personalize this to you, then join my thing."

> "It just depends on how much you value your time."
