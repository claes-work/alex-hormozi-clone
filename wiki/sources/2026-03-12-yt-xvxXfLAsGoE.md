# Go Into the Fire

## Summary
Solo monologue on why so few people are willing to "go into the fire" — the hard thing that others avoid (entrepreneurship, content creation, launching a product, outreach, or a career pivot). Alex's core argument: the thing others tell you not to do is often the right path, because their discouragement "helps them justify the risk that they chose not to take." He anchors this in the word **exceptional** — to be exceptional you must "become the exception," which means doing what others don't agree with; if everyone agrees with you, "you can be sure that it's the wrong path." He recounts quitting his consulting job (Vanderbilt in three years, aced GMATs, Ivy grad-school track) to be a minimum-wage personal trainer, and later selling all his gyms "for basically nothing in 90 days" right when people had finally accepted him — illustrating that approval from your close circle is the wrong signal. His rule: **"Don't listen to the people who are closest to you. Listen to the people who are closest to your goals."** He distinguishes mentors/heroes from your true **reference group** — the voices you actually hear when making a big decision — and says if those voices aren't close to your goal, don't listen; sometimes the best advice is to do the opposite of what they did. He then illustrates the **delayed, lumpy payoff** of hard things with the Gym Launch outbound build: a book said it would take a year, he thought 12 weeks, it took 12 months; first sale at 90 days, an executive-team "intervention" calling it a shiny-object distraction, but he pushed through ("this is what hard feels like") until outbound became 50% of revenue. He generalizes with the Wall Street saying "it happens slower than you expect and then faster than you can imagine" — you lose 5K, 5K, 5K, then suddenly make 25K, 50K, "all at once." Closes with an expertise theory: **masters have more ways to win** — expertise equals the number of ways you can measure progress, so masters mark many small wins across a long project while beginners "only see the output" and feel nothing is happening. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
