# How to Build Unshakeable Fortitude in Your Business

## Summary

Alex Hormozi defines fortitude as the intensity of behavior change after a person's tolerance threshold has been surpassed. In plain terms: once someone has had enough and cracks, how far do they drop? High fortitude means a small behavior change even after cracking; low fortitude means a large destructive change such as quitting, blowing up relationships, or spiraling.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Fortitude is the intensity of behavior change after tolerance has been exceeded.
- In simpler language, it is how much a person changes how they act after they have had enough hard things.
- High fortitude means the person cracks only a little: they breathe, take a short break, and return.
- Low fortitude means the person reacts with a steep and destructive behavior change.
- Fortitude is distinct from resilience: fortitude is how far the behavior drops; resilience is how quickly it returns.
- Alex distinguishes tolerance, fortitude, resilience, and trauma as separate observable constructs.
