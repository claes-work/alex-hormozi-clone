# Is AI making people dumber? | Hormozi Hotline

## Summary
Hormozi Hotline clip. A caller (Kevin Gong, from Alex's School of Advisory group) cites a claimed MIT study finding that over 80% of ChatGPT users experience "cognitive decline" — the theory being that outsourcing thinking to AI atrophies the brain like an unused muscle — and asks how to maximize AI's benefits while avoiding "cognitive death." Alex agrees the long-term risk is real ("I 100% think that's a problem" for humanity) and frames his answer around a **doing-vs-thinking split**: he'd rather outsource his *doing* and keep the *thinking* to himself, because outsourcing thinking is "the most valuable thing" to give away. His practical rule is to reserve AI for **repeated / boring work** and to keep doing the **hard, thought-provoking, novel work** by hand — he points out he did *not* use AI at all to write his Money Models book, and won't outsource the core writing of the next one, because writing is his hardest work and the work he enjoys most, and doing it keeps his brain sharp. He rejects the idea of a neat three-step process: the only real answer is discipline — "you have to keep using your brain," like not being lazy at the gym. He shares that he already sees the atrophy in his two younger brothers (16 and 8), who outsource all their thinking, and jokes about a meme of "AI talking to AI." He also endorses phone/focus discipline, using an app that blocks his phone for hours while he works. A second question about what behaviors he wants more of in the School of Advisory group draws his community philosophy: if everyone shares tactical wins (rather than becoming an "askhole"), everyone gets more than they wanted. Ends with the standard 10-stage roadmap promo.
