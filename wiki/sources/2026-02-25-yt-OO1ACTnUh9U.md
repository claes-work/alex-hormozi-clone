## Summary

A short MoreMozi Q&A clip. An audience member (Jesus Hernandez, a community-business owner — **not** Alex) writes in that his paid ads "stopped working" after he raised his price and then lowered it again, and asks whether he should start over. Hormozi first pushes back on the diagnosis: without knowing how many people actually saw the offer, there's no evidence the price change caused the drop, and he argues the more likely culprit is ad-audience fatigue — the ad platform exhausts the warm pool who already know the advertiser, then pushes to colder audiences that convert worse. He then pivots to the structural fix he says he keeps seeing outperform: running **two communities**, a free one and a paid one, with a deliberate free-to-paid conversion system. He lays out opt-in and lead-work benchmarks, three (plus an optional fourth) conversion mechanisms inside the free community, and a simple viability formula based on covering customer-acquisition cost from the first payment of a recurring paid membership.

## Key claims

Attribution note: all claims below are **Alex Hormozi, paraphrased**, from 2026-02-25, unless marked. The questioner's own figures (that he quit his 9-to-5 thanks to Skool, that ads stopped after the price change) are **audience / self-reported, attribution uncertain** and are not persona data.

- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase)** Ads intermittently working and then not working is a very common pattern; it happens to lots of advertisers and is not by itself a sign something is broken.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase)** He is not confident the price change caused the ads to stop, because the questioner never stated the sample size (how many people actually saw the site from the ads). A price change is only implicated if you can isolate it — and here the price was moved back yet ads still didn't recover, which suggests price was **not** the deciding factor.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase) Ad-fatigue diagnosis:** Facebook/the ad platform tends to start by serving people who already know who you are. If your audience pool is tiny, it burns through those warm people quickly — you make those sales — and then it moves "one degree removed" to people who don't know you at all, who are much less likely to convert. Perceived "ads stopped working" is often this warm-pool exhaustion, not the offer or price.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase) The structural fix — two communities:** the people he sees consistently outperform run **two** communities: a **free** community and a **paid** community. It's easy to convert people into a free community, which then becomes the engine that feeds the paid one.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase) Free opt-in benchmark:** aim for **20%+ of clicks to opt into the free community (1 in 5)** as a good normal baseline. Therefore **cost per new free member ≈ cost-per-click × 5**.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase) Lead-work benchmark:** if you **manually work the leads** (which he recommends, especially for newer operators), you can get about **10% of free members to buy the next (paid) thing**.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase) He likes three distinct free-to-paid conversion mechanisms** running inside the free community:
  1. **Onboarding call** — offered to new free members; functionally a sales call where you lead them through the material and show them what's going on.
  2. **Five-video "module" sequence** inside the free community, structured like a **VSL / webinar**: (1) an **origin story** that frames the opportunity / big idea behind what you do; (2)–(4) **three stories / case studies** of different representative people who each overcame a different obstacle — he buckets these as **"myself, other people, and circumstances"**; and (5) a **call to action** explaining what the paid community (or next step) is. Members who go through this **self-book** calls.
  3. **One live call per week into the free community** where you add value and answer questions, then **soft-toss the CTA** — because you've just solved people's problems, you can mention that more advanced help exists in the paid community, and a handful convert each week.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase) Optional fourth mechanism — DMs:** you can DM people who join the free Skool to open an **authentic dialogue**. He warns against immediately soliciting — "take them out to dinner before you try to take them home" — i.e. build rapport first.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase) Viability formula:** take your **cost per free member and multiply by 10** (the inverse of the ~10% free-to-paid rate). If your paid community price still lets you make money at that number, the model is viable.
- **(2026-02-25, paraphrase) Recurring-revenue economics:** because the paid community is **recurring**, if the **first payment covers the entire acquisition cost ("the first nut")**, then **every payment after that is profit**. This is his core test for whether the paid membership is worth running against paid traffic.
