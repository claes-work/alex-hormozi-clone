# How to Maximize Your Sales Team's Conversion Rate

## Summary

Alex explains sales-team utilization as a constraint. Fully booked reps create longer and
less convenient booking windows, reducing show rates, follow-up, outbound, and total lead
conversion even if absolute sales rise. Under-booked reps lose momentum and develop
"commission breath." His preferred range is roughly 60-85% utilization, with 70-75% as the
sweet spot.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-17, Alex says a fully booked sales team can make more absolute sales while
  lowering conversion rates and raising CAC.
- He says when calendars are booked out, prospects must book later or at less convenient
  times, reducing show rates.
- He says fully utilized reps stop doing outbound and stop following up on near-close
  pipeline opportunities.
- He says very low utilization can hurt morale and momentum because each call matters too
  much.
- He identifies roughly 70-75% utilization as a sweet spot, with a range of about 60-85%.
- Self-reported: he says he has yet to make less money by hiring more salespeople.
- He says adding salespeople often beats changing commissions because it lets reps squeeze
  opportunities better.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you have your sales team completely booked out, fully utilized... your conversion rates will go down."

> "I found 70% is kind of the sweet spot."

> "I have yet to make less money by hiring more sales people."
