# Fixing a Sales Agency With a 40% Cancellation Rate

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a sales agency selling electricity plans and backup power at about $300K/month revenue. The owner says fulfillment delays from the provider create a 120-day wait and about 40% cancellation. Alex says that level of cancellation is a sales problem: the reps likely set the wrong expectation. The fix is to sell the lower rate with the true wait time up front.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A 40% cancellation rate after a known fulfillment delay indicates a sales expectation-setting issue.
- If customers already have electricity, the offer should not be sold as urgent immediate installation.
- Salespeople should state the real timeline before the commitment, not keep pushing the date afterward.
- A delayed-savings offer can work if the customer understands they are signing now to be first in line for the lower rate.
- Managing expectations can be the simplest fix when fulfillment time is structurally delayed.
