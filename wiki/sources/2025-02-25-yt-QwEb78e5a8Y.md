# Answering Your Top Business Questions for 1 Hour

## Summary

Alex answers business-owner questions at Acquisition.com headquarters. The session covers customer recovery after service mistakes, onboarding for low-consumption offers, why founders should exhaust the working path before chasing a new avatar, leadership training through behavior-level skill gaps, constraint diagnosis, and supply-constrained scaling. He repeatedly pushes owners to collect data before ideating, do more of what already works, and define vague people problems as observable behaviors.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2025-02-25 - Service recovery**: If a company wrongs a customer, a refund may leave the customer net negative; the company may need to make it more than right to actually repair trust.
- **2025-02-25 - Consumption before renewal**: Low renewal and high churn are often preceded by low consumption; onboarding should create repeated booked commitments and may require one-on-one ramping before group delivery.
- **2025-02-25 - Data before ideation**: Alex says many entrepreneurs have a problem but do not collect the preceding data, so they waste time imagining solutions without knowing what is true.
- **2025-02-25 - Exhaust the working path**: For a profitable B2C company considering B2B, Alex says the lowest risk-adjusted move is to do more of what already works and ask what blocks scaling that path.
- **2025-02-25 - New avatar risk**: He says moving to a new avatar is basically starting a new business because offer, acquisition, sales process, and delivery all change.
- **2025-02-25 - Behavior-level leadership**: He says leaders should not tell employees to stop being "lazy"; they should define the observed behaviors, train the smallest missing skill, and check the context.
- **2025-02-25 - Recruiting as the business**: For supply-constrained services, Alex reframes the company as being in the business of recruiting the scarce producer, not merely selling the end service.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You can't just make it right. You have to make it more than right."

> "When you don't know what to do, get data first."

> "The lowest risk adjusted return move in any business is to do more of what's already working."
