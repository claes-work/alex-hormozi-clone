# How to Get Known Fast as a Tax Strategist

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a CPA/tax-strategy firm doing about $3.2M revenue and $1M bottom line. Alex recommends becoming known for a small, repeatable set of tax strategies rather than trying to talk about everything. He says the founder should present the same core webinar to many groups, explicitly ask attendees to invite him into other groups, and pair proven viral hooks with audience-specific tax content. He also says the same narrow strategy package should improve delivery because repetition creates templates, efficiency, and margin.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - CPA/tax-strategy firm owner. His revenue, client mix, tax strategies, Facebook groups, advisor referrals, content plateau, and team size are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Tax is a major drag for business owners, so a clear tax-savings offer can be compelling.
- The firm should get known for a small package of about three strategies.
- A simple repeatable package helps people reference and remember the brand.
- The owner should present the same core presentation as many times as possible across groups.
- At the beginning, middle, and end of each webinar, he should ask attendees to invite him to relevant groups.
- Members of one group are often members of other groups, making each webinar a source of more webinars.
- Volume can negate luck in this kind of group-presentation channel.
- Content does not need constant novelty; a durable framework can be applied to many avatars and situations.
- Viral hooks should be paired with tax-specific content for the target audience.
- Narrowing fulfillment to the same few strategies should create templateization, economies of scale, and better incremental margin.
