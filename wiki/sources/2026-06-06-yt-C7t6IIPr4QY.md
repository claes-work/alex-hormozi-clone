# The Best Business to Start With No Money

## Summary

In this Q&A segment, Alex Hormozi and another coach answer what business they would start today with little or no capital. The other coach favors performance-based recruiting. Alex agrees with the performance-based principle and adds that a no-capital founder should sell something with many zeros behind it, such as headhunting or customer acquisition for local businesses. He describes the Gym Launch-style model of negotiating cheap fulfillment capacity, selling the front-end offer for much more, and using early cash to fund ads.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- With no capital, the founder has skills, so the best model is often performance-based.
- The target should be a small percentage of a large number: larger deals, executive headhunting, real estate, or high-value local customer acquisition.
- Headhunting executives is economically different from recruiting low-ticket labor because the fee is a percentage of a much larger compensation package.
- A customer-acquisition operator can negotiate delivery capacity from a local business, sell the front-end offer at a much higher price, and leave the back-end LTV to the operator.
- A high-paying customer may be more valuable to the delivery business than a cheap customer because they can spend more after the first sale.
- If broke, Alex says he would first make a small amount manually, then use it to fund ads so the business owner does not control the ad-spend leverage.
