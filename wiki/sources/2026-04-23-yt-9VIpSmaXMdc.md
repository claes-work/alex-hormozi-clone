# Finite vs Infinite Games

## Summary

Alex discusses happiness, ambition, business as an infinite game, and death as a
perspective tool. He says conditions often do not permanently move subjective
well-being, that he expects goals to keep moving after a billion, and that he keeps
playing business because he likes the game rather than because there is a final win
condition.

## Key claims

- Alex says many people return to a subjective well-being baseline even after major
  life-condition changes.
- He says enough is less about an endpoint and more about becoming the person capable
  of larger aims.
- He says he expects to pursue $10B after $1B because he loves playing the business
  game.
- He distinguishes finite games, with agreed rules and a win condition, from infinite
  games, where the point is to keep playing.
- He says health, marriage, and business are infinite games that people often mistake
  for finite games.
- He says thinking about death gives him freedom to take bigger shots because few people
  will remember anyone for long.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If the goalpost is to play, then you win by playing."

> "You don't win health."

> "I think about death all the time."
