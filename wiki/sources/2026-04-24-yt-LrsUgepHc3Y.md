# Helping an E-Com Brand Scale Past $3M

## Summary

Alex coaches a two-way-radio e-commerce owner doing about $3.5M per year, mostly from
B2C hobbyists. He advises pushing the existing B2C motion from roughly $3M to $10M
before trying to become a government or enterprise B2B seller, because that shift is a
different sales process. The near-term levers are better creative, conversion-rate work,
upsells/AOV, and using the current customer list to find B2B contacts.

## Key claims

- Alex says the owner's current B2C machine is not exhausted yet and should likely be
  scaled before a B2B pivot.
- He says government/enterprise sales are meaningfully different from direct consumer
  e-commerce and should not be treated as the same acquisition motion.
- He recommends producing high-volume UGC-style creative and improving the sales page,
  checkout, and upsell path before changing the business model.
- He suggests using the hobbyist customer base to ask for introductions to police,
  firefighter, government, or department buyers.
- He says free radios can be a rational incentive if the customer-acquisition cost for a
  department account is high enough to justify it.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I would absolutely focus B2C to 10 million first."

> "It's a totally different process."

> "Do 50 pieces of UGC a month."
