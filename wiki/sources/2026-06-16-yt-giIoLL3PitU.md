# "We Did $3M But Only Keep 7%. What Do I Fix First?"

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a baby-health-products company in Australia/New Zealand that does wholesale, Amazon, and D2C sales but has margin compressed to about 7%. Alex separates wholesale from D2C to find the real margin problem, identifies wholesale pricing and distributor terms as the likely issue, and shows that a 10% price increase could more than double profit even if some volume is lost. He also tells the founder to be bold and stick with either a distributor-control decision or a direct-control decision long enough to make it work.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - baby-health-products founder. Her revenue, margin, geography, channel mix, distributor issues, and pricing constraints are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The first step is separating wholesale from D2C instead of treating advertising spend as one blended margin problem.
- If wholesale is the majority of revenue, the margin problem must be diagnosed inside wholesale economics.
- The likely answer is a combination of raising prices and lowering costs.
- A small price increase can more than double profit when baseline profit margin is low.
- If distributors undercut the brand's retail price and destroy price integrity, raising wholesale prices may force the issue.
- Losing low-margin or price-destructive stockists can be acceptable if it restores margin and control.
- Even a severe volume-loss scenario may still produce more profit if price increases more than double profitability.
- The founder should be bold and commit to the chosen channel-control strategy rather than staying in limbo.

> "Just be bold either way."
