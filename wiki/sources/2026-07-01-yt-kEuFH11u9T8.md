# You're Making $1.2M and Still Clocking In at Your Day Job

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Cory Cooper, a firefighter and design-build ADU business owner who wonders when to leave his day job. Alex first reduces the question to math, then uncovers the real issue: Cory is comfortable, believes the business could replace the salary, but is afraid to cut the parachute. Alex frames work as central to meaning, says a man must have a quest, and pushes the founder to name the real trade rather than rationalize around security.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Cory Cooper** - the coachee. His firefighter role, father, ADU business, margins, salary, and family context are background only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The financial version of the question is simple: compare the day-job salary with the business income and the incremental profit required to replace it.
- If leaving the job would make the founder less productive because the job currently provides structured "study hall," quitting can be a double negative.
- If the founder can clearly generate enough incremental profit from the extra time, the real issue may not be math but fear and comfort.
- Alex says he believes "a man must have a quest" and sees work as inextricable from life.
- The founder should identify whether he genuinely does not want to quit or wants to quit but is afraid to do it.
- Once the rationalizations are stripped away, the decision becomes whether he is willing to cut the parachute and focus.
