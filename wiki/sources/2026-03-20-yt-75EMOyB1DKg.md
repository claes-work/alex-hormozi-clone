# The Offer Is the Most Important Part of a Business

## Summary
Alex Hormozi argues that the offer remains the single highest-leverage element of any business, using a recent book-donation funnel as a case study. He describes a "pay only shipping for three books" offer on which he loses money, yet reports it produced the best marketing metrics of his career, and lays out his rules for building compelling, simple offers. He closes with a promotion for the free acquisition.com scaling roadmap.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex maintains that "the offer is still king" and matters more than any other component of a business — the same as prior years.
- As of March 2026, Alex says a book-donation funnel offering three books for only the cost of shipping loses him money even with prepaid books, yet delivered the best metrics he has ever seen in his marketing career, which he attributes to the strength of the offer itself.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that each bonus or component in an offer should be worth more than the entire value of the core thing being sold, because most buyers are "single-issue buyers" who buy on one decisive reason, not on the aggregate value of many items.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises deleting any offer component complex enough to require explanation, because the "real estate" needed to explain it is the most expensive part.
- As of March 2026, Alex uses a "textable offer" razor: if the essence of an offer cannot fit in a short text message that prompts a "yeah, I'm in," it must be made better or shortened.
- As of March 2026, Alex predicts his book *$100M Offers* will outsell his other books long-term because nailing the offer makes almost everything else irrelevant, while noting *Money Models* is currently selling well largely because it is new.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues buyers are intelligent and can sense a genuinely good deal (e.g. knowing three shipped hardbacks cost far more than $15), so copy and persuasion are only "dressing" on top of the underlying offer.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The offer is still king, just like it was last year, just like it was the year before that. The offer still matters more than everything."

> "Right now I actually lose money, like literally lose money on that even with the books being prepaid. And still, what's crazy though is that the metrics that I've seen from that offer and that funnel are better than any that I've ever seen in my entire career in marketing."

> "It's working because it's the offer, stupid. Like, how do you make an offer so good people feel stupid saying no?"

> "Each bonus should be worth more than the entire value of the thing you sell."

> "I believe that most buyers are single-issue buyers, like single-issue voters. They keep listening until they have that one light, that one key, that one unlock, that one reason why that says, 'That was worth it.'"

> "If the bonus itself is complex enough that you have to explain it, delete it. Because the most expensive part about you explaining different components of the offer is the real estate it takes up to actually explain it."

> "The offer should be textable. Can I text to someone and then them say, 'Yeah, I'm in'?"

> "If you nail the offer, in a lot of ways nothing else matters. It's just getting it right is so hard, but there's nothing that you can do that can make your business have a bigger step change in your business's revenue and profit than really making the offer better."

> "I think Ogilvy said this, he said, 'The prospect is not an idiot, she's your wife.' We talk to these amorphous masses, but they're real people who are intelligent, who can make decisions, and they know when something smells off, and they know when something is a good deal."

> "At the end of the day, you should be able to make the pitch by just stating the offer and shutting the fuck up and pointing to how to pay, and people should buy. The rest of it is just dressing."

> "Sometimes we need to be reminded more than we need to be taught, and this year was a wonderful reminder for me of like, it's the offer, stupid. Make it better."
