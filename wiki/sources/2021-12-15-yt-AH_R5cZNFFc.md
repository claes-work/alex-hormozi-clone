# *REVEALED* Compensation plans that ACTUALLY WORK...

## Summary

Alex shares a variable-compensation method he learned from a friend and planned to implement in his holding company. Instead of only company-set MBOs, employees set a monthly public goal tied to the department or company objective; the method uses autonomy, social pressure, competition, and variable reward to drive performance.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2021-12-15)

- Alex says entrepreneurs need better people and more output from the people they already have.
- He describes Acquisition.com's existing MBO split as partly company performance and partly individual controllable objectives.
- He says the new method lets the employee set the monthly goal, as long as it aligns with the company goal and is quantifiable.
- He says public goal-setting creates social pressure that self-corrects goals that are too small.
- He says the system provides intermittent reinforcement, autonomy, social pressure, and competition.
- He says this is useful for roles not directly tied to sales or obvious bottom-line metrics.
- He expects the method to reduce employee churn because people feel progress and challenge.
