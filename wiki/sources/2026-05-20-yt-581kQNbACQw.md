# The Real Math Behind a $27/Month Membership

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a seven-figure crafting membership founder whose $27 per month offer
creates a cash-flow lag. He recommends selling the annual offer as the only offer during
the event, adding annual-exclusive bonuses, then using a post-event mop-up campaign to
sell monthly access without the main bonuses. He also suggests a future physical-product
premium to make the purchase tangible.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A $27 per month membership with $60 collected in the first 30 days and a $90 CAC can be
  profitable by LTV but painful on cash flow.
- Alex calculates $27 divided by 7% monthly churn as about $385 in true LTV.
- Cohort tracking is needed to know the LTV of members acquired through a specific ad
  funnel.
- During a 3- to 5-day selling event, the founder should sell the expensive thing.
- For a consumer audience, Alex places the impulse-purchase window around $300 to $600.
- The annual membership should be the only offer available during the event.
- One or two large annual-exclusive bonuses should make the annual offer more attractive.
- After the event cart closes, run a mop-up campaign to the $27 per month purchase page
  without the major bonuses.
- A second cart close can sell the monthly option with fewer bonuses.
- Physical products can increase consumer willingness to buy because they make the offer
  tangible and give the buyer an excuse to justify the purchase.
- Alex says a crafting offer might support a $1,000 price point if it includes the right
  physical kit.
- It is acceptable to make the undesired monthly option less convenient while still being
  truthful.

## Notable verbatim quotes
