# The Cheapest Way to Get Local Customers

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an outdoor-lighting company with seasonal Christmas-light revenue and off-season lead-flow issues for permanent lighting. Alex first frames the strategic choice as staying seasonal and selling all year for a short delivery window versus adding related home services. For the immediate permanent-lighting problem, he says the issue is likely creative and offer quality, not the landing page, and recommends a stronger lead magnet that gives a small lighting install or partial step rather than a free sales pitch.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - outdoor and holiday lighting business owner. His revenue, seasonality, prior lawn-care work, permanent-lighting offer, and ad context are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The business has an existential choice between a seasonal lighting-only model and a broader home-services model.
- Alex prefers selling the seasonal business all year and delivering in a short window because it focuses the company on marketing and sales most of the year.
- For permanent lighting, the ad creative is likely the issue because the product is visual and can be shown with before/after imagery.
- Local markets are strongly offer-driven.
- A free demo is weaker because it can feel like a free sales pitch.
- Alex recommends starting with a "banger" offer and then adding qualification friction.
- A lead magnet can be one part of a multi-step process, such as lighting a driveway or a small house section.
- The company can qualify by home value using address data and prioritize high-value homes.
- The goal of the front end is simply to get prospects to say they want lighting; the sales process can handle the rest.
