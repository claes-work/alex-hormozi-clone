# Helping a $1M Gym Owner Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches a large-group fitness studio owner doing more than $1M who wants a path to
$50M. He says that goal is unlikely through the current large-group studio model. The
lowest-risk adjacent path is converting the existing business toward small-group training,
which he believes has better returns on capital, is operationally easier, and borrows from
the owner's existing skill base.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-18, Alex says reaching $50M from a large-group fitness studio is unlikely.
- He says small-group fitness is, in his opinion, the best current fitness model because
  it can earn exceptional returns on capital and be operationally easier.
- He recommends transforming the current model before starting something totally new,
  because the adjacent move uses the founder's existing skills and facility.
- He says a six-month turbulent transition can be worth it if it sets up the next five to
  ten years.
- Self-reported: he says this was the first year of his life where he did not have FOMO,
  because he finally realized how hard doing anything well is.
- He says entrepreneurial life has a limited number of big "seasons" or swings, so choosing
  the game matters.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you want to make 50 million dollars, the likelihood that you get it there from a large group studio is very low."

> "I do believe that the best fitness model right now is a small group."

> "I finally realized how hard it is to do anything."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: large-group to small-group fitness adjacent vehicle; FOMO reduction
through recognizing hard-everywhere reality; limited entrepreneurial seasons.
