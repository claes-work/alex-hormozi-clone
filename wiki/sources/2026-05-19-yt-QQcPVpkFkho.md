# Outwork Your Self-Doubt

## Summary

Alex Hormozi defines "good enough" as knowing he could not have tried harder. He uses the
*Money Models* launch as proof: hundreds of thousands of registrants, 19 drafts, 4
rewrites, 2 years of morning writing, and daily presentation rehearsals. His self-esteem
standard is not outside praise; it is whether he can honestly say there was nothing else
he could have done.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex's biggest fear is reaching the end of life and thinking he was not good enough.
- He defines "good enough" as "I could have tried harder."
- He says confidence comes from a stack of undeniable proof, not affirmations.
- He says about 500,000 people were registered for the book launch.
- He wrote 19 drafts of the book and did 4 full rewrites.
- He worked from about 6 a.m. to noon every day for 2 years on the book.
- He says the launch presentation had about 900 slides for a 60-minute talk.
- Every day before the launch, he mentally rehearsed the presentation, then rehearsed
  aloud, recorded it, reviewed the recording with the slides, and fixed weak spots.
- He wants to step off stage knowing he did everything he could, even if tech or checkout
  fails.
- He calls himself the judge, jury, and executioner of his own self-esteem.
- High standards mean he must choose fewer things and do them well.

## Notable verbatim quotes
