# Helping a Software Company Scale

## Summary

Alex diagnoses a 100-day app-development service as a hidden-cost business: clients do
not understand how much work will be required of them, so they reach launch exhausted.
He recommends either making that burden explicit before purchase, doing more pre-work
for the client, or offering a slower 200-day path that preserves enthusiasm.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says hidden customer labor can be a major cost even when a
  service delivers on time and within budget.
- A company can address the hidden cost through higher expectations before purchase or
  more provider-side pre-work that makes delivery feel easier.
- Compressing all required client decisions into 100 days can make founders infer that
  operating the software will remain equally exhausting.
- Alex suggests contrasting a miserable 100-day option with a more manageable 200-day
  option and allowing the buyer to choose.
- The worst outcome is reaching launch with a client too fatigued to engage with the
  product that was built.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have a massive hidden cost business."

> "Higher expectations up front, or more pre-work on our side."

> "The worst thing that can happen is that we get to the finish line and you don't even want to see this thing again."
