# I Increased My Wealth Using INVERSE Thinking (how to get paid more)

## Summary

Hormozi applies Charlie Munger's inversion — solve "how would I get paid as little as
possible?" and then flip it — to the question of getting paid more, whether as an employee
or a business owner. He argues pay is set by three (really four) variables: value created,
negotiation/ask, supply-demand (replaceability), and who you serve.

## Key claims

- Inversion: to get paid little you'd (1) provide little value, (2) ask for little of it,
  (3) be easily replaceable. Flip all three to get paid more. (paraphrase)
- The three pay variables are value creation, ability to negotiate/ask, and supply-demand
  dynamics (replaceability); a fourth is who you serve — the same service is worth more to
  a wealthier customer with more at stake. (paraphrase)
- You should not set your own prices, because "what comes easy to you is typically not easy
  for other people" — let the market set the price. (paraphrase)
- Employees and business owners are structurally the same; the employee's "prices" are
  their wages. The path to outsized pay is variable compensation tied to the value/revenue
  you can be directly credited with. (paraphrase)
- Escape competition by becoming a "category of one" — niche down, then add features,
  bonuses, guarantees so no one else can be directly compared to you. (paraphrase)
- The size of your opportunity vehicle caps your pay: a dry-cleaner employee can't make a
  million a year because the business doesn't; pick a vision big enough that others' visions
  fit inside it. (paraphrase)
