# How I Built A 20+ Sales Team [The Process]

## Summary

In this early channel talk (delivered to a live audience alongside a co-presenter he calls "Marcus"), Alex Hormozi lays out two connected systems for building consistent sales. The first is the **CLOSER framework** — his script structure for running a single sales conversation: Clarify why they're there, Label the problem, Overview past pain, Sell the vacation, Explain away concerns, Reinforce the decision. The second is a set of **five C's** for managing a sales team so results stay consistent month over month: Closer (the framework/sequence), Call recordings, Comms cadence, Cut (the bottom performers), and Competition. He stresses that every element of a sales script must be phrased as questions — never statements — so it stays scalable and requires no intelligence to run. He backs the advice with self-reported experience running B2B and outsourced sales teams, including flying salespeople to gyms to fill them in 30 days.

## Key claims

- The CLOSER framework governs a single sales conversation and stands for: **C**larify why they're there, **L**abel them with a problem, **O**verview past pain, **S**ell the vacation, **E**xplain away concerns, **R**einforce the decision.
- Everything in a sales script must be a **question, not a statement** — reps read statements verbatim and can't think; questions let them pause and ask the next question, requiring "no intelligence," which is what makes the script scalable.
- **Clarify**: ask why they got on the call, their goal, why it's important, and what it would look like 12 months out if the ideal outcome were achieved.
- **Label**: reflect back what they've done and the missing link, get a "yes" — framed as "you now have cancer, I can cure it."
- **Overview / pain cycle**: ask what they've tried and why it didn't work; the rep always has the advantage because the prospect is on the phone with them (proof the past didn't work). Run the pain cycle until they have nothing left, then recap the pain.
- **Sell the vacation**: tell three short (~30-second) illustrative stories of what the prospect is missing. Example for weight loss: fitness, nutrition, accountability. Example for leads: exclusive, timely, geographic.
- **Explain away concerns**: there are only three objections — **price, stall, and decision-maker**. Price = a value discrepancy (they don't understand the value). Decision-maker = rely on the prospect's own implied past agreements with their partner. Objections must be drilled with flash cards.
- Teach the prospect a decision-making process (do you like me / the product / believe we can help / have access to the money — yes/no), then make them confront the decision and ask for their main concern.
- **Reinforce the decision** after the close because people back out — use a 30-second founder video, personalized handwritten card, or t-shirt to "make their feet nice and hot."
- The five C's for managing a sales team: **Closer** (framework/sequence), **Call recordings**, **Comms**, **Cut**, **Competition**.
- **Call recordings**: record every call — it's a compliance requirement and makes reps accountable to the script. He recommends the tool **Gong** for teams selling via Zoom.
- **Comms cadence**: daily huddles (quick — share testimonials so reps remember why they sell), weekly one-on-ones, monthly. In one-on-ones, have reps tag their best/worst/average call and show where they clarified the problem, outlined pain, overcame objections, and asked for the sale.
- "You can never make a sale if you never ask for it."
- **Cut**: regularly cut the bottom 10% of the team — he claims this produces a ~30% jump in productivity every time.
- **Competition**: publish a leaderboard reps see and get notified on daily (a bell in person, or a thread with bell GIFs remotely); run ~six-week competitions with three-person teams (so they share best practices and root for each other rather than being cutthroat).
- Prize budget guidance (self-reported): ~25% of one month's check per person — e.g. if reps make ~$70[k], spend ~$1,500/guy for a two-to-three-day trip; "not expensive, five grand, but it is worth every dollar."
- Self-reported track record: ran a lot of sales teams and a B2B outsourced sales company; flew people out to gyms to sell and filled them in 30 days; refers to himself closing ~4,000 people one-on-one in weight loss.
