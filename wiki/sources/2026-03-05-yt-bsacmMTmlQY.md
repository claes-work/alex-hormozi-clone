# How I Give Bonuses to Sales Teams

## Summary
Short interview clip (an off-camera host asks a few questions; the substance is all Alex). Alex explains the **pooled team bonus** he now uses for sales teams: a shared "pot" that pays out to *everybody* only if the whole team collectively clears a threshold, rather than a purely individual, cutthroat commission. The design intent is behavioral — it makes teammates pull the weakest performer *up* ("Shoot, Johnny's low… let's drill, let's drill, then we can all get paid") instead of competing against each other. His deeper principle: he wants the team's **enemy to be external, not internal** — "in the trenches side by side, not against each other" — so the competitive energy is aimed at out-selling rival companies ("let's put those guys out of business… let's outsell them") who he frames as ripping off customers, not at beating colleagues. He explicitly flags this as an **evolution in his own thinking**: his older material was "cutthroat" ("everybody has to kill each other"), but as his teams grew bigger and more profitable he shifted, because the collaborative pot "creates a much better culture." Pressed on whether he's personally competitive, he separates the *money* motive from the *winning* motive — he loves winning independent of the money ("just win"). Ends with the standard acquisition.com/roadmap promo (the $100M scaling roadmap quiz/free gift).
