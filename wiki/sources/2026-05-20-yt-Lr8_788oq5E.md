# They Can't See You Because You're Already Successful

## Summary

Alex Hormozi tells the origin story behind "success is the only revenge." A high-school
teacher, Mr. Given, taught him to lift weights and challenged his teenage revenge fantasy
by showing how petty it would look in real life. Alex says the only revenge is winning so
big that former critics shrink into irrelevance.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Criticism hurts most when it contains truth or something the person does not want to
  examine.
- Alex says "twisting the knife" means asking what if the critic is right.
- Alex was about 14 or 15 when a teacher or administrator noticed he might work out and
  offered to show him how.
- The teacher, Mr. Given, worked out with Alex every day in high school and taught him to
  lift weights.
- Alex described revenge fantasies about proving people wrong at a reunion.
- Mr. Given challenged the fantasy by playing out how it would look if Alex admitted he
  had done everything to prove one person wrong.
- Alex says success is not the best revenge; it is the only revenge because every other
  revenge is petty.
- The goal is to win so much that critics become irrelevant and are not thought about.
- Copycats copy the wrong visible things because they do not understand why something
  worked.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What if they're right?"

> "Success is the only revenge."

> "Win so big that all of them constantly compare themselves to you and then you'll forget they exist."
