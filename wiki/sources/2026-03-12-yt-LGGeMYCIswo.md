# Work = Volume × Leverage

## Summary
Solo monologue in which Alex defines work as **volume × leverage** — how many times you do something multiplied by how much you get per rep. Early on you must do far more volume; the volume builds skill, skill raises leverage, and leverage compounds your output over time. He frames this as **"100x, not 2x"**: a mindset he says applies across his whole life. He illustrates with two stories — paying a large personal brand for growth advice and being told bluntly he wasn't doing enough volume (the advisor was doing 10x more), after which he ramped up, got better, and now produces far more output from the same effort; and a billionaire friend getting into content who "was making seven pieces a week" while Alex made 450, and who instantly understood because he'd already learned the 100x lesson in another domain ("it has been 100% domain specific"). Alex then zooms out to his current work intensity: the first 100 days of the year he took no day off, and he argues the difference between working hard when young and now is that he gets far more done and at higher quality because **"you learn how to work — you get better at working the more you do it."** He recounts a French legal internship at Arkema where a partner dismissively told him he "didn't even know what work is," a lesson he only understood later. The core skill he identifies is **focus as endurance** — the ability to "whip yourself back in" past the urge to quit; the dip in output lasts only ~5–15 minutes before you catch the next draft. He parallels this with dieting cravings (set a 20-minute timer and the craving usually passes), warning that people "take off working for the day" the way they blow a diet. His personal creed: **"I will do what is required. How I feel about it is relatively irrelevant."** He rejects "do what you love and you'll never work a day" as untrue — most work isn't unpleasant, it's just work. Closes with a philosophical frame: the only things worth keeping are the minimum requisites (sleep, food, rest) that sustain focus on the one highest-leverage thing; everything else is a distraction. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
