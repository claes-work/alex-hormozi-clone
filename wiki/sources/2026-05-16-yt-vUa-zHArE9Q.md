# The Happiness Formula I've Discovered

## Summary

Alex says the common happiness formula, "when this happens, I will be happy," has failed
for him repeatedly. Wins did not bring lasting joy; he finds enjoyment in pursuit,
preparation, and the "Rocky cut scene" of doing the work. The clip overlaps with his
larger belief that big, far goals can extend the period of pursuit, which is where he
finds most of his enjoyment.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says he has repeatedly set up the equation that he will be happy when a future
  outcome happens.
- He has enough evidence that those outcomes did not bring the joy promised by that
  formula.
- He says the hot button for joy is what he is doing right now in the work.
- Preparation and reps before the event are often more enjoyable than the event itself.
- After a win, Alex often feels little or nothing, which made him inspect what he really
  enjoyed.
- He finds the most enjoyment in pursuit.
- He sets big, far goals partly because they let him stay in pursuit longer.
- Other people may see constant work as undesirable because they project their own
  expectations onto his life.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "When this happens I'll be happy."

> "It's always in pursuit."

> "In pursuit is my button for enjoyment."
