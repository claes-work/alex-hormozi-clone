# "The biggest mistake in your 20's that will make you poor in your 30's" (2022-03-09)

## Summary

A solo storytelling video about reputation, told through Hormozi's freshman year at
Vanderbilt. After nearly flunking out (a ~1.9 GPA) while chasing status and a "womanizer"
reputation, his father's advice — stop trying to change what people think and change who
you are — reframed his whole approach. The lesson: reputation can't be curated at scale;
the only durable fix is to change yourself and let perception catch up, starting with your
reputation with yourself.

## Key claims

- **You can't curate reputation.** There are too many people you interact with to manage
  what each thinks; the only lever is changing who you actually are. (paraphrase)
- **Change yourself first; perception lags.** Reputation change is slow — it took ~2 years
  to reverse the reputation he built in his first 3 months of college. (paraphrase)
- **Self-witness over external validation.** The hardest and most important audience is
  yourself: "we are our own witnesses." Build the reputation you'd respect even if everyone
  else hated you. (paraphrase)
- **Evidence beats overwhelming testimony.** If one person calls you a name, ignore it; if
  everyone you know says it, "you might be" — the evidence matters. (paraphrase)
- **Be-the-person framing.** Shift from "how do I appear this way" to "I want to be this
  way"; ask what that version of you does (studies hard, works out, is a good friend). (paraphrase)
