# Helping a Car Rental Business Scale

## Summary
Short live-coaching clip. The client runs a car-rental business and frames the constraint as marketing: big competition, and consumers just want the cheapest option. Alex's core answer is an **underdog / commoditized-market playbook**. You cannot beat the national incumbents nationally, so pick one specific market and do something genuinely better than them there — win one market at a time. His model is Avis's famous "We Try Harder" #2 positioning: the winning strategy for the underdog is *superior service*, and the good news is the incumbents at the top tend to deliver a poor experience (he references how bad car-rental service historically was). So beating them on actual service should work — specifically by leaning into "soft-touch," human-touch things that are less scalable and less efficient but let you win in a smaller market. He's candid that it won't be an easy business: it's "largely commoditized" — one company's economy car is literally the same product as another's — but there's a ton of demand. From there the levers are availability (a huge component), plus traffic and the ability to convert. His differentiating financial lever is the **sales process**: you should be able to outsell competitors on upsells (he points to his *$100M Money Models* book), so dialing in that sales process lets you make more per rental than they do. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (excluded from claims).
