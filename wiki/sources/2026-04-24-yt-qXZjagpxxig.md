# Helping a $560K Gym Double Revenue

## Summary

Alex coaches a women's kickboxing gym owner who bought the location three months earlier
and wants to grow from about $560K to $1.2M. Since acquisition is working but churn is
around 25%, Alex focuses on retention: member consumption, community, weekly rituals,
internal promotions, and making the gym feel like a smaller tribe as it scales.

## Key claims

- Alex says the owner's constraint is retention rather than acquisition.
- He frames the "five horsemen" of gym retention as a way to approximate small-tribe
  social bonding once the member base grows beyond Dunbar-number scale.
- He recommends alternating internal referral plays, Fast Cash Fridays, weekly themes,
  and every-other-month seasonal internal promotions.
- He says check-ins should ask about fun, workouts, and life updates rather than
  repeatedly reminding members about weight or stalled results.
- He says churn is ultimately driven by consumption: members who keep attending, enjoy
  the sessions, and like the trainers are harder to lose.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "No one cancels a membership they use."

> "Churn is driven by consumption."

> "You're trying to approximate tribe."
