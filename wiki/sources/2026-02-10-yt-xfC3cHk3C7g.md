# My Investment Thesis for 2026

## Summary
Asked how his investment thesis has evolved, Alex describes (as of 2026-02-10) a thesis he calls "unsurprisingly obvious," reached by doing the opposite of what he once believed. Echoing Warren Buffett's shift from "terrible businesses at fabulous prices" to "fabulous businesses at fair prices," he reports that in acquisition.com's first 24 months the firm did 24 deals and gave back or lost the invested money on all but three; the surviving few — an 80/20 outcome — were great businesses with strong founder relationships that kept growing. He draws two lessons: with a weaker operator you constantly want to take over the business yourself, while an operator as good or better than you won't cede much equity, so the best deals he did came at higher valuations, less equity, and all-cash terms than he wanted, with his own help simply assumed as part of protecting his return rather than sold as a consulting service. His current TL;DR: "we just write checks" — meaning money for equity, not necessarily full buyouts, with any additional help offered without expectation.
