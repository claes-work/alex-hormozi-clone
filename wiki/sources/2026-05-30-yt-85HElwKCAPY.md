# Why You're Not Burnt Out (You're Just Weak)

## Summary

Alex Hormozi links work capacity to training capacity. After losing everything and then making more in the next 12 months, he says he stopped seeing work as wasted because he himself is the output of the work. He compares burnout fears to overtraining fears in fitness: recovery capacity is trainable, and if the person does not die or get injured, they adapt. He then argues that experts become experts partly because they learn to be rewarded by the work itself.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- No work is wasted because the person becomes the output of the work, not only the external outcome.
- Work capacity increases through work, similar to training volume in fitness.
- Recovery capacity itself can be trained.
- In fitness and work, the person either stops, gets injured/dies, or adapts; if they avoid injury and continue, they improve.
- The more Alex works, the better he says he becomes at working and the more productive he becomes per unit of time.
- Experts become experts because they learn to get rewarded from the work itself.
- People can reduce self-disappointment by judging themselves by actions taken rather than thoughts had.
