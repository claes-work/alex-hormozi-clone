# How to Make Hard Decisions: Choose Your Regret

## Summary

Alex Hormozi and another speaker discuss "choose your regret" as a decision-making frame.
Instead of asking which option someone wants, Alex says to compare which regret is more
livable. This uses loss aversion and the "run away from" engine: the pain of wasting a
decade in a hated path may be more motivating than the upside of starting a business.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Difficult decisions can be reframed from "which do I want?" to "which regret could I
  live with?"
- Every meaningful choice carries regret because choosing one path closes another.
- A short painful conversation can be compared against the larger regret of wasting years
  in a life someone does not want.
- Loss framing can be more motivating than gain framing because humans are loss-averse.
- Alex defines his "not good enough" fear as reaching the end of life and believing he
  could have tried harder.
- He likes the frame because it uses fear constructively rather than only aspiration.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Which regret could I live with?"

> "I could have tried harder."
