# You Just Need Skills

## Summary

A short clip in which Alex Hormozi recounts a sales demonstration to make one
argument: the value of a skill equals the income delta it would unlock. He
describes seeing a salesman at a conference write "$1,000,000" on a whiteboard,
ask an audience member her income ($50,000/year), subtract it, and declare that
not knowing how to make a million dollars a year "costs you $950,000 every
single year." Hormozi generalizes this — the value of any skill is the
difference between where you are and where the skill would take you, and it is
usually not one skill but many that "ladder up" to that value. His practical
takeaway: people should appraise the gap not by the money and time currently in
their possession, but by the money and time the skill would give them; doing so
would make far more people willing to invest in their own education (a word he
notes people find taboo/off-putting). The clip closes with a CTA for his free
10-stage zero-to-$100M+ company roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap. This
extends the existing "invest in skills" thread. See [[../topics/mindset/mindset]]
and [[../topics/wealth/wealth]].

## Key claims

_(All from 2026-02-19; single speaker, Alex Hormozi.)_

- (paraphrase) The value of a skill equals the delta between your current
  outcome and the outcome the skill would produce — e.g. the gap between a
  $50,000 income and a $1,000,000 income is the value of the million-dollar-a-year
  skill.
- (paraphrase) ★ Not having a valuable skill has an ongoing opportunity cost: not
  knowing how to make a million dollars a year "costs you $950,000 every single
  year." The framing is a recurring annual loss, not a one-time gap.
- (paraphrase) The logic scales without limit — the value of a
  billion-dollar-a-year skill is the delta between your current income and a
  billion; answering the troll who pushed the numbers higher, he affirms the
  principle holds at any level.
- (paraphrase) High-value outcomes are rarely one skill but "many skills that
  ladder up" and combine to create that value.
- (paraphrase) ★ People should appraise the delta by their *potential* earnings
  and time (what they *would* be making / how much time they'd have and what
  that's worth), not by the money in their wallet or the time they currently
  have; done that way, far more people would invest in their education.
- (paraphrase) "Education" is a taboo/disliked word because people equate it with
  work, but becoming an entrepreneur requires learning a large volume of material.
- (paraphrase, CTA) He offers a free 10-stage roadmap from zero to $100M+ — a
  path he says fewer than 1% of companies finish and he has completed multiple
  times across software, physical products, service, and brick-and-mortar
  businesses — broken down by eight business functions with the constraint,
  symptoms, and steps taken to graduate each stage (acquisition.com/roadmap).
