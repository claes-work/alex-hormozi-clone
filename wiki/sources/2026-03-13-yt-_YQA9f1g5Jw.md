# How I'd Scale a Restaurant

## Summary
Live consulting clip. Alex walks a restaurant owner (a rebranding, sit-down spot in a tourist town where ~65–70% of traffic is non-locals) through a concrete turnaround playbook, structured by his standard sequence: **fix the back end (monetization/profit) first, then drive the front end (traffic)**. The margin levers are small, invisible-to-the-customer menu tweaks: add ".99" to every price, tack a "+3% card fee" in fine print at the bottom of the menu, and **reorder the menu by absolute gross profit (dollars) rather than price**, boxing the three highest-GP items as "can't miss" picks for weekend visitors who only eat there once. Because liquor carries the highest margins, he lays out a **three-pronged alcohol strategy**: pair each meal with a specific wine/spirit on the menu, run a weekly mixologist "drink of the week" (team tastes it so they can recommend with conviction) promoted via a table A-frame, and script drink upsells into service. The service half is a **four-conversation scripted customer journey** — first interaction (Chick-fil-A-style consistency), drink special, meal-paired drink, and review/return ask — with the manager doing a post-bill table walk to request a five-star review via a QR code (a staffer gets a "spiff"). For traffic he covers **grand (re)openings** he'd repeat as often as possible; a **Wendy's-style loyalty hook** (their first-500 golden Frosty keychain → 500 regulars) adapted here as a "first 500 free margherita pizza" promo requiring a review + a posted video across three platforms; **influencer/local-ambassador seeding** (invite locals with followings framed as "ambassadors," turn their videos into Meta ads); handwritten A-frame specials (print "never works"); and a **carryout spend bonus** ("spend over $75, get a thing") to nudge order values above the average. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex sequences turnarounds **monetization/profit first, traffic second** — "fix the back end first… then we can drive more on the front end."
- As of March 2026, several margin levers are effectively invisible to customers: add ".99" to every menu price, and add a "+3% card fee" in fine print at the bottom of the menu, which lifts margin ~4–5% across all transactions with essentially no objection.
- As of March 2026, menus should be ordered within each section by **absolute gross profit in dollars, highest to lowest** (not by price and not by margin percentage), with the top-GP items boxed as an "out-of-counter can't-miss" set to steer one-time visitors.
- As of March 2026, none of these business tactics matter if the food is bad — "if the food sucks, nothing will work"; the tactics are additive to the thing that matters most (the food), which he explicitly can't coach.
- As of March 2026, liquor typically carries a restaurant's highest gross margins, so his three-pronged alcohol play is: (1) print a meal-specific drink pairing beside each dish, (2) a weekly mixologist "drink of the week" the whole team tastes so they can recommend it with conviction, promoted by a table A-frame, and (3) scripted drink upsells in service.
- As of March 2026, service should run on four scripted daily conversations — first interaction, drink special, meal-paired drink upsell, and review/return ask — modeled on Chick-fil-A's identical every-time counter interaction ("great establishments work that way; most don't, which is why most aren't great").
- As of March 2026, the review engine: manager walks the table right after the bill is delivered, asks "how was everything?", and on a positive answer requests a five-star review via a QR code (to Yelp/Google/OpenTable); a named staffer "gets a spiff." Done daily, "within 6 months the thing has thousands of reviews" and a higher average score.
- As of March 2026, Alex would run grand (re)openings as often as possible because "no one even knows you exist anyways" — a rebrand/repaint is a natural trigger for a "grand reopening."
- As of March 2026, his favorite opening hook is Wendy's': the first 500 people get a golden Frosty keychain redeemable for free Frosties forever, which manufactures 500 repeat customers who bring paying friends. The restaurant equivalent here: a **first-500 free small margherita pizza** (cheap to make, ~$2.25 cost, loved) — optionally limited to one year.
- As of March 2026, the promo should require reciprocity: redeemers must leave a five-star review and post a video of getting their pizza/keychain across up to three platforms, generating ~500 organic videos usable as ads plus a review boost.
- As of March 2026, best reopening tactic is seeding local influencers/creators to eat free and post — find them via the nearest local hashtags and DM locals, or invite anyone ~10k+ followers, but **frame it as "we're looking for local ambassadors"** rather than "come promote my restaurant."
- As of March 2026, physical promotion runs on A-frames; if the venue isn't standalone / lacks street foot traffic, put the A-frame just inside the door so people "walk right into it," and **always handwrite specials — "print never works."**
- As of March 2026, a **carryout spend bonus** lifts takeout margin: once you know the average order value (~$56–60 here), offer a reward for spending over a threshold set just above it (e.g. "spend over $75/$80, get a thing") to nudge orders upward.
