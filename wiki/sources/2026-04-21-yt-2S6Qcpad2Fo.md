# Helping a $400K SaaS Business Fix Churn

## Summary

Alex coaches a low-priced SaaS serving Amazon sellers. He says the model of free
information plus paid implementation/software is directionally right, but software needs
organic compounding growth before paid traffic. The company should fix churn or create
referral/viral replacement before adding gas.

## Key claims

- Alex says the owner's constraint may be expectations because software often takes a
  long time to get right.
- He says software should ideally have a resting growth rate from organic compounding
  before paid traffic is added.
- He says Skool became attractive to him because the metrics showed it kept growing
  before he added attention.
- He says low-priced software needs large traffic volume and cannot afford a leaky
  bucket.
- He describes two SaaS growth paths: referrals exceed churn, or retained customers
  expand enough to offset lost logos.
- He says paid marketing is irrelevant if customers fall out the other side, so the
  bucket must be fixed before scaling traffic.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You want a resting growth rate."

> "Fix the bucket."

> "Then you dump the traffic."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: SaaS resting growth rate before paid traffic; Skool metrics; referral growth vs expansion revenue; fix churn/bucket first.
