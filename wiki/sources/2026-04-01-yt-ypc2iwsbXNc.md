# How to Win Every Single Day

## Summary

Alex recommends defining daily wins through controllable leading inputs rather than
lagging outcomes. His current standard is the gap between actual effort and possible
effort: when nothing remains in reserve, he counts the day as won even before the external
result arrives.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex distinguishes lagging metrics, which report outcomes, from
  leading metrics, which record the actions that create them.
- Calls made and calories eaten are his examples of controllable inputs, contrasted with
  sales and bodyweight outcomes that can vary in timing.
- He recommends redefining winning around completing the repeated input rather than
  waiting for an eventual result.
- His personal criterion is the difference between how hard he tried and how hard he
  could have tried; a zero gap means he won by his chosen standard.
- For careers requiring many repetitions, an input definition of success sustains effort
  longer than fixation on a slow external result.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The real things to track are what you're doing to create the outputs."

> "If I do this every day, I have won."

> "The thing that I try and focus on now is the delta between how hard I tried and how hard I can possibly try."
