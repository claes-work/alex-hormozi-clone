# Alex Hormozi Tells the Story of His First Business

## Summary

Short interview clip (Acquired / @ACQofficial) in which Alex recounts his earliest
business origin. He frames his **first business as a charity "free training project"**
whose testimonials he then converted into a for-profit online training business (~20
clients), before apprenticing under a California gym owner and opening his first gym in
Huntington Beach. He notes he was "writing the book right now" at the time of the
interview. The clip contains no dates or ages.

## Key claims

- His **first business was a charity project** he calls the "free training project":
  people donated **$500–$1,000 to the charity of their choice** and he trained them in
  return; it generated **testimonials** (self-reported).
- He **used those charity testimonials to launch a for-profit online training business**
  — his first for-profit venture.
- He switched to for-profit because he **wanted to do fitness full-time** and **wasn't
  making any money** on the charity; **~20 clients ("20 guys") signed up** for the paid
  online training.
- He framed the switch as keeping the same goal but delivering "in a slightly different
  way" (host's framing, which Alex affirms).
- He decided the **next step was to start a gym**; he ran online training clients on
  weekends while pursuing it.
- He was **on the East Coast** and **drove out to a gym owner in California**, asked him
  to **mentor** him, and apprenticed for **~3 months**, matching the owner's hours
  ("when he went to the gym I went to the gym," "from four to four").
- He then opened his **first gym in Huntington Beach** — "my first business experience."
