# Your About Page Isn't Selling — Here's Why

## Summary

Alex reviews an about/sales page and pushes it toward concrete promise, proof, and
feature-specific testimonials. He advises getting about 10 testimonials by helping people
for free, then pairing each feature with proof that the feature created value. He also
warns against confusing "rock bottom" with "rock top" storytelling and says the page
must clearly answer who is served, what result they get, how, by when, and why it works.

## Key claims

- Alex recommends collecting roughly 10 testimonials by helping people for free before
  relying on a thin about page.
- Testimonials should be written or screenshot-backed and highlight specific key points.
- Each feature should be paired with proof related to that feature.
- A page should lead with a concrete promise and proof, not poetic copy that fails to
  explain what the buyer gets.
- A story should choose either a "rock bottom" or "rock top" frame; mixing a money flex
  with a bottoming-out story can confuse the audience.
- Sales-page clarity starts with who, what, where, when, and why.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Help people for free."

> "You have to pick whether you're going rock bottom or rock top."

> "What am I going to help them do, how am I going to help them do it, when am I going to help them do it by?"
