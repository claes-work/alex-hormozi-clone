# $500K Power Washer Spends $2K/mo on Ads and Has No Idea If They Work

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a power-washing / solar-panel-cleaning / window-cleaning owner doing about $500K revenue and $250K take-home. The owner wants more recurring B2B work, but Alex says the first issue is not a new avatar; it is the absence of measured acquisition. Because door knocking and possibly paid ads are already producing residential work, Alex recommends installing attribution, then scaling what already works before changing the business model.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - local cleaning business owner. His revenue, profit, service mix, door-knocking volume, ad spend, and commercial-interest context are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Referrals are not a controllable acquisition channel if the owner is waiting for the phone to ring.
- Before switching to a commercial avatar, the owner should understand existing acquisition channels.
- The four non-referral channels discussed are paid ads, organic content, outbound, and affiliates.
- If a third party runs ads, they should provide attribution tracking.
- Without attribution, the owner cannot know whether $2,000/month in ads is working.
- If ad spend is producing more than one deal per week at the current economics, it may already be profitable.
- The first step is attribution, then scaling spend if the return is good.
- Local businesses can sometimes run more than one acquisition channel because small geographies require reaching the same people in multiple ways.
- The owner should do more of what already works before changing the model.
