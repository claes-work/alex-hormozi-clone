# The Best Entrepreneurs Do These 2 Things Better

## Summary

Alex cautions against armchair business advice: simple prescriptions look obvious from the
outside, but operators are managing inventory, people, rent, payroll, marketing, and sales
at the same time. He defines the best entrepreneurs as resource allocators who do two
things unusually well: curate opportunities and then prioritize them under limited
resources. He equates that combination with strategy.

## Key claims

- Outside observers underestimate the number of simultaneous constraints a real operator
  faces.
- The best entrepreneurs are allocators of resources: they get more output for each unit
  they put in.
- Strategy means curating opportunities and prioritizing them against limited resources.
- Many people can rank obvious options when given a list; stronger entrepreneurs also see
  the hidden option that others did not notice.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The best entrepreneurs are allocators of resources."

> "That is the basis of strategy."
