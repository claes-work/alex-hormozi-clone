# How to Get Good at Anything

## Summary

Alex demonstrates skill decomposition with a basketball free throw. A learner should
break broad performance into measurable components—result, follow-through, shoulder
position, and alignment—then improve the specificity of those measurements over time.
Adult neural plasticity may decline, but skill at learning can improve through better
diagnosis.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says winning requires breaking a skill into specific measurable
  behaviors even when that feels overly complex.
- A free throw can be tracked through nested outputs and technique components rather than
  only makes and misses.
- He says adults may have less raw learning plasticity than children but can become more
  skilled at identifying the exact behavior that needs to change.
- Early measures will be imperfect; the learner becomes better at describing and
  quantifying the skill through observation.
- Without tracking, there is no reliable way to know whether performance is improving.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Welcome to winning. We have to break things down."

> "You have to quantify to the highest degree possible."

> "If you do not track, you do not care. Period."
