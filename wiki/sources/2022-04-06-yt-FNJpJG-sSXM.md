## Summary

Hormozi walks through the main bullets of the April-2022 Biden tax proposal (top marginal
rate to 39.6%, capital gains taxed as ordinary income above $1M, corporate rate to 28%,
carried-interest change, a $500K cap on 1031 exchanges, and a 20% minimum tax on income
*including unrealized gains*) and explains the entrepreneur impact of each. He spends most
of the video attacking the unrealized-capital-gains tax as economically destructive, then
floats two deliberately radical alternative tax ideas of his own.

## Key claims (all dated 2022-04-06; commentary on a proposed, unpassed bill)

- The proposal he's most alarmed by is a 20% minimum tax on income including *unrealized*
  gains; via a worked example (a $200M-valued company growing $50M in a year) he argues it
  can produce an effective ~80% tax rate and force founders to sell equity or borrow to pay
  tax on gains they never realized. (paraphrase)
- He rates the bill's chance of passing as low, but calls it "one of the scariest tax bills
  I've ever seen" because it makes founder risk "virtually untenable." (paraphrase)
- Core value-belief driving his objection: the private sector allocates capital ~10x more
  efficiently than government, so taxing away builders' capital kills innovation and startup
  funding. (paraphrase — a recurring Hormozi theme, potentially durable but dated here)
- His two "radical" alternatives: (1) drop income tax toward 0% and tax capital gains
  heavily (poor earn via income, rich via investments); (2) a ~100% "death tax" with low
  income/capital-gains taxes during life, to force the wealthy to give money away privately
  rather than build dynastic legacy. (paraphrase)
- Anti-legacy philosophy: legacy is "a myth" over a long enough horizon and inherited money
  "ruins people when you have not earned it." (paraphrase — this is a more durable personal
  value, though still stated 2022)
