# The Math of Hard Days (And Why You Need Them)

## Summary

Alex gives a solo reflection on hard days, reframing, money loss, and good-day design.
He says bottom-10% days are part of the distribution that makes top-10% days legible,
and argues that reframing reality is one of the most important skills. He also describes
a recent multi-eight-figure bill due in 72 hours and how he used impermanence,
utility, and moment-by-moment progress to keep his headspace functional.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice)

- A person can be in a good mood for no reason if they can be in a bad mood for no
  reason.
- Bottom-10% days are a normal part of the distribution of days.
- Without bottom-10% days, top-10% days would be harder to recognize.
- Alex says reframing reality may be the most important skill.
- A powerful reframe asks how a hard event could become an origin-story pivot decades
  later.
- Alex self-reports a multi-eight-figure bill that had to be paid in cash within 72
  hours.
- He says the bill changed only his expectation of the future, not his food, work, home,
  or daily life.
- For long obstacles, he recommends making a little progress every day.
- His personal good-day formula is eating with people he likes, lifting with people he
  likes, and writing something.
- He says removing rush from those activities turns good days into better days, and he is
  willing to trade some upside for consistently better days at this career stage.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you can be in a bad mood for no reason, then you might as well be in a good mood for no reason, too."

> "The most important skill that you can have is being able to reframe reality."

> "Eat with people I like. Lift with people I like. Write something."
