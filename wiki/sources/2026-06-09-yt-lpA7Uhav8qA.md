# Make an Offer So Good They Feel Stupid Saying No

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a wellness-dentistry practice owner who also runs an education/coaching platform for other practices and is considering a DSO-style roll-up. Alex says the owner should make practice owners an offer so good they feel stupid saying no: give them more than they would get alone, keep the upside above that threshold, and use the coaching/education business as the goose that produces acquisition candidates and operating improvements. He also describes option rights, drag-along rights, and IP licensing as deal-structure considerations.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The offer to practice owners must clearly beat what they could get selling alone.
- A sample structure: if they might get 4x alone, offer them value around 7x with Alex/the aggregator keeping upside above that.
- For the first proof-of-concept, giving up some upside may be better than outlaying large capital.
- The coaching business is the goose and the practice roll-up exits are the eggs; do not necessarily sell the coaching engine.
- Coaching clients can be standardized through systems, pricing, training, and operations before roll-up, which does much of the post-acquisition integration work early.
- To avoid holdouts, the aggregator needs rights agreed upfront, such as options for 100% of the business and drag-along rights above a threshold.
- IP/AI/coaching assets can be retained separately and licensed to the practice group or acquirer through negotiated terms.
