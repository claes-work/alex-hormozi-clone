# When to Launch a Paid Community After Going Free First

## Summary

Alex advises a behavioral-therapy community owner who gained 230 organic members in two
days. He says not to stop the promotion that filled the free community: keep the first
hours of each day dedicated to promotion and use the later part of the day for delivery.
For the paid launch, he recommends teasing something valuable for four to six weeks,
using that time to interview members and find the smallest deliverables that feel worth
paying for, then launching with his "whisper to shout" cadence and four conversion
points.

## Key claims

- A founder should not stop the daily promotion that filled the free community.
- Alex recommends scheduling promotion first each day, then doing delivery work later.
- Before launching paid, spend four to six weeks seeding the coming offer and collecting
  data from members.
- The paid offer should be built around the two or three core deliverables that members
  perceive as worth paying for.
- Alex recommends using the "whisper to shout" launch cadence from his book and then
  using four conversion points: lead magnet, free group, weekly webinar/onboarding/module
  CTA, and direct DM engagement.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You don't want to stop doing the things that filled up your free community."

> "I want to talk to people."

> "Figure out what those core deliverables are."
