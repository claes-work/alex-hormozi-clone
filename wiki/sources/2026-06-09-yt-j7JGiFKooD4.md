# Why We Refused to Join the $100K Club

## Summary

In this Q&A, a newly married attendee asks Alex and Leila Hormozi how they closed knowledge gaps when they first started working together. Alex says he tries to become the best student and best success story of anything he buys, follows "replicate before you iterate," and buys access because it pays down time. Leila adds that they exposed themselves to the same information by joining events, masterminds, and books together. They also say they refused entry to a premium network that would accept only one of them or required Leila to lie, because they were a package deal and would not compromise integrity.

## Key claims (attributed)

- **Alex** says he aims to be the best student and best success story of anything he purchases.
- **Alex** says that being others' success story can backfire because people later claim ownership over his outcomes.
- **Alex** says he operates by "replicate before you iterate": follow instructions exactly, reproduce the result, then make informed tweaks.
- **Alex** says he buys access because it pays down time, not because any program solves everything.
- **Leila** says she and Alex closed knowledge gaps by exposing themselves to the same information and stimulus.
- **Leila** says they did not join masterminds separately and shared books/events so both partners had the same inputs.
- **Alex and Leila** say they refused a premium network that would not let them join together; Leila says she would not lie to get in.
