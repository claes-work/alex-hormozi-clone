# How to Kill Your Ego

## Summary

Alex argues that ego keeps more people poor than it makes rich. The visible internet
examples of egotistical wealth are a biased sample; many genuinely rich people are quiet.
He says ego shrinks the pond someone competes in, while moving to a harder level forces
inadequacy and growth.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-15, Alex says many more people stay poor because of ego than get rich from it.
- He says visible business influencers are a biased sample and many rich people are
  anonymous.
- He says the most legitimate business creators he names are comparatively humble because
  they know what hard feels like.
- He says if someone feels amazing, they probably have not moved up a level.
- He says the way to dissolve ego is to fight harder opponents and move into a bigger pond.
- Self-reported: he says he is reminded daily of his inadequacy because Acquisition.com
  moved him into a bigger investing/deal game.
- Self-reported: he compares himself to Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger, tracks Buffett's
  net worth by age, and uses that as a reference point.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "More people stay poor because of their egos than get rich off them."

> "If you're a big fish you're not in a big enough pond."
