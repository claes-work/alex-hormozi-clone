# The Better Way to Make Big Decisions

## Summary

Alex describes a decision frame used in business and life: ask which problems the team
would prefer, rather than focusing only on upside. By listing the downsides of each
path, including the paths not chosen, the decision-maker reduces regret and is less
tempted to romanticize the alternative.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-07)

- Alex says he often thinks about life frames first through business strategy.
- He says his team asks, "Which problems would we prefer?" when making big strategic
  decisions.
- He recommends imagining the concrete problems created by each option before choosing.
- He says focusing only on upside makes the path not chosen look unrealistically good.
- He says regret often comes from imagining only the upside of the unchosen path.
- He says good decision-making includes remembering the downsides that came with the
  path not chosen.
- He says this frame helps him feel glad that some problems are no longer his to solve.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Let's really sit in what each of these problems is."
