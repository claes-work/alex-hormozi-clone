# Helping an AI Consultant Scale From $100K to $1M

## Summary

A short live coaching Q&A. The questioner (introduces herself as **Haley**) sells custom
GPTs built from authors' frameworks to coaches, founders and consultants — she reverse
engineers a framework and builds it into a branded GPT for the client. She reports doing
~$100k and wants $1M in the next ~18 months. Her offer stack: a one-time branded-GPT
build at **$497–997**, an AI platform / "operating system" with all the frameworks at
**$3,800/year**, and a four-week **AI boot camp at $997**. She frames her own problem as
not having created enough YouTube content.

Alex reframes it immediately: **"You have a model issue"** — it's not a content problem,
it's an offer-stack problem ("It's all stuff," which Haley concedes: "I know. I need to
cut stuff out."). His prescription, after Haley says the two things she loves selling are
the boot camp and the operating system: **(1) Kill the $497–997 one-time GPT build** as a
paid product and fold it in as a **free deliverable inside the boot camp** ("what's the
clear outcome that they're going to get?"). **(2) Upsell from the boot camp into the
annual platform at ~$4–5k/year**, priced against customer economics — for her audience of
coaches and consultants, $4,000 is roughly one customer, so "5,000 a year will probably do
no problem." **(3) Point all ads at the boot camp**, which he says will sell easily
because with AI "everyone feels like they're behind." **(4) A single-funnel content
motion:** the YouTube CTA is outcome-plus-timeframe ("if you want to build something like
this in five days or less that you can monetize, come to my boot camp"); she can make a
free widget/lead magnet for every video ("super easy"), but every widget must still lead
to "the one funnel [to] rule them all," which leads to the boot camp on whatever cadence
she wants. **(5) Price the boot camp low, not high:** to "make the boot camp a little
better, charge like 97 bucks." When Haley counters that she'd charge $1,000, Alex explains
he sees the low price **as a feature** — his goal is to get prices as low as possible so
the most people show up, "so that I can sell them on the back." The video ends with the
standard acquisition.com/roadmap free-gift CTA (10 growth stages, quiz, per-function
prescriptions across product, marketing, sales, customer success, recruiting, IT, HR,
finance; thank-you page books a call, in-person Vegas session if they can help).

## Key claims
_All dated 2026-07-17. Paraphrased unless quoted. Alex-attributed unless marked._

- **A stalled sub-seven-figure consultant with many small offers has a model issue, not a
  content issue.** Haley self-diagnoses insufficient YouTube output; after hearing one
  price point Alex cuts in: "You have a model issue." Too many overlapping offers is
  "all stuff" that needs cutting.
- **Kill the low-ticket one-time offer; repackage it as a free deliverable inside the
  flagship front end.** The $497–997 one-time GPT build should stop being a product and
  become a free deliverable of the boot camp, anchored to a clear outcome the attendee
  gets.
- **Price the recurring back end against the customer's own unit economics.** Upsell the
  boot camp into the annual platform at ~$4k/year — for coaches/consultants, $4,000 is
  roughly the value of one customer to them, so ~$5,000/year "will probably do no
  problem."
- **Concentrate all ad spend on the single front-end event (the boot camp).** He is
  confident it will sell because of AI-era urgency: "everyone feels like they're behind."
- **Content CTA formula: outcome + timeframe + monetization, pointed at the one front
  end.** For her YouTube videos where she teaches and implements a framework: "if you
  want to build something like this in five days or less that you can monetize, come to
  my boot camp."
- **Per-video lead magnets are fine, but every freebie must feed one funnel.** She can
  make a widget for every video — "super easy" — but all widgets should still lead to the
  single funnel that leads to the boot camp, run on whatever cadence she wants.
- **Low price on the front-end event is a feature, not a compromise.** To make the boot
  camp better, charge ~$97 rather than $997 (Haley would charge $1,000). Alex's stated
  goal: get the price as low as possible to maximize show-up volume, then sell the back
  end — monetization happens on the ascension, not the ticket.
- _(Outro, promotional)_ The "$100M scaling roadmap" free gift at acquisition.com/roadmap:
  ~200 hours of reviewing portfolio companies distilled into 10 growth stages with a quiz
  that locates a business and prescribes actions per function (product, marketing, sales,
  customer success, recruiting, IT, HR, finance); thank-you page books a call, with an
  in-person Vegas session if they can help.
