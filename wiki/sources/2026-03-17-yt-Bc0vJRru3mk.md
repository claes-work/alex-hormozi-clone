# Helping a Consulting Firm Get More Leads

## Summary
Live coaching clip. "Reeds" runs a 12-year-old data-consulting firm doing ~$500k/year (mostly serving enterprise / mid-tier ~500-person companies) that wants to reach $1.2M. His stated constraint is "leads, leads, and leads" — the business has been ~99% referral-driven and has "never really sold anything," and he finds the message hard because "no one thinks they need data" (everyone wants AI). He's just started LinkedIn content, LinkedIn ads, and a not-yet-launched cold-email motion. Alex reframes the problem on two axes. First, **messaging/content**: stop talking about data itself and talk about the *benefit* of data — "we talk about Maui, not how we're going to get there" (the vacation, not the plane flight/TSA). Content should demonstrate the outcome/ROI of the service, and should carry CTAs that generate inquiries. His signature suggestion is a high-value lead magnet: **give away five free one-on-one calls** (asterisk: only to companies meeting qualifying criteria) — calls the firm would have to take for qualified leads anyway, but framed as a gift everyone perceives as valuable. On those calls, *describe* the future outcome, don't *explain the how* ("we're going to describe it, not explain it") — because the moment you drop into "we're going to click here," the prospect is gone; describing the destination is how you reset beliefs and build excitement. Second, **pricing/offer**: Reeds bills hourly, which Alex reacts to with mock horror. He prescribes an outcome-based **calculator close** — at the end of the five calls, tally up all the savings/efficiency gains the assessment surfaced (e.g. "$200,000"), then charge a percentage of the value created. His rule: the max you can charge on any result-based service is **30%** of the value — save $200k, charge 30% = $60k. Charging for outcomes instead of hours, he says, could alone "triple or quadruple" income independent of the lead-gen changes. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
