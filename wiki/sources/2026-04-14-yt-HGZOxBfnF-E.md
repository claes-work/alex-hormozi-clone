# Building a $1,000,000 Business for a Stranger in 26 Minutes

## Summary

Live "Cash Cows"-style coaching session in which Alex Hormozi advises Luis Lora, owner
of Optimum Works, an online railing store doing $2.5M revenue / $384K profit at 15% net
margins with a broken 1:1 LTV:CAC. Alex diagnoses a data-attribution problem (81% of
sales credited to Google Ads with no reliable tracking) and deliberately gives only two
or three high-leverage moves: fix attribution, reorganize the whole business around the
least price-sensitive DIY-plus-custom buyer via a one-step call funnel with a VSL, and
add a twice-weekly long-term email nurture. A one-year follow-up shows Luis grew revenue
44% (to $3.6M, $540K profit), with custom orders rising to 50% of the business.

## Key claims (Alex's advice / frameworks)

- Prefer giving a coachee only **two or three high-impact moves** over 20 tactics, so
  the likelihood of actually executing stays high.
- A 1:1 (or here 0.41:1) LTV:CAC that shows you losing money on 80% of sales, while the
  business is clearly profitable, signals a **data-attribution problem, not a real one**:
  "we have numbers, but they're not useful."
- To scale from 3 to 10 to 100, you must **know your unit-economics data "like the back
  of your hand"**; without attribution you can do "zero optimization."
- The blunt way to find out where sales really come from is to **shut off the ads and
  watch how sales change** (framed as the "ballsier" option, with the caveat of not
  wanting to destroy the business).
- **Optimize for the least price-sensitive segment you already have the most of.** Here:
  DIY + custom, because they are less price sensitive, already a strong foothold, and
  emotionally obsessed with the product, unlike contractors/designers who commoditize you.
- When average order value is similar across segments, chase the segment with **higher
  gross margin**, not the higher headline ticket. Bumping DIY from ~$873 to ~$1,000+
  keeps AOV roughly flat while margin rises sharply.
- Reframe the site as "the home for DIY railings" showcasing custom work; funnel buyers
  into a **one-step funnel**: custom button -> form + calendar together -> post-opt-in
  **video sales letter** -> SMS nurture -> sales call.
- Use **BANT (budget, authority, need, timing)** in the SMS nurture, framed softly:
  "we're not a sales company, but I'm happy to hop on the call to figure out what you
  need." Ask who else must be on the call to decide.
- Frame the call as a **buying call, not a long sales process** — "no one's wasting
  anyone's time."
- On the call, **A/B the close on payment method**, not on whether to buy: "do you want
  to use your credit card or do you want to finance?" Offer Klarna/Affirm/Shopify financing.
- Offer a **$200 discount for moving forward on the call**, justified as saving the
  administrative headache of extra calls.
- Estimated impact of dialing in this process: **~50-60% close rate** and roughly double
  the average customer value — "double the close rate and more than double the gross
  margin ... that's a 4x."
- **VSL structure = the same as a YouTube video**: hook, then Proof, Promise, Plan (with
  a picture/road map), open a loop to the pricing at the end, deliver the "how to pick a
  handrail" content, then price ranges and expectations, then the call CTA. Target 5-7
  minutes.
- **Anchor with a value reframe**: railings add more home resale value than they cost, so
  "railings are basically free" — then let the buyer dream with a few "really sick"
  custom looks.
- Put the **custom-order flow on every product page**, not just best sellers, and add a
  **sticky top banner** ("custom designs available") instead of a disappearing one.
- Don't **force continuity** in a business that doesn't need it; instead run **consistent
  long-term nurture** so you're first in mind for the customer's next expansion. Compounds
  "like a snowball."
- **Email nurture cadence: twice per week to the whole list**, rotating (1) before/after,
  (2) "cool" showcase image, (3) FAQ answering the top objections of people who don't buy.
- **Email structure**: subject -> immediate reward (a striking image / "check this out")
  -> single CTA/link -> PS (discount or joke). Home-improvement buyers treat inspiration
  imagery "like porn ... they get super obsessive with it."
- **Sequence the build**: set up the funnel/system first, then drive traffic to it, then
  layer long-term nurture in parallel (delegated — "you're going to write a check and get
  somebody else to do it").
- Growth followed from execution, not the advisor: "this is all because of Luis actually
  taking action ... it's not because of me or some sort of magic." (One-year check-in.)
