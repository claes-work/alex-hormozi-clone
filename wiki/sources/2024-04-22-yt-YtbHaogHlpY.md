# Alex Hormozi reveals the blueprint to go from broke to rich

## Summary

In this podcast appearance (ACQ Official, hosted by a Welsh former bodybuilder), Alex Hormozi lays out a stage-by-stage growth blueprint: sell one thing to one type of customer on one channel to go from zero to six figures, do those same activities *consistently* to reach seven figures, then add backend products to raise customer lifetime value on the way to a million a month. He gives a concrete "if you lost everything today" starter sequence (LLC via LegalZoom → business bank account → Stripe/PayPal → message prospects → sell → deliver). He then frames strategic decision-making around three questions — what problem are we solving, play it out, which problem do you prefer — and argues that success is inevitable on a long enough timeline if you know and repeat your "input-output equation." A substantial middle portion of the video is the host telling his own come-up story and complimenting Alex; that segment is not Alex-attributed.

## Key claims

- Zero to six figures = sell one product, to one specific type of customer, through one channel (Instagram DMs, YouTube, ads, cold calls, affiliates, or referrals — the specific channel doesn't matter).
- Six to seven figures = doing each of those sales-generating activities *consistently*; most people make a sale and then stop working.
- Reaching ~$1M/month = adding backend products to increase each customer's lifetime value, which then lets you afford new channels to acquire more customers.
- "Lost everything" starter sequence: (1) set up an LLC via LegalZoom, (2) open a business bank account, (3) connect it to Stripe or PayPal, (4) message people with what you're selling and ask them to hop on a call, (5) close and run their card, (6) deliver the thing you promised.
- Three questions for any big strategic decision: What problem are we trying to solve? Let's play it out. Which problem do you prefer?
- Worked example: chasing "bodybuilder" when the real goal is money is solving the wrong problem — becoming an influencer without competing could earn significantly more.
- There are only a limited set of ways to get customers: individual reach-outs (friends/family or strangers), ads, content, affiliates, or referrals.
- If you don't know your "input-output equation" (the daily inputs that stack over time), you will not succeed; if you know it and repeat it long enough, success becomes "unreasonable" to avoid.
- Scaling isn't about adding to your plate — it's about removing the time-wasting things already on it; people further ahead have no more time, they just spend it differently.
