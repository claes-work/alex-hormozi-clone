# I Sell Custom Luxury Home Products. How Do I Get More Leads?

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client builds and manufactures one-off custom luxury home products — high-end gaming/pool/casino tables with features like backlighting — doing ~$2.5M/year on ~20% margins (~$500k/year profit), sold mostly via Google Ads → email → phone. They want to reach $10M+ and asked how to 2–3x quality leads "without breaking the bank." Alex reframes the premise hard: at $15–20k/month ad spend they have "saturated" nothing — he claims a cool-custom-gaming-product business could scale to ~$2M/month before approaching saturation, so their problem isn't the market, it's tactical media buying (wrong keywords). His core mindset shift: treat all profit you don't need to live on as an **experimentation budget**, not money you can "lose." He frames it as being willing to "burn $200k this year on bad keywords to find another six [winners] we can scale to $100k/month" and 5x the business — a $200–300k spend to go from $2.x M to $10M. He credits this to wearing both "entrepreneur and investor" hats: he judges everything on return on capital and would rather reach $10M faster by reinvesting the cash. A side-quest tangent covers where service businesses should reinvest profit: (1) **talent & culture** — in service businesses your price signals how good you are, so a virtuous cycle of good work → more demand than supply → raise price → buy better talent → better work should push price *and* talent up forever; if you can't charge more than competitors, "it's because you aren't better"; and (2) **brand** — big aspirational demonstrations (not direct-response), citing Red Bull's Stratos space jump as spend that "makes no sense except it makes a ton of sense" because it earns talk. For scaling cold traffic he invokes **Eugene Schwartz's five levels of market awareness** (unaware → problem-aware → solution-aware → product-aware → most-aware), explaining their current keywords target the most/product-aware bottom of the market; to reach the far larger, ~10x-cheaper cold traffic upmarket, they need **bridge pages** (advertorial pages) that carry a colder visitor from a broad keyword to the sales page — e.g. problem-aware angles like "Is your house boring?" or "Do you have an extra room you don't know what to do with?" Finally, Alex says if *he* took over, keywords wouldn't have been his first move: with such a visual, high-ticket product he'd go to **Instagram influencer outreach** — gift creators sick custom pieces (Mr. Beast-style) for shout-outs/tags routed to an inquiry or checkout page (creators earn more promoting high-ticket), then **whitelist** the highest-converting influencer story as a Meta/Instagram ad. He nonetheless coaches within their existing keyword strategy ("let's just find more keywords"). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
