# Why Most People Don't Win

## Summary

Alex argues that people do what they have been rewarded for and avoid what they have
been punished for, but he still treats belief and action as choices. When someone offers
an excuse, he sometimes agrees with the excuse to force the person to confront its
natural endpoint: never winning. He also rejects the personal-development formula "be,
do, have," arguing that identity and outcomes are both downstream of doing.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex agrees that people repeat rewarded behaviors and avoid punished behaviors.
- He says childhood conditioning can be hard to break, but the adult still chooses
  whether to keep believing it.
- He sometimes tells excuse-seekers they are right and will never win, because the
  reaction can surface the part of them that wants to fight.
- He says many excuses are reasonable-sounding ego protection.
- He says the reason someone gives for not doing something can become the reason to do
  it.
- He rejects "be, do, have" because being is a function of doing.
- He says identity is described by actions, so doing creates both identity and outcomes.
- He says a person does not need to feel confident or be in a good mood to do the action
  that produces the result.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're right. You're never going to be successful and it's not your fault."

> "Being is a function of doing."

> "You don't have to feel confident to do shit."
