# Why You Don't Have What You Want

## Summary
Solo motivational clip on the gap between wanting something and taking the action that would get it. Alex's thesis: the life you have now is the direct result of the decisions you've repeatedly declined to make — you get "all the way up to the precipice, all the way to the front of the line, and then you choose not to." He reframes inaction as itself a choice with a cost: if you genuinely believe you'll make the bet/call "sooner or later," you might as well do it sooner and start enjoying the benefits now, since delaying only delays the payoff. He then diagnoses *why* people freeze — the reason is usually "one or two voices in your head," and, crucially, the voice often isn't yours. The exercise he offers: name the voice ("whose voice does this belong to?"), then ask whether that specific person deserves control over your entire life. He illustrates with a personal admission: he almost didn't sell Gymshark because he thought the ~$46 million wasn't enough to impress "a specific colleague" he respected (not family, not a friend). Once he named the voice, the hesitation collapsed as "ridiculous." He generalizes it into named examples — "Am I really going to let John be the reason that I don't get married to this girl? Is Joe really going to be the reason that I don't raise my prices?" — arguing that people (himself included) surrender major decisions to the imagined judgment of others every day. Ends with the standard acquisition.com/roadmap scaling-roadmap promo.
