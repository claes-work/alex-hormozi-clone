# The #1 Rule of Marketing I'll Repeat Until I Die

## Summary

Alex rejects the premise that business success requires unethical behavior. He frames
ethical business as solving a real problem for people who voluntarily exchange money for
that solution. His central marketing rule is to state the facts and tell the truth; he
contrasts that with deception, which he says is especially poor long-term business
practice.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex rejects the premise that one must be unethical to be successful in business.
- He says if someone asks that of a successful person, the question already implies the
  successful person is unethical.
- He frames business as identifying a problem, knowing how to solve it, and offering the
  solution to people willing to pay.
- He says deception is poor business practice, especially over the long term.
- He states his number-one marketing rule as stating the facts and telling the truth.
- He says voluntary exchange where both parties say thank you is not unethical.
- He warns that believing making money is wrong makes it unlikely someone will make
  money.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I reject the premise."

> "State the facts and tell the truth."

> "If you believe that making money is wrong, you will never make it."
