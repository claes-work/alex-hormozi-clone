# Wish for the Skill Not Just the Weapon

## Summary

Alex tells a story from around age 20 or 21 about buying a Powerball ticket and feeling
terror that he might win because it would prevent him from proving he could build wealth
himself. He says luck can deprive someone of the gauntlet that creates the person they
become. He uses the Magic card "Burning Wish" as a metaphor: people often wish for the
weapon, not the skill to wield it.

## Key claims

- Alex says he feared winning a billion-dollar lottery because it would remove his chance
  to prove he could do it.
- He believes going through the gauntlet is part of becoming the person who can wield the
  outcome.
- A large resource without the skill to use it can be a burden rather than a blessing.
- Alex uses scale comparisons to stress how hard it is to understand the magnitude of a
  billion dollars.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I will never be able to prove that I could do it."

> "They wished for a weapon but not for the skill to wield it."

> "Wish for the skill."

Synthesis candidate: lottery fear; skill-over-resource/weapon metaphor; hardship as proof-building gauntlet.
