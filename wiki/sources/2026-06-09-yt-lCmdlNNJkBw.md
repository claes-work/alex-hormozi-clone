# We Have More Demand Than We Can Handle. How Do I Get to $10M?

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi gives substantially the same advice captured in [[2026-06-10-yt-EiUW4sCi23s]] to a restaurant-operations consulting business on track for about $3.2M and trying to reach $10M. He defines the problem-solving frame "logic, evidence, utility," recommends a pre-call indoctrination process such as a VSL, explains good friction and pre-framing, and ties a fast-cash play to client activation requirements.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- "Messaging is off" must be converted into a defined term, measurable evidence, and business utility.
- Logic, evidence, utility means: what does it mean, how do you know, and why should anyone care?
- If prospects are qualified but do not understand the offer, the company needs pre-call indoctrination and pre-framing.
- A VSL and pre-sale questions can reverse-engineer the eventual sale.
- Good friction qualifies good prospects in and bad prospects out; bad friction blocks everyone.
- More good friction can raise cost per call while improving ROAS and sales efficiency.
- Fast-cash plays should use existing underutilized resources such as old customers, dead contacts, lists, or social followers.
- Activation requirements, bonuses, discounts, and guarantees can be tied to specific client actions, then reversed back to the application and sales motion.

> "Most fast cash plays operate off of existing resources."
