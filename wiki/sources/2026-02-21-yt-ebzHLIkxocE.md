# 'I Run a $7M/year Agency. How Do I Sell the Company?'

## Summary

Live coaching Q&A (MoreMozi format). The **questioner** — owner of an SEO/PPC agency
serving roofing, HVAC, and home-services companies — is NOT Alex; he is
audience/uncertain attribution, and every business number he gives is self-reported.
His stated goal: grow from **$7M to $14M in revenue and sell the company for $20M**,
and he believes margin (~13% EBITDA) and AI/market risk are what's holding him back.

Alex's answer reframes the problem around two levers: (1) the **enterprise-value math**
and (2) **margin, not growth**. He runs his "hypothetical max" calculation — sales
velocity × LTV — to show the agency is already pacing toward ~$12M run-rate with no
changes, so the growth is essentially banked; the real job is making the incremental
(and existing) revenue **drop to the bottom line**. He argues a niched SEO agency
should run far better than its current **50% gross margin** (his service-business rule
of thumb is **80%**), dismisses industry averages as an anchor to low standards, and
lays out the path to the $20M exit: get to ~$12M revenue with ~$6M of operating margin,
which at a ~3.3x multiple supports the $20M sale.

On the "how," Alex gives a **"12-months-ago answer" (nearshore the team to recover
margin)** and a **"today answer" (become an AI-native agency)**: he claims ~80% of what
the agency does is automatable with AI *today*, and pushes the owner to stop framing AI
as a threat and instead weaponize it against slower competitors ("be the big bad wolf,
not the sheep") — get fluent in AI agents and make.com-style automation, since
everything but account management can largely be done by AI.

## Key claims (2026-02-21, paraphrased unless quoted)

- **Hypothetical-max / enterprise-value formula:** a business's projected max run-rate
  = **sales velocity (units/month) × LTV**, where **LTV = price ÷ churn**. Worked live
  on the guest's self-reported numbers: $5,000/mo price ÷ 5% monthly churn = **$100K
  LTV**; 10 units/mo × $100K = **$1M/mo → ~$12M/year** projected run-rate. ★ (paraphrase)
- Because the agency is already at ~$7M but pacing toward ~$12M, roughly **$5M of
  revenue is effectively already sold and will arrive even if the owner "changes
  nothing"** — so the $14M growth target is nearly banked and growth is not the real
  constraint. (paraphrase)
- **The real job is margin, not revenue:** make more of the *existing* revenue, and all
  of the *incremental* revenue, **drop to the bottom line.** (paraphrase)
- Guest's self-reported economics: **$7M revenue, ~13% EBITDA, 50% gross margin, 5%
  monthly churn, ~$5K/mo average price, ~10 units/mo, 5 employees**, a fractional CFO +
  his wife running money, building out a nearshore team (adding ~10 in 3 months).
  (self-reported)
- Alex's **rule of thumb for service businesses: target ~80% gross margin.** A niched
  SEO agency running only 50% gross margin is the reason EBITDA is stuck at ~13%; there
  is "zero reason" a niched agency can't run 50%+ *net* margins. (paraphrase)
- **Throw out industry averages entirely** — benchmarking against them only anchors you
  to lower standards, because most people (and most businesses) are bad at what they do.
  (paraphrase)
- **The path to the $20M exit:** get to ~$12M revenue with ~**$6M in operating margin**,
  and you can "sell for 20, for sure" — no changes to pricing or sales motion required,
  only operational/margin improvement. (This implies roughly a **~3.3x revenue / ~3.3x
  operating-profit** basis for the target price.) ★ (paraphrase)
- Ops-heavy businesses (like an agency) demand **more financial discipline** than a
  lighter model such as a course business; a fractional CFO alone is not a "really good
  finance arm." (paraphrase)
- **"12-months-ago answer": nearshore nearly the whole team** to recover the margin the
  business needs. (paraphrase)
- **"Today answer": become an AI-native agency** — Alex estimates **~80% of what the
  agency does is automatable with AI today** (not "tomorrow"), everything except account
  management. This is both the margin fix and the future-proofing move. ★ (paraphrase)
- **Reframe AI from threat to weapon:** do NOT treat AI as a risk to yourself — it's a
  risk to lazy competitors who won't adopt it. "Be the big bad wolf, not the sheep." A
  tool that 10x's your staff's productivity while competitors ignore it is a gift.
  (paraphrase)
- To capitalize, the owner must **get up to speed on AI agents and on make.com-style
  automation** — framed as the successor to what Zapier automations used to be.
  (paraphrase)
- **LLM SEO is the future-proofing concern:** ChatGPT is cited at ~**500M weekly users**
  (more than social platforms) and is already making commerce recommendations, which
  they will likely monetize — so building an LLM-SEO service line is "thing one" for
  future-proofing. (paraphrase; guest is already building this service)
- Overall diagnosis: the desired exit is **"in shooting distance"** with no changes to
  pricing or sales — the constraint is purely **operational flow / margin**, solvable
  via nearshoring + AI automation. (paraphrase)
- Alex plugs his free **acquisition.com/roadmap** — a 10-stage 0→$100M+ roadmap broken
  down by 8 business functions (constraint symptoms per stage). (paraphrase; promo)
