## Key claims (attributed)

All claims below are **Kale Owen's / Gym Launch's**, not Alex's:
- A pre-sale questionnaire lets you "sell against" a prospect's own written answers, closing without a script.
- Mimic a doctor's office: check-in (with pre-stacked sheets for social proof), hand them a form, they fill it out, then the consult — this manufactures authority and compliance.
- Question design captures current state, desired outcome, timeline, past failures, and pre-sells accountability/coaching by asking "would an expert help you get there faster?" (self-evident yes-questions).
- Weigh/body-fat-scan the prospect to expose the delta between perceived and actual numbers, amplifying pain.
- Sell a short 6-week challenge ($300 + $300 = ~$600) rather than a 12-month agreement — "sexier offer" — then convert to long-term at the halfway point.
- Don't state price until the end; by then prospects are "already grabbing their cards out."
