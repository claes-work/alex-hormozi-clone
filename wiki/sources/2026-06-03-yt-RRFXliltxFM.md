# He Spends $6,700 to Make $22,000 (Is That Good?)

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a property-investor buyers-advisory company with strong revenue, meaningful profit, a roughly $22K fee, and a CAC near $6.7K. The owner does not know which channels are working because customers touch many assets before buying. Alex says the owner can likely double the whole acquisition mix first, then improve allocation using qualitative first-touch surveys and more disciplined attribution. He also advises studying the best performers and turning their behaviors into standards.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If the business can afford to double its current acquisition mix and has no physical constraint stopping it, doubling is likely to work even before attribution is perfect.
- Qualitative first-touch surveys give directional signal: ask customers where they first heard about the company.
- Last-touch attribution overweights direct-response assets closest to the sale; first-touch attribution can overweight old discovery events.
- A blended rule can treat conversion events within 30 days as last touch, and older journeys as first touch.
- Keep supporting touchpoints if they nudge complex, high-touch buyers toward conversion.
- To duplicate top performers, study exactly what they do differently, break those differences into observable behaviors, and make them the standard.
- Some traits such as adherence and work ethic are skills, but may not be worth training if a person will not adopt the standard.
