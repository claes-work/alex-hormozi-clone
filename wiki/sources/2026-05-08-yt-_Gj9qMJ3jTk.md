# The Problem with Sharing Your Struggle

## Summary

Alex reflects on how public struggle is interpreted differently before and after
success. He says people cheer the underdog because it reminds them of their dreams,
but some try to tear the person down once the person passes them. He connects this to
leaving environments that reinforce the old identity.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says posting a hardship while it is happening gets a different response than
  posting the same hardship after success.
- He self-reports liking writing for a long time and having had a creative-writing
  scholarship that he did not take.
- He self-reports writing an essay titled "Everyone believes in the American dream
  until it comes true."
- He says people rooted for him while he slept at the gym, then criticized him after
  he had staff and locations.
- He says people may root for someone on the way up because the person reminds them of
  their dreams, then resent them when the person reminds them they gave up.
- He says people often want others to do well, but not better than them.
- He says outsiders may recognize the current person more readily than the hometown
  that remembers the weaker earlier version.
- He advocates leaving an environment when trying to become a different person because
  old environments reinforce the old identity.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Everyone believes in the American dream until it comes true."

> "When did I go from the underdog to the man?"

> "The environment you have is reinforcing the person you used to be."
