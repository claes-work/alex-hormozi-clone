# I Put On 20 Pounds of Muscle During My Worst Year

## Summary

Alex says achievement often requires sacrificing the very things one hoped achievement
would make available. He gives training as a sacrifice he decided to reverse in 2025:
working out is one of the few things that reliably brings him joy, so he made a rule
that he will not train rushed. He says this helped him during a difficult season and
that he put on about 20 pounds of muscle that year.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and opinion, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says high achievement often requires giving up proportional amounts of the things
  one hoped the achievement would create.
- He says training was his first love and one of the first things he became obsessed
  with.
- He says in recent years his workouts had sometimes been only two or three days per
  week and often 60 to 90 minutes.
- He says in 2025 he realized he had money and freedom but still was not letting himself
  train as long as he wanted.
- He made a rule that he will not work out if rushed.
- He lists working out with someone he likes, eating with someone he likes after
  training, and writing as three things that bring him the most joy.
- He says training became a life raft during a difficult season.
- He says he had put on about 20 pounds of muscle so far that year.
- He says a strong workout can make him feel he has won the day.

## Notable verbatim quotes
