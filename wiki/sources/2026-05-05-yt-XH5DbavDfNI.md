# 1 of 3 Paths to starting your first business

## Summary

Alex maps new Skool/community builders into three situations: beginners with no money
made yet, existing business owners using community as nurture or delivery, and audience
owners with weak monetization. Beginners should pick from pain, profession, or passion
and start free before moving toward a free trial. Existing businesses can attach a
community before or after purchase. Audience owners can monetize through a community and,
in Alex's view, usually benefit from free trials because Skool's marketplace data showed
higher GMV after the feature shipped.

## Key claims

- New community builders fall into three buckets: no prior monetization, an existing
  business, or an audience with inefficient monetization.
- Beginners can build around a pain they overcame, a professional skill, or a passion.
- For a beginner, Alex recommends starting "free free," building engagement, and later
  moving to a free trial or paid model.
- Existing businesses can use community as lead nurture before purchase or as a delivery
  component after purchase.
- Audience owners can use community as the primary monetization path around the skill,
  pain, or passion their audience already follows.
- Alex reports that after Skool introduced free trials, marketplace GMV and engagement
  rose, which he treats as evidence that free trials make more creators money.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Pain, profession, passion."

> "If you have a good product, then you just want people to try it and lower the barrier."
