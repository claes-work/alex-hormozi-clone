# How to 3.5x a Service Business in 6 Months

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a financial-advisor client-acquisition agency that grew from about
$20k/month to a $71k month in six months. He says the current constraint is delivery, not
customer acquisition, and recommends thinking in workflows rather than roles. He applies
the same process logic to sales: replace founder expertise with a question-based framework,
update the VSL using actual buyer pain points from recorded calls, and keep raising the
quality bar on the avatar to avoid a churn factory.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If customers are easy to get but the founder is the delivery bottleneck, the constraint is
  delivery systems and workflows.
- Founders should list all workflows required to deliver value before hiring for vague roles.
- Asset creation such as VSLs, nurture campaigns, ads, creative, and copy can now be heavily
  AI-assisted, even if not perfect.
- Build the customer-onboarding/delivery flow so new clients trigger repeatable assets and
  setup processes.
- Once delivery is systematized, the next likely constraint is sales.
- A non-founder closer cannot rely on founder authority; the sales process must work through
  a scripted, question-based framework and pre-call VSL.
- Recorded sales calls can be mined with AI for recurring buyer pain points.
- Alex says putting actual "why people buy" pain points into a VSL doubled acquisition in
  one of his own cases.
- For a $5k/month SMB offer, scale depends heavily on avatar quality; if customers need the
  agency to save their business, the agency may create a churn factory.
- The bar for who qualifies as a customer should rise over time.

## Notable verbatim quotes
