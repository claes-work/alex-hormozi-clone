# Why Most People Underestimate the Time to Success

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that people underestimate success timelines because time and
consistency are difficult to observe. Outsiders see before-and-after moments, but the
middle period of repeated action is what wins. He also says in-person proximity to him
and Leila can speed behavior change because people absorb repeated operating traits.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- People underestimate timelines because time cannot be easily seen in a short clip or
  snapshot.
- Consistency is especially hard to observe because it requires seeing repeated action
  across long periods.
- Beginning and ending states are easy to photograph; the middle is usually invisible.
- The middle period of repeated behavior is where the race is won.
- Alex believes proximity to him and Leila speeds behavior change because people
  assimilate repeated traits and standards.
- He says he learned some consistency from watching his father go to work every day.
- Seeing behaviors in person can teach in a way abstract advice does not.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The middle is where the race is won."

> "The thing that you can't see is consistency."
