# What's the Meaning of Life...

## Summary
Short interview clip (Alex + an off-camera interviewer, marked `>>`). Asked for his favorite philosophical answer, Alex offers a deliberately provocative one: **the reason you don't kill yourself is the meaning of your life.** He frames it as a diagnostic — ask someone who says they don't know life's meaning "why don't you kill yourself?" and the first thing that pops into their head (mom, family) *is* the meaning. He adds that the answer can change over time and doesn't have to be one thing for life, because **meaning is entirely self-generated** — "we create meaning… you can create and destroy meaning as you see fit."

The interviewer pushes toward metaphysics (Descartes/dualism, simulation theory, whether a super-civilization controls us, UAPs/USOs). Alex declines to invest curiosity there, applying his **razor: "what does it change about my behavior?"** He puts zero effort toward the simulation question or aliens because it changes nothing about what he does — if aliens were confirmed, *then* it would change his behavior and *then* he'd be interested. He generalizes this into a three-question filter for any hard problem: **logic, evidence, utility** — "what does it mean? how do I know that? why do I care?" — with utility (why do I care / did it change how I live?) being the most important. He walks through applying it to "I'm stressed out": define the trigger, verify it's real and about you, then ask whether it actually changed anything — if not, don't care. The interviewer contrasts himself as the opposite (consumed by knowing every useless thing) and says he's inspired.
