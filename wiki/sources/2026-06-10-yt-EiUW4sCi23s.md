# Helping a $3M Restaurant Consultant Get to $10M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a restaurant-operations consulting business on track for about $3.2M that wants to reach $10M. The owner says demand exists but messaging, marketing, sales process, and a fast-cash mechanism are not working. Alex introduces a problem-solving discipline of defining terms and requiring logic, evidence, and utility; then he recommends pre-framing and indoctrination before calls, likely through a VSL and qualifying questions. He also explains good friction, fast-cash plays from existing resources, and using incentives/guarantee contingencies to force client activation.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When someone says "messaging is off," the first step is to define what that means, how they know, and why it matters.
- Alex frames problem solving as **logic, evidence, utility**: what does it mean, how do you know, and why should anyone care?
- If organic demand is high but prospects are unprepared, the business needs an indoctrination process before the call, such as a VSL.
- Questions before the sale can reverse-engineer the sale by pre-framing the buyer toward the next offer.
- A simple checkbox in a book-launch opt-in produced a 16% sales lift because it pre-framed people for a VIP offer.
- Good friction filters bad-fit prospects out and good prospects in; bad friction just frustrates everyone.
- More good friction can raise cost per call while improving ROAS and sales efficiency.
- Fast-cash plays usually use existing underutilized resources such as dead contacts, old customers, lists, or social followings.
- The goal is to make the client money quickly so the service pays for itself from "found money."
- If clients fail to take action, structure carrots and sticks: bonuses, discounts, guarantees contingent on actions, and explicit activation requirements.
