# How to Get Your First Clients With Zero Proof

## Summary

Alex Hormozi answers a question from a high-ticket lead-generation founder with no social proof. Alex says the founder must compensate for lack of proof by leading with free service, high owner involvement, and aligned incentives. The early goal is to create testimonials and proof. If the founder is good, some free clients will want to keep paying; if they do not, the testimonial can still sell later buyers who do not have the free anchor.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A marketer who cannot get customers for themselves begins with an obvious proof gap.
- Proof matters above everything when selling marketing help.
- With no track record, the founder should win on service and owner involvement.
- The founder should explicitly align incentives by saying they need testimonials and will work hard to create a win.
- Prospects are rationally hesitant because they may still need to spend ad money even if the service is free.
- The founder must get in front of more people and offer free one-on-one help.
- If the founder is good, free clients may later want to pay because the service makes them money.
- If free clients do not pay but leave testimonials, those testimonials can help sell future customers who do not have the free anchor.
