# Stop Solving Problems You Don't Have

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a $20M website-as-a-service company worried that AI will degrade its product and that cold calling will get harder. Alex says the AI threat is currently a narrative, not yet a metric, because churn has only slightly ticked up and the target SMB customers are laggards. He recommends solving the current business: double down on inbound acquisition, use quarterly prepay for new customers to offset CAC, and use AI internally to reorganize workflows and reduce headcount rather than first changing the product.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Website-as-a-service founder** - the coachee. His revenue, customer price, outbound channel, churn, EBITDA, and customer base are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Do not solve problems that do not yet exist in the business metrics.
- If AI is not showing up as escalating churn or another major business metric, the founder may be reacting to a story rather than a present constraint.
- The SMBs buying low-priced website services are often laggards; the people excited about AI tools probably were not buying this service before AI.
- The product may not need to change unless a business metric proves it is broken.
- With roughly 29-month average customer life, the acquisition side is the game.
- The founder should double down on inbound / paid ads.
- Quarterly prepay should be tested on new customers, not imposed on existing customers, to offset CAC and improve cash flow.
- The company is heavy on people and should use AI internally before worrying that customers will use AI externally.
- Alex would spend six months reorganizing workflows and using AI to reduce headcount, increase EBITDA, and fund acquisition even if the front-end offer goes negative for a quarter.
