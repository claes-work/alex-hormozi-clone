# The 8-Hour Rule That Separates Winners From Losers

## Summary

Alex argues that building anything great requires the ability to sit still, ignore
notifications, and focus on one task for long periods. He says focus is created by
removing everything else, not by forcing attention while distractions remain. His
tactics include timer-based time blocking, grayscale phone mode, turning off
notifications, blocking people during deep work, and eliminating email as a channel.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says he does not know successful entrepreneurs who cannot enter hyper-focused
  work periods.
- He says greatness is about what is required, not what someone wants.
- He rejects passion-only advice because successful people often describe their current
  delegated life rather than what they did to reach it.
- He says moving to the next level often requires sucking at new skills for a period.
- He uses timers to estimate how long tasks should take and pauses the timer whenever he
  is distracted.
- He says the timer exposes how much real time on task a person has versus how long they
  claim to work.
- He says focus is what remains after everything else is removed.
- He cites Jerry Seinfeld's no-window room/notepad practice as an example of making the
  desired work the default.
- He makes his phone less distracting through grayscale, no notifications, and modes
  where no one can get through.
- He says he does not use email with his team after hearing that Bill Clinton did not use
  email.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you can't sit still and ignore notifications and focus for a day, nothing great will get accomplished."
