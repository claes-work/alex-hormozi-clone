# The Two Channels With the Highest Return Right Now

## Summary

In response to a question about relaunching an AI-automation brand, Alex Hormozi and another speaker discuss proof, channels, and content strategy. The other speaker emphasizes leaning on existing digital-marketing proof and building new proof with live clients. Alex's attributed section says channel ROI can mislead because down-funnel assets look better, then identifies Instagram and YouTube as the strongest blended-return channels because they combine discovery with nurture. He argues short-form content is best treated as a path into deeper, higher-surface-area interactions such as long videos, podcasts, and books.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- ROI by channel is deceptive because the channel closest to the transaction often appears to have the highest return.
- Podcasts can be extremely loyal and down-funnel, but that does not mean a business should allocate only to podcasts.
- Instagram has high blended value because it supports many monetization paths: stories, posts, calls to action, DM automation, and team setters.
- Instagram's weakness is short-form persuasion; longer captions can add education and make the platform more persuasive.
- YouTube has strong discovery plus both shorts and long-form, making it one of the top blended-return channels.
- Influence grows through the number of positive interactions with a prospect and through total brand surface area.
- A single long podcast can create more time-on-brand than many shorts; shorts should pull people into longer interactions.
- Shorts still matter for facial recognition and as ads for longer content.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Think about your shorts... as ads towards getting people into the longs."
