# Helping a Coaching Business Fix 50% Churn

## Summary
In a coaching session, Alex Hormozi advises a fitness/coaching business owner whose back-end semi-private offer suffers 50% churn (roughly 30% weighted) driven by low consumption — only ~7% of members show up to calls. Alex diagnoses low consumption as the upstream cause of low renewal, and prescribes a dedicated one-on-one onboarding ramp (four to six sessions) before graduating clients into group settings, plus "book-a-meeting-from-a-meeting" (BAM FAM) scheduling and reminder sequences. The conversation opens with a separate anecdote in which Alex over-compensates six customers who flew in for a mistakenly-sold, non-existent event, illustrating his "make it more than right" service-recovery principle.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames low renewal/high churn as typically preceded by low consumption; the first fix is getting attribution/tracking in place so the real problem is visible. (Caller reported ~7% call attendance and 50% churn on both the $195/month and 4-month/$595 semi-private offers — unverified self-reported figures.)
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that when you have no data preceding a problem you just "keep circling" on hypothetical solutions; collecting data first usually makes the solution obvious.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends replacing a drop-in group onboarding with four to six one-on-one sessions before a client "graduates" into group training, which raises buy-in and puts the group offer on a pedestal so clients feel they earned it.
- As of March 2026, Alex prescribes "BAM FAM" (book a meeting from a meeting): the sales rep books the onboarding call, and each session ends with the coach booking everyone's next attendance, backed by automated reminders plus a manual reach-out.
- As of March 2026, Alex suggests an A/B close ("group or one-on-one with me/John?") and structuring the offer as a "big head, long tail" pricing system; if selling a six-pack of one-on-ones, gift one or two extra so the client doesn't feel the group offer was downgraded.
- As of March 2026, Alex reiterates his service-recovery view that you can't merely "make it right" — you must "make it more than right," citing a Disney stat that it takes 37 magical moments to overcome one tragic moment, and Leila's point that over-compensated wronged customers become your biggest ambassadors.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You can't just make it right. You have to make it more than right in order to actually make it right."

> "It takes 37 magical moments to overcome one tragic moment. And so the moral of that statistic is not, 'Let's do 37 magic moments.' It's avoid the tragic moments if at all possible."

> "Believe it or not, the people that you wrong and then super compensate to make it super right become your biggest ambassadors."

> "When you don't know what to do, get data first and then it makes the path easy."

> "Nine times out of 10 the entrepreneur has a problem, but doesn't collect any data that precedes the problem. And so then the first step is to just go collect the data. And once you have the data, typically the solutions become obvious."

> "If you take people and just toss them into the group, it's much harder than having kind of a more dedicated onboarding experience."

> "The group sessions are now on a pedestal. It's like, 'You're not ready for that yet.' Right? It's like you got to earn that. And all of a sudden it becomes the prize."

> "So book a meeting from a meeting, right? And so the sales guy should obviously book to the onboarding call."
