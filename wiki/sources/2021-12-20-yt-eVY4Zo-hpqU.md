# How I Put Out So Much Content Despite Being Part-Time

## Summary

This is a group-call excerpt with Leila and Alex answering a fitness entrepreneur's content question. Leila observes Alex's content habits, then Alex gives his own rules: make content when the thought is fresh or emotional, speak only from lived experience, know exactly who the audience is, and avoid declarative claims outside your domain authority.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2021-12-20)

- Alex says he makes videos either to document a thought process for himself or to anonymize a lesson from a client, employee, or portfolio company conversation.
- He says creators should assume no one will see the content and make it anyway as a long game.
- He says the messenger is part of the message; people ask why they should listen to you.
- He says content should be tied to things the creator has actually done or directly experienced.
- He says if the source of one's content is only other people's content, do not make it.
- He says inexperienced creators should use client stories and lived examples rather than broad declarative claims.
- He says creators should not say anything if they do not have anything worth saying.
