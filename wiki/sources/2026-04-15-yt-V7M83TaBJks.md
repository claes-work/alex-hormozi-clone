# Helping a $5.2M Service Business Scale to $20M

## Summary

Alex coaches a structural-engineering services firm with lumpy, recurring commercial work.
He recommends fully loaded CAC over a longer time horizon and conservative LTV math. The
principle is to overestimate CAC, underestimate LTV, and still demand that the numbers work.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-15, Alex says marketing and sales spend used to nurture existing customers
  can still be included in blended CAC.
- He says lumpy businesses should expand the measurement window, such as trailing six or
  trailing twelve months, because longer horizons smooth volatility.
- He recommends fully loaded CAC, including commissions, software, media spend, nurture,
  and sales costs.
- He recommends underestimating LTV by using conservative cohort math, such as total
  revenue divided by total customers acquired, even though newer customers have not had
  time to mature.
- He says if the conservative version still works, the business is in a safety zone.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "CAC's CAC."

> "The further out you blend it, the more accurate it'll be."

> "I want to overestimate CAC and I want to underestimate LTV."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: fully loaded CAC including nurture; smooth lumpy CAC over 6-12
months; conservative LTV underestimation as safety-zone math.
