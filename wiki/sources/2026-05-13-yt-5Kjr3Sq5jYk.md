# I Worked 12-Hour Days for 2 Years and Made $30K

## Summary

A Slovenia-based agency owner describes working long days for two years while reaching
only about $30,000 in annual revenue. Alex diagnoses a market and offer mismatch: the
caller is selling content services locally at low prices while trying to serve more than
one niche. Alex advises him to use his own content process as the product demonstration,
translate or dub it for an English-speaking audience, focus on realtors, and package a
specific lead magnet around making many pieces of content quickly.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-13)

- Alex says the caller's Slovenian price point and delivery effort create a poor
  opportunity-cost tradeoff.
- He advises moving the long-term target market to the United States, where the same
  perceived service can justify much higher prices.
- He recommends narrowing to realtors rather than splitting attention across realtors
  and home-improvement businesses.
- He says the caller should make content that teaches exactly how he creates content for
  clients, turning delivery knowledge into marketing.
- He suggests translating or dubbing Slovenian content into English and reposting it to
  U.S.-oriented pages.
- He frames a tactical lead magnet around a specific promise such as creating 100 days of
  content in 100 minutes.
- He tells the caller to "add a zero" when moving from local Slovenian prices to a U.S.
  market, as a rough positioning heuristic rather than a verified pricing benchmark.

## Notable verbatim quotes
