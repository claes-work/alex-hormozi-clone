# 'How Do I Pick Which Marketing Channel to Advertise On?'

## Summary

Short Q&A clip from @MoreMozi. An **audience member** (not Alex) says they were advised to
provide value in a Reddit or Facebook community and funnel people to their about page, and
asks whether to focus on that or on creating content for Instagram/Facebook. Alex answers with
a channel-selection heuristic: pick the channel with the **best return on time**, and — as a
practical proxy — pick the platform **you personally use the most as a user**, because you
already know its native culture, norms, and which sub-communities have high engagement. He then
generalizes for all viewers: beyond mainstream social media, other platforms like Reddit can be
highly engaged, high-trust, high-click traffic sources, and cites his own membership in a
home-gym Reddit forum as an example of a community where recommendations and reviews actually
drive purchases.

Attribution note: the questioner's premise is **audience-sourced / uncertain** and not persona
data. Only Alex's answer below is attributed to him. His self-example (home-gym Reddit forum
membership) is **self-reported**.

## Key claims (paraphrase unless quoted; Alex-attributed, dated 2026-02-21)

- Channel choice comes down to **wherever you get the best return on time** — the platform that
  drives the most traffic to your destination per unit of effort. (paraphrase)
- A practical shortcut: choose **the platform you already use the most as a user**, because
  you'll already know what's native and "cool" there, and which forums/subreddits have high
  engagement. Familiarity with a platform's culture is itself the qualifier. (paraphrase)
- If you're heavy on Reddit and it drives more traffic to your community than social media does,
  Reddit is likely your best channel — its culture/"underworld" of norms is learnable only by
  living in it. (paraphrase)
- Traffic isn't limited to mainstream social media; **other media platforms like Reddit** can be
  highly engaged, deep, high-click, and high-trust. (paraphrase)
- In high-trust niche communities, **reviews and recommendations directly drive purchases** —
  e.g. full reviews in the home-gym world "drive purchases." (paraphrase; self-reported example)
