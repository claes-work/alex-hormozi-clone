# Helping a $1.5M/Year Consulting Business Get to $5M

## Summary
In this short coaching exchange, a cloud-computing consultant explains that he builds highly customized cloud solutions for clients (a long, time-and-material, founder-dependent process) and wants to sell a higher-margin managed service on top. He asks whether to first scale the custom-build side or skip straight to selling the managed service. Alex Hormozi frames the business as a "big head, long tail" front-end/back-end model and recommends a hybrid: sell a smaller, faster upfront engagement (e.g. a quick assessment or improvement patch) that both delivers value and sets up the sticky managed-service back end.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex reads the caller's setup as a classic front-end/back-end (a customized upfront build followed by a natural managed-service upsell) and expects the managed-services side to carry very high gross margins.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the bottleneck is the long, founder-dependent front-end build, and suggests replacing the full 1.5-year build with a smaller upfront offer — for example finding a ~20% improvement to a client's existing cloud infrastructure — that is faster to sell and staff.
- As of March 2026, Alex reasons that a smaller upfront engagement doubles as the system assessment the consultant would have to do anyway before managing the client's infrastructure, and increases back-end stickiness — getting him "the 80/20" of the desired outcome.
- The caller states (unverified, self-reported) that the business makes ~$1.5M/year and his goal is $5M; that the custom build takes ~1.5 years and is time-and-material and dependent on him as the "trusted advisor"; and that demand outstrips his ability to hire because there is no talent pipeline for the niche skill.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "To me this looks very much like a front and back end ... like you build the very customized thing and then the natural solution is like, hey, you just bought this one-and-a-half-year build, don't you want to make sure that I make sure it keeps running. I feel like everybody will say yes to that, and you probably have tremendous gross margins on the managed services side."

> "I'll bet you if you were to look at someone's cloud solution that they currently have, you could probably come up with like a 20% improvement that would have a disproportionate upgrade for their infrastructure."

> "It's almost like getting paid to do an assessment that you'd have to do anyways in order to do the managed services."

> "Even if you can just magic wand a little bit on the front end — I'm just being real — it'll probably help with the stickiness on the back end."
