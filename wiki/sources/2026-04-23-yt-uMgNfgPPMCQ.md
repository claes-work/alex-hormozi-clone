# Helping a $1.7M Medical Clinic Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches an owner in a ketamine-clinic business where paid ads are constrained. He
advises finding referral/distribution nodes and enlisting them deliberately, rather than
advertising the restricted treatment directly. He also warns the owner not to dilute a
high-margin, growing core business with too many additional services.

## Key claims

- Alex says that if direct ads for a regulated offer are constrained, the business should
  seek distribution partners or referral nodes.
- He says word of mouth is node-based: a small number of customers or partners create
  disproportionate referrals.
- He recommends making referral or affiliate economics the advertised/compliant object,
  while partners use their own networks to drive demand.
- He says adding five or six services to a high-margin ketamine business sounds like a
  classic newbie 80/20 error.
- He recommends focusing on the main thing and solving current problems rather than
  chasing missed opportunities.
- He says regulatory risk is likely slower and less immediate than the distraction risk
  from over-expansion.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Distraction is going to kill the business significantly faster than the FDA will."

> "Stay with the main thing."
