# Experts Without Influence Are Just Authorities

## Summary

Alex answers whether to partner with an expert who has a working course but no audience.
He says it is difficult because a Skool/community business needs both the content inside
and the advertising outside. Ideally one partner brings both. If forced to choose, Alex
would generally prefer traffic over authority because access to an audience may be enough
and the operator can find something valuable for that person to provide.

## Key claims

- A community business needs both the inside value and the outside advertising engine.
- An expert without influence may overestimate what they bring because they lack business
  experience.
- Alex distinguishes authority from influence: expertise alone does not bring traffic.
- If choosing between traffic with less authority and authority without traffic, Alex
  would often prefer traffic.
- In some communities, access to a high-traffic person can be enough value by itself.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Experts without influence are just authorities."

> "Ideally, one partner can bring both."

> "Sometimes, when somebody who has a lot of traffic, just access is enough."
