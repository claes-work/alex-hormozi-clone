# 5 Things that BILLIONAIRES think about that MILLIONAIRES don't

## Summary
Reframing a viewer's question about millionaires-vs-poor, Hormozi lists five things billionaires prioritize that millionaires do not, drawing on Blackstone founder Stephen Schwarzman and Grant Cardone. His through-line: think in long time horizons, because every other trait flows from that. He argues many millionaires have "terrible beliefs" and that learning to promote is enough to become a millionaire but not a billionaire.

## The 5 things (exact list)
1. **Size of the opportunity** — billionaires weigh total addressable market / potential scale; millionaires chase small "table stakes" opportunities with little growth potential.
2. **Product over promotion** — billionaires ask "will people keep buying this over and over"; millionaires over-index on promotion because that's how they made their first money.
3. **Army of smarter people, not the "genius with a thousand slaves"** — billionaires hire 10-out-of-10 talent; Schwarzman's second criterion is "can I find 10 out of 10 who want to pursue this opportunity."
4. **Time horizons** — billionaires see time as an asset that compounds and works for them; millionaires think week-to-week (payroll, rent, the next 90 days).
5. **Brand / reputation** — billionaires know "you only get one name," and brand compounds with integrous, long-term decisions.

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated 2021)
- Following Grant Cardone: don't listen to millionaires, listen to billionaires, because many millionaires have terrible beliefs (2021).
- Niching down when starting is not stupid — many huge businesses started niche — but only if the niche can scale to something enormous (2021).
- Schwarzman (worth ~$30B) reportedly said it's harder to start a small business than to start Blackstone, because big talent won't pursue a small opportunity (2021).
- Big goals aren't just for the founder — they let everyone around you stomach the trials, because they believe in a compelling vision and the compensation (2021).
- His talent scale: a 10 = best in the world; 9 = best you can attract; 8 = very good, takes initiative, outsize returns; 7 = functional but needs direction; 6 and below = don't want them; most businesses are run by sixes-and-below (2021).
- "I prefer to play games where if you wait you win" — success as an inevitable consequence of time; he'd rather get rich slowly-but-surely than gamble on the next 90 days (2021).
- Brand = reputation; most people have a so-so brand because they sacrifice integrity for the short dollar; the top of the mountain is sparsely populated and those people seek out others who see the world the same way (2021).
- Small goals and big goals are "equally hard" (crediting Schwarzman), so you might as well pursue the big one; arguably it takes less time to make a billion a year than to grind for $100k (2021).
- The single biggest takeaway is time horizon — every other trait rolls into it (2021).
