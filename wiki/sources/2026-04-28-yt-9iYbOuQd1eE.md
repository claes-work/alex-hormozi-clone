# Helping a $3M Dental Practice Scale

## Summary

Alex gives a dental practice tactics to reduce appointment no-shows. He says the issue is
probably not the practice's main constraint, but recommends an integrity tie-down after
choosing a time, pulling appointments sooner when possible, and layering manual reminders
on top of automated ones. The manual reminders should happen at 24 hours, morning-of, and
60-90 minutes before the appointment. He also recommends a personalized "goody bag" or
choice that makes the patient feel the office has prepared something for them.

## Key claims

- Alex says the dental cancellation problem is not necessarily the primary constraint of
  the business.
- An integrity tie-down asks whether anything could possibly prevent the person from
  showing up after they choose a time.
- Bringing appointments closer can improve show rates.
- Automated reminders can handle far-out reminders, but manual reminders should happen
  closer to the appointment.
- Alex recommends manual iPhone-style texts 24 hours before, morning-of, and 60-90
  minutes before.
- A personalized item or choice can create reciprocity by making the patient feel the
  business incurred cost for them.
- Far-out calls and confirmations are weaker because the appointment date feels like a
  different universe to the patient.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Is there anything that will possibly get in the way of you showing up?"

> "You're reminding too far out."

> "I will bet you dollars to donuts that that on its own would work."
