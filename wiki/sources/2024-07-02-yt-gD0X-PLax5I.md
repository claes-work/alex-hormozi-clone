# You're Not Behind: My System for Outworking Everyone

## Summary

A live solo presentation in which Alex Hormozi argues that most business owners
who claim to "work hard" are not actually working, because they conflate effort
or hours-on-clock with real output. He redefines work as `output = volume ×
leverage`, insists a business only grows via four levers (more traffic, higher
conversion, higher prices, more purchases), and says the growth-relevant inputs
should occupy ~4 hours a day of focused activity. The second half is a tactical
system for creating that time: separating "maker" from "manager" time, protecting
long uninterrupted blocks, and buying back ~96 hours/month of chores for ~$1,500
so that time can be reinvested into revenue-producing work.

## Key claims (his work / productivity system)

- **Work is not effort, hours, or physics.** Rejecting "work = force × distance,"
  time-on-clock, and "how hard you try" as useful definitions for knowledge
  workers. Effort/hours are participation trophies; only output counts.
- **Work = output; output = volume × leverage.** Volume = number of times you do
  something; leverage = how much you get out of each time. To work faster:
  `(volume × leverage) / time` = output per unit of time.
- He normally teaches **leverage**, but here focuses on **volume**, because after
  talking to many businesses he found most owners simply do not put in the volume
  he had assumed they did.
- **Only four levers grow a business:** get more customers (more traffic OR higher
  conversion) or make customers worth more (raise prices OR increase purchase
  frequency / decrease churn). Everything you do must "ladder up" to one of these
  four; if your schedule doesn't, you're not doing money-making work.
- Small businesses especially should skew disproportionately toward **traffic
  (advertise more/better) and conversion** — without customers there's no one to
  raise prices on or resell.
- **"Advertise better by advertising more."** Excellence comes from repetition;
  volume is even used as a proxy for skill (e.g. "4,000 sales consults"). Commit
  to ~4 hours/day of the chosen advertising activity (ads, outreach, content, or
  affiliates) so it becomes "unreasonable for you to suck."
- **Strategy = prioritization; let some fires burn.** You can neglect non-limiting
  problems for a season; when a problem becomes the true constraint, attack it 4
  hours/day until solved, then train someone else on the mastered skill.
- **All problems are solvable with skills; all skills come from repetition +
  feedback.** Once acquired, a skill takes less time (leverage) — you shift from
  growing a skill to the easier task of maintaining it (analogy: maintaining
  weight/muscle vs. gaining it).
- **Maker time vs. manager time.** Maker time = 4–6h uninterrupted blocks, low
  urgency / high importance, ~14 per week, very valuable; "a productive maker
  calendar is an empty calendar." Manager time = 5–15 min slots (100+/week) for
  training, coordinating, firefighting; goal is to fill it with no gaps. Disaster
  happens when the two mix.
- **His maker habits:** pick the single most important project (can repeat for a
  while); up at 4am, work until 9am (5 hours); notifications off; dark windowless
  room, earplugs; treat weekends like weekdays; consistent sleep time; recognize
  "there are no emergencies."
- **Buy back time.** Food ~50h/mo (~$800), cleaning ~25h/mo (~$500), laundry
  ~16h/mo (~$200) → ~$1,500/mo buys back ~96 hours/mo (two work-weeks). If your
  time is worth more than ~$15/hr, make the trade — but reinvest the reclaimed
  time into revenue work, not Netflix.
- **Productivity comes from elimination, not optimization hacks.** "You have one
  battery"; spouse, kids, chores, employees all drain the same source. Cutting
  drains yields permanent energy reallocation.
- **The 30x claim:** 96 productive hours vs. 3 real productive hours/week = ~30x
  faster movement, even though both people "work all day."
- Buying time is a **one-time trigger** (you can't re-spend the $1,500 for another
  96 hours), and it's a form of reinvesting in your most valuable employee: you.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "Work equals outputs and outputs equal volume times leverage... the number of
  times you do something times how much you get out of each time you do it."
- "If you want to get unreasonably good at advertising, do so much advertising
  that it would be unreasonable for you to suck after doing that much of it."
- "You have to learn how to let some fires burn. Some problems are problems but
  they [are] not the problem of the business today."
- "All problems are solvable with skills, and all skills are attainable [through]
  repetition and feedback."
- "A productive maker calendar is an empty calendar. An interruption always
  destroys an entire time block."
- "There are no emergencies. There's 911, someone's dying, in which case call them
  not me — and then there's the emergency that your business isn't going to grow
  for the third year in a row, which you need to solve now."
- "You will become more productive through elimination than by adding
  optimization."
- "We all have 24 hours a day... people who move faster just do better things with
  their time. You can cut grass with scissors and it will take all day, or you can
  make one call and have someone else do it."
- "I don't have the time — find the [expletive] time, buy it. It's right there,
  it's for sale."
- "The secret is that there is no secret. You have one battery, and you just try
  and eliminate as many drains as you can that aren't aligned with your goal."
