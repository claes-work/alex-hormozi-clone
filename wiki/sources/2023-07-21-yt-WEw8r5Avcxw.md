# Alex Hormozi: Stop Being So Angry All The Time

## Summary

In this interview clip, the host asks Alex how he moved past being driven by a "chip on
his shoulder" toward his father. Alex recounts the escalating goal ladder — make as much
as his dad, then more, then more than his dad ever made — and a pivotal cold call in which
his father apologized "for everything," to which Alex responded coldly. He describes
realizing that chasing his father's approval meant he was winning someone else's game
(money as the measure of a person's worth), and that maturing meant defining his own game
and rules for what matters most.

## Key claims

- Alex's motivation toward his father escalated in stages: make as much as his dad → make
  more than his dad → make more than his dad had ever made.
- After leaving home to pursue the gym business, Alex and his father barely spoke for
  roughly five years — brief texts and short calls.
- Alex says that around age 27, once the gym launch business was "printing money," he was
  taking home about 1.5 million dollars a month (self-reported).
- His father cold-called him out of the blue and apologized "for everything"; Alex says he
  felt nothing in the moment, which he found curious.
- Instead of accepting the apology as an olive branch, Alex told his father he would never
  thank him from a stage because his father hadn't been there and hadn't believed in him;
  his father replied, "well, we'll see how long it lasts."
- Alex frames his upbringing as money-driven — common, he says, in many foreign families —
  where a person's worth and their name were immediately tied to how much they made.
- His breakthrough: he had stopped blaming his father but was still playing his father's
  game, so he was winning someone else's game rather than his own.
- Defining your own game means taking on more responsibility, because you must set the
  rules of what matters most to you.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "First the goal was make as much as my dad, then it was make more than my dad, then it was make more than my dad had ever made."
- "I realized that the approval that I had, that I saw, it was always going to be moved."
- "He says, hey, you're gonna want to sit down for this one. I was like, okay. He's like, I'm sorry. I was like, about what? He was like, everything."
- "I remember in the moment actually feeling nothing, and thinking that was curious."
- "Because you weren't, and you didn't believe in me."
- "It was after that phone call that I realized that everything that I'd done to that point was to try and beat him at his game."
- "I don't blame my dad anymore, but I'm still playing his game. And so I'm winning not my game, I'm winning someone else's."
- "I have to define the game and the meaning of the game that I want to play. I have more responsibility now because I have to define the rules of what matters most to me."
- "Whenever he introduced somebody you'd tell me how much they made immediately... it was just like the worth and the name was like immediately tied together."
