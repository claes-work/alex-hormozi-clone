# How I Learned to Market and Sell

## Summary
In this conversation, Hormozi recounts how he learned selling from watching gym-owner Sam Bakhtiar frame hard requests in a light, non-accusatory way, and folds that into a broader walkthrough of his phone-sales and objection-handling method: setting the frame, "childlike curiosity," selling from the "back foot," and agreeing with objections rather than fighting them. He and his interlocutor trade specific scripts drawn from his gym-days weight-loss sales calls. The framing is autobiographical, describing habits he built from age 14 in direct sales through his early gym career.

## Key claims
- Self-reported (as of 2026-03-26, recounting earlier career): He learned selling from Sam Bakhtiar, the first person he says he mentored, whom he calls "wicked" and "ghetto fabulous"; he did not teach him directly — Hormozi says he learned by watching.
- Self-reported (recounting move to California): To get into gyms, he quit a white-collar job that paid well and worked for minimum wage at Sam's gym for three months before starting his own job; his Persian father was horrified.
- Self-reported: He watched Sam soften a harsh request to a client (put your weights back) by framing it lightly and non-accusatorially, and applied that framing "to every single sale my whole life afterwards."
- Self-reported: He started direct sales at age 14 and from then on always set a frame so prospects would "know how to treat me."
- Paraphrase (belief, dated 2026-03-26): Overcoming an objection is "a dance, not a fight" — the goal is zero aggression and "childlike curiosity" ("Huh, that's wild. Tell me more").
- Paraphrase (belief): You should agree with the prospect's stated obstacles (expensive, hard) and then say that is still a great reason to buy — like Eminem owning his own negatives in a rap battle.
- Paraphrase (belief): In high-pressure moments make no statements and no commands, only questions, because "you can't disagree with someone who asks you a question."
- Paraphrase (belief): Sell "from your back foot" — never leaning in; signal "if you want it, I'm here" and be willing to exit.
- Paraphrase (belief): If you don't close a sale, it means you didn't understand the prospect enough; a God-level knowledge of the prospect would let you close them. Curiosity is "probably the number one thing I teach our guys."
- Self-reported (technique): On internet-lead callbacks he used disarming humor, an award/social-proof question ("did you hear about us on Internet's Best Boot Camps?"), and made the prospect state their own buying criteria so they "tell me how to sell them."
- Paraphrase (belief): He warns against "commission breath" — adrenaline-driven neediness that makes prospects go defensive; role-play reps remove that adrenaline so you can be present.
