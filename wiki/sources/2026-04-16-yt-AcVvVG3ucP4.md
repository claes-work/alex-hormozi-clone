# Helping a $1M Cold Email Agency Get to $10M

## Summary

Alex diagnoses a cold-email agency's churn as likely structural. The agency sells to other
agencies that either cannot close the leads or are at capacity after closing them. Alex
says the model may be right but the customer is too small: volatile customers pass their
volatility upstream. The alternatives are to go upmarket or rebuild the whole model as a
very low-cost leader.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-16, Alex says high churn can be structural when the customer's own industry
  or business size is volatile.
- He gives a CRM-for-gyms example: even a sticky CRM can churn if a third of customer gyms
  go out of business each year.
- He says large agencies like Ogilvy, NP Digital, and Vayner work because they serve large
  enterprises that can pay, close, keep contracts, and have process.
- He says the agency may not be in the wrong business but may be serving the wrong customer.
- He says if an intangible skill like sales ability is required, the company can test for
  it or move upmarket to a size where the capability must exist.
- He says adding guarantees or onboarding will not fix a volatile customer base.
- He says serving the low end can work only if the whole business is built as a very
  low-cost tech-enabled service or software.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're not in the wrong business, you might just be serving the wrong customer."

> "You have a business model that is better served to a bigger customer."

> "That volatility will not change."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: structural churn from volatile small customers; go upmarket or
low-cost-leader; test for required customer capability vs set revenue bar.
