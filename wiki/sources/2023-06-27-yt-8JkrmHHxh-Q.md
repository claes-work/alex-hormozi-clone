# Alex Hormozi Started Lifting Weights at 19 (@ACQofficial, 2023-06-27)

## Summary

Short interview clip in which Alex Hormozi recounts the origin of his weightlifting
habit as a teenager. He tells the story of a high-school teacher, "Mr Givens," who
noticed him in the hallway, told him he had the build for it, and then trained with him
roughly two hours a day for about nine months. Alex describes Givens's homemade
exercise vocabulary (invented names, quizzing him on trivia mid-set) and one brutal
ab routine he still remembers as the most sore he has ever been. The clip is anecdotal
and nostalgic rather than instructional.

## Key claims

- A high-school teacher ("Mr Givens") first pulled Alex into serious training after
  spotting him in the hallway and telling him he had the genetics/build for it (self-reported).
- Givens trained with Alex about two hours every day for roughly nine months (self-reported).
- By the time Givens took him on, Alex says he had *already* been training for "a year
  and a year and change" on his own (self-reported).
- Givens used improvised, non-standard names for exercises (e.g. "I don't knows,"
  "three Huggers," "Givens UPS") and would quiz Alex on trivia (e.g. "what's the capital
  of Zimbabwe") mid-exercise as a distraction/teaching device (self-reported).
- "Givens UPS" was a progressive ab drop-set: start flat on an incline, bump the incline
  one degree every ~four weeks; sets of straight-leg raises and knee-to-chest rolls,
  roughly 10/10/10 for three sets. Alex calls it the most sore he has ever been
  (self-reported).
