# Don't Quit Until the New Thing Matches Your Income

## Summary

Alex advises a VP of Sales building a coaching community not to frame the decision as
head versus heart. He says the issue is conflicting priorities and risk tolerance. His
practical recommendation is to keep the stable job until the community produces matching
income for at least a quarter, maybe six months, while reinvesting job income into the
community and learning to work under constraints.

## Key claims

- Alex rejects "head versus heart" language because it confuses biology with decision-
  making.
- The real issue is conflicting priorities, risk tolerance, and deciding what matters
  more.
- Alex likes stability because it can keep the new business fun instead of turning it into
  immediate survival work.
- He would not quit the job until the community consistently matches the job income for a
  quarter or possibly six months.
- The founder should live cheaply and use job income to buy learning, ads, software,
  courses, or communities that help the new business grow.
- Working both roles teaches constraint and ensures the founder does not quit only to
  waste the freed-up time.
- If the community matches income with less time, quitting can allow it to double or
  triple after the trade.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have conflicting priorities."

> "I like having stability."

> "Do both. It's an and."
