# Founder Has No Job (Business Prints $500K)

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the founder of a national 5v5 soccer tournament business doing about $1.5M with strong margins and an operator already in place. Alex identifies the constraint as sales manpower: the company has clubs and leads, but the president is also the sales bottleneck. Alex tells the founder to spend his freed-up time building a recruiting channel for salespeople.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - founder of a 5v5 soccer tournament company. His revenue, margins, growth rate, operator, and sales process are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If the business can operate and grow while the founder is away for weeks, the founder should move to the next highest constraint.
- The core input in this model is local clubs that host tournaments and bring teams.
- The company has enough lead flow and market; the bottleneck is conversion capacity.
- If the president can only handle a limited number of sales calls, sales manpower is constraining growth.
- The business needs an acquisition channel for salespeople, including career path, recruiting, compensation, onboarding, and management.
- If five times the salespeople would produce materially more growth and fulfillment can handle it, the founder's job should become sales recruiting.
