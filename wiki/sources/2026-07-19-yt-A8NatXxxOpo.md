# My Funnel Stopped Working. Do I Fix It or Replace It?

## Summary

In this coaching exchange, a caller named Brian Decker describes an investing-education
community ("The Wealth Society," on Skool) that teaches high-income professionals to
restructure their stock portfolios around the AI/robotics "super cycle." He launched it
18 months earlier using a purely organic funnel: reels pushed viewers into a weekly live
webinar, which closed directly into the paid community at a $6,500 price point. Early on
a single reel could fill 500 attendees; now the same motion draws roughly 150 per week,
though close rates are steady. The business has done $3.2M in revenue and has never run
paid ads. His question is whether to keep optimizing the existing funnel or move to a new
channel.

Alex Hormozi's diagnosis is that the funnel itself is not broken and does not need much
optimization — show rates are strong (about 50%) and back-end close rates on booked calls
(50–60%) are good. The one soft metric is the ~10% webinar-to-call booking rate (he says
a good list typically yields ~20%), which he attributes not to a conversion problem but to
the "mish mash" lead quality inherent in organic traffic. His first concrete fix is to
score/qualify leads at opt-in (e.g. capture whether registrants are actually high-income
earners) so the caller can see what share of attendees are genuinely "sellable," which he
says stabilizes close rates and stops the volatility that makes one-to-many sellers "go
insane."

His larger point is that scaling from $3.2M to $10M is a top-of-funnel volume problem
solved by paid ads, not funnel repair. He explains the post-"Andromeda"-update shift in
Meta and other platforms toward hyper-personalized delivery: advertising is no longer
"boiling the ocean" but "boiling a hundred small ponds." This rewards heavy audience
callouts — literal ("are you a doctor?") or visual (a white lab coat, an old lady in the
creative), because the platform's AI reads the imagery and serves each ad to the matching
audience. The trade-off is enormous creative volume — a hundred ponds means a hundred times
the ads — which he says produces the best results one can get while making the advertiser
"lose your mind." He advises running ads straight to the webinar (as long as its promise is
compelling), split-testing the promise, and building fully personalized funnels/webinars
per avatar because "green all the way through" converts better than generic.

- (2026-07-19) Typical funnel math cited by the caller: ~400 register, ~200 show up, ~10% book a call with a closer, and closers convert 50–60% of those calls. _(attribution: audience — caller)_
- (2026-07-19) The existing funnel does not need much optimization: show rates (~50%) are good and back-end close rates (50–60%) make sense; only the ~10% webinar-to-call booking rate is a little low.

- (2026-07-19) Leads should be scored/qualified at opt-in (e.g. whether a registrant is actually a high-income earner), because it reveals whether a low headline close rate is really a high close rate on a small qualified subset.
- (2026-07-19) In one-to-many selling you must "qualify the room"; knowing what percentage of accounts are marketing-qualified/sellable makes close rates significantly more stable and removes the volatility that makes sellers feel like they are going crazy.

- (2026-07-19) Audience callouts win under this model — literal ("are you a doctor?") or visual, because the platform's AI reads background and on-screen persons and serves creative to matching audiences (e.g. white lab coats convert with white-lab-coat viewers; an old lady in the ad gets served to old ladies).

- (2026-07-19) The more personalized the funnel/webinar (e.g. a dedicated webinar for dentists/doctors/healthcare), the better it converts; a funnel that is one "flavor" all the way through converts better than one that starts personalized and then goes generic — it is just more work.
- (2026-07-19) The caller references advice from "Dion" to "call your people out" (call out doctors, etc.); Alex frames this callout advice within the Andromeda/personalization thesis. _(attribution: audience — caller, citing Dion)_
