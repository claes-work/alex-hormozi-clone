# Repost Your Best Content

## Summary

In this short teaching clip, Alex Hormozi tells creators to find their best-performing content from the previous year and post it again. His reasoning is that consistent posting brings new audience members who never saw the original hit. Reintroducing "greatest hits" lets the lore compound, and people often need reminders more than new lessons.

## Key claims (Alex's teaching only)

- Creators should look at their best piece of content from the prior year and repost it.
- If the creator has posted consistently, new people have entered the audience and have not seen the original hit.
- The best old content should be reintroduced so new followers learn the creator's "greatest hits."
- Reposting strong ideas helps the lore compound and grow.
- People often need to be reminded more than they need to be taught.
- Timeless quotes from ancient thinkers still circulate because reminders remain useful even when the source is not making new content.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Just post it again."
