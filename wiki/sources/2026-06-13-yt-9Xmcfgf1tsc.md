# How to Triple Profit Without Growing Revenue

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a residential plumbing and air-conditioning owner pacing around $15M who wants to reach $40M but is worried that profit is not rising with revenue. Alex suggests a different annual target: hold revenue flat and triple bottom line. He diagnoses likely data gaps, sales-motion/pricing opportunities, and talent/infrastructure sequencing, arguing that the business should improve real-time margin visibility and cash flow before pushing more top-line growth.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - residential plumbing and air-conditioning owner. His revenue, margins, leadership hires, growth pace, ownership, and goals are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A business can choose to stop increasing top line and focus on tripling bottom line instead.
- Real-time gross and operating margins by product line can reveal where EBITDA can be improved.
- Industry averages should not define the target margin.
- A home-service business at roughly 13-16% net margin likely has room to improve toward 30%+.
- The likely improvement sequence is sales motion, then pricing/packaging, then cash flow, then talent, then infrastructure.
- Fast growth with insufficient data and talent can create chaos and mistakes.
- If the owner wants a cash-flowing lifestyle business rather than an exit, EBITDA quality may matter more than revenue size.
