# Should You Sell or Scale to $10M?

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the same boat-membership operator covered in [[2026-06-21-yt-1TPCKPF-1Ag]]. The clip repeats the core diagnosis: the business has strong economics, the caller's lack of love for boats does not automatically mean he should sell, and the growth path is to spend more intelligently on ads, improve creative and targeting, tighten attribution, improve the referral system, and apply basic conversion-rate optimization.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible, but heavily duplicates [[2026-06-21-yt-1TPCKPF-1Ag]].
- **Coachee** - boat-membership operator. His revenue, debt, margins, licensing structure, lead costs, close rates, and contract terms are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The clip reinforces that passion for the product category is not required if the business is a vehicle for excellence and skill-building.
- The boat-membership economics are attractive because acquisition cost is low relative to upfront and recurring revenue.
- The debt burden explains the caller's emotional pressure, but it does not make the underlying business unattractive.
- The licensing fee described in the call is not treated as the key constraint.
- Alex rejects industry-standard conversion rates as a useful benchmark.
- The recommended growth path is more ad spend, better creative, better keyword targeting, referral-system improvement, attribution, and CRO.
- The caller's reported close rates suggest the offer and sales motion are not the immediate broken pieces.
