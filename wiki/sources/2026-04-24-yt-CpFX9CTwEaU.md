# Hire People Who Are Smarter Than You

## Summary

Alex names Sharran Srivatsaa, Acquisition.com's president, as someone who takes him to
school. He says Sharran is strong in data pipelines, infrastructure, real-time dashboards,
finance, tax, entity structure, and enterprise-value structuring. Alex contrasts that with
his own core skill set: making people aware, getting them to pay, making sure they like the
product, and getting them to tell friends. He says true A+ talent behaves as a partner and
is "clouds to dirt," able to operate from high-level strategy down to frontline execution.

## Key claims

- Alex says Sharran Srivatsaa teaches him and gets better as the company gets bigger.
- He describes Sharran's strengths as data pipelines, infrastructure, dashboards, finance,
  tax ramifications, entity structure, and enterprise-value structuring.
- Alex describes his own core acumen as awareness, sales, product/customer satisfaction,
  and referrals.
- He says much of his people/talent thinking relays Leila's operating philosophy.
- He says true A+ talent are partners, not employees, and see themselves that way.
- He defines a thought partner as someone whose advice he seeks on complex problems.
- His litmus for C-level executives: does he want to talk to this person about the complex
  problem?
- He says exceptional leaders are "clouds to dirt" / full-stack: able to work from
  strategy down to the frontline job.
- Example: the best sales leader can think about sales strategy and messaging, but can also
  make calls, book appointments, cut onboarding time, and understand rep training.
