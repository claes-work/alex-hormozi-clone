# The best way to invest your money...nobody will tell you (2021-06-21)

> ⚠️ **Not financial advice; self-reported, US-specific, illustrative (as of Jun 2021).**
> Return figures and thresholds below are Hormozi's own rules of thumb, rounded and unverified.

## Summary

Hormozi answers "when should an entrepreneur take money out and start investing?" by arguing
that for most people the **best investment is yourself and your income**, not the markets —
because the opportunity cost of the *attention* active investing consumes will drag down your
main income more than the extra return is worth. His prescribed order: (1) invest in your own
skills, (2) regularly pull dividends out of the business as downside protection and park them
in **truly passive** index funds, and (3) only shift focus to managing wealth once ~10% of
your net worth's growth exceeds your annual income.

## Key claims (2021, paraphrase)

- (2021) **Take dividends out of the business regularly** as it grows — this is downside-risk
  protection, not upside. He cites that ~90–95% of businesses fail within five years and only
  ~1% ever sell, so don't stack chips on a "91-to-1 bet"; take chips off the table. *(self-reported stats.)*
- (2021) **Judge investing by opportunity cost of attention.** Passive index investing (e.g.
  S&P 500) returns ~10%/yr; active investing might add ~5–10% (a 50–100% improvement on the
  return *of the money*) — but on a $1M portfolio that's only ~$50–100K, while active
  investing eats ~half your mindspace and can cost you far more in lost income (e.g. growing a
  business from $500K to $750K–$1M/yr). *(self-reported figures.)*
- (2021) **The highest-return investment is your own skills** — for both employees and
  entrepreneurs. Learning to sell (which "might take you two years") can move you from ~$60K
  to ~$250K–$500K/yr (top 1%). You are the source of all income, so improving "the machine"
  makes everything else easy.
- (2021) **Keep ~6 months to 2 years of living expenses in cash** to sleep well; put the rest
  in truly passive vehicles.
- (2021) **Index positions aren't illiquid** — banks will lend ~50–70% loan-to-value against
  S&P holdings at ~1–2%, which you can redeploy into a new business or real estate. (Echoes
  the borrow-against-assets theme from his tax video three days earlier.)
- (2021) **"Truly passive" is the point** — real-estate flipping, crypto, and speculative
  "side hustles" are *not* passive; they steal attention and make your main income suffer, so
  net worth grows more slowly.
- (2021) **The switch signal:** keep almost 100% of your attention on income until the point
  where ~10% of your net-worth *growth* exceeds what you make per year — then shift focus from
  growing income to growing wealth.
- (2021) Biographical: he states he has started **13 businesses** to this point and now owns
  **eight more**. *(self-reported.)*
