# 7 figure CONFIDENTIAL meeting LEAKED - business growth STRATEGY exposed...

## Summary

Alex coaches a three-location gym owner who is considering licensing/franchising or opening a fourth location. He rejects the expansion impulse because the existing units are not profitable enough, arguing that the owner should consolidate/focus, fix the unit economics, and build one excellent, owner-independent gym before discussing franchising.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2021-12-22)

- Alex says a gym with roughly $30K/year profit per location is not ready for licensing or franchising.
- He says the owner may make more money by focusing on one location than by operating three weak units.
- He says small-town gyms should often have higher margins because costs and competition are lower, but lead supply can dry up.
- He says the owner should ask whether the goal is status or money.
- He says the gym should be made extremely profitable and owner-independent before it becomes an asset worth leveraging.
- He references Panda Express as a model of doing the same thing for decades and getting excellent at the unit.
- He recommends reviewing leases, considering consolidation, and returning to strong monthly profit before expansion.
