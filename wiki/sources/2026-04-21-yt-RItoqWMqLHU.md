# What Would You Do If You Weren't Afraid?

## Summary

Alex says fear is the emotion most likely to cause failure because it produces endless
reasons not to act. He recommends inverted thinking, making vague fears specific, and
playing out the real worst case until it becomes concrete enough to handle.

## Key claims

- Alex says fear is the emotion people most often fail to control.
- He says fear supplies the 100 reasons not to do something.
- He recommends inverted thinking: identify all the ways to kill the business, then do
  the opposite.
- He says the brain is better at spotting problems than opportunities, so problem
  spotting should be used deliberately and flipped.
- He says fear usually lives in the vague, not the specific.
- He says people overestimate downside and underestimate upside, which helped humans
  survive but hurts money-making.
- He recommends making worst-case scenarios concrete, such as asking who would provide a
  couch and for how long.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What would you do if you weren't afraid?"

> "Fear typically only lives in the vague."

> "We underestimate the upside and we overestimate the downside."
