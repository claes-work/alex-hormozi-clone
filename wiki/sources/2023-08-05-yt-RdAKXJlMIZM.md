# How to get SO rich you question the meaning of making money

## Summary

A recorded live keynote (rewritten the night before after a Yogurtland conversation with attendees) on three claims: (1) making money really comes down to **leverage**, (2) chasing **new** things keeps you poor — you have to focus, and (3) **better is leverage**. Hormozi redefines work as output (volume × leverage), attacks the "no one can outwork me" trope, and argues focus plus boring, repeated improvement compounds into wealth. Closes with a live "$1,000 book offer" leverage demonstration.

## Key claims

- Work is not force×distance, hours, or effort — "work equals outputs," and outputs = volume × leverage (how many times you do something × how much you get each time). Work rate = output ÷ time. (paraphrase, "Alex's definition")
- Leverage is uncapped while activity is capped by time/physical ability; out-leverage competitors instead of out-working them. Uses Buffett taking $500M in Coca-Cola dividends vs the CEO's $50M for less time worked. (paraphrase)
- Five ways people *fail* to get leverage: uninformed optimism (the change curve / valley of despair), the roofing "don't leave money on the table" trap, the magic-wand story, saying no to "the woman in the red dress" (shiny objects), and unlearning the quit-and-get-rewarded reflex. (paraphrase)
- Focus: "one thing all in." Picking (and the ability to say no) is what grows a business, not which thing you pick. Diane Hendricks (ABC Supply, roofing) cited as #1 self-made woman — boring industries, done long. (paraphrase)
- "Better comes from boring": weekly split tests, daily sales role-play, calling leads within 5 minutes (claims it can triple sales). A 9.5/10 book vs 7/10 is ~20x the work but ~1000x the sales (≈50x per unit of input). (paraphrase)
- All income is dollars-per-hour; "passive income" isn't passive, just *less active* — it's a spectrum, not binary. (paraphrase)
