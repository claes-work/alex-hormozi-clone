# Helping a Home Service Company Scale

## Summary
In a live Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises James, owner of a custom branded railings business that ships product nationwide (roughly 420 sales last year, all inbound via SEO with recently added Facebook ads). James wants to shift from small residential "minnow" orders toward high-value "whale" clients (e.g. one $50,000 handrail order) by building a referral network of architects, designers, and contractors. Hormozi frames this as an affiliate/B2B outreach strategy, walking through the three ways he structures affiliate deals and warning that adding whale-hunting is a fundamental, higher-risk change on top of the lower-risk play of simply scaling existing SEO and adding PPC.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex teaches that architects, designers, and contractors who can specify products in their plans act as affiliates — businesses that already have your customers and can market/sell your product to them (a concept he attributes to his "Leads" book / Core Four framework).
- As of March 2026, Alex names three ways to integrate your product into an affiliate's offer, ordered easiest to hardest: (1) they give away your lead magnet for free with their purchase; (2) they sell your lead magnet separately and keep 100% of the markup while you get the customer contact for upsells (his stated favorite); (3) they sell your actual offer and take a commission/kickback (his least favorite).
- As of March 2026, Alex argues a standard ~20% referral kickback fails because "everyone does it" and it doesn't change anyone's behavior — the offer must be irresistible enough to actually change how the affiliate runs their business; if it doesn't, either make the offer better or make it easier.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames customer acquisition cost through the lead-magnet lens: if the lead magnet is the CAC and you close one of three introductions, your CAC is roughly 3x the lead-magnet cost for a $50,000 customer — "usually pretty darn good."
- As of March 2026, Alex advises against greed over how much money an affiliate makes reselling your product; if they make a fortune, you make even more on upsells. He credits Leila (Layla) Hormozi with the maxim "never count the other guy's money."
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends the lowest-risk move first: add PPC on top of already-working SEO and produce far more articles to rank for more keywords — the "for sure will work" play — before layering in the riskier whale-hunting shift.
- As of March 2026, Alex endorses raising direct-to-consumer prices when adding a discounted channel for affiliates, so the ~20% margin can be split between a customer discount and an affiliate kickback.
- Caller-reported (unverified): James states he sold ~420 railings last year, all inbound via SEO; a single ~$50,000 client and a ~$60,000 Airbnb-development client both came from architect/construction-company referrals; his lead magnet is "250 deck rail ideas"; Facebook ads started ~3–4 weeks ago and qualify about half the leads.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Why not add zeros to things?"

> "Option two is that you give them some morsel of something that you sell that they can sell for a 100% markup. They can keep all the money but you get the introduction. This is my favorite way of doing it."

> "The offer has to be good enough that it changes what they do with their business. If it doesn't change what they do, the offer wasn't compelling enough. Or you made it too hard. So it's either make the thing better or make it easier for them."

> "A lot of people get very greedy about what other people are making off of their stuff. Just focus on the money you're making. If the deal makes sense for you, that's one of the pieces of advice that Leila actually taught me. She said never count the other guy's money."

> "Everyone does it. Who here does 20% kickbacks for anybody who refers you business? ... And it's because no one cares. That's kind of the point. And so it has to be an irresistible offer."

> "Think about it as like my CAC for a $50,000 customer is the cost of the lead magnet. And if I close one out of three introductions then it's 3x the cost of lead magnet is my cost to acquire a customer, which is usually pretty darn good."
