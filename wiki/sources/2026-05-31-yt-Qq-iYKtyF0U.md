# You Don't Need 2x More Effort, You Need 10x

## Summary

Alex Hormozi answers a podcast challenge question by telling listeners to define the input-output equation that moves them toward the goal, then 10x the input. He says many people are doing the right activity at the wrong amount because they think in doubles instead of orders of magnitude. He then connects 10x and 100x input to leverage: hiring, recruiting, and relinquishing control multiply output beyond what a solo operator can do.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The first step is defining the input-output equation: the action that moves the person closer to the desired outcome.
- Without a defined input-output equation, effort is detached from a goal.
- Many people are doing the right activity, but far too little of it.
- In business, people underestimate required volume because they lack context.
- To make 100x more, the person often needs 100x more input, usually through leverage rather than only personal effort.
- Entrepreneurship unlocks leverage by relinquishing control.
- The thing that rewarded the founder early - taking total control - must later be unlearned.
- Scaling requires giving up production, delivery, admin, sales, marketing, management, leadership, finance, and eventually the desire to be needed by the business.
