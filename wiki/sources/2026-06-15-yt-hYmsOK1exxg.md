# One Location Prints Cash. The Other Bleeds It.

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a cosmetic-dentistry / veneers business with two locations, one of which is struggling. Alex pushes back on opening a third location because the existing two locations have far more unused capacity than current demand. He estimates that if each location could do multiple cases per day, the company should focus on moving from about $2.5M to $25M within existing capacity. He recommends local ads, visual before/after content, collaboration posts, regular CTAs, and staff DM follow-up to new Instagram followers.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - custom-porcelain-veneers business owner. Her revenue, locations, case size, capacity, doctors, and personal-brand question are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Opening another location is premature when the existing locations have large unused capacity.
- The business may have a 10x revenue opportunity inside current capacity before adding any new capacity.
- The constraint is demand, not supply, if locations can perform many more cases than they currently book.
- Cosmetic dentistry is highly visual and therefore easy to advertise with before/after proof.
- Collaboration posts with prominent or semi-prominent customers can build local brand and demand.
- The business should regularly use CTAs in stories to capture warm demand.
- Staff can DM new Instagram followers to identify whether they are browsing or considering treatment.
- New followers can become a simple lead source if triaged consistently.
- Local ads and organic social should work together.
- Collaboration posts can include the founder, doctors, location, and customer pages.
