# Helping an SEO Agency Scale

## Summary
In a live Q&A, a business owner who runs an SEO/AEO agency (self-reporting ~$20M in revenue, serving mid-market/enterprise clients like QuickBooks and Nike) asks Alex how to stand out when building a personal brand in a market saturated with educational content. Alex reframes the question around the brand's objective (making more money) and advises making content aimed squarely at the type of buyer the person wants to attract, disregarding vanity metrics. He recommends an 80/20 split: 80% straight, high-value business content, 20% personal "you" elements.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex advises that a personal brand should be defined by its objective; when the caller's stated goal is more stages and speaking to get more clients, Alex reduces it to "so to make more money" and builds the advice around that.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends making content about the topics that attract the specific type of buyer you want, and disregarding views, likes, and other vanity metrics in favor of maximizing "the absolute highest value per second" for that audience.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames format/packaging (hooks, visual hooks, pacing) as the "ticket of entry" that is obvious and necessary, while the core content should stay tightly focused on what your buyers want.
- As of March 2026, Alex suggests an 80/20 approach for a long-lived personal brand: ~80% kept "straight down the barrel" on the core topic, with the remaining share reserved for personal/distinctive elements ("the things that make you you").
- As of March 2026, Alex says he anchors nearly all his own content to business even when he covers philosophy or fitness, treating those topics through a business lens (e.g. getting through hard times to keep doing business, working with his wife as a business partner).
- As of March 2026, Alex affirms he would do exactly the same thing again starting from scratch, adding "it wasn't easy."
- Unverified caller claim: the caller states his SEO/AEO agency does about $20M in revenue and sells to mid-market/enterprise clients including QuickBooks and Nike.
