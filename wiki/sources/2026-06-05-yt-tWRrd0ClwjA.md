# "I'm Closing 10% of My Sales Calls. What Am I Doing Wrong?"

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a small client-acquisition agency serving financial advisors, doing about $20K/month revenue and closing about 10% of qualified offers. Alex says the founder likely underestimates the volume needed and lacks proof. He recommends a free-trial-plus-penalty model to generate more clients, reps, proof, and testimonials, then explains the likely agency path: proof, VSL, higher pricing, churn factory, and eventually either low-cost automation or upmarket whales.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Sub-$1M businesses often dramatically underestimate the prospecting volume required.
- Short-term lead-quality variation may be normal if the sample size and time horizon are too small.
- A founder from sales should think about making sales easier, not merely becoming better at sales.
- If proof is weak, a free trial plus penalty can generate yeses while still requiring client behavior.
- The client should pay ad spend; the agency can make the service free while it earns proof.
- Small-business acquisition agencies often end in one of two viable states: low-cost/high-volume automation or premium upmarket whales.
- The early "crap period" can be useful if its purpose is to gather proof to move upmarket or downmarket intentionally.
