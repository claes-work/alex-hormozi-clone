# Why I Don't Listen to People Who Can't Even Attend My Funeral

## Summary

Alex uses his own funeral as a death-frame for ignoring other people's opinions. He says
many people in one's current life may not attend or remember six months later, which he
finds freeing rather than hopeless. He then defines intelligence as rate of learning and
learning as changed behavior under the same condition; consuming information without
changing behavior is procrastination, not learning.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says imagining his funeral makes most current opinions feel less important.
- He frames the fact that people move on after death as a source of freedom.
- If people will not remember or discuss someone after death, Alex says that reduces the
  weight of their current judgment.
- Learning is not passive consumption; it requires doing.
- Alex defines intelligence as rate of learning.
- He defines learning as the same condition producing a new behavior.
- If a person consumes information and returns to the same behavior under the same
  conditions, Alex says they learned nothing.
- He applies the learning frame to hard conversations, delayed decisions, and cutting
  relationships.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Why on earth would I listen to them while I'm alive?"

> "Intelligence means rate of learning."

> "Learning means same condition, new behavior."
