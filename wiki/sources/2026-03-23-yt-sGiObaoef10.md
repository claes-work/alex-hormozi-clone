# Helping a Real Estate Education Business Scale to $3M

## Summary
In this MoreMozi coaching segment, Alex Hormozi advises Henry, who runs a Spanish-language education business teaching fix-and-flip real estate to the Hispanic market. Henry reports (unverified caller claims) $1.3M in his second year, two 600-person events annually, a $12,000 high-ticket mentorship converting ~20% of the room, and no repeat offer afterward. Hormozi frames education businesses as inherently "graduating" customers and gives two paths depending on Henry's goal: since Henry wants only to make more money (not sell the company), the simple answer is to run the same event playbook more times in new geographies to double revenue with minimal operational risk.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames education businesses as ones that naturally "graduate" their customers, so the way to build continuity in education is to always have something else to learn or teach (undergrad → master's → PhD analogy).
- As of March 2026, Alex diagnoses Henry's issue as being at the "productized" stage where customers have nothing else to buy; the fix is to connect customer success to product and create a new back-end offer.
- As of March 2026, Alex says the choice of strategy depends on the owner's goal: if you want to sell the company you must build revenue retention to make it valuable; if you just want more money, added complexity is unnecessary.
- As of March 2026, Alex's recommendation to a make-more-money owner is to simply run the same playbook more times — e.g., add two more events on the other side of the US to attract different customers and roughly double the business with essentially no operational risk.
- As of March 2026, Alex favors "downselling the upsell": moving $12,000 high-ticket buyers into a cheaper recurring membership (~$3,000–$5,000/year) for continued access to the network, vendors, and services, which he says people stick with far more readily.
- As of March 2026, Alex describes a CAC-offset mechanism: if the $12,000 sale merely breaks even but feeds a high-margin ~$3,000/year membership that doesn't churn, you can outspend competitors who must profit on the front-end $12,000.
- Unverified caller claims (Henry): $1.3M revenue in year two; two annual events of ~600 people each; a $12,000 one-year mentorship; ~20% room conversion; events run East Coast and West Coast out of Miami; goal of $3M this year.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You're in education. Education graduates people. That's how education works. So if you look at the education system overall, the way to create continuity in education is just always have something else to learn or to teach."

> "The easiest thing to do is just double the amount of events you're doing."

> "You have a really simple solution. Like just run the same playbook more times."

> "If they paid you 12, maybe they'll stay for 3, 3 to 5, just to have access to the network and some of your vendors and the stuff that you kind of like provide. And I think that's something that people would be far more likely to stick with."

> "You could break even on the $12,000 if you know that someone gets into a $3,000 a year membership, that's pretty much all margin that doesn't leave. So it's like that allows you to spend way more than your competition, cuz they need to make money on the 12, you can break even on the 12. And then you just have this stack of $3,000 bills that just keep stacking up."

> "The nice thing is that you're honest about just wanting to make more money. Makes this a lot easier. Don't say I want to make an impact."

> "Why add complexity if that's not his goal?"
