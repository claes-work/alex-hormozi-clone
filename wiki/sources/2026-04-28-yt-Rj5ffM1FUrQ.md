# How to Scale a Local Service Business

## Summary

Alex says local services are strong beginner businesses because they require no inventory
and the acquisition process is relatively straightforward. Local trust lowers the proof
burden compared with national marketing, where the business must overcome anonymity with
more proof, content, and marketing skill. The tradeoff is that local services are usually
harder and more expensive to scale, though not impossible.

## Key claims

- Alex likes local services for beginners, partly because he came from that space.
- Local service businesses often have no inventory and simple acquisition routes such as
  door knocking, calls, email, and showing up.
- B2B and B2C local services can both work.
- Local businesses benefit from inherent trust because the customer is near them.
- National marketing must compensate for lower trust with more content, proof, customer
  success, and skill.
- Local services can be a low-cash starting point that funds bigger opportunities later.
