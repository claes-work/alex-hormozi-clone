# Helping a $580K Marketing Agency Scale

## Summary

Alex advises a low-priced marketing agency not to split sales from account management
merely because that structure is conventional. Its representatives both acquire and
retain clients, producing unusually low reported churn. Because the model already works,
he recommends adding more representatives and tolerating that mature books may turn
hunters into farmers, provided retention and revenue remain strong.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says 2% monthly churn is unusually good for agency services and
  should not be put at risk without more evidence.
- He usually separates sales and customer service, but treats this case as an exception
  because relationship ownership appears to drive retention.
- Alex says a $200-$300 monthly SMB price point contributes to low churn; higher price
  tiers tend to create new churn thresholds.
- At 2% monthly churn, he estimates roughly a 50-month average lifetime and about $15,000
  LTV on a $300 monthly product.
- He recommends doing more of what works rather than restructuring to solve a problem
  that has not appeared.
- If a representative maintains a $100,000 book, Alex says reduced hunting matters less
  than the retained output; growth can come from hiring more representatives.
- Revenue, retention, sales, and representative figures are owner-reported and unverified.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I think you do more of what's working."

> "You've solved something that many people have struggled to solve."

> "Just bring more guys in."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: preserve an unconventional integrated sales/account model when retention proves it works.
