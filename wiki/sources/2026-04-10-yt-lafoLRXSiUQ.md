# Why Partnerships Make You Poorer

## Summary

Alex warns against risking sufficient wealth to pursue unnecessary faster gains, using
his own failed partnerships as the example. Equity is the most expensive payment and a
partner should contribute money, experience, or time and energy that the founder lacks,
with that contribution defined explicitly rather than merely possessed in theory.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says people should only need to get rich once and should not
  risk what they need for more that they do not need.
- Partnerships reduce individual earning power immediately because founders give away
  equity before the venture has produced results.
- A partner must contribute at least one missing resource: money, experience, or time
  and energy.
- Possessing a resource is insufficient; the partnership must specify how it will be
  applied for shared return.
- Alex self-reports nine failed partnerships before Leila, one of his two large losses
  arising from a partner, and insecurity about operating alone as a cause. These claims
  are not independently verified here.
- He distinguishes those failures from later successful partnerships, including with
  Leila.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You should only have to get rich once."

> "You pay with the most expensive thing you have, which is equity."

> "A partner needs to have one of these three things that you do not have."
