# Alex Hormozi Reveals Planet Fitness EVIL GENIUS Business Model

## Summary
In this short clip from the Acquired podcast, Alex Hormozi is asked what he thinks of Planet Fitness's marketing (the "lunk alarm," etc.) from a business-model perspective and calls it "brilliant." He explains that Planet Fitness deliberately engineered its facilities and policies to attract members who sign up but rarely attend: they lose money on heavy users, so removing heavy weights, squat racks, and chalk screens out the ~80% who would actually use the gym, while pizza nights and a frictionless cancellation experience keep sign-ups and word-of-mouth high. The economic result is that a single location can churn the same ~10,000 active members through a much larger local population over time.

## Key claims (Planet Fitness model mechanics, as narrated by Alex)
- The model starts from the premise that most people (Alex cites ~92%) don't actually want to use anything in the gym — they want to sign up for a gym and not come.
- Heavy users are the unprofitable ones: on a per-usage basis the gym loses money on the people who actually show up and use the facility (down to details like taking protein shakes on the way out).
- Heavy users are identified by what they use — heavy weights, squat racks, and chalk. Eliminating those three things removes roughly 80% of the people who would actually use the gym.
- With heavy users designed out, a facility can sign up as many people as possible who will never come, then incentivize them further (e.g. monthly pizza) so the membership feels worth the low price.
- The pricing framing: at ~$10/month, coming once a month just for the free pizza already "covers" the fee — so the member rationalizes keeping the cheap membership even though they don't work out.
- Unit economics: the average Planet Fitness location has ~10,000 members, and the model requires ~10,000 active (paying) members to work.
- Deliberately easy exit: unlike most gyms that enforce contracts and send delinquent members to collections, Planet Fitness makes cancellation frictionless ("no sweat, come back when you feel good again") because it depends on volume and positive word-of-mouth.
- The easy-cancel, no-shame approach removes the fear of returning, so lapsed members re-join later — the same churn engine feeding the model.
- Geographic churn: a location in a city of ~30,000 people can cycle through the same ~10,000 active members over time as people get motivated, sign up, get demotivated, and lapse — churning "right through them."
