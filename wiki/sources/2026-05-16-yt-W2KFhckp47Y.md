# The Sentence That Made Me Want to Change My Life

## Summary

Alex says a self-help term, "wantrepreneur," bothered him around age 20 or 21 because it
made him aware that he was reading and talking about business without doing business. He
uses the example to argue that criticism bothers people when it points to a deficiency
they have been ignoring. The clip also covers plan B: Alex says his fallback of driving
Uber and stripping did not make him less successful, but made his plan logical.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice)

- Alex says the term "wantrepreneur" was one catalyst that pushed him toward
  entrepreneurship around age 20 or 21.
- He describes a wantrepreneur as someone who reads and talks about business without
  doing business.
- He says painful phrases do not break people so much as reveal ignored deficiencies.
- People are bothered by true things they do not like to look at.
- Alex says his old plan B was to drive Uber during the day and strip at night, save
  money, and take another shot.
- He does not believe having that plan B made him less successful; he says it made him
  logical.
- If people have succeeded with and without a behavior, Alex tends to treat that behavior
  as nonessential.
- He prefers deletion and simplification: improve life by removing what is not part of
  the few things he enjoys.
- He thinks about death a lot and says that grounds him in life.
