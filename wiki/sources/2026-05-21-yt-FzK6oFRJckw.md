# The Harsh Truth About What Others Think of Your Life

## Summary

This clip repeats Alex Hormozi's holiday family-table story about being told his life was
unbalanced, then adds a defusing tactic for criticism: accept the critic's projected
version of him with "you're right" and move on. He frames the comment "I would never live
your life" as something that can simply be answered with "good, don't."

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says the line about not listening to people who do not have what one wants was a
  message to his younger self.
- He repeats a holiday family-table story where a family member criticized his lifestyle
  as unbalanced.
- He asked what part of his life was unbalanced: health, marriage, or finances.
- The family member's core statement was that she would never live his life.
- Alex's response is that this is fine because it is not her life.
- He says people often make projections that are not actually real.
- In this case, he thinks the family member was genuinely trying to help rather than
  acting from envy.
- A mentor taught him to answer some hostile criticism with "you're right" so the other
  person has nothing left to argue with.
- Alex frames this as accepting the other person's idea of him, not necessarily believing
  it.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Good. Don't."

> "It's not your life. It's mine."

> "You're right."
