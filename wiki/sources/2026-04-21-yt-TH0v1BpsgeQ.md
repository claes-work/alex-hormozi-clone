# Helping a Business Owner Structure a Roll-Up

## Summary

Alex coaches a roofing contractor considering a regional roll-up of 12 companies with
about $10M consolidated EBITDA. He suggests using options or purchase contracts at a
multiple that is better than the owners could get alone, then controlling enough of the
combined sale to sell at a higher multiple and keep the spread.

## Key claims

- Alex says a $10M consolidated EBITDA roofing roll-up would be interesting to buyers.
- He suggests offering each owner an option for 100% of the business at a multiple above
  what they could get alone.
- He compares the structure to wholesaling: get the purchase contract, then sell the
  package for a higher multiple and keep the spread.
- He says owners can receive slices of the proceeds based on their relative contribution
  to the combined EBITDA pie.
- He says the group needs one person talking and one person in control, otherwise the
  deal becomes a mess.
- He says solving that control and coordination problem is why the organizer can get
  paid millions.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "There needs to be one person who's talking and one person in control."

> "You make the spread."

> "That's why you get paid millions of dollars."
