# The Wealthiest 1% Get There By Doing THIS

> Source: [yt-Kj3xhPRJtQA](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Kj3xhPRJtQA) — @AlexHormozi, 2021-01-08. Single speaker (Alex Hormozi). Short clip.

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that most of the world's wealthiest people made their money in short, "punctuated" windows of time when a temporary arbitrage opportunity opened up. The entrepreneur's job is to build a full stack of skills in advance so that when such a window opens, they can go all in.

## Key claims

- (2021) Most of the wealthiest people made their money in **punctuated periods of time** — brief windows where an arbitrage gap gives disproportionate return on investment before it closes. [[../topics/wealth/wealth]]
- (2021) Examples cited: Kevin Harrington got free infomercial airtime (eight hours a day for ~three years when networks had no programming) with essentially zero cost of acquisition; Mark Cuban made most of his money on one sale; Kevin O'Leary on one deal.
- (2021) These arbitrage windows open and close; no opportunity lasts forever.
- (2021) The entrepreneur's job is to build a **full stack of skills** ahead of time so that when the door opens, you can go all in — "put everything on black."

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you look at the wealthiest people in the world, most of them made their money in punctuated periods of time."

> "There are these arbitrage opportunities that open up and they close, and what we have to do as entrepreneurs is look at the skills that we need to have so we have a full stack of skills so that when that door opens we can go all in and put everything on black."
