# "I will never lose sleep again after learning this.." (2022-03-14)

## Summary

An unusually long solo video (a repurposed internal presentation) walking through 55+ sleep
habits organized into four buckets — beliefs, environment, intake, and habits. Hormozi
frames sleep as an entrepreneurial performance lever (impulse control and intelligence),
but leads with the meta-point that the beliefs matter most: don't turn sleep into a
superstition of "must / have to." The bulk is a practical checklist; the persona-relevant
core is the anti-superstition, non-judgmental mindset.

## Key claims

- **Sleep is a business input.** Sleep deprivation performs like drunkenness — weakened
  impulse control (more reactive/emotional) and roughly halved ability to store and recall
  learning with as little as 2 hours' deprivation. Worse sleep → worse decisions → less
  money. (paraphrase)
- **Beliefs beat tactics.** The most important bucket is beliefs: remove "have to / must /
  need to / should" from sleep, because each implies a threat ("or what?"). Treat it as
  80/20, not perfection. (paraphrase)
- **"I don't need a lot of sleep" is a useful belief.** Believing he can sleep anywhere and
  doesn't need much removes sleep anxiety — which paradoxically lets him sleep more. 90% of
  the benefit is "being in a dark room with your eyes closed." (paraphrase)
- **Don't judge yourself.** Labeling yourself a "bad sleeper" is the source of most sleep
  anxiety; drop the good/bad judgment. (paraphrase)
- **Front-load one-time fixes.** Environment hacks (cold room ~67°F, blackout, white noise,
  good mattress/pillow) are do-once, benefit-forever — same logic he applies in business. (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "A sleep-deprived brain basically has us performing as a drunk person."
