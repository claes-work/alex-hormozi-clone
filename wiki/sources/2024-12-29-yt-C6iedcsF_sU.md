# The best Business to start in 2025 | Alex Hormozi

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains why he made "the biggest investment of my life" to become a
co-owner of Skool (school.com), framing it as a mission to fix a broken education
system by giving anyone the tools, community, and education to teach a skill and get
paid. He argues that for beginners, an **online service business** built as a paid
Skool community is the easiest business to start in 2025 — cheapest to start, near-zero
cost to deliver, low overhead, and doable from anywhere with a laptop. He cites a
Skool "strike rate" (self-reported) and pitches a free weekly onboarding call built on
patterns reverse-engineered from thousands of successful paid communities.

## Key claims

- Alex is now a co-owner of Skool (school.com) and calls it "the biggest investment of
  my life." (self-reported)
- 54.1% of people who start a *paid* community on Skool make money — which he claims is
  a better strike rate than OnlyFans (he admits he doesn't know OnlyFans' actual rate).
  (self-reported)
- The best business for a beginner in 2025 is an **online service business**, because it
  costs the least to start, costs almost nothing to deliver, has low recurring overhead,
  and can be run from anywhere with the tech you already own.
- A low-stakes first business is where you should make your inevitable early mistakes.
- The #1 reason people cancel or don't start something is **overwhelm**; the design goal
  of Skool was to strip friction and funnel action into clear step 1 / step 2 / step 3
  instructions.
- His audience skews toward existing business owners ($1M–$100M+), but his content and
  the Skool bet are aimed at people trying to make their *first dollar*.
- The onboarding playbook (the "rule of 300") is reverse-engineered from studying
  thousands of paid communities that are doing well, boiled down to "do this X times per
  day, this Y times per week."
- He believes the education system is broken: teachers are underpaid and under-resourced,
  students learn things that don't matter in the real world and take on debt for "a piece
  of paper."
- He offers a free personal onboarding call every Monday for beginners (via
  school.com/games).
