# The Overnight Success

## Summary
Podcast clip (Alex plus an interviewer). Theme: the "overnight success" illusion and enduring the long, unseen middle. Alex argues you only ever see the moment someone accomplishes the goal, so everyone looks like an overnight success — which means "the 10 years where they sucked, no one saw." The fear of people noticing your failures is therefore irrational: they barely notice when you succeed. His strongest coping frame: "this will be the story that I will one day tell" — the harder it is, "the bigger the dragon, the more epic the story… the more epic the hero." He contrasts winners (who "define themselves by what they made happen") with losers ("by what happened to them"), and notes the "Rocky cut scene lasts 90 seconds in the movie and 5 years in reality, with no promise of it ever ending." Using the drowning-mouse studies (a mouse rescued once swims far longer the next time) and a hold-your-breath analogy, he argues humans endure far more when they know an end is coming — but entrepreneurship gives no known end, so you must "fight like the second mouse." His only certainty: it's the same thing everyone who made it went through, "and you won't die. And if you do die, you won't care… best case, you win. Worst case, it won't matter." Ends with the roadmap promo.
