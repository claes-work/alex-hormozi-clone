# Delegation Is the Price of Scaling

## Summary

Alex describes entrepreneurship as progressively relinquishing control: founders first
delegate execution, then management, leadership, and sometimes vision. His current razor
is that a process does not work if it requires him, expressed internally as "scale zero."
He also explicitly dates a change in his capacity-planning view: after roughly the first
10-12 years of his career, when he would have increased demand first, he now adds supply
first to protect reputation and create bandwidth.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex calls delegation and loss of control the price of scaling.
- He describes the entrepreneurial path as giving away execution, management,
  leadership, and potentially vision as the organization grows.
- He says hard workers tend to seek high-effort solutions and may resist delegation
  because doing the work makes them feel useful.
- His current operating razor is: if a process requires him, it does not work.
- He and Leila use "scale zero" to mean reducing his required involvement to zero so an
  operation can scale without him as its limiter.
- Alex says founders may sacrifice profit in the short term by hiring or delegating but
  can gain more scalable profit later.
- He explicitly says his view changed in the source year: earlier in his career he would
  increase demand first and force capacity to catch up; now he increases supply first.
- Increasing demand beyond delivery capacity risks reputation, while increasing supply
  first costs short-term profit but protects quality and creates room for later demand.
- He self-reports that Acquisition.com postponed a February book launch until August and
  enabled it by hiring four directors and leaders roughly 90 days beforehand.

> ⚠️ CONTRADICTION: Alex identifies his earlier demand-first advice as a position he has
> now reversed. The current April 2026 position is to increase supply first when the
> business is at equilibrium.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Delegation is the price of scaling."

> "We have to scale me down to zero so we can scale it up to infinity."
