# Why Your Best Employee Will Leave Without This One Thing

## Summary

Alex advises a chiropractor/clinic owner who is shifting from patient delivery into
promotion. He says high-skilled service businesses need to train up the practitioner who
can take the workload, reduce key-person risk with additional clinicians, and provide a
path to 10-20% upside through profit share or profits interest. The promoted practitioner
should feel like the facility is theirs. As the owner leans into promotion, the new
business skill becomes recruiting, hiring, training, and culture.

## Key claims

- Alex says the owner has effectively already chosen to lean into promotion if promotion
  makes more money per unit of time.
- He says high-skilled service businesses need to train up other practitioners before the
  owner fully exits delivery.
- He recommends a path to roughly 20% ownership/upside for the key practitioner to reduce
  the risk that the practitioner walks away with the old practice.
- He says adding one or more additional chiropractors can reduce key-person risk over time.
- He compares this to law firms, accounting firms, and consulting firms with path-to-partner
  models.
- He says the upside does not need to be actual equity; it can be profit share or profits
  interest that vests over time.
- He says the practitioner should feel like it is their facility, including symbolic
  ownership/status.
- The owner's next skill is recruiting, hiring, training, and culture because scaling
  people becomes the business.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Having a path to like 20% ownership... is fairly [important]."

> "You want them to feel like it's their facility."

> "Lean into promotion. It's a higher leverage skill."

> "When you scale people that's the business."
