# How to Shrink the Gap Between Thought and Action

## Summary

Alex argues that people often delay disliked tasks for longer than the tasks would take.
He says he has tried to shrink the time between recognizing a needed action and starting
it. He defines personal power as the distance between thought and reality, then
distinguishes work-relevant preparation from fragile productivity rituals.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex says people delay doing things they dislike for longer than it takes to do them.
- He says starting often reveals that the task is smaller and more concrete than it felt
  in avoidance.
- He calls starting "pulling the thread": the unknown becomes tangible and breaks into
  solvable problems.
- He says procrastination reinforces itself in reverse, making more postponement easier.
- He says he used to define power by the distance between thoughts and reality.
- He says shrinking the gap between wanting or knowing something should be done and
  having it done is a direct indication of personal power.
- He applies the same frame to waking up, beginning work quickly, and minimizing
  task-to-task transition time.
- He distinguishes preparation from routine: preparation is work-related setup, while
  unrelated rituals are not part of the work.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "People delay doing things they don't like for longer than it takes to do them."

> "I call it pulling the thread."

> "Shrink the gap."
