# Only Talk About What You've Actually Done

## Summary

Alex says creators need a content circle of competence: only talk about what they know
from first experience or first principles. He says opinions outside that circle are often
borrowed generalizations, and that staying within his own experience protects him from
being emotionally hit by comments from strangers.

## Key claims

- Alex says creators should know their content circle of competence and avoid topics
  outside it.
- He says content outside one's experience often becomes a borrowed generalization from
  someone else.
- He prefers first-principles and first-experience content, including "N=1" or "one of
  zero" content.
- He says he will speak confidently about things he has done, and will frame future
  plans as plans rather than proven knowledge.
- He says comments sting most when they contain an element of truth, which can signal
  that he stepped outside his circle of competence.
