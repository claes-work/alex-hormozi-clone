# How to Use AI in Your Business in 2026

## Summary

Solo Alex Hormozi talk arguing that businesses do not need to *become* AI companies — they need to *use* AI as a tool, exactly as every business adopted the internet without becoming an internet company. He frames the owner's "cloud-to-dirt" (top strategy down to API-level) knowledge of the business as the real edge, because only the owner can see which technical capabilities are valuable. He walks through concrete AI use cases across marketing, sales, customer service, risk/fraud, and legal, and urges viewers to fully automate one workflow of their day as a starting habit, while stressing that AI does not repeal the psychology of persuasion or the need for real proof.

## Key claims (practical AI-in-business advice)

- **You don't need to become an AI business.** Like the internet, AI is one tool among many used to deliver whatever you sell; you don't advertise that you use it.
- **The owner's "cloud-to-dirt" / vertically-integrated knowledge is the alpha.** A hired "tech nerd" will default to commoditized automations they've seen before; only the owner who understands the whole business sees which combinations create real value. Business acumen overlaid on technical acumen is where the big gains come from.
- **Practical starting method:** find a YouTube video on automating a specific task, feed the link/transcript to an AI, say "help me build this thing," follow the directions, and when stuck, screenshot the page and ask "what do I do now?" — repeat over and over.
- **Compare apples-to-apples.** Owners install a half-built AI function, compare it to a process optimized over 6–20 years, and conclude AI "doesn't work." You must give the AI the same training/SOP investment you gave the human process.
- **AI doesn't remove the sales motion.** Dropping an "AI setter" into a business is like dropping a new sales rep in — the first attempt will be bad. It's worse if your underlying sales process is weak. AI makes what you do faster/easier; it doesn't mean you skip the work.
- **Human psychology doesn't change.** Persuasion rules still apply; whatever you did to persuade people you still have to do.
- **Risk reduction via AI** (cited examples): PayPal cut fraud losses by ~$700M in a year via faster pattern recognition; JP Morgan's "COIN" saved ~360,000 lawyer-hours reviewing ~12,000 credit agreements in seconds; Klarna replaced ~700 customer-service agents and saved ~$40M in a year.
- **His own example:** at his book launch they spun up five agents that handled ~120,000 support tickets and resolved ~90% without human intervention.
- **The one ask:** dedicate a block of time (evenings/weekends/mornings) to fully automate one portion of your day, soup-to-nuts. You should automate your *own* workflow, because it frees you to find more valuable work.
- **De-mystify your "intuition."** Treat gut/instinct as pattern recognition — observable behaviors that produce an outcome — so it can be described and automated.
- **Proof is the bottleneck for AI content.** On the B2B side an AI avatar won't work well without real-world proof behind it; on the consumer side (e.g. a beautiful AI skincare avatar) the look itself lends proof.
- **The "AI equivalent of the webpage":** anything a human touches, an AI can interact with at a basic level, across every department.
- **Marketing use cases:** AI to generate ideas; script/package ideas (headlines, thumbnails, topics); auto-run thumbnail tests and build a winning-pattern skill over time; trend research (formats, verbal and visual hooks) cross-referenced with your brand to produce a Venn-diagram of 10 ideas to pick from.
- **Ad automation ("self-licking ice cream cone"):** content auto-overlaid with a CTA and pushed into a daily ad campaign running yesterday's content; auto-generated static images off data sets (e.g. a Skool community's "wins" tab feeding six high-converting visual templates run as ads).
- **Sales use cases:** enrich leads, fast personalized responses (automated voice note, dynamically personalized image/text, Instagram DM), and dynamic scheduling via AI-to-AI handoff that raises speed-to-contact.
- **Time window:** claims roughly "18 months" where large wealth can be created by people starting with nothing — a big opportunity.
- **Legal example:** one general counsel running agents (first response, second response, cease-and-desist) instead of ~100 paralegals.
