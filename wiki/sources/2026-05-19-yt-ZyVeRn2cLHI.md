# He's Doing $3.4M But Wants Out of the Stress

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a founder with a talent agency and photo-studio business, then a gym
owner with three locations. For the first founder, the main advice is focus: pick one
business because maintaining two separate models prevents growth. For the gym owner, Alex
prescribes a six-week onboarding challenge, rollover-credit annual sale, supplements, and
simple ad creative.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A founder running a modeling agency and photo studios is effectively running two
  different businesses with different operating problems.
- If the agency and photo studio produce similar profit, the founder should pick one and
  go all in.
- The modeling agency may scale more easily, but the founder sounds like he dislikes it.
- Business switching has a larger context-switching cost than ordinary task switching.
- The photo studio could explore annual recurring updates if models need updated pictures
  yearly.
- A personal-training operator with three locations and underfilled capacity needs a
  scalable customer-acquisition system.
- If CAC is about 200 to 250 euros and the front-end offer is around 97 euros plus a setup
  fee, the issue is not cash constraint but undercharging on the front end.
- Alex recommends a six-week onboarding offer around 600 euros before the normal monthly
  program.
- The gym offer should address injury fear, accountability, and visible progress.
- The six-week fee can be credited toward an annual commitment halfway through the
  challenge.
- A nutrition consultation can sell 200 to 300 euros of supplements to liquidate CAC.
- For gym ads, Alex says static ads are currently outperforming video for many Gym Launch
  operators, though strong video can still win.
- His preferred gym video is customers moving in unison with the offer in the copy.
