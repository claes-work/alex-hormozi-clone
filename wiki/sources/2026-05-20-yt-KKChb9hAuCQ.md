# Social Media is About To Change Forever

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says influence is moving toward rawer, longer, more live formats. He uses a
conversation with MrBeast about a creator soccer game to rank applause for traditional
celebrities, short-form creators, long-form creators, and live streamers. His lesson:
shorts can point people toward long-form, but deeper influence comes from more
reinforcement cycles and more hours with the audience.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex discussed the future of media and content with MrBeast a few weeks before this
  clip.
- At a creator soccer event, Alex says traditional celebrities received little applause,
  short-form creators received more, long-form creators received much more, and live
  streamers received the most.
- Shorts can be useful as a way to get someone to watch long-form content.
- The time risk for the viewer is higher with long-form, so shorts can reduce perceived
  risk.
- Long-form creates far more reinforcement cycles than shorts because the audience spends
  more time with the creator.
- Alex estimates that two 1-hour pieces equal the exposure of hundreds of 15-second
  shorts.
- He sees long podcasts and interviews as powerful influence tools.
- He plans to do more live streams because live formats approximate raw reality.
- The internet tends to move toward truth, in his view: from curated celebrities toward
  rawer podcasts and live streaming.
- Influence should still include SPCL elements: status, proof/credibility, likeness, and
  direction.
- High-volume content operators such as Rogan, PBD, and Dave Ramsey build influence by
  publishing hours of content consistently.
- Acquisition.com has made about 35,000 pieces of content, according to Alex.
- Alex says Acquisition.com is producing roughly 100 times the content volume of some
  lower-revenue content businesses, and expects roughly more prospects from that volume.
- Consistency is underestimated and can make someone a dangerous competitor.
