# Helping a Paid Ads Agency Fix Lead Quality

## Summary

Alex redesigns a paid-advertising agency's two constraints: customers bringing work in
house and low-quality leads. He proposes a planned transition from managed service to
consulting, plus advertising and outbound messages that explicitly exclude companies
below the agency's preferred $100,000 monthly media-spend threshold.

## Key claims

- The owner says ideal customers spend more than $100,000 per month on media and that the
  pipeline currently comes from affiliates and referrals; these claims are unverified.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends presenting in-housing as a planned second stage: the
  agency manages transition and consults until the internal team outperforms it.
- He estimates post-transition consulting at roughly 20%-30% of the prior service fee,
  using 3% of ad spend after a 10% managed-service fee as an illustrative starting point.
- The changed role aligns incentives and moves the agency from subordinate provider to
  teacher while extending customer lifetime value.
- To attract the desired large accounts, he prioritizes the owner's own paid-ad expertise,
  followed by targeted outbound and narrowly relevant content.
- Ads should state the spend threshold bluntly so unsuitable prospects self-exclude and
  the conversion signal is not trained on them; LinkedIn is suggested as a quality channel.
