# "My Best Store Is Dying and My Team Blames the Economy"

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a golf-club retailer with a long-standing original store, a newer high-performing location, and an internet/warehouse business. The owner says the original location has declined since a key person left, while the team blames the market, politics, tariffs, and saturation. Alex diagnoses the issue as undertalent after key-person loss, not market saturation, and urges the owner to keep the hiring bar high rather than hiring merely to stop pain.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A location can look like overexpansion only after the key person who made delegation work leaves.
- Market saturation is rarely the right diagnosis when a previously strong location loses talent.
- Brick-and-mortar culture is dictated by the leader's standard for acceptable effort and performance.
- The business may have to either kill the location or invest money and talent into saving it.
- Over time, the bar for excellence should rise and tolerance for mediocre talent should fall.
- The best talent is usually in the future, so owners should not settle for today's mediocre replacement.
- Hiring should feel like being pulled toward a person, not merely trying to make the pain of vacancy disappear.
