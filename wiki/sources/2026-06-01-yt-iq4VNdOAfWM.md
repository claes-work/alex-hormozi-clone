# 'I want to grow my business but I don't want to sacrifice family time.'

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a roofing/exterior-remodeling owner who has replaced himself operationally, works only a few hours a week, and wants to reach $100M without sacrificing family time. Alex frames the issue as preference and tradeoff rather than right/wrong. The owner can want less, trade more, or pay for higher-level talent and accept lower short-term profit. Alex also warns that side investments should remain passive; making them active costs the time and focus needed for the bigger goal.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Regret often comes from imagining the upside of a path without imagining the cost required to get it.
- Work-life balance is a preference tradeoff, not a universal moral answer.
- Dissatisfaction comes from wanting both sides of a trade without paying either cost.
- The choice is to want less or trade more.
- If the founder wants growth without adding personal hours, the trade may be lower short-term profitability to hire higher-level talent.
- Scaling from this stage moves the founder into the "who" game: the next level depends on better people.
- The best talent is usually in the future, not already behind the founder.
- Passive investments should stay passive; if an investment becomes active, it should justify itself as active income.
- Building something big means giving up many exciting new things.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Either want less or trade more."
