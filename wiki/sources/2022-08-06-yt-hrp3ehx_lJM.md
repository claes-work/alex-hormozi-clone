# How I Hacked My Brain to Feel Gratitude All the Time

## Summary

Hormozi admits he has struggled to feel gratitude his whole life and that journals
and meditation never stuck for him. He shares a reframe he calls the "grandfather
frame": each morning he imagines he is his 85-year-old self (whom he pictures as a
multi-billionaire) time-traveled back into his current 32-year-old body to
re-experience his life one more time — which converts ordinary present moments into
nostalgia, i.e. gratitude in the present. He ties it to lowering anxiety over
outcomes and to his broader arc from anger to joy.

## Key claims

- He does not naturally feel gratitude; standard practices (gratitude journals, daily
  meditation) never worked for him, though he acknowledges they work for others.
- The "grandfather frame": imagine your much-older self re-living your current life;
  everyday things (your body without joint pain, your spouse, your city) become
  precious because you know you're re-experiencing them.
- His definition: "nostalgia is gratitude in the present for past circumstances" —
  the frame manufactures nostalgia for the present.
- The frame also reduces anxiety/stress over outcomes and improves long-horizon
  decision-making ("I'm 85, I don't have anything to prove"), pulling him away from
  short-term-dollar decisions.
- He distinguishes happiness (external, circumstantial) from joy (internal,
  independent of circumstance) and says the elders he admires possess internal joy.
