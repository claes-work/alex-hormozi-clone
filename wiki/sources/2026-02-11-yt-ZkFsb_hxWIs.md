# My Framework for Brand Associations

## Summary
Asked how he decides who to associate his personal brand with (prompted by his appearance on Logan Paul's podcast), Alex Hormozi lays out his working theory of branding. He defines brand as the association made between two things through an outcome: place a thing people don't know next to a thing they do know, and positive associations transfer until the known anchor is no longer needed — fundamentally "teaching at scale." Any brand change loses some audience and gains others; good branding nets more than it loses. He breaks brand into three elements — reach (how many see it), influence (likelihood people comply with a request), and direction (whether that influence runs for or against you) — illustrating with Trump (high influence, polarizing) and parents (very high influence, tiny reach). Applying this, he judges associations by whether they cost him audience versus expose him to new people, and says the fastest way to lose trust is doing things against his stated values. He explains he does few podcasts, rarely headlines others' stages, and avoids the "incestuous" speaking circuit because unwanted associations attach whether he wants them or not. He closes on credibility in the direct-response/education world, arguing the core problem is people lying about credentials, results, and expectations, and offers his reputation-preserving inverse of marketing: "promise nothing, deliver a ton."
