# Should I Have Contractors or Employees?

## Summary
Short Q&A clip. An audience member (Zack Fine) asks whether a lean, owner-operator remodeling company is better than hiring subcontractors. Alex refuses to pick a universal winner and instead frames it as **trade-offs — "pick your poison of what problems you're going to have."** His breakdown: subcontractors give you *less control* and more problems with loyalty and flakiness; employees/owner-operator give you *more control, loyalty, and stability* but also more responsibility, oversight, and compliance burden. His actual decision rule is to work backward from the **goal**: what are you optimizing for, and how fast do you need to get there? He uses gym-scaling as a parallel — owner-operate vs. franchise depends on whether you want to build "the best in its niche" (an enormous long-term business) or "something you can sell quickly." Because the right answer is goal-dependent, he says he's "really bad at answering questions like this" without knowing the person's objective. The clip closes with his standard acquisition.com/roadmap promo (the free 10-stage zero-to-$100M+ roadmap broken down across eight business functions). Note: despite the packaged title framing this as contractors-vs-employees, the transcript is specifically about subcontractors vs. an owner-operator model, and the core-vs-peripheral / contractor-bench framing suggested by the topic brief does **not** appear in this clip.
