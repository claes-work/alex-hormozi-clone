# Fear Exists in the Vague

## Summary
Alex Hormozi argues that fear only survives when it is vague, and that writing out in step-by-step detail exactly what you fear will happen usually reveals the feared outcome is trivial (sleeping on a couch, a stranger insulting you online). He uses the public $100M goal for launching the *$100M Money Models* book — questioned by a team member who asked "what if we don't hit it?" — to illustrate committing to effort divorced from outcome. He frames all fear of loss as absurd given impermanence, citing Marcus Aurelius, and reports they ultimately hit $106M against the $100M target.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that fear "exists in the vague" — that spelling out a feared failure in excruciating, step-by-step detail dissolves it, because it only exists while hazy, not when crisp.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the social rules people fear breaking are largely imagined, "because nobody cares that much about anyone besides themselves."
- As of March 2026, Alex maintains commitment should be to the effort you put forth, "completely divorced of the outcome" — control the controllable at 100%, then accept what you cannot control.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims people take you as seriously as you take yourself, and that no one worth caring about faults you for "daring greatly."
- As of March 2026, Alex recounts setting a public company goal of $100M in a weekend for the *$100M Money Models* book launch, being viscerally disgusted when a team member asked what if they missed, and reports they hit $106M.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites Marcus Aurelius ("What are you so afraid of losing when nothing in this world belongs to you?") to argue fearing a permanent outcome from an impermanent existence is irrational.
