# Helping a Home Services Company Scale to $100M

## Summary
Live coaching Q&A: the owner of a property-damage / emergency-repair company ($13.5M last year, ~35% margins, mostly California, PPC-driven) is losing premium pricing as insurers drop coverage and clients start price-shopping, and wants to pivot the whole business to a Costco-style low-cost membership. Hormozi warns against blowing up the entire model and instead pushes a backend membership/upsell tested on top of the existing emergency-lead flow.

## Key advice (Alex)
- Changing the entire business model is the "Armageddon button" — exhaust smaller moves first: find customer pockets that still have full coverage, or tighten the sales process to close on first contact (blend cash up front to cover CAC + insurance on the back end).
- A one-time solution sold to someone in acute pain is far easier than a recurring solution sold to someone not in pain — recurring memberships to people without a current emergency is a harder, more competitive sale.
- He's seen many (usually venture-backed) home-services membership plays; none profitable and clean. What works: technicians who come to fix the emergency and then sell the membership on the back end.
- The 1.0 test: keep answering the emergency inquiries, but don't let that be the only sale — after the work, pitch prevention/membership. If you can't close those people, definitely don't switch the model.
- The emergency sales motion works the same regardless of severity — an AC failure in Vegas summer is as much an "emergency" as a burst pipe — so a compelling backend offer is very sellable.
