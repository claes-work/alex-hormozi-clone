# The reason you are not successful...

## Summary

Alex argues that many marketing channels appear not to work because the operator dramatically underestimates the required volume and effort. He introduces a throughput lens of volume, skill, and time, then illustrates it with flyers, ad creatives, content volume, portal re-recording, book editing, and his own early lesson that he did not know how to work.

## Key claims (Alex's advice/self-report, dated 2022-02-04)

- Alex says boring work is often avoided because it feels tedious or beneath the founder.
- He says there are only four activities that generate new customers: warm outreach, posting to an audience, cold one-to-one outreach, and advertising.
- He introduces volume, skill, and time as multiplicative inputs to throughput.
- He says beginners often need extraordinary volume because skill is low and time has not compounded.
- He self-reports initially testing only 300 flyers before learning a successful operator tested no fewer than 5,000 and could distribute 100,000/month.
- He self-reports moving content from once per week to once per day after comparing volume with Grant Cardone, and says growth increased disproportionately.
- He says the difference between an 8/10 and 10/10 effort may require far more work but can create far more outcome.
