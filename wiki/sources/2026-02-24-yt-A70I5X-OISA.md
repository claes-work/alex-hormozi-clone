# You Need to Start for Free

## Summary

Short Q&A clip. Alex Hormozi answers a beginner who is starting a marketing services
business with zero experience and wants to sell to big companies. His advice: you have
to earn your way up-market — big clients require more trust, experience, and know-how,
so start with smaller businesses and work up. With no experience, start for free to
manufacture proof: getting your first 10 *paying* customers is far slower than getting
your first 10 *free* customers. Serve the 10 free ones, then leverage their
testimonials, learnings, and examples to win the next 10 paying customers — who don't
know the first batch was free. That compounding of proof is the leverage.

## Key claims (2026-02-24, paraphrased unless quoted)

- To sell services to bigger companies, you generally have to earn your way in; bigger
  clients require more trust, experience, and know-how than beginners have. (paraphrase)
- The work it takes to serve a bigger client is roughly the same as for a smaller one —
  which is why people consistently move up-market. (paraphrase)
- Expecting to land an enterprise-scale client (e.g. "taking Amazon down as a client")
  with zero experience is highly unlikely; start with smaller businesses and work your
  way up. (paraphrase)
- With no experience, start for free — get proof before you try to get anything else.
  (paraphrase)
- Trying to make your first 10 customers paying customers takes far longer than getting
  your first 10 as free customers. (paraphrase)
- The mechanic: take 10 free customers, then leverage their testimonials, learnings, and
  examples to win the next 10 — the buyers don't know the first 10 were served for free.
  Then use those next 10 to get the following batch. This compounding is the leverage.
  (paraphrase)
