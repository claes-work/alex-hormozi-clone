# Helping a Sales Coaching and Lead Generation Agency Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client sells sales coaching plus lead generation to financial advisors, runs paid ads straight to a VSL then a phone team, is at $6.6M and wants to reach $20M+. Their stated pain is churn: they pivoted from lead gen to sales coaching because advisors can't close, and they now use a "big head, long tail" model (sell the coaching up front, sell lead gen on the back end). Alex diagnoses the problem across three fronts. First, **sales training delivery**: at Gym Launch they ran a "boiler room" that drilled and role-played trainers/front-desk staff every morning, the same way they trained their own team, broken into five rotating parts (intro, disco, offer, objections/looping, and a fifth "hot topic" day) to keep every part of the script "crisp." Second, **the churn/monetization structure**: education businesses wrongly try to build continuity out of front-end value that drops once the client has the skill, so you must separate the one-time (high-value) deliverables from the consumables — and ask what the client would still pay if they already had the skill. He warns their real lever is "more value or lower price," and endorses the big-head/long-tail with a near-no-brainer continuity so clients pay just to stay "in the network." Third, and where he pushes hardest, **it's an avatar and activation problem**: because activated clients stick, all effort should go to activating them better and faster. He splits avatars into three buckets — demographics, quantifiables (e.g. business size), and behaviors — and argues you should only sell to the higher-converting avatars even though CAC rises, because selling everyone (his personal-trainer example at Gym Launch) floods you with clients who can never pay enough to justify the resources needed to help them. The activation process is reverse-engineered from the behaviors that distinguish clients who stuck from lookalikes who didn't. He also reframes their meta-question: his team structure won't transfer because it's a different business; Gym Launch's arbitrage was community/network plus a licensed-creative lead-gen model (teach owners to run local ads once, then license fresh creative monthly) that avoided needing hundreds of account reps. On time-to-value he notes Gym Launch emailed the owner's list to produce a sale within the first 7 days.
