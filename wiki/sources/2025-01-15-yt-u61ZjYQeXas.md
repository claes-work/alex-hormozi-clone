# How To Discover Your True Passion (2025-01-15)

## Summary

Panel-style conversation in which Alex Hormozi attacks the "find your passion"
mantra, arguing you create passion by getting good at something you initially
suck at and refusing to quit. He describes his own ruthless-focus origin story
(dollars-per-hour, sacrificing everything non-essential) and reframes the goal
of life as doing meaningful "epic" work rather than chasing happiness — a
frame he says freed him from years of a happiness-obsessed funk.

## Key claims

- You don't find passion, you create it: start at something you suck at, get
  good, then enjoy the competence/status feedback loop. (paraphrase)
- Set the baseline expectation that "everything will suck forever," then become
  the person who does it anyway — starting IS the perfect condition. (paraphrase)
- Stop taking advice from successful people, because most of it describes what
  they do now, not what they did at your stage (the climb vs. the mountaintop). (paraphrase)
- His tactical engine early on was ruthless dollars-per-hour focus: after a
  first $500 sale in 30 minutes he outsourced/eliminated anything worth less
  than ~$250/half-hour (cooking, cleaning, grocery shopping). (paraphrase, self-reported)
- Writing down what you're willing to sacrifice beats to-do lists — if you
  sacrifice everything, only the one thing that matters is left (cites Seinfeld
  sitting to write; the ultimate productivity hack is "no"). (paraphrase)
- His goal is to do "epic shit" / be useful, not to be happy; meaning lasts
  longer than hedonic pleasure, and relinquishing the demand to be "happier"
  ended his funk. (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "You don't find [passion], you create it, and you create it by getting good
  at something."
- "I'd rather set the expectation that everything is terrible all the time
  forever... [then] let's see if we can become the person who can still do it

- "The ultimate productivity hack is no. You stop doing shit, and then when you

- "[My goal is] to do epic shit, genuinely — to be useful."
- "If you feel like you have potential left over, then it will eat you alive
  until you do something about it."
- "A well-lived life is one which in retrospect you are glad that you lived."
