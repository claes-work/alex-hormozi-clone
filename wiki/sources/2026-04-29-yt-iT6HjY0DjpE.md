# You Feel Like an Imposter Because You're Lying

## Summary

Alex repeats his imposter-syndrome diagnosis through an agency-owner example. The owner
felt fraudulent after leaving agency delivery to sell appointment-setting for agencies;
Alex says the fix is to state the exact facts: he was good at getting customers and bad
at retention/delivery, so he now helps agencies that are good at delivery get
appointments. Alex argues that honest deficiencies make positioning more compelling, not
less.

## Key claims

- Alex says he avoids imposter syndrome by stating facts and telling the truth.
- If a founder feels fraudulent, Alex looks for a mismatch between the real story and the
  public positioning.
- A failed or partial background can still become a strong offer if the founder honestly
  isolates the skill that worked.
- Stating deficiencies can make a founder seem more authentic and persuasive.
- Alex says honest positioning can both reduce imposter feelings and sell more.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "State the facts and tell the truth."

> "You're lying and that's why you feel like an impostor."

> "If you were just honest about your deficiencies, you will become more compelling."
