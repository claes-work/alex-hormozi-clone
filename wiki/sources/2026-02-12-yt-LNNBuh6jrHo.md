# I'm a Ghostwriter. How Do I Write Better Content?

## Summary
A caller who works as a brand ghostwriter for executives on LinkedIn tells Alex Hormozi that despite posting daily, his reach is stuck at roughly 200 views per post, and asks how to break through the plateau. Hormozi first notes the irony that a ghostwriter's own content is stuck, then guesses the caller is leaning heavily on generic GPT/AI output that reads like everyone else's AI content on LinkedIn.

His core prescription is differentiation through things AI cannot produce: AI "cannot live in reality," so real narratives, personal stories, real businesses, and concrete examples are what nobody else can copy. He contrasts this with commodity content — if he lists "the six steps to XYZ," a hundred other people will list the same six steps, but the specific examples and proof he uses are uncopyable. Because the caller is presumably trying to win more clients, Hormozi argues he should produce the kind of story- and example-driven content that executives on LinkedIn would actually consume.

Hormozi then diagnoses the likely root cause: the caller probably doesn't yet have enough of his own customers to draw stories from. His fix is to take on clients for free or at steep discounts to build a track record and accumulate stories to tell, which he expects will make the content more valuable and more interesting.
