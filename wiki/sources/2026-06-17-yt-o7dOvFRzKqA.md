# He Charges $300. I'd Charge $3,000.

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a four-location fitness-center owner with group training, one-on-one support, and a 24/7 open-gym component. Alex says the marketing does not need to explain the whole ecosystem upfront: market the thing prospects already want, then deliver the broader thing they need after they enter. When the owner asks whether to lead with a powerful personal transformation story, Alex says not to bolt on a personal brand if the goal is to build a sellable business that currently does not require one.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - fitness-center owner. His revenue streams, locations, open-gym retention idea, personal story, and exit goal are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Prospects do not need to understand the full nuance of the business model before buying.
- Marketing should lead with the thing the customer wants.
- Delivery can then give the customer the broader thing they need.
- Trying to explain the entire ecosystem in marketing creates unnecessary friction.
- A founder's personal story may create stickiness, but it can also create founder dependence.
- If the owner wants to sell the business and already has a model that does not require personal brand, he should not tack one on.
- Personal brand can make sense for inspiration-driven acquisition, but it may reduce sellability when the business otherwise stands alone.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You market the thing they want."

> "When they come in, you give them the thing that they need."

> "Don't try and explain the thing that they need before you give them the thing they want."

> "You already have a business that doesn't require a personal brand. I wouldn't tack one on."
