# Reuse Your Best Hooks Forever

## Summary

In this short Q&A clip, Alex Hormozi is asked to share his framework for writing hooks, given that he reportedly spends about 90% of his time on them. His answer is deliberately simple: he does not invent most hooks from scratch. Instead, he reviews everything he has done before, rewrites new hooks from his old proven ones, and reuses his "greatest hits" repeatedly. He makes each reused hook look fresh by changing the background, shirt, and props while keeping the hook itself more or less the same.

Roughly 80% of his effort goes to this bread-and-butter of proven hooks, and 20% goes to testing new ones. To source new material, he deliberately stays a non-premium (ad-supported) user of every platform so he is served ads, and he watches both viral organic content and high-performing ads. He saves all of it into a folder, then pulls it up in ad-creation mode and adapts hooks across niches (e.g. reworking a hotel or weight-loss hook for a different vertical).

The underlying principle is that the hook is everything: the first 3 to 5 seconds decide whether the audience stays, and roughly 80% of viewers are lost in that opening window. His conclusion is to concentrate 80% of creative effort on the hook, after which the rest of the content becomes easy. The clip closes with a promotional offer for a free $100M scaling road map.
