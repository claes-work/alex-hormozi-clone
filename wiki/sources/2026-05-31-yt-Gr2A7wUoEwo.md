# Why You Should Always Build a Business Top-Down

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues for building top-down by selling to the top 1% first. Using Tesla as the example, he says starting with a high-priced, limited-production product creates a stronger brand narrative and reduces operational strain. Moving downmarket later is easier than moving up from a budget position. Serving the mass market can work, but requires thin margins, extraordinary volume, and enough capital/operations to support it.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Selling to the top 1% is often one of the most effective ways to build a business.
- A high-priced first product can reinforce brand narrative and make later lower-priced versions feel aspirational.
- Moving from high-end to more affordable is more believable than moving from budget to luxury.
- Operationally, serving fewer high-ticket customers is easier than serving mass-market volume before the company has the capacity.
- The bottom of the market has money, but only at thin margins and extraordinary volume.
- Without capital and systems to scale to the masses, founders can end up squeezing low-spending customers too hard.
