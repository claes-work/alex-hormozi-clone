# How I Started Gym Launch

/ [[../../persona/biography|Biography]] · [[../entities/gym-launch|Gym Launch]]

## Summary

In this short clip Alex Hormozi recounts, in first person, the origin of Gym Launch:
he ran a chain of gyms, was unusually good at filling them via Facebook ads, and
turned that skill into a done-for-you service where he flew out and sold memberships
in other owners' gyms. That model scaled to ~32 gyms in ~18 months with a six-person
sales team but broke down because he "didn't control delivery" (struggling owners
couldn't retain the clients he sold), creating chargeback risk, and the business died.
The pivot came when, while trying to exit the gym business, he priced the "how-to" kit
at $6,000 to a desperate owner, got an instant yes, and made ~$60,000 in a day "selling
air" — the licensing model that became Gym Launch, finishing that year at ~$6.8M and
the next at ~$26M (~$17M profit), sold to private equity in 2021 for $46M. The clip
also contains a detailed, previously-uncatalogued vignette of how Leila decided to join
him. All content is self-reported.

## Key claims

- He was "pretty good at filling gyms up before we'd open them up"; the business
  constraint became simply needing more gyms to fill, so he offered the fill-up service
  to other owners.
- Method: fly out, set up a table, sell ~200 memberships in ~20 days — via "just
  Facebook ads in 2013" (the "golden era," when a bikini + "lose weight fast" creative
  worked).
- Economics: getting "30 to 1 up front" — put ~$1,000 into ads and make ~$30k or ~$100k
  in 30 days; says he was 26 at the time.
- Scaled the done-for-you turnarounds to ~32 gyms in the next ~18 months with a sales
  team of six guys flying out.
- The DFY model broke down because he "didn't control delivery": the struggling owners
  who hired him lacked trainers/systems, told the sold clients to leave and take
  refunds, creating a "big chargeback risk." That business died.
- Pivot to licensing: instead of flying people out, sell the kit/"how-to." Every month
  the number of gym slots had to match the number of sales guys (~8 gyms/month), which
  he found unscalable.
- Deciding to exit, he quoted a desperate owner "the highest number I could think of" —
  $6,000 — to deter him; got an instant "It's done," then ~7 more calls, ~$60,000 in a
  day. Escalated pricing on subsequent calls: 6 → 8 → 10 → 12 (thousand), all "done."
- Result: Gym Launch "took off like a rocket" — ~$6.8M that year, ~$26M the next with
  ~$17M profit (says he was ~28); sold to a private equity firm in 2021 for $46[M].
