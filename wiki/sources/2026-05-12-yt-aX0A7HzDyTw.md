# The Two Questions Every Sales Rep Must Ask

## Summary

A B2B SaaS-service agency founder says his company does about $1.2 million trailing
twelve months but remains bottlenecked because he is the only reliable closer and
ascension salesperson. Alex diagnoses the front-end issue as weak discovery. He tells
the founder to drill two questions, "Tell me more about that" and "Can you give me an
example?", train one section of the sales process each day, correct roleplays in real
time, take ascension calls personally for now, and raise prices because the offer may be
so cheap that sophisticated buyers do not believe it.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex separates the founder's problem into front-end sales duplication and back-end
  ascension.
- He says the salesperson is likely failing at discovery rather than the whole sales
  process.
- He recommends drilling two questions repeatedly: "Tell me more about that" and "Can
  you give me an example?"
- He says these questions are preferable to repeatedly asking "why" because they dig for
  pain without sounding as confrontational.
- He says founder-led consultative selling often cannot be handed to a team unless the
  script makes the process teachable.
- He recommends roleplaying different parts of the call on different days: intro,
  discovery, pitch, close, and the weakest area.
- He says the pitch should be short, roughly 60 to 90 seconds, and built around three
  components with strong proof examples.
- He tells the founder to correct mistakes in real time during roleplay, then repeat a
  corrected move several times to lock it in.
- He says the offer is probably underpriced for buyers with $30,000+ annual contract
  value and may be below the point of marginal cheapness.
- He recommends the founder drill discovery, raise the price, and personally take
  ascension calls.
