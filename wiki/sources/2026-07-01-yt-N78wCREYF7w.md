# I Can't Make the Hats Fast Enough - $2M Cowboy Hat Owner

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a custom cowboy-hat founder with high demand, strong organic following, high ROAS, and a supply constraint because master hatmaking is scarce. Alex reframes the constraint as talent acquisition and pricing. If the business has pricing power and margin, it should pay more to attract rare craft talent, potentially raise prices to fund talent, and use the dying-trade narrative as recruiting alpha. He allows pushing delivery latency from six to eight months if that generates cash without materially hurting conversion, but the durable fix is supply first.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Custom hat founder** - the coachee. His revenue, followers, ROAS, prices, location, production process, and delivery wait are background only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If demand is effectively unlimited but delivery cannot keep up, the constraint is recruiting and training, not marketing.
- A dying trade can be a recruiting advantage: struggling artisans may value a stable, better-paid opportunity doing the craft they already love.
- If a business is supply-constrained and making money hand over fist, it is probably underpricing talent.
- The founder should calculate how much more he must pay to attract hatmakers to Kentucky, then test whether margins still work at current prices or after a price increase.
- "Jack the price, jack the talent" if customer acquisition and margin survive the increase.
- Alex says Acquisition.com pays high-level operators far more than $100K because A-player knowledge can justify it; the same logic applies to rare craft talent.
- If the business already has multi-month wait times, pushing from six months to eight months may not materially change conversion because both feel like "forever."
- The best durable sequence remains supply first, even if a temporary demand push can generate cash.
