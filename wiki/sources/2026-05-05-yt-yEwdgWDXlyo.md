# Your YouTube Description Is Costing You Subscribers

## Summary

In a channel/page review for a chess creator, Alex says the channel is fundamentally sound
but the description/about area should give strangers more origin story and
self-edification. Existing audiences already know the creator; new viewers need a concise
reason to trust the creator. He repeats the principle: do not brag, just state what you
have done and tell the truth.

## Key claims

- Creators make content to bring new people into their world, so channel descriptions
  should not assume prior familiarity.
- The description/about page is a place to explain who the creator is and establish
  credibility for strangers.
- "Don't brag, just say what you've done" is the tone he recommends.
- For rank-dominant niches such as chess, visible credentials/rank likely matter more
  because the audience uses rank as a credibility shortcut.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "People don't know who you are."

> "Don't brag, just say what you've done."
