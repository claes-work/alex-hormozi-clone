# Scaling a Pro Volleyball Brand Past $150K/Month

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a professional volleyball player with a large audience who is monetizing mostly through one-off brand deals. Alex frames professional athletes and sports personalities as media companies, then lays out monetization paths: stay entertainment-first and sell sponsorships, become an educator and sell training products/services, or partner with a company through equity or royalty economics.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - professional volleyball player and creator. His follower count, deal flow, and sport context are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A professional athlete with an audience is effectively a media company because sports are entertainment.
- Entertainment content should keep people watching and deepen audience connection beyond repeated highlight clips.
- The sponsorship path is simpler operationally but more limited: it depends on outbound sales to brands and continued audience attention.
- The education path can monetize more deeply through training programs, apps, workouts, spike feedback, or other volleyball-performance offers, but it creates delivery work.
- A third path is to become the spokesperson for a company the athlete likes and negotiate equity, royalty, or traffic-based upside.
- Merch and signed products can work if the athlete's audience has enough demand for them.
