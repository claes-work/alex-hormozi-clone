# Doing Everything Is Keeping You Stuck

## Summary
In this clip Alex Hormozi coaches a book-publishing entrepreneur (~$1M revenue, targeting $3.2M) who runs every acquisition channel at once — referrals, organic/speaking, cold email, and Facebook ads. Hormozi's advice is to stop diffusing effort across all channels and instead concentrate on the highest-leverage one: the owner makes significant revenue every time he speaks yet only speaks about once a year. Hormozi frames the fix as simply doing far more of the proven activity, using a friend who scaled a real estate brokerage via hundreds of yearly speeches as the model.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that spreading effort across every acquisition channel ("doing all of them") is a sign of unfocused scaling; the leverage question is which channel takes the least time or costs the least money while producing the most revenue.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that if a single activity (e.g. one yearly speaking event) already produces significant revenue, the obvious growth move is to multiply its frequency — potentially 50x-ing the business by doing 50 events instead of one.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites a close friend who took a publicly traded real estate brokerage from $200M to $1.2B in revenue over 24 months primarily by speaking at real estate events (66 speeches in Q4, 270+ per year), attributing the result to sheer volume most competitors underestimate.
- As of March 2026, Alex says getting booked on stages follows the same "Core Four" outreach playbook (do outreach, post content, reach out to people), and that paying for slots or getting a booth-plus-speaking-slot are always available ways to get on stage.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You're doing all the acquisition channels."

> "He rightly identified that he simply needed to do more."

> "As soon as he said, 'I only do one speaking event a year,' I was like, 'I don't need to know anymore.' Like if that's a significant amount of revenue, and it's one day once a year, I see that and think, 'Okay, well we could 50x the business if we just did 50 of those.'"

> "So either that's going to be which thing is taking you the least amount of time that makes you the most amount of money, or which one's the thing that cost you the least amount of money that makes you the most amount of money. So it's either the time leverage or it's money leverage or both."

> "No one understands how much more we do than them."

> "But then that's you follow the same core four. You do the outreach, you post content, and then you reach out to people to find out. And sometimes you have to pay to be on the stages, sometimes you get a booth and then they'll give you a speaking slot. There's always ways to get on if you need to."
