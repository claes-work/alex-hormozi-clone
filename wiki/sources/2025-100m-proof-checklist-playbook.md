# $100M Proof Checklist Playbook (2025)

**What it is.** A ~25-page Acquisition.com playbook subtitled "How to Get Strangers to Believe You Instantly," in which Hormozi argues that *proof* — not promises — is the real differentiator in marketing, and lays out a 13-point checklist for making proof more compelling. It opens with a July-2024 origin story: after the fund that bought Gym Launch reported that GL's ad costs had doubled, Hormozi had four hours before a lunch meeting to diagnose the problem, ranked his "top 50 ads of all time," and found the single variable separating winners from losers was proof. Raw text: `raw/books/2025-100m-proof-checklist-playbook.md` (Google Drive export; OCR artifacts present, cited approximately by section).

## Thesis

Everyone can make the same promise, but no one can have the same proof. Prospects' biggest obstacle to buying is *risk* — the fear the thing won't work for them, the way they want, on the timeline they want. Marketing and sales exist to move a prospect along a **Belief Continuum** (from 100% doubt toward 100% certainty), and proof is what moves them. Since claims get copied but results cannot be, proof — not the offer or "unique mechanism" — is what cuts through a competitive market. It is especially the up-and-comer's biggest weapon.

## Structure and content

1. **Start Here** — the July 12, 2024 Gym Launch ad-cost story and Mr. X's advice: compare best-performing assets to current ones, bucket them, and find the differences. Discovery: 80% of winning ads had no education, weren't offer-focused, didn't put Hormozi center-stage, and told one story — "This. Product. Works."
2. **How Proof Works** — defines proof and advertising claims; the Belief Continuum; the "Match Your Claims To Your Proof" pro tip; "Your promise is not a differentiator. Your proof is."
3. **How I Make Proof More Compelling** — spectrum from "result before they buy" (live demos, free work) to a faceless 10-year-old testimonial; introduces the 13 dimensions.
4. **Proof Checklist** — the 13 dimensions, each with a "Make It Happen" tactic and example.
5. **Summary / Why You Should Actually Do This** — proof works at every step (click → close → retain); two lists of upside (do it right) and downside (do it wrong).

### The 13 proof-compelling dimensions

1. **In-Person > Virtual** — seeing with one's own eyes beats a digital version; fly best customers to events.
2. **Live > Recorded** — a live testimonial ("living proof") beats the same story on video.
3. **Raw > Processed** — least-tampered, iPhone-shot testimonials read as most authentic; build testimonial prompts into the customer journey.
4. **Show > Tell** — visual before/after beats description (why unboxing videos convert); capture emotional milestone moments (e.g. final weigh-ins).
5. **Others Saying Your Stuff's Great > You Saying It** — you are the most biased person alive; add a review-request step to the customer journey.
6. **Identical to Them > Opposite of Them** — feature people who literally look like the target market (age, gender, ethnicity); the ad's cast determines who you attract.
7. **Personal > Generic** — specific, vivid pain ("this is me") beats generic language; probe in interviews for concrete before/after detail.
8. **Big Results > Small Results > No Results** — bigger results are more compelling; small still beats none; pay/invest to capture your biggest.
9. **Newer Proof > Older Proof** — stale testimonials imply no recent wins; constantly refresh (Amazon has "most recent," never "sort by oldest").
10. **More Proof > Less Proof** — quantity can overcome quality limits; walls/galleries of hundreds-to-thousands of reviews; floor-to-ceiling in-person.
11. **Third-Party Verification > Zero Verification** — accreditations, awards, certifications, endorsements ("believe them, not me").
12. **Proof With Numbers > Proof Without Numbers** — plainly stated numbers (with how/when collected) are hard to argue; visualize with charts.
13. **Metaphors > Technical Jargon** — connect the unknown (mechanism) to the known via 2-3 metaphors; taught to sales staff and used in ads.

## Key claims

- Proof is defined as *data that supports your advertising claims*; an isolated instance (a client's weight loss week-to-week) is a "proof point" (Proof Checklist, "How Proof Works").
- An *advertising claim* is anything you say has happened, might happen, or will happen about what you sell; the more compelling the claim, the more proof quantity/quality it requires (2025, "How Proof Works").
- Your promise/claim is not a differentiator because rivals will copy it; only proof (results others cannot reproduce) differentiates — stated from Hormozi's own experience (2025, thesis).
- Belief is not binary but a continuum from 100% doubt to 100% certainty; proof's job is to move prospects toward certainty (2025, "How Proof Works").
- Pro tip "Match Your Claims To Your Proof": collect data first, then make only claims the data already supports — "claim your proof" rather than prove your claim; if proof isn't compelling, "fix reality" (give free services, improve the product) instead of escalating claim wars (2025).
- Diagnosing the July 2024 Gym Launch ad-cost doubling, Hormozi found 80% of winning ads were pure proof — no education, not offer-focused, not him center-stage — telling one story: "This product works" (2025, "Start Here").
- Working for free, to the extent affordable, can be the most persuasive proof because it delivers results *before* the prospect buys (the extreme end of the compelling-proof spectrum) (2025, "How I Make Proof More Compelling").
- **Datapoint:** 54.1% of people who start a paid community on Skool make money (pulled July 2024) (2025, "Proof With Numbers").
- **Datapoint:** in their first complete year, the average Gym Lord adds over $100,000 in profit to their gym (2025, "Proof With Numbers").
- **Datapoint:** the average Acquisition.com portfolio company increased its EBITDA by 3.1x in its first 12 months (2025, "Proof With Numbers").
- Acquisition.com's brand is largely built off two big results: Hormozi's sale of Gym Launch and the size of the portfolio (2025, "Big Results").
- Cale, Hormozi's successor at Gym Launch, took his gym from a handful of members to 200+, fully outsourced, and sold it within 18 months for $250k; his testimonial converted better than any other (2025, "Big Results").
- Hormozi ran a proof page with over 5,000 testimonials and a book-a-call CTA every 3-5 testimonials; people reported staying up all night reading it (2025, "More Proof").
- Proof compounds across the whole funnel — click, opt-in, sale (live or recorded), and post-purchase retention — and is one of the rare business assets you can never have too much of (2025, "Summary").
