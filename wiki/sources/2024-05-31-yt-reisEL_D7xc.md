# 13 Years of Marketing Advice in 85 Mins

## Summary

Solo 85-minute compilation in which Alex Hormozi distills what he frames as 13 years of
marketing experience into 13 numbered lessons, spanning offer/pricing sequencing, ad
creative production, unit economics, market selection, and content strategy. It is a
back-to-basics greatest-hits talk built on his signature frameworks (more/better/new,
volume negates luck, the value equation, LTV:CAC, the Core Four, the five levels of
awareness). Content overlaps heavily with the "14 years of marketing advice" masterclass
([[../topics/marketing/copywriting-hacks|copywriting-hacks]] source, `JDR-R--4HhM`) — treat
as a sibling recap rather than net-new material.

## Key claims

- **Start at low prices or free, then ratchet up.** Free/discounted first customers pay
  off four ways: they convert to paying, refer, leave testimonials/reviews, and give
  feedback. His own arc: first fitness clients "donated to charity" in exchange for
  training; Gym Launch gym turnarounds done free across 30+ markets before licensing to
  5,000+ locations. Tactic: after a first batch of ~10-20 case studies, raise price ~20%
  every 5 sales until conversion-rate × price starts to fall — that reveals the sweet spot.
- **more / better / new (in that order).** When starting, *more* is almost always the
  answer; volume is usually 100x, not 2-3x, what beginners do. Shift to *better* only when
  the return on optimization exceeds one incremental unit of *more*. Do *new* last, because
  the cost of change is guaranteed but the return is not — spend ~90% of effort on more/better
  of what already works, ~10% on new.
- **Paid ads are an auction for attention; win on LTV.** Whoever makes the most per customer
  can outbid everyone to acquire them — an "ethical monopoly." The chiropractor agency doing
  $2M/yr had not "saturated Facebook"; competitors' spend is your opportunity.
- **Optimize advertising front-to-back; build the business back-to-front.** Cites David Ogilvy
  ("after you've written your headline you've spent 80 cents of your advertising dollar"). He
  spends ~10x effort on the first 5 seconds / hook. Dean's Larry King infomercial flopped at
  $250K until he re-shot only the first 30 seconds → $100M in sales. A portfolio company lifted
  booking rate 62% by changing only the landing-page headline (opt-in rate unchanged).
- **LTV:CAC (he prefers LT*GP*) is the one metric he'd pick.** LTV = lifetime gross profit,
  not revenue. Rule of thumb ≥ 3:1; ideally recover CAC within the first 30 days. Starbucks
  ~$14K LTV lets them spend absurdly. His biggest wealth came from 30:1–200:1 windows — dump
  cash in while they last; ratios compress as you scale (100:1 → 5:1).
- **Ad-creation process:** always collecting swipe files; weekly session, same day he records;
  reviews best historical ads; writes ~50 hooks (40 recycled proven, 10 new), 3-5 "meat"
  angles, 1-3 CTAs. **Kaleidoscope process** — squeeze a winning ad 10 ways (new backdrop,
  props, re-enact, reorder, filters, VFX, fonts/captions, pacing, music, reuse the hook).
- **The Core Four** ways to let people know about your stuff: warm outreach, cold outreach,
  content (one-to-many, people who know you), paid ads (one-to-many, strangers). Sub-$3M owners
  should spend the first 4 hours of every day advertising.
- **State the facts and tell the truth.** "Clickbait backed by truth." If you don't track, you
  don't care; measurement itself is intervention. Present data as % of people who achieve X
  outcome in Y time under Z conditions — fewer conditions = more compelling.
- **Say/show what only you can say/show — "how I" vs "how to."** Do epic stuff, then talk about
  what you did. Value is *value per second*, not seconds of value. Show > tell (play a real
  lead call; live software demo; the door-to-door cleaner scrubbing gum off his gym turf).
- **Scaling ladder:** 0→6 figures: one product, one avatar, one channel. 6→7: same, but
  consistently. 7→8: add a 2nd product (upsell). Multiple 8: add a 2nd channel. Stay on one
  avatar well past 8 figures.
- **Expanding the market — 5 directions:** up-market, down-market, adjacent, narrower, broader.
  But most people should get *narrower/better* first. Learned from Vista Equity's head of
  pricing: "we know the customer better than they do" — find the 20% that drives 80%, cut the
  rest, expand the 20% to 100% (same-size business, ~5x revenue). "Better creates bigger;
  bigger creates bloat."
- **The value equation** (from *$100M Offers*): dream outcome, perceived likelihood of
  achievement (↑), time delay (↓ = speed), effort & sacrifice (↓ = ease).
- **Give away the secrets, sell the implementation** — make your free stuff better than their
  paid stuff. 99% who consume your free thing never buy, so your reputation is made by that 99%.
  Registered-dietitian example: <$1M/yr profit from only 5,800 hyper-niche Instagram followers.
- **All advertising works — it's a matter of efficiency.** Scaling ceilings usually mean your
  ads aren't good enough to reach the next of Eugene Schwartz's five levels of awareness
  (unaware → problem → solution → product → most aware). "The size of the plane is directly
  correlated with the length of the runway" (price + coldness = how much selling is needed).
- **We need to be reminded more than we need to be taught.** "The news never changes, just the
  names." Reruns get rewatched; new audience never saw the old content; creators tire of their
  own content long before the audience notices — Henry Ford: "We haven't even started running
  it yet."
