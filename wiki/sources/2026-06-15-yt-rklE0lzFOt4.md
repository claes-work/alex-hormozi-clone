# How to Scale a Business in a Legally Gray Industry

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a psychedelic mushroom e-commerce business operating in a legal gray area where ads and organic accounts are difficult. Alex names the enemy as irrelevance: not enough people know the business exists. He treats organic content as advertising and recommends massively increasing output by posting cross-platform, turning transcripts into posts and infographics, batching ideation/recording/editing, and adding safer CTAs to a diagnostic or guide lead magnet. He discourages moving to long-form or live formats before short-form is systematized.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - psychedelic mushroom e-commerce founder. His revenue, legal-gray context, content constraints, account-risk concerns, and product details are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The core enemy is irrelevance: not enough people know the business exists.
- Content should be treated as advertising.
- Posting one reel per day is not enough if marketing is the constraint.
- Content should be distributed cross-platform to Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Facebook, LinkedIn video, X, and similar surfaces.
- Transcripts can become posts, and transcripts can also become infographics, multiplying output.
- End-to-end make-and-post production is inefficient; ideation, recording, and editing should be batched.
- Planning hooks, trends, memes, and formats before filming can dramatically increase output.
- A diagnostic or guide lead magnet can create a safer CTA and provide many hooks without creating a new lead magnet for every post.
- Short-form should be mastered and systematized for three to six months before adding long-form, live, or other layers.
- Advertising channels should be stacked like a foundation rather than repeatedly abandoned for the next tactic.
