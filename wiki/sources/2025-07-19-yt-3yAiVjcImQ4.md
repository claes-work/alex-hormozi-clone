# I Blew Up a Business Through 3 Small Changes (Just Copy Me)

## Summary

Alex Hormozi walks through a teardown of an attorney's (lawyer/legal services)
business that was losing ~$100,000/year because its model was broken, and shows the
changes he made to fix it. He organizes the fixes around four problems — an unfocused
"help everyone" product mix, leaky/undirected traffic, an hourly-billing trap, and the
absence of a repeatable sales process — and maps them to his scaling roadmap (the
client was at "stage four, prioritize," constraint = product). Core thesis: businesses
behave in patterns, so the same patterns of fixes recur. He plugs his upcoming book
*$100M Money Models* and its August 16 launch event, and the free assessment tool at
acquisition.com/roadmap.

## Key claims

- **Scaling rule "111": one product, one avatar, one channel until $1M/year** — sell one
  thing, to one person, in one way, until you hit a million. This is the frame the whole
  teardown returns to (find the one ideal customer / ICP to sell the one product to on
  one channel).
- **Problem 1 — "help everyone" / kill the vampire.** The attorney launched courses and
  software "to give back"; the software was losing money and eating time that should
  have gone to the profitable services (the cash cow). Fix (change #1): **kill the
  unprofitable software** ("the vampire" sucking cash flow), **reposition the cheap
  education as *free* education** as a front end that ascends people into the services,
  and **double down on services by re-pricing** — he saw an "easy double or triple for
  the right avatar." Don't average A/B/C avatars into a 2x price when an A client can
  spend 10x a C client; serve the A's (ICP) first.
- **Problem 2 — broken/leaky traffic.** Leads came from YouTube → Instagram, but with no
  CTA telling viewers to go there and no profile prompt to DM. Five fixes: (1) add CTAs
  at the ~30% mark of YouTube videos to go DM on Instagram; (2–4) put the same CTA in the
  description, the pinned comment, and the YouTube profile; (5) proactively DM new
  followers ("Hey, you here for free stuff or do you want help with your legal stuff?")
  to both capture and qualify leads. Principle: take existing customer behavior and
  "lubricate it," don't break it. Estimated $20–50k/month was being left on the table.
- **Problem 3 — hourly-billing trap.** He charged $250/hour; average client value ~$1,500
  (≈6 hours). Clients came for a **result, not hours**, so hourly under-priced the
  outcome and made him a commodity. Fixes: **ditch hourly for $5,800 upfront** (a high
  close rate signals under-pricing), add a **small performance commission**, and create
  an **A/B offer** — the "B" being a **$25,000 zero-commission anchor** that makes the
  $5,800 look like a steal. Price on value/dream outcome, not time/features.
- **Problem 4 — no sales process (ad hoc).** Fix it into a repeatable motion:
  (1) a 5–7 minute **VSL** (video sales letter) answering who/what/where/when/why + FAQs
  in the prospect's own words; (2) **BANT** qualification (Budget, Authority, Need,
  Timing — "from IBM in the '60s"; sell to people who say today/tomorrow);
  (3) convert consultation → **sales call with a script** using his **CLOSER** framework
  (Clarify, Label, Overview past experience, Sell the vacation, Explain concerns,
  Reinforce) and the **+ - - + - - +** persuasion structure (more good / less bad if you
  work with me; more bad / less good if you don't); (4) a **downsell** (~$2–3k
  do-it-yourself) so there are anchors on both sides making the $5,800 core offer the
  best path.
- **Businesses behave in patterns**, so they can be solved with the same patterns — the
  roadmap diagnosis (stage four "prioritize," product constraint) prescribes: specialize
  the product, re-price for the niched-down avatar, make better free stuff, add
  qualification/friction so extra volume self-selects.
- **TL;DR (his three points):** keep the main thing the main thing and cut what's not
  working; traffic is useless without a clear direction; price based on value, not time.
