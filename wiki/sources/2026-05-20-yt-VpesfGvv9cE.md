# Get RICH in the AI Revolution 2026

## Summary

Alex Hormozi lays out an AI operating system for small businesses: AI hygiene, prompts as
SOPs, and data sources from work the business has already done. He says AI should
outsource typing, not thinking, and that the idea remains the alpha. The practical system
is context, clear prompt instructions, and relevant data sources.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Start a new AI chat for each unrelated topic.
- More iterations and feedback loops inside the same chat improve output quality.
- Facts, figures, and cited studies from AI outputs should be verified.
- Decision-making remains the owner's responsibility; AI should not replace thinking.
- AI should outsource typing, not judgment.
- The idea is still the highest-value part of content, sales, and offers.
- A prompt is an SOP: a standard operating procedure for producing an output.
- Most business prompts are too short because they try to hand off complex work in one
  sentence.
- Repeated workflows should have standardized prompts stored in a prompt repository.
- Prompts can be organized by role, master docs, folders, or repeated calendar blocks.
- Existing business output is data: tweets, emails, sales-call transcripts, support
  transcripts, lead-nurture sequences, video transcripts, FAQs, and VSLs can become AI
  data sources.
- AI sounds generic when it has no business context, voice sample, or data source to
  pattern-match against.
- A business can use sales calls to generate scripts, VSLs, emails, follow-up, and sales
  feedback.
- Prompt chains can turn an offer into a sales page, sales-call script, and VSL.
- Long prompts narrow output; short prompts often create broad, long, unfocused answers.
- The three wheels of better AI output are business context, strong prompts, and data
  sources.
- AI should be used like a workhorse to increase output while keeping quality, not to make
  decisions for the operator.
