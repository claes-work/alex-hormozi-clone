# You Need Recurring Revenue

## Summary

Alex Hormozi tells a real-estate-agent coaching and event founder that the business has a
promotion engine but lacks revenue retention. He says if he rebuilt Gym Launch, he would
focus completely on revenue retention until the product no longer churned. He also warns
that better entrepreneurs get better shiny objects and should test whether the current
business can run without the founder before starting something new.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The founder's $12 million per year coaching/event business appears strong at promotion
  but weak at revenue retention.
- Alex says the key problem is revenue retention: finding a product or service customers
  do not cancel.
- If he redid Gym Launch, he says he would focus 100% on revenue retention until it was
  solved.
- Before starting a new business, the founder should test whether the current business
  can run without him for 3 to 6 months.
- Shiny objects become more attractive as a person becomes more capable.
- New opportunities can steal attention from the current business through fantasy rather
  than demonstrated economics.
- Regret comparisons often ignore the downside of the alternative path.
- For a real-estate-agent audience, vertical stacking into brokerage, title, escrow, or
  related services may monetize existing attention better than only more events.
- Broader local-business coaching may work only if the founder has proof cases, a strong
  VSL, and viral acquisition that match the new avatar.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Revenue retention."

> "The better you are, the better the opportunities you have to say no to."
