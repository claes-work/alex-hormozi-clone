# Why You Misjudge Risk & Miss Opportunities

## Summary

Alex says people over-detect threats, overstate downside, and understate upside. He
connects this to business and life decisions: asking someone out, starting a business,
taking a loan, selling, or making content. His practical frame is to "play it out" in
specific steps until the actual downside is clear and often much smaller than the vague
fear.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- People detect more threats than opportunities.
- They overemphasize how catastrophic threats could be and underestimate possible upside.
- This mismatch makes people bad at risk-adjusted return bets in finance and ordinary
  life.
- A 50% chance of a 10x with a 50% chance of zero can be a rational shot to take.
- Alex calls one frame "play it out": make the vague fear specific.
- Fear exists in the vague, not the specific.
- Playing out a feared failure often reveals that food, shelter, and breathing remain.
- The true downside can be close to nothing while the upside may be everything the person
  wanted.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "We rarely identify the upside."

> "Fear exists in the vague not in the specific."

> "My actual downside risk is nothing, but my upside is everything I've wanted."
