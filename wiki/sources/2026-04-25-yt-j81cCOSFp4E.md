# How to Win When Everything Takes Longer Than Expected

## Summary

Alex says worthwhile goals are harder, slower, and more expensive than expected, and that
the pain usually reflects poor expectations rather than bad reality. Difficulty is part of
why the reward exists: if the path were easy, more people would already have the result. He
also attacks entitlement as expecting life or other people to give something without it
being earned, and compares success to running a marathon with no known finish line.

## Key claims

- Alex says hard goals usually take longer and cost more than expected because the
  difficulty is what keeps the reward scarce.
- He frames the person becoming harder as more important than the achievement itself.
- He says entitlement is the expectation that life or other people should give someone
  something without earning it.
- He says the world gives credit for achievement, not for trying.
- He reframes complaints about difficulty as false expectations about the price tag of the
  goal.
- He compares success to running a marathon, not watching one: outsiders see the start and
  finish, while the runner lives through the long middle.
- In success, unlike a marked race, the person often has to keep running without knowing
  whether the finish line is one mile away or 300.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's always harder, takes longer, and costs more than you think it will."

> "The world doesn't work that way. It gives you credit for achieving."

> "People think success is like watching a marathon, not running a marathon."

> "You have to get used to running alone."
