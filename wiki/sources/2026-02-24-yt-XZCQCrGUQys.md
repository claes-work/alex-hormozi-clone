# AI Will Only Make Your Services Easier to Deliver

★ **L3-candidate for `persona/beliefs.md`** — a dated (2026-02-24), forward-looking
position on AI's impact on service businesses. His view on AI may evolve, so promote
it as a dated belief rather than a timeless one.

## Summary

A short solo clip in which Alex Hormozi answers a fearful question from a video
editor worried that AI will erase the scope of their work. His response: don't fear
AI — harness it. For the short-to-medium term he sees "tremendous leverage" in AI
because it makes a service provider's job far easier to scale, potentially letting
someone do 100x the volume with the same effort. He reframes the common "AI is coming
for everyone's job" fear through the technology-adoption curve: disruption is real but
gradual (early adopters → laggards → never-adopters), and plenty of businesses still
make money without adopting new tech at all — his examples are firms that still use
fax machines or run purely in-person with paper and clipboards. His key distinction:
as long as it is *humans competing against humans using tools*, the human who wields
the best tools wins — and that's how it has always been. Only when the contest becomes
*AI versus human directly* does AI win. For now, his advice is to use AI to grab as
much leverage as possible rather than fearing it.
