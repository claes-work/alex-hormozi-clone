# Why Money Stops Buying Happiness After $70K

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that spending money effectively is a skill most people never acquire.
Beyond the obvious categories of house, car, food, travel, and clothes, money can buy back
time, improve the daily environment, and create memories that pay "memory dividends." He
repeats his practical time-buyback math: about $1,500/month can recover roughly 90 hours
per month through help with food, cleaning, driving, and clothing logistics.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Spending money well has skill levels; earning more does not automatically teach someone
  how to spend effectively.
- Many people stop at the obvious status or comfort categories and miss money's higher-ROI
  uses.
- Buying back routine time is one of the easiest high-return uses of money.
- Alex estimates that roughly $1,500/month can buy back about 90 hours/month by outsourcing
  food, cleaning, driving, and clothing preparation.
- For an entrepreneur, an investor may get very high ROI by buying back 100 hours/month of
  the entrepreneur's time.
- The trade is rational when the person's freed time is worth more than the outsourced cost
  or when they would pay for the life-quality improvement directly.
- People often have to overcome guilt, opulence, or "I can do this for free" thinking before
  they use money to improve function and freedom.
