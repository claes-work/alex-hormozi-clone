# How to Get Rich [Full Interview with Dave Ramsey]

## Summary

Alex Hormozi interviews Dave Ramsey about Ramsey Solutions as a business, not mainly about personal finance. Dave explains the company's trust-brand model, media flywheel, product expansion, and anti-debt philosophy. Alex uses the interview to probe business-model choices and shares a self-reported wealth-allocation lesson: after a liquidity event, he realized that his own competence was business, so he and Leila chose to concentrate on buying and growing companies through Acquisition.com.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2023-09-13 - Interview frame**: Alex says the conversation is aimed at business owners and focuses on how Ramsey Solutions was built.
- **2023-09-13 - Reputation**: Alex says Acquisition.com has a belief that the people who hate you are "never ahead of you."
- **2023-09-13 - Trust at Acquisition.com**: Alex says most of his audience will never transact with him, so he still has to protect reputation and trust because that reputation compounds over time.
- **2023-09-13 - Self-reported liquidity/investing**: Alex says he and Leila had taken about $40 million home before a later liquidity event, that he mostly kept money in index funds while building, and that advice from Dave/Graham helped him decide to stick to business investing.
- **2023-09-13 - Acquisition.com focus**: Alex says he showed Leila the investing clip and concluded that they should "just buy businesses and grow them," which he describes as what they did with Acquisition.com.
- **2023-09-13 - Debt/risk question**: Alex says Acquisition.com had done all-cash deals up to that point and asks Ramsey for the anti-debt reasoning in business acquisitions.

## Context / guest-attributed claims

- Dave Ramsey says Ramsey Solutions was doing a little over $300 million in annual revenue at the time of the interview.
- Dave describes Ramsey Solutions as a trust brand where no endorsement or advertiser is worth harming customer trust.
- Dave says Ramsey Solutions avoids selling financial products directly and instead uses vetted endorsement/media partnerships.
- Dave argues debt introduces compounding lifetime risk and that entrepreneurs often fail to risk-adjust leveraged business decisions.

## Notable verbatim quotes
