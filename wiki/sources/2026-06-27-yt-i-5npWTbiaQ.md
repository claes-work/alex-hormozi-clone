# Burn $200K on Google Ads to 5x a Boring Business

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a luxury gaming-table manufacturer doing about $2.5M that gets most leads from Google Ads. Alex says $15K-$20K per month in ad spend is nowhere near market saturation and frames additional spend as an experimentation budget to find more profitable keywords. He also explains service-business reinvestment, awareness levels, bridge pages, and why a highly visual product could later scale through influencers and whitelisted creative.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - luxury gaming-table manufacturer. Her product, ad spend, revenue, margins, and lead-quality issue are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- At $20K per month, the company has not saturated demand for custom luxury gaming tables.
- The lead-quality problem is likely a tactical media-buying and keyword problem, not proof that Google Ads cannot scale.
- Alex would be willing to burn $200K-$300K testing bad keywords if that finds enough winners to scale the business from about $2.5M to $10M.
- Founders should treat profit they do not need to live on as an experimentation budget, not as lost money.
- For service businesses, reinvestment usually goes into talent/culture and brand rather than obvious physical capex.
- To scale ads into colder markets, the hook and click journey must match the prospect's awareness level.
- Broader keywords may need advertorial or bridge pages that move prospects from problem-aware to product-aware before the sales page.
- Because the product is visual and high ticket, Alex would personally test influencer outreach, custom tables for creators, affiliate-like promotion, and whitelisting the highest-converting creator story.
