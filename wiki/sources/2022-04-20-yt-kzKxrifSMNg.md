# How the Best Entrepreneurs REDEFINE Reality (labeling & suffering)

## Summary

Sparked by a debate with a friend about mental illness, Hormozi delivers a philosophical
rant on labeling: the mind is a "meaning-making machine," and much human suffering comes
from labeling normal, transient states (tired, sad, stressed) as "bad" and upgrading them
into long-standing problems. He argues resilience and clear communication both come from
carefully redefining terms and separating emotion from judgment.

## Key claims

- The mind is a meaning-making machine built "to keep us alive, not to help us thrive";
  labeling is one of the most powerful forces in the mind. (paraphrase)
- Labeling persuades: attach a positive label to someone and they act to stay consistent
  with it (cognitive dissonance) — usable in sales scripts. It also harms in reverse.
  (paraphrase)
- The human experience is a continuum (happy/sad, energized/tired); labeling one pole "bad"
  makes people treat normal variation as a problem, upgrading "a finite emotional condition
  into a long-standing emotional disturbance." (paraphrase)
- "Suffering is optional" — invoking Viktor Frankl, between circumstance and response lies
  choice; you can't always control the emotion but you can control the judgment you apply.
  (paraphrase)
- "Wisdom begins with a definition of terms" (attributed to Socrates): most disagreements
  and much emotional disturbance dissolve once terms are defined — he ties this to 50+ page
  acquisition contracts that are half definitions. (paraphrase)
