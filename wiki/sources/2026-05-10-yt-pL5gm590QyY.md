# The One Thing Most People Don't Realize About Their Time

## Summary

Alex argues that free time is not automatically available for other people's requests.
When someone treats an empty calendar as claimable, the person effectively makes their
time belong to others unless someone else has already booked it. Alex frames time as the
main currency someone has before money and says a calendar predicts future outcomes
better than a bank account because it shows where attention is invested.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says an empty calendar does not mean other people are entitled to the time.
- He says if a person only protects time once someone else claims it, their time belongs
  to other people.
- He says time is often the only currency someone has before they have money.
- He argues that giving time away cheaply prevents it from being invested in self-improvement
  or goals.
- He says a calendar is a better measure of wealth than a bank account.
- He says a calendar can predict where someone will be in one or five years because it
  shows current time investments.
- He says today's life reflects work done six to twelve months ago, not just today's
  behavior.
- He frames visualization as useful when it helps someone imagine the delayed reward of
  today's action while the real feedback loop has not arrived yet.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Just because you have free time doesn't mean anyone who asks for it is entitled to it."

> "Your calendar is a better measure of your wealth than your bank account."

> "The life that I'm living today is a result of the work that I did 6 and 12 months ago."
