# If You Make $15 an Hour, Stop Doing Chores

## Summary

Alex argues for buying back low-income household time while remaining frugal about
objects. If outsourced food, cleaning, and laundry cost less per hour than income-producing
work, replacing chores creates both immediate earnings and more repetitions at the valuable
skill. He traces the realization to a $500 gym sale that took about 30 minutes.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says people dedicated to earning and skill building should buy
  back low-value time and pour the recovered time into income-producing work.
- He self-reports realizing after a $500, 30-minute gym sale that a few extra sales could
  pay for a month of groceries and household support.
- His heuristic says roughly $1,500 can replace 96 monthly household hours, making
  outsourcing rational above about $15 per hour; the figures are unsupported estimates
  in this source, not a universal threshold.
- Household work consumes energy in addition to time, so recovered hours may be higher
  quality for practice.
- More work repetitions can improve skill, creating a second return beyond the immediate
  time-arbitrage earnings.
- Increased skill is likely to be rewarded only when the worker asks or otherwise captures
  the increased value.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Buy time like a rich person, buy stuff like a poor person."

> "Buy time, not stuff."

> "You also become more skilled. So you get better faster."
