# Why I Cut Relationships The Moment I See The Red Flag

## Summary

Alex Hormozi defines intelligence as rate of learning and learning as "same condition,
new behavior." He applies that definition to relationships: if he believes he will
eventually cut a relationship, he would rather act immediately and start receiving the
benefits of the decision.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Learning is not passive consumption; if behavior does not change, learning did not
  occur.
- Alex operationalizes intelligence as rate of learning.
- He operationalizes learning as same condition, new behavior.
- If life shows the same "red card" and a person repeats the old behavior, the person has
  not learned.
- Workshops, podcasts, and books only count as learning if they create new behavior in a
  repeated condition.
- A high rate of learning means changing behavior quickly after new information.
- Conversations someone avoids and decisions someone delays are examples of "red cards."
- Alex sees his willingness to cut relationships quickly as a function of learning speed:
  if he expects to cut the relationship eventually, he asks why not today.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Learning isn't a spectator sport."

> "Intelligence means rate of learning."

> "Learning means same condition new behavior."
