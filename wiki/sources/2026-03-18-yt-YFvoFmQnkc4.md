# Helping an Online Coach Advertise Better

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A solopreneur who sells coaching to real-estate agents (~$2.5M revenue, no employees, 100% organic, group-coaching delivery) wants to double the business and has been told by a mentor ("Ed") he needs a **brand manager**. His question: how do you find that person, given "99% of the stuff out there" is scams. Alex reframes "scam" as usually just incompetence/deception, then diagnoses the case: because delivery is group coaching and not capacity-constrained ("you could double customers and it wouldn't triple, quadruple"), this is a **pure advertising play**. His frameworks: (1) **Paid ads give a one-time ~3–5x lift off an organic baseline** — not a promise, just typical for someone who's scaled this far on organic alone. (2) The **pyramid/audience model** — you currently monetize the "super hot" top slice of your audience; growing *organic* (more platforms, more consistent, higher volume, higher quality) grows the whole base so the same monetized percentage yields more dollars (long-term seed-planting), whereas *ads* keep the base fixed and instead "move the line down" to monetize deeper into the existing audience. You want **both in parallel** on a 10-year horizon: plant seeds with organic, skim the top with ads. (3) **How to hire a brand manager: poach.** Do outreach to people who've "crushed it" at brands you admire and offer to pay them more; find who's behind a brand via LinkedIn or a ChatGPT search, since good media people usually have their own presence. (4) **Don't outsource the brand — bring it in-house.** Alex's core-vs-ancillary distinction: every business has attraction, conversion, delivery (core to customer value creation); IT, recruiting, finance are ancillary — necessary but not core. The brand is "arguably the most important asset you have," so keep it in-house working directly for you. (5) **Warning about turning on ads:** the current straight-to-checkout challenge motion (one-to-many five-day launch) "is going to break" when ads bring cold traffic — you won't convert cold at the same rate as warm, "by a lot," so the whole funnel economics change and he plans to move toward book-a-call single-closer sales alongside launches. **Next steps:** recruit the brand manager to build the base; ads plus sales motion must come in tandem because both must be good — "if the ads are great and the sales motion sucks, it won't work," and vice versa. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, if delivery isn't capacity-constrained (e.g., group coaching that can absorb double the customers), doubling a self-promoting brand's revenue is "a pure advertising play" — just "pull the ad lever."
- As of March 2026, paid ads typically deliver a one-time ~3–5x lift off an organic baseline for someone who's scaled this far on organic — framed as typical, not a promise or guarantee.
- As of March 2026, organic vs. ads do different jobs: growing organic (more platforms, more consistent, higher volume, higher quality) grows the whole audience base so the same monetized top-percentage produces more revenue (long-term); ads keep the base fixed and instead monetize deeper into the existing audience ("move the line down"). You should do both in parallel on a 10-year horizon.
- As of March 2026, the way to hire a brand/media manager is to **poach**: outreach to people who've excelled at brands you admire and offer them more money; identify who's behind a brand via LinkedIn or a ChatGPT search, since strong media people usually keep some public presence.
- As of March 2026, the brand should **not** be outsourced — bring it in-house working directly for you, because the brand is arguably the most important asset a self-promoting business has.
- As of March 2026, Alex's core-vs-ancillary function split: attraction, conversion, and delivery are core to customer value creation; IT, recruiting, and finance are ancillary — they must occur for the business to exist but aren't core to value creation, so they're outsourceable in a way the brand is not.
- As of March 2026, turning on paid ads "breaks" a straight-to-checkout funnel because selling to cold traffic converts far worse than to warm — the entire funnel economics change and require adjustment (e.g., moving toward book-a-call single-closer sales).
- As of March 2026, ads and sales motion must improve in tandem: great ads with a weak sales motion won't work, and a great sales motion with weak ads won't work.
