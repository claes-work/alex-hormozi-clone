# You Don't Have to Feel Good Enough to Win

## Summary

In this short mindset answer, Alex Hormozi responds to a question about harnessing resentment and jealousy. He says the motive matters less than the action: people often do not truly know why they do what they do, and any story that increases the likelihood of doing the required actions is useful. He adds a self-reported reflection that earlier in his career he felt not good enough, but it became freeing to realize he could still do the required actions and get the outcome.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The only thing that ultimately matters is what a person does.
- People often do not know why they do what they do, so the assigned motive matters less than the behavior.
- Jealousy, rage, depression, or another emotion can be used if it increases the likelihood of taking the required actions.
- A person can feel undeserving or not good enough and still get the result by doing the required work.
- Alex repeats a prior framework: hyper-successful people can combine grandiose visions, crippling insecurities, and impulse control.
- Alex says Leila has worked on herself more than anyone he has met and calls her a better human than he is.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)
