# How to Know When It's Time to Cut a Friend

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says freedom often lets someone refuse games they did not agree to play. He
connects that to friendships: if friends mock growth and only talk about the past, they
will likely become less compatible as someone becomes the person they want to become.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex connects freedom with the ability to refuse a game someone did not agree to play.
- Friends who say "must be nice," "you think you're better than us," or criticize changed
  habits may not stay compatible with the future self.
- Such friends may reject the new path harder and harder over time.
- Eventually the only shared topic may be the past.
- A leading indicator for cutting a friend is that they only talk about the past.
- Refusing the old game is uncomfortable the first time but gets easier with repetition.
- Alex agrees that trading self-respect for someone else's respect is a bad trade.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're not going to be friends with them eventually anyways."

> "When they only talk about your past."

> "But I would know."
