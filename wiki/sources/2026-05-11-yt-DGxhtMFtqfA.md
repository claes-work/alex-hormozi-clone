# The Bar For Excellence Is Embarrassingly Low

## Summary

Alex uses Charlie Munger's inversion of failure to emphasize consistency. He says
consistency does not guarantee success, but inconsistency can guarantee failure. He then
turns the point into practical work standards: do the boring work, prepare for meetings,
measure real time on task, start working instead of ritualizing around work, and exploit
the fact that the bar for excellence is often low.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex cites Charlie Munger's inversion frame about guaranteeing failure.
- He says consistency is one of the hardest virtues because humans are attracted to
  novelty.
- He says Gym Launch had a value of "do the boring work."
- He says boring work can include follow-up sequences, repeated split tests, and meeting
  preparation.
- He says 20 minutes of preparation can make someone appear dramatically smarter in a
  meeting.
- He says he has done only three consulting days and once prepared seven pages of
  improvement notes after about four hours of research.
- He uses a small timer to measure true time on task and stop the clock when distracted.
- He says people often spend a long time near a computer while doing few minutes of real
  work.
- He says starting is the greatest productivity hack because the work mood often arrives
  after beginning.
- He recommends leaving an open loop, even stopping mid-sentence, to make returning
  easier.

## Notable verbatim quotes
