# Alex Hormozi reveals how Acquisition.com builds companies

## Summary
In this short clip, Alex Hormozi explains the operating method behind [[../entities/acquisition-com|Acquisition.com]]. He frames the firm around the theory of constraints — every business is bottlenecked by a single constraint, and the firm's job is to identify it (sales, marketing, conversion optimization, pricing, delivery, customer success, logistics, etc.) and deploy an internal expert to solve it. The second half of the model is recruiting: they use their personal brand and family-office/private-equity status to attract "level A" talent from larger companies in the same niche and place those operators inside smaller portfolio companies. The stated goal is to build durable value inside each operating company so it can stand on its own rather than depending on the holding company.

## Key claims
- The firm operates from "the theory of constraint": every business is not growing because of a single constraint, and a system grows until it is constrained again.
- Much of their work is identifying a business's constraint, then solving it if it is within their wheelhouse — and that fit is a key filter for which businesses they invest in.
- They diagnose one layer deeper by category: sales, marketing, conversion optimization (CRO), pricing, delivery, customer success, or third-party logistics.
- They keep internal experts with deep niche expertise (building/scaling outbound sales teams, building/scaling inbound sales teams, video sales letters, CRO across pages, etc.), and task the relevant expert with solving the identified constraint.
- The "other half" of holdco is a recruiting function; their operating theory is "you find the people and the people build the business."
- They recruit operators who have "been there, done that" — e.g., for a company doing ~$10M/year in a niche like mortgage sales, they recruit a head of sales/marketing/operator from a company that was at $30M, $50M, or $100M in that same niche, then place them.
- Two claimed recruiting advantages: (1) their personal brand attracts better talent, and (2) their status as a family office / private-equity firm draws more responses from higher-level individuals than a standalone ~$10–20M business could attract on its own.
- Stated competitive advantage ("mode"/moat): they can tactically add value at the operating-company level and recruit top talent into those roles, who then interface with holdco.
- Process pattern: identify the constraint (e.g., sales) → recruit a proven director into the role → hand them the internal expert's playbook → they implement it inside the company, so value is built in the company rather than at holdco.
- If all companies relied on holdco to function, that would make them less valuable, because it means holdco is required; a company that can stand on its own and grow on its own becomes more valuable in itself.
- This is "a harder solution but the better long-term solution," and it is how they build companies.
