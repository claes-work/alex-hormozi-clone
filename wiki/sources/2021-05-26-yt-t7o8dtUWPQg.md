# Millionaire Reveals 7 Things I Wish They Taught In School (2021-05-26)

## Summary

In this solo monologue, Hormozi lays out seven "disciplines" or levels of education he
believes every entrepreneur (and human) must learn to feel complete, framing them as a
pyramid that builds from foundational literacy up to philosophy. He argues that formal
school teaches only the first level well, leaving people deficient in the rest, and that
naming one's deficiencies (rather than treating them as fixed identity) is the first step
to growth. He names philosophy — the search for meaning — as the most important level.

## The list — the 7 disciplines (exactly, in order)

1. **Foundational** — reading, writing, and math (the only one school teaches well; the gateway to learning the rest).
2. **Physical** — how to eat, move, sleep, train to get the energy, longevity, and body you want.
3. **Professional** — the skills to provide value to the marketplace / a career path (mostly learned by doing, not school).
4. **Emotional** — understanding your own feelings and thoughts; naming your deficiencies and being okay with them.
5. **Relational** — how to communicate, influence, and deal with others; being likable and trustworthy.
6. **Financial** — first personal finance (spend less than you make), then how to multiply money and make it work for you.
7. **Philosophical** — the values you choose to live by; the source of meaning (he calls this the most important, despite listing it last).

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- Hormozi describes having built multiple eight-figure companies and a portfolio doing about a million dollars a week, and says this success exposed how deficient he was in non-business areas (2021).
- He frames the seven disciplines as a pyramid: foundational literacy is the base, and fulfillment/meaning increases as you move up (2021).
- School only reliably teaches the foundational level; if he redesigned education he'd teach reading, writing, and math, then build the rest around these disciplines (2021).
- Emotional and relational skills are taught essentially nowhere, so most people have no education in them and must learn them alone (2021).
- Wealth is a ratio, not a number — the ratio of what you earn to what you spend — so cutting expenses can increase wealth (2021).
- Financial literacy has two stages: first getting money (personal finance / spending less), then multiplying it; he says he is currently focused on learning to multiply money (2021).
- Language patterns reveal (and create) limiting beliefs; saying "I am X" fixes it as identity, whereas "I have a deficiency in X" makes it a fixable opportunity (2021).
- He claims he can predict how likely an employee or business is to grow from their language patterns (2021).
- Philosophy — knowing *why* you do things and where you derive meaning — matters most, because you can improve every other discipline and still not feel better (2021).
- He says all of his biggest shifts in subjective well-being came from shifts in his philosophy about being human (2021).
