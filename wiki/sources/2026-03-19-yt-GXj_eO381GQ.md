# Helping a Marketing Agency Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The founder runs a done-for-you sales-and-marketing agency: they take over "two-thirds of the business" (sales + marketing) for high-ticket coaching/done-for-you offers, leaving the client only to fulfill. They have ~6 clients, are doing ~$5M revenue, and want to scale past that to "50 clients plus," running many closers across different sales teams. Their stated bottleneck is *fulfillment on the client's side* — they sell more than the clients can deliver. Alex diagnoses the real problem differently: what they're selling for is **biz op** ("done for you" businesses where a buyer pays ~$50k for someone to build them a business and take a revenue share). He calls it "super regulated," "gray," "arguably illegal, just not enforced when it's small." His core reframe: the agency has acquired a genuine, valuable skill (marketing + sales / turnkey selling) but is "applying it to the wrong opportunity." He predicts the scale dream collapses on itself — 50 customers becomes 50 "minnows," ~4 of which are 70% of revenue, and any equity you'd want in the underlying businesses is worthless because they're biz op. His prescription: **switch what you're selling — sell franchises instead of high-ticket biz op.** Franchises are legit, regulated (which protects both sides), and far more profitable per sale (a 10-year franchise sold for ~$150k up front, the salesperson keeps ~$150k of it — likely a mangled figure meaning a large share; plus 10–30% equity stakes in the franchisor parent co that carry real enterprise value). The ideal target is a franchisor with a good model who can't promote — common, because only ~5% of franchisors ever hit 100 locations; many spend $250–500k on legal setup, sell friends/family, stall at 10–15 locations, and won't pay pro franchise-sales people what they cost. On operations, the founder cites high sales-rep turnover; Alex reframes churn in **cohorts** (hire 3, keep 1, but that one stays 3 years = fine; sales is inherently higher-churn) and argues the deeper cause is selling biz op with **mercenary** reps who "jump town as soon as they get 1% more somewhere else" — reps who believed in the mission wouldn't churn as much. He also warns the whole "offers" / direct-response world is dying: CAC going up, trust going down, not sustainable long-term. His growth math: don't go from 6 to 50; in 12 months go from "six to six" but replace the six with legit clients, and if one really scales, become true partners and grow that one thing to $100M — far easier than growing 20 mediocre clients to $5M. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
