# If You're an E-Com Brand, Steal This Strategy

## Summary
In this short clip, Alex Hormozi advises an e-commerce brand to make a customer-only community (referencing Skool) the centerpiece of its media strategy. He argues that a community where customers post and show off their purchases drives repurchases, feeds organic and paid content plus a low-effort long-term email cadence, and closes the product-feedback loop for future versions. The segment ends with a promotion for his free $100M scaling roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames a customer-only community as the "centerpiece" of an e-commerce brand's media strategy — a chamber where enthusiast customers post and show off what they bought.
- As of March 2026, he claims such a community increases repurchases: when a buyer is weighing several brands and is inside a group with one of them (especially if the owner interacts), they buy from that one.
- As of March 2026, he argues customer posts become a triple-duty asset — organic content, paid content, and a long-term email cadence — and that emails simply showing people using the product are among the best long-term cadences.
- As of March 2026, he recommends curating the best community posts into emails that feature and honor the customer (with permission and links to the products they use), so the brand doesn't have to write email content from scratch.
- As of March 2026, he notes the community also sources product ideas and feedback, telling the brand what to build next (V3/V4) and how to improve existing products, closing the loop.
- As of March 2026, he cites his own behavior — following "band activators" (brand/gear enthusiasts) as the number-one thing on his Instagram and being an anonymous member of multiple hobby communities where he buys products — as evidence the pattern works.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "This community is actually going to be like the centerpiece of what I think you can do for a media strategy."

> "When people are thinking about buying, if they're thinking about five different brands and one of them they're inside of a group with, they're going to buy with that one, especially if the owner interacts and says, 'Hey, oh that's awesome. I love seeing that.'"

> "One of the best long-term email cadences for you is showing people using the stuff. Just show it like everybody who's into this is into it."

> "You check the best post and then you can honor those people and be like, 'Hey, John, do you mind if I send this out to the whole list? I think it'd be really cool for people to see it.' They're usually stoked about it."

> "It also will help you source basically products and feedback... they'll tell you what they want next and they'll tell you what to do better with the existing product. So it'll just close the loop for you."
