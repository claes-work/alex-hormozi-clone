## The 5 frameworks / SOPs
- **5 Star Service Framework** (`PDF p. 6-13`) — a customer-service delivery standard built on five stars: Concern, Courtesy, One & Done, Educate & Empower, Timeliness; plus a 1–5 post-interaction survey to grade reps.
- **Gametape Review Framework** (`PDF p. 14-18`) — a recurring weekly cross-department meeting where Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success watch a real sales/onboarding/service call together, each with a distinct objective, and commit to ONE "big domino" action.
- **High Performance Communication Framework** (`PDF p. 19-25`) — a model of team communication as three ingredients: Energy, Engagement, Exploration; with tactics to improve each and a self-grading deployment step.
- **Monday Hour One Framework** (`PDF p. 26-30`) — a ~1-hour Monday time-blocking ritual (Look Back → Look Forward → Prioritize → Schedule Priorities → Overflow/Ad-hoc → Color Code) giving managers full transparency into everyone's week.
- **Pay Increase Framework** (`PDF p. 31-37`) — a transparent raise philosophy: four performance categories with % raise bands, rules on frequency, and a scripted "how to ask for a raise" guide handed to employees.

## Key claims
- Leila claims her portfolio's average Net Promoter Score is 88, which she frames as above Chick-Fil-A (58) and Apple (61) (`PDF p. 6`, self-reported).
- She cites a McKinsey & Co study that customers are more likely to leave due to bad service than due to price or product (`PDF p. 6`).
- The 5 Star Service standard is: Concern, Courtesy, One & Done, Educate & Empower, Timeliness — she summarizes One & Done as "no leftovers" in customer service (`PDF p. 7`).
- Reps should be graded, not just trained: send a 5-question, 1–5 scale survey after each interaction (Concern, Courtesy, Educate & Empower, One & Done, Timeliness, plus overall), ideally automated via CRM/CS platform (`PDF p. 11-13`).
- The Gametape Review host is the Head of Sales; core attendees are Heads of Marketing, Sales, and Customer Success; calls are watched at 1.5x speed (`PDF p. 16`).
- Teams should commit to only ONE change per gametape review ("big domino"), because too many simultaneous changes exceed the team's execution bandwidth (`PDF p. 17`).
- She states she is "the CEO of a $500 million portfolio" and still attends gametape calls personally to pinpoint departmental problems (`PDF p. 15`).
- On communication she cites an MIT electronic-badge study, claiming communication was a greater predictor of team success than individual intelligence or skill, and that team energy + engagement outside formal meetings explained 40% of the variance in dollar productivity among groups (`PDF p. 20-21`).
- Speed of communication has the closest correlation to "energy"; slow communicators worsen teams (`PDF p. 22`). She keeps a daily end-of-day "praise" calendar block to message people who did something impressive (`PDF p. 22`).
- Monday Hour One is framed as ~1 hour of planning that saves an average of 10–12 hours over the rest of the week; priorities are scheduled first and everything else around them (`PDF p. 26-28`).
- Pay raises are assigned by four categories with bands: Standard/Not Meeting Criteria 1–3%, Consistent/Reliable 4–5%, Excellence/High Performance/Added Responsibilities 5–10%, Promotion/New Skills/Significant New Responsibilities 10–20% (`PDF p. 33-34`).
- Raises are case-by-case (not a fixed schedule, not distributed equally) and generally awarded no more than once per calendar year absent a promotion (`PDF p. 34`). She advises employees to "lead with data, not emotions" when asking (`PDF p. 35`).
- Closing pitch: she says she hosts monthly Scaling Workshops at her Las Vegas HQ (usually 50–75 businesses / ~100 people), run by the team that runs her "$500M Portfolio" (`PDF p. 37`).
