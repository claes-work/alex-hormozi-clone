# Helping an SEO Agency Get to $14M/Year

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a $7M SEO/web/PPC agency serving home-services companies. The founder wants to reach $14M and sell for $20M but has low EBITDA margins and sees AI as market risk. Alex reframes the business using sales velocity, LTV, hypothetical max, and service gross-margin standards, then says AI should be treated as the founder's weapon, not a threat.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - SEO/web/PPC agency founder. His revenue, churn, pricing, margin, nearshore plan, and sale target are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- At 10 units sold per month, $5K monthly price, and about 5% monthly churn, Alex estimates a $100K LTV and roughly $12M hypothetical max revenue.
- If the company is already at $7M, about $5M in future revenue is implied by the current sales velocity if nothing changes.
- The issue is making sure that future revenue and more of the current revenue drop to the bottom line.
- Alex's rule of thumb for service-business gross margin is 80%.
- Industry averages should not anchor standards because most businesses are poorly run.
- Nearshoring was the "12 months ago" answer; the current answer is becoming AI-first in operations.
- Alex says most of the agency's work, except account management, can be automated with AI agents and Make-style automations.
- AI should be treated as a risk for competitors, not a risk for the founder, if the founder adopts it aggressively.
