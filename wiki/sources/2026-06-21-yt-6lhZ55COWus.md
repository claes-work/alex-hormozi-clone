# Sell to the Rich (The Top 1% Has More Than the Bottom 90%)

## Summary

In this short teaching clip, Alex Hormozi uses U.S. income and household-net-worth distribution to support his recurring advice to sell to richer customers. He argues that entrepreneurs often compete for low-spending customers because those are the people they know and see daily, but the actual money is concentrated at the top of the market. The business implication is to design offers and markets around where buying power exists.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - sole speaker. The teaching below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says the top 10% of U.S. earners earn roughly 40% of U.S. income.
- He says U.S. household net worth was roughly $163 trillion the prior year.
- In his illustrative $100 distribution, he says the bottom 50% holds about $2, the next 40% about $28, the next 9% about $38, and the top 1% about $32.
- He uses that illustration to argue that the top 1% has more wealth than the bottom 90% combined.
- The practical business lesson is to sell where the money is rather than competing for low-spending customers.
- He frames reluctance to charge higher prices partly as a belief issue caused by only knowing lower-income buyers.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Sell to the rich, they pay better."

> "If you want to make money, go where the money is."

> "You're trying to compete and slice these $2 a hundred different ways."
