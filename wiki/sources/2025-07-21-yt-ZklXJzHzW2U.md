## Key claims (attributed to Leila / the operator — NOT Alex)

- **Competence-to-humility ratio.** The operator was surprised to find highly competent
  people who are also humble; contrasts this with Goldman Sachs, where ego was tolerated
  in smart individual contributors. (operator)
- **Culture is shaped by who you hire and fire.** Leila describes cutting prominent
  leaders who had drive but no humility ("they step on others to stand out"), citing the
  book *Multipliers* and the test "would I want 10 more of them in the organization?"
- **Environmental premium.** A supportive environment gets a new hire ~50% of the way on
  a trait like "sincere candor"; a little skill takes them to ~70%. People underrate how
  much value the right environment adds.
- **Hiring is a two-way, mutual process** — you must interview and sell simultaneously;
  the interview is "the beginning of onboarding," which reduces churn through congruence.
- **Front-load the job-description design.** Writing every bullet of the role yourself
  (the operator wrote the general-counsel spec; Leila wrote operator job descriptions)
  attracts better-fit candidates than outsourcing to recruiters copying generic resumes.
  A strong JD is "copy… it is an ad."
- **Write pain first.** Framework for a JD: list all the pain / needed future capacity,
  then translate each into what the person will do; AI can polish but "can't come up with
  the pain that you have."
- **"Nothing great ever happened without it being written down"** / **"Write a memo"**
  (internal hashtag "#WFM — write a freaking memo"). Meetings don't run without a
  pre-submitted memo; people can't think well in meetings. Cited influence: Jeff Bezos /
  Amazon memo culture.
- **Memo framework: Why → What → How → Now.** A one-to-two-page doc; both writer (deep
  understanding) and readers (~70% nuance, "instant MBA upgrade") level up, raising
  organizational IQ.
- **Separate cultural and technical interviews.** Don't blend them or flood candidates
  with 17 technical interviews; people unrelated to the role should run the culture
  interview ("no skin in the game").
- **Titles and equal standard.** Stop calling people "VA"; give functional titles;
  include contractors in Slack; hold everyone to the same standard regardless of
  hierarchy or location (anecdote: Nigerian contractors treated equally outperformed).
- **Capacity.** Bringing in a senior partner should free capacity to do something more
  valuable, not to "cruise"; together partners can build faster "with less friction and
  more fun."
- **Be "unreasonable" about timeframes.** Ask "can we do 6 months of work in 60 days?";
  reject default quarterly/"86 days left" thinking; end each day asking "what do I have
  to show for it?"
- **Start the day it's assigned** + **"super fast shitty first draft"** (credited to a
  friend, "Moore"); inspiration and momentum are perishable.
- **Accordion between future and present** — Leila can move fluidly between future
  vision and present execution; described (by the operator) as a gift.
