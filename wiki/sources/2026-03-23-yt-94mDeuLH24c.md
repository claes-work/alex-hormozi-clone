# Helping a B2B Lead Gen Agency Improve Lead Quality

## Summary
In this short clip, Alex Hormozi coaches an agency owner whose main constraint is lead quality: they generate many leads but skew toward low-salary, junior profiles, when higher-salary, more-senior prospects yield better ROI. The owner is considering switching ad platforms from Meta to LinkedIn or Twitter/X. Hormozi advises adding a performance-based revenue component before switching platforms, arguing that raising lifetime value transforms the economics enough that platform changes and creative work become secondary. The caller reports a current 3-to-1 return and roughly $10K/month in Meta ad spend.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that when a channel is already profitable, you should keep it running ("don't stop the flow") and layer new experiments on top rather than shutting off a working source of revenue.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends fixing lifetime value / adding a performance-based revenue piece before changing ad platforms, because a higher LTV can shift returns dramatically without any platform change.
- As of March 2026, Alex illustrates that adding the performance component could raise per-customer value from roughly $3K to $10–12K, turning a 3-to-1 return into a 12-to-1 return on the same spend.
- As of March 2026, Alex notes that even with diminishing returns as you scale spend (e.g. dropping from 3-to-1 to 2-to-1), a high starting LTV keeps the campaign strongly profitable (e.g. 12-to-1 falling only to about 9-to-1).
- As of March 2026, Alex affirms that LinkedIn is well-suited to reaching executive/senior audiences, but positions platform-switching as a lower priority than raising LTV.
- Caller claim (unverified): the agency spends around $10K/month on Meta ads, currently sees a 3-to-1 return, and hit diminishing returns when they tried to increase Meta spend.
