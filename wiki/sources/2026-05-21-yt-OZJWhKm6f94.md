# Why Pain Beats Passion Every Single Time

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says people often search for passion or purpose while ignoring the pain that
is already available as fuel. He argues that pain can either use a person or be used by
the person, and that specificity about the pain makes it more actionable.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says many high performers have abundant pain rather than only passion or purpose.
- When he was starting out, he looked for passion and purpose before recognizing the
  motivating pain already behind him.
- If someone remains in a bad situation, the pain is using them.
- A person can instead use the terrible situation as something to run away from.
- Avoiding pain or numbing it is less useful than confronting it and deciding to act.
- Heroes in their own stories do not avoid pain; they choose to turn terrible situations
  into growth.
- Alex says being specific about pain is helpful.
- Operationalizing "twisting the knife" means naming the exact person, phrase, or
  condition that hurts and using it as fuel.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Pain, like oodles of pain."

> "I'm going to do something about this."

> "Being really specific about your pain is helpful."
