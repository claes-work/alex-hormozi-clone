# We Raised Prices 4 Times and Still Can't Grow

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a physical therapy business with brick-and-mortar capacity limits, recent hiring trouble, and strong close rates. Alex recommends immediate cash-flow levers: more patient flow, financing, and further price increases. He then identifies the caller's idea as the Money Models decoy offer: sell a high-priced front-end treatment plan, or give it free when the customer joins a recurring membership, using price spread to control the split between upfront cash and continuity.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If a high-ticket health business is not using financing, it is likely leaving revenue on the table.
- Alex says a good financing partner often creates about a 30% revenue lift across high-ticket contexts.
- A very high close rate without a VSL or financing suggests there may still be room to raise price.
- A VSL or pre-visit nurture asset can support selling higher-priced packages.
- The caller's continuity idea is a decoy offer from Money Models: sell the package outright or give it free with membership.
- Price spread controls how many buyers choose the one-time purchase versus continuity.
- Alex says parity occurs when the one-time offer is about 30% higher than the recurring equivalent, based on his reported test.
- A waived-fee continuity structure can require repayment if the member cancels before a defined period.
