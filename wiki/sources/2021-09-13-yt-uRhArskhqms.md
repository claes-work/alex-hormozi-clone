# Entrepreneurs, your lack of focus is killing your business (2021-09-13)

## Summary

Hormozi recounts the period around 2016–2017 when he was simultaneously running
roughly nine to eleven businesses (six gyms across multiple partnerships, two
agencies, and in-person gym turnarounds) while drinking heavily to cope with the
stress, culminating in a drunk-driving head-on collision. He frames the crash and
his coach's warning as the catalyst that forced him, within roughly 48 hours, to
exit three partnerships and fire-sell his gyms so he could focus solely on Gym
Launch. The talk is an early, autobiographical articulation of his focus/attention
thesis: attention — not time — is the entrepreneur's scarcest resource, and unmade
decisions silently drain it.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- (2021) The most valuable resource for an entrepreneur is not time but **attention**;
  you can have all the time in the world and still be useless if too many things are
  competing for your attention. *(proto-version of his later focus/attention framework)*
- (2021) He hired an "attention coach" who told him he was "spread all over the place"
  and needed to aggregate his attention — advice he initially nodded along to but
  didn't act on until the crash forced him.
- (2021) **Unmade decisions and unconfronted conversations accumulate** and each one
  quietly allocates a chunk of your thinking/decision-making power, so the more that
  pile up, the more reactive and depleted you become. *(proto "unmade decisions tax"
  idea)*
- (2021) Analogy: an unsolved problem you sleep on becomes obvious in the morning
  because you have full attention available on it — business and life work the same way.
- (2021) He claims he tends to only make hard decisions when confronted with his own
  mortality; both quitting his job and this consolidation happened under that pressure.
- (2021) You should sequence businesses correctly rather than run many at once; he had
  ~9–11 businesses largely to feel high-status while barely making money.
- (2021) "You're only one decision away from changing your life" — quitting a job,
  starting a business, ending a partnership are each single decisions.
- (2021) Most people don't regret doing things; they regret not doing them — he'd
  rather do things and fail than carry the regret.
- (2021) Consolidating onto Gym Launch (started 2016) gave him back bandwidth,
  confidence, and self-belief, which created the initial momentum for its takeoff.
- (2021) His ruthless discipline with focus is not natural to him — it was a decision
  forced by near-catastrophe, not an innate trait.
