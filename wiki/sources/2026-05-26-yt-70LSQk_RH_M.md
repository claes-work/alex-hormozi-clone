# Why You Shouldn't Put Your Actions on a Pedestal

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that identity is not separate from behavior; it is the aggregation of behaviors. When people put a specific action on a pedestal, they make behavior change feel like a threat to the self. Seeing oneself as a list of behaviors makes change easier: if changing one behavior costs little and benefits an important relationship, trade it.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex's worldview treats identity as the aggregation of behaviors.
- When asked who someone is, people usually describe what that person does.
- Holding a behavior on a pedestal makes change feel emotionally rigid.
- Seeing the self as many behaviors lets the person ask what each behavior is worth.
- If changing a behavior costs little and significantly benefits someone important, the rational move is to change it.
- This frame reduces emotional resistance to behavior change.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Identity... is just the aggregation of all of the behaviors."
