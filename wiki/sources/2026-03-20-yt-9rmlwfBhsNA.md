# Helping a Young Entrepreneur Decide If He Needs a Partner

## Summary
In a live Q&A, an 18-year-old caller who has been a solopreneur for two years asks Alex Hormozi whether to partner with a friend who owns a pest-control company (~$200k/year revenue), where the caller would earn 5% equity contingent on driving $2M in revenue as the sales person. Hormozi reframes the question: a 5% contingent stake is not a partnership, and the caller should first decide what he actually wants. He argues that at this stage the caller should optimize for learning over earning, ideally by gaining experience inside a larger, faster-scaling company before starting his own venture.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that a real partnership requires the other party to bring something you lack — money, time, experience/skill, or network/distribution; if both partners are the same, one is unnecessary.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames a 5% contingent equity stake as "having a stake in the company," not being a partner — a distinct thing.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that if the caller can drive $2M in revenue to the friend's business, nothing structurally stops him from driving that revenue to his own business instead.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises that someone just starting out is in "maximizing skill acquisition mode" — the goal is to learn, not earn — and that it is highly undervalued to join a company scaling from roughly 15 to 100 head count and let someone else carry the risk while you learn to do it right.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that clarity on what you want is 99% of the challenge; getting it is the easy part — and that judging yourself less avoids analysis-paralysis and existential angst over decisions.
- As of March 2026, Alex cites a Rockefeller idea: great businesses rarely come out of great friendships, but great friendships commonly come out of great businesses — so build the business first, then the friendship.
- Caller claims (unverified): he is 18, has been a solopreneur for two years, previously did "mailbox replacement" hustles, and the friend's pest-control business does ~$200k/year with the friend owning 100%.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "If you want to do a partnership, they got to have money you don't have, they got to have skills you don't have, they got to have time you don't have, or they got to have network or distribution you don't have."

> "If we're both the same, then one of us is unnecessary."

> "Figuring out what you want is 99% of it. Getting what you want is the easy part."

> "Rockefeller said this... it's rare for great businesses to come out of great friendships. But it's common for a great friendship to come out of great businesses."

> "You're in maximizing skill acquisition mode right now. That's the entire point."

> "Realistically, you getting 5% is not you being a partner. It's you having a stake in the company. Very different."

> "It's worth letting someone else take that risk so you can learn how to do it right."
