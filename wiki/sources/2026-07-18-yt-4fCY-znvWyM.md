# 'I Close 30% But Can't Get People to Show Up'

## Summary

A live coaching Q&A ("last question" of a session). The questioner runs a restaurant
management software company — inventory/admin systems, launching a POS with payments and
accounting — with strong retention (~86%, self-reported) and a business "growing because
of retention" despite what he calls a terrible sales problem. His funnel: Meta ads to a
$99 self-liquidating offer (training manuals for restaurants), roughly 10% of clicks
opt in, ~20% of those buy (~1:1 ROI), ~25% of buyers self-schedule an onboarding call —
and then only about 1% actually show up, even after six or seven chase calls. When they
do show, he closes ~30%. His explanation: restaurateurs are uniquely busy, everyone is
selling to them, they stay on the couch.

Alex's core reaction is to refuse the data before accepting the excuse: **"Nothing has
an attendance rate of 1%."** He has never seen a number like that among *paying
customers*; a figure that extreme means "something is disconnected" — either the
tracking is wrong or a funnel step is mechanically broken (buyers never actually
contacted, broken scheduler integration, invisible calendar, missing reminders, wrong
time zones). He walks the funnel step by step on the whiteboard-style exchange
(1,000 clicks → 100 opt-ins → 20 buyers → 5 scheduled → ~zero shows) to isolate where
it dies.

His second diagnosis is **offer–agenda mismatch**: the caller admits the $99 manuals
are "just a hook" for the software, and the call is framed as software onboarding. Alex's
rule: whatever they just bought, the scheduled call's promised agenda must match it as
closely as possible ("we'll teach you how to use them, how to get the most out of
them") — the new product gets introduced *on* the call, not in the invitation. A
secondary possible cause is segmentation: manual-buyers may not be the decision-makers.

Third, he demolishes the "my clients are too busy" story: **"Your ideal client is not
special."** Every entrepreneur is busy; there is nothing fundamentally different between
the food business and the medical business — doctors say the same and still attend sales
calls. He says he has analyzed a company doing exactly the same thing as the caller with
normal metrics, so the market is not the problem — the funnel is. Prescription: stop
waiting on self-scheduling and reminder texts — **call the buyers** ("Hey, you bought
this. Let me tell you about it"), because outbound calls to existing buyers will
out-perform this funnel easily ("If I call 20 of them, I would talk to four"); even cold
calling gets better attendance than 1%. His closing verdict: the business works, the
data doesn't — the sales team is failing and the tracking is probably incorrect. The
video ends with the standard acquisition.com/roadmap free-gift CTA (10 stages, quiz,
functions from product through finance, thank-you-page call, in-person Vegas session).
