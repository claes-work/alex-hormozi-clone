# Helping a B2B Manufacturer Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A B2B manufacturer who sells duct-cleaning equipment and supplies to duct cleaners is at ~$1.2M revenue and wants to reach ~$3.5M. He gets all customers organically via word of mouth (no demand gen), averages ~80 new customers/month, and has almost no repeat business because his equipment is durable. Alex diagnoses two problems at once. First, on **acquisition**: relying solely on word of mouth means he does "nothing" to generate demand, so his growth is capped; he must pick one deliberate acquisition motion — outbound (door-knocking, cold email, cold call, DMing), paid ads, affiliates, or content. Second, on **monetization/LTV**: Alex reframes the business as a classic "three wheels of revenue" model (like auto dealerships, large equipment, or printers/ink) — you sell (1) the machines, (2) the consumables the machines use, and (3) the service around the machines. Sophisticated players often sell the machine at cost or a loss because all the margin lives in consumables and service. The client currently makes money on the hard part (the machine) while capturing almost none of the recurring, high-margin annuity. Alex's prescribed fix: build the recurring engine first — aim to **retain 90–95% of customers annually on consumables** so it becomes a compounding, super-profitable base, sell maintenance programs and subscriptions **up front at the point of initial sale** (get them on a cadence), and re-engage the existing base (who've never been sold consumables via outbound). On the client's own idea of raising value-per-customer, Alex pushes back: making the *individual* customer more valuable is hard; it's easier to sell a **higher-tier customer** (someone who cleans more ducts, buys in volume or for fleets) — but that requires a different sales motion, so start with a new acquisition system for existing customers (a known flow) before going upmarket. He closes the session with three levers: sales-motion change, offer change, and back-end re-engagement. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
