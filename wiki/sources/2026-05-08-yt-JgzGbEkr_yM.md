# Why Your Next Business Always Grows Faster

## Summary

Alex says later businesses grow faster because the entrepreneur has pattern recognition,
especially about talent. The first business teaches what competent operators in each
function look like; after enough repetitions, building another business becomes more
like assembling known roles.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says most entrepreneurs he asks report that their second or later business grew
  faster than the first.
- He says the basic reason is pattern recognition, especially around talent.
- He says the first million or few million in revenue force the founder to learn
  marketing, sales, delivery, management, and other functions.
- He says businesses plateau while the founder searches for competent people in each
  role.
- He lists sales-role pattern recognition from SDR through VP of sales as an example
  of what takes time to learn.
- He says once the founder knows the roles across departments, new businesses feel
  less like building and more like assembling.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's pattern recognition."

> "You could do the job. This is amazing."

> "You're not even building the business, you're just assembling it."
