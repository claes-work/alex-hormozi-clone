# Stop the Side Hustle and Grow the Golden Goose

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a done-for-you video podcast agency that has started selling a licensing/implementation offer. At first, Alex challenges the move as a shiny-object distraction from a profitable, sticky agency. After clarifying that the new offer helps entrepreneurs launch their own podcasts and includes recurring backend staffing/consulting, he changes the diagnosis: if the new front end has higher conversion and higher lifetime gross profit, it may be a superior model. The transition should happen by selling the new offer until it surpasses the old business, then letting done-for-you clients roll off rather than replacing them.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A profitable, sticky service business should not be abandoned for a shiny object unless the new model is mathematically better.
- Doing both models at once lowers the odds of winning at either, unless the transition is deliberate.
- The right comparison is not emotional excitement; it is conversion rate and lifetime gross profit.
- If more people want the new offer, more people convert, and lifetime gross profit is higher, the new offer is fundamentally superior.
- Transition by continuing to sell the new front end until its revenue and profit exceed the old line, then stop replacing old done-for-you clients as they churn.
- Existing done-for-you demand can potentially be sold into the new customer base as a spread/cross-sell.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You have a good business. Just grow it."

- **Speaker attribution**: Business model, margins, client retention, and offer details are the owner's claims. Alex's advice is the focus/transition logic.
