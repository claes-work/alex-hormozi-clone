# Helping a Life Coach Make Better Content

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A life-coach-certification seller (≈$3M/yr, 250k Instagram followers built on relationship content) tells Alex her content attracts men and women who fight in the comments but nobody who wants to become a certified coach. Alex diagnoses "you baited the hook with the wrong bait" and prescribes a *reframe*, not a rebuild: keep teaching her trauma/healing frameworks, but frame every piece from the perspective of *how a coach experiences dealing with a client* ("when you have a client who comes to you with this…") so she attracts business owners/aspiring coaches instead of end-customers or victims. He warns that pro-victim framing attracts victims. He also revisits his "give away the secrets" stance and floats a two-tier model (sell coaching → satisfied clients want to do it for others → sell them the cert), citing a friend who reached $50M/yr this way. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex reframes the algorithm as "interest-based media, not social media anymore," arguing it's now *simpler* than 5 years ago to pivot an audience because a single strong piece of content reaches people interested in that topic regardless of your existing following.
- As of March 2026, he says her problem is framing, not substance — she's "probably like 6 inches off," just needs to "change the wrapper," not the whole business.
- As of March 2026, he argues content marketing *as though you are the practitioner* attracts customers (end-clients); to attract business owners you must frame content around *what your customer (the coach) experiences dealing with their clients*.
- As of March 2026, he warns that a "pro-victim narrative" attracts victims who want to stay in that state; tweak the angle so it doesn't sound pro-victim.
- As of March 2026, he restates his standing "give away the secrets" position: fear of giving away secrets is unfounded because (1) "no one does anything anyways" and (2) even if they act and succeed, they'll want more.
- As of March 2026, he still says content must "follow the laws of content" — lead with a strong hook (e.g., "so she slapped her husband…") before delivering the teaching frame.
- As of March 2026, he floats a two-tier certification model (sell the transformation/coaching first; satisfied clients then buy the cert to do it for others), citing an unnamed friend who reached ~$50M/yr in franchise coaching trainings — "the biggest that I know of… apart from Tony [Robbins]."
