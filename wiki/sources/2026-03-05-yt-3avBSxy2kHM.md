# Why I Bought the UFC Headquarters

## Summary
Interview clip (podcast-style; interviewers pepper Alex with questions, ">>" turns). Alex explains why he bought the former UFC headquarters building in Las Vegas and turned a long-remote company into an in-person operation. Self-reported deal facts: it's **three floors of ~12,000 sq ft each = ~36,000 sq ft total**. The purchase is described as "part of the grand plan" of what he's investing in — heavily in **media**: the building is being actively built out with "a ton of studios" and multiple sets for higher-quality, higher-frequency content. The strategic goal is to **increase inputs into the content machine**: Alex notes his brand's growth has come from only ~half a day of actual filming a week (or every other week), with the rest carried by his team's cutting/distributing/repurposing — and he now wants to raise his own filming volume. On content direction, he clarifies he is **not** building a talent stable under the Hormozi brand; instead he and Leila will personally do *more*, facilitating more *types* of content (new shows, new characters) rather than "more talking ads." He credits a visit to **Dave Ramsey** and Ramsey Solutions as a direct inspiration, offering a two-part analysis of why Ramsey built a ~$300M/year education/information business without being seen as a scam: (1) an unbeatable promise ("if you spend less money, you will have more left over"), and (2) a relentless focus on brand/reputation, such that Ramsey's biggest revenue slice is media (~$10M/month on media alone, which Ramsey shared on the podcast). Bonus takeaways: Ramsey runs an event every week, and both Ramsey and PBD each spend ~20 hours/week making content — far more than Alex currently does, since Alex spends more time on the portfolio side. Floor one is planned as a **massive commercial gym** (~6,000 sq ft of gym with the other half of the bottom floor as parking, with a roll-up warehouse door); Alex calls himself a gym-equipment "connoisseur/aficionado" who wants multiple brands of the same machine — framing it as a want, not a utility question ("this is my purses… my Coco Chanel"). The gym should be structurally done in ~a month with a first wave of equipment, and equipment orders continuing for a year. The building has three suites with bathrooms and full facilities; the previous owner was international (Alex thinks Asian) and would sleep at the building between flights. Alex says he's newly "super pro office" after a career run almost entirely remote — an in-person environment speeds up teaching/upskilling the team and shaping culture at scale, and he wants a rewarding no-rules environment (no fixed 9-to-5) people "wouldn't want to stay home from." He also likes that a separate office makes home feel more relaxing, and that an on-site gym lets him record content while working out (something he avoids at public gyms). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
