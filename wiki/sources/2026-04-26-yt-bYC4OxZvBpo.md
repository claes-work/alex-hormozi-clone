# Most Entrepreneurs Are Too Arrogant to Focus

## Summary

Alex explains why focus is hard even when entrepreneurs intellectually know it matters. He
says the "I'll see which one works" fallacy is arrogant because the founder is competing
against people giving one market their full attention. Commitment means eliminating
alternatives. He also says he judges himself by actions rather than feelings: he can feel
impatient but still do the patient thing.

## Key claims

- Alex says many newer entrepreneurs believe they can test several businesses at once and
  see which one works.
- He calls that arrogant because 25% attention across four projects competes against
  someone else's 100% in a real market.
- He defines commitment as eliminating alternatives and links decision-making to killing
  off options.
- He compares focus to pruning a tree: limited nutrients, time, and attention require
  removing branches.
- He credits Warren Buffett with the idea that very successful people say no to almost
  everything.
- He says focus remains hard because the opportunities he now rejects are things he would
  once have wanted badly.
- He judges patience and focus by behavior, not internal impulse.
- He says experiential knowledge is different from knowing about something; experience
  gives depth that an opinion does not.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Any of them could work, but the only one that will work is the one that you work on."

> "It's arrogant to think that you going 25% in on four things... is going to do anything against someone's 100%."

> "One of the definitions of commitment that I like a lot is the elimination of alternatives."

> "I can feel impatient but still do the patient thing."

> "No man with an experience is ever at the mercy of a man with an opinion."
