# The #1 Sign It's Time to Cut a Friend

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says a leading indicator that it may be time to cut a friend is that the
friend only talks about the past. He frames dismissive comments such as "must be nice" or
"you think you're better than us" as signs that the relationship may not fit the person
someone is trying to become.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Friends who mock growth with lines like "must be nice" or "you think you're better than
  us" may not remain compatible with the person someone is becoming.
- If someone wants to become a different person, some old friendships may reject that path
  more strongly over time.
- A leading indicator for cutting a friend is that the friend only talks about the past.
- If the only shared material is the past, the relationship may already be ending.
- Alex agrees with the self-respect frame that trading self-respect for someone else's
  respect is costly.
- His "but I would know" self-judgment standard applies to relationship choices too.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The only thing that you'll talk about is the past."

> "I know."

> "But I would know."
