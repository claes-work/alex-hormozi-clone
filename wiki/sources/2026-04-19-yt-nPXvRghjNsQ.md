# Helping an Events Business Hire an Operator

## Summary

Alex advises an events operator considering a role with a charismatic speaker whose
business needs both event operations and front-end sales/marketing. He recommends
clarifying expectations with an org chart: define what the role is, what other roles are
needed, and where the candidate fits, rather than accepting four jobs hidden inside one.

## Key claims

- Alex says finding one high-value intrapreneur role likely requires unscalable outreach
  to high-value targets.
- He says the candidate should tell the founder directly if the role is really several
  80-hour-per-week jobs.
- He recommends presenting the needed org chart, the candidate's seat, and the other
  roles required.
- He says strong executive candidates can create value in interviews by showing whether
  a role is realistic.
- He warns that managing a function one has not managed before can taint performance in
  the area one is actually strong in.
- He says expectations should be clear from the beginning.
