# What Has Value in the Age of AI

## Summary

Alex Hormozi answers what still has value when information is free and AI can produce assets like ebooks and graphics. He says information has been effectively free for a long time; the value is in selection, curation, time compression, ease, and friction removal. In communities, real human struggle and consumable recurring value become more important as AI makes generic outputs easier. Community itself is consumable, and the operator should identify recurring inputs that feed the customer's system.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Information has been free for a long time; AI changes convenience, not the underlying fact that raw information is abundant.
- When information is infinite, value moves to selection and curation.
- Most offers sell time compression, ease, or friction removal.
- AI will replace more human-doable tasks over time, but real people and real struggles may become more valuable in a fake-content environment.
- Community is consumable because people continue needing it month after month.
- The operator should separate the one-time teaching component from the recurring consumable component.
- Recurring consumables can include winning ads, trend lists, vetted property lists, stock picks, or other niche inputs.
- Pricing and retention should reflect whether the value is one-time or recurring.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The value is in the selection and curation."
