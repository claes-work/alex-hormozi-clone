# The Harsh Truth About Time and Taking Risks

## Summary

A short mindset clip in which Alex Hormozi reframes starting from nothing as an
advantage: having nothing to lose means you can take unlimited "shots on goal." He
argues time is your only currency before you have money, that guarding your calendar is
essential, and that your calendar is a better measure of wealth — and of where you'll be
in a year — than your bank account. He closes on visualization as a way to approximate a
feedback loop during periods when nothing seems to be happening yet.

## Key claims

- Having "nothing to lose" is the perfect condition for taking risk: your worst-case
  outcome is your current baseline, so you can keep taking shots (paraphrase).
- People with something to lose must be increasingly selective about the risks they take;
  those with nothing can take far more attempts than they realize (paraphrase).
- Before you have money, time is your only currency — the only thing you can spend to
  improve yourself or move toward your goals — so giving it away freely is irrational
  (paraphrase).
- Your calendar is a better measure of your wealth than your bank account, and the
  easiest predictor of where someone will be in a year is where they invest their time
  (paraphrase).
- Today's life is the result of work done 6–12 months ago; people wrongly read their
  current condition as a result of today's behavior (paraphrase).
- Visualization is more powerful as "I'm doing this thing today and imagining it
  happening" — an approximation of the feedback loop you want — than as passive wishing
  (paraphrase).
