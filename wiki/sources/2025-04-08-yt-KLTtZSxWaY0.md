# How to Actually Achieve Happiness | Alex Hormozi

## Summary

A two-person conversation (Alex Hormozi + a British co-speaker, almost certainly Chris Williamson) on why achievement fails to produce happiness. Alex's core thesis: he has repeatedly hit goals that were "supposed" to make him happy and felt nothing afterward, so he now locates joy in *pursuit* rather than attainment — and deliberately sets goals huge and far away to maximize time spent pursuing. He adds two motivational/gratitude frames: building "the man I want to become" through deliberate struggle, and imagining his 85-year-old self waking up in his 30-year-old body to feel grateful for the mundane present. The co-speaker contributes a parallel but separate set of presence/mindfulness ideas (Sam Harris, Conor McGregor, "feet underneath you").

## Key claims

- Alex names the "happiness formula" people run: *when/if X happens, I'll be happy* — and says his life is a pile of evidence that hitting X brought no lasting joy. (Alex)
- Anticipation is the "hot button" on pleasure; for Alex the hot button on **joy** is what he's doing right now — being in the work/reps, in flow (the "Rocky cut scene"), not the finish line. (Alex)
- He calls himself "a pretty existential nihilist overall," derived from that repeated evidence. (Alex)
- The line "once the presentation goes well, I'll feel good" is, he says, a lie he tells himself — he now half-expects post-achievement *disappointment* because he no longer gets to do the pursuit. (Alex)
- His fix: set a goal "really big, really far" so he can stay in pursuit for the longest possible time — because "in pursuit is my button for enjoyment." He reframes outsiders' "you're always working" as simply choosing to live in pursuit. (Alex)
- Motivational frame — the person as destination: "hell is when you look at who you could have been and realize that you're not that person." He asks what he'd "put that man through" to forge him — answer: toil and struggle, reaching for things just above his threshold, over and over. (Alex)
- "Mental mirror" self-talk during hard times: "I'm making you. I'm not there yet, but I'm making you." (Alex)
- Gratitude frame — his single most powerful one: imagine his 85-year-old self waking up as his 30-year-old self; suddenly the un-hurting knees, clear (fog-free) mind, energy, and Leila beside him become precious. Lets him "enjoy the mundane." Lasts only a few minutes, but those minutes are the part of the day he remembers. (Alex)
- **Co-speaker (not Alex), separate ideas:** the "golden years" frame (this-right-now will be the golden years); presence via Sam Harris ("five moments a day where you're where your feet are underneath you"); Conor McGregor's "this time I'm going to be present"; an embodiment coach's peripheral-vision cue; the perforated-steering-wheel / leaf sensory trick; "your brain has one megabyte of RAM — you can only hold one thought."
