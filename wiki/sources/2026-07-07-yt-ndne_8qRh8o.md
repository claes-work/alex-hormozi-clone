# Helping a Marketing Agency Scale from $1.2M to $8M

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Stephanie Fox, owner of a $1.2M/year paid-media and SEO agency who says yes to everything and serves clients across every industry. Alex reframes her "which service?" question as an avatar question, and argues that the only viable path for a service agency is to move up market: fire the small paid clients, keep the high-LTV SEO/AEO clients, and reposition to serve only larger businesses. He contrasts the two extreme agency models (cheap high-volume SMB vs. premium up-market) and warns her current path caps at $3–5M with rising pain.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The "which service to focus on" question is really an **avatar question** — deciding who you serve solves the service/product question.
- Doing many different things for many different types of customers is a source of operational pain, independent of revenue.
- To pick the right service, find the 80/20: identify which clients **pay the most, that you like the most, and that stay the longest** — for her that was SEO clients — and start there.
- Sell the AI/AEO ("AI stuff") offer while it's early, and run **paid ads to that offer**; that combo plus positioning up market is his recommended play.
- A marketing agency on the everything-for-everyone path **caps at ~$3–5M**, and getting there is more painful than the current state — margins compress, talent gets harder to find, offloading top clients to junior reps degrades service and can trigger margin collapse.
- At sticking points, the correct moves are usually **counterintuitive** — if the intuitive move worked, you'd already be past the sticking point.
- The prescribed move: say **no to the smaller paid customers, yes to the bigger SEO/AEO customers**, and position as someone who only serves customers above a revenue threshold (he suggests requiring clients doing **$10M/year**, or at least $5M).
- Niching to specific industries can come later; first just do one thing for people above a certain size, then a second 80/20 will reveal which industries you're best at.
- Narrow to the industries where **LTV:CAC becomes absurd** (clients never want to leave) — that's the "money printing machine," and up market is the only way agencies scale.
- Agencies have **two polar extremes**: sell to SMB (must be priced cheap, high volume) or sell up market. If selling SMB, you're probably **priced too high** — price to the client's **worst month**, not what they'll say yes to, or you build a "churn factory."
- SMB pricing is usually **$400–600/month**; that model only works if you engineer costs down from day one (the Walmart analogy — cheap by design, not "do the same thing and charge less").
- With a decent SMB service (map listings, ranking, etc.) you can hold **30–40 month LTVs** on cheap acquisition — but you must build the whole business around volume.
- You can gauge how far along a service-business owner is in their career by **the price point they charge and the customer level they serve**; the biggest agencies in the world all serve the biggest customers.
