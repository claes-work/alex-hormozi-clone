# A Players Will Make You Rich

## Summary
Alex Hormozi argues that A players make a company rich, but sustaining an A-player culture requires ruthlessly removing low performers because mediocre teammates drag down the strong ones. He illustrates with his own experience cutting a 14-person sales team down to six (which then closed more deals) and explains that a leader's definition of an "A player" keeps rising as they get better. He closes with a promotion for his free "$100 scaling road map" at acquisition.com/roadmap.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex claims that mixing high performers ("eights") with mediocre ones ("sixes") drags the eights down toward six-sevens; the fix is to fire the sixes, after which the eights rise to become nines.
- As of March 2026, Alex says A players will self-manage, but only if everyone is an A player — otherwise standards get accepted as lowered, and A players either drop their own bar or leave.
- As of March 2026, Alex recounts cutting a 14-person sales team (which had a "cancerous" member who was stealing, known to some) down to six, and those six closed more deals than the fourteen; he says he has done similar axe-cuts across many teams including media.
- As of March 2026, Alex says his own definition of an A player rose continuously as he grew — from a $50k/year employee at his gym up through $75k, $100k, $200k, $400k, $1M, and multi-million-dollar performers — and cites a quote he attributes to "Shauwny": "the best talent is always in the future."
- As of March 2026, Alex offers his "razor": he has never found an A player he didn't recognize as an A player within the first 14 days, and his tolerance for non-A players has dropped to near zero.
- As of March 2026, Alex notes the standard varies with hiring level (fast-training low-wage roles differ from long-ramp roles), but character and work ethic show up as leading indicators almost immediately — and those, not day-to-day skill, are why most people get fired.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "A players will make you rich, but here's the downside, the hard part about A player culture."

> "If you had five eights and five sixes on a team, what really happens is that the sixes pull down the eights, so now you just have a bunch of six sevens. Anyways, the smart move is to fire all five sixes, and what happens is your eights become nines, and you never needed the sixes to begin with."

> "We ended up cutting, I think, from 14 to six. And those six guys closed more deals than the 14."

> "A players will self-manage, but everyone has to be an A player. Otherwise, they'll say, 'Oh, that's accepted.' And either they'll lower the bar, or they'll leave."

> "And then I had my first $75,000 a year player, then my first $100,000 a year, then the first 200, then my first 400, then my first million, then my first multi-million. And at each level, I'm like, 'I didn't even know they made people like this.'"

> "This is a quote from Shauwny. He said, 'The best talent is always in the future.' And so, we always want to make sure room. We want to build a business that has a vision that's big enough that their big dreams can also fit inside of it."

> "I have yet to find an A player that I don't immediately know is an A player within the first 14 days. It just hasn't happened."

> "You can still see leading indicators in terms of character and work ethic almost immediately. And those are the reasons that most people get fired, not because of kind of the day-to-day skill stuff."
