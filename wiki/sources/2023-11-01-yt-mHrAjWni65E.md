# How to get rich this Black Friday

## Summary

A tactical holiday-promotions video framed as Hormozi opening five wrapped "gifts," each delivering one Black Friday / holiday money-making tactic: the loss leader, the gift-card referral play, high-ticket/high-scarcity, buy-X-get-Y-free (free with prepay), and the "MAGIC" wrapper for naming/packaging any promotion. Concrete, service-business-oriented, with specific pricing math and scarcity mechanics.

## Key claims

- **Loss leader:** Sell a well-understood product (diapers) below expected price to bring people in the door, then profit on ancillary items whose price they don't know (bottles). For promotions, "splinter your offer" — peel one component off your bundle and put a huge discount (e.g., 90% off) on that single piece to break through the noise (cites diapers.com's strategy).
- **Gift-card strategy:** Sell one-unit-value gift cards (e.g., $200) at 80–90% off, limit two per customer, restricted to gifts for friends/family. You get paid to acquire new (referral, higher-quality) customers; collect the recipient's contact info as a lead, do a three-way intro, then upsell the redeemer (mark the $200 off a $1,000 offer = $800 sale). Notes gift cards have low redemption rates (Best Buy example) and are effectively an interest-free customer loan.
- **High-ticket / high-scarcity:** Build a 10x–50x-priced premium version, limit to ~5% of your customer base (recommends 5% first time so it sells out). Rationale: customers are "fractal" / 80-20 twice — the top 5% are ~two standard deviations above and will spend far more; selling five $5,000 packages to 100 customers can double a month's revenue.
- **Buy X get Y free (free with prepay):** Raise the price of the "one" and give away more than you charge (e.g., buy one at $10, get three free = a hidden ~30% discount but far more compelling). Cash is front-loaded/prepaid — powerful but can be abused (he's declined to invest in businesses too dependent on it). Also works as a grand-opening cash-flow play.
- **MAGIC wrapper (packaging/naming):** M = Magnetic reason why, A = Avatar (who it's for), G = Goal (dream outcome), I = time Interval, C = Container word (blueprint/accelerator/intensive/getaway). Example: "busy mom's 30-day relaxation getaway for the holidays." You don't change the business, you change how you present it.
