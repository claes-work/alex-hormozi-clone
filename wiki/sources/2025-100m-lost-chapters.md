# $100M Lost Chapters

**What it is.** A "Private Volume" compilation of unreleased/cut chapters from the `$100M Series` — subtitled "Lost Treasures from $100M Offers, $100M Leads, & $100M Money Models" (PDF p. 1, 3). It is not a new argument but an anthology: Hormozi wrote these chapters, judged them "not required to execute the strategies" and cut them for being too theoretical, too math-heavy, too niche, or out-of-scope, then bundled them with a short blurb before each explaining why it was cut (PDF p. 9–10). Structure: one intro chapter plus four sections (A–D). Raw path: `raw/books/2025-100m-lost-chapters.pdf`, ~164 PDF pages.

> ⚠️ **DATE UNCERTAIN.** The filename and this page's `source_date` say 2022, but the book's own copyright page reads "Copyright © 2025 by Bumble IP, LLC" (PDF p. 5, 109) and it repeatedly references `$100M Money Models` (published 2025-08) as an already-existing book (e.g. PDF p. 9, 193, 337). This compilation was therefore almost certainly assembled/published in **2025**, after Money Models shipped. The "2022" label likely reflects when some source chapters were originally drafted, not the compilation's release. Treat 2025 as the real publication year.

## Structure and content

- **Intro — "Your First Avatar"** (PDF p. 3–9): cut from `$100M Offers`; a 4-step process (survey customers → find biggest spenders → find common qualifiers → execute: respeak avatar + re-engineer buying process) for choosing an ideal customer. Hormozi calls it "one of the most important chapters I've written" (PDF p. 9).
- **Section A: Attract** (PDF p. 11–35): cut from `$100M Money Models` for being "too theoretical"; how to wrap a Grand Slam Offer in Premium, Free, and Discount promotions to lift take-rate (PDF p. 19, 21).
  - Premium Promotions (PDF p. 15–20), Free Promotions (PDF p. 21–26), Discount Promotions (PDF p. 27–35) — each with pros/cons in a Socratic "flip the script" dialogue and Hormozi's own split-test data.
- **Section B: The Expensive Customer Problem** (PDF p. 37–65): the last cut from `$100M Money Models` — the conceptual math behind Customer-Financed Acquisition (CFA).
  - The Three Levers of CFA (CAC, LTGP, PPD) (PDF p. 39–41), then deep worked examples of each: CAC (PDF p. 45–47), LTGP (PDF p. 49–51), Payback Period / 30-Day Cash (PDF p. 53–61), Levels of CFA 1–3 (PDF p. 63–65).
- **Section C: Advanced Offer Stacking** (PDF p. 67–128): money models and an offer-stacking primer cut from `$100M Money Models`/`$100M Offers`.
  - Back End: The Value Grid (PDF p. 69–73); Advanced Offer Stacking How-To (PDF p. 75–83); then a "hodgepodge" of individual offer chapters: Free Presentations (PDF p. 85–91), Freemium (PDF p. 93–96), Free Pick-Your-Price (PDF p. 97–101), Free With Alternate Revenue Stream (PDF p. 103–108), Lifetime Upgrades (PDF p. 109–115), Lifetime Discounts (PDF p. 117–123), Discount + One-Time Fee (PDF p. 125–128).
- **Section D: Expanded Employees Chapter** (PDF p. 129–153): >half of the `$100M Leads` "Employees" chapter, cut for creeping into general management.
  - Why employees make you wealthy, the Five Lead Stages, the "Internal Core Four," the 3Ds training model, the Performance Diamond, ROI math (PDF p. 129–145); plus a bonus **"Maker or Manager?"** chapter on time management (PDF p. 147–153).
- **Free Goodies** (PDF p. 155): back-matter CTAs to acquisition.com/training, careers, and podcast (PDF p. 155).

## Key claims

- Choosing the right avatar is worth more than getting more customers: a same-vertical competitor making the same number of sales made "70x less profit" because they "accepted anyone with a pulse and a credit card" (2019-ish; PDF p. 7).
- Gym Launch's LTGP was "north of $45,000" vs. competitors' $6,000–$8,000, and the profit gap that produces is far larger than the 6–8x headline (PDF p. 8).
- 78% of Gym Launch's top customers had consumed at least TWO pieces of long-form content before buying; they then re-engineered the sales process to force that consumption (PDF p. 7, 95). — genuinely new stat.
- You make more money "because of who they are, not because of who you are": improving a page from 5%→7% conversion is worth 100x more to a $10M/mo client than a $100k/mo one for the same work (PDF p. 5).
- Four independent split-tests (10 markets each) of free vs. premium offers: close rate and average ticket were identical; only lead volume/cost differed — free front ends cut lead cost 5x+ (PDF p. 25–26).
- "Free Makes More Money": the #1 mistake is running a premium offer at free-offer prices; Price, Prospect, Process, Promotion, and Product must ALL match one structure — "You can't just mix and match" (the 5 P's framing; PDF p. 26).
- Four ways to *display* a discount — Percentage Off, Absolute Amount Off, Relative Equivalent Off, Simply the Discounted Price — the same promotion, tested for resonance (PDF p. 28). — new tactical framework.
- "30-Day Cash" is Hormozi's "most prized metric": gross profit extractable from a new customer in their first 30 days, keyed to the interest-free window of a credit card; getting 30D Cash > CAC yields "free customers using other people's money" (PDF p. 56). Claim: it took him "from $1,036 in my bank account to $100M+ in sales" (PDF p. 56).
- Three Levels of CFA: L1 (30-day GP < CAC, needs outside cash), L2 (30-day GP = CAC, credit limit = ad budget), L3 (30-day GP > 2x CAC, business self-finances and can ~double monthly) (PDF p. 63). At L3, 1 customer compounds to 4,095 in 12 months paying only for the first (PDF p. 64).
- First-year Gym Launch got "$100 back in profit for every $1 spent" — 100x ROAS; growth was then capped by operations, not acquisition (PDF p. 63).
- The **Value Grid** replaces the linear value-ladder: customers buy non-linearly (skip offers), so model LTV as a grid of take-rates; a stacked example lifted 30-Day Cash from $75 to $1,763 (~14–15x) for the same business (PDF p. 71–73). — genuinely new visual/analytic tool.
- Offer-stacking choreography: Attract → Up-Front Cash → Upsell/Downsell → Continuity, then repeat (Continuity #2 → Up-Front Cash #2) (PDF p. 77–78). Offer-selection filter: **Right Stage, Right Problem, Right Way, Right Time** (PDF p. 82–83). — new frameworks.
- "The no-sale sale": give a rejecting prospect a free at-home program (USB), then a free nutrition orientation, and sell supplements instead — these no-buyers spent 50% MORE on supplements than regular clients (PDF p. 103–104). Whether the first upsell is bought is "the greatest predictor of back-end conversion" (PDF p. 107).
- Continuity "stick" via bonuses = a "when" (delay or milestone) × a "what" (one-time, variable, or lifetime-upgrade bonus); the Gym Launch "new play every 14 days" bonus grew revenue ~13x, from ~$300k/mo to ~$4M+/mo (PDF p. 111, 113). — new stick framework + biographical figure.
- Initiation fees and "the bigger the head, the longer the tail": higher up-front payment → longer stick (Sunk Cost Fallacy); tanning-empire mentor John's $100 sign-up + $10/mo churned near-zero vs. $19-down/$19/mo (PDF p. 121–122, 127). — new principle.
- Employees are just "customers you pay": the Core Four map 1:1 to hiring (warm outreach→network, cold outreach→recruiting, content→job posts, paid ads→promoted posts) (PDF p. 135–136). — new reframe. Bottleneck-diagnosis story: the real problem was 40 interviews per frontline hire, fixed by group screening (PDF p. 129–130).
- **3Ds training** (Document, Demonstrate, Duplicate) and the **Performance Diamond** — 4 causes of dropped performance: Communication, Training, Motivation, Circumstances (PDF p. 137, 139–142). — new management frameworks (out of scope for Leads).
- **Maker vs. Manager**: makers need large uninterrupted blocks; a 30-min meeting costs a manager one slot but a maker "10x more" — Hormozi runs mornings as maker time, meetings after 1pm scheduled back-to-front (PDF p. 147–152; credits Paul Graham). — new time-management chapter.
