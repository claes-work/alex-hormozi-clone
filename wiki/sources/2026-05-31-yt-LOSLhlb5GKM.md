# You're Not a Victim of Your Circumstances. Keep Going.

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that work is never wasted because the worker becomes the durable output. He reacts to victim-language comments and then tells a self-reported story: after building and selling five gyms, he put the proceeds into the next venture, lost the money through a partner who disappeared and filed bankruptcy, and felt as if five years had vanished. In retrospect, he says the real output of those five years was his skill and capacity, which let him make more in the next 12 months than in the prior five years combined.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Victim-language can implicitly blame children, family, or circumstances for a person's unrealized goals.
- People do not have to feel good to keep going; feelings pass while the person remains.
- Hard work is not wasted if it changes who the person becomes.
- The work "works on" the person more than the person works on the project.
- Even if a project fails to produce the expected outcome, the skills and experience from the work can compound into the next opportunity.
- Smaller mountains build the ability needed for larger mountains.
- The more able a person becomes, the more able they realize they can become.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The work works on you more than you work on it."
