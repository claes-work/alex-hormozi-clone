# Helping a $50M Trucking Company Double Revenue

## Summary

Alex diagnoses an intermodal trucking company as supply constrained, not demand
constrained. The company has freight demand but cannot recruit enough owner-operators.
Alex reframes the business as a recruiting-independent-contractors business, then applies
customer-acquisition math to talent: cost to acquire talent, payback period, referral
spiffs, and a paid funnel for independent operators.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-16, Alex says the trucking company is supply constrained because it has more
  freight demand than owner-operator capacity.
- He reframes the company as being in the recruiting-independent-contractors business, not
  merely trucking.
- He introduces cost to acquire talent (CAT) as an analogue to cost to acquire customers
  (CAC).
- With founder-supplied gross-profit economics of roughly $70K/year per trucker, he says a
  much larger referral bonus could make sense if paid after productivity.
- He suggests a split bonus such as some cash upfront and the rest after six months,
  creating an internal champion for retention.
- He suggests pairing referral cash with route/load priority to reduce current operators'
  concern that new drivers hurt their own economics.
- He says paid ads and a simple application funnel with a short VSL can work for recruiting
  owner-operators when the target geography is known.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You're actually not in the cleaning business. You're in the recruiting cleaners business."

> "You're in the recruiting independent contractors business."

> "There's also cost to acquire talent, just CAT."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: CAT cost-to-acquire-talent frame; supply-constrained trucking as
contractor recruiting business; large productivity-contingent referral bonus.
