# When Money Stopped Mattering (2021-06-07)

## Summary

In a recorded community/group call, Hormozi answers a member's question about whether
his preference for wanting *less* (living on ~$30k while holding $1M in the bank) was
innate or learned. His answer: happiness depends on external circumstances, but **joy
comes from within** and is chosen; external wealth is "ephemeral" and disappears, so
he anchors meaning in **eternal values** rather than achievement.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- **Redefine happiness as joy.** Happiness is a reaction to outside circumstances; joy
  comes from within, so one can be sorrowful and still joyful — joy is how you *choose*
  to experience circumstances.
- **External achievement is ephemeral.** It exists only on the physical, temporary
  plane; all accumulated wealth and achievements will eventually disappear and have no
  lasting impact.
- **Casino-of-life analogy.** You're dealt a token, play, and stack chips — but unlike
  a real casino you can't cash out; you leave the table with your chips still on it. So
  it's "a fake game with fake money."
- **The shovel analogy.** Accomplishments are just "the shovel you use to dig the hole
  you'll fill back up when you die" — so choose the shovel (the game) you inherently
  find joy in.
- **Shift from outcome-based to value-based decision-making.** Only values last past
  the physical plane. Aligning what you believe, say, and do produces identity
  alignment; contradiction between them produces dissonance ("most of our lives").
- **Living your values = the small uncut corners.** You live in alignment when the
  small corners no one else would notice (a rushed email, a phoned-in ad or VSL) are
  done to your true standard, because only *you* know.
- **Questions why hard work is laudable** — we should be doing things we derive joy
  from, so being praised for working hard is odd.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Happiness... is a reaction. Joy comes from within, which means that I can be sad and
> sorrowful and filled with joy."

> "You're playing a fake game with fake money."

> "You can buy a castle, you can buy a house, and there's a person in India who has no
> shoes who has more joy in their life."

> "The only thing that I can dedicate my life to that is eternal are things that will
> last past the physical plane, which are values."

> "If you can shift your decision-making process from outcome-based... to value-based...
> you'll always feel in alignment with your identity."
