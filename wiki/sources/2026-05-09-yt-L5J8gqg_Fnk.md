# Start a Nuisance Business and Beat Lazy Owners

## Summary

Alex answers a 21-year-old veteran who feels business is too much alone by saying solo is
actually simpler because there are no employees to manage. He recommends "nuisance
businesses": existing categories where the founder wins by doing undesirable work better
than lazy owners or poorly trained employees. The early advantage is owner incentive,
responsiveness, and willingness to try harder.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says being solo can be easier because the founder only has himself to manage.
- He says many people can start nuisance businesses rather than inventing a new category.
- He says most people who make money copy existing business categories and execute well.
- He says many business owners are lazy or have poorly trained employees, creating room
  for a new owner-operator to compete.
- He says the owner can pitch that the customer gets the owner directly for the same
  price as an incumbent.
- He says early customers may take a shot because the owner wants testimonials and
  referrals.
- He says the small player can try harder than an employee who lacks owner incentives.
- He says business feels hard because of skill deficiencies and many steps, not because
  it is inherently impossible.
- He says people learn by trying, receiving feedback, identifying why they suck, and
  continuing until they suck less.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Start what I call nuisance businesses."

> "For the same price as that guy, you actually get the owner."

> "Everything you don't know how to do feels hard until you learn how to do it."
