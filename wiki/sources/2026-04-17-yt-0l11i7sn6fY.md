# How to Outlast the 10-Year Grind Before You Win

## Summary

Alex calls the long unrewarded season before success the "Rocky cutscene." Unlike a movie
montage, it usually lasts five or ten years. He frames hard tasks, lonely work, and lack of
support as selection effects: the harder the path is, the fewer people will keep following it.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-17, Alex says nearly every successful person he has encountered went through
  many years of boring work without reward.
- He calls that season the "Rocky cutscene," but says it usually lasts five or ten years,
  not five minutes.
- He says hard is not necessarily a warning sign; if the path is right, hardship can be a
  competitive filter.
- He says the frame can shift from "this is hard" to "no one else will be able to do this."
- He says people often root for someone only after they no longer need support.
- He says the exceptional path is lonely because being an exception means not being with
  everyone else.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Instead of lasting 5 minutes, it just usually lasts 5 or 10 years."

> "This is what hard feels like, and that's okay."

> "If it's hard, good. It means no one else will do it. More for you."

> "You always have to be the person who roots for you before everyone else does."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: Rocky-cutscene grind as 5-10 years; hard as selection effect; slow
clap/self-rooting before external support.
