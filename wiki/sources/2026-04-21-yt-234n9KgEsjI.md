# Why Most Coaching Businesses Have to Keep Launching New Offers

## Summary

Alex coaches a Christian coaching business deciding between an old coaching offer and a
new accredited-degree partnership. He says the real issue is not merely rising CAC but
LTV being too low. Since CAC advantages are temporary, he recommends solving back to
front by increasing lifetime value through better products and back-end offers.

## Key claims

- Alex says rising CAC can reflect a creative problem, neutral or negative word of
  mouth, or a formerly mispriced acquisition channel normalizing.
- He says negative word of mouth can compound and is often more sensitive than positive
  word of mouth.
- He says many coaching businesses keep launching new offers because the first thing was
  never good enough.
- He says the arms race of business is LTV, not CAC.
- He says CAC is often comparable within an industry and ephemeral, while LTV is the
  sustainable competitive advantage.
- He recommends fixing businesses back to front by increasing LTV before chasing lower
  CAC.
- He says he would prefer a two-to-one LTV:CAC product he controls over a three-to-one
  partnership he cannot control if the owned product can be expanded with a back end.
