# The Problem with Wanting to be a Millionaire, Not the Climb

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says people often want the view without the climb: they want what being a
millionaire allows but not what becoming one requires. His solution is to make the person
one becomes the goal, so progress can be won daily rather than only at distant
milestones.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Most people want to become millionaires because of what money allows, not because of the
  work required to become capable of earning it.
- Alex agrees with the frame that many people want the view without the climb.
- If who someone becomes is the goal, they can win every day rather than once per decade.
- Achievements become less emotionally powerful as they become more likely and more
  familiar.
- In lifting, a goal that once looked extraordinary can feel obvious by the time the
  lifter reaches it because many near-goals came first.
- Becoming the person who can become a millionaire is more durable than merely wanting the
  millionaire milestone.
- Making identity growth the achievement reduces resistance to the required actions.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Wanting the view without the climb."

> "If we make who we become the goal... we can win every day."

> "Become the person who can become a millionaire."
