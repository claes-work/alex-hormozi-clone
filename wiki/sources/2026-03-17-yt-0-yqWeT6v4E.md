# Helping a Personal Trainer Get to $1M/Year

## Summary
Live coaching clip in Alex's own origin domain (gyms/PT), where he leans directly on the **Gym Launch** playbook. Angelo runs a semi-private personal-training model (1-on-6, one 20-minute session/week, "no-sweat" in normal clothes, ~€80/month) across three locations in a 45–65+ demographic, doing ~€300k/year combined and wanting to reach €1M. He believes he's cash-constrained and short on leads. Alex reframes the diagnosis: **it's not a cash-constraint problem, it's a front-end pricing problem** — Angelo isn't charging enough up front to fund customer acquisition. Alex's prescription is his classic gym **onboarding / on-ramp offer**: sell a **defined six-week onboarding for €600** (up from just enrolling at €80/month) built around the *actual* fears of the 45+ client — accountability (why you put dollars on the line) and getting hurt (why you over-onboard) — with a measurable body-fat goal, weekly weigh-ins, and a meal plan. Frame the goal as money-back if hit, but the real purpose is to *start the process*, not to hit the goal. **Pitch the year-long continuity halfway through, never at the end**: praise their consistency, then "match their commitment" by crediting the €600 toward staying for the year. Mechanically: raise the monthly price from €80 to €130, then knock €50/month off for 12 months (= the €600 credited back), landing them back at the €80 they'd have paid anyway; most prepay for the year. To liquidate acquisition cost, run a **nutrition consultation** as the first appointment and sell €200–300 of supplements (roughly two months' worth for a six-week program), so products alone cover the cost to acquire a customer. On lead-gen, Alex says static ads are currently outperforming video for most people (per his Gym Launch team) because most people are bad on camera; his own preference is video of many customers doing an exercise (sit-ups, push-ups, jumping jacks) **in unison** because it's attention-grabbing, with the offer in the copy. He tells Angelo his one real homework is to read the *Gym Launch Secrets* book. Ends with the roadmap promo (ignored for claims).
