# The Biggest Predictor of Future Success

## Summary

Alex says he was always motivated and high-achieving, but also argues that a lack of past
success does not prevent someone from starting now. The work required for success stays
the same whenever a person begins. His practical frame is to generalize from any past
success, however small, and then build standards upward like adding weight to a bar.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice)

- Alex says he always wanted to make a lot of money and was always motivated.
- He self-reports being strong at school, entering college with 13 AP credits, finishing
  college in three years magna cum laude, and holding powerlifting/fraternity leadership
  roles.
- He says past success is the biggest predictor of future success.
- A person can generalize from success in one domain to build confidence in another.
- If someone has no obvious momentum, they can regress the win to getting out of bed or
  taking a ten-minute walk.
- Goals should become heavier over time like adding weight to a bar.
- The standard for excellence moves as a person improves.
- People should use their own current standard, not someone else's.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The biggest predictor of future success is past success."

> "The work that needs to be done for someone to succeed remains unchanged."

> "The bar moves."
