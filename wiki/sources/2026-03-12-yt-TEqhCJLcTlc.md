# But Are You Obsessed?

## Summary
Short solo monologue reframing "obsession" as the differentiator between people who succeed and everyone else. Alex's core move is to invert the label: what normal people call "obsessed," he calls merely *trying* — "obsession isn't really obsession. It's actually just trying and no one else tries." He considers the people others label obsessed to be the *sane* ones, and everyone else insane "because they do nothing and waste their lives." He grounds this in a first-principles memento-mori argument: life is short, hours are finite, so refusing to do what you want because of other people's "face noise" (their preference about how you live) makes no sense. He then decodes hate/criticism: he translates every insult to a single statement — "you do not live your life in a way that I prefer" — and argues hate usually signals jealousy plus a belief that you're undeserving of some specific part of your life (attention, money, or body), because "life has unjustly given you the benefit that they believe they rightfully deserve." On work-life balance he's blunt: people who obsess about it are "typically mediocre at both"; obsessed people apply obsession to everything and just call it *life*. He accepts work-life balance as "a wonderful goal" but says it won't happen if you want to be the best. He closes by dismantling "work smart, not hard": that only works against people who aren't smart — against other smart people the only edge left is working hard, so "work smart AND hard," because he'd rather just win.
