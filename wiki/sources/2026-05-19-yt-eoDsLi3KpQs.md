# The Conversation That Will Change Your Life

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says difficult emotions often point to a hard conversation he is avoiding.
He describes compressing the time between recognizing the needed conversation and having
it. He also connects a past DUI and 60 mph head-on collision to avoided partner
conversations, saying the near-death event forced him to make calls he had been avoiding.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When Alex feels anxious, insecure, angry, or sad, he asks what conversation he needs to
  have but is not having.
- The time between recognizing a needed conversation and having it has compressed for him.
- After a hard breakup, firing, quitting, or partner conversation, momentum can make the
  next hard conversation easier.
- Alex had multiple partners in gyms and a chiropractic/dental marketing agency, and those
  partners relied on him for money.
- He says the stress of avoided conversations contributed to a DUI and a 60 mph head-on
  collision.
- He walked away from the crash with no injuries.
- He interpreted the event as a wake-up call to have the conversations, not primarily to
  stop drinking.
- He called the first partner the next day, then immediately called the next one.
- Death or near-death has repeatedly acted as a catalyst for decisions in Alex's life.
- He connects this to leaving home and standing up to his father from a distance.
- Having nothing to lose can make someone dangerous because action has less downside.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What conversation do I need to have that I'm not having?"

> "I'd rather be alive."

> "I have nothing to lose."
