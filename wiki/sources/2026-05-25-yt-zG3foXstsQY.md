# Why Determination Beats Brilliance in Entrepreneurship

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says entrepreneurship is more a game of the heart than the head because the
decisive ability is persistence after repeated non-reward. He frames determination as a
long extinction curve: many people never knock, many knock once, and very few knock 100
times after no door opens. If someone keeps getting better and does not stop, winning
becomes a function of a long enough time horizon.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Entrepreneurship depends heavily on persisting after repeated non-reward.
- A long extinction curve means continuing the behavior long after most people quit.
- A brilliant person who does nothing loses to a determined person who keeps acting and
  improving.
- If someone has only two rules — get better and do not stop — winning becomes more likely
  as the time horizon expands.
- Entrepreneurship as an infinite game makes the goal to keep playing.
- This is why Alex frames entrepreneurship as heart more than head.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Get better, don't stop."

> "The only goal is to not stop playing."
