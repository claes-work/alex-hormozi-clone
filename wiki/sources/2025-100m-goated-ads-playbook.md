# $100M GOATed Ads Playbook (2025)

**What it is.** A short (~26 pp) Acquisition.com playbook by Alex Hormozi, subtitled "How to Make Ads That Get the Right People to Click," teaching a repeatable assembly-line system for producing hundreds of paid ads per week. Hormozi frames it explicitly as "to be used on top of the paid ads chapters in $100M Leads. Not instead of" — it "takes those ideas and puts them on steroids." Raw: `raw/books/2025-100m-goated-ads-playbook.md`.

## Thesis
Businesses that stall on paid ads have not saturated the market — they have hit a wall in **ad quality**. The fix is not spending more per ad but making dramatically *more and better* ads: the better the creative, the colder (and larger) the audience it can profitably convert. You scale ads by industrializing their production, not by hand-crafting a handful per month.

## Structure and content
Follows the table of contents: **GOATed Ads** (origin story, the wall) → **Why Ads Hit A Wall And How To Scale Past It** (Eugene Schwartz's 5 levels of awareness) → **The Ad Assembly Process** (hooks, the 80/20/0 prep split, 5 hook sources, expansion hooks by awareness) → **The Ad Meat** (5 creative formats) → **CTA – Call To Action** (Clear > Clever) → **Scale It** (repetition + Kaleidoscope Ads teaser) → **Cheat Sheet** (5 steps). The core mechanic: **50 hooks × 3–5 meats × 1–3 CTAs = 150–750 ads per week**.

## Key claims
- **Origin (Summer 2020 – December 2021):** a portfolio founder selling weight loss on Facebook spent ~$200k/month, made "at least five" new ads per month, and believed they had saturated the market at $5–8k/day spend, where CAC "shoots through the roof." (GOATed Ads, origin story)
- Hormozi diagnosed it as an **ad-quality wall, not a market wall**: "You haven't saturated Facebook. But you have gotten all the low hanging fruit." (GOATed Ads)
- The prescribed fix: go from ~5 ads/month to **~500–600 ads/month** by devoting every Friday to advertising and running winners through a "kaleidoscope" to spin off 50 hooks × 3–5 meat variations = ~150 ads/week. (GOATed Ads)
- **Result claim:** "The company doubled in two quarters." Same process runs ads for Acquisition.com, Skool.com, and their other companies; the process "took me 13 years to develop." (GOATed Ads)
- Uses **Eugene Schwartz's 5 levels of awareness** — Most Aware, Product-Aware, Solution-Aware, Problem-Aware, Completely Unaware — as an audience-temperature map, warmest/smallest at the bottom to coldest/largest at the top. (Why Ads Hit A Wall)
- Hormozi adapts Schwartz rather than applying him rigidly: "I've never met these people in Eugene's pyramid. Labels are clean. Reality is messy" — the colder/larger the audience, the more varied and exceptional the ads must be. (Why Ads Hit A Wall)
- **Assembly-line reframe:** making many ads at once is hard, but making them in *parts* is easy — "I wasn't really creating ads at all. I was assembling them." Side benefit: competitors cannot reverse-engineer your top performers. (The Ad Assembly Process)
- **Prep split: 80% Hooks / 20% Meat / ~0% CTAs**; "90% of the work is preparation" and "ads are made in research, not in recording." (The Ad Assembly Process)
- **Five sources of high-converting hooks:** (1) your own previous winning ads, (2) your own free content, (3) other people's ads (saved to a phone album), (4) other people's free content, (5) platform ad libraries (last resort). "Winning hooks in one industry often work in others." (The Ad Assembly Process)
- **Expansion hooks map to awareness level:** Most Aware → offer-driven; Product-Aware → proof-driven; Solution-Aware → promise-driven; Problem-Aware → pain-driven; Completely Unaware → curiosity-driven — with B2C (XFast weight-loss shake) and B2B (DigitalBoost agency) worked examples per level. (The Ad Assembly Process)
- **Widest-hooks pro tip:** memes / meme-like content attract the largest audience slice; when in doubt go broader. The Subway "$5 Foot Long" works only because the whole nation already knows the brand. (The Ad Assembly Process)
- **Five meat (creative) formats** that worked across all industries: Demonstration, Testimonial, Education, Story, and Faceless — the meat only gets 20% of attention and rotates less often (3–5 per weekly session suffices). (The Ad Meat)
- **CTA rule: "Clear > Clever."** Spell out the next step and *show AND tell* it; a good CTA covers what to do, how, when, what they get, and (optional) what happens next — test by running 3 identical hook+meat combos changing only the CTA. (CTA)
- **Scale by repetition:** "New customers enter your market everyday… It'll still be the first time they see it" — double down on wild outperformers; the next level beyond this playbook is **Kaleidoscope Ads** ("how I take ads to the moon"). (Scale It)
