# Hard Work Won't Make You Rich (2022-10-05)

## Summary

Hormozi attacks the "no one can outwork me" guru mantra as objectively false and,
more importantly, irrelevant: output = volume of activity × the leverage of that
activity, and at the highest levels leverage — not work — determines results
(Warren Buffett makes a couple of good decisions a year). He reframes the
entrepreneur as the coach of a team of complementary specialists (marketing,
sales, product, customer success), not the star player, and maps a three-level
evolution: individual contributor → functional executive (CMO/CSO/CPO) → coach/CEO
who recruits top talent and builds a farm system to create a dynasty.

## Key claims

- **Output = volume × leverage.** Hours are capped and roughly equal across
  hard workers, so activity volume is a "newbie measure"; the differentiator is
  the leverage of the inputs you choose.
- **"No one can outwork me" is bragging about the wrong metric.** Better boasts:
  "I can't be out-recruited / out-managed / no one can build a culture or lead
  like me." Work is an irrelevant activity metric at scale.
- **Acquisition.com's growth method:** examine a CEO's calendar and org chart,
  identify low-leverage activities, and either hand them to someone in the chart
  or hire a role for them. You can't add hours, only swap what fills them.
- **The entrepreneur should be the coach, not the star player.** A team of
  complementary roles beats a lone all-star with a poor supporting cast (Apple
  beats the solo scorer). Uses an RPG war-party analogy (healer, mage, paladin).
- **The CEO must have the highest return on time in the org**, always solving the
  next problem; the value of a great CEO/coach is the delta between what the team
  produces with vs. without top leadership.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "Hard work will not make you rich."
- "Waking up early doesn't matter, going to bed late doesn't matter, not sleeping
  a lot doesn't matter. The few things that matter are the inputs that go into the
  system that have the most leverage to create the most output."
- "A lot of entrepreneurs, as I see in them, are trying to play the game like
  they're the star player and they want to score all the points, but they lose to
  Apple because Apple's got a team."
- "It should be like, I can't be out-recruited, I can't be out-managed, no one can
  build a culture like me... no one can lead like me. But 'no one can outwork me'
  — it's just an irrelevant activity metric."
- "I love working, I work all the time, but I would never claim that no one works
  harder than me. I tend to have very good leverage with the activities that I do."
- "The value of the CEO... they need to have the highest return on time out of
  anyone in the organization because they need to always be solving the next
  problem."
