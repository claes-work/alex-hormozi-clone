# You're on the Right Path

## Summary

Alex says supporters can turn hostile when an underdog's success surpasses their own
reference point. He anchors the idea in his first gym: clients encouraged him while he
slept there, then mocked his status after he hired a manager. A performance coach later
taught him to treat hatred as a prerequisite and leading indicator of success. Chris
Williamson adds examples about social pushback from old friends.

## Key claims

- Alex self-reports receiving a college writing scholarship, serving as newspaper vice
  president, and editing a creative-writing magazine.
- He self-reports sleeping in his first gym, showering at the YMCA, and hiring staff and
  a manager within nine months.
- Alex dates the underdog-to-target experience to age 23 and says the threshold depended
  on each observer's own level of success.
- As of April 2026, he says people often want others to do well but not better than them.
- He credits an unnamed performance coach for reframing hatred as a prerequisite and
  indicator of success.
- Alex says criticism from below can reflect the critic's abandoned ambitions rather
  than useful information about the target.
- Chris Williamson, not Alex, supplies the Modern Wisdom listener and old-friends social
  examples in the middle of the excerpt.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Everyone believes in the American dream until it comes true."

> "People want you to do well, but not better than them."

> "No one hates you from above."
