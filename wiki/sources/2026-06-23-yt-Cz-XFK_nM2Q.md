# Helping a $21M Orthopedic Surgeon Scale His Practice

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an orthopedic/regenerative-medicine practice shifting from insurance to cash-based services. The practice has a very high close rate and credibility from a Joe Rogan podcast appearance, but needs more top-of-funnel demand. Alex recommends treating the Rogan episode as a clean VSL-like asset: drive the right audience to the episode, especially within a local radius, then have a salesperson follow up proactively.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - orthopedic surgeon/regenerative-medicine practice owner. His revenue, cash-pay shift, FDA/FTC concerns, Rogan exposure, and close rate are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A credible podcast appearance can function like a video sales letter if the right prospects consume it before sales follow-up.
- The simplest first move is to put the highest-likelihood candidates into an audience and encourage them to listen to a truncated or full version of the podcast.
- If compliance or relationship concerns exist, the cleanest version is to send traffic to the original episode rather than altering it.
- The return can be measured by the next action after the audience listens.
- If the practice has both local and national patients, Alex would start with a 50-mile local radius around the practice and the podcast asset.
- The missing piece is proactive sales follow-up: someone should call leads, ask whether they listened, discuss their issue, and book them with the doctor.
- The broader channel choice could be affiliate, content, or paid ads, but the Rogan asset is the obvious first leverage point.
