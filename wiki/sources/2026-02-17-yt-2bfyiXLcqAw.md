# How to Hire A-Players

## Summary

A short interview clip (host asks questions, Alex answers — only Alex-attributed
material below) in which Alex describes how he *recognizes* an A-player **after**
they start rather than how he sources or screens them pre-hire. His core diagnostic:
you can tell within the first week or two by watching how a new hire changes your
own life. An A-player rapidly takes work off your plate, acts proactively, and
produces "high activity, high alignment." He offers a simple three-way onboarding
test — your life gets *worse* (bad), your life *doesn't change* (also bad), or your
life visibly *improves / time gets reallocated dramatically* (the A-player signal).
The clip closes with an aside on what he refuses to delegate (all his own writing)
and a plug for the free acquisition.com/roadmap lead magnet.

**Distinctness vs. sibling hiring videos:** This one is narrow and *post-hire /
first-two-weeks diagnostic*, not a pre-hire sourcing or screening method. It does
**not** repeat the 3-part rubric, four-elements-of-equity, barbell hiring, or the
referral cascade that the hiring topic already covers. The only genuinely new
mechanic is the "does my life get better / worse / unchanged after they start?"
onboarding litmus test plus the "high activity, high alignment" phrasing. Otherwise
thin — likely heavy overlap in spirit with the two sibling clips but a different lens.

## Key claims

All paraphrased from Alex, dated 2026-02-17.

- ★ **First-two-weeks A-player test.** Alex says he has never had an A-player he
  didn't recognize as an A-player within the first week or two of them starting —
  detection is fast, not a slow reveal.
- ★ **Onboarding litmus: watch how your own life changes.** Three-way read on a new
  hire: (1) if your life gets *worse* when they start, that's a bad sign; (2) if your
  life *doesn't change* at all, that's also not a good sign; (3) an A-player produces
  a *dramatic reallocation of your time* — especially if they were hired to take
  things off your plate. A little onboarding friction is expected and fine.
- ★ **"High activity, high alignment."** The two-factor signature of an A-player:
  a tremendous amount of activity combined with alignment, so they move many things
  in the right direction — and ideally you get attention/capacity back rather than
  spending more.
- **Signs of an A-player:** they decrease the likelihood of failure ("support"),
  take things off his plate quickly, and are proactive in their decision-making and
  the actions they take.
- **The test scales.** Alex says this read works "at all levels," not just senior hires.
- **What he refuses to delegate (aside):** he writes all his own books, all his own
  emails, does all his own recording, and writes all his own tweets — every piece of
  written word out of acquisition.com is him, which he acknowledges is a huge amount
  of work.
