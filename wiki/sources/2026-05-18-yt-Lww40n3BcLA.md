# Success Is the Only Revenge

## Summary

Alex tells the origin story of "success is the only revenge." As a high-school freshman,
he fantasized about returning to a reunion and proving critics wrong. His teacher and
lifting mentor, Mr. Given, challenged the fantasy by pointing out that direct revenge
would reveal that the critics still owned Alex's attention. Alex says the only real
revenge is winning so completely that the critics become irrelevant.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice)

- Alex says a teacher named Mr. Given saw him as an angry teenager and trained with him
  every day in high school.
- Alex says he once fantasized about using future success to humiliate people at a
  reunion.
- Mr. Given, as Alex recounts it, told him that explicit revenge would make Alex look
  petty and show that the critics had won by occupying his attention.
- Alex frames success as the only revenge because every other form keeps the critic at
  the center.
- Winning should become so large that critics shrink into irrelevance.
- Copycats are behind because they copy surface traits without understanding why the
  original worked.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Success is the only revenge."

> "Everything else does show that you were thinking about these people all day long."

> "Copycats will always be behind."
