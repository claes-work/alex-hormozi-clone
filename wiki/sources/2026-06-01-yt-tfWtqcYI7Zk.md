# 1 Habit That Fixes 90% of Problems

## Summary

Alex Hormozi gives a short solo lesson on one high-leverage behavior: going to bed on time. He defines leverage as getting many outcomes from one effort, then argues that a consistent bedtime prevents many downstream problems, especially for younger adults: drinking-related mistakes, poor sleep, hangovers, weaker work performance, accidents, and missed career advancement. His tactical recommendation is to set an alarm for bedtime rather than wake-up time.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A high-leverage behavior creates many positive downstream outcomes from one action.
- Going to bed on time is one such behavior, especially for people in their 20s and early 30s.
- A consistent bedtime reduces exposure to nightlife-related mistakes and improves next-day performance.
- Regular sleep timing improves sleep quality and can compound into health and appearance benefits.
- Setting an alarm for bedtime is more useful than relying only on a wake-up alarm.
- Going to bed early makes waking up naturally much easier.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Set your alarm for when you go to bed."
