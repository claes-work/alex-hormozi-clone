# The Future of Ads: Content and Ads Are Merging

## Summary

A music lawyer who previously appeared on Cash Cow says he grew substantially after
Alex's advice but now needs more qualified leads. Alex says the business has a pure
demand problem: make more and better content, run the best content as Meta ads, and
dedicate the first four hours of each day to content and ads. He adds that content and
ad creative are merging because platforms want ads to feel like native content.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex says if the caller can handle more customers, the next controllable levers are
  Meta ads and more content.
- He says content and ads can accomplish the same objective when top-performing organic
  posts become ad creative.
- He says the future of ads is that content and ad creative merge.
- He argues that ad and content algorithms are converging because platforms prefer an
  ad-funded experience that still feels content-native.
- He says the caller's content cadence is adequate, so the bigger improvement is content
  quality.
- He recommends placing proof, such as helping artists close over $5 million, front and
  center in introductions and videos.
- He says the business has already fixed model, conversion, and pricing, so the current
  issue is demand generation.
- He recommends spending the first four hours of every day on making more content and
  turning that content into ads.
- He says industry events and associations can help, but content gives broader
  distribution.
