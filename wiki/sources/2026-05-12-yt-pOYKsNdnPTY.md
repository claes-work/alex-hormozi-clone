# What's the Best Way to Land Enterprise Clients?

## Summary

Alex answers a question from a 3D-imagery provider serving off-plan real-estate
developers. He says enterprise clients require finding smaller, more dispersed ponds
where decision makers gather or where trusted intermediaries can introduce the seller.
The fastest path is unscalable relationship access through trade shows, conferences,
centers of influence, affiliates, law firms, and architects; the long-term path is
building a brand strong enough that the market comes to the seller.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex says enterprise selling still follows the principle of going where the fish are,
  but the ponds are smaller and more dispersed.
- He recommends trade shows and conferences as concentrated access points for enterprise
  buyers.
- He recommends finding centers of influence, such as law firms or architects that
  already serve the same real-estate developers.
- He says enterprise sellers often need internal or trusted champions because many gates
  sit between the seller and the decision maker.
- He frames thought leadership and long-term brand building as the best path, but not
  necessarily the fastest one.
- He says the fastest route is more aggressive outreach, event attendance, and
  partnership or affiliate access to the same buyers.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You typically want to go where the fish are."

> "The ponds are much smaller and more disparate."

> "The best way is build a big brand."
