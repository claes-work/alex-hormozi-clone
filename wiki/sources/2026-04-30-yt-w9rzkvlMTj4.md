# 300 People at $300/Month = $100K Working 2 Days a Week

## Summary

Alex compares a low-ticket course/group-call community against a higher-priced support
community and recommends doing the unscalable. For many creators, he prefers one-to-one
or one-to-small-group help, possibly around $99-$299 per month, because a cohort model
can still reach meaningful revenue with little staff. He warns creators not to obsess
over scale when they have very few members.

## Key claims

- Reducing price and support after reaching 10 members penalizes early movers; Alex
  recommends incentivizing early movers instead.
- For many early communities, Alex prefers one-to-one or one-to-small-group support.
- He suggests one-on-six cohorts as a practical way to deliver personalization without
  pure one-on-one labor.
- Alex models that 300 people at $300/month can create about $100,000/month while the
  founder works roughly two call days per week.
- Founders with small groups should deliver heavily before worrying about scalability.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Do the unscalable."

> "Deliver like crazy and then think about scale later."

> "Are you upset about making a million dollars a year doing that like with no employees?"
