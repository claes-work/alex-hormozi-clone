# The #1 Online Business You Should Start Right Now

## Summary

A scripted Skool VSL (video sales letter) narrated by Alex Hormozi promoting the "Skool Games," a free competition to build a paid online community on Skool. Alex frames a paid community as the lowest-time, lowest-skill, zero-risk online business anyone can start — even without an audience, expertise, or tech skills — and cites a string of member success stories and platform stats to pitch signing up. Content is heavily promotional; the durable persona value is his positioning of the paid-community business model and the analogies he uses to explain it.

## Key claims (Alex's framing)

- A paid online community is the online business that takes the **least time to start** (you can make money in hours) and requires the **least skill** — "if you can invite people to a party, you can make this business work."
- You **don't need to be an expert**; you invite the people and let them do the heavy lifting — analogized to Wikipedia, which is "not the expert at anything, they just bring the people together."
- To pick a community topic, choose a painful experience you overcame, a passion, or a skill you learned — no matter how small (organizing folders, meal prepping for kids with food allergies, travel hacks).
- Claims **54.1% of people who start a paid community on Skool make money** ("more than one out of two"), with the **average paid community owner earning $1,360/month**.
- A paid community is like an **online party**: the host just invites guests, provides the place, and sets the rules; the guests make the party, but the host gets the credit and collects the money at the door.
- People already pay hundreds to thousands to get into digital communities "the same way they do nightclubs or cafes."
- Starting a community is **faster than building a course or coaching program** because "you don't need to have all the answers."
- Everything needed (calendars, payment processing, software) is built into Skool; membership can be crowdsourced from the platform's existing "millions of users," so you don't have to ask people you know.
- Positions the offer as **zero-risk**: worst case you walk away with "thousands of dollars worth of knowledge"; there's an unconditional money-back guarantee on the trial.
