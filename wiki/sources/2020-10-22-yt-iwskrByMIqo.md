# How To Win Sales Without Being Skilled (2020-10-22)

## Summary

In an early-morning (4:30 a.m.) video recorded while building phone-sales training for
his Gym Launch / GymLords gyms, Hormozi argues that the person who wins a sale is not the
most technically skilled seller but the one who most genuinely cares about the buyer's
best interest. He illustrates it with a gym owner who went from 0-for-25 to 5-for-9
overnight — same script — after switching from selling on his own agenda to selling on the
buyer's. He extends this to conviction in the product, sales-as-coaching (confronting the
buyer's limiting beliefs from a place of care), and a broader three-stage model of
entrepreneurial development: skills, then character traits, then beliefs.

## Key claims

- **(2020)** A sale is always made — "either they sell you or you sell them"; the person
  who makes the sale is the one who cares most about the buyer's interests.
- **(2020)** Buyers can sense self-interest — he calls it "commission breath"; people smell
  whether you're in it for your agenda or theirs.
- **(2020)** To win, you must genuinely believe you care more about the prospect's best
  interest than they do about themselves; if they feel that, they'll trust you and be real
  with you.
- **(2020)** The moment you get a prospect to be real (drop their emotional baggage, shame,
  guilt), the sale is essentially already won.
- **(2020)** Personality-driven, genuinely kind-hearted salespeople "get away with so much
  lack of skill" and still close, because buyers believe they act in their best interest —
  which is why conviction in your product matters so much.
- **(2020)** Sales is just coaching "with an outcome that's defined over a period of time";
  genuine care lets you confront and break the buyer's limiting beliefs systematically
  (e.g. making them own responsibility for their result) so you can actually help them.
- **(2020)** The gym owner used the *same script* before and after the turnaround; the only
  change was whose agenda he sold on — realization hit at "rep 25," which he jokes is a
  "magical number."
- **(2020)** Advanced salespeople are advanced because of their *outcomes*, not different
  tactics: "they never don't do the basics." Analogy: advanced lifters don't use different
  tactics than novices, they just lift more weight and are more consistent over time.
- **(2020)** Three breaking points in an entrepreneur's professional development, treated as
  cyclical (each new level demands all three again on top of maintaining the prior level):
  1. **Skills** — you enter entrepreneurship with nothing of value; "getting customers" is
     really many stacked skills (copy, creative, landing pages, buying/placing traffic,
     nurturing leads, choosing the sales/conversion mechanism, scripting, tonality,
     objection handling, processing payments).
  2. **Character traits** — patience, endurance, discipline to execute the skills
     consistently; this separates the very successful from the moderately successful.
  3. **Beliefs** — top-down from the founder: beliefs about the world, the market, what's
     possible, and tolerance for speed/excellence in the organization.
- **(2020)** Growth is painful; he frames entrepreneurship as a "masochistic desire" to keep
  improving and suffer the whole way while loving the process.

## Topic notes

This is an early (2020) proto-version of ideas the mature [[../topics/business/sales-communication]]
page treats as delivery mechanics. Where the 2025 sources dissect *how* to say the words
(tone levers, breathing the script, discovery, seven universal closes), this 2020 talk is
purely about the *inner stance* underneath: genuine care for the buyer and conviction in the
product as the true drivers of closing.

Continuities and proto-versions to note:
- **Care / "commission breath" → belief-continuum.** The core thesis ("the person who cares
  most about the buyer wins," buyers can *smell* self-interest) is an early form of the
  conviction/belief material that later sources formalize. Conviction in the product is
  named explicitly here as the reason unskilled but caring closers succeed.
- **Sales = coaching; confronting limiting beliefs.** Breaking the buyer's limiting beliefs
  "from a place of genuine care" prefigures the later discovery/objection-handling logic in
  [[../topics/business/sales-communication]] — here framed emotionally (get them to be real,
  drop the shame/guilt) rather than mechanically.
- **"Never don't do the basics" / "no one is above the script."** The 2020 line that advanced
  performers win on outcomes via relentless basics rhymes with the later insistence that
  consistent words (the script) are the non-negotiable foundation.
- **Three-block development model (skills → character traits → beliefs)** is a mindset/
  entrepreneurship framework that may warrant its own topic page if corroborated by other
  sources; noted here as an L2 catch.
