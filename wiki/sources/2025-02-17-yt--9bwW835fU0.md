# The Game-Changing Million Dollar Rule

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains his "rule of 100" — doing 100 primary income-producing actions
per day (100 minutes of content, 100 outreaches, or $100/day on ads), which he estimates
takes about four hours of pure input. He escalates it to "open to goal" (work until the
goal is hit, no matter the hours), a habit he attributes to a gym owner and says he sees
across high achievers. The core thesis: work is defined by output, output equals volume
times leverage, and disproportionate success comes from ruthlessly focusing on the one
input that most closely tracks the output you want.

## Key claims

- **The rule of 100**: do 100 primary actions per day — e.g., 100 minutes of content,
  100 outreaches (if outbound), or spend $100/day on ads (and if the ad money is already
  spent, do 100 minutes/day writing ad copy, studying other people's ads, and finding
  hooks). Alex notes he did not invent it.
- The rule of 100 takes most people about **4 hours a day**, and this is **pure input,
  not output** — but he has already defined which inputs create the desired outputs.
- **"Open to goal"** (the rule of 100 "on steroids"): work open until you hit your goal —
  sometimes done by noon, sometimes 5 a.m. to midnight. Discussed in *$100M Leads*;
  learned from a man who owned 13–14 successful gyms whose managers "work open to goal."
- First figure out **which input most closely correlates with the output you want**, then
  jam as much as possible into that input. People "freeze" when told to just "get 10
  customers" because the output isn't an actionable input.
- **Behavior skew**: beginners spray scattered effort ("shotgun") until they find the one
  thing that works — then they should "jam that button as hard as you possibly can."
- **Work = output; output = volume × leverage** (how many times you do the thing × how
  much you get each time). Work smart *and* hard: max reps at max leverage.
- Skill is the leverage multiplier (100 skilled calls can yield 10× more), and the only
  way to gain skill is by doing more inputs — a cycle of doing more, then getting more per
  rep.
- Most business owners work a fair amount but on the wrong stuff, done the wrong way, so
  they get little for their effort.

- "Sometimes that means they hit their goal by noon and they can cut out, or that means
  that they have to go from 5:00 a.m. until midnight that night, because that's how long
  it took them to hit the goal."
- "I've been disproportionately successful in different domains of my life by ruthlessly
  focusing on one input."
- "Once you hit that, then you just jam that button as hard as you possibly can."
- "They get so little for their effort that they wonder, when they're at home empty-handed
  in bed, why isn't this working when I am working."
- "I define work by output, and in order to get output it's volume times leverage — how
  many times you do the thing times how much you get for each time you do it."
- "The only way you get skilled is by doing more inputs, by working more."
