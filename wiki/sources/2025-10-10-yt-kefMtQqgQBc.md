# If you went broke today, how would you start over?

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a man exiting his primary business — a regional financial-services operation where he was the #2 rep out of ~3,000 — who is deciding whether to rebuild that same business (a non-compete forces him to relocate 60 miles and effectively start over) or pour his ~$1M exit proceeds into two side ventures (a real-estate portfolio and a junk-removal company). Alex's answer is emphatic: rebuild the thing you're already elite at, because you'll do it in a third of the time, and stop letting side hustles dilute your focus. His central "start over" test is the gun-to-the-head question — what would you do if you *had* to make money — and the answer is always the skill you can execute in your sleep.

## Key claims (Alex's start-over playbook)

- If you're #2 out of 3,000 reps, you're in the top 0.1% — that elite skill is the thing to rebuild, not abandon. **Do that.**
- The side ventures "sound sexy and interesting," but that appeal is a trap that distracts from the sure thing.
- Rebuilding a business you've already built is far faster the second time — "a third of the time" — because you keep all the lessons. He frames the man as **~24 months from a brand-new version of the same business**, minus the mistakes.
- Don't turn your **side hustle into your main hustle.** Keep the passive real estate for cash flow ("if I go become a vegetable there's cash flow for everybody"), but know what your *main game* is.
- **Returns on real estate are not as good as returns in business, period.** Real estate makes more millionaires; private equity / business makes more billionaires. Private-equity owners are, on average, far wealthier.
- **Low-risk, high-likelihood business — once you've gained the skills — is always where all the upside is.**
- Running multiple side hustles feels like a hedge against your own risk, but it actually *increases* risk: it lowers the odds that the one sure thing stays a sure thing. You're already losing focus.
- The **gun-to-the-head test**: if you *had* to make money right now, what would you do? Whatever that is (for the guest, "sell insurance"), go do that thing — you'll make far more money.
- He's speaking from experience: he ran a dental agency, a chiropractor agency, and a gym; if he went broke tomorrow with a $100K bill, he'd relaunch a gym "in his sleep" because that's the skill he can execute without thinking.
- The unpopular but correct advice reduces to: **do you believe you can win? Then do the main thing and forget the other stuff.**
