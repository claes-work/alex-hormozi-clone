# Helping a $9M Tax Firm Get to $25M

## Summary

Alex answers a question about whether departments can pursue different constraints at
once. He says his current view is that the business has one main constraint and the
whole team should know it, because parallel priorities take longer and dilute support.
The team should keep core operations running, but resource allocation should be violent
about the single most important objective.

## Key claims

- Alex says he has gone back and forth, but currently believes there is one business
  constraint that unlocks growth.
- He says everyone should know the one constraint the company is working on.
- He says other fires create urgency to solve the first constraint rather than
  justification to split attention.
- He says parallel fixing often takes longer than solving one constraint and then the
  next.
- He says the "silent six" means ordinary business functions must keep running, but the
  company still needs one priority.
- He says entrepreneurs are chief allocators of resources and must be able to say one
  important thing is more important than another.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "There is one constraint."

> "What is the one thing that matters most?"

> "This one is more important."
