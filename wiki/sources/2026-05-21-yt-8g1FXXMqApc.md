# How to Fix a Broken Sales Funnel

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a sexual-health medical practice on funnel friction and paid
acquisition. He separates quality percentage from absolute volume: adding application
friction improved call quality and close rate, so the next constraint is driving more
qualified traffic into the now-filtered funnel.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If adding friction filters out unqualified applicants, the percentage of qualified calls
  should rise even if absolute call volume falls.
- A higher close rate after adding friction means the quality problem improved.
- Once funnel quality improves, the next problem is driving more front-end traffic.
- The practice should identify which content attracts quality buyers, not just quantity.
- Existing customers can be surveyed or incentivized to report which content brought them
  in.
- Sales calls should ask what video or content type caused the prospect to book.
- Alex uses in-person workshop attendees to identify which content formats attract
  higher-quality business owners.
- Retargeting current YouTube viewers or site visitors is low-hanging fruit if compliant.
- Meta ads may be easier to crack first than YouTube ads, even for a YouTube-heavy
  business.
- The earlier Meta attempt happened before the funnel friction was fixed, so the old ad
  result may not apply.
- Save-worthy shorts or reels often show higher purchase intent than merely share-worthy
  posts.
- Alex recommends running the top 10% of reels by saves-to-likes ratio as Meta ads into
  the improved funnel.
