# If You Get No Hate, You're Not Successful

## Summary

Alex reflects on the moment he felt he had moved from underdog to "the man" while
running his gym at age 23. He says a performance coach reframed hatred as a requirement
and indicator of success, not something to avoid. Alex connects later criticism and
social pushback to this frame: no one hates from above, and discomfort from peers can be
an early signal that the person is moving away from the old group standard.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex says that at age 23, while running his gym, he first experienced moving from
  underdog to the kind of person others might attack.
- He says the threshold varied by observer: less successful people shifted earlier, while
  more successful business-owner members kept supporting him longer.
- He says a performance coach told him hate is not avoidable but requisite for success.
- He adopted the frame that no hate means insufficient success.
- He says this made online and social criticism easier for him to handle.
- He says no one hates from above, meaning criticism often comes from people below or
  behind the target on that dimension.
- He frames friends' discomfort, jokes, and pushback as leading indicators that someone
  is changing in a worthwhile direction.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If you get no hate you are not successful."

> "No one hates you from above."

> "I had gone from underdog to the man."
