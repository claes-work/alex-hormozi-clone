# The 3 Fastest Ways I Know To Grow Any Business

## Summary

Solo Alex Hormozi video laying out three (plus a bonus fourth) ways to "10x your
income," applicable to both employees and business owners. The three levers are
arbitrage (buying/selling across markets, geographic or network), giveaways (giving
away high-value stuff free to expand awareness and generate demand), and pricing
(making customers worth more via price anchors and profit maximizers). He frames wealth
as a ratio (spend vs. earn, not an absolute number) and closes with a bonus fourth
lever: monetizing your existing contact list. Illustrated throughout with his own
business history (gym, Gym Launch, ALAN, Prestige Labs, Skool, Acquisition.com).

## Key claims

The 3 fastest ways to 10x income (paraphrase unless quoted):

1. **Arbitrage** — buy cheap in one market, sell dear in another, capitalizing on the
   price difference for the same asset. Applies to businesses and employees. Forms:
   *geographic* (live where it's cheap, e.g. Thailand, sell services to expensive
   markets like the US/Europe); *network* (overpay to access high-net-worth people, as
   an entrepreneur in his high-rise did to fund his company — Alex calls this risky and
   says he wouldn't do it that way); *spend reduction* (share housing, buy only from
   Costco — how he lived early on). All profit is fundamentally arbitrage: you know how
   to do something for less than it costs someone else, and the gap is your profit.
   Wealth is a ratio — spend relative to earn — so you can "make more or need less."
2. **Giveaways** — give away amazing stuff for free to increase how many people know
   you. "Free" and "new" are the two most powerful words in marketing. The giveaway
   must have real hard cost/tangible value or there's no reciprocity. It only works
   long-term if there's a back-end monetization system. His favorite business model:
   look at what everyone else is buying, make it better, make it free, monetize another
   way. Examples: the gym six-week challenge (vs. competitors' 14/21-day trials); Gym
   Launch Secrets book (give away the secrets, sell the implementation); ALAN — gave
   away unlimited coaching to software users, went 0 → $1.7M/month in six months;
   Prestige Labs "Supplement Selling Secrets"/S3; Skool $100K / Cybertruck contest;
   Acquisition.com free books + free Scaling Roadmap. Structure giveaways as lead
   magnets: reveal a problem, offer a trial, or a one-step-in-a-multi-step process
   (cites $100M Leads chapter).
3. **Pricing** (make customers worth more) — price is the most powerful lever on
   profit. *For employees*, three options: ask for a raise (weakest), take on risk
   (tie compensation to a metric you control — strongest; owners earn more because they
   bear more risk; cites Eduardo Saverin's $30K first Facebook investment), or switch
   roles (the within-company swap is underrated). An employee is essentially a business
   selling services on a recurring contract; the only real difference from an owner is
   risk (a 1099 is a business owner, a W-2 employee isn't). *For businesses*, implement
   a **price anchor** — a price set far above your normal menu that reframes everything
   else as cheap (people frame all numbers relative to the first number they hear; drop
   big numbers early in a pitch). Distinguishes **luxury** (price itself confers value/
   status by proxy — Nusret's gold-foil $2–3K steaks) from **premium** (value is in a
   materially better result/experience). The **profit maximizer**: the thing you sell
   most isn't the thing you make most money on (McDonald's makes its money on fries and
   Coke, not burgers; corporate's real business is real estate). A friend added a
   $5,000/month tier to a $500/month offer he never intended to sell — more people
   bought it *and* a higher share bought the old top tier because it now looked
   reasonable.

**Bonus 4th way** — monetize assets you already have: hit your existing list (cell
phone contacts, email, socials) with the combination of the above. Cost is near zero
because you already paid to acquire those customers. His largest portfolio company ran
a 3-email campaign to a webinar and made $2.3M; scaled down 10x that's $230K, 100x is
$23K.
