# Helping a Hospitality Consulting Business Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches a bar/hospitality consulting business deciding between a lower-priced volume
offer and a white-glove enterprise offer. He says the right question is often not "what is
more scalable?" but "how do we scale what is unscalable?" He prefers the white-glove
customer if those clients are easier to retain and have higher margins/LTV, but only if
the company can identify the acquisition activity that produces them. Low volume makes a
channel feel volatile; once the activity is identified, the answer may be 10x or 100x more
of that activity.

## Key claims

- Alex says operators often avoid the best customer because the fulfillment feels hard and
  unscalable.
- He asks which customer is easier to find, easier to retain, higher margin, and longer
  lifetime value, even if harder to fulfill.
- He says the best companies often come from doing the unscalable thing and then figuring
  out how to scale it.
- He prefers the white-glove enterprise customer if it has stronger retention, margin, and
  enterprise value.
- He still requires an acquisition activity; if the company cannot find the white-glove
  customer, then the lower offer and ads may be the practical path.
- He says low volume makes channels feel volatile.
- He gives an events example: if every event produces contacts and closes, the answer is to
  do dramatically more events.
- He says content/customer acquisition can look random when volume is low; higher volume
  reveals the ratio.
