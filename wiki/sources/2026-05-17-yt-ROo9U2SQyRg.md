# You Can Get to $10M Year With Any Business

## Summary

Alex argues that most entrepreneurs can reach $10M a year in their current vehicle and
are usually too impatient, not in the wrong business. He explains the cost of switching:
starting a new opportunity may look faster in year one, but the existing business has a
multi-year head start. He then compares business-model "shapes" and says many founders
mistake the inherent hard part of a model for proof that the model is broken.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- With limited exceptions, Alex says a $10M/year goal is achievable in many business
  vehicles.
- Most people's goals are achievable within their current vehicle, but they chase shiny
  objects because they believe another path will be faster.
- Sticking with a business preserves the head start already built.
- Growth can become easier at larger size than at smaller size.
- Founders should distinguish bugs from features: every business model has an inherent
  hard part that creates compensation.
- Info/coaching can make money quickly but becomes hard to scale because revenue
  retention is weak.
- SaaS starts slowly and can burn time and money before scaling.
- E-commerce can scale but carries inventory, cash-flow, supply-chain, and 3PL problems.
- Service businesses are slower and steadier but are people-heavy.
- Inexperienced founders often interpret the normal constraint of their industry as
  something wrong with their specific business.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Sticking with it is how you keep the head start."

> "This is a feature, not a bug."
