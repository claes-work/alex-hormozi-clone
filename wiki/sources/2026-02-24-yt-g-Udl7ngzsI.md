## Summary

A short solo clip in which Alex Hormozi recounts a give-back book campaign that
did **$106 million in sales in 72 hours**. The mechanism: over two years (from
his "Blue Book" to "Green Book") he wrote 17 books, which he used as incentives
for business owners to *donate* 200 books each (a ~$6,000 value) to other
entrepreneurs who couldn't afford them. Each donating entrepreneur received a
code to distribute to their own audience/email list; redemptions were fulfilled
centrally and shipped out. He notes that hitting **$100 million** was the target
he wanted the campaign to culminate in — and it worked — but says the part he
found "cooler" was that millions of people now get physical books, because he
finds something "very visceral about holding a book." He stresses the books (and
the accompanying course) are already free on his podcast and site with no opt-in
required. He frames the campaign as possible only because he has been
deliberate about building "a deeper audience of people who have money" who
"share my values," so when he wanted to give back, that audience mobilized.
