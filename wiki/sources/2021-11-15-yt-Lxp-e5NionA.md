# How I recruit the best employees in the world (2021-11-15)

## Summary

In this early (2021) recruiting video, Alex Hormozi presents his central hiring
framework: the five customer-acquisition "core functions" of a business (credited
to Alex Tarfin as an *external* framework) have an exact *internal* mirror for
acquiring employees. Lead generation → application generation, lead nurture →
application nurture, sales → interviewing, fulfillment → training, and retention &
ascension of customers → retention & ascension of talent. He argues the same six
traffic channels that get customers also get employees, that talent is the only way
to scale, and that building an aspirational employer brand ("make it Harvard") is
what lets you attract and keep the best people. He cites acquisition.com doing
"about $85 million a year in revenue" (self-reported).

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- The five *external* core functions of any business (per Alex Tarfin) are: lead
  generation, lead nurture, sales, fulfillment, and retention & ascension — a
  sequential pipeline. [2021]
- You should never fully outsource those five functions; a founder should know how
  to generate leads, work them, sell, fulfill, and retain/ascend customers. [2021]
- There is a parallel *internal* five-function pipeline for talent, and recognizing
  it is what let his company scale by reusing the same acquisition methodology on
  employees. [2021]
- Function 1 — application generation: you get employees the same way you get
  customers; an ad, cold outbound, etc. can recruit talent just as it recruits
  prospects. [2021]
- The same six traffic channels apply to both customers and applications: paid ads,
  earned media, owned media, manual outbound, affiliates, and word of mouth. [2021]
- "You don't need a what, you need a who" — instead of figuring out a skill
  yourself, hire someone who already has that skill and experience. [2021]
- Function 2 — application nurture: high-quality candidates find a job within ~8
  days (he says "10 out of 10 … within 8 days"), so slow follow-up loses them;
  applications deserve as structured a nurture process as sales leads. [2021]
- Function 3 — interviewing *is* selling: you are still selling, just selling the
  candidate on why they should work for you rather than a competitor. [2021]
- There is a heightened "war for talent," driven by the shift to remote work: small
  companies now compete with Netflix, Apple and Google for the same people, so
  work-from-home and flexible hours are now "table stakes," not an advantage. [2021]
- Function 4 — training: mirrors customer fulfillment; you deliver on the job you
  sold them — skills, communication cadences, reporting lines, job description,
  KPIs, compensation structure, and how to earn raises. [2021]
- Function 5 — retention & ascension of talent: keep motivating people to perform,
  identify leaders early, and give a clear path of titles and milestone-based pay
  increases; without a visible future they are "a ticking time bomb" for leaving. [2021]
- The biggest companies are biggest because they employ the smartest people and
  have strong talent-acquisition, retention and ascension systems. [2021]
- Build an aspirational employer brand — make your company a "stamp of approval on
  someone's resume," like Harvard — so people want to join, will take a pay cut to
  gain the skills you teach, and are harder to poach. [2021]
- His sales team is heavily recruited by others but is retained because they are
  paid well and have a clear, high-earning career path (self-reported). [2021]
- If you don't know the metrics of your talent-acquisition system, it is no wonder
  you don't have the talent you want. [2021]
