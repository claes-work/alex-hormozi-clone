# What Offends You Controls You

## Summary
Short solo clip on blame, offense, and self-ownership. Alex's thesis: **"What offends you controls you"** — whatever you point your finger of blame toward is where you hand your power. Tying it to his earlier point about excuses ("I can't do it because X"), he argues the "X" is the thing you've given power over your life. He illustrates with a personal example: he once believed he could never sustain a long-term committed relationship because his mother had hurt him as a child ("I didn't get enough hugs"). By repeating "it's her fault I'm broken / can't trust / can't love," he was effectively saying his mother controlled his love life — so he had to consciously drop the excuse ("maybe these things happened, maybe they didn't — also, who cares? *I* care, I'm the one affected"). He extends this to a subtler parent dynamic: if you were "parented tough," you may unconsciously believe that becoming successful would *validate* the way you were parented — so you stay unsuccessful to punish them, hurting yourself to get back at them (you do hurt them, "but you hurt you more"). You stay stuck "as long as you want them to control you"; the day you choose to control yourself is the day you "divorce your results from the very viable reasons you have to not win." Ends with the acquisition.com 10-stage roadmap promo.

- As of February 2026, you stay stuck "as long as you want them to control you"; choosing to control yourself means choosing to "divorce your results from the very viable reasons you have to not win" — i.e. the reasons may be valid, and you must let them go anyway.
