# How to Sell Your Education Business for 8 Figures

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a Brazilian accounting college doing about $12M USD. The owner wants to sell the company and is considering building AI/software for accountants. Alex says software could make sense for exit value, but warns that software does not automatically create a software multiple. The valuable traits are growth, gross margins, revenue retention, and possibly net negative churn. His preferred lower-risk path is to partner with the best existing accounting software in Brazil and bundle sticky software revenue with continuing education instead of building a new software company.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If the goal is to sell an education company, recurring continuing education can improve sellability without building software.
- Professional-service education can be packaged as annual continuing education, forums, webinars, and expert access.
- If building software, the owner likely needs a strong technical co-founder and meaningful equity reserved for key engineers.
- Software does not inherently make a business valuable or give it a software multiple.
- Software businesses are valuable when they have growth, high gross margins, strong revenue retention, and sometimes net negative churn.
- Adding software to a business does not make it a software company unless the software itself becomes exceptional.
- Building software is hard, and Alex says he has not seen many people do it well from this position.
- A better path may be partnering with the best existing accounting software, negotiating a profitable deal, and attaching that sticky revenue stream to the education base.
