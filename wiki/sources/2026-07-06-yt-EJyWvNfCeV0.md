# The TikTok Shop Playbook That Prints Creative at Scale

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Kyle Armor, whose supplement brand sells mainly through Amazon and wants to reduce single-channel risk while scaling creative. Alex recommends a TikTok Shop creator play: outbound to many creators, front them cash and product, organize them in a Skool community, generate hundreds of posts per day, identify the winners, put the best creators on retainers, and whitelist winning creative on Meta. He warns that the main risk is brand dilution, so the creator army needs brand guidelines, approved hooks, demographic fit, and manual review before paid amplification.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Kyle Armor** - the coachee. His supplement business, Amazon dependence, revenue, and product category are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A supplement brand with Amazon concentration should build a TikTok Shop creator engine to diversify acquisition.
- The play is aggressive creator outbound: give creators cash and product, put them in a community, and get enough of them posting that the brand has a large daily creative stream.
- If 200 creators post two or three videos a day, the brand can generate roughly 600 videos per day; most will be poor, but a small number will be strong enough to scale.
- The top 20-30 creators can then be put on retainers and whitelisted for Meta ads.
- TikTok Shop scaling creates brand-dilution risk, especially for a still-small brand.
- The brand should be stricter up front than the founder thinks necessary: provide brand guidelines, high-performing hooks, rules against negative hooks, demographic matching, and manual review of any creative that crosses a spend threshold.
