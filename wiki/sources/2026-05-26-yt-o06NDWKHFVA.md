# The Real Reason You Haven't Succeeded Yet

## Summary

Alex Hormozi attacks the "must be easy" frame as a sign of victim thinking: the person wants an outcome but protects themselves from the pain of inadequacy by attributing success to external ease. He argues that uncertainty is what makes pursuit meaningful; if success were guaranteed, it would lose the point. The durable output is the person becoming someone through the work, not just the external result.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- "Must be easy" reveals a desire for the outcome plus a refusal to own the gap between desire and action.
- Attributing success to outside ease removes agency.
- Alex would rather believe he has power and have nothing yet than believe life is externally controlled.
- Life can feel like a long training montage without glory, certainty, or romance.
- Certainty would make the pursuit less worthwhile because mystery and risk are part of the value.
- The fallacy of pursuit is worrying it will not amount to anything while ignoring that the person is becoming something in real time.
- People should stop complaining about results they did not get from work they did not put in.
- Becoming more successful than most people requires doing what most people will not.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Stop complaining about the results you didn't get from the work you didn't put in."
