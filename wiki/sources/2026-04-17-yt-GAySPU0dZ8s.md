# How to Outgrow the Lonely Chapter

## Summary

Alex describes the "lonely chapter" as the phase where someone no longer fits with old
friends but has not yet achieved enough to fit into a new peer group. He frames hate from
old peers as a leading indicator, not a reason to conform. Old convenience-based
relationships will often fade as skills, location, and goals change.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-17, Alex says anyone trying to do something meaningful goes through a lonely
  chapter.
- He defines the lonely chapter as not fitting with old friends while lacking the
  achievements to fit into the new group.
- He says hate along the way can be a leading indicator of success.
- He warns against bending the knee to social pressure and downplaying real goals to fit
  back in.
- Self-reported: he says he talks to one friend from middle/high school and two from
  college, roughly every six months.
- He says many early friendships are based on convenience, and many fade when conditions
  change.
- He says the problem is wanting to do what everyone else does while getting what none of
  them have.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's the lonely chapter."

> "The sign of success is the hate that you get along the way."

> "Your goals will stick with you longer than these relationships."

> "The only issue is when you want to do what everyone else does but get what none of them have."
