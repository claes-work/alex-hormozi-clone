# My Criteria for Buying a Company

## Summary

Alex discusses Acquisition.com's deal flow and current acquisition posture. He says deals
come through the deal-flow pipeline, roughly 3,000 per month, with about two LOIs per
month. The minimum is about $1M EBITDA, but he prefers companies around $3M-$4M EBITDA
because Acquisition.com can help them jump much larger. He says the firm has moved firmly
toward bigger ownership chunks, targets 49% in many deals, recently did a majority deal,
and now does both minority and majority. Majority ownership speeds implementation because
there is less founder emotion to manage.

## Key claims

- Alex self-reports that Acquisition.com receives about 3,000 deals per month and writes
  roughly two LOIs per month.
- He says the minimum is about $1M EBITDA, while his preferred zone is around $3M-$4M
  EBITDA.
- He says he is comfortable holding forever and compounding tax-free rather than needing a
  liquidity event.
- He frames holding-company investing as having "deal fit," analogous to product-market
  fit, but with much slower feedback cycles.
- He says Acquisition.com has moved toward bigger ownership chunks: targeting 49% in many
  deals, bigger minority slugs, and now also majority deals.
- He says the same work can be required at 20% as at 51%, and majority can sometimes be
  less work because implementation is faster.
- He self-reports a recent majority deal and a price-change case where six calls were
  needed under minority influence; the change then doubled profit in 30 days.
- He says the firm has never taken a vote in a portfolio company and prides itself on not
  needing to pull contracts back out after a deal.
