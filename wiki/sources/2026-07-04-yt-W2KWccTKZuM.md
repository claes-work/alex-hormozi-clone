# You're Stuck Because You Built the Bottom Layer, Not the Top

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Wouter, whose skincare business does about $4.5M per month and wants to reach $25M per month. The business solved a sales-team manpower constraint but now lacks senior leadership. Alex diagnoses the issue as a missing top layer: the founder has built the bottom operating layer but is still pulled into daily fires. He recommends selective headhunting, CEO-led outreach for top roles, hiring one constraint role at a time, and using outside expertise to vet technical functions the founder cannot personally evaluate.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Wouter** - the coachee. His skincare business, revenue, phone-sales backend, sales-team constraint, and hiring plan are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A business at this scale can have the bottom layer built while still missing the top layer: senior leaders who remove the founder from fires.
- For one-time senior hires, an internal recruiting team may be less useful than targeted headhunters or founder-led outbound.
- The founder's existing network is likely strongest for sales and marketing leaders, but weaker for finance, tech/data, and people/HR leadership.
- Finance becomes increasingly important as the business scales; tech/data can also become a major constraint.
- When Alex and Leila face a major hiring constraint, they may pull themselves out of normal work for a six-week interview sprint, especially for C-suite hires.
- CEO outreach has higher response rates; Alex suggests a low-pressure referral-style message that asks whether the candidate knows someone like themselves.
- The founder does not need to replace all six weak roles at once; recruit the most on-fire role first, then attack the next worst constraint.
- A major speed limiter in business is the ability to recognize talent patterns across roles and levels.
- For roles the founder cannot technically judge, use external experts or high-quality search firms to perform technical vetting, then separately judge culture fit.
- Search fees should be framed as investments: if a $40K fee helps produce $400K, the risk can be rational.
