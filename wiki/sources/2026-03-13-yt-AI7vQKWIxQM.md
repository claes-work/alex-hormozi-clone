# Don't Open Multiple Locations Until You Do This

## Summary
Live coaching clip. Lauren and her husband run an in-home therapy company doing ~$40k/month total, already spread across five or six different cities with staff in each location, and ask whether to focus on one city or keep scaling across all of them. Alex's answer is unambiguous: focus first on raising **revenue per location and profit per location**, because $40k spread over 5–6 cities (~$6–7k each) is "pretty thin." His governing principle is **"you've got to nail it before you scale it"** — nail the model before you scale the model. Concretely, "nailing the model" means having the entire customer journey mapped and optimized in each location: knowing the first product you sell, the second, and the third, and having maximized revenue and profit per customer *before* adding locations. His reasoning is that retrofitting is brutal — once you've hired and trained people on the old (unoptimized) model, adding those products and steps later is far harder than getting it right in location one first. He frames added scale as added complexity via the **rowboat vs. Titanic** metaphor: one location is a rowboat (easy to steer, pivots fast), but the more locations you have, the more you become the Titanic (slow, hard to steer — you can see the iceberg, turn, and still hit it). When making these decisions you want to be a rowboat, so keep the model as lean as possible; he believes they can extract a lot more revenue and profit per location before expanding further.
