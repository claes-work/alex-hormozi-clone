# I Helped 6 Business Owners Make More Money

## Summary

A live Q&A / diagnostics session where Hormozi answers six business owners one at a time on leads, focus, personal environment, gym economics, partnerships, media businesses, relationships and skill-stacking. Practical, tactical answers grounded in his own playbook, with several candid personal segments.

## Key claims (per diagnosis)

- **Zev (lead gen + cutting people out):** Three ways to get leads — manually assemble, buy from brokers, or scrape with software; he usually starts by scraping (cheapest), tests the top 4-5 tools on 100-500 leads each, doubles down on highest response/throughput. On free value: "give them something free that they would normally pay for." On environment: the #1 way to change behavior is to change your environment; moving even 30 minutes away resets who you frequent; expect to lose friends; treat family the same as everyone (fair/abundant exchange, reset boundaries, no infinite moral IOU). (paraphrase)
- **Selena (free-to-paid timing):** "Give until they ask." A purchase is a prediction of future value; best way to raise conviction is to deliver value in the present. Talking to 100 people: one buys a direct ask, but ~20 accept "can I solve a problem for free?" → convert half → 10 sales (10x). Value comes from *hours of work* (200 hrs on a presentation; 6 hrs/day for 2 years on the next book). "Be queen of a puddle" then pond, lake, bay, ocean. (paraphrase)
- **Joe (semi-private gym launch):** Loves the semi-private model — $300-400/mo, 2-3x/week, higher-income avatars, 1,200-1,400 sq ft, single manager (~$40-50k base + 10-20% profit share) + two helpers, ~120 members at ~50% margin, ~$500-600k/yr. References Dunbar's number: micro-gyms cap ~120-150 because manufacturing human connection gets complex past that. Do a 3-month pre-launch, over-spend (~$30k) to open at capacity/in the black. On partnerships: a good partnership has a "yes man and a no man" (he's yes, Leila's no); ask the cautious partner what checklist they'd need to greenlight the next location. (paraphrase)
- **Nicole (creator → newsletter):** Subscribers/followers are vanity metrics — on TikTok-style algorithms content quality wins ("click, retain, reward"); good for new creators since a great video beats a big-but-stale account. Newsletter subscribers behave like customers; hyper-niche, high-value, higher-price newsletters have best LTV. Grow top-of-funnel content → newsletter → sell ads/own products = a media business. (paraphrase)
- **Andy (skill-stacking order):** Order skills by how you actually make money — (1) a base skill/product to sell, (2) promotion/advertising, (3) conversion/sales, (4) leadership & operations. "$1k→$1M is skills, $1M→$100M is character, $100M→$1B is time." Speed of scaling correlates with speed of talent recruitment; it's a two-sided funnel (attract customers *and* attract talent). (paraphrase)
- **Enterprise/B2B owner:** Two profitable moves he's seen — hire people out of the Fortune 100 departments you're targeting (they bring the account/relationships), then grow via cross-selling. Legion doesn't really change: involve as many stakeholders as fast as possible, orient the pitch to making them look good with no career risk. Loves "BAMF — book a meeting from a meeting"; never let a deal stall. Reframe: enterprise sales isn't 12 months, it's ~48 one-hour meetings you can compress. Gated/value content (McKinsey white papers, Goldman analysis) still works. (paraphrase)
