# How to Get 14 Times More Done Than Everyone Else

## Summary

Alex defines work as output and argues that weekly task cadences conceal how quickly
many jobs can be completed. Asking for real hour estimates and resetting deadlines can
compress two weekly cycles into one day. Volume must include an improvement loop, and
beginners should invest surplus cash in education and skills before seeking meaningful
passive income.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex defines work by output rather than effort or elapsed time.
- A nominal weekly deliverable that requires three or four hours can often be completed
  by noon; repeating the process can compress 14 calendar days into one working day.
- High volume only builds skill when each repetition reviews what worked and changes the
  next attempt.
- Repetition should make a skill automatic so attention can move to the next skill.
- Beginners lack enough capital for passive income to matter and should first learn to
  earn while awake.
- Alex recommends an education-investment budget and says early paid rooms were valuable
  partly for relationships he could serve with his sales skill and later trade with.
- He self-reports his father lost financial assets but retained the portable skill of
  surgery; this anecdote is not independently verified here.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I define work as output."

> "It's raw effort with an improvement loop."

> "Learn how to make money while you're awake before trying to learn how to make it while you sleep."

## Notes

Reinforces iteration compression, automaticity, and learn-before-earn investing.
