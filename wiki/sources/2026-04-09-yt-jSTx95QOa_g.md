# Wait 36 Months

## Summary

Alex gives a defensive plan for people with less than $100,000 in savings and no strong
earning skill: cut lifestyle costs, reject paid social obligations, seek higher-paid work,
learn valuable skills, and double the highest worker's input. The final instruction is to
wait 36 months—impatient with inputs, patient with delayed results.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex distinguishes advice for a broke beginner from advice for a
  skilled high earner who can profitably buy back time.
- Beginners should minimize food, clothing, and social spending to accumulate capital
  for education, inventory, software, or other opportunities.
- Cash reserves extend the time horizon by reducing survival pressure.
- Alex says a person earning $40,000 monthly while spending $4,000 gains ten months of
  runway each month and can take larger calculated bets.
- Friends who pressure someone away from a stated goal do not function as friends in his
  framework.
- He recommends applying for better jobs, adding side work, learning higher-hourly-rate
  skills, and doubling the strongest worker's measurable input.
- After changing direction, he says to wait 36 months for the new trajectory to bear fruit.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Having a big stockpile allows you to be patient."

> "Wait 36 months."

> "Patient with your outputs, impatient with your inputs."

## Notes

Reinforces defensive saving, skill acquisition, input intensity, and long time horizons.
