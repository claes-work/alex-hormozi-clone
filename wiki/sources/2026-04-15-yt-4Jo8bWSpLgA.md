# Why Your Team Isn't Doing What You Ask

## Summary

Alex helps a men's-salon owner get staff to capture consistent before-and-after photos.
He says rewards work better with low latency, while a distant $100 monthly bonus is too
weak and delayed. He first recommends embedding photography into customer checkout and
removing social friction; when the owner explains that employees chose bonuses instead
of a front-desk hire but still do not perform, he recommends a 30-day team-wide
accountability deadline before redirecting that money to a dedicated employee.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says the effectiveness of a reward depends heavily on how soon
  it follows the desired behavior.
- He recommends an immediate small payment after each completed photo rather than a
  distant, bundled monthly reward.
- He suggests gradually making reinforcement intermittent after the behavior becomes
  established.
- Before demanding more effort, he asks what social cost or workflow friction makes the
  behavior difficult.
- He prefers making before-and-after photography a required checkout step so employees
  cannot complete the customer process without it.
- If staff accepted bonus money in exchange for the duty but do not perform it, he
  recommends a 30-day deadline and then using that money to hire someone who will.
- For team accountability, he recommends making the bonus contingent on every member
  completing the process rather than rewarding isolated compliance.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's all about latency."

> "How do I make this easier for them rather than demanding they do it harder?"

> "If one of you doesn't do it, none of you get it."
