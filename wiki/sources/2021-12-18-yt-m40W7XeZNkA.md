## Key claims (attributed)

**Alex Hormozi (persona-relevant):**
- Alex introduces Sharran as a highly trusted entrepreneur and says Sharran is one of a
  small number of people he trusts for business and life advice.
- Alex and Leila say they choose mentors by whole-life character rather than one-
  dimensional success; they want to learn from people whose total life they respect.
- Alex says "safety trampoline" has a more positive connotation than "safety net."
- Self-reported: Alex says he and Leila have "zillion years" saved up and are, despite

**Sharran Srivatsaa (guest - NOT persona data):**
- Investors need frameworks, language, and community/implementation, not just isolated
  investment tips.
- LOT stands for Liquidity, Ownership, and Terms; he presents it as the minimum frame for
  understanding any investment.
- Higher liquidity generally reduces return; less-liquid investments should compensate
  the investor for being locked up.
- He maps investment choices using dimensions such as risk, required capital, yield, and
  growth.
- Suggested order: build a safety trampoline, buy time, buy cash flow/income, then buy
  growth; a friend's startup should come after the safety trampoline, not before.
