# Champions Interpret Anxiety as Excitement

## Summary

Alex discusses reframing by comparing the same bodily sensations in a workout versus in a
car, then applies the idea to performance anxiety. Champions can interpret anxiety as
excitement because the sensation is similar and the frame changes the experience. He also
says that if anxiety is still high, the practical answer is more practice until the work
is so familiar it becomes boring.

## Key claims

- Alex says framing changes the experienced meaning of identical physical sensations.
- A therapist friend asks patients, "What would it take for this to be amazing?" to
  reframe painful events.
- Champions interpret anxiety as excitement before performance.
- If someone is still very anxious before a presentation, meeting, launch, or release,
  Alex sees that as evidence they likely need more practice.
- The goal is to repeat the stimulus until the person is almost bored by it and has no
  adrenal response.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "What would it take for this to be amazing?"

> "Champions just interpret anxiety as excitement."

> "When you're sick of it is the point where you'll have no adrenal response."
