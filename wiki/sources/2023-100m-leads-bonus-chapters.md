# $100M Leads — 2 Bonus Chapters

**What it is.** A ~24-page bonus PDF given to readers who promoted the *$100M Leads* (2023) book launch.
Framed as "two" bonus chapters "(there's an extra one in here because you guys came through so hard)" and
described as early drafts from his next book (`PDF p. 1`). All content sits under **"Section A: Attract"**
and covers promotional "wrappers" around a Grand Slam Offer: an intro on Free & Discount promotions, then
three sub-chapters — **Premium Promotions**, **Free Promotions**, and **Discount Promotions** (`PDF p. 2, 6,
12, 18`). Raw path: `raw/books/raw/books/2023-100m-leads-2-bonus-chapters-alex-hormozi.md`.

## Key claims
- Origin story (March 2016): a conference talk on his pre-sold Free 6-Week Challenge went viral in the
  room; he later turned 100+ collected contacts into the first Gym Launch offer by "giving something away
  for free: me" — flying to 33 gyms, working their leads for free, keeping only what he sold. This made his
  first million outside his gyms (`PDF p. 2-4`).
- Governing sequence, stated repeatedly: **"Get Flow. Monetize Flow. Then Add Friction."** — generate
  demand first, then monetize, then add friction, in that order (`PDF p. 4, 24`).
- Free and discount offers are "wrappers" around the core premium (Grand Slam) offer; the point of a
  promotion is to enhance the offer, not change it — "wrapping paper," e.g. newspaper vs. a Chanel bag
  (`PDF p. 4-5`).
- **Premium promotions:** present the Grand Slam Offer on its own at a high ticket. Worked example — a
  $10,000 offer closed at 5% on 190 leads yields ~$90,250 vs. $9,500 from 190 low-ticket sales, making
  advertising ~9.5x more profitable with far less volume/operational drag (`PDF p. 6-7`). Requires a very
  valuable offer and a selling process that demonstrates value (`PDF p. 7`).
- Premium-offer pros/cons are taught via a role-reversal device where "You" (the reader) teach Alex: pros =
  simplest math, only quality customers, high lead quality; cons = highest cost per lead, must be a strong
  copywriter who knows the avatar, must be good at sales, least efficient way to capture a market, must have
  an extremely compelling offer (`PDF p. 8-11`).
- **Free promotions:** "the most powerful offer of all time." Cites Dan Ariely's "penny gap" — 9x more
  people take a free Hershey kiss than one priced at $0.01 (`PDF p. 12`). Big companies (Facebook, YouTube,
  Dropbox, Uber, Netflix) run free front ends (`PDF p. 13`).
- Five friction levers to raise lead quality on free offers: (1) more qualifications, (2) more information/
  heavier questions, (3) more steps, (4) forced consumption (e.g. a 40-min video before any CTA — his
  favorite), (5) longer ads/copy (`PDF p. 14-15`).
- "Free brings broke people" is a myth: across four split tests (10 markets each), free vs. non-free had
  the same close rate and ticket size, but free typically cut lead cost by 5x or more (`PDF p. 16`). "If I
  only had one offer... it would be a free offer" (`PDF p. 17`).
- **Discount promotions:** only "massive" discounts (50%+) drive action; marginal 5–25% just cuts margin
  (`PDF p. 18`). Best used to "splinter" a small component of the offer, not the whole thing (`PDF p. 23`).
  Biggest benefit is the **two-step sale** — collect a card on a cheap front-end (e.g. $19 promo) to upsell
  seamlessly later (e.g. $2,100 program), and near-eliminate no-shows since paid prospects show up 85–90%+
  (`PDF p. 20-21`).
