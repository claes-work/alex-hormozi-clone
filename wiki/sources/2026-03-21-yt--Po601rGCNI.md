# Why Is My Business So Hard to Run?

## Summary
In a live consultation, Alex Hormozi advises a used-car dealership owner (a former pipe fitter/welder, 8 years in the business, ~100 cars on the lot plus a service shop and a commercial-van line) who lists many simultaneous problems: branding, leads, sales scripting, product mix, and staffing. Hormozi reframes the tangle as a single people problem — the owner needs to hire skilled operators rather than juggle everything himself. He prescribes hiring a finance-disciplined person first (a controller/CFO) who can find their own salary in cost savings, then an operator to run divisions, freeing the owner to pursue the higher-margin van opportunity.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the owner's list of six problems (branding, leads, product, sales process, etc.) collapses into one root issue: he needs people to help, not more tactics.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends hiring a finance-disciplined controller/CFO first, because that role can likely "carve out" its own salary through expense discipline and then fund the next hire.
- Alex's hiring-negotiation tactic: tell a candidate you can't afford them, offer a set pay, and task them to "find your salary and cost in the next 90 days" — a disciplined finance hire will find it.
- Alex estimates the owner could roughly double his margin and find ~$250,000/year of savings through basic, regular finance discipline (caller-context figures: ~$500–600k profit, $2k margin per typical car).
- As of March 2026, Alex frames scaling as "buying back time" via progressively more expensive trades — each higher tier of talent pay (250k, 500k, 1M/year) is a "new territory" one grows through, analogous to escalating daily ad spend.
- Caller claims (unverified): 8 years in business, grew lot from 15 to 30 to 45 to 100 cars, ~$2,000 margin on a typical car vs. $6,000–$12,000 on commercial vans, already holds ~40 vans, and runs a full-time service shop.
- Alex's "perfect hire" profile: someone who scaled a dealership from ~$5–10M to $25–30M in receivables with a strong financial background; absent that, an operations person, or a finance-disciplined operator from a slightly larger dealership.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "To me, hearing all that, when I hear like six problems, it's people. You need help."

> "You don't have problems, you just need people to help you learn."

> "To a good controller or a good CFO, you just say like, 'Listen, I can't afford you. I'm willing to pay you this. You need to find your salary and cost in the next 90 days.' And they'll find it."

> "I think you're at the point right now where you need to make a bet on trading — you need to buy some time for you back. And it's just that the time that you now need to buy is more expensive time."

> "It's easy to make the early trades cuz it's like lower value skill. But as you get better, you have to... it's kind of like breaking into new territory for ads."

> "I've never paid someone 250 grand a year. I've never paid someone 500 grand a year. I've never paid someone a million dollars a year. Like they just become levels that you just get through."

> "You still have to keep the wheels on, pun intended, the core cash cow business."
