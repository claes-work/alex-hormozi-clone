# 3 Ways to Do What You Love (and get wealthy too)

## Summary

Hormozi lays out three career paths every entrepreneur eventually chooses between: the
**Artist** (loves doing the craft itself), the **Entrepreneur** (business itself is the art
form), and the **Hybrid** (owns the one function they love and builds the business around
it). He argues there is no "right" path — only the one that fits your current season, and
you can switch later. He closes on a product-first hierarchy (product > marketing > sales),
using his book *$100M Offers* selling 25,000 copies/month with no ads as proof that an
exceptional product compounds through word of mouth.

## Key claims

- **Three paths, no right answer.** Artist (do the craft, scale price not units:
  commodity → premium → luxury), Entrepreneur (the business is the painting; build,
  install an operator, generate cash flow), Hybrid (own the component you love —
  product, marketing, or finance — and have others build around you). You can change
  paths as you evolve.
- **He personally went Artist → Entrepreneur:** started obsessed with fitness, but "as
  soon as I actually started a gym I realized I enjoyed business even more than I enjoyed
  Fitness."
- **Advisor-quality hierarchy** (paraphrase): best advice comes from someone who has done
  it themselves AND taken others where you're going multiple times; worst is family/
  neighbors who never ran a business "and have no right to have an opinion."
- **Skill-value order is product → marketing → sales.** Sales is easiest to learn (weeks),
  marketing longer, but an exceptional product gets customers to promote for you, which is
  what actually scales an enterprise past the plateau where marginal acquisition cost
  climbs.
- Marketing can scale anything only to a point; without word-of-mouth referrals a company
  plateaus and becomes untenable.
