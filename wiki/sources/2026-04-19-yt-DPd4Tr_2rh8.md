# How to Become the Person You Want to Be (Step by Step)

## Summary

Alex says traits are buckets of smaller skills, so becoming a desired future self
requires breaking broad words like "charismatic" into observable actions. He connects
that skill breakdown to business math: translate desired income into units sold, inputs,
daily activities, and eventually repeatable roles.

## Key claims

- Alex says the gap between current self and desired self is a gap in traits and skills.
- He defines traits as shorthand for buckets of smaller skills.
- He says the more skilled someone is, the more general a command they can execute.
- He breaks charisma into observable behaviors such as greeting people, using names,
  eye contact, nodding, and asking questions.
- He says people should think of themselves as toddlers when learning, because it
  removes ego and makes skills chunkable.
- He connects daily activities to business targets by translating desired income into
  units, customers, inputs, and calendar blocks.
