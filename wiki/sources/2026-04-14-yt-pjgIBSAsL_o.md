# Helping a Manufacturing Company Scale Outbound

## Summary

Alex coaches a manufacturer selling patented machinery to granite-countertop
fabricators. Because the product visibly reduces labor and steps, he recommends
demonstration-heavy content, case studies, and before-and-after proof. For outbound, he
suggests opening with a named competitor so the prospect's curiosity earns a callback,
then explaining the competitor's operational advantage.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says a visibly superior industrial process should use "show,
  don't tell" marketing.
- He says modern distribution can make a low-view post worthwhile when the total market
  is only about 15,000 buyers and a high share of viewers are qualified.
- For buyers who are not active online, he favors door-to-door outbound and product
  demonstrations over relying on a website.
- He recommends before-and-after evidence and case studies from existing equipment
  installations.
- His preferred outbound opener says the call concerns a recognizable competitor; once
  the prospect responds, the seller explains what advantage that competitor is using.
- Market size, retention, and process-improvement figures are reported by the owner and
  are not independently verified in the excerpt.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I think it's all show, don't tell."

> "I wouldn't worry about views and things like that."

> "They're smashing you because they're using this thing that you're not using."
