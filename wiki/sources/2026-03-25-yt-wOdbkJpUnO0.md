# Work 12 Hours a Day

## Summary
Alex Hormozi promotes an exercise he calls the "12 by 30": working 12 hours a day for 30 days straight with no weekends, arguing it is more sustainable than people expect and teaches you that you have another gear. He frames the payoff as acquiring the trait of being relentless, which produces "supreme confidence" and removes anxiety in future high-pressure situations. He shares that he once did an even harder 15–16-hour version for nine straight months during his gym-owner days.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends a "12 by 30" — 12 hours a day for 30 days straight, no weekends — as a self-directed intensity exercise, claiming it is less unsustainable than people assume.
- As of March 2026, Alex lists the lessons of the exercise: you aren't made of glass, you could work more, you could achieve goals faster, you could improve daily, you don't need to work like this forever but have the gear if needed, and others are outworking you.
- As of March 2026, Alex says he personally works roughly "six to six" daily (a "12 by 100" mindset), with many 15–16-hour days when events, dinners, or being on camera are involved.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues being "on" — on stage, on camera, meeting customers — is far more exhausting than working alone, because you cannot take a break and every non-peak second is a loss.
- As of March 2026, Alex recounts a nine-month stretch running his gym on ~15–16-hour days (up at 4:00, gym open 4:30, classes from 5:00, then billing and lead follow-up until 10:00–11:00), which he calls "the kind of tired a good night's sleep couldn't fix."
- As of March 2026, Alex claims the value of extreme effort is learning you have that gear, which gives veterans confidence and removes stress because they have "seen this before."

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "You work 12 hours a day for 30 days straight, no weekends. By the last day you realize one, you aren't made of glass."

> "There are people who are working more than you, who are beating you. And you get all of those lessons for the low price of getting more done in a month than you probably did last quarter."

> "Working alone for a full day is not as exhausting as being on stage for a full day, being on camera for a full day, because every second that you are not peak, you are losing."

> "Every minute that is not recorded is basically a minute wasted. There's a whole team here behind this camera that is making this stuff for you, and I'm costing them every second I'm not showing up."

> "I used to describe it as the kind of tired a good night's sleep couldn't fix."

> "You have acquired the trait of being relentless. That you do not relent, you do not let up, you do not stop until you get what you want."

> "The difference between the newbie and the veteran is that the veteran looks back on what they have done to get to that point and says, 'I've seen this before. This is another one of those.'"
