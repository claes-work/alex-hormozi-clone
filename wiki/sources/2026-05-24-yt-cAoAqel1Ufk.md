# Why Winning Isn't for Everyone

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says capacity to work is trainable and sometimes work seasons become lopsided.
He does not deny sleep and health matter, but frames launches and extreme growth pushes as
"wartime" seasons where trade-offs are unavoidable. He encourages thinking about work-life
balance over longer horizons, such as a work-heavy decade followed by different seasons
later.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says work capacity is trainable.
- Sleep and health matter, but there are wartime periods where the work season becomes
  lopsided.
- Winning requires trade-offs; there are no solutions, only trades.
- People trade long-term for short-term or short-term for long-term through everyday
  choices such as Netflix, work, drugs, productivity, or sleep.
- Long-term health and longevity in the game matter, but ultra-successful people often have
  seasons of imbalance.
- Work-life balance should be evaluated over longer time horizons, not only day by day.
- If someone is not where they want to be and lacks money, work is the primary way forward.
- Violent, unreasonable, ruthless effort over extended periods is required before optionality
  exists.
- People can use whatever fuel they have, whether running toward something or away from
  something.
