# How to Actually Learn Any Skill Faster

## Summary

Alex Hormozi explains skill learning by chunking broad labels into smaller behavioral
units. "Business" is not a single skill; it contains marketing, sales, product, hiring,
and many sub-skills. To learn faster, he says to keep breaking the desired capability down
until it becomes an understandable unit where behavior can change.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Skills can be chunked up into broad categories or chunked down into sub-skills.
- Broad labels like "basketball" or "business" contain many skills underneath.
- Some skills generalize across domains, such as hand-eye coordination, reading, writing,
  speaking, and learning itself.
- Specific skills generalize less; shooting a basketball may help beer pong more than chess.
- "Business" is a bucketed term; people must break it into marketing, sales, product,
  hiring, and then smaller behavioral units.
- A skill is an adaptive chain of behaviors.
- People stall because they keep looking for a recipe instead of breaking the target skill
  into smaller parts they can practice.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Business is not something you can get good at. It is a bucketed term."
