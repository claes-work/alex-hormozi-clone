# How to Stay Motivated When You Don't Feel Like Taking Action

## Summary

Alex defines motivation behaviorally as the likelihood of taking a desired action. He
says action need not wait for a favorable feeling, and argues that deprivation creates
the gap between a desired outcome and present circumstances. Reference groups can widen
that gap by making higher achievement feel both real and attainable.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex compares motivated action to courage: act despite an
  unfavorable feeling rather than requiring the feeling to change first.
- He operationally defines motivation as the likelihood of taking a desired action.
- He says deprivation around an outcome increases the likelihood of action.
- Because money is psychological rather than physiological, a person's comparison group
  helps determine whether financial deprivation is felt.
- Meeting people who earn far more can expand perceived possibility and create a
  "rubber band" between current and desired states.
- Alex distinguishes the science of achievement from the art of fulfillment; comparison
  may increase action while reducing well-being.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Why do you require motivation in order to take action?"

> "The likelihood that someone takes a desired action."

> "We're talking about the science of achievement, not the art of fulfillment."
