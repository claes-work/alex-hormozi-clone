# Your Bank Won't Fund You Because You're Taking Too Much Cash Out

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises Andrew, whose Florida dental group has about 10 offices, $25M in revenue, and wants to reach $50M. Andrew names talent acquisition and access to capital as constraints, but Alex observes that de novo offices cost roughly $250K-$300K and the business has enough EBITDA to fund growth if owners reduce distributions. He frames growth as a trade of profitability for growth and security for upside, then applies the same logic to recruiting: repeated core roles deserve internal recruiting capability; specialized one-time roles may justify headhunters.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Andrew** - the coachee. His dental offices, revenue, EBITDA, capital access, podcast, and hiring needs are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If a business produces enough EBITDA to fund de novo openings, capital may not be the real constraint; owner distributions may be.
- Using someone else's money can feel less risky, but lenders can still take assets, so it is not automatically safer.
- The key metric for repeated de novo expansion is return on invested capital (ROIC).
- If a new office costs roughly $350K including facility and team recruiting, and can produce meaningful profit within 12-18 months, the reinvestment case can be strong.
- Taking profit out today trades risk for security, but also trades away upside and growth speed.
- Talent acquisition follows the same trade-off: spend more to go faster.
- If the business needs roughly one hire every two weeks, it is time for someone dedicated to hiring.
- Repeated core roles should be recruited, hired, onboarded, and trained internally because they are part of the economic engine.
- Specialized one-time roles can be outsourced to headhunters; for repeated core roles, Alex prefers hiring someone from a recruiting firm to do the work exclusively for the company.
