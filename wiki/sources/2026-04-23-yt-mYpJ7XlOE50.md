# The Reason I Wrote The Books

## Summary

Alex says books are a poor direct monetization vehicle because they are physical
products with long lead times, inventory, platform or publisher economics, and low
relative profit compared with his portfolio companies. He still says the books are what
he would be most proud of if he died tomorrow because they are likely to outlive him.

## Key claims

- Alex says writing a book to make money from the book itself is a poor monetization
  strategy.
- He says books have physical-product constraints: lead times, inventory, platform fees,
  publisher economics, and weak margin compared with his companies.
- He says his books do make money, but less than any of his portfolio companies.
- He says, self-reported on 2026-04-23, that if he died tomorrow he would be most proud
  of the books because they would likely outlive him.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Writing a book to make money... is possibly the worst monetization vehicle."

> "The thing I'd be most proud of is the books."
