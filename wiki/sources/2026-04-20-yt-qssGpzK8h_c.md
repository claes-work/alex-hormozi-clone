# The Real Cost of Excellence

## Summary

Alex says excellence costs the opportunity to try many other things because the work has
to be concentrated into one thing. He describes excellence as an asymptote or power law:
moving from good to great takes dramatically more work, but one truly excellent product
can keep paying for a long time. He uses `$100M Offers` as his clearest example.

## Key claims

- Alex says excellence requires undeniable proof that one is who one says one is.
- He says the real cost of excellence is all the things someone cannot do because time
  must be put into one thing.
- He says moving from a 7 to a 9 takes far more work than moving from a 5 to a 7.
- He says a 9.5-to-9.9 book can be the difference between a normal book and an asset that
  pays for the rest of one's life.
- He self-reports that `$100M Offers` does around $300K per month in sales revenue and
  roughly $1M per year net.
- He says the book was a rare case where he could control nearly every variable: pages,
  drawings, audio, words, and cover.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Excellence has a huge price tag."

> "It's an asymptote when it comes to work and excellence."

> "One amazing product."
