# The 10x Move for Healthcare Service Businesses

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a virtual-assistant company serving healthcare practices whose leads come mostly from speaking at events. Alex first identifies webinars as the 10x move for uncapping the owner's calendar, then, after hearing about existing sponsor relationships, says B2B partnerships may be the better first order of operations because they need less tech and can plug into existing trust. The long-term ideal is partner integration into the sales motion, where the VA offer helps the partner justify its own cost.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If speaking-driven growth is capped by the founder's calendar, virtual stages/webinars can scale the stage.
- For healthcare practice owners, ad creative should call out the specific avatar and show concrete use cases for virtual assistants.
- A webinar should answer core fears and FAQs: English quality, training, management burden, payment, employment status, and implementation.
- The CTA can be a free savings assessment using growth rate and payroll to show bottom-line impact.
- The pitch should be "better and costs less," not merely cheaper labor.
- Existing B2B partners may be the highest-likelihood first move when the relationships and goodwill already exist.
- The long-term partnership goal is integration into the partner's sales motion, not one-off co-webinars.
- The partner pitch is that the customer's payroll savings can make the partner's service feel free or easier to afford.
