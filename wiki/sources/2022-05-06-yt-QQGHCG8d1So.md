# Lessons and failures of 2021 (I emailed myself all of my failures for the last 5 years)

## Summary

Alex's annual "lessons and failures" video, drawn from a five-year private email chain
he kept to himself (now migrating to public Twitter documentation). A long, wide-ranging
solo monologue covering meaning/legacy, the psychology of money after wealth, talent and
hiring, time horizons, leverage, scarcity, and content strategy — reflecting on the year
(2021) he sold three companies and took a year mostly off, which he found empty and
depressing.

## Key claims

- Legacy is largely a myth: extend the time horizon far enough and everyone is
  forgotten; meaning comes from what you choose to deem meaningful, not a prescribed
  capital-M Meaning. He and Leila plan to give away their wealth at death because
  inheriting large wealth "ruins the person." (Paraphrase.)
- "The wealth is built through the owning and not the selling" — minimize transactions,
  keep compounding assets you own; set up games where "if I wait, I win." (Paraphrase.)
- People are made, not born, entrepreneurs; entrepreneurial-ness is a movable point on a
  continuum. Hiring lesson: better to find a ".400 hitter" than to teach someone to
  swing; great people build great strategy, not the reverse. (Paraphrase.)
- Entrepreneurship is "a battle of will, not intellect" — an infinite game where the
  point is to keep playing; the person with the longest time horizon wins. (Paraphrase.)
- Leverage = the four C's (his renaming of Naval's four): Collaboration (labor), Capital
  (other people's money), Code (software), Content (media); the more you stack, the more
  multiplicative your output. (Paraphrase, credits Naval Ravikant.)
- Content strategy: only make what is true to you (you did it or it's true for you);
  people sense intention, so the easiest way to be trusted is to be trustworthy; make it
  as art you believe should exist, assuming no one will see it. (Paraphrase.)
