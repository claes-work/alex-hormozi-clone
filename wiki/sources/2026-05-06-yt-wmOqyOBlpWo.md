# How to Introduce Yourself in Every Video

## Summary

Alex says long-form videos should assume viewers do not know who the creator is. Introduce
yourself within the first 30 seconds, make the introduction tight, and connect your
credibility to the exact topic being taught. Short-form videos do not always have room for
this, so the profile and description carry more of the credibility load. He also explains
that his YouTube description uses his origin story because that exact story had already
performed as his best LinkedIn post.

## Key claims

- In long form, introduce yourself in roughly the first 30 seconds.
- The intro should be memorized, tight, and relevant: sales credibility for sales videos,
  marketing credibility for marketing videos, audience/content credibility for content
  videos.
- The goal is not a two-minute life story but a brief credibility bridge tied to the
  topic.
- Short-form content can rely more on the profile/about page and description because the
  clip itself has less room.
- Self-reported: his long YouTube origin-story description is based on his best-performing
  LinkedIn post and helps strangers understand who he is.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Introduce myself and give credibility related to the topic at the same time."

> "Don't brag, just say what you've done."
