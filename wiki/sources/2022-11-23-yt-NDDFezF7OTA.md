# STOP trying to be happy.

## Summary

A personal, contrarian solo talk arguing that the "purpose and passion" gospel is a lie and that negative emotion — anger, pain, disgust — is legitimate, sustainable fuel for achievement. Hormozi reframes his own angry teenage years and his early "just don't be broke" mission as motivation, and closes with his standard "I will not do my best, I will do what is required."

## Key claims

- The college-taught message to "pursue your purpose and passion" is propaganda from people who never worked in a real economy; you don't need passion — you can build the life you want on anger and pain. (paraphrase)
- Half your life is below average by definition; not using that negative experience as fuel is a huge disadvantage. Olympic medalists don't love winning, they hate losing (relief, not euphoria); Steve Jobs, MJ, Kobe manufactured suffering to motivate. (paraphrase)
- You are not poor because you lack passion; "you were poor because you're not doing the activities that make you rich," and you can fuel those activities however you please. (paraphrase)
- The wealthiest people share three traits: a big goal, massive fear of never being enough, and impulse control to stay on goal. (paraphrase)
- Persistence is about the pain of moving forward being less than the pain of going back / the imagined pain of not acting; outcome is what matters, why you do it is your business. (paraphrase)
