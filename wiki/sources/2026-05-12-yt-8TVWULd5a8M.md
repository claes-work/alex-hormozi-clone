# Helping a $1M Agency Hit $10M (17% Monthly Churn)

## Summary

A 24-year-old agency owner says his done-for-you content and ads business serves
door-to-door and life-insurance salespeople, does about $1 million per year, and churns
around 17% per month. Alex diagnoses the main issue as selling too broadly downmarket
instead of messaging to the narrow avatar that actually retains: high earners with a
team, a training system, and enough volume to use the recruiting leads. He recommends
separate pricing for "minnows" and "whales," avatar-specific messaging, segmented churn
tracking, and AI-enabled delivery changes to raise gross margin.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex asks which avatar works and has the caller define the high-retention customer by
  earnings, team size, and training system.
- He turns the caller's answer into a rough ICP: $750,000+ annual personal commissions,
  a training system, and at least about 10 salespeople.
- He says 3K and 4K monthly packages are effectively the same buying bucket.
- He recommends a short-term lower tier for "minnows," such as $5,000 per quarter, to
  create runway and cash flow.
- He recommends a higher tier for the best customers, such as $5,000 per month and
  possibly more later.
- He says messaging must address the correct avatar; lower-level buyers may still apply,
  but they should not define the message.
- He says the caller should separate ICP and non-ICP churn and care most about the churn
  of the correct avatar.
- He says a sub-3% to 5% monthly churn rate among the correct avatar would justify
  scaling.
- He tells the caller to change the delivery model, use more AI, and raise gross margins
  rather than price from the old delivery model.
