# Should You Be Fueled by Faith or Anger?

★ **L3-candidate for `persona/beliefs.md`** — reveals a durable self-narrative about
what actually fueled Alex's rise: not faith, but **anger and fear**. Also touches
biography (early Gym Launch mindset, "million a month in our 20s") and his evolution
away from anger toward enjoyment. Promote precisely on next synthesis pass.

## Summary

Solo Alex answering an audience question (the questioner's prompt appears in the
transcript, but all substantive content is Alex). Asked whether he acted out of fear or
faith — and how one shifts from fear to faith — Alex rejects the premise that faith
drove him. He says **anger and fear** are what drove him to take action; he "did not
have a lot of faith." It took him **years (roughly six)** to even begin shifting away
from always being driven by anger and the need to prove people wrong / prove he was
right. He does not claim to be "purely faith driven" now — he says it isn't a binary for
him; over time he has less anger and more of "some other stuff," chiefly **enjoyment**:
he does a lot of what he does now because he genuinely enjoys it and works hard because
hard work has historically paid off for him.

He anchors this in the early Gym Launch era: there was **zero faith / zero vision** — the
"vision" was literally "don't be broke." He and Leila were taking home ~$1M/month in
their 20s but he was convinced it wouldn't last (told her they had "18 months"), so they
lived cheap and banked everything. Gym Launch was still "crushing it" six years later,
but he didn't know that at the time.

He closes with a practical mindset point: it is very hard to make strategic, long-term
decisions when you're in survival mode (fight-or-flight over rent and payroll). The move
is to **decrease your liabilities and recurring stressful expenses to the greatest degree
possible** so you can "get your head above water," breathe, and make better decisions —
because competitors stuck in that fearful state can't. Ends with a CTA for his free
zero-to-$100M+ 10-stage roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap.

## Key claims

_All paraphrased from Alex, dated 2026-02-20 unless quoted._

- What drove him to take action early on was **anger and fear, not faith** — he "did not
  have a lot of faith."
- His early anger was tied to **proving people wrong** and making the point that he was
  right.
- Shifting away from being always-driven-by-anger took **years — roughly six** — and even
  now he wouldn't call himself "purely faith driven."
- He frames fuel as **not a binary**; over time he has less anger and more "other stuff."
- His current primary fuel is **enjoyment** — he does a lot of what he does because he
  enjoys it, and works hard because hard work has reliably paid off for him.
- He doesn't overthink his motivation now: he just does the things he enjoys and has been
  rewarded for in the past.
- He now operates on a **much longer time horizon** than in the Gym Launch days.
- Early Gym Launch had **zero faith and zero vision** — the entire "vision" was "don't be
  broke."
- Biography/self-report: he and Leila took home **~$1M/month in their 20s** with Gym
  Launch; he believed it wouldn't last, told Leila they had **"18 months,"** so they lived
  cheap and banked every dollar. It was still "crushing it" **six years later**.
- Survival mode (worrying about rent/payroll, fight-or-flight) makes **strategic,
  long-term decision-making very hard**; entrepreneurs with savings can think on a 10-year
  horizon precisely because shelter and food aren't at risk.
- Practical prescription: **decrease liabilities and recurring stressful expenses to the
  greatest degree possible** so you're not in fight-or-flight every month, can breathe,
  and can make better decisions — an edge over competitors trapped in that state.
