# Alex Hormozi reveals why he wants to become a billionaire (not what you think)

## Summary

In this short clip, Alex Hormozi explains that the billion-dollar net-worth goal
does not actually matter to him as money — it is an "easy target to shoot for"
that, once hit, simply resets to the next number (10, then 30 or 50). His real
motivation is credibility: reaching a billion would serve as public "proof" or
"evidence" that what he has been saying all along is true, granting him greater
certainty and a broader audience. He says he and Leila are on track to the goal,
credits deliberate content as a way to expand their "luck surface area," and puts
his honest timeline at roughly five years (5–7).

## Key claims

- The billion-dollar figure isn't intrinsically meaningful to him; it's "an easy
  target to shoot for," and once reached it will just become the next larger number.
- His deeper motivation is validation/credibility: hitting a billion would be public
  "proof or evidence" that what he has been saying is true — echoing a story he
  attributes to Patrick Bet-David, whose newfound confidence after a large exit came
  from the feeling that "what I was saying all this time... I was right all along."
- He is deliberately building this goal publicly because he thinks "it'll be cooler to
  call the shot and then hit it."
- He believes reaching a billion would give him a "significantly wider, broader
  audience" because he'll have proof.
- He says he and his company are on track; he acknowledges the role of luck and
  "lucky breaks," but frames content creation as a way to "increase your luck surface
  area" and "deliberately create more luck."
- Math baseline: putting the money in the S&P at ~9.7% would compound to the target
  over 30–40 years "kind of regardless," but he's hoping to beat that.
- His honest timeline for hitting a billion is about 5 years (ideally 5–7).

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "Why a billion? Why does this matter to you? I don't think it actually does matter
  to me... it's just an easy target to shoot for, and then once we're at one it'll be
  10, you know what I mean, when it'll be 10 it'll be 30 or 50 or whatever after that."
- "I could say the exact same thing at a billion in net worth and probably have a
  significantly wider, broader audience because I'll have proof or evidence that what I
  am saying is true."
- "I'm trying to build this publicly so that... I think it'll be cooler to call the shot
  and then hit it."
- "You can increase your luck surface area, and I think a lot of what we've tried to do
  with the content and whatnot has been to deliberately create more luck for ourselves."
- "If we were to just take the actual money and just put it in the S&P... if it
  compounded at whatever, 9.7%, for the next 40 years or 30 years, we'd hit it kind of
  regardless — but I'm hoping to beat that."
- "If we can do it in 5 to 7, that would be pretty cool... if my honest answer to what
  are you really shooting for, it's probably five years."
