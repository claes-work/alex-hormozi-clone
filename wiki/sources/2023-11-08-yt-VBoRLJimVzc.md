# If I Wanted to Become a Millionaire In 2024, This is What I'd Do [FULL BLUEPRINT]

## Summary
A long-form solo blueprint in which Alex Hormozi lays out his complete four-level path from
zero to millionaire and beyond. Level one covers the fundamentals of wealth creation
(net-worth definition, earn-vs-own, anti-diversification, the five emotional phases of a new
opportunity, and building "the long game" with a foundation). Level two is the tactical
first-million playbook: find a hungry crowd, one avatar / one product / one channel, the
value equation, the eight ways to advertise (core four + four lead-getters), sales scripts,
paying yourself, and activity-based goals (the Rule of 100, scientific-method goal-setting).
Level three is staying rich and getting richer (get rich once, the quad marketing calendar,
building Enterprise Value by removing yourself, reputation/brand as a "bouquet" of Goodwill,
and compounding). A final "level above levels" reframes enjoying wealth around freedom and
infinite games. Throughout he grounds authority in his own record and cites Buffett, Munger,
Bezos, and Churchill.

## Key claims (the blueprint)
**Level 1 — Fundamentals of wealth creation**
- A millionaire = net worth (assets minus liabilities), *excluding* primary residence, in
  excess of $1M. Liquid net worth (tradeable cash/assets) differs from net worth tied up in
  assets like a house.
- Self-reported track record: became a millionaire in his early 20s, first cash million in
  the bank at 27, crossed $100M net worth by 31; claims to have helped 100+ people build
  million-dollar-plus net worths.
- Two ways to become a millionaire: **earn** your way (e.g. save 100% of $200K/year for ~10
  years, needing ~$2M pre-tax to net $1M) or **own** your way (build a business doing $250K/yr
  automated profit, sell at ~4x = $1M). Owning is faster because time and the multiple work
  *for* you instead of taxes and living expenses eating your earnings.
- **Don't diversify** early. Diversification is "a hedge against ignorance" — you only need it
  if you don't know what you're doing. When capital is scarce, your most valuable resource is
  time and attention; concentrate it on the one opportunity closest to "spilling over."
- The wealthy DID make wealth by going all-in on one income stream, THEN diversified to
  maintain it — the "average millionaire has seven income streams" myth confuses how they
  keep wealth with how they made it.
- Five emotional phases of pursuing a new opportunity: uninformed optimism → informed
  pessimism → valley of despair (where most quit and jump to a new "greener" opportunity that
  is equally full of manure) → informed optimism → achievement. Winners push through the
  valley instead of restarting the cycle.
- **The long game / build a foundation.** Rushing makes you build a tall, flimsy tower that
  caps out; you can't stack more stories on a business built wrong. Build the product first,
  then advertise, then keep fixing the product (make customers happy so they refer) rather
  than just cranking marketing on a bad product. Hire for talent (interview 20 for one role),
  not just "a body in the seat."

**Level 2 — Tactics of the first million**
- **Find a hungry crowd** (the hot-dog-stand-on-the-best-corner parable). Three levers, in
  order of power: (1) the **market** (supply/demand is the strongest force in business —
  toilet paper during COVID); (2) the **offer**; (3) your **persuasion** ability.
- Four criteria for a market: people in **pain** (must-have, not want), **purchasing power**
  (ability to buy), **easy to target** (findable, e.g. nurses vs. "psychedelic Aztecs in
  Croatia"), and a **growing market**. You need all four, not three.
- **One avatar, one product, one channel** — the simplest formula to $2M+. Don't add products
  or channels under ~$1M; it adds complexity without enough customers. You can hit $1M+ on any
  single channel (outreach, content, or paid ads) by doing more/better of it.
- **The value equation** ($100M Offers). Value has four variables: increase **dream outcome**
  and **perceived likelihood of achievement**; decrease **time delay** (micro and macro) and
  **effort & sacrifice**. Perfect offer = biggest dream, guaranteed, instantly, with zero
  effort. The most expensive part of an offer is often the hidden costs (what the buyer must
  start doing), not the money.
- Offer is the largest lever because changing it changes marketing, sales, and delivery —
  everything. acquisition.com portfolio averages (self-reported): first 12 months 1.8x
  revenue / 3.0x profit; first 24 months 2.88x revenue / 4.7x profit. States acquisition.com
  invests in and buys businesses, does not sell coaching.
- **Marketing & sales.** Eight ways to advertise: the **core four** (warm outreach, post
  content, paid ads, cold outreach) that YOU can do, plus four **lead-getters** that others do
  for you (customer referrals, employees, agencies, affiliates/influencers). Start with one of
  the core four. Founders should personally do both marketing and sales early — cheaper, and
  the skills let you later teach/vet lead-getters.
- Build a **choreographed, repeatable sales process/script**: throw stuff at the wall, find
  what gets someone from interest to payment, backtrack the winning steps, and recreate them,
  because successors are never as convicted/charismatic as the founder.
- **Paying yourself** ("Mordor"). Most subjective topic; hinges on personal risk tolerance.
  His regret: reinvested every dollar from his gym chain, took nothing out, then lost it all.
  Taking cash out forces cash flow to appear and forces personal spending discipline; a full
  bank account makes for less-stressed, better decisions. Two frameworks for capital-intensive
  businesses: 33/30 split (33% to you, 30% to growth, rest to reserves) or a **watermark**
  (leave a fixed profit level in the account, distribute the rest).
- **Goals: focus on activities, not outcomes.** Make the inputs the goal so you can control
  and always hit them. **The Rule of 100**: do 100 primary actions/day for 100 days to get
  your first result (100 outreaches, 100 minutes of content, or $100/day of ads). Also uses
  the **scientific method** for portfolio goals: (1) what problem/constraint, (2) hypothesis
  (if X then Y), (3) how to measure X and Y, (4) did we do it and did it work.

**Level 3 — Stay rich and get richer**
- **Get rich once.** A mentor's Goldman Sachs line: "you should only have to get rich once."
  After that, play with house money and never bet the empire on a pot of gold. Outsize returns
  come from betting against conventional wisdom; take 10%-chance/100x-payoff bets, but once you
  win, never bet it all on black again. "Fortunes are created by taking huge risks with small
  amounts of money, and preserved by taking small risks with lots of money."
- **Quad marketing calendar.** Market in all four directions at all times: prospects,
  customers (repeat), candidates, employees. Growing the left side (getting customers) requires
  growing the right side (getting/keeping the people to deliver).
- **Build Enterprise Value by removing yourself.** A "genius with 1,000 hands" doing $2M/yr has
  no EV because it depends on him. Make the structure/systems/people the genius — remove
  yourself — and the same profit becomes a sellable asset worth a multiple (e.g. take a $2M/yr
  business to $10M EV at a 7x multiple → a $1-3M net worth becomes $70M). Making it reliable,
  risk-free, and lasting is one of the fastest ways to create wealth.
- **Reputation/brand = a bouquet.** Combining consistent life experiences/skills/traits creates
  a reputation; one broken flower (a scandal) distorts the whole bouquet. A brand is an
  association between something known and something unknown. Providing value builds customer
  surplus / **Goodwill**, which compounds *multiplicatively* and faster than revenue (The Rock's
  Teremana, Kylie's beauty line, McGregor's Proper 12, Clooney — billion-dollar brands off
  reputation). Never risk your reputation for short money (his "if I started an OnlyFans tomorrow
  I'd make money but it'd be a broken flower" example).
- **Compounding** is the eighth wonder; only unlocks with a long-term horizon and no
  diversification interrupting it. Equity is one of the greatest compounding vehicles because you
  can reallocate capital within the same vehicle (10 locations growing 10%/month; Panda Express
  added 600 locations in one year after 45 years to reach 2,000). "Boring is what makes you
  rich." Money is made in the wait (Munger); patience = figuring out what to do in the meantime
  (marshmallow test — the successful kids had strategies/skills to distract themselves).

**Level above the levels — Enjoy your wealth**
- Two levels of wealth: personal needs covered, then the "unlimited"/game level. Living like the
  1% is cheaper than people think (~$50K/month after tax buys unbelievable living); figure out
  your actual personal dream list rather than chasing "billionaire" for no reason. He bought a
  Bentley, didn't care, sold it back at a loss.
- Enjoying wealth is not about *not* working — it's about getting "addicted to freedom" (having
  the *option* to work). Humans are best with purpose and an opposing force; strength is
  use-it-or-lose-it. Raise the difficulty to become your best self.
- The best games are **infinite, not finite**: the point isn't to get married but to stay
  married; not to win at business but to stay in business and keep playing. "You win as long as
  you never quit" (Churchill). You die and can't take it with you, so play the game because you
  enjoy the game.
