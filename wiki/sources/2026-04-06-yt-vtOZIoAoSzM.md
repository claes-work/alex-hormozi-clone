# Ignorance Is Costing You More Than You Think

## Summary

Alex defines the annual cost of ignorance as the difference between current earnings and
a desired, learnable earning level. Skills and education survive a failed bet, allowing an
experienced entrepreneur to recover from zero faster than a novice. He therefore treats
unknown but needed information as more expensive than visible fixed costs.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex values education by the result gap it can close; a person making
  $50,000 toward a $1 million goal carries a hypothetical $950,000 ignorance gap.
- He says entrepreneurs who rebuild after losing financial assets retain education and
  experience that a novice lacks.
- A failed market bet can still produce experience that improves a later bet.
- Alex calls missing but necessary information the most expensive purchase because its
  cost is invisible and repeats through foregone results.
- The ignorance debt can affect financial, relational, and physical outcomes.
- Alex says he does not regret prior ignorance because those decisions produced his
  current life, while also claiming he would become wealthier if starting again; both are
  self-reported opinions, not testable forecasts.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It costs you $950,000 a year not to know how to make a million dollars a year."

> "They didn't lose the education."

> "The most expensive thing you ever pay for... is the information that you don't know but should."

## Notes

Reinforces ignorance debt and education as a durable asset.
