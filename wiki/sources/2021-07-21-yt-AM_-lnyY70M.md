# How the rich avoid paying taxes - the Augusta Rule LOOPHOLE

## Summary
In this short solo video Hormozi explains the "Augusta Rule": a small-business owner can rent their personal home to their own business for up to 14 days a year, generating tax-free income for themselves while their business takes a deduction — "double dipping" with an asset they already own. He demonstrates from his own home, notes he built out recording studios that both raised the home's value and support a higher fair-market day rate, and frames the tactic as one of many small "golden BBs" of tax efficiency rather than a silver bullet.

## Key claims (2021; paraphrase unless quoted; tax figures self-reported)
- The Augusta Rule lets you lease your home to your business for up to 14 days per year; at your fair-market day rate × up to 14 days you can write yourself a tax-free check, provided you actually used the home for business (employee meetings, member events, client visits, small masterminds/workshops, recording). (Alex, self-reported)
- It is "double dipping": tax-free income to you personally *and* a tax deduction for the business, using assets you already have. (Alex, self-reported)
- His own numbers: he says a professional studio would rent for $5,000–$10,000/day; he spent ~$30k on one room and ~$50k on another (~$80k total) building out studios, which both increased his home's value and let him lease to his business at a higher rate. (Alex, self-reported)
- Worked example: someone making $100k/year leasing their home at a fair-market ~$500–$1,000/day could write themselves a ~$14,000 tax-free check and deduct it from business income. (Alex, self-reported)
- Overarching tax philosophy: unless you move to Puerto Rico there's no silver bullet — taxes are saved through many small "golden BBs" that reduce your "tax drag"; but don't get obsessed, put most attention on the business creating value. (Alex)
- He first heard the strategy ~2–3 years earlier at a mastermind, where a speaker said he'd save everyone the cost of the mastermind in five seconds. Advises viewers to tell their CPA "it's the Augusta Rule" and let the CPA implement it. (Alex, self-reported)
- Bio detail: self-describes as owner of Prestige Labs, Gym Launch, "Alan/ALAN" (auto-caption "Allen"), and five other portfolio companies, "about $85 million a year." (Alex, self-reported, 2021; not registry-verified)
