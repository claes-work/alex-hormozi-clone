# From Broker to Dealership: Should He Make the Jump?

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a class-B RV broker/consultant with a 4-million-follower audience who is considering becoming a dealership. Alex says the dealership path is clearly the bigger opportunity because it moves from an affiliate/broker structure to owning the ecosystem, but it is harder and carries more barriers. Because the founder has a large audience and can return to the current model if needed, Alex encourages him to take the leap.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - class-B RV broker/consultant. His revenue, profit, manufacturer deals, audience size, and dealership concerns are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The coachee is at a fork: keep the low-risk broker model or pursue the higher-risk, higher-reward dealership model.
- The dealership path is the larger opportunity because it means owning a real asset and customer ecosystem rather than acting like an affiliate or broker.
- The barriers that make the dealership harder also deter competitors, so the reward is proportional to the difficulty.
- Legal, capital, and operational barriers are not reasons by themselves to avoid a larger opportunity.
- Alex says the coachee has an unusual insurance policy because the 4-million-follower audience lets him return to the current model if the dealership fails.
- Alex frames the current business as plan B, which makes the jump more attractive.
