# Helping a Construction Company Doing $11M/Year Get to $100M

## Summary
Live coaching Q&A: the owner runs a commercial construction company ($11M/yr, 18% margins) and, after reading Built to Sell, started a more sellable/recurring elevator company (~$3M in year one, 30% margins). He splits his time between them and asks what to do to get from 10 to 100. Hormozi gives the "reasonable" answer (hire leaders to run construction hands-off) but leans into the "unreasonable, quote-Alex" answer: fire-sell the construction firm and go all-in on the elevator business on pure opportunity-cost logic.

## Key advice (Alex)
- The elevator company is the orders-of-magnitude better opportunity — recurring, super-niched, faster-growing, and it grew to $3M on split attention (might be $8M on full attention).
- Reasonable path: it might take ~$800K of construction profit to hire the 2–3 leaders needed to run it without you so it keeps existing.
- "Quote-Alex" path: he burns things down. Exit the construction firm fast, even for far less than it's "worth," because the opportunity cost of winding it down exceeds the sale proceeds — he can rebuild that enterprise value in the better business in a quarter.
- Precedent: he fire-sold six gyms in 90 days, made on all six what he "should have made on one," and within six months was doing $1M/month on the next thing.
- Everyone (wife, dad) will call you crazy when you do this — but "none of them are wealthier than I am"; no one wealthier than him ever told him he was crazy. Reframe the sunk effort: it wasn't wasted because "I was the asset" — you learned the skills you now apply.
