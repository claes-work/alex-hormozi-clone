# Stop Networking

## Summary
Solo monologue. Alex's contrarian take on networking: his objection is not networking itself but *when* it happens. Borrowing Naval's exploration/exploitation frame, he argues life has seasons — early on you go broad ("taste lots of stuff") to discover what you have a proclivity for; once you find it, you cut everything else and go deep. The broad period is never when you make money (for him this exploration phase lasted ~5 years). Beginners fall into a "yes trap" (saying yes out of fear and scarcity until overwhelmed); the only way out is learning to say no. His tactical rule: when choosing between a networking event / a "let me pick your brain" lunch and doing the actual work, always choose the work — "when the work works out, the people will still be there to take your call," but no amount of networking gets the work done for you. Winning opens 100 doors; losing (via distraction) closes the one you had. "Losers congregate, winners isolate." Second half pivots to relationships as a subset of the same logic: transitioning from exploration to exploitation means moving from many loose connections to a few tight ones. He's openly transactional (everyone does cost/benefit math), and to raise your life's standard you must raise the standard of the people around you — change your reference group, create productive deprivation. The universal currency that lets you into circles you "don't deserve" is work ethic; people gladly help someone who will execute, and hate wasting time on someone who won't help themselves. He frames friendships as seasonal — he's shed friends every season, keeps a very small close circle, and gives himself permission to let go of past-season friends without resentment so he can reinvest that time in relationships that help him reach the next objective. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames life via Naval's exploration→exploitation model: early on you go broad to find what you have a proclivity for, then cut everything to go deep. It's a continuum, not a binary; for him graduating from the broad phase took about 5 years.
- As of March 2026, the broad/exploration period is "never the period where you're going to make money."
- As of March 2026, beginners fall into a "yes trap" — you say yes out of fear/scarcity until overwhelmed; the only way out is learning to say no, which means changing what you do ("welcome to entrepreneurship").
- As of March 2026, his tactical rule: when choosing between networking (events, "pick your brain" lunches, "let's synergize/collaborate") and doing the work, choose the work — because when the work works out the people are still there to take your call, but no networking gets the work done for you.
- As of March 2026, distraction has downside asymmetry: if you lose your current small success by getting distracted, people who'd take your call today won't take it later; but if you win, 100 doors open.
- As of March 2026, "losers congregate, winners isolate" — success at the top is inherently for few ("the top of the mountain isn't supposed to fit a lot of people").
- As of March 2026, don't take advice on your idea from people who have no idea and don't have the life you want ("a great way to make a mistake is to ask someone else's idea about your idea who has no idea").
- As of March 2026, the exploration→exploitation shift moves you from many loose connections to a few tight ones; he calls himself transactional and says everyone runs a benefits-vs-costs calculation, some just openly.
- As of March 2026, "the fastest way to change your life is to change who you're friends with" — your reference group sets what you compare yourself to, and raising it creates productive deprivation that pulls you up.
- As of March 2026, work ethic is the only thing that lets you into circles you "don't deserve" to be in — "the universal currency of respect" across cultures, time, and generations; people happily help someone who will execute but resent helping someone who won't help themselves. He says this is his "secret" for punching above his weight class in connections.
- As of March 2026, he treats friendships as seasonal — he's shed friends every season, keeps a very small close circle, and gives himself permission to release past-season friends without expectation so he can reinvest that time toward his next objective.
