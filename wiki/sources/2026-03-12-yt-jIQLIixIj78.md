# You Need to Have More Agency

## Summary
Interview clip (unnamed host; Alex answers). Asked what surprises people about billionaires observed up close, Alex names their **level of agency**: they come to their own conclusions independently, and if everyone disagrees, all the better — "you got there by disagreeing with the masses." His central argument is that outsize outcomes and doing what everyone else does are *antithetical*. He frames it as "life beta": follow the norms and you get a normal outcome; if you want an abnormal outcome, by definition you can't follow the norm, which makes you an outcast / out-group, and other people then have to resolve that apparent discrepancy. The more outsize the return you want, the more willing to disagree with convention you must be. Beyond agency he cites a focus on **leverage** — return on time and effort — and argues that making decisions for yourself is what *enables* the highest returns. On maintaining that stance, Alex argues **conviction must be self-derived**: if you believe or act only because of others' approval or because the idea is someone else's, you can't hold conviction for long — "if you find yourself waffling, it's simply because the ideas you're adhering to are not your own." If you build your reasoning from zero and trust the math, then when the whole world shouts that 2+2=5 you hold — and if there's a big pile of money in being right, you're *excited* most people disagree, because it means a bigger pot at the end. He notes this is hard because "thinking is very hard… it takes effort." He describes his own stress-testing habit — "poke holes in the logic," identify the assumptions, ask "how wrong can I be and still be right," and name the **need-to-believes** for a big bet; if there's really only one need-to-believe and it's likely, "let's push our chips in." Finally he adds a wrinkle to high-agency + leverage: the **willingness to change on a dime** when part of the reasoning is proven untrue — the most successful people are "not romantic with the truth," nor with their process of getting to it; they'll say "you're right, we need to change everything" and then take action. He endorses the host's "strong conviction loosely held" framing. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
