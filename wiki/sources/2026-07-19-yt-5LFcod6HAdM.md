# The Answer to Organic Growth Is Disgusting Volume

## Summary

In this coaching clip, a caller named Gabriel — who sells solar and runs a personal brand
helping Christian men — asks Alex Hormozi how to split test organic content across
platforms (Meta, TikTok, etc.) the same way he split tests paid ads with 10 hooks, three
bodies, and different CTAs. His team is already producing 350–400 pieces of content a
month and calls organic their biggest constraint.

Hormozi's answer is that you essentially don't split test organic the way you do paid:
you just make the content. Posting 10 variant hooks of the same piece would look odd on a
page, and until Instagram's recent "trial reels" feature, no creator in social-media
history had that ad-style testing function. Instead, organic growth comes from a "gross
and disgusting amount of volume for an unending period of time," paired with a batch
learning loop — make ~100 pieces, review which ones did well, isolate what was different
about the winners, add two points to a checklist, and apply those to the next hundred.

Hormozi frames the ongoing rhythm as an "accordion" between "better and more": in the
beginning you do a ton of volume, then when a few pieces pop off you invest more time per
piece and make fewer, then scale that quality up in count, repeating indefinitely. He
closes by debunking posting-frequency mythology (caps of two Instagram posts a day, four
TikToks, specific posting times), citing a Bollywood Instagram account that posts ~100
times a day for ~9 billion views a month, and argues the platform — not the creator — is
incentivized to distribute valuable content, so creators should just give it more to
distribute.
