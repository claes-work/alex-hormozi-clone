# How to Script a VSL

## Summary
In a coaching exchange with a backyard-renovation business owner (Lonestar), Alex Hormozi walks through his framework for structuring a video sales letter (VSL). He demystifies the format as a five-part structure — intro/promise, pain, a unique "secrets method," objection-handling via a four-step belief-breaking formula, and a CTA — rather than a "magic thing." He also shows how a second VSL further down the funnel is the identical script with different examples and only a changed CTA.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex frames a VSL around five pieces — he references "the five P's" (promise, pain, proof, plan) plus an intro and a unique differentiator — and insists the format is simple, not magical.
- As of March 2026, Alex prescribes an intro/setup section of roughly 1 to 3 minutes (ideally 60–90 seconds) that states the promise, agitates the pain, presents a proprietary "secrets method" (e.g. "LS Secrets Method"), and gives a visual roadmap of where the video is going.
- As of March 2026, Alex teaches that every objection is broken down with the same four-step "belief-breaking formula": what they think → why it's wrong → what's right → proof — repeated for each of the big objections a prospect raises (e.g. dead grass, mud).
- As of March 2026, Alex advises brainstorming every major objection a prospect will raise and addressing each one in turn using that formula, closing with a CTA for the desired next action (e.g. text a number).
- As of March 2026, Alex says a second VSL deeper in the funnel is the same script with different examples; only the CTA changes to match the next step in the process (e.g. from "text us" to "look forward to the person showing up").
- As of March 2026, Alex describes the VSL's job as getting the prospect excited while pre-framing the full end-to-end scope of services (e.g. hardscape and all offerings).

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "A lot of people like to make these seem like they're this crazy, magic thing. It's not."

> "Promise, pain, proof, plan. Basically, these are our five pieces."

> "All of these get broken down the same way, which is what they think, why it's wrong, what's right, and then proof. And that is literally the belief-breaking formula for everything."

> "You hit every one of the objections, and you just go: what they think, why it's wrong, what's right, proof — and you just do it for all of them."

> "What's the difference between the first one and the second one? Nothing besides the fact that you just do the video again with different examples. It's the same thing."

> "The fundamental — just the CTA changes, but that's it."
