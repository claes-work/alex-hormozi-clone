# Helping a Home Service Business 3x Profit Without Getting More Clients

## Summary

Alex advises an Aspen landscaping and maintenance company to test immediate price
increases before pursuing more volume. He frames the recurring maintenance service as
protection for an expensive landscaping purchase and suggests expanding the talent pool
nationally with relocation and signing incentives.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports roughly $7 million in revenue, 11% net margins, and a revenue
  mix that is about 70% recurring maintenance; none of these figures is independently verified.
- As of April 2026, Alex recommends raising prices until total collected revenue begins
  to fall, using actual customer behavior rather than fear to locate the ceiling.
- He estimates that a 20% price increase at the reported cost base could roughly triple
  profit, but this is illustrative arithmetic rather than a verified forecast.
- Maintenance can be positioned like insurance for an $80,000-$180,000 landscape
  installation: a comparatively small recurring payment protects a large purchase.
- For a geographically constrained labor market, he recommends recruiting nationally
  and paying relocation or signing packages instead of accepting local supply as fixed.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Raise your prices until you make less money."

> "You could literally triple your profit without getting a single new customer."

> "You need to advertise nationally for talent."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: empirically price to the revenue ceiling; protect-the-anchor maintenance positioning; national talent acquisition for local services.
