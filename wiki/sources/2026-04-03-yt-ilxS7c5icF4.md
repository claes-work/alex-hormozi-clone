# Helping a $1.5M Business That Runs on 30 Minutes a Day Scale

## Summary

After clarifying several times that the company is an asset-light helicopter-tour broker
using contractors rather than an aircraft operator, Alex says the existing business can
remain a cash-flow machine but does not match the owner's $500 million ambition. If the
ultimate version is not something the owner wants, plan an exit. One option is to transfer
operations to contractors in exchange for seller-financed profit or a 50/50 split that
eliminates calls to the owner.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says a business capable of reaching a large number is still the
  wrong vehicle if its ultimate form is not what the founder wants to own.
- The owner reports pivoting from aircraft/pilot ownership to brokering unused capacity,
  using two contractors and roughly 30-60 minutes of owner involvement; all figures are
  self-reported and unverified.
- Alex sees tourism demand as durable and relatively AI-resistant, although delivery
  technology can change.
- A broker dependent on the owner's sales has limited enterprise value; transferring the
  final owner-dependent function would create a more independent asset.
- He suggests selling to operators through two years of profit payments or giving half
  the profit in exchange for the owner's phone no longer ringing.
- Role-playing escalations with "do not call me" as the correct answer makes the autonomy
  requirement explicit.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If the ultimate version of this business isn't what you want to own, then get out tomorrow."

> "I'll give you half the profit. Never call me again."

> "Every answer that's not call you is the right answer."
