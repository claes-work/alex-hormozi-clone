# 42 Recession Proof Money Hacks

## Summary

A rapid-fire compilation of 42 money "secrets" Hormozi says worked across boom and bust economies over his ten-year career. The recurring themes: leverage comes from needing nothing, wealth comes from protecting compounding (don't interrupt it), attention concentrated on one game beats diversification, and deals are won on terms and trust rather than headline returns.

## Key claims

- "He who gives the money has the power" — the lender/investor dictates terms; go as far upstream on the money flow as possible for control and leverage. (paraphrase, #1, #17)
- Leverage comes from *needing nothing*: money flows to the person who needs it least; you sell from the buyer's wallet, not your own, and always keep an "oh-sh** fund" so you can stay on offense. (paraphrase, #14, #18, #22)
- Never trade reputation for money — money returns, reputation compounds and can't be rebuilt; trust lubricates deal velocity, so do a small "starter deal" with new faces and don't take all the meat off the bone. (paraphrase, #2, #26, #27)
- Concentrate, don't diversify prematurely: "money flows where attention goes," pick one game and take it to its natural conclusion; diversification is "a hedge against ignorance" (Buffett) and only helps if you don't know what you're doing. (paraphrase, #6, #32)
- Returns are in the terms, not the headline number: think in doubling/tripling time rather than IRR, buy as if you can never sell (Munger's "money is made in the wait"), use house money, and always know the exact steps to get your principal back. (paraphrase, #34, #38, #31, #35, #36)
- Pace decisions: "FOMO means go slow," "mistakes love a rush decision," and markets "take longer to adjust than you expect, then move faster than you can imagine." (paraphrase, #21, #23, #39)
