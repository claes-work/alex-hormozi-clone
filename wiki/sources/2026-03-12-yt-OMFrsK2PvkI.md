# The Problem I See Most Business Owners Struggle With

## Summary
Interview clip (an unnamed host asks the questions in the `>>` turns; only Alex's words are used as voice data). Asked for the single biggest problem people have in business, Alex's diagnosis is **overthinking used as a proxy for action** — people "think that they're being productive by thinking more about the business they want to start rather than just starting it," and underneath it is **fear of rejection**: "people are okay with failing. They're just not okay with other people seeing them fail." On the think-vs-act ratio, he says beginners should "think a lot less and do a lot more," and it flips as you advance (Warren Buffett makes "one move a year" and spends the rest of the time getting the decision right). This is governed by **leverage** — defined as the gap between what you put in and what you get out — via his equation **volume of repetitions × leverage = output**: with no leverage you "just need to crank on volume until you can create leverage," and one of the things that creates leverage is **skill**, which itself comes from prior volume ("you have to work hard in order to work smarter"). On where action comes from if someone can't find motivation, Alex reframes **motivation as the equal-and-opposite of deprivation** (thirst→drinking; hunger→eating), noting money is odd because rich people often act *more* money-motivated than poor people — because deprivation is *perceived*, and the gap between his current wealth and his billion-dollar goal feels larger to him than a $5k→$10k gap does to someone else. Tactically, the fastest way to change behavior is to **change your environment** (join a great swim team to get good at swimming); people who know they should move but don't simply aren't deprived enough / are too comfortable, and "if you don't want to be that successful, that's fine." He closes on his **North Star**: not the billion dollars itself (an arbitrary measure he'll just move to $10B), but to "die with nothing left to give" — using up every ounce of his potential, and eventually merging a huge helpful audience with aggregated wealth into "something really magical." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

- As of March 2026, the **fastest way to change behavior is to change your environment** (join a great swim/ping-pong team for fast feedback loops and comparison); if your environment doesn't support the behavior you want, you need to move.

- As of March 2026, his **North Star is not the billion dollars** (an arbitrary measure he'll reset to $10B); it is to "die with nothing left to give" — using up all of his potential — with the billion-dollar contrast forcing him to level up.
