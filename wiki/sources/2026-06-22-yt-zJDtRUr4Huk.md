## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- **Real-estate development is analogous to private equity / ACQ** — "you're scaling ACQ which is private equity, very similar to my opinion." A development business has two possible lead types: **the deals** and **the investors**; you must first identify which one you're actually short on. (Richard: capital is self-funded, so the constraint is deals.)
- Because the target is so specialized (specific size, specific area), **deal acquisition is fundamentally an advertising/outreach problem**, not a broad-content problem. Build a **brand around the niche** (Alex frames it as "all blue book — land and conversations").
- **Content should be a legitimacy signal, not a primary channel.** Make "sufficient content that people would know that I'm legit and I do this for a living" — don't expect specialized content to generate high volume.
- **Outreach (outbound) + follow-up is the primary method** for attracting deals in a small, specialized market.
- **Conferences are one of the most effective channels in small, top-heavy industries** — "the conference or shooting them in the barrel method." If starting over in a new industry, Alex says he'd attend one conference a week and meet everyone he could, because **"it's not the person you meet, it's the person that person introduces you to."**
- **A few big wins compound your pull** — once you close a few good, big deals, they attract more attention and more capital.
- **Build a broker network over time** — Alex notes his own firm does a lot of real estate by having focused on one market for ~6 years, and now most brokers bring their off-market deals to them first "because they know we can close." This is slow by nature: "it's a long business."
- **There is no single secret** — the answer is volume, outreach, and time ("it's going to take a lot of violence for this").
