# How to Actually Become Successful (Stop Copying Me)

## Summary

In this short Q&A, Alex Hormozi answers whether someone can become like him and provide value in business. He says not to copy him; the goal is to become more fully oneself. To provide value, a person needs either proof from already achieving something or effort that becomes proof. He recommends doing epic things, talking about them, helping others get what they want, and starting with free work or free trials plus a penalty when proof is lacking.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - solo Q&A answer. The statements below are Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.

## Key claims (Alex's answer only)

- The listener should not try to become like Alex; they should become the most unlocked version of themselves.
- A person cannot beat Alex at being Alex, and Alex cannot beat them at being themselves.
- Value comes from knowing who one can help most and around which context or topic one has unique insight.
- Beginners need proof from either notable achievement or visible effort.
- Large effort can itself create valuable lessons, such as making thousands of videos and reporting what was learned.
- The first step is doing epic things; the second is talking about them.
- Personal brand is built on the amount of value a person can provide.
- If proof is missing, start for free, work for free, get experience, and serve the first clients.
- Free trial plus penalty is a valid early offer structure.
