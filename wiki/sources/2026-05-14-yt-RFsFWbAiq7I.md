# If Your New Hire Has No Black Book, Don't Hire Them

## Summary

Alex describes the "snowball of talent": leaders who treat people well and produce good
outcomes over time accumulate people who follow them from business to business. When
hiring a C-level executive, he expects them to bring some network or "black book" of
strong people. If no one wants to follow the executive, that may indicate weak judgment
or weak leadership.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Good leaders with repeated good outcomes can accumulate a core team that follows them
  from thing to thing.
- Alex calls this the snowball of talent.
- A C-level executive with no network of past strong performers is a warning sign.
- The absence of a black book may mean the executive did not identify strong people or
  strong people do not want to follow them.
- A leader's black book is a litmus test for leadership quality.
- When building a function quickly, Alex expects a senior hire to bring some talent to
  the table.
