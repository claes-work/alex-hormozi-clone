# How to Pick the Right Acquisition Channel for Your Business

## Summary

Alex compares search ads with interruption advertising for residential solar. Search
needs less creative but becomes an LTV bidding contest; interruption can produce cheaper,
less-contested leads but requires frequent creative replacement. He chooses a starting
channel by the operator's smallest skill deficit, not by a universal ranking.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex generally prefers interruption advertising to PPC.
- Search ads can run with little creative turnover because new intent-driven prospects
  arrive, but competitors bid the channel toward whoever can afford the highest CAC.
- Interruption ads often sell without simultaneous competitor comparison and may be
  cheaper, but creative must be refreshed roughly every three weeks locally and every
  six to eight weeks nationally.
- At scale, a business may use both channels; initially, Alex recommends the one closest
  to the team's existing skill set.
- He does not favor content as the primary solar acquisition channel, but recommends a
  weekly post on advertised platforms to provide proof of activity and aid conversion.
- A genuine LTV advantage makes PPC attractive because the company can outbid rivals.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I prefer interruption-based advertising in general to PPC."

> "It comes down to an LTV battle of who can spend more."

> "Which of these things do you have the smallest skill deficiency in?"
