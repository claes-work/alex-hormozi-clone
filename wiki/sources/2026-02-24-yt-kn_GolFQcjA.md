# What I Think the Meaning of Life Is

★ **L3-candidate for `persona/beliefs.md`** — articulates a durable, first-person worldview

## Summary

A short clip (apparently the tail end of a longer interview) in which an off-camera
interviewer asks Alex, "What is the meaning of life?" After a joke ("obviously 42"), Alex
gives his genuine answer: the meaning of life is **learning**. He defines learning as being
exposed to new conditions that, over time, change your behavior — which is why you are
different now than you were 10 years ago. He frames "meaning" as the *output* of a process,
drawing the parallel to business: the meaning/purpose of a business is whatever its output
is (e.g., Elon's mission output is getting people to Mars). Applied to life, the output of
experience is learning — and, he stresses, learning happens whether you want it to or not.
The open question he leaves is not *whether* you learn but whether **you are learning the
things you want to**. The clip ends with a promo for his free acquisition.com/roadmap lead
magnet (10-stage 0→$100M+ roadmap).

Speaker note: solo Alex except the single interviewer question ("What is the meaning of
life?"). All substantive content is Alex-attributed.
