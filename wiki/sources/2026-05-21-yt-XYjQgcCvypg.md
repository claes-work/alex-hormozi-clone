# The Secret Language of the Highly Successful

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says becoming more successful requires understanding that successful people
may define words differently. In this clip the key word is work: some people hear work as
pain, while others define it as challenging activity worth doing. He also tells a holiday
family-table story about refusing to justify his life to someone whose life he did not
want.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The first step to becoming more successful can be understanding the language successful
  people use.
- Successful people may define common words differently.
- Work can mean a challenging, worthwhile activity where someone loses track of time, not
  merely pain.
- People who are addicted to work make that easier by choosing things worth doing.
- People should spend limited life on the few things worth doing.
- Alex says the line about not listening to people who do not have what one wants was a
  message to his younger self.
- He describes a holiday family-table conversation where a family member attacked his
  lifestyle as unbalanced.
- Alex says he asked which part of his life was unbalanced: health, marriage, or finances.
- His conclusion from "I would never live your life" was: good, do not.
- He frames justifying actions to people with unwanted lives as a waste of time.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "They're actually defining the word differently."

> "If they don't have what you want, don't listen to what they say."

> "Good. Don't."
