# We're posting and praying': A $13M CEO With No Metrics

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an experiential-marketing company doing about $13M and aiming for $50M. The company does one-time event/tour work and lacks clear marketing metrics. Alex says it must measure current acquisition first, but then gives a higher-leverage frame: instead of reacquiring one-time end customers every year, treat agencies or producers as distribution nodes and build a customer journey around them so they repeatedly send projects.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If a business cannot say what its current acquisition activities produce, the first step is measurement.
- Episodic, one-time work creates a structural problem because the company has to resell most of its revenue every year.
- The owner should "chunk up" one level from end customers to agencies or producers as the real distribution layer.
- A distribution node should be evaluated by what it can send over time, not just by one project.
- The business should build a customer journey around making the distributor's life easier and increasing their revenue.
- If the company can retain and activate distribution nodes, one new node per month can stack into a compounding business even when end-client work is one-time.
- The same logic applies if the true distribution node is an individual producer rather than the formal agency.

> "That creates a stackable, compounding business that makes the business valuable even though the nature of the work itself is one-time."
