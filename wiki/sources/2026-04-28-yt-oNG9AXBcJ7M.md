# You Don’t Need a New Business

## Summary

Alex coaches a seasonal holiday-lighting business doing strong EBITDA. He presents two
viable paths: build the seasonal "Halloween store" model by using the off-season to
create workforce and pre-sales infrastructure, or build a permanent/landscape-lighting
ascension path. His main warning is not to do both at once. He defines patience as
figuring out what to do in the meantime and argues that not delivering year-round does
not mean the business is not year-round.

## Key claims

- A highly profitable seasonal business can be attractive without adding a second
  business line.
- One path is to use the off-season to build recruiting, training, pre-sales, and rapid
  workforce infrastructure for the seasonal spike.
- Another path is to upsell temporary-lighting customers into permanent or landscape
  lighting.
- Alex says both paths can work, but the owner should choose which problem he would
  rather solve.
- He defines patience as figuring out what to do in the meantime.
- A business can be year-round even if delivery happens in a 90-day window, because the
  rest of the year can build demand and capacity.
- Alex recommends not pursuing both the seasonal-scale path and the permanent-lighting
  business at the same time.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I define patience as figuring out what to do in the meantime."

> "I would recommend not doing both."
