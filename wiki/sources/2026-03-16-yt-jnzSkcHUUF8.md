# How to Win with AI in 2026

## Summary
Live workshop clip. A participant runs a ~$4M/year firm selling fund-launch services and CFO services to technologist-entrepreneurs who become GPs (~$3–3.5M from fund launch, ~$1M from CFO services growing 4x YoY); he wants to reach $10M but feels he is the bottleneck — he has to personally deliver to keep quality, and his takeaway from the workshop is "more SOPs, more productizing the service, take myself out of the equation." Alex uses this to deliver a general **AI playbook for 2026**, and the core move is a mindset shift: **stop thinking in roles/org charts, start thinking in workflows/flows.** He argues every business function, taken at its most granular level, is just a series of actions, and most people never do the work of decomposing it. His worked example: an "editor" role is not one job but a chain — idea → trend research → ranking what's doing well → cross-listing what aligns with the brand/expertise → scripting → outline → intro → hook → roadmap → stories/proof woven in → packaging → "one damn YouTube video." Telling an editor "go make a YouTube video" leaves those steps implicit; the entrepreneur's job is to break the business down to that granular level and give each step clear directions. Why it matters now: **agents are doing each of these steps.** He cites Acquisition.com — media output "tripled in the last month" with **no headcount increase** — because they built prompts that eat 30/40/50% of the workload, so one person does the work of three, "then five, then ten." He redirects the participant away from just hiring (much of his own work is administrative) and toward decomposing his own time-sinks. On training the GPs, Alex gives a second lever: **zero explanation, only tell people the actions to take** — cut the theory/psychology pontificating because fewer words-to-action means faster comprehension and higher stick rate. The participant lands on "I'm just going to build AI agents," which Alex endorses ("it's like the internet again"). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
