# $100M Marketing Machine (2025)

**What it is.** An Acquisition.com "$100M Playbook" titled *Marketing Machine* with the subtitle "How to Turn Your Business Into an Ads Machine" (`PDF p. 1`). A ~30-page operational playbook (raw: `raw/books/2025-100m-marketing-machine.pdf`) teaching a checklist system that harvests customer-generated proof (videos, images, screenshots) from work a business already does, then converts it into paid ads and sales collateral (`PDF p. 5-6`). Raw text: `raw/books/raw/books/2025-100m-marketing-machine.md`.

> Note: running headers throughout the interior read "$100M PLAYBOOK: CHURN CHECKLIST" (`PDF p. 5-30`) — an apparent template/copy-paste garble; the actual document is the Marketing Machine, per the title page and table of contents (`PDF p. 1`, `PDF p. 3`).

## Thesis
A business whose customer acquisition depends on the founder's face is risky and hard to sell; the fix is a repeatable "Marketing Machine" — a checklist of "nodes" that systematically captures proof from customers and turns it into a self-refreshing supply of ads, removing the founder from the ads without doing anything the business isn't already doing (`PDF p. 4-8`).

## Structure and content
- **Origin story (2019 Gym Launch)** — bankers called the business "unsellable" because it depended on Alex's face; the walk, the "hit by a bus" reframe, and the awards-dinner epiphany that "the bottom of the list feeds the top of the list" (`PDF p. 4-6`).
- **Why This Matters** — face-dependent companies are worth less; put customers, not yourself, in the spotlight (`PDF p. 5`, doc `PDF p. 8`).
- **How The Marketing Machine Works** — the machine as a checklist of "nodes"; the seven-item master list (`PDF p. 6`, doc `PDF p. 9`).
- **Lifecycle Ads** + Local "Upon Delivery" ads (`PDF p. 7-8`, doc `PDF p. 10-11`).
- **Social Media Scrape** — hashtags, tagged-media capture, studs-vs-duds (`PDF p. 9`, doc `PDF p. 12-14`).
- **In-Person Events** — stage ads, professional testimonials, man-in-the-street (`PDF p. 12-16`).
- **Communication and Communities Scrape** — chats, community wins, review sites (`PDF p. 17-19`).
- **Bonuses and Award Ads** — bonus unlocks + four-proof award testimonials (`PDF p. 19-20`).
- **Competitions** — testimonial contest judged by ad performance (`PDF p. 21-23`).
- **Putting It All Together / Final Thoughts** — weekly + semi-annual cadence, recommended implementation order (`PDF p. 23-25`, doc `PDF p. 26-28`).
- **Implementation Checklist** — one-page tear-out of all components (`PDF p. 27`, doc `PDF p. 29-30`).

## Key claims
- (2025) The Marketing Machine is a checklist where each checked "node" represents a reliable stream of videos, images, and words usable in paid ads, content, outreach, sales pitches, webinars, and VSLs (`PDF p. 6`, doc `PDF p. 9`).
- (2025) The seven master nodes: Lifecycle; Social Media; Events; Communication, Communities & Review Site Scrape; Bonuses, Milestones & Awards; Competition; Delivery (`PDF p. 6`, doc `PDF p. 9`). A later tear-out lists "all 9 components" (`PDF p. 25`, doc `PDF p. 28`).
- (2025) Lifecycle ads clip a customer's journey from recorded calls — "before" from sales calls, "during" from support calls, "after" from ascension/award calls — to show rather than tell (`PDF p. 7`, doc `PDF p. 10`).
- (2025) Consent is required: compliance/permission language goes in every customer contract, and clients are still asked verbally; a "no" is honored (`PDF p. 8`, doc `PDF p. 11`).
- (2025) Professional testimonials at events follow a fixed 6-point script: internal struggle, external struggle, skepticism, overcame, external victory, internal victory (`PDF p. 15`, doc `PDF p. 18`).
- (2025) Award testimonials can extract FOUR forms of proof from one award per person: in-group results post, emailed video, unboxing video, and on-stage presentation (`PDF p. 20`, doc `PDF p. 23`).
- (2025) Competitions run a raw "iPhone-style" testimonial contest judged by real ad performance — most conversions over 30 days wins; run only once or twice a year (`PDF p. 21`, doc `PDF p. 24`).
- (2025) Chat/community/review scraping keys off "win" terms — the list given: PIF, Sales, $, Profit, Revenue, Monthly, close, closed, leads, show, scheduled, win (`PDF p. 17`, doc `PDF p. 20`).
- (2025) Incentive economics baked in: $50 to a handler for each off-list testimonial captured; an optional $100 gift card weekly to any rep whose screenshot becomes a winning ad (`PDF p. 15`, `PDF p. 18`, doc `PDF p. 18`, `PDF p. 21`).
- (2025) Recommended starting order for a beginner: Competition, Chat scrape, Community scrape, Reviews scrape, Social Media scrape, Bonus Unlocks, Award Unlocks, Events, Lifecycle — because the first gives a big influx and the next four use assets you already have (`PDF p. 24-25`, doc `PDF p. 27-28`).
- (2025) Cadence: a weekly proof-content checklist for the marketing team, plus a semi-annual influx from events and competitions; run one competition and two events per year to get a big flow "every twelve weeks" (`PDF p. 23-24`, doc `PDF p. 26-27`).

## Company / biographical numbers
- (2019) Bankers deemed Gym Launch unsellable because acquisition depended on Alex's face; the business brought in "millions every month" (`PDF p. 4`).
- (2019) After building the "GL Marketing Machine," member-generated ads outperformed even Alex's best direct-to-camera ones (`PDF p. 6`).
- (~2020) The deal closed for **$46.2M** twelve months after the second bankers' meeting, credited to the Marketing Machine removing founder dependence (`PDF p. 7`).
- Of the top 50 Gym Launch ads reviewed years later, **40 (80%)** did not feature Alex's face — they were customers (`PDF p. 5`, doc `PDF p. 8`).
