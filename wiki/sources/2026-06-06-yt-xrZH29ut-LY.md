# I'm Trying to Build an Empire — Hormozi Stops Her Cold

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the same premium nail salon owner from the related 2026-06-06 clip. The owner wants an ecosystem of salon, social media, education, product line, and future salon openings. Alex tells her she is doing too much and reframes the path: improve the current model, remove founder dependence, use media as national demand generation, and sell a franchise rather than a vague coaching offer.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A broad ecosystem can be a valid vision, but the plan must narrow to the model that makes the ecosystem economically real.
- The current salon model should work without founder social media before it is franchised.
- Paid acquisition assumptions matter because a franchisee will not inherit the founder's audience for free.
- The founder should become an online educator whose offer is a high-ticket franchise, not a general coach.
- Training is the key bottleneck in a skill-intensive service franchise.
- The business may be relatively AI-resistant in the short run because skilled physical services are harder to automate, though humanoid robotics could change that later.
