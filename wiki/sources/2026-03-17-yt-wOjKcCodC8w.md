# Helping a Sales Agency Fix Their Sales Process

## Summary
Short live coaching clip. The client runs a sales agency that sells electricity plans and backup power (canvassing neighborhoods to sign residents onto a shared-generator subscription), doing $300k/month and wanting to reach $1M/month. The stated bottleneck is fulfillment: the installer teams can't keep up, the wait to go live has stretched to ~120 days, and ~40% of signed customers cancel before installation because they get tired of waiting (they already have working electricity, so there's no urgency). Alex reframes the problem: a 40% cancellation rate means "something's wrong with the sale," not just fulfillment. He cites a near-identical company that avoids the problem by getting the commitment and banking information up front, canvassing an area until it hits the density threshold it needs, and only then sending the electrician company to install — so the wait is baked into the model and expectations are set honestly. He diagnoses the likely failure as sales reps *mismanaging expectations* — telling customers "it'll be live next month" and then pushing the date out repeatedly, which breeds the cancellations. His fix is to set the true timeline at the point of sale and frame the wait as a benefit: confirm the customer wants a lower rate, then tell them plainly it will take ~4 months, that they don't have to do anything but sign, and that signing now makes them "the first ones to get the lower rate." Ends with the $100M scaling roadmap promo (acquisition.com/roadmap).
