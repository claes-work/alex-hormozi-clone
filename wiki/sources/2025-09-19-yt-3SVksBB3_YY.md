# Give me 20 Minutes and I'll Give You Back 20 Years of Your Life

## Summary

Solo video in which Alex Hormozi lays out seven "investments" — in a recommended
order — that buy back personal time by outsourcing what he calls "humaning" (the
mundane maintenance tasks of daily life). The core thesis: if you have more dollars
than time and are willing to trade, you can reclaim roughly a full extra week per
week (~45 hours) by paying others to handle meals, laundry, house cleaning, sleep
optimization, landscaping, driving, and travel. He frames this as one of the
highest-ROI uses of money — beating the S&P 500 — because it increases active income
and skill-building time.

## Key claims

- The seven investments, in Alex's recommended order: (1) meals/groceries,
  (2) laundry, (3) house cleaning, (0/priority) sleep optimization, (5) landscaping,
  (6) driving/transport, (7) semi-private travel.
- You don't need to own a company — you just need more dollars than time and a
  willingness to trade. Some recommendations start paying off at ~$15/hour of income.
- Meals: average American spends ~13.5 hours/week on grocery runs, meal planning,
  cooking, cleaning, and portioning. He recommends two delivered/pre-made meals a day
  (shake in the morning), ~$750/month. Worth it if you make more than ~$16/hour.
- Laundry: ~4 hours/week (16 hours/month); drop-off service ~$1.50–$2/lb, most people
  30–40 lbs/month, so ~$60–$80/month.
- House cleaning: ~6–7 hours/week (26 hours/month); ~$200–$300/month; makes sense if
  you earn more than ~$20/hour.
- Sleep is labeled "investment zero" — do it for sure, but it's a one-time expense
  rather than an hourly trade. Blackout curtains, earplugs, electrical tape over
  small lights; optionally a ~$2,000 cooling mattress that also lowers heating/cooling
  bills.
- Sleep tiredness ("no juice in the middle of your brain") is not depletion — it's
  accumulated waste in the brain that sleep clears out.
- Landscaping/lawn care: ~8 hours/month; ~$300/month.
- Driving: average commute is 1.5 hours/day (~30 hours/month, up to 50); use Uber /
  future driverless fleets or a driver; ~$600–$1,000/month; consider it if you make
  over ~$50/hour.
- Semi-private travel (e.g. JSX, some JetBlue): skip TSA/security; ~triple commercial
  cost but a fraction of full private. Vegas→LA example: ~$20k private, ~$300 JSX,
  ~$50–$99 Spirit. ~$1,000/month on top of normal travel spend.
- Total: all seven ≈ $3,100/month (or ~$2,000/month if you travel less) to reclaim
  ~45 hours/week — roughly a whole extra week per week.
- As you move up the list, the dollars-per-hour saved goes down — that's "the trade"
  of becoming more successful. You only get each time-saving once, which is why it's
  such high ROI.
- Reclaimed time should be reinvested into increasing your skill set / active income,
  not wasted. He tweets and checks Slack while being driven.
