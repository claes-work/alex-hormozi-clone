# Every Product You Launch Sucks

## Summary

Alex tells a creator stuck in perfectionism to post more and treat publishing as the
start of learning. He cites Sam's line that every product sucks at launch and says the
goal is feedback, not an immediate win. Acquisition.com publishes about 450 pieces of
content per week, reviews the metrics, then does more of what worked and less of what did
not.

## Key claims

- Perfectionism in content is often fear of rejection disguised as quality control.
- The first version of a product or piece of content is not expected to be great.
- Publishing creates feedback that improves future versions.
- Alex says his team publishes about 450 pieces of content per week and learns from the
  metrics.
- High output accelerates learning because the creator gets more feedback cycles.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Every product sucks that you launch."

> "The point is to get it out and then learn."

> "Just do a load of it and then you'll learn faster."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: publish-to-learn content iteration; perfectionism as fear of rejection; 450 pieces/week operating standard.
