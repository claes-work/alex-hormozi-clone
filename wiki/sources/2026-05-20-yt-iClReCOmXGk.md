# Why You Should Start with Income-Generating Skills

## Summary

Alex Hormozi advises beginners to start with income-generating skills. He reduces a basic
business to promotion, conversion, and delivery, then says business ideas often come from
three places: passion, profession, or pain. He prefers most beginners start with services
because trading time for money is low-risk.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Beginners who want to get good at something should start with skills that generate
  income.
- A simple business has three elements: promotion, conversion, and delivery.
- Alex also phrases this as track, convert, deliver.
- Trying to imagine every possible business is harder than looking at problems already
  present in one's life.
- Many businesses come from one of three Ps: passion, profession, or pain.
- Passion means an area someone is inherently interested in.
- Profession means something someone is already paid to do.
- Pain means a problem someone personally went through and had to solve.
- Alex prefers most beginners start with services.
- He says about 78% of U.S. businesses are service-based because services are easiest to
  start.
- Service businesses are low-risk because the founder is mostly trading time for money.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Track, convert, deliver."

> "Passion... profession... pain."

> "You just trade time for money."
