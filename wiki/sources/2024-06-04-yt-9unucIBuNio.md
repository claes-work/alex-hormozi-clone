# 10x Your Profit. 10x Your Pricing. 10x Your Business. [Here's how]

## Summary

Despite the pricing-themed title, this solo talk is a six-step framework for building a
personal or business brand, which Hormozi argues is the highest-leverage way to improve
the fundamental business arbitrage (LTV vs. CAC). He defines branding as the deliberate
"pairing" of an unknown thing (your business/you) with a known thing people already
like, done repeatedly until the liking rubs off. He walks the six steps through a worked
example (an accounting firm choosing to target Med Spa / anti-aging clinic owners) and
closes on keeping promises via the product as the most important, compounding step.

## Key claims (the framework)

- **The payoff math**: brand can get customers to pay ~10x more, convert on ads ~10x
  higher (a tenth of the cost for 10x more) — "a 100x differential," how big brands
  print money over time. Cites his own 40-month run: 0 to 8M subscribers across all
  channels; portfolio to over $250M/year.
- **Step 1 — What is branding?** Branding = the association between two things. "Pairing"
  is the active verb entrepreneurs use to build it. Pair the thing people don't know
  (your business/you) with a thing they know and like; repeat until you can remove the
  known thing and the liking remains. A chaotic brand (many unrelated associations) is a
  meaningless brand. Analogy: prune the tree / weed the garden — a bed of all red roses
  reads as a strong single association; mixed random flowers say nothing.
- **Step 2 — Why bother?** The fundamental arbitrage of business is CAC vs. LTV (gross
  profit over the customer's lifetime); e.g. $1 to acquire returning $10 gross profit =
  10:1 LTV:CAC. Strong brand lowers CAC, raises conversion %, raises price above the
  commoditized market price, increases purchase frequency, and makes buyers not buy from
  anyone else (starves competitors). Branding is teaching a pairing (like teaching a kid
  the stove is hot, in reverse) — it takes time.
- **Step 3 — Who** (the target audience). Four qualifications (crash course from *$100M
  Offers*): (1) **growing** market, (2) **can afford** your stuff / has money, (3) **easy
  to target / find**, (4) **in pain** / actually wants what you sell (demand exists).
  Worked example: accounting firm → Med Spas / anti-aging clinics serving Boomers (growing
  via health/aging trends, moneyed, easy to find, in pain from aging).
- **Step 4 — What they like.** List what the who wants and struggles with. Med Spa owners:
  more customers, more profit, easier life / less time in the business, saving money, a
  sellable business, recruiting and retaining talent (nurses / NPs are scarce and costly).
- **Step 5 — Associate (= advertise).** Make/give away content (videos, blogs, cheat
  sheets, PDFs) that solves those problems, pairing yourself with what they like.
  Distinction: **advertising is the process of making known; branding is what you make
  known** (the content of the advertising). Four ways to advertise = the "Core Four" from
  *$100M Leads*: make content, reach out to people you know, reach out to people you
  don't know, run ads. Good advertising + bad association = bad branding (the
  Budweiser / Bud Light + Dylan Mulvaney example: exceptional advertising, wrong pairing
  for their core who, so bad branding and lost sales — but the same pairing could be good
  branding for a different, more progressive who).
- **Three levels of influence**: (1) you tell them (weakest — a movie trailer; new
  viewers judge 100% off your first exchange, so quality matters); (2) others tell them
  (word of mouth, far more powerful — the *Top Gun: Maverick* example, 10 people told him
  to see it); (3) what they experience themselves (they see the movie / use your service
  and decide).
- **Step 6 — Keep your promises (the product)** — the most important step. Deliver what
  you said, how you said, by when you said. This completes the **virtuous cycle of
  branding**: make a promise → keep it → others tell others you kept it → they try it →
  you keep the promise to them → repeat and compound. Micro-promises kept in content
  raise belief that you'll keep the macro-promise of the paid product.
- **You are the niche, but keep one core pillar first.** Your unique traits are the
  thumbprint of your personal brand, but around a central pillar (his = business). You can
  sprinkle identity (marriage, fitness, philosophy) but business must stay first because
  it's the common thread of the whole audience; leading with marriage would lose everyone
  who isn't there for that. He notes a 3–6 month "tiny mistake" experimenting with wider
  topics (a "white rose" among the red roses — e.g. his relationship with Leila).
- **Every new association is a trade**: you lose some audience and gain others; good
  branding means you gain more of the who you want and lose the who you don't. He
  deliberately declines "red pill / black pill" and porn-industry podcasts — not from
  personal objection (says he's nihilistic and doesn't judge how people legally earn) but
  because the trade would disenfranchise a segment of his audience that cares.
