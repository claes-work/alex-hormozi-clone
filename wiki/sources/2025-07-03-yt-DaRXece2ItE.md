# Copy This Strategy, It'll Blow Up Your Business

## Summary

Alex Hormozi walks through, in real time, how he is building the affiliate
(lead-getter) structure for his `$100M Money Models` book launch, using the same
playbook that drove ~500,000 registrations for the `$100M Leads` launch (roughly a
third of them referred for free). He breaks affiliate marketing into a repeatable
sequence: build a two-tiered offer, build the "machine" (sales page, pre-written
collateral, behind-the-scenes access), promote it across the Core Four, and then
support affiliates with onboarding, custom tracking links, a leaderboard, and
tiered rewards. The video is deliberately "meta" — it is itself an affiliate
recruitment asset, showing the machine being assembled as he explains it.

## Key claims

- An **affiliate is a lead-getter**: an independent business that tells its own
  engaged audience to buy your stuff in exchange for money, free stuff, or both.
  Affiliates look like referrals but differ — they run their own businesses and
  their own advertising.
- Treat the affiliate like a **new customer**: start with the offer (what you give
  them). For the Leads / Money Models launches he uses **free stuff, not cash** —
  paying "a gazillion people with 1099s" is a paperwork nightmare, and non-monetary
  value can exceed a small percentage kickback, especially for a low-priced item
  like a book.
- Use a **two-tiered offer**: something big for the "killers" with large audiences,
  and something for the "everyman" long tail (people who can bring 3–10 friends).
  You want both, not one or the other.
- The prize must benefit the affiliate **and** their audience, so promoting doesn't
  burn the affiliate's goodwill — a **win-win-win**. Example: top-10 affiliates get
  an in-person fireside one-on-one Q&A; their audiences click the affiliate link to
  submit questions the affiliate asks on their behalf.
- Reward tiers for the launch: **Top 10** get in-person fireside Q&A plus a dinner;
  **Top 50** get a 3-hour in-person Q&A session at HQ; **10+ opt-ins** get a
  separate affiliate-only audiobook ("70+ one-minute tactics that drive profit"), a
  recording, and a raffle entry for 50 in-person seats.
- Set the **minimum activation threshold** by friction: high-effort actions (bring
  someone in person) justify a low count; low-friction actions (get an opt-in) need
  a higher count. He set it at **10+ opt-ins** — same as last year, which worked.
- Prefer prizes that are **big, sexy, and unscalable at the top** (a fireside chat
  can't be done with 1,000 people), then create "approximations" of that top prize
  at lower tiers. He spends ~two days just designing the offer permutations.
- Build a dedicated **affiliate sales page** that sells people on becoming an
  affiliate: explains each prize, exactly what they must do (opt in → get a custom
  link → send traffic → rank on a leaderboard), and how you'll help them.
- **Support affiliates with done-for-them collateral** ("blackbooks"): pre-approved,
  pre-written, previously-proven emails, posts, ads, and text/DM messages so they
  can copy-paste. Any friction and they stop; make it easy, fast, and personalized
  to each affiliate type.
- Give affiliates **behind-the-scenes access** during the launch — sharing how each
  email and ad is performing — an underrated benefit that lets them learn the
  process while promoting.
- Promote the affiliate program across the **Core Four** (warm/cold outreach,
  content, email, paid), but sparingly on his main list (email it only ~2 times) so
  he promotes the book, "not promoting the book." He also re-recruits last year's
  ~30,000+ affiliates and adds an affiliate opt-in button on the book's thank-you
  page. He does **not** run ads directly for affiliates — ad flow goes to book
  pre-orders/registration, which then feeds the affiliate loop.
- Affiliates need **onboarding + activation with fast feedback**: an immediate
  custom link (proving good attribution tracking) and a frequently-sent
  **leaderboard** (people check it daily; it gamifies the launch). His director of
  marketing / team personally reaches out to top movers to help them do more.
- Split collateral into two waves: the **starter set**, then the **winners** (the
  ads/hooks/angles actually converting) so affiliates double down on proven material
  using his data.
- Compliance is simple: **don't lie, don't exaggerate, state the facts** — writing
  the collateral also keeps messaging aligned and expectations consistent.
- "Affiliate" has many industry names for the same thing: promoter, influencer
  advertising, UGC, affiliates (direct response), referrals (brick-and-mortar),
  centers of influence / COIs (finance), joint ventures. He tailors collateral to
  each type's format (Instagram/YouTube warm audiences, email/Substack/X/LinkedIn
  text, or pure paid-ads operators).
- Affiliate marketing is one of his most scalable, free advertising channels across
  every business: **Skool** traffic is majority word-of-mouth, **Allan** (software
  company he sold) was almost entirely affiliate-driven, and **Prestige Labs** is
  ~100% affiliate sales via brick-and-mortar distributors.
