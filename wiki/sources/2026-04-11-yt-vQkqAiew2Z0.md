# Helping a $12.5M E-Commerce Brand Scale to $50M

## Summary

Alex tells a stationery brand to keep its proven Amazon product-launch engine as the
"silent six" while making one annual bet on building an owned customer list through
TikTok Shop and Shopify. He recommends a small separate team and a well-paid director of
marketing, while rejecting the founder's personal-brand ambitions until the core
business has produced the larger win.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex defines the "silent six" as continuing the proven base strategy
  without threatening it while pursuing one major new bet.
- Anticipated Amazon margin compression justifies an annual bet on an owned customer
  list through TikTok Shop and Shopify.
- The new bet can be staffed by the founder and a small team rather than redirecting all
  25 employees; company figures are owner-reported and unverified.
- Alex promises that delegating the founder's current work will reveal other valuable
  work rather than leave the founder idle.
- He ranks winning the core e-commerce business above speaking and personal-brand work;
  the win would open those doors later with more credibility.
- He recommends using the owner's e-commerce podcast to headhunt a proven marketing
  director and paying heavily for the right person.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "We're not going to threaten the base to try and pursue the new thing."

> "I promise that if you replace what you were doing, you will find something else to do."

> "What matters more? The win. So get the win."
