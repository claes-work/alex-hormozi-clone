# GROWTH LEVERS: Big Business Owner Explains Real World Tactics (2021-12-01)

## Summary

A recorded keynote (single speaker: Alex Hormozi) delivered around the launch window
of *$100M Offers*, opening with reflections on wealth/status before teaching a
four-part offer-and-growth curriculum: de-commoditize the offer, pick the right market,
charge premium prices, and engineer value via the value equation. Its distinctive
growth-levers contribution is the "business genie" money game, which ranks three ways
to double a business (new customers vs. price vs. purchase frequency/retention) and
shows price is by far the strongest lever. All content is Alex-attributed.

## Growth levers (the framework)

**The "business genie" money game** — Hormozi's ranking of growth levers, illustrated
with a worked example (30 new clients/mo, 33% monthly churn → 3-month lifetime, $100/mo,
20% net margin → $10k revenue / $2k profit). A "genie" offers to *double* exactly one of
three variables:

1. **Door 1 — double new customers** (30 → 60/mo). Everything scales linearly: profit
   $2k → $4k (2x). Ranked the **worst** door.
2. **Door 3 — double purchases per customer / halve churn** (3-month → 6-month lifetime).
   Slightly *more* than 2x profit, because top-line revenue matches Door 1 but
   customer-acquisition cost is unchanged (same 30 acquired, each now worth 2x).
   Ranked **second best**.
3. **Door 2 — double price**, everything else held constant ($10k → $20k revenue).
   The extra $10k/mo is *pure profit* (nothing changed in what's delivered): profit
   $2k → $12k = **6x income (a 600% increase)**. Ranked the **best** door.

Takeaway: price is the highest-leverage growth variable; retention/frequency beats
new-customer acquisition because it reuses already-paid-for CAC.

The keynote's surrounding four-lever offer curriculum:

- **Lever 1 — De-commoditize the offer.** Move from a price-based to a values-based
  decision by selling in a "category of one." Better offers multiply: more leads x
  higher conversion x higher price compound (2x·2x·2x = 8x).
- **Lever 2 — Pick the right market.** Four attributes: (1) desperate need/pain,
  (2) purchasing power, (3) easy to target, (4) growing market.
- **Lever 3 — Charge what it's worth.** Price-to-value discrepancy; virtuous vs.
  vicious cycle of price.
- **Lever 4 — The value equation.** (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of
  Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice).

## Key claims (paraphrase, dated 2021-12-01)

- The offer is the fastest shortcut to material success in a business; when taking over
  a business as an investor, the first thing he looks at is the offer stack. (2021)
- A values-based decision means the buyer has nothing to compare against and must accept
  the price if value is deemed sufficient; a price-based decision means "these are close
  enough, pick the cheaper." (2021)
- Case study — a B2C weight-loss offer at $99/mo that "couldn't even give away for free"
  was reframed as a **free six-week challenge**, turning a below-break-even offer into
  **30:1** ad returns. (2021)
- Case study (software co. "ALAN," selling to brick-and-mortar agencies) — switching a
  $1,500/mo retainer to a pay-once-then-pay-per-show "Grand Slam Offer" produced a
  **22.4x** increase in cash collected up front (response rate 2.5x, appointments 40→100,
  close rate 16%→37%, price 4x), with no change to creative, funnels, or nurture — only
  the offer. (2021)
- Niching the *same* product raises its price: a generic time-management book (~$19) →
  "for salespeople" (~$99) → "for outbound sales reps" (~$500) → "for outbound power-tools
  & gardening sales reps" (~$2,000). The product is virtually unchanged; only the defined
  avatar narrows. (2021)
- Market selection dominates skill: at a mastermind (~$600M aggregate revenue) the
  highest earners weren't better at sales/marketing/fulfillment — "they're just going
  after better markets." (2021)
- A resume-improvement service failed despite a growing market, easy targeting, and
  desperate pain, because unemployed buyers lacked purchasing power — money is a
  non-negotiable market attribute. (2021)
- Price confers value: cites a wine study where three bottles (labeled cheap/mid/high)
  were ranked accordingly despite being identical wine. (2021)
- Raising price triggers a **virtuous cycle**: higher emotional investment → higher
  perceived value → better client results → less demanding clients → more profit per
  customer to reinvest in fulfillment. The reverse is a vicious cycle. (2021)
- "Sales is a transfer of conviction… through a bridge of trust"; the #1 converter is the
  prospect's belief you can help them, which must start with the seller's own conviction.
  (2021)
- The value equation's bottom half (time delay, effort/sacrifice) is where the biggest
  businesses focus and where the operational competitive moat is built; beginner
  marketers over-focus the top half. (2021)
- Perceived likelihood of achievement scales with proof: he raised prices going from 100
  testimonials to 2,000 testimonials. (2021)
- "Fast beats free": the easiest way to add value is to do what everyone else does in
  half the time. (2021)
- Offer enhancers named (from the book, not fully taught here): scarcity (function of
  units), urgency (function of time), bonuses, and four guarantee types — unconditional,
  conditional, implied, anti-guarantee. Any performance offer is an "implied guarantee."
  (2021)

## Topic notes

- **Growth levers → constraint diagnostics.** The four-lever offer curriculum and the
  "first thing I look at is the offer stack" framing are a **proto-version of his later
  constraint/diagnostic framework** — here the offer (not the whole Core-Four/leads
  system) is presented as the master constraint. Relates to
  [[../topics/business/business-diagnostics]].
- **Proto LTGP:CAC.** The Door 3 vs. Door 1 reasoning (retention/frequency wins because
  CAC is already paid and each customer becomes worth 2x) is an **early, informal
  statement of the LTV/LTGP-to-CAC logic** he later formalizes — stated qualitatively,
  without the ratio notation. Relates to
  [[../topics/business/scaling-and-enterprise-value]].
- **No explicit Core Four / lead-getting framework here.** Lead generation is treated as
  downstream of the offer ("wherever the eyeballs are"); the named channels (cold
  outbound, cold email, ads, earned media, list) prefigure but do not yet formalize the
  Core Four.
- **Value equation** appears in its mature four-variable form and matches the
  *$100M Offers* definition, useful as an early dated anchor for that framework.
- The pricing "virtuous cycle" and price-to-value discrepancy feed pricing/retention
  topics under [[../topics/business/scaling-and-enterprise-value]].
