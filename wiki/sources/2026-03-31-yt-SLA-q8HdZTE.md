# How to Charge 10x More for the Same Product

## Summary

Alex restates the value equation behind his offers framework: increase the desired
outcome and perceived likelihood while reducing delay, effort, and sacrifice. He uses an
Acquisition.com PR investment to show how narrowing to fundraising customers reportedly
improved specificity, retention, response, and pricing.

## Key claims

- Alex self-reports learning in 2013 to make an offer customers would feel foolish
  refusing, shortly before opening his first gym.
- As of March 2026, he defines core value through dream outcome, perceived likelihood of
  achievement, time delay, and effort/sacrifice.
- Scarcity concerns limited units and urgency concerns limited time; both can enhance an
  offer without changing the underlying item.
- He self-reports that an invested PR company found 15% of customers bought its most
  expensive package and retained, then narrowed to fundraising clients and raised price
  tenfold; figures and outcomes are not independently verified.
- Specific targeting can simultaneously improve outbound response and enable more value
  for the selected customer because the service is redesigned around one use case.
- He calls the offer the highest-leverage business decision because it affects pricing,
  profit, marketing, sales, and delivery at once.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Offers are the terms of exchange."

> "Scarcity is a function of units, urgency is a function of time."

> "If we can't get enough people to say yes, we need to make the offer better."
