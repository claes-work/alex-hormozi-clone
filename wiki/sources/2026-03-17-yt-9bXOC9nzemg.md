# you've already achieved goals you said would make you happy

## Summary
A two-person conversation (Alex plus an interviewer, apparently Chris Williamson) built around a confrontational prompt read to Alex: "you've already achieved goals you said would make you happy." Alex takes it as a note-to-self about **hedonic adaptation**. He names the "happiness formula" everyone runs — *when/if X happens, I'll be happy* — and says he has so much evidence that hitting those targets "brought me nothing" that it's made him "a pretty existential nihilist." His resolution: joy for him lives in **pursuit**, not arrival. Building on an earlier point that anticipation is "the hot button on pleasure," he says the hot button on *joy* is the work itself — the "Rocky cut scene" of putting in reps — and that "once the presentation goes well I'll feel good" is a lie he tells himself; in fact he now half-dreads finishing because he won't get to do the work anymore and will have to pick a new target. His conclusion: set goals "really big, really far" so you can stay in pursuit as long as possible — "in pursuit is my button for enjoyment" — which reframes the outsider critique that he's "always working." He then offers two motivational/gratitude frames: (1) picture the man you want to become ("bulletproof" in marriage, business, etc.), ask what you'd have to put him through to make him — never easy wins, only toil just above your threshold — and, in hard moments, look in the "mental mirror" and think *I'm making you*; hell is "when you look at who you could have been and realize you're not that person." (2) His most powerful gratitude frame: imagine your 85-year-old self waking up in your 30-year-old body — no aching knees, clear head, energy, the person you love still young — which lets him "enjoy the mundane" and be present, if only for a few minutes at a time. The interviewer adds material on presence (a Sam Harris conversation, Conor McGregor "being present" at a weigh-in, an embodiment coach's peripheral-vision cue, and the opening of *Gladiator*), aiming at "enjoy being while you're in the process of becoming." Ends with the acquisition.com scaling-roadmap promo.
