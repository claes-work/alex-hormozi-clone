## Summary

Despite the "cookbook" title, this is a **literal recipe/cooking video** (a "part two"
by popular demand), not a business or systems metaphor. Alex demonstrates ~12
**high-protein, near-zero-prep, low-carb/low-fat** meals he personally eats — yogurt +
Crystal Light + protein cereal bombs, egg-white "shakes" with zero-sugar chocolate
syrup, salmon/cream-cheese open-face sandwiches, shrimp, lean-beef-and-cauliflower with
a homemade "secret sauce," tuna mustard bowls, jerky + string cheese snacks, and "bro
nachos." The tone is comedic and self-deprecating throughout.

## Key claims

- His eating philosophy: **maximize protein (~100 g per meal, ~200 g/day), minimize fat
  and carbs**, keep prep and dishes near zero, and stick with the same meals for long
  stretches. He is self-described "a lowfat guy."
- **Purity-of-protein heuristic:** he benchmarks foods against a scoop of whey (~120
  cal / ~24 g protein); the closer a food's calorie-per-gram-of-protein ratio, the
  "leaner"/purer it is (shrimp, tuna, egg whites, protein locks are near-pure).
- **Calorie-stretching tricks:** dip the utensil in sauce (put sauce on the underside,
  against the tongue) rather than mixing it in; weigh condiments on a scale instead of
  eye-measuring spoons; layer cereal on top in peelable layers to keep it crunchy.
- Egg whites are **ultra-pasteurized**, so they can be drunk raw or microwaved (no pan)
  — his fastest 100 g protein with zero prep.
- Practical "no prep" assumes some **batch prep exists** (e.g. lean ground beef cooked
  once a week), then meals are assembled from shelf-stable/protein staples.
