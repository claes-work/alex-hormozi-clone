# Stop Trying to Make Passive Income

## Summary
Alex Hormozi argues that people with little capital should invest in their own income-earning skills rather than chasing compounding returns on small sums. He reframes "I don't have time" as a lie (everyone has the 5–9 am and 5–9 pm windows), redefines patience as "figuring out what to do in the meantime," and recounts how he chose to skip business school and instead spend savings on building a business.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that a beginner with ~$1,000 gets far more by improving their skills than by trying to compound the money; adding 50% to your skill set lifts annual earnings ~50%, dwarfing the extra ~$100 from a better return.
- He claims young people often lose small investments by gambling on speculative assets (he uses Dogecoin as the example), erasing weeks of saved wages "in seconds."
- He notes stock returns aren't guaranteed — by his estimate, markets fall in roughly one out of four years.
- "I don't have time" is, per Alex, self-deception: everyone has the 5–9 am and 5–9 pm windows (~8 hours/day) even with a 40-hour job or full-time studies.
- He reframes comfort as costly: the thing that makes you comfortable today is what keeps you uncomfortable for a decade; being uncomfortable for a day buys comfort for decades.
- Work pays twice when starting out: you earn money while working, and you don't spend money while working — so savings stack with no drains.
- He redefines patience as "figuring out activities to do in the meantime" — you work to generate more capital to invest while earlier capital compounds.
- Biographical: Alex studied for and did well on the GMAT intending to attend business school, then calculated that ~2 years and ~$180,000 (plus forgone income) for an average ~$10,000/year earnings bump was a bad trade. He had saved ~$50,000 and chose instead to spend it learning to reach ~$10,000/month via his own business. (Self-reported.)

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "Learn how to make money before you start trying to learn how to make money make money."

> "If you learn how to add 50% to your skill set, it'll take whatever your annual earning is up by 50%. And that's going to be a hell of a lot more than 10."

> "When you say you don't have time, just immediately think to yourself, 'I'm lying. This is bullshit.'"

> "The thing that makes me comfortable today is the thing that's going to make me uncomfortable for a decade... if I could become uncomfortable for the day, I could be comfortable for decades."

> "Work makes you money in two ways. One, you work and you make money. And secondly, when you're working, you're not spending money."

> "Patience is figuring out activities to do in the meantime."

> "I think with this 50, I'll be able to spend it to learn enough to make 10 grand a month. And that's what I did."
