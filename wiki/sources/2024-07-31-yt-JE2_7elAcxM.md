# How to Sell Better than 99% Of People (4 HOUR ULTIMATE GUIDE)

## Summary

A four-hour compilation stitching together Alex Hormozi's best sales presentations
and workshop recordings into one "ultimate guide," delivered solo but drawn from
several settings (some polished to-camera, some live-audience mastermind sessions).
It moves through a philosophical "decision / power" framing of closing (helping
people confront the self-limiting arguments that keep them from buying), his 21
beliefs about selling, the nine things elite salespeople do differently, a live
metrics case study (show / offer / close / cash-collected / units), the CLOSER
framework, building an in-house sales team, and the six-C system for scaling and
drilling a team. Because it is Alex first-person throughout, the whole thing is
persona/voice-training material; it substantially overlaps the "Ultimate Sales
Training" video (StVqS0jD7Ls) but contributes distinct blocks noted below.

## Key claims (the sales curriculum)

### Decision / power framing (workshop opener)
- Selling is helping a prospect make a **decision to help themselves** — which does
  **not** mean buying from you. Keep the prospect, not the sale, as the priority.
- Buyers sit on an **emotional↔logical continuum**; emotion lowers the action
  threshold and gets the step, but **logic makes it stick** and makes a good
  customer. "We have a high moral responsibility to be rational" (invokes Charlie
  Munger).
- **Obstacle vs. objection:** an obstacle is a disagreement raised *before* you ask
  for the sale (easy to confront and destroy); an objection is disagreement *after*
  the ask (the "Red Zone" — all closing). Handle obstacles early so they never
  become objections.
- To buy, a prospect must want the goal and believe three things: the product will
  get them there **the way they want to get there**; that you and others will
  support them; and that it will work **for them specifically**.
- **Confront the self-limiting argument:** the reason someone gives themselves *not*
  to do the thing is usually the very reason they need to do it (no time / can't
  afford / depend on spouse). Whatever chain they're holding is the thing they're
  casting their power to.
- **Invest in yourself / time-tax of ignorance:** skills can't be taken (Persian
  family fled Iran, lost land and money; his father rebuilt from $1,000 on skills).
  You "buy time" by buying the lessons other people's mistakes taught them; the
  ignorance of not knowing how to make $1M/year is costing you $1M/year.

### 21 beliefs about selling (selected)
- People want to believe you; help their logical brain justify the decision they
  already want to make. Selling happens before the ask; closing happens after.
- Expect and plan for **no** — it is not failure, it is the job; if they'd already
  say yes, you aren't necessary. **No is the job.**
- If a price tag doesn't get a **gasp**, you didn't go high enough — then you can
  anchor down to a "beautiful" price ($100K first makes $1K a rounding error).
- Closing is a dance, not a fight — **sell from your back foot**; the goal is not
  to beat them into submission.
- Selling is a **transference of belief over a bridge of trust** — you must believe
  it to transfer it, and have trust to make the transfer. Hot→cold streaks come
  from losing belief in *why* you sell, not from losing skill.
- The person who cares most about the prospect wins the sale; be more convicted than
  they are about their own excuses. Record all your sales (study your hot streaks).

### Nine things elite salespeople do (three jobs: maximize opportunities, convert %, sustain it)
- **Availability was the single strongest predictor of total sales** (from Alon,
  ~4,000 appts/day). The rep with the most hours sells the most; work weekends.
- **Pull calls up:** drag later appointments into today (higher show + interest);
  fill freed slots; qualify-and-close-now or fill-a-later-slot (solar-rep tactic
  that 4×'d one rep's units).
- **Answer questions with questions; be like smoke** — never ask for the sale
  unless you already know they'll buy (lawyers don't ask questions they don't know
  the answer to). Educate the prospect toward their own conclusion; ask what they
  liked/didn't like about past solutions, then mirror only the liked features.
- **Breathe the script** (memorize it like the Bible so you can be fully present);
  be concise — bloated scripts grow from superstitious positive reinforcement
  (the "cough before the close" story). Commands vs. questions to break patterns
  ("tell me about your day").

### Metrics case study (before/after)
- Track **show rate, offer rate, close rate, % cash collected up front, units
  sold** — individually and team-wide, so you can locate the constraint (a rep
  gaming close rate by under-offering shows up in the data).
- Benchmark ~**70% show rate** for any appointment business; a starting 49% show
  rate is a 40%+ improvement opportunity — bigger than the S&P's ~9%/yr.

### CLOSER framework (E = explain away concerns)
- Memorize only two things going into the Red Zone: the **30-second stories** about
  what you sell, and the **obstacle overcomes**. Sit in the pain; if they haven't
  clarified why they need a solution, don't ask for the sale.
- Three big objections: **price** (you didn't articulate value), **decision-maker**
  (you erred earlier — rely on past agreements; support not permission), **stalls /
  "think about it"** ("what's your main concern?"). The B-decision: do you like the
  product, do you like us, do you have the money.
- First **48 hours** post-sale decide long-term belief — personalized video,
  handwritten card, gift.

### Building & scaling a team
- **Hire one to hire ten:** hire a sales *director* first — been-there-done-there,
  metric-driven (the quality/quantity/order of metrics someone volunteers reveals
  their competence), servant demeanor. **Core 4** to source: outreach, ads,
  content, recruiter — but ads/recruiters rarely land senior sales talent.
- **Hack conviction** to fix a team's tone unconsciously (read testimonials daily).
- **Six C's to scale in ≤7 days:** CLOSER sequence (question-based script),
  Consistent daily training (60 min/day: 25 min tonality drill, 5 min obstacle
  drill, 30 min call-listening — right/wrong/next-time), Call recordings (Gong),
  Communication cycles + one-piece feedback (golf-swing analogy), Cuts (cut fast —
  easier to take a 6 to a 9 than a 2 to a 5), Competition + career path (six-week
  cycles, mini-teams, deal-count milestones). Separate inbound/outbound and
  setters/closers.
