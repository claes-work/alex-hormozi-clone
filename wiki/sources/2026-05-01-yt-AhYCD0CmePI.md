# You Might Make More Money From Affiliate Links Than Clients

## Summary

Alex coaches a food-growing community owner with three years of personal experience. He
first pushes for a clearer promise by talking to the market and learning whether buyers
care most about saving money, avoiding chemicals, freshness, sustainability, or another
pain. Because the niche naturally requires equipment and supplies, he says the business
might monetize better through honest affiliate recommendations than through direct
clients.

## Key claims

- A founder should clarify the promise by talking to people in the niche instead of
  guessing the main pain point.
- Possible food-growing promises could include saving money, avoiding chemicals,
  freshness, or sustainability, but the market must confirm which matters.
- Alex recommends calls and community/forum conversations as the way to gather demand
  data.
- Product-heavy niches can monetize through affiliate links for supplies, equipment, and
  tools.
- Alex cites Garage Gym Reviews as an example of a trusted review business monetized by
  affiliate links.
- Affiliate trust requires honest reviews; do not recommend inferior products solely for
  commission.
