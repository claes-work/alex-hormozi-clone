# Helping an SEO Agency Get to $14M in Year 1

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a $7M SEO/web/PPC agency serving roofing, HVAC, and home-services companies. The content substantially duplicates the 2026-06-23 version of the same call: Alex uses sales velocity and LTV to estimate the agency's hypothetical max, challenges industry-average margin beliefs, and says AI should be an offensive operational advantage rather than a threat.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - SEO/web/PPC agency founder. His revenue, churn, pricing, margin, nearshore plan, and exit target are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Sales velocity times LTV gives a practical estimate of the business's current revenue ceiling if nothing changes.
- The founder's current metrics imply that the business is already on a path toward about $12M run-rate revenue.
- The key is increasing margin so the incoming revenue drops to the bottom line.
- Alex challenges the founder's 50% gross margin and says service businesses should aim far higher.
- Industry averages should not set the standard.
- AI and agent automation should replace nearshoring as the current operational answer.
- The agency should become the attacker with AI rather than fear AI as market risk.
