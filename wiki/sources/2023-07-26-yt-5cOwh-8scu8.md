# Make Money Online (Without Destroying Your Reputation)

## Summary

Solo talk (a preview from *$100M Leads*) on how to sell to your own audience/network without burning goodwill. Hormozi reframes GaryVee's "jab jab jab, right hook" into "give until they ask you," argues the audience — not any single piece of content — is the asset, and frames content as artificially shifting the demand curve while the owner controls supply to raise pricing power.

## Key claims

- Master the "gift-to-ask ratio": media companies have studied it (TV runs ~13 min ads per 47 min content; Facebook ~1 ad per 4 posts, roughly 3:1). His personal preference is far more conservative — ~30:1 give-to-ask — to keep growth compounding. (paraphrase)
- "Give in public and sell in private." Giving deposits goodwill; the cost of an ask is not money but goodwill (lost audience). (paraphrase)
- The audience is the asset, not the content. A video that disappears in three days still permanently adds ~300 people to the audience, provided you don't over-ask early. (paraphrase)
- Two ask formats: **integrations** (natural mentions, better for long-form, keep ≥3:1) and **intermittent** asks (better for short-form — e.g. one promo post per 10-11 posts, ≤3% frequency). (paraphrase)
- Deliberately "leaving money on the table" (declining early right-hooks/endorsements) pays off because the audience compounds; the same offer is worth 10-100x more 12-24 months later. (paraphrase)
- Content owners control supply and can inflate demand while cutting supply, driving price and profit up. (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "Instead of give give give ask I'd like to just change it to give until they ask you and that's when you know your audience is ready for you to make an offer."
- "The audience is the asset not the content you make."
- "Part of the game is quote leaving money on the table."
- "Operationalizing patience is just figuring out what you do in the meantime. You don't actually wait, you just figure out what you do in the meantime."
- "My goal is to always have significantly less supply than I have demand... that is the Holy Grail of what you want."
- "When we make content what we're actually doing is artificially shifting the demand curve in our favor."
