# Reflecting on Why I Was Wrong About Myself (Patience & Self Judgment)

## Summary

Alex Hormozi reflects on judging himself by evidence rather than feelings. He used to call himself impatient, but realized he could become patient by doing patient actions even when he did not feel patient. He rejects binary traits like patient/impatient or loyal/disloyal and treats character traits as continuums built by repeated proof. He connects this to courage, motivation, humility, fitness, and the proof threshold he needed before teaching gyms or fitness.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- People should judge themselves by the same evidence standard they use for others.
- Character traits are not binary; the better question is how much evidence supports the trait.
- A person can act patiently without feeling patient, and that action is evidence of patience.
- Repeated actions place "pennies on the scale" until the person has proof of the identity they want.
- Courage, motivation, and patience are action-defined, not feeling-defined.
- A person may never feel like they have a trait while still having strong evidence for it.
- Alex says his personal-development north star has been giving himself proof.
- Alex says he did not feel qualified to talk about gyms until he had six gyms, and did not feel qualified to talk about fitness despite a long six-pack period and state records until people kept asking.
