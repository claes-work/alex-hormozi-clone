# Alex Hormozi: Master these traits to become wealthy

## Summary

In this interview clip on the @ACQofficial channel, Alex Hormozi lays out his blueprint for someone starting a business from scratch and the traits needed to build wealth. He argues focus is the first requirement — pick one avatar, one product, one channel — and names three sequential skills (advertising, selling, product) that get a beginner to six figures. Consistency, formalized as his "Rule of 100," is what carries someone from six to seven figures. He then diagnoses why most people fail despite having the blueprint: fear (of judgment and lack of validation) and an inability to make short-term sacrifices for long-term achievement, rather than any missing skill.

## Key claims (the wealth/success traits)

- **Focus over opportunism.** When starting out, "everything looks like an opportunity" — all are opportunities but none work unless you pick one. You must say no to all the "other Mistresses."
- **Six figures = "sell something to someone."** Concretely: one avatar, one product, one channel. Don't try to be everywhere.
- **Pick one channel and one advertising method, run it consistently.** The channel is Facebook / Instagram / YouTube / Twitter / etc.; the method is one of cold outbound, content, paid ads, affiliates, or word of mouth.
- **Three skills, in order.** (1) Advertising — defined as "the process of making known." (2) Selling — closing people once they engage. (3) Product — the thing being advertised and sold.
- **Six ways to advertise.** He notes there are six methods of advertising (he lists five: cold outbound, content, paid ads, affiliates, word of mouth).
- **Seven figures = doing the same thing consistently.** Beginners are sporadic; consistency alone gets people toward $100k/month.
- **The Rule of 100.** 100 primary actions per day for 100 days — 100 minutes of content creation, OR 100 reach-outs, OR $100 of ad spend per day (pick one). Do it for 100 days and you'll be making six figures.
- **The main failure mode is fear, not skill.** Fear of judgment or of not being validated — a "second voice" that criticizes everything, which often isn't even real.
- **Resourcefulness asymmetry.** People find the money when an unexpected bill hits (tax, car, health) or when it's for someone else, but won't apply that same resourcefulness to make money for themselves.
- **Most people already know what to do.** Whether getting in shape, a better marriage, or more money — they know at a basic level (e.g. "eat fewer donuts and move more"); the gap is action and discipline.
- **The behavioral definition of the failure:** they do not make the short-term sacrifice of discomfort for the long-term achievement.
