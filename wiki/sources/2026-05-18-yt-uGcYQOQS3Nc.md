# The Only Sign of Success Nobody Talks About

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says people pursuing something meaningful go through a lonely chapter: they
no longer fit with old friends and do not yet have the outcomes to fit with a new group.
He frames hate and discomfort as signs that the goal has a real cost, and the cost is what
makes the goal worthwhile.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- People who want to do something meaningful often pass through a lonely chapter.
- In that chapter, they do not fit with old friends but lack the outcomes to fit with the
  next group.
- They consume tutorials and content, work on new skills, and wonder if it is worth it
  without signs of success.
- Alex says hate along the way can be a sign of success.
- The person should not bend the knee to hate and return to comfortable conformity.
- Once someone imagines an ideal, they compare themselves with that ideal and lose the
  excuse of ignorance.
- Knowing better makes bad choices feel more personally accountable.
- Every worthwhile goal has a cost.
- The cost is what makes the goal worthwhile; if the outcome were immediately available,
  it would be available to everyone and would not be valuable.
- People should decide whether they want to pay the price rather than resent the price
  tag.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's the lonely chapter."

> "The sign of success is the hate that you get along the way."

> "We can't resent the price tag."
