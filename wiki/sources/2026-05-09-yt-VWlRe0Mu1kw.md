# The Real Reason You're Not Getting Work Done

## Summary

Alex answers a question about avoiding rabbit holes by defining focus as subtraction. If
someone wants to do one thing, the answer is to stop doing everything that is not that
thing. He says work becomes interesting when it is the only alternative to boredom, so
his environment is designed to remove faster-feedback attention sinks and make work the
natural default.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-09)

- Alex says one prevents business rabbit holes by focusing on the problem in front of
  them.
- He defines focus as subtraction.
- He says a hypothetically perfectly focused person would do only one thing.
- He says the practical answer to doing one thing is to stop doing everything that is not
  the thing.
- He says if a person were in a white room with only the work, the work would become the
  most interesting thing available.
- He says work becomes more interesting than boredom when there are no alternatives.
- He says attention alternatives win because they have shorter, faster feedback loops.
- He says he is militant about removing attention sinks so work becomes the only way out
  of boredom.
- He frames this as environment arrangement rather than heroic willpower.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Focus is subtraction."

> "Just stop doing everything that's not the thing."

> "Work becomes more interesting than boredom."
