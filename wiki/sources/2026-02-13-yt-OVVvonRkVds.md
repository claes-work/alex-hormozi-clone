# What Book Should I Write Next?

## Summary
In this short clip, Alex Hormozi thinks out loud about which book to write next, after having already written three others over the preceding years. He lays out three candidate topics and asks the audience to weigh in, noting their input might shift his decision by "like a 1% shift."

The first candidate is a book on profit — the many tactics and hacks for driving profit in a business, tentatively framed around pricing. The second is a sales book, which he says he has avoided because he feels he knows too much about the topic to fit it into a single book, though he suspects that very overload is the reason he should write one. The third candidate is a broader personal-development book about "operationalizing" amorphous terms and character traits — defining what words like trust, loyalty, integrity, guilt, shame, and trauma actually mean in operational terms. He references a livestream from two or three weeks prior where he broke mental toughness into four components (tolerance, fortitude, resilience, adaptability) as one demonstration of this approach, and says he already has a full outline for this book, of which mental toughness would only be one chapter.
