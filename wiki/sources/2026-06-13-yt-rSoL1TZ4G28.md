# How to Escape the Revenue Roller Coaster

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex and Leila advise a founder who helps business owners get funding and is stuck between selling/marketing and delivery. Leila says to delegate revenue last: the founder should hire for delivery first because revenue generation is closest to the money in this business. Alex adds nuance that the revenue-proximate activity depends on the business, but in this case promotion/deals should remain with the founder. They also discuss attraction offers for cold outbound, where Alex leans toward a decoy offer rather than a broad giveaway.

## Key claims (Alex/Leila advice, attributed)

- **Leila:** Delegate revenue last; hold onto the work closest to revenue the longest.
- **Alex:** The activity closest to revenue depends on the business; in a product-led software case it might be product, but in this funding-service case it is promotion/deals.
- Delivery appears process-oriented and therefore easier to delegate first.
- Giveaways work better with one-to-many channels like content or paid ads than with cold outbound.
- For cold outbound in this kind of funding offer, Alex would consider a decoy offer: bring prospects in for something free, then explain that the real solution is a broader, higher-value system.
- ACQ Ventures uses diligence and weighted scorecards across founder, business, market, and economy factors.
