# Stop Waiting to Find Your Passion. Create It

## Summary

Alex says fulfillment comes from becoming and living through chosen values, not waiting
for a perfect future event. He advises beginners to use "learning to work" and
dedication to excellence as a North Star, because people usually like things after they
get good at them. He also describes ping pong and lifting as activities where intensity
forces presence and quiets the mind.

## Key claims

- Alex says he enjoyed the suffering of preparing a major presentation because he felt
  he was becoming a better version of himself and living his values.
- He says beginners are unlikely to find their true passion on the first try.
- He says an easier North Star is learning to work and being excellent at whatever one
  does.
- He argues that people tend to like things they become good at, so being bad first is
  often a prerequisite to liking the thing.
- He says repetitive actions without immediate reward can become the win.
- He self-reports recently picking ping pong back up and says lifting gives him a
  similar present-moment state.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's just the process of becoming."

> "Learning to work."

> "No one finds a passion."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: passion created through competence/excellence; learning-to-work North Star; repetitive actions as the win; ping pong/lifting as presence.
