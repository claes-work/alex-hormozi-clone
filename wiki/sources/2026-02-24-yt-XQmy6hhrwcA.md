# The Reason Why You Haven't Hit Your Goals...

## Summary

A short solo clip in which Alex Hormozi argues that the mental energy people
spend on envy, comparison, and tearing others down (even privately) is the same
energy that, redirected inward, would let them reach their own goals. He offers
several "hard truths": no one is doing as well as you assume, you don't win by
beating rivals but by growing into your potential until they become irrelevant,
and your real threat is a mediocre version of yourself. He extends the point to
business: the operators who get copied most are the ones who ignore their
copycats and obsess over the customer — because customer focus is the thing that
actually works, so imitators copy it. The clip closes with a promo for his free
acquisition.com/roadmap (a 10-stage zero-to-$100M+ map broken down by eight
business functions).

## Key claims (2026-02-24, paraphrased)

- If people worked for their goals as hard as they envy others for achieving
  them, they would already have achieved those goals themselves. (paraphrase)
- Hard truth 1: no one is doing as well as you think they are, so by comparison
  you are actually better off than you believe. (paraphrase)
- Hard truth 2: you don't win by beating people; you win by growing into your
  potential and letting rivals shrink into irrelevance as a consequence.
  (paraphrase)
- Hard truth 3: your biggest threat is not your competition — it's a mediocre
  version of you that never realized what you could become. (paraphrase)
- Another person doing well changes nothing about your own reality. (paraphrase)
- The people who get copied most by competitors are the ones who ignore the
  competitors copying them — because they focus on the customer, which is the
  thing that actually works, so others imitate it. (paraphrase)
- The real threat is that no one copies you at all — because that means you're
  doing nothing. (paraphrase)
- The effort spent comparing yourself to others and tearing them down (even only
  in your mind) is finite energy; redirecting it into improving yourself is the
  only one of the two that will actually help you. (paraphrase)
- Promo: acquisition.com/roadmap is a free 10-stage roadmap from zero to
  $100M+ that fewer than 1% of companies finish, broken down by eight business
  functions with the constraint symptoms and the steps taken to graduate each
  stage; validated across software, physical products, service, and
  brick-and-mortar businesses. (paraphrase)
