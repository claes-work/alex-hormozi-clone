# 10x Revenue with 1 New Sales Process (You Can Steal It)

## Summary

Solo video in which Alex Hormozi lays out his "diagnostic sales process" — a way to
take a purely transactional, one-off sale and repackage it as a custom, goal-based,
recurring sale without changing what the business actually delivers. He illustrates it
with a brick-and-mortar chain Acquisition.com bought (grown from 14 to 32 locations,
average revenue per customer from $200 to $800, recurring revenue on the target product
line 10x'd from ~$20k to ~$250k/month) and with parallel examples from the gym/weight-loss
and aesthetics (filler/Botox) worlds. The core mechanic: get the customer to self-identify
their current state and desired state, position price as the vehicle to close that gap,
and default them into prepayment or a payment plan that recurs.

## Key claims (the diagnostic sales process)

- **The value came from repackaging, not from changing delivery.** "We didn't change
  what we delivered we only changed how we presented it." Same gym workouts, same
  aesthetic services — only the sale changed.
- **Diagnostic sale sits between transactional and enterprise/relational sales.** He
  took a purely transactional sale and moved it toward custom, bridging a
  standardized product with a personalized-feeling sale.
- **The framework's spine: current → obstacle → desired.** Ask where they are now,
  where they want to be, and what's in the way; the price is tied to closing that gap.
- **Five outlined steps:** (1) secret shop the business; (2) gather information /
  identify the constraint (here: an offer/packaging constraint); (3) — folded into the
  current/desired/obstacle diagnosis; (4) "kill zombies" — anticipate objections
  before you present price, because it's much harder to sell after the price is on the
  table; (5) implement the diagnostic process on an ongoing basis.
- **The in-sale sequence:** pre-sale questionnaire (agitates the pain, elevates its
  importance) → collect info **and credit card up front** ("just standard procedure")
  before selling anything → current / desired / obstacle → present price in relation to
  goal (with a guarantee tied to following the steps) → offer a prepay incentive.
- **Make the customer set the goal.** Let them self-identify on a scale/chart ("the
  scale called you fat not me"; the Yogurt Land self-serve pricing analogy) so the
  price and the decision feel like theirs.
- **Sell the goal, not the membership.** In weight loss: 200 lb → 140 lb goal, ~1.5
  lb/week → 45 weeks × $99/week = $4,500, guaranteed. "I'm not selling a membership
  anymore I'm selling exactly what they want and putting a price tag on it."
- **Sales step-downs (offer ladder):** prepay in full (biggest discount, ~20%) →
  partial / half-down (smaller discount, ~10%, plus credit-card or third-party
  financing) → continuity / payment plan → if all else fails, just book the next visit.
  "Everyone buys something."
- **Present price at the highest (full payment-plan) rate first** so it anchors high and
  prepaying reads as a benefit — instead of anchoring low then adding interest, which
  makes you "the bad guy" (like a bank).
- **Transition to recurring** by appending an automatic recurrence at the end of a
  payment plan at the same price — what you *call* it (payment plan vs. membership)
  changes retention even though the cash flow is identical. Two friends' examples: a
  $300/mo membership reframed as a $10k program with 36-month financing took LTV from
  3 to 8 months; an agency cut churn by moving to payment plans.
- **Upsell pricing rule:** an upsell should be ~5x the current price. Given the 80/20
  split (top 20% have ~5x spending power), if 20% take a 5x upsell you double average
  ticket. The acquired business's ~$20 upsell on a ~$200 customer was near-worthless.
- **Secret shop your own business** and be horrified — the real sales process on the
  floor is rarely the one you think you have.
- **Simplicity enables low-skill labor.** Automate the point of sale so reps can't
  advance without completing each checkbox, forcing script adherence; drill the script
  until they can say it "without thinking about it."
- **Results:** recurring revenue on the product line 9% → 60%; memberships 0% → 30%;
  visits per customer ~1–2 → 4–6; ~$20k/mo → ~$250k/mo added and still growing.
