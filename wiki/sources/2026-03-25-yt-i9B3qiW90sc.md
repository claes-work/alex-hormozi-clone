# Helping an Indoor Golf Business Scale

## Summary
In this short clip, a business owner who franchises five indoor golf-simulator locations (with FDDs to scale to 12) tells Alex Hormozi that his seasonal business suffers from low utilization in the summer off-season. Hormozi advises leaning the messaging into the two strongest customer benefits — speed (a fast round of golf) and comfort (air-conditioned, consistent conditions) — and warns against ever calling any period the "off-season." He closes with his standard scaling-workshop pitch.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that a seasonal business should never label any period the "off-season," because doing so makes everyone (including staff and customers) treat it as such, causing continuous lost revenue.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends leading the messaging with two customer benefits he considers strong: a time component (a much faster round of golf) and a comfort component (air-conditioning, consistent weather), because "everyone wants faster and everyone wants things to be easier."
- As of March 2026, Alex speculates that locations in the hottest summer climates may benefit most from the indoor/air-conditioned angle ("get out of the summer heat").
- As of March 2026, Alex draws a distinction between "branding" and "messaging," telling the owner what he described is better called the messaging.
- Caller claims (unverified): he owns five indoor golf-simulator franchise locations across the country with FDDs in place to scale to 12; his summer utilization "drops right off the table"; customers value the shorter time to play indoors and the consistent weather.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "If you're in a seasonal business, don't call it the off-season, cuz then everyone's going to treat it like the off-season. You just lose money all the time."

> "You have a time component and you have a comfort component, which I actually think are pretty strong. Everyone wants faster and everyone wants things to be easier. And so you do both."

> "I'd be like, want to do 18 in under 30 minutes and it'd be air-conditioned with a drink in hand. Like, I mean, you could sell me."

> "Instead of branding, I'd just say this is the messaging. Yeah, just for a delineation."
