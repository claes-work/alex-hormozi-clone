# How We Trained Sales Teams Across Thousands of Gyms

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a $6.6M business selling sales coaching and lead generation to financial advisors. The owner has shifted from lead gen toward sales coaching because clients cannot close. Alex explains how Gym Launch trained client teams through a daily "boiler room" role-play cadence and then reframes the churn issue around activation, avatar quality, consumables, and separating one-time skill acquisition from ongoing value.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Gym Launch trained client sales teams through daily group role-play, not only one-on-one coaching.
- Team sales training can rotate through intro, discovery, offer, objections/looping, and a fifth hot-topic day.
- Education/coaching continuity fails when the ongoing offer is merely the same front-end skill after the client has learned it.
- One-time skill acquisition can be worth much more than ongoing consumables, but the ongoing price should reflect what the client still needs after acquiring the skill.
- If activated clients stick, the main problem becomes activation speed and avatar selection.
- Better-fit avatars may increase CAC but fix retention and engagement.
- Segmenting customers requires observable demographics/quantifiables plus behaviors that predict activation.
- Gym Launch created fast time-to-value by emailing the gym's list and making a sale in the first seven days.
