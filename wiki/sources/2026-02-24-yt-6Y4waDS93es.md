# Pain Is the Price of Progress

## Summary

A short solo clip in which Alex argues that pain is the unavoidable cost of growth.
His central claim: the fastest growth periods are usually the most miserable, so anyone
who wants to progress should get used to pain. He grounds this in the mechanics of
growth itself — building a muscle means stretching and breaking it down (painful);
growing pains in a child come from growing too fast; a company expanding is "supposed to
be good stuff" but is no less painful for it. Elite athletes don't get stronger during
easy workouts. His conclusion is a stated trade-off: you cannot both want progress and
live an easy life — the two conflict. The clip closes with a promo for Alex's free
"10-stage roadmap from zero to $100M+" (at acquisition.com/roadmap), which he says he
broke down across eight business functions and multiple business types (software,
physical products, service, brick-and-mortar).

## Key claims (2026-02-24, paraphrased unless quoted)

- Pain is the price of progress; the fastest growth periods are often the most miserable,
  so if you want to progress you should get used to pain. (paraphrase)
- Growth is inherently painful because it means stretching past your point of comfort —
  building muscle means stretching and breaking it down; growing pains come from growing
  too fast. (paraphrase)
- A company expanding is technically good but "doesn't make it any less painful." (paraphrase)
- Elite athletes don't get stronger during easy workouts; progress requires accepting the
  attached price of pain. (paraphrase)
- You cannot both want progress and live an easy life — these two things conflict. (paraphrase)
- (Promo) Alex offers a free 10-stage roadmap from $0 to $100M+ that fewer than 1% of
  companies finish, broken down by eight business functions — the constraint at each stage,
  its symptoms, and the steps taken to graduate — validated across software, physical
  products, service, and brick-and-mortar businesses. (paraphrase)
