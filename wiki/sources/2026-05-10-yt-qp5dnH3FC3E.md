# Get U S  Clients From Anywhere by Doing This

## Summary

Alex answers a question about entrepreneurs in India who want U.S. clients. He says the
first leverage point is speaking English without an accent and presenting online in a
way that gives U.S. buyers no obvious reason to price the seller as offshore. He adds
that objections about location are usually sales-frame and trust issues, not proof that
the seller must physically leave the country.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says the number-one leverage point for getting U.S. clients from India is
  speaking English without an accent.
- He says a seller can stay in India if the buyer encounters the seller online in
  English and perceives the value as U.S.-market value.
- He recommends making profiles and videos sound and read like they are meant for the
  U.S. market.
- He says objections about charging U.S. prices from India are sales issues if the seller
  loses trust or frame.
- He says buyers pay based on perceived value, risk, and what share of the expected gain
  they are willing to give the seller.
- He says someone with an Indian accent can still sell the value, but it will likely be
  harder.
