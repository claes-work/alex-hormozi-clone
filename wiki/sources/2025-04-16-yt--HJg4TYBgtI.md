# Watch These 51 Minutes if You Want to Grow Your Business in 2026 (Dave Ramsey Interview)

## Summary

Alex interviews Dave Ramsey again, this time on Ramsey Solutions' profit centers, new-project testing, leadership structure, in-person work, family-office separation, a failed debit-card project, and Ramsey's five stages of business scaling. Dave supplies most of the frameworks and company examples. Alex contributes useful frames around "who does new," distraction risk, trust as the speed of decisions, and his own Acquisition.com family-office structure.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2025-04-16 - New initiatives**: Alex says many entrepreneurs want to do more new things, but the risk is team whiplash and neglecting the main game.
- **2025-04-16 - Speed of trust**: Alex says the speed of a company is based on the speed of trust, because trust allows faster decisions and less bureaucracy.
- **2025-04-16 - Acquisition.com structure**: Alex says he and Leila have run almost everything inside Acquisition.com, including buildings and apartment complexes through entity structures and the team that manages them.
- **2025-04-16 - All-cash acquisitions**: Alex says Acquisition.com had done all-cash deals up to that point when discussing debt in business acquisition.
- **2025-04-16 - Debt risk question**: Alex frames debt as something that needs a risk-adjusted view, especially in private business acquisitions.

## Context / guest-attributed claims

- Dave Ramsey says Ramsey Solutions has about 14 profit centers and a 650,000-square-foot campus.
- Dave says new ideas must be defended by ROI, margin, leadership bandwidth, and opportunity cost; Ramsey Solutions tests and iterates before killing projects.
- Dave describes a special-projects incubator for orphan ideas and broken business areas.
- Dave says Ramsey Solutions separates personal/family-office investing from the operating company and uses separate entities for real-estate risk and estate planning.
- Dave describes killing a Ramsey debit-card project after fraud and bank-risk exposure made the downside potentially much larger than the sunk cost.
- Dave outlines five business stages: treadmill, Pathfinder, Trailblazer, Peak Performer, and Legacy, with six drivers such as personal growth, purpose, people, and profit planning.
