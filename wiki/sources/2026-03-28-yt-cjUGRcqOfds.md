# Stop Getting Leads. Start Getting Lead Getters.

## Summary
In a live coaching exchange, Alex Hormozi advises an insurance business owner (infinite-banking niche, 29 agents) who is deciding whether to keep being the face who feeds leads to his agents, or to go all-in on recruiting and training more agents. Hormozi argues the higher-leverage move is to build a "lead-getter" machine: recruit agents and install a training system that reliably teaches them to self-generate their own leads. He frames insurance as a demand-constrained (not supply-constrained) business where the largest companies are fundamentally recruiting machines. He closes by referencing his "Leads Book" scenario framework to illustrate why acquiring lead-getters compounds output at the same level of personal effort.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex advises that recruiting and training more agents ("lead getters") is significantly higher leverage than personally generating leads to feed existing agents.
- As of March 2026, Alex holds that the true limiter of such a business is the ability to teach agents to reliably self-generate their own leads; solve that and "the sky's the limit."
- As of March 2026, Alex says the biggest insurance companies in the world are that big because they are fundamentally recruiting machines, not because of superior lead generation.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames insurance as demand-constrained rather than supply-constrained ("you can sell as much insurance as you want"), so growth comes from adding and enabling sellers.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends the owner keep his existing content brand but alternate the calls-to-action — some aimed at insurance customers, some at recruiting agents — to "double dip" without changing the underlying content strategy or killing existing lead flow.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises teaching agents whatever single acquisition method has the highest success rate across the most people (cold calling, in-person networking, or a combination), rather than defaulting to what worked for the founder personally.
- Alex references his "Leads Book" scenario model (cited on-screen as "175") contrasting getting leads (scenario one) vs. getting lead-getters (scenario three), where new agent "nodes" generate extra customers at the same founder effort.
- Caller-reported (unverified): the owner has 29 agents (20 currently cold-calling real estate investors), 15,000 YouTube subscribers, 12,000 Instagram followers, 6,000 Facebook followers, and roughly 15% of leads come from online with the rest from referrals and networking.
- Caller-reported (unverified): he reread 100M Offers and 100M Leads, applied the value equation, and concluded real estate investors were the highest-value lead segment to target.
