# The Realization That Reshaped My Approach To Business

> Source: YouTube (@AlexHormozi), "The Realization That Reshaped My Approach To Business", 2021-01-19
> [[../../index]]

## Summary

Prompted by a conversation with Ed Turney, Hormozi recounts how a "chip on his shoulder" —
a vendetta to beat gym-space guru Bedros Keuilian — drove his growth but poisoned his mental
well-being. The reshaping realization: when he surpassed his rival, the win felt empty and,
crucially, his success had zero effect on the rival's life, proving his "enemy" was never
harmed by him and that the market is far larger and less zero-sum than competitors assume.

## Key claims (paraphrase; dated 2021-01-19)

- **"Everyone roots for the American dream until it comes true."** (2021) He wrote an essay
  by that theme during his gym years; clients cheered him while he slept on the floor, then
  turned ("too good for us now") once he succeeded and stepped out of the business.
- **A chip on the shoulder drives you but doesn't make you happy.** (2021) He set a goal to
  beat Bedros Keuilian, telling himself stories about the rival wronging people to stay
  "amped up" and angry.
- **Surpassing the rival felt empty — and the rival was unaffected.** (2021) He believes his
  coaching program (Gym Launch) became bigger than the rival's, but found the rival still
  wealthy, pursuing other ventures, and completely unaffected; Hormozi was likely "not even
  a blip on the radar."
- **The vendetta only poisoned himself.** (2021) "The only person it poisoned was me" — one
  of the most humbling and freeing moments of his life.
- **Use anger to fuel yourself, never to attack.** (2021) Throwing shade burns bridges and
  reputation; people at the top talk to each other and share deal diligence. He references
  the Michael Jordan documentary (inventing slights others never said) as the two-sided
  nature of the chip.
- **People remember how you made them feel, not who you insulted.** (2021) Habitually
  speaking negatively about others makes people associate the negativity with *you* — a
  persuasion-book lesson he applied deliberately.
- **Markets are rarely winner-take-all / zero-sum.** (2021) Most businesses can't even handle
  existing demand; different people are attracted to different providers by psychographics.
  He notes his own audience is "81% male."
- **Abundance shift, applied to scaling.** (2021) After the realization he began calling
  competitors offering "how can I help you grow?"; helping others didn't hurt his success but
  massively improved his mental well-being. "The ocean's very very big."
