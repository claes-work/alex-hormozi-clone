# Do You Need A Mentor?

## Summary

Alex answers a viewer question — "Do mentors matter?" — by reframing it as: what is
actually *required* to win, and is a mentor one of those things? His answer: no, a mentor
is not required, but learning from people ahead of you is. He recasts the whole idea as
**"models, not mentors"** — you don't need a formal mentor relationship, you need to
*model* the behavior of people who've already won so you can win faster. Everything he
does, he says, ladders up or down to **behavior change**: increasing the likelihood that
someone (a stranger, an employee, himself) takes a desired action. This lens lets him
dismiss "surface-level fluff" like manifestation — he argues the content and the outreach
did the work, not the "vibes."

He describes how he learns by proxy from figures ahead of him (Elon Musk, Bezos, Zuckerberg),
consuming everything they put out to study their *decision-making process* and apply it to
his own context. He closes with the origin story of his first gym: quitting his job, driving
across the country, and showing up unannounced at "Seven Figure Sam's" gym to be mentored —
then paying to join Sam's community of gym owners, where in a single day of feedback he
absorbed years of other people's mistakes (pricing, equipment sourcing, square footage,
lease rates). The video ends with a plug for the free acquisition.com/roadmap.

This overlaps heavily with his existing **"buy your way up / proximity / pay for speed"**
learning thread (see [[../topics/mindset/mindset]]). The distinct contribution here is the
★ **"models, not mentors"** framing and the explicit reduction of all learning to *behavior*.

## Key claims
_(All dated 2026-02-17; paraphrased unless quoted.)_

- ★ **"Models, not mentors."** You don't need a mentor; you need to learn from people ahead
  of you. The right question isn't "who mentors me" but "what can I *model* about their
  behavior so it changes my behavior and increases the likelihood I win faster."
- A mentor is **not required** to win. You can figure everything out through trial and error
  on your own — you *will* eventually get there. The only real variable is **time**.
- Whether to pay for mentorship comes down to **how valuable your time is to you** — would
  you pay in time or money to skip forward five years and avoid five years of mistakes?
- Humanity advances by **standing on forefathers' shoulders** — we pick up the baton where
  the last generation left off rather than re-deriving everything (light bulbs, the internet).
- ★ Everything he does **ladders up or down to behavior change** — training employees,
  advertising, sales — it's all just increasing the likelihood a person takes a desired
  action. This framing strips away confusing "surface-level fluff."
- He is skeptical of **manifestation / "energy and frequencies"** thinking: a meeting happens
  because you produced content consistently and someone did the outreach — not because of
  "vibes." His test: remove the vibes and the outcome still happens; remove the content +
  outreach and it doesn't — so those are the causal core.
- His method for learning from **heroes ahead of him** (Elon, Bezos, Zuckerberg): consume
  everything they publish or are interviewed on, and study their **decision-making process**
  relative to what they did, then apply it to his own context in terms of behavior.
- **Learn in whatever way is fastest for you.** There's no single required path.
- He has "always been willing to pay in **whatever currency was required** to get knowledge
  he doesn't have" — time or money.
- **Proximity / community over solo mentorship in practice:** the highest-leverage move in
  his gym launch wasn't one mentor but paying into a *room of operators* who had already made
  the mistakes; in a single meetup they corrected his pricing, equipment sourcing, square
  footage, and lease-rate assumptions — collapsing years of trial and error into a day.
