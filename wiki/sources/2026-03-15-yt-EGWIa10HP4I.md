# Don't Open a New Location Until You Do This

## Summary
Live coaching clip (brick-and-mortar/facility owner). Alex lays out the test for when to open a second location. First, the current single location must be genuinely dialed in — good enough that the owner doesn't want to build out more at that site. Then comes what he calls **"scale zero"**: getting the owner to do *zero* in the business while it continues to run "at the same or better" than when the owner was running it. Only once the business runs without the owner do you have a **duplicable model** worth cloning. The mistake he warns against is the common sequence: owner makes money working *in* the business, assumes "this is working," pours the cash and all their time into a second location — and then the numbers collapse. He models it: revenue drops ~20% and margin ~25% at the immature second site, so profit craters, while overhead has doubled and the new location grows slower than the first did (new staff, no accumulated word-of-mouth, none of the years of sweat equity). Net result: working twice as hard for less money. His belief rule: **expand only when you have more money than you know what to do with AND more time than you know what to do with** — never expand *because* the current facility isn't making enough. He flags this is a mistake he made himself. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (the $100M scaling roadmap quiz + optional Vegas call).

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex's precondition for expansion: the first location must first be scaled to the point where it's "really good for this facility" and the owner no longer wants to build out more there.
- As of March 2026, the real gate before duplicating is **"scale zero"** — engineering the owner out so they do zero in the business while it operates "at the same or better" than when they ran it; only then is the model duplicable.
- As of March 2026, the classic failure sequence: owner makes money working in the business, assumes it's working, then reinvests all the cash *and* all their time into a second location.
- As of March 2026, his illustrative economics of a premature second site: revenue drops ~20% and margin ~25%, so profit falls to almost nothing while overhead doubles — the owner ends up "working twice as much and making less money."
- As of March 2026, a new location grows slower than the first did because it has new staff, no accumulated word-of-mouth, and none of the "years and years of sweat effort" invested in the original.
- As of March 2026, his expansion rule (framed as belief): expand only when "you're making more money than you know what to do with and you have more time on your hands than you know what to do with" — not because the current facility isn't making enough.
- As of March 2026, Alex says he knows this is the wrong way because he did it himself.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "The next step is actually me doing what we call scale zero, which is how do we get you to do zero in the business and then have the business still continue to operate at the same or better than it was with you operating it. Once that happens, then we have the duplicable model."

> "They went from, you know, making a lot of money to making almost nothing, but now they doubled their overhead… And all of a sudden, now you're working twice as much and making less money. That's what happens most times and that's what sucks."

> "That is how so many people think about it and I only say I know that because I did it. And it's the wrong way to do it."
