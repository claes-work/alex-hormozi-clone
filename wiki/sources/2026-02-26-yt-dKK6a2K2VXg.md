# How I Balance Working With My Wife

## Summary

In this short interview clip, Alex Hormozi describes how he and his wife and business
partner Leila Hormozi combine marriage and running companies together. He recounts that
they began working together essentially from their first date, that they deliberately
"don't try to balance" the two domains but instead flag issues to each other as they
arise, and that they divide decisions by domain expertise while talking through big
strategic calls until they agree. He also tells the story of their single significant
business-related argument — his decision to build a software company against Leila's
objection — which he now concedes was a mistake in timing. The material is Alex-attributed
throughout; the interviewer's questions are conversational prompts only.

## Key claims (paraphrase; dated 2026-02-26)

- [2026-02-26] On their first date, Alex and Leila talked only about business for ~4 hours,
  and he told her that if things didn't work out romantically she should quit her job and
  work for him "no matter what." He repeated the pitch every day for ~21 days until she
  agreed after 3 weeks.
- [2026-02-26] They had an intimate working relationship before an intimate dating/romantic
  relationship, and had to learn the personal side afterward.
- [2026-02-26] They have never had a business "fallout"; the only falling-outs have been on
  the relationship side, not the business side.
- [2026-02-26] Their approach to balancing marriage and business is deliberately not to
  balance it: when one of them senses they aren't having a good time, they simply ask for
  more "us time," the other corrects, and they let things keep going without over-judging it.
- [2026-02-26] Decisions are split by domain ownership — e.g., Leila's call on hiring/talent
  she knows better; Alex's call on marketing/brand angle — and each defers to the other's
  domain. On big strategic decisions they talk it through until they agree.
- [2026-02-26] They have had only one big business-related argument in their entire marriage.
- [2026-02-26] That argument was over building a software company while Gym Launch (his B2B
  gym-licensing business) and Prestige Labs (their supplement company) were doing well.
  Leila thought it was a terrible idea; Alex overrode her ("steamrolled"), deployed himself
  onto the software company, and spread the team thin.
- [2026-02-26] Alex now concludes Leila was right: the software company did okay, but the
  opportunity cost of taking his eye off the two well-performing businesses was large. The
  idea may have been good but the timing was too early — they lacked the infrastructure and
  team. Leila never rubbed the correct call in his face.
- [2026-02-26] He characterizes their relationship as "super traditional" on marriage and
  "very modern" on business.
- [2026-02-26] Biographical/character detail on Leila: an early mentor of Alex's, when Alex
  was dating her, advised that most partners cost you money while she makes you money, and
  told him to check whether his "stats" were up. Leila is described as hardcore about
  fitness, with an ~11-year unbroken MyFitnessPal food-logging streak that she eventually
  chose to end deliberately.
