# Helping a Business Consultant Niche Down

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client runs a plastic-surgery-business consulting firm doing $1.2M/year and wants $5M in three years (an "arbitrary" number Alex validates as normal). She does everything — new-practice startups, business assessments, and ongoing fractional-COO-style consulting — and reflexively wants to fix every problem she finds (HR, hiring, staffing), which spreads her thin. Alex diagnoses this as the classic 1–3M-range constraint: people good at one thing tend to be good at many things and want to help in all of them, but scaling requires **getting narrower on avatar and more specific on products/services**. His probing questions surface the leverage: the HR/hiring/medical-staffing line is the most repeatable, needs the least high-level consulting manpower, makes the most money long-term, and every practice needs it — so he pushes her toward **medical staffing as the primary offer** ("you sell time to people who have none, and they have money"). On operations, he reframes the delivery: her assessment is a 2-day on-site plus 60 days of implementation for $15k (modeled on Gym Launch's original on-site format); Alex says raise the price, **map pricing to headcount**, and drop the on-site if it isn't needed — clients "just care that the problem gets solved" — pricing the same outcome at $35–50k for this avatar. He anchors value in the buyer's own units: plastic surgeons denote things in number of surgeries (his father is a plastic surgeon), so frame it as "one day of work so I can give you 20 back forever." On growth, he notes conferences are her real acquisition channel (referrals/word-of-mouth are ancillary) — she speaks at 8/year and travels 3 weeks a month, so doing a conference every other week could hit $5M run-rate in ~12 weeks. On whether to stay strictly plastic surgeons or take med spas, Alex delivers his key niche principle: **niching down matters for operational efficiency, messaging, and sales motion — but if the delivery mechanism doesn't change, you don't need to narrow the avatar**; you can run multiple acquisition angles with different hooks/messaging over the same back-end delivery. Ends with the standard scaling-roadmap promo (acquirethis.com/roadmap).

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex says being "all over the place" on products/services is super common between $1M and $3M in revenue, because people good at one thing tend to be good at many and want to help in every way — but this range is exactly where you must get narrower on avatar and more specific on products/services.
- As of March 2026, Alex's niche-down test: find the thing you do that is very valuable, takes less time, and is more repeatable than your other work — that is the candidate to build the business around.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the right offer to scale is the one that is most repeatable, needs the least high-level (founder) manpower to deliver, makes the most money long-term, and that every customer needs — in this case medical staffing/HR ("you sell time to people who have none, and they have money").
- As of March 2026, Alex says it is not the business's job to satisfy the founder's intellectual curiosity or emotional needs; its job is to serve customers — if it chased the founder's interests it would change every day and give customers a horrible experience. Growth comes from repeating successful actions and doing the boring work ("boring businesses make lots of money").
- As of March 2026, Alex's operational reframe: raise the price of the core service, map pricing to headcount, and drop delivery steps (like a 2-day on-site) the client doesn't actually need — buyers "just care that the problem gets solved," not the mechanism.
- As of March 2026, Alex says the same assessment offer priced at $15k could sell for $35–50k for this avatar, because value should be framed in the buyer's own units (plastic surgeons think in number of surgeries: "it's four surgeries… one day of work so I can give you 20 back forever").
- As of March 2026, Alex says the real acquisition channel is usually the one that drives the needle (here: speaking at conferences); referrals and word-of-mouth happen ancillary. Doubling conference cadence (8/year → every other week) given existing heavy travel could reach a $5M run-rate in ~12 weeks.
- As of March 2026, Alex's core niche principle: niching down is important for operational efficiency, messaging, and sales motion — but if the nature/delivery mechanism of the work doesn't change across avatars, you don't need to narrow the avatar. You can run multiple acquisition motions with different messaging and hooks while the back-end delivery stays the same (so taking on med spas alongside plastic surgeons is not inherently limiting).
