# The Fallacy of the Perfect Pick

## Summary

Alex uses a Sartre line about a "toothless life" to argue against waiting for the
perfect opportunity. He says people imagine one perfect pick that will take them all the
way, but the real advantage is the habit of biting. The act of starting strengthens the
teeth; waiting for the perfect first bite wastes the time when action would have made
the person stronger.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex cites Sartre's image of someone saving their teeth for later until the teeth are
  gone.
- He adds that teeth can get sharper and stronger through use.
- He calls the belief in one perfect opportunity "the fallacy of the perfect pick."
- He says the habit of biting matters more than where someone chooses to bite first.
- He says he and the other speaker have learned to bite and trust that their teeth will
  get stronger along the way.
- He says waiting to begin never got anyone anywhere.
- He says it will never be easier than it is now to gain muscle, make money, build
  habits, or start improving.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Starting is the perfect condition."

> "It's the habit of biting that takes you all the way."

> "It'll literally never get easier."
