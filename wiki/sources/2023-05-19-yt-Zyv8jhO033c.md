# How to Build World Class Company Culture

## Summary

Alex Hormozi, appearing as a guest on the Iced Coffee Hour podcast (hosts Graham
Stephan and Jack), lays out his theory of company culture: culture is simply the
mission, vision, and values of the founder, which "drip down" and progressively
dilute at each layer of the organization. He argues elite talent works for people of
elite character, that no one will exemplify values more than the person at the top,
and that culture must be actively reinforced through a cadence of annual, quarterly,
and monthly "vision meetings" where every decision is tied back to the values. Values
function as decision-making filters for hiring, portfolio-CEO selection, and saying no
to opportunities. He gives Acquisition.com's own values as the concrete example.

## Key claims

- Culture is "the art side" of business; the science (tactics) exists, but without
  heart and intention it will not happen. (paraphrase)
- Most people with very big businesses are people of elite character; Bezos/Musk are
  outliers, not the template. Elite talent wants to work for people of elite character.
  (paraphrase)
- Hormozi frames his own job: not to be the smartest in the room, but competent and
  sound enough that the smartest person wants to work for him, within a vision big
  enough to contain their vision. (paraphrase)
- Culture starts at the top and dilutes as it drips down each organizational layer;
  bigger organizations lose the mission/values unless reinforcement is an immense,
  deliberate effort. (paraphrase)
- Culture is more important than talent: "without culture, talent fails." (Alex, when
  asked which matters more)
- Nobody will work for a leader whose values are worse than their own — they will go
  find someone with better values. (paraphrase)
- Tactics for instilling culture: annual meeting, quarterly meetings, and monthly
  "vision meetings" that repeat the mission/vision/values and show how goals tie to
  them. (paraphrase)
- Values and mission are decision-making filters. Example: a mission "help a thousand
  women lose weight" rules out a men's weight-loss program. (paraphrase)
- Acquisition.com's values are sincere candor, unimpeachable character, and
  competitive greatness; every portfolio-CEO pick and every hire is run through a
  scorecard against these values, and values fit is always the first question — skill
  is irrelevant if the values do not fit. (paraphrase; values quoted verbatim below)
- In monthly vision meetings, call out specific people by name for decisions that did
  or did not align with the values. (paraphrase)
- A mission sits above goals/KPIs: hitting a million subscribers is a goal; the mission
  is the greater "why" beyond it, usually amorphous, and it lets you attract better
  talent and give people clarity. (paraphrase)
- Values are "constraints of how you get to the vision" — e.g. a company whose value is
  "pig in the mud fun" constrains itself to have fun on the way to a billion-dollar
  vision. (paraphrase; phrase quoted below)
- Decision filter for saying no: run every incoming opportunity through mission/values;
  if it fits, maybe pursue; if not, decline — even a great opportunity may simply not
  be "our opportunity." (paraphrase)
