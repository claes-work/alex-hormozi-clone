# Humans Overestimate the Downside

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that humans systematically overestimate downside and underestimate
upside because ancestral survival rewarded pessimism. In modern business and life, he says
the biggest gains sit on the other side of uncertainty and delay. The practical frame:
unless the downside is death or true ruin, failure mostly means trying again with more
information.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Humans are biased toward downside because ancestral survival favored avoiding fatal risks.
- Modern upside often lives where the outcome is uncertain and delayed.
- The downside for a normal person going to zero is usually less likely than feared.
- Uncertainty is where the arbitrage lies because everyone else is also afraid of it.
- People avoid uncertain/delayed paths because they do not know how much they must pay or
  how long they must pay it.
- If someone else can solve a problem, the solver is not made of different tissue; the
  answer is learn, find help, trade money/favors/relationship capital, or keep trying.
- Alex quotes Jocko Willink's "all failure besides death is psychological" idea as a
  useful borrowed frame.
- If an attempt fails and does not kill you, the next move is to try again.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "All the upside that you want in your life is on the other side of uncertainty and delay."

> "What if it doesn't work? Okay, what if it does?"
