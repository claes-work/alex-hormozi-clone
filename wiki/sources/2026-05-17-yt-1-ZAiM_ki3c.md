# When the Only Way to Win is to Stop Playing

## Summary

Alex discusses whether founders must personally beat each "level" of a business skill or
can win by choosing another path. He says business functions must occur, and the founder's
job is to make sure they happen. The cheapest route is often learning the skill oneself;
the faster route may be recruiting or buying the skill, depending on the gap.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- For most entrepreneurs, the entrepreneur is the constraint of the system.
- Core business functions such as advertising, recruiting, finance, leadership, and
  management must happen somehow.
- If a founder does not know a required function, they either learn it or recruit someone
  who already has it.
- Many pieces of advice can work if executed with enough conviction.
- Alex increasingly focuses on increasing conviction around advice rather than merely
  changing the advice.
- Different entrepreneurs may need different routes to the same outcome because their
  existing skills and skill gaps differ.
- The cheapest way to solve a skill gap is often to learn it oneself; the faster way can
  be bringing in someone who already has the skill.
- Alex frames this as a buy-versus-build decision applied to a single capability.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "For most entrepreneurs, they are the constraint of the system."

> "All of them would work. But they will only work if you do."

> "It's buy or build but done on a n equals one."
