# The 5:30 AM Parking Lot Test

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that consistency is rare because it can only be observed by people who are also consistent enough to see it. He uses Acquisition.com as an example, saying the parking lot is full at 5:30 a.m. because the team is there to win. He pushes the listener to ask what resources would be required to do 100x more and whether the goal is worth that cost. He also says the work itself usually receives no credit; only the outcome does.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Consistency is rare because observing it requires some consistency from the observer.
- Environment can raise standards when everyone around the person is producing at a higher level.
- If 100x more output would guarantee the goal, the practical question becomes what resources are required and whether the trade is worth it.
- More for one day is not enough; the tally resets and must be rebuilt daily.
- Going pro is a decision.
- The work must be done for the person, not for external credit.
- People get credit for outcomes, not for the work; often they are criticized for the work before the outcome arrives.
