# Helping an Online Coaching Business Break an Ad Ceiling

## Summary
Alex Hormozi advises the owner of an online fitness-coaching business who scaled entirely on single-image lead-form ads on LinkedIn targeting executives. The owner reports strong lead quality and an LTV:CAC around 3.7, but says he stops being profitable above roughly $1,300/day in ad spend because he personally is the bottleneck on ad-creative production. Alex reframes the offer around a diagnostic, sell-to-goal model, recommends adding a supplement affiliate revenue stream, and confirms the real constraint is creative volume, not the sales team.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends the owner switch from selling fixed program lengths (a single program packaged as 3-, 6-, and 12-month options at 2K/3K/5K) to a **diagnostic, sell-to-goal** structure: price per week (e.g. ~$129/week), compute weeks from the client's target weight loss (~1 lb/week plus a recovery/maintenance period), and sell to the outcome rather than the timeline — which he says converts a far higher percentage of people.
- As of March 2026, Alex pairs sell-to-goal with a guarantee: keep working with the client until they hit the goal (provided the client meets stated conditions), which reframes the purchase as buying the result regardless of how long it takes.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises adding a **supplement affiliate line** — sold not on the first package but on a second appointment (a nutrition consult ~48 hours after the diagnostic) — as roughly 500-1,000 in supplement sales at ~100% margin with zero delivery cost for the coach; he says supplements can add about another 50% in sales.
- Alex prescribes a specific supplement-close mechanic: on a Zoom close, walk through the client's daily habits (coffee in the morning, gym trips) and anchor each supplement to an existing habit/location so they don't forget, then bill the card already on file and ship to their house.
- As of March 2026, Alex identifies the growth bottleneck as **ad-creative production volume** — the owner cannot produce enough ads — and confirms this is the correct constraint to attack, not the sales team.
- Alex states the sales team is not a constraint: closing above 40% is fine and "shouldn't be" the limiter — the ads should be.
- Caller claims (unverified): they scaled the whole program on single-image LinkedIn lead-form ads to executives; lead quality is "amazing"; LTV:CAC is ~3.7 (self-described as conservative/slightly miscalculated after a business revamp over the prior 13 months); the most common client goal is losing ~30 lbs; the 6-month package is by far the most popular; profitability breaks down above ~$1,300/day in ad spend; the sales team he is training closes 40%+.
