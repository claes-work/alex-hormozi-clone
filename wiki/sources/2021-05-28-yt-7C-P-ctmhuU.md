# 4 Perspective Shifts That Keep Me Going (2021-05-28)

## Summary

Hormozi argues the number-one driver of entrepreneurial success is **longevity**
(staying in the game; quitting is the only way to fail) and that longevity requires
managing your mental state to keep making high-quality decisions. He shares four
Stoic-flavored "perspective shift" strategies he uses to eliminate pressure and stay
in the game.

## Key claims (paraphrase, 2021)

- **Longevity is the number-one thing that creates success** — the ability to keep
  playing the game. The only way to fail is to quit. Bigger revenue multiplies stress
  proportionally ("add a zero... 10 times the potential for stress").
- **Shift 1 — Go 1000x negative, then re-evaluate.** For minor/medium grievances,
  imagine the bad thing happening over and over (negative visualization) until you'd
  become desensitized — then fast-forward that immunity to the present. Nothing
  changed except your perspective.
- **Shift 2 — Emotionally brace for the worst.** When starting a hard habit (e.g.
  working out), pre-plan the worst mental state (exhausted, unslept, stressed) and
  pre-commit to acting anyway. Humans have strong resilience if given a moment to
  brace; do the same to yourself in advance.
- **Shift 3 — Pain anchor / imagine worst case.** Deliberately soak in a genuinely
  catastrophic scenario (death of a spouse, parent, child) to gain perspective; the
  small problem shrinks into irrelevance. Most problems are never solved — you just
  realize you no longer care. The word "should" creates the gap between reality and
  expectation that produces unhappiness.
- **Shift 4 (his most-used) — Cosmic insignificance / "fine mist."** He believes over
  a long enough horizon the universe expands, stars burn out, and everything becomes
  a "fine mist" — so nothing has inherent meaning. He finds this *liberating*, not
  depressing: **he alone assigns meaning**, so he can choose to only play games he
  finds meaningful. This is his "ultimate trump card" for keeping emotions at bay.
- **His stated purpose is to learn**, and he learns by teaching — which is why he
  makes the videos. **Values are eternal**; external achievements are not.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The number one thing that I've seen that creates success is longevity... in order
> to decrease the likelihood that we quit, which is the only way to fail, we have to
> think through our problems with a defined framework."

> "I believe that the universe will continue to expand until eventually everything
> becomes a fine mist... anything that I accomplish in this world will eventually
> disappear and be meaningless."

> "I am the only person that ascribes meaning to anything... I am the master of my own
> universe. I get to decide what has meaning."

> "If nothing has meaning except for what I choose to give meaning, then it means I can
> choose to only play games that I find meaningful."

> "My purpose in life, to my knowledge at this point, is to learn."
