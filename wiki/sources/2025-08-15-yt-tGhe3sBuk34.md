# My final update (yt-tGhe3sBuk34)

## Summary

A behind-the-scenes documentary short filmed ~30 days before the August 16, 2025 live
launch of Alex Hormozi's book *$100M Money Models*. Filmed July 16, 2025, it intercuts
Alex prepping ads on a private jet, visiting the North Las Vegas fulfillment warehouse,
and shooting launch ad creative, while explaining why he wrote the book and how he is
using the launch itself to demonstrate the book's core concept (a money model). Heavily
promotional — the video is part of the launch campaign it documents.

## Key claims

- The core concept of the book is the **money model** — a business's monetization
  structure: how it collects money and charges for what it does. (Alex, paraphrase)
- A money model is "offering the right thing at the right time in the right way to the
  right customer." (Alex, verbatim)
- He wrote the book because he "hadn't heard anyone talk or write anything about it at
  all," and saw it as one of the most important things you can put in a business.
- Business advice: rather than switching businesses because you aren't earning what you
  want, fix your money model / monetization structure first.
- Customer-acquisition advice: instead of worrying you're spending too much to acquire
  customers, control what you can — "make more from each one."
- The launch is engineered as a live demonstration of the book: this time there is an
  offer to liquidate ad spend, so the goal is to "spend as much money as humanly
  possible while maintaining break even or better" to maximize reach. (Alex, paraphrase)
- Last launch had no offer to liquidate ad spend; he "just printed the money for the
  advertising." (Alex, paraphrase)
- Launch logistics: hoping to sell/ship over a million copies; distributing across
  three US warehouses; goal to get all shipments out within 72 hours; 300+ support
  staff for the launch; the North Las Vegas warehouse alone held ~80 pallets
  (240,000 books at 24 per box). (self-reported)
- The event is framed as "an event by us for us" — for entrepreneurs, who he says
  "get [criticized] most of the time" and "get told we're bad people."
- The launch includes five mystery headliners, four free pre-order bonuses, and one
  "secret project" he says he has been working on for multiple years, free with
  registration. (promotional)
