# Alex Hormozi Slept on Gym Floors Before He Got Rich

Interview clip on the @ACQofficial (Acquired) channel. Alex Hormozi is the main
speaker recounting his origin story; the host is [[../entities/acqofficial|context]]
only.

## Summary

Alex recounts his rock-bottom origin: at 22 he quit a defense-contracting consulting
job, drove across the country without telling his family, and showed up unannounced in
Southern California to apprentice under a gym owner he found by Googling. He spent 12
weeks apprenticing, then opened his own gym in Huntington Beach — sleeping on the gym
floor because he could only afford one rent. He credits a chance weekend Facebook-ads
workshop (2013) plus a six-week weight-loss challenge offer for letting him launch gyms
at full capacity with no capital of his own, growing ~$5,000/month for the first
seven or eight months.

## Key claims

- Self-reported: He was a gym owner in Southern California; before that he did a
  consulting job in defense contracting / cyber intelligence "through a sub under Bruce
  Allen," which he hated.
- Self-reported: He quit, drove across the country, and deliberately did not tell anyone
  until he was halfway there because his dad would have talked him out of it.
- Self-reported: He showed up in California "with no plan," found the gym owner by
  Googling "successful gym owner"; the owner's crappy HTML site said "Seven Figure Sam."
- Self-reported: Sam ran a small group of gym owners who shared their playbooks; Alex
  learned their past mistakes before opening his own first gym (e.g. rent per square
  foot, not going too small or too big).
- Self-reported: Sam worked a 4am–4pm shift; Alex apprenticed for 12 weeks, then opened
  his own gym in Huntington Beach.
- Self-reported: The six-week weight-loss challenge offer was iterative — an outside
  entrepreneur originated a "money back if you hit your goal, in exchange for a
  testimonial" deal (8 weeks); Sam adapted it to personal training (6 weeks); Alex added
  a nutrition template, made it one-to-many boot camp, and added Facebook ads.
- Self-reported (economics): leads at ~$5 each, sold 1 of 4 into a ~$600 challenge, so
  ~$20–25 customer acquisition cost against $600 up front; supplements added $200–300 two
  days later; membership then set $50 higher and discounted by crediting the $600.
- Self-reported (launch model): He would sign the lease, put the deposit down, run ads
  immediately, and open at full capacity in ~30 days using only the incoming cash — "every
  day I'd spend 100 I get 2,000 back."
- Self-reported: Opened a new location every six months until he had five locations; met
  [[../entities/leila-hormozi|Leila]] around that point and got asked to speak on
  marketing brick-and-mortar gyms.
