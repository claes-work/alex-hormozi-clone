# How to Make Your Haters Irrelevant

## Summary

Alex Hormozi coaches a founder (an actress running an acting academy) who is being
called a "scam" by industry critics after she added a results guarantee to her offer
following his book *$100M Offers*. His core advice: ignore the criticism entirely
rather than address it, because responding only associates your brand with the
negative word. Instead, get louder about who you are and what you stand for — both
externally to your audience and internally to your team — and let the critics burn out
on their own "extinction curve."

## Key claims

- Everyone gets accused of being a scam or a fraud until they have built enough trust
  with a broad customer base; outsized or hard-to-understand results make people
  underestimate you or call you invalid.
- The best response to negative attention is to completely ignore it. Weigh the upside
  vs. downside of addressing it: the upside (persuading strangers who may never buy) is
  small; the downside (associating your own name with the word "scam") is large.
- "Extinction curve": a persistent hater's attacks naturally taper off over time
  (3x/week → 2 → 1 → 0) if you do not feed them, like a fire starved of fuel. Some
  curves are long (months), but they die if unfed.
- Talking about what you are *not* does not help your brand; talking about what you
  *are* grows it. Brands built by attacking others can get big, but they don't build
  trust.
- Prescription: ignore critics AND get louder about your own message, sustained over
  years, until the critics (who really just want your attention/content) give up.
- People who obsessively attack others do so because they have "so little going on" in
  their own lives — a sign their own business isn't doing well.
- Apply the same "get louder about who you are" principle internally. Present an
  accurate depiction of who you are and what you stand for; this deliberately repels
  people who don't fit, and that filtering is healthy.
- Hormozi recounts that during Gym Launch he faced smear campaigns and competitors
  running ads calling them a scam.
- Hormozi recounts making a presentation to his Gym Launch team stating who they were
  and how they operate; two people quit right after, which he welcomed and respected as
  a valid opt-out.
