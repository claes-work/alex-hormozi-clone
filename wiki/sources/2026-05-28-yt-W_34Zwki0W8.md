# How Making More Money Affects Your Life

## Summary

Alex Hormozi discusses wealth tiers and diminishing returns. He says early income jumps materially change daily life, especially up to six figures, then around $10K-$20K/month, $40K-$50K/month, and roughly the top-1% income level. He argues that money continues to improve life beyond common study thresholds, but people often lack the skill of spending money to improve life. The best spending buys time back, removes annoyances, and funds meaningful long-term projects.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Early income gains create large life changes because they affect housing, school options, vacations, food, clothing, and daily constraints.
- Around the top-1% income level, many visible lifestyle variables can be substantially covered, depending on saving vs spending preference.
- Alex says he spends a small percentage of income relative to income and is more savings-oriented than his absolute spending might imply.
- Spending vs saving is partly a risk-preference question.
- Money can continue improving life beyond $100K/year, but people may lack the skill of spending it effectively.
- Spending money well is learnable.
- High-value spending buys time back, removes annoyances/frustrations, and supports meaningful long-term impact.
- He finds a roughly $650K-$700K/year diminishing-returns point for quality-of-life claims believable, while acknowledging higher income can buy additional luxuries.
