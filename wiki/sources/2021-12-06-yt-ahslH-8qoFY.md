# The Truth About Retiring At Age 31

## Summary

Alex describes a 9- to 12-month pseudo-retirement after becoming financially independent and mostly removing himself from the portfolio. He says the lack of meaningful challenge made him depressed and stagnant, then explains the new mantra that pulled him out: hard work itself is the goal, not the outcome.

## Key claims (Alex's advice/self-report, dated 2021-12-06)

- Alex self-reports that passive investments were making multiple millions per year while the portfolio did about $85M/year.
- He self-reports crossing roughly nine figures of net worth by age 32, with Leila age 29.
- He says needing nothing created a problem: without challenge, he had nothing meaningful to do.
- He says work shapes the worker more than the worker shapes the work.
- He says values exist only when tested: loyalty, honesty, and hard work require the opportunity to do otherwise.
- He says he felt the same at $100K, $1M, $10M, and nine-figure net worth; money did not create the meaning he expected.
- He frames his current season as making hard work itself the standard, including in business, training, and marriage.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The work is the goal."

> "Hard work is who we are."

> "There's nothing there."
