# The Only Metric of Success That Actually Matters

## Summary

Alex rejects outside definitions of work-life balance. He says the relevant metric is the
outcome someone personally chooses to optimize for. For him, that outcome is "the game":
business, business content, business books, and the rest of his day spent doing business.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-16, Alex agrees that heroes are regular people who got very good at one thing
  while neglecting other things.
- He says he does not want hobbies for their own sake and does not want to garden because
  he does not care.
- He rejects sacrificing what he prefers to do in order to satisfy someone else's measure
  of work-life balance.
- Self-reported: he says he enjoys playing the game, which is why his podcast is called
  *The Game*, he draws business pictures, writes business books, makes business content,
  and spends the rest of the day doing business.
- Self-reported: he says all drawings in *$100M Offers* are his, and *$100M Leads* has
  roughly 100 doodles.

## Notable verbatim quotes
