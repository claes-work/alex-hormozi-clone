# Accept Suffering Without Letting It Run Your Life

## Summary

Alex says he has spent major mental effort accepting suffering without turning every bad
feeling into proof that something is wrong. He describes correcting the story he tells
himself rather than always fixing the external thing, and says punishment fades faster
than reward, which means people often remember the good more than the bad.

## Key claims

- Alex says he has worked on accepting suffering without labeling bad experiences as
  evidence that something is wrong.
- He says the useful intervention is often fixing the story he tells himself rather than
  fixing the external condition.
- He says punishment fades with time while reward sticks more strongly.
- He says this can be hopeful because people tend to remember the good parts of life
  disproportionately.
- He warns that this becomes a problem if someone laments the present, which is the only
  time they actually live in.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Fix the story that I tell myself."

> "Punishment fades with time."

> "Reward sticks."
