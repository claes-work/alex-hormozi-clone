# The Biggest Business Opportunity of 2026

## Summary

Alex says web design and similar service businesses remain viable in 2026 because the
largest opportunity is using agents and subagents to simplify formerly high-headcount,
high-complexity service operations. He recommends shifting from role-based expansion to
workflow-based expansion: map the activities that turn attention into money, then train
AI agents and humans around those workflows.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says an "MR web design" or similar service business remains viable in 2026.
- He says the 2026 opportunity is taking high-operational-complexity, high-headcount
  businesses and running them with agents and subagents.
- He names legal, accounting, marketing agencies, and SEO as people-heavy businesses with
  large demand.
- He says many such businesses already have product-market fit because every business
  needs customers, taxes, and legal support.
- He says the constraint is operational complexity at scale, not demand.
- He says businesses need to move from role-based expansion to workflow-based expansion.
- He defines workflows as chained activities that turn attention into money.
- He says his own content workflow now uses prompts and subagents to identify hooks,
  interesting moments, payoffs, clip versions, and trial short-form content.
- He says the human editor's role becomes review and brand/quality assurance rather than
  doing all production manually.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The opportunity of 2026 is taking what was formerly a very high operational complexity, high head count, high coordination business and doing it with sub agents and agents."

> "No longer a roles-based expansion."

> "You have to think in workflows."
