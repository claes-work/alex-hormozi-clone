# The Rule I Made After Reading 10 Self-Help Books

## Summary

Alex recalls a pre-entrepreneur phase in Baltimore when he read about ten self-help books
and noticed that nothing in his life had changed. Because the advice conflicted and kept
accumulating, he made a rule: he would not read another book until he had done everything
from the next one. He uses the story to distinguish knowing that something matters from
knowing how to do it through practice.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-13)

- Alex says that in Baltimore, before becoming an entrepreneur, he read roughly ten
  self-help books and realized his behavior had not changed.
- He says contradictory advice across books pushed him toward action rather than more
  consumption.
- He made a personal rule not to read another book until he implemented everything in the
  next book.
- He says that rule preceded major action, including quitting his job.
- He says he now reads less nonfiction and reads with intent, notes, and action items.
- He distinguishes "knowing that" something matters from "knowing how" to do it, using
  sales books versus making a first cold call as an example.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I'm not reading another book until I do everything in this book."

> "Knowing that is not knowing how."

> "I hadn't done anything."
