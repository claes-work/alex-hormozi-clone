# How to 10x Your Business Overnight with Influencers

## Summary

Solo Hormozi workshop laying out a three-step "super influencer process" for partnering
with influencers/affiliates to grow a business fast: (1) pick the right influencer,
(2) get them to talk to you, (3) get them to promote your product. He frames influencer
partnerships as an "insurance policy" that lets a business compress years of
audience-building into weeks, and grounds it in his own spend (over $20M on Prestige Labs
influencers, over $22.8M on Acquisition.com's software affiliates, 23,000+ affiliates for
his book). The talk closes with two case studies — a large-business example (Tony
Robbins/Dean Graziosi launching "The Art of Living" with Matthew McConaughey) and a
small-business example (two UK operators, "Mos & Fahad," reaching $44K/mo recurring by
partnering with bodybuilder Eddie Abbew).

## Key claims (the influencer/affiliate playbook)

- **Core thesis**: One super influencer can 2x/10x/100x a business "overnight." If you can
  reach a large audience for free (via someone else's platform) and your product sells for
  more than it costs, you make money — the only question is *which* partnership makes the
  most, not *whether* it makes money.
- **Two models, decided by who has the bigger audience**:
  1. You own a branded product and get the influencer to promote *your* brand (Nike →
     Michael Jordan; Nike was the bigger brand, so Jordan promoted a line under them).
  2. You find the influencer and build/deliver a product branded around *them* (Congo →
     Logan Paul's Prime; the influencer was bigger, so the company delivered for him).
- **Army of small vs. one super influencer**: Many small (~100K) influencers takes longer
  but you retain more ownership (you stay bigger than them) — what he did with Prestige
  Labs and Acquisition.com's software (Allan). One super influencer is faster/easier to
  manage but you give up more. He now leans toward the super-influencer route (branding,
  speed to market, aggressive growth) — and did exactly that with Skool, where he co-owns
  and acts as his own influencer.
- **Step 1 — Pick**: Identify your ideal customer (ICP/avatar) → find the influencers they
  follow → pick the super influencers that best fit your product. Contact in expanding
  circles: (a) influencers you know personally, (b) ask your network for intros (you can
  cut them in on the deal or pay a bounty for an intro), (c) influencers you follow/have
  heard of — reach out on multiple channels (Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok, email, direct
  mail), (d) formal channels/agents. Super influencers cost less than people assume — "a
  couple hundred grand a year." Pro tip: opt in / contact support on their website inbox,
  which is far less crowded than their Instagram DMs.
- **Step 2 — Get them to respond**: (1) Verify yourself on the platform (blue check → 5–10x
  response rate for ~$10/mo) and make your profile look legit (clear face photo, bio
  stating you partner with influencers, relevant posts). (2) Send "the magic message" —
  short, personalized, states the recurring income they could generate, includes proof
  screenshots (can be of other people's results), offers them the majority of profits
  while you do the majority of work, asks for a 15-minute call. (3) Rule of 100: send ~100
  messages/day across platforms (~4 hrs/day) until you get replies. Bonus: offer to fly
  out ("I'm out that way all the time"), send an obscene *personalized* gift (put *their*
  logo on it, not yours), and share the target influencer's stories/posts to build affinity
  and "incur a debt" of goodwill before you even pitch.
- **Step 3 — Close the deal**: "Over-preparation equals profit." Prepare mockups of the
  product with their branding + graphs showing "this is you now / this is you with me"
  (followers × posting frequency × assumed reach × ~1% conversion → 12-month projection).
  Structure the pitch as **Promise → Proof → Plan → Offer**: promise the upside; prove it
  (past results, or stats from comparable people; bring the product as a demo); plan the
  next steps and *make your side of the deal look better than theirs*; then make a "Michael
  Jordan offer" so good they feel stupid saying no. For mid-sized influencers, offer growth
  (resources, hires, content help) instead of/in addition to money.
- **Every position has an advantage**: If you're brand new, sell personal attention, care,
  flexibility, going all-in ("you're my only partner"). If you're the "800lb gorilla," sell
  track record, best practices across accounts, infrastructure, specialists, and brand
  association. Sell to your position.
- **"Nos or yeses" (zoom out)**: Over-prepared decks for one influencer become proof for
  the next. Showing polished work you did for other (even un-pitched) super influencers
  signals professionalism ("imagine what he'd do if we actually worked together") and lets
  the target pick a structure they like.
- **"One is enough"** recurring mantra: you only need *one* super influencer to change your
  life; the gap between no and yes is where life-changing money is.
- **Free-product entry path**: If you have no product, build a paid community for an
  influencer (their audience already wants more access to them) — he points to Skool
  (skool.com/games, free to start) as the one-size-fits-most vehicle.
