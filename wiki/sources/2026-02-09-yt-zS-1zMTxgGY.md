# One of My Biggest Mistakes in 2025

## Summary
In a short interview clip, Alex Hormozi reflects on a costly 2025 mistake: he invested in a company without sufficient due diligence, later discovered it was doing non-compliant things, and — unwilling to be associated with it — handed the equity back and walked away, losing millions of dollars. Pressed on what he'd do differently, he lists deeper review of internal data, more in-depth interviews with key employees, and more customer-call reviews across sales, marketing, and delivery. But he reframes the bigger pattern: acquisition.com's largest source of mistakes isn't due diligence, it's *people* — because every deal and every hire is fundamentally an agreement between two parties, and the sheer volume of deals guarantees errors. His decision rule for whether to fix or cut a situation hinges on a founder's *willingness to make things right*: an unknowing founder who fixes a compliance gap (he cites a TCPA opt-in disclosure example) is fine; a founder who's told and does nothing is not. He closes on hiring/testing philosophy: acquisition.com used to run heavy personality and ability testing but pulled back because the recruiting team became over-reliant on scores; his view is that giving a team a decision-making tool triggers the "law of least effort" and produces worse decisions than using all available judgment. He'd only lean on a test as a red-flag check when results starkly contradict what they observe (implying the candidate is lying). The clip ends with a promo for a free acquisition.com 10-stage "zero to 100 million" roadmap.
