# the lonely chapter

## Summary

Alex Hormozi and another speaker discuss hard beginnings, loneliness, and the need to become the first person rooting for yourself. Alex frames hardship as selection: hard paths filter out competitors, and early lonely work becomes an origin story. He also explains a personal decision razor: he tends to choose the option that will make the most epic story, including documenting the path before the audience arrives.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Hard can be reframed as selection: if the path is difficult, fewer people will follow.
- The lonely period often comes when a person is too different from old peers but not yet successful enough to join the desired peers.
- People often root for winners after the support is least needed; before that, the person must root for themselves.
- The beginning is often the hardest part because there is little reward, little leverage, and few allies.
- Early unglamorous work can become later advantage because it builds context and skill.
- Alex has used future-casting the story he will tell about a bad period as reinforcement during hard times.
- He says his decision razor is to choose the path that would make the most epic story.
- He frames documenting the journey early as planting breadcrumbs for future viewers to revisit.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You always have to be the person who roots for you."
