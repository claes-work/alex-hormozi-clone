# Why I Believe Earning Less Than Someone Else Can Be a Blessing

## Summary

Alex Hormozi says Kylie Jenner's Forbes cover triggered an ego response, then a learning
response. He decided that if someone makes more money, they know something he does not,
and their success can serve him. In this case, the lesson he took was brand, which he says
kicked off his content and brand-building journey.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex was about 27 when Kylie Jenner appeared on a Forbes cover as a young billionaire.
- His first response was ego: explaining away her success through her mother, reality TV,
  and advantages.
- He then asked what he could learn from her.
- Alex believes that if someone makes more money than him, they know something he does not
  know.
- Treating others' success as lessons lets every person's success serve him.
- Pointing out others' deficiencies mirrors the fear that others will point out one's own.
- If someone has more followers or a viral video, there is something to learn from that
  outcome.
- The lesson Alex took from Kylie was brand.
- He says the Forbes cover kicked off the journey that led him to content and brand
  building.
