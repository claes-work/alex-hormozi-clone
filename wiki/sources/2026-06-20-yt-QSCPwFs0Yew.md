# How to Build a Business Serving High School Coaches

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a former football coach who has spent years trying to monetize products and community for high school and college coaches. Alex says this is one of the rare cases where he would advise exiting a market, because the avatar has a structural monetization problem: lower-level coaches are often broke, while elite coaches get recruited without needing the caller's help. He recommends pivoting the caller's football-derived leadership and team-building lessons into the corporate market while still using football-coach content and relationships as audience, recruiting, and mission fuel.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - former football coach and entrepreneur. His 20-year coaching history, X views, spreadsheet product, coach network, and possible Tackle Life concept are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex says he rarely advises entrepreneurs to exit a space, but this case likely warrants it.
- The high school and lower-level coach market has a monetization problem because the target customers often lack money.
- If an avatar is broke, the business usually has to flip the model so the avatar becomes the product rather than the paying customer.
- Recruiting as the flipped model is unattractive here because very good coaches are already visible and easier for schools to recruit.
- Corporate training and leadership is a much better opportunity vehicle for the caller's football-derived lessons.
- A pivot is justified when an underlying assumption has been disproven by new information.
- The caller can still serve the football-coach mission by making content, attracting coaches, and potentially employing them in the corporate-facing business.
