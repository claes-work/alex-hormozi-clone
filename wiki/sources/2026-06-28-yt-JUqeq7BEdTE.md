# There's No Easy Business

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a CPA/CFO advisory founder doing about $2.9M who wants to reach $20M and is tempted to market books and courses. Alex pushes back: the founder has stopped selling because fulfillment is hard, so the constraint is supply, not demand. Alex recommends fixing operating leverage first through delivery redesign, offshoring, data architecture, and then AI, while saving the books and courses as future marketing assets.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - CPA/CFO advisory founder. Her business model, revenue, books, courses, and fulfillment issues are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- A company that has stopped taking new clients will not grow from more marketing.
- The founder is supply constrained, not demand constrained; fulfillment difficulty caused selling to stop.
- Starting a course business because the service business is hard is not an escape from difficulty; the course business will have its own hard problems.
- The higher-value move is to increase operating leverage in the existing sticky service business before creating a weaker new business.
- Offshoring can give the existing team more leverage before AI is fully usable.
- Alex argues that AI-first requires data-first: without complete data and architecture, AI has nothing useful to work on.
- Once the service delivery layer has more capacity, the books and courses can be used as demand-generation assets.
