# Why You Can't Get Over Your Ex (Real Reason)

## Summary

Alex responds to the idea that if someone does not want the lifestyle required for a
result, they should release the desire. Operationally, he says desire is difficult to
release directly and is better replaced: choose a new reinforcing target, then justify
why wanting that target is better.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-07)

- Alex agrees with the frame that craving a result without wanting the process
  guarantees disappointment.
- He references the idea that desire is a contract to be unhappy until the desire is
  met.
- He says if someone will not do the work for a goal, continued desire creates an
  unsatisfactory life.
- He says he does not think people release desire so much as replace it.
- He compares getting over a breakup to finding enough exposure to new reinforcers to
  replace the removed reinforcer.
- He says healing often means finding something that returns baseline joy to normal.
- He says the replacement could be a person, tennis, gardening, business, or another
  reinforcing activity.
- He recommends reasoning why wanting something different is better, rather than trying
  to simply release the old desire.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I don't think you can release desire, but you can replace it."

> "Choose to want something else."

> "Try and justify or reason why wanting something different is better."
