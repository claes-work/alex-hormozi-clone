# You Need to Build a Business That Runs Without You

## Summary
Solo clip on **removing the founder's face from customer acquisition** — a specific slice of the "business that runs without you" problem. Alex frames founder-driven acquisition (you are the breadwinner, the promoter, the one who brings the business in) as "one of the biggest risk factors in business": it's why you can't leave, and when you do, revenue drops. He notes the trap is emotional — being irreplaceable "feeds your ego," makes you feel like "a special snowflake" — but "in order to make an important business, you need to become less important." If your face gets the customers, "you are the key man." His fix: **install systems that capture media and marketing on your behalf** instead of relying on "direct-to-camera founder ads." He rattles off ~six or seven installable processes: (1) a weekly process to screenshot praise from your customer community (e.g. a Skool) and turn it into ads; (2) incentivized testimonials, using unlockables/credits/trials of a higher service tier as a "double whammy" that both ascends customers and generates marketing; (3) **life-cycle ads** — you already record sales calls, onboarding, delivery checkpoints and testimonials, so pull the recordings for a happy client from CRM/scheduling and compress the journey (scared/hesitant → amazing outcome) into an ad; (4) documenting emotive deliverable moments (kitchen reveal, weight on the scale, revenue milestone) via an incentivized process; (5) paying customer-support reps ~$5 per mini in-chat testimonial they capture; (6) turning existing Google/Yelp/Trustpilot/Amazon reviews (local businesses often have hundreds, "rarely made into marketing") into ads — including using funny one-star reviews à la **Liquid Death** to repel the wrong customer and thereby attract the right one. Further plays: affiliate program, TikTok Shop plus whitelisted creator ads. All of it "removes your face from your market."
