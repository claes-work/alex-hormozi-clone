# "Do I need courses to start a community business?"

## Summary
Short live-coaching clip. A community operator asks whether they should turn on
traffic to their community even though the classroom (course) content is still
"half-baked" — their instinct was to finish the offer/courses first, then flip on
the lead machine, but they need market feedback to know what to build. Alex's answer
is an emphatic **"Yes"** — start now, without finished courses.

His reasoning inverts the usual course-first assumption. (1) **Building live is often
more valuable than recordings.** He cites the most recent Skool Games winner, the
"Belmar Bros," who launched with **nothing** in their classroom; early members get
the promise of watching it built in real time and having their questions answered
live while the presentations are being made — a value-add, not a deficiency. (2) He is
**"not even completely sold on having classroom as a net add for a lot of
communities,"** because people get overwhelmed by classroom content and, above all,
**people want community above content.** Judged by *usage* (not by what people say they
want), the vast majority of activity on the platform is **in-feed** — posting,
commenting, interacting with one another. So a community where people can talk about
the topic and get their questions answered is itself the valuable product. (3) Rather
than "wasting a lot of time guessing what they want," you find out what they want by
**asking them and keeping your ear to the ground** as soon as the community starts.
He closes with an engagement tactic: someone recently got their community engaging by
the "crazy idea" of simply **asking people to engage** — post, ask a question — because
people often feel they need permission (afraid of looking stupid).
