# Helping a Home Service Business Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The owner runs a residential plumbing and HVAC business pacing for ~$15M this year (grew 4M → 8M → 11M → pacing 15M after hiring two leaders from outside the industry) and wants to reach $40M. His stated problem is "chaos": rapid over-expansion is causing lots of mistakes, so top line grows but **net profit stays flat** (~14% YTD, was 15.7% last year, ~16% at the $4M mark). He is the sole owner, everything is paid off, no debt taken on to grow. He identifies as more of a perfectionist who'd go slower, but the two new leaders are corporate-bred, top-line-revenue-driven — and, crucially, **compensated on net** yet still driving top line. He wants a lifestyle "cash cow," not an exit. Alex reframes the whole goal: instead of chasing $40M, **hold top line flat and triple bottom line**. The owner immediately likes it — he'd happily stay at $15M and run 30% net. Alex's diagnosis: (1) **data** — he'd bet the owner lacks real-time gross operating margins on each product line; getting that lets you "drive EBITDA through the roof," and in this business tax efficiency is very achievable (he questions whether they have a good CFO — the owner doesn't think so, though his own background is accounting). (2) There's almost certainly **sales motion that can be improved and pricing that can be optimized** to improve cash flow. Alex dismisses industry averages ("American averages is fat, overweight, and divorced — why would I care?") and says he'd target 30%+ net minimum. He lays out a **sequence**: fix the sales motion → fix pricing and packaging of each service → generate sufficient cash flow → hire the talent you need → build up the infrastructure. That becomes a four-part annual plan to roughly double EBITDA (from ~2–3M to ~6M). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (excluded from claims).

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex's reframe for a top-line-obsessed but margin-flat business: set the year's goal as **"do not increase top line at all and triple bottom line"** rather than chasing a bigger revenue number.
- As of March 2026, the first likely constraint is **data** — without real-time gross operating margins on each product line you can't optimize; getting that visibility lets you "drive EBITDA through the roof."
- As of March 2026, for a home-service business, **tax efficiency is highly achievable**, and a good CFO/finance function is a lever the owner was missing.
- As of March 2026, Alex ignores industry-average margins on principle ("American averages is fat, overweight, and divorced") and would target **30%+ net minimum** for a service business currently around 13–14%.
- As of March 2026, the two most probable profit levers in a scaling service business are **an improvable sales motion and un-optimized pricing/packaging** of each service line.
- As of March 2026, Alex's fix sequence: **improve the sales motion → fix pricing & packaging of each service → generate sufficient cash flow → hire the needed talent → build up infrastructure** — in that order — packaged as a four-part annual plan.
- As of March 2026 (implicit diagnosis): compensating leaders on **net** while they still drive **top line** signals that margin isn't actually being managed — flat net during fast growth means mistakes are eating the gains.
