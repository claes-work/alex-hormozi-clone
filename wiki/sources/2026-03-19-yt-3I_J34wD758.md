# How to use AI to grow your business

## Summary
Long training session (live Q&A with a room, promoting ACQ AI / "ACQAI"). Alex's core thesis: AI gives you *amplification, not automation* — "more leverage, not infinite leverage." The alpha is always the idea; AI can't have your real-life experience, so it remixes your language patterns, structure, and format but not the core value. He shares concrete leverage multiples on his own workflow (Mozi Minute emails 4x, tweets ~7x, YouTube 8x, memos ~4x, VSLs a full day → minutes) and stresses that these gains only come *because he already produced a huge corpus* (6,000+ tweets, years of bangers) for the AI to train on: "you are rewarded for the work that you have done." He teaches a three-part system — (1) business context [the "47 questions"], (2) rules of engagement / SOP repository, (3) data sources (past work) — and walks through ~14 copy-paste prompts (sales "mother script," newsletter generator, objection playbook, writing-style analyzer, VSL, sales-call feedback, YouTube/shorts script templates, lead-magnet builder, follow-up sequences, and an 11–14 "offer architect" *prompt chain* where each output feeds the next). Includes his rule "the longer the prompt, the shorter the output" and which tools he uses for what. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex's central AI position: what you get is **amplification, not automation** — "you will get more leverage, not infinite leverage. True automation is infinite leverage… that is uncommon."
- As of March 2026, he insists "AI does not come up with the idea… the ultimate alpha is the idea." AI is "a remixer," "not original yet"; it replicates format/structure/sentence patterns, not the core value.
- As of March 2026, self-reported leverage multiples on his own work: Mozi Minute emails ~4x (60–90 min → ~15–20 min; one half-day/month for the month's emails), tweets ~7x (~90 min/week vs. ~1.5 hr/day), YouTube videos ~8x (2 hr → 15 min), memos ~4x (4 hr → ~1 hr), sales scripts and VSLs "a full day" → "really quick."
- As of March 2026, self-reported revenue attribution: the Mozi Minute email generates "about 20% of the revenue of ACQ… the advisory practice"; YouTube is "where 40% of lead flow comes from"; tweets feed "20 to 30% of all of our content."
- As of March 2026 (biographical/volume): he wrote "6,000 [tweets]… just me" before using AI and is "at 7,000 now" — the data bank is why the AI output sounds like him.
- As of March 2026, the three requirements to "use AI well": **business context**, **rules of engagement / SOP repository (how we do it)**, and **data sources (past work)** — "those are the three wheels."
- As of March 2026, prompting rule of thumb: "the longer the prompt, the shorter the output; the shorter the prompt, the longer the output" — more context narrows the answer.
- As of March 2026, good-prompt structure = role + task + rules + output ("the four kind of structure for good prompting").
- As of March 2026, he describes "prompt chains" (prompts 11–14): the offer architect's output becomes the input to the sales-page generator, the sales-call script generator, and the VSL generator — "output one becomes input two," letting you "build out entire marketing universes."
- As of March 2026, he says beginners who churn AI content still lose "because they have no experience… the AI can't live your life for you"; the fix is his standing rule — do stuff in the real world, document it, then talk about it: "AI just allows you to talk about more of it faster."
- As of March 2026 (tool preferences, self-reported): he uses "all the LLMs"; ACQ AI most for business strategy / offer / pricing / sales-flow / copy; Gemini and GPT for images; GPT for personal/health questions; Grok for "edgy" or politically-neutral questions "that I don't need the GPT to judge me" on.
- As of March 2026, he notes a copyright distinction between AI-*assisted* (still heavily edited by him) and pure-AI output.
