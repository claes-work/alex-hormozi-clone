# Why Nobody Good Applies to Your Job Post

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an HVAC cleaning business that has improved its marketing but will soon become supply-constrained by technician hiring. Alex tells the owner to let the current lead-generation fix settle rather than solving the same problem twice, then build a scalable recruiting machine. Because the role is trainable and personality-driven, he recommends broad local ads, high application volume, and group interviews rather than expensive out-of-state recruiting.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Corey / coachee** - HVAC cleaning business owner. His revenue, profit, marketing agency change, technician economics, and hiring process are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Do not solve the same problem twice while a chosen solution is still inside its expected implementation window.
- If the marketing/lead fix is already underway, the next likely constraint is technician supply.
- A business that wants to grow 12x must scale talent acquisition, not merely keep hiring one technician every three or four months.
- Talent acquisition should be treated as its own machine.
- The owner should understand gross profit per technician before deciding acquisition spend.
- If the role is trainable and selected mainly for personality, the recruiting pool can be much broader than experienced technicians.
- Low-skilled / trainable roles should use local ads and group interviews to process volume efficiently.
- Book many candidates knowing show rates will be low; use quick group-interview screening and one-on-one follow-up.
- If the owner is willing to spend $10,000 on relocation, spending that amount on recruiting ads may produce far more local candidates.
- The company should cast a broad net and screen for attitude.
