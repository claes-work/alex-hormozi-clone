# Helping a Financial Services Company Fix Their Margin

## Summary

Alex coaches a $2M estate-planning and real-estate legal-services business with near-zero
EBITDA despite steady growth. He says the first constraint is not leads or sales but the
core model: pricing and compensation. Because the business is mainly payroll rather than
fixed machinery or rent, zero profit suggests the company is underpriced, overpaying, or
both. Fixing margin creates the cash flow needed to attack acquisition later.

## Key claims

- When a business has zero EBITDA, Alex wants to understand why it is not making money
  before adding more leads.
- In a legal-services business, the main cost is people/payroll rather than large fixed
  assets.
- If the team believes it is at capacity but profit is poor, Alex suspects underpricing,
  overpaying, or both.
- Fixing pricing and compensation can free cash flow for front-end growth.
- Alex says getting more customers is probably not the solution until the core economics
  can breathe.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Do nothing for zero."

> "You have a pricing issue."

> "We have to free up cash so you can breathe."
