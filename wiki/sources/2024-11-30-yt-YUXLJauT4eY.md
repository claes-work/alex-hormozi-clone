# REVEALING Black Friday Secrets To Use In Your Business (feat. Shopify's President)

## Summary

Alex and Shopify president Harley Finkelstein review Black Friday examples from brands such as Gymshark, BarkBox, Burger King, Fly By Jing, and ButcherBox. Harley contributes Shopify-wide retail context and brand examples; Alex turns the examples into tactical marketing principles: focus the product suite during high-intent events, use coupons without cheapening perceived retail value, collect data through quizzes or pop-ups, create urgency around a real buying day, and use media/assets when a business has superior monetization.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2024-11-30 - Focus beats breadth**: Alex points to the jam-choice experiment to argue that businesses should narrow the buying path during high-intent periods instead of presenting too many options.
- **2024-11-30 - Coupon framing**: He says applying discount codes at checkout can preserve retail value in the customer's mind while still giving the savings close to purchase.
- **2024-11-30 - Data as resource weaponization**: He says early pop-ups and broad short-form testing can create useful data before the main push; he analogizes this to using hundreds of short-form content shots to select a few long-form winners.
- **2024-11-30 - First-time buyer incentives**: Alex says high-attention moments are good times to make unusually strong offers because action thresholds are lower.
- **2024-11-30 - Market creation**: In the Burger King/Shopify TAM discussion, Alex says businesses should not only steal share from current buyers; they can grow the pie by activating people who were not yet in the market.
- **2024-11-30 - Media acquisition logic**: Alex says acquiring a media asset can make sense when the buyer has a stronger monetization engine than the media company itself.

## Context / guest-attributed claims

- Harley provides Shopify's live Black Friday data and most of the platform-level brand examples.
- Harley says Shopify stores were processing millions in sales per minute during the session and highlights first-sale "fireworks" as new entrepreneurs making sales.
