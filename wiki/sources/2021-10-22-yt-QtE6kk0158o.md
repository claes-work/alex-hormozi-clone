# Every business is limited by the constraint. Here's how to find it

## Summary
In this solo video Hormozi tells the story of a quarterly leadership meeting where his sales manager set the same goal — add six outbound reps — for a third straight quarter without progress, and uses it to teach a proto Theory-of-Constraints method for diagnosing what actually limits a business. He argues every system has potential and constraints, and that a business grows only up to its constraint and no further, so the leverage is in identifying the one true bottleneck (here: the talent-acquisition pipeline, not the sales manager) and fixing it. He introduces a secondary framework, the "quad marketing calendar," which maps four marketing directions a business must run, including marketing to acquire employees as a parallel to acquiring customers.

## Key claims (2021)
- Every system has potential and constraints; any system grows up to its constraint and no further, so most entrepreneurs waste effort adding potential while the real constraint goes unaddressed (proto Theory-of-Constraints framing).
- "Macro speed" comes not from frenetic energy but from knowing which problems to solve and in what order.
- The constraint-finding method he demonstrates is a backward, quantified walk through the pipeline: define the goal, then interrogate each stage's throughput and conversion rates until the true bottleneck reveals itself.
  - Worked example: goal = 6 new reps/quarter. HR sent ~1 qualified candidate/week; it took ~5 interviews to produce 1 qualified candidate, and ~4 final interviews per hire → ~20 people through the funnel per hire. Process yielded ~1 new rep/month while churn was ~20%/quarter (~2 reps lost/quarter on a team of ~10), netting only ~1 new rep per quarter — far short of 6.
- The misdiagnosed constraint was the hiring/talent-acquisition process, not the sales manager's training or recruiting effort; pressure had been applied to the wrong person for two quarters.
- Fix: stop one-on-one final interviews (they existed only due to a miscommunication) and switch to group interviews — HR still runs ads and organizes, but the sales manager runs ~5 group interviews of one hour/week, picks 1–2, and onboards fast, enough to hit the goal.
- He claims the misdiagnosis cost ~$10–12M in annual top-line revenue that could not be recovered (each outbound rep generates ~$2M/year; 6 reps ≈ $12M); only ~1 quarter's worth (~$2–2.5M) can now be captured this year.
- Frontline positions churn more than manager/leadership positions as a rule of the game.
- Quad marketing calendar: businesses should run four marketing directions across two axes (internal vs. external, employees vs. prospects): (1) internal-to-employees — market the vision/mission so people keep wanting to work for you; (2) external-to-prospects — turn prospects into customers (the one most people do); (3) internal-to-customers — market to existing customers to buy again; (4) the most-missed — an external acquisition process for new talent that mirrors the customer funnel.
- Talent acquisition should mirror the customer funnel one-to-one: run ads for employees, generate leads, work the leads, set interviews (interviews are sales), make job offers (like normal offers), onboard in parallel to customer onboarding, then ascend/manage employees as you ascend/resell customers.
- Self-reported: acquisition.com portfolio companies do "about 85 million a year" (2021).
