# Your Business Won't Scale Until You Hire Better People

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a premium home-services operator in Bozeman whose growth is constrained by technicians and by the founder's belief that he must be involved in every part of the company. Alex reframes "can't let go" as "won't let go," recommends a time study to rank the founder's activities by value, and says premium customers should produce enough margin to hire premium people. For recruiting, he suggests national ads plus a generous relocation/signing bonus rather than trying to misclassify employees as contractors.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Premium home-services founder** - the coachee. His company, local market, clients, revenue, profit, and technician economics are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Technician recruiting is the constraint if each tech produces roughly $180K-$300K/year after cost and the founder has stopped recruiting.
- The founder's belief that he must be involved in everything is a choice, not an unavoidable fact.
- Key-man risk reduces business value and also burns out the founder.
- The first diagnostic is a time study: every 15 minutes for a week, record the activity, then rank activities by revenue value and uniqueness.
- The bottom half of the founder's activity list should be delegated to an existing person with capacity or to a new hire.
- Premium customers should allow premium margins, and premium margins should fund premium people.
- For scarce local technicians, Alex recommends national recruiting ads and a generous relocation/signing bonus, such as half upfront and half after month six.
- If workers must show up at set times and do work the company's way, they are employees; the business should become more legitimate, not less.
- If hiring a tech returns half the founder's time and the founder can double the business with that time, hiring is the game.
