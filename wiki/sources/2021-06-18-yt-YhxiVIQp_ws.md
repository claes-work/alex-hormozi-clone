# How the rich avoid paying taxes...MY strategy - Part 1 (2021-06-18)

> ⚠️ **Not tax advice; self-reported, US-specific, illustrative (as of Jun 2021).** Every
> figure and tax claim below is Alex Hormozi's own stated understanding, rounded and
> unverified against tax code or registries. He frames the video as his personal conclusions
> after spending on tax advisors, and states his core takeaway is a shift in perspective
> rather than specific "hacks."

## Summary

Hormozi argues the "tax strategies of the ultra wealthy" are mostly perspective, not hacks:
because the majority of net worth comes from the untaxed **appreciation of owned assets**,
the winning move is to buy assets below value, hold them long-term, and — when you need cash —
**borrow against them** rather than sell (a loan is not income, so it is tax-free). He
dismisses most touted shelters (conservation easements, captive insurance) as marginal "BBs,"
notes residency-based savings (low-tax states, Puerto Rico, expatriation), but says he
personally rejects Puerto Rico because the freedom cost outweighs the money. He states he has
spent ~$750K on tax advisors and paid ~$18M in taxes over the prior three years.

## Key claims (2021, paraphrase)

- (2021) Most YouTube tax advice comes from people who are not ultra-wealthy and have no
  visibility into what the ultra-wealthy actually do; he frames himself as speaking from
  inside that group. *(self-reported.)*
- (2021) He has spent ~$750K on tax advisors/strategies and paid ~$18M in taxes to the US
  government over the prior three years — "happily done so." *(self-reported figures.)*
- (2021) **Residency is the biggest initial lever.** State level: live in a zero-tax state vs.
  California's ~15–16%. Federal level: US persons face global taxation, and there are only two
  ways around federal tax — (1) live in **Puerto Rico** ≥6 months/yr and export services
  (~4% federal, 0% state, "100% legit"), or (2) **expatriate** / renounce citizenship (e.g.
  citizenship-by-investment / a ~$1M "golden visa," then stay <6 months/yr). *(self-reported.)*
- (2021) **The vast majority of net worth comes from growth, not contributions** — and it
  comes mostly from starting early. Hence live on as little as possible for as long as
  possible to let compounding work. "$500/month at 20 beats $5,000/month at 45."
- (2021) **Buy below value = instant untaxed gain.** Buying something worth $100 for $30
  triples your money with no tax event, and it keeps compounding untaxed as it grows.
- (2021) **Tax only matters if you sell** (capital gains). Buy-and-hold (his Buffett examples:
  See's Candies, Coca-Cola, GEICO held for decades) gets efficient treatment; buy things you
  believe will last 30 years rather than what's "sexy now."
- (2021) **Loans are never treated as income but are spendable** — so plow money into
  tax-free-growing assets, then take **loans against them** to live on; as long as the loans
  are smaller than the asset growth, you compound tax-free for life and the estate is
  rectified at death. *(This is the "buy-borrow-die" mechanic — see topic notes.)*
- (2021) He **dismisses most shelters as "BBs"**: conservation easements can't absorb your
  whole income; captive insurance would have saved him ~$250K/yr — "doesn't make a big dent"
  — and he passed because it felt "fishy," after which the IRS reversed its ruling and
  back-charged people with interest and fees. *(self-reported.)*
- (2021) The US tax code isn't "fair" — the government can change rules and back-tax prior
  years — so he treats it like content on a platform: just align within the rules that exist,
  and long-term, if you don't sell, you don't get taxed.
- (2021) He **personally rejects the Puerto Rico move**: the difference between $100M and
  $180M net worth "makes no difference" to his life; money only has value insofar as it buys
  freedom, and being a "prisoner of an island" six months/yr inverts the goal. He chose Texas
  over California because the lifestyle is the same but the taxes are lower. *(self-reported.)*

## Topic notes — tax timeline (feeds [[../topics/wealth/tax-free-liquidity]])

This 2021 video is the **earliest** dated articulation in the wiki of Hormozi's tax thinking,
and it already contains **both** halves of the later, seemingly-evolving position:

- **Same core insight as 2022 and 2024:** wealth is the *untaxed appreciation of owned
  equity*, not income — "the vast majority of your net worth is going to come from growth."
- **Anticipates the 2024 "leverage the asset" stance** ([[../sources/2024-05-08-yt-6x3re5-Ms1E]]):
  he already describes **buy-borrow-die** explicitly here in 2021 — borrow against tax-free-growing
  assets and live off the loans, rectifying the estate at death. So the borrow-against-assets
  move is **not new in 2024**; it is present three years earlier.
- **Aligns with the 2022 "anti-shelter" stance** ([[../sources/2022-01-27-yt-w5g0JiO7OdE]]):
  he already dismisses artificial shelters (conservation easements, captive insurance) as
  marginal "BBs" with clawback risk, and stresses the win is appreciation + buy-and-hold, not
  hacks.
- **Nuance vs. 2022:** unlike the 2022 "just pay your taxes" headline, the 2021 video leans
  harder on residency levers (states, Puerto Rico, expatriation) as the "biggest initial
  difference" — while still *personally* rejecting Puerto Rico on freedom grounds.

**Timeline takeaway:** the 2021 → 2022 → 2024 arc is better read as **one consistent
worldview** (untaxed appreciation of owned equity + skepticism of shelters + borrow-don't-sell)
with shifting *emphasis*, not a reversal. The 2021 source predates and undercuts any reading of
2024's buy-borrow-die as a new position. Flag on the [[../topics/wealth/tax-free-liquidity]]
position-evolution note that the borrow-against-assets mechanic is documented as early as
2021-06-18.
