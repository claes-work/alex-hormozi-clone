# $500K Power Washing Business Stuck on Referrals

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a power-washing / solar-panel-cleaning / window-cleaning owner doing about $500K revenue and $250K take-home. The owner wants recurring commercial clients, but Alex says the immediate issue is that the business relies on referrals and does not know whether its paid ads work. He recommends measuring attribution, then increasing ad spend if the economics are good, rather than building a new commercial acquisition motion before scaling the channels already working or likely working.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - local cleaning business owner. His revenue, take-home, service mix, door knocking, ad spend, and commercial-client goal are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Waiting for referrals means the business lacks a controllable acquisition channel.
- The owner is considering both a new channel and a new avatar at the same time, which may be premature.
- The business already has door knocking and paid ads, so it should measure those first.
- If someone manages ads, they should be able to provide attribution.
- At $2,000/month in ad spend and roughly $500 average jobs, the ads may be profitable if they produce more than one deal per week.
- Spend can be increased gradually once attribution proves returns.
- Local services are an exception where multiple channels can make sense because the local market is finite.
- The business should avoid changing the model before scaling what is already working.
