# When Should You Quit a Business?

## Summary
Podcast clip. Alex gives his framework for quitting vs. persevering, which he reframes as "push or pivot." He first situates it among the "continuums to be managed, not problems to be solved" (e.g., how much to invest vs. consume — "delayed gratification in the extreme results in no gratification"). His secret rule: "I won't quit if no new information has come to light" — if the original assumptions still hold, it's a *push*; if new data changes why/what/how you're pushing, you *pivot*. And nothing is "quitting" unless you actually stop — pivoting and pushing are both activity, so you'll eventually "stumble upon something that does work," then do more of it. He stresses using pre-identified *leading indicators* (not the final outcome) to know you're on the right path even before achieving "Mr. Beast-dom," just as he pre-identifies the *problems* success will bring and accepts them. He closes with his tweet-thesis: "the world belongs to those who can continue to work without seeing the result of their work" — big goals just take longer because everyone conquers the little hills quickly, so there's enormous opportunity in persisting on the correct path without external positive reinforcement. Ends with the roadmap promo.
