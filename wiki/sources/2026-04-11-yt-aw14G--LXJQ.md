# The One Hiring Rule That Fixes Almost Every Bad Hire

## Summary

Alex reconciles attitude-versus-aptitude hiring by treating hard and soft traits as
skills and selecting the candidate with the smallest relevant skill deficiency. He adds
learning potential to estimate how quickly the remaining gap will close. For senior
talent, hiring shifts from applicants selling themselves to the company selling its
mission, leader, brand, and career opportunity.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says businesses should always hire for the smallest skill
  deficiency, whether that gap is hard or soft.
- Kindness is a skill composed of many condition-specific behaviors and can be harder to
  teach than a narrow task such as setting sales calls.
- For scarce specialist roles, hiring the experienced candidate with the least damaging
  soft-skill gap may provide a better resource return than training a novice.
- No hire is complete on arrival; intelligence or rate of learning determines how fast
  the remaining bridge closes.
- Alex prefers a less-experienced, highly intelligent and hardworking candidate when
  they can catch the veteran and continue improving faster afterward.
- A leader needs admirable character, mission, opportunity, and external brand to
  attract people smarter than the leader.
- Alex self-reports that Acquisition.com can attract materially stronger talent than Gym
  Launch could, and that elite candidates require prolonged courting.
