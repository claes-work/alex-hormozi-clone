# Helping an Agency Doing $2M/Year Get to $5M

## Summary

Alex recommends turning a nearly unmonetized photography course into free acquisition
content for the owner's implementation agency. He outlines a funnel that distributes the
course openly, captures leads, filters them by revenue, schedules qualified prospects,
and trains the advertising platform only on qualified conversions.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports just under $2 million in agency revenue, less than $10,000 in
  annual course revenue, and a $5 million year-end goal; these figures are unverified.
- As of April 2026, Alex says the course should be free because its direct revenue is
  negligible relative to its potential as top-of-funnel material for the service.
- The material can stream publicly on YouTube while ads still collect opt-ins and deliver
  links to the same videos, creating both platform-native and paid lead flow.
- Calls to action should invite people who want implementation help, while the sales team
  follows up with course opt-ins.
- A revenue question on the form should separate qualified prospects from smaller leads
  before showing the scheduling page.
- Placing the advertising pixel only on the qualified scheduling path gives the algorithm
  a stronger signal for finding more economically relevant prospects.
