# Helping a $3.3M SaaS Business Fix 66% Weekly Churn

## Summary

Alex coaches a founder with a $3.3M AI SaaS for real estate agents and photographers
that adds about 150 subscribers weekly but loses about 100. Alex identifies onboarding,
activation, segmentation, and billing-cycle mismatch as the constraints. His advice is
to temporarily "atomic bomb" the problem with human group onboarding, collect channel
and avatar data, align billing to the customer's value cycle, and avoid scaling until
churn is fixed.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Alex first asks about onboarding, price, activation signals, and whether a human is
  involved.
- The founder should verify which activation actions actually correlate with retention.
- Alex says the company needs more hands-on onboarding in the short term, even if it is
  not scalable yet.
- He recommends two group Zoom onboardings per day, with users booked automatically.
- Watching customers use the product will reveal friction in the checklist and product
  flow.
- The business should segment data by channel, avatar, and activation actions to find
  the highest-converting and highest-LTV customers.
- If low-frequency customers only need the product quarterly, billing should align with
  the value cycle.
- Alex recommends testing annual-only pricing, while noting that keeping monthly can be
  useful because it creates a faster churn signal.
- He advises not scaling past the current level until churn is fixed; cutting churn by
  two-thirds could itself triple the business.
- The onboarding-call flow should use text, call, email, and the in-product next step.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "We need to atomic bomb this."

> "We want the value cycle to be aligned with the billing cycle."

> "I wouldn't even try to scale past where you're at right now until you fix this."
