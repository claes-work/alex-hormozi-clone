# Wealth Loves Time. Poverty Loves Indecision.

## Summary

Alex says career earnings depend on total value created, negotiation for a share of that
value, and how many other people can create the same value. He argues that uniqueness
usually comes from a stack of lived skills rather than one secret skill, and that time
invested wisely becomes a moat.

## Key claims

- Alex says the more money someone makes for other people, the more money they can make.
- He frames career earnings as a function of total value created, negotiated share of
  that value, and scarcity of people who can create it.
- He says a valuable skill such as sales does not guarantee exceptional pay if many
  others can create the same value for less.
- He says unique value usually comes from a stack of skills created by lived experience.
- He cites his own path through fitness, gyms, turnarounds, licensing, supplements, meal
  prep, software, exits, and private equity as a hard-to-copy skill stack.
- He says time invested wisely becomes an asset and competitive moat.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Your time is your weapon if you invest it wisely."

> "Money loves speed. Wealth loves time. Poverty loves indecision."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: wealth equation value x negotiation x scarcity; skill stack as moat; lived time as competitive advantage; money-speed/wealth-time/poverty-indecision aphorism.
