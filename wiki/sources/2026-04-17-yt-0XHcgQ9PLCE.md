# How to Get More Customers Without Ever Spending More on Advertising

## Summary

Alex teaches customer acquisition through leverage. The first-order question is how to
make the product so good that every buyer tells at least one other person. He then restates
the Core Four ways one person can make the market aware: warm outreach, cold outreach,
public content, and paid ads. The leverage version is getting customers, employees,
agencies, and affiliates to do those same awareness actions for the business.

## Key claims

- On 2026-04-17, Alex says founders should prefer plans that do not require luck.
- He says if every buyer told at least one other person, the business would not need to
  advertise again; therefore the product/referral problem deserves disproportionate focus.
- He defines leverage as the difference between what someone puts in and what they get out.
- He frames brand as leverage because it is built once and used many times.
- He says the only four things one person can do to let people know about an offer are:
  reach out to friends, reach out to strangers, post content publicly, or run paid ads.
- He says the leveraged version is getting customers, employees, agencies, and affiliates
  to perform those Core Four activities on the company's behalf.
- He says affiliates are businesses or influencers that already have the customer base.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If every person who bought my product told at least one other person, I would never need to advertise ever again."

> "Leverage... it's the difference between what you put in and what you get out."

> "Those are the only four things that one person can do to let other people know about their stuff."

## Synthesis candidate

Synthesis candidate: no-luck plans; product/referral as advertising replacement; leverage
definition; Core Four plus customers/employees/agencies/affiliates doing Core Four for you.
