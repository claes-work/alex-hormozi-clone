# Why I Give Away Free Products

## Summary
Short interview clip (an off-screen host asks the questions; Alex answers). The host praises *$100M Offers* — discovered via Audible, passed around his friend group — and thanks Alex for selling "that jam-packed value" as effectively a 99-cent service, then asks how Alex thought about pricing the book. Alex's answer lays out his **give-then-monetize / distribution-first** logic: the point of the book was never to make money from the book, it was to get the material into as many hands as possible and feed a **very long play** for Acquisition.com. He expects the biggest inflow of deals to arrive ~5 years out, from people who consumed his free/cheap material, grew to roughly $3–20M, and then want to work with Acquisition.com. Crucially, he argues **99 cents beats free for distribution**: giving the book away wouldn't have reached as many hands, because if Amazon makes money Amazon promotes the book. At 99 cents Amazon takes ~two-thirds of the revenue (vs. one-third at $2.99), and a 99-cent thing is easy to sell — so the small price *buys* Amazon's traffic and mass distribution. He adds a smaller psychological reason: there's an emotional difference between free and paid — paying makes someone "a customer" rather than "a voyeur." He downplays the sophistication of it ("there wasn't too much more thought than that"). The clip ends with his standard roadmap promo (the free $100M scaling roadmap / quiz at acquisition.com/roadmap, book-a-call, Vegas invite) — itself a live example of the same free-value-as-lead-gen mechanic.
