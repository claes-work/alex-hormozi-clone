# Helping a $600K Lawn Care Business Hit $25M (People Problem)

## Summary

A lawn-care franchise owner says the business reached about $600,000 in its first year
but is constrained by people: experienced technicians are not culture fits, and
culture-fit hires are not being trained successfully. Alex reframes the company as a
recruiting and training business, not merely a lawn-care business. He tells the caller to
raise prices because a 70% close rate indicates pricing power, pair speed incentives
with quality penalties, enforce rules, and build culture through observable behavior,
public praise, and correction.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-11)

- Alex says a lawn-care company with this model will always be constrained by talent.
- He frames recruiting, hiring, and onboarding as the process to nail before scaling.
- He says the company is supply constrained, which usually creates pricing power.
- He applies his pricing heuristic: close rates far above about 35% usually indicate room
  to raise prices.
- He recommends raising price by at least $10 per hour and possibly more.
- He says any performance KPI needs a paired metric, using quality and speed as the
  classic pair.
- He says if a technician cuts corners, the person should return on their own time and
  may lose bonuses or eventually the job if the behavior repeats.
- He says rules without enforcement are suggestions.
- He says the business owner must protect reputation because it can disappear quickly.
- He defines culture building as observable standards, demonstration, practice,
  immediate correction, public kudos, and repeated reinforcement until peers enforce the
  standard themselves.
