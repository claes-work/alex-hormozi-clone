# What Nobody Tells You About Scaling to $30M

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a sports-entertainment social-media services founder doing about $12M who wants to reach $25M-$30M. The founder sees a trained-bench constraint for specialized directors who can service large sports sponsors. Alex reframes recruiting as a mirrored acquisition problem: compare lifetime gross profit per employee to the cost of acquiring talent, then spend aggressively when the math works.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - founder of a sports-entertainment social-media services company. His clients, revenue, margin goals, and role economics are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- The company's stated bottleneck is a manpower and recruiting constraint.
- For specialized roles, the founder should calculate annual gross profit per employee and lifetime gross profit per employee.
- Alex explicitly analogizes LTV:CAC on the customer side to lifetime gross profit per employee versus cost of acquiring talent on the recruiting side.
- If a specialized person can produce about $1.25M in annual gross profit, paying a meaningful one-time recruiting fee is rational.
- Specialized one-off roles often justify paying outside recruiters rather than building a full internal acquisition process for that role.
- Repeated roles justify real recruiting infrastructure because they recur like a repeated customer-acquisition channel.
- Alex gives a portfolio-company example of hiring 80 salespeople to keep 50 by using many headhunting firms plus internal recruiting assets.
