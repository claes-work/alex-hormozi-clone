# What Would You Do If You Weren't Afraid?

## Summary

Alex describes fearlessness as a life goal and uses the question "What would you do if
you weren't afraid?" as a decision-making filter. He connects it to his content principle
of making only things he or his company can uniquely make, which evolved internally from
"one of one" to "one of zero." He also recalls quitting his job as still the hardest
decision he has made and says he reduced the fear by playing out the realistic downside.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice, dated 2026-05-12)

- Alex says one of his life goals is to be fearless.
- He uses "what would I do if I weren't afraid?" to identify the bigger action he
  probably should take.
- He says his content principle was originally "one of one": make only content that his
  team uniquely can make.
- He says the concept evolved into "one of zero," meaning doing things they do not yet
  know they can do.
- He says he does not regret failures as much as things he did not try.
- He says quitting his job remains the hardest decision he has made.
- He says he reasoned that even if entrepreneurship failed, he would have a strong story
  for business school or a future job.
- He says people catastrophize failure to death instead of playing it out a few realistic
  steps.
- He quotes Leila's frame that fear is "a mile wide and an inch deep."
