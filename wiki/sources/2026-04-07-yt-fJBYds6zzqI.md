# How to Know When to Rest and When to Keep Pushing

## Summary

Alex says his view of rest continues to evolve, but his April 2026 position rejects a
universal season-of-rest prescription. He prefers short, low-stimulus recovery and
sleeping until no longer tired, while maintaining fewer daily hours across more days. He
also treats work capacity as trainable and says his own capacity increased with practice.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex advises people to ignore external rest expectations and continue
  working if that is what they want.
- He self-reports taking 11 days off in the source year to date and returning early from
  a planned two-day resort trip with Leila; the exact recording date within 2026 is not
  independently established beyond publication.
- His preferred recovery is roughly three hours in a dark, low-stimulus room with an
  audiobook, Netflix, or pizza rather than a long vacation.
- Alex theorizes that working fewer hours per day across seven days can create more output
  than taking full days off.
- He self-reports sustaining roughly 6 a.m.-6 p.m. workdays, sometimes ending earlier on
  Sunday, and says he works more with more energy now than during Gym Launch.
- His current rule is to sleep when tired and continue once no longer tired; this is a
  personal practice, not medical guidance.
