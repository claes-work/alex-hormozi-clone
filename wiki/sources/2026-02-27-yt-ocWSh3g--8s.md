# 'I Serve 2 Avatars, How Do I Niche Down?'

## Summary
Short live-coaching clip. A creator (Abukar) runs a $50 Skool community serving **two different avatars** — people trying to break *into* tech roles, and *experienced* professionals seeking higher-paying salaries — and wants to scale past 1,000 members. He has a decent LinkedIn following but is new to video, and asks how to get maximum traffic and where to direct it (lead magnets, an email list, or straight to the Skool about page). Alex's answer: **pick one of the two avatars** because they are very different. His own preference — possibly counterintuitive — would be the *experienced* professionals, because they're already gainfully employed with earning potential, so you can deliver a **big delta in income with limited work on your part**. He frames this via his value/cost heuristic: he looks for the largest gap between how much value he can provide and how much it costs him to provide it, and "huge differences" there make "very interesting businesses." He suggests a possible offer structure: a six-month program with a **clear guaranteed outcome** — a raise in six months or it's free (or they continue until they get a raise), as long as they follow the process. On traffic, he'd run it **straight to the about page** as the simplest option, and adds that the about page converts much higher when you're very clear on **who** you're promising an outcome to.

## Key claims
- As of February 2026, when a business serves two very different avatars, Alex advises picking **one** — the two audiences require different messaging and offers.
- As of February 2026, given the choice between "breaking into tech" beginners and "experienced professionals seeking raises," Alex would prefer the **experienced** avatar because they already have earning potential, so a small amount of work can produce a large income increase for them.
