# How to Scale an In-Person Service Business

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a dance-school owner using the school as a test kitchen for a consolidator role. Alex calculates that the business has strong unit economics: roughly $100 CAC against $1,600 year-one revenue and four-year customer tenure. Because the four walls can handle more students, he says the constraint is advertising and lead handling, recommends materially increasing Google and Meta spend, and says hiring one full-time person to work leads is a straightforward use of profit.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - dance-school owner and equity partner in a consolidator. His revenue, retention, school-year sales cycle, market size, spend, and capacity are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- With 72% annual retention, the caller has roughly four years of value per student.
- If the facility can handle more students, the problem is primarily advertising.
- A $100 CAC against $1,600 annual revenue and multiple years of retention is extremely strong.
- The caller is not near maxed-out spend if total Google and Meta spend is about $1,500 per month in a 100,000-person market.
- Alex estimates the business could potentially reach $7,500 to $10,000 per month on Meta alone and more on Google before efficiency falls.
- Even if ad efficiency drops from very high levels to 2:1 or 3:1, the economics can still work.
- If lead handling is the constraint, hiring a full-time person for roughly $60,000 per year is justified by the upside.
- Content creation should continue as an additional support.
