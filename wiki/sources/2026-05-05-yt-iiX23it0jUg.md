# The Real Reason Your Meta Ads Aren't Converting

## Summary

Alex diagnoses a high-ticket holistic dentistry practice that misread paid-social lead
quality as an ad problem. He separates referral, PPC/search, and Meta lead temperature,
then argues the main failure was the sales/set process. For cold Meta leads, the practice
needed front-end screening (BANT), VSL pre-framing, financing/card-on-file process, and
creative that visually matches the premium buyer rather than the owner-manager.

## Key claims

- Compared with referrals, cold paid-social leads will look low quality; that does not
  mean the channel cannot work.
- Meta can provide more volume than search because it is interruption-based, but the leads
  are colder and require heavier screening.
- For high-ticket dental work, he expects roughly 2-5% of Meta leads to convert if the
  funnel and sales motion are right.
- The set call should qualify for BANT: budget, authority, need, and timing.
- Deposits solve show-rate problems; if show rate is not the issue, the call/set process
  is the higher-leverage fix.
- Meta creative finds people who look like the avatar in the ad, so the ad should feature
  the desired customer profile and premium signals.
- He recommends hiring a practitioner from a PPC dental agency for an hourly keyword audit
  rather than guessing at search setup.
