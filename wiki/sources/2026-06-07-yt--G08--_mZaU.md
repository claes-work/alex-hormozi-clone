# The Cobbler's Shoes Problem Killing Your Agency Growth

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a UK performance-marketing agency doing about $4.5M that wants to reach $1M/month. The owner says the agency cannot run enough of its own ads because the team is busy serving clients. Alex identifies a supply constraint and recommends either raising prices or reinvesting cash flow into talent. He broadens the lesson: low-capex service businesses should reinvest profit into A-player talent and brand plays, not only direct-response spend.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- When a service business has more demand than capacity, the obvious cash-flow move is to raise prices.
- If the owner refuses price increases, the lower-risk move is to hire into the capacity constraint with existing profit.
- Founder magic often outperforms delegated work; expect roughly a performance drop when replacing the founder, and model it.
- "Scale zero" means getting the founder's required percentage of a function to zero so the company can scale.
- Low-capex service businesses usually reinvest profit best into talent and brand, not inventory or equipment.
- National and international businesses cannot rely only on direct response as trust falls; brand investments compound over longer horizons.
- Direct response may produce better 30-day ROAS, while brand can produce much higher 12-month ROAS.
