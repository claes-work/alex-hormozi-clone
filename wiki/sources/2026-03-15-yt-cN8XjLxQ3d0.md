# Helping a B2B Consulting Firm Scale

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The owner of a B2B cybersecurity-compliance consulting firm (serving mid-market climbing to enterprise) explains that revenue slid from ~$5M in 2023 to ~$3.6M, with net margin dropping from 35% to 18% as he reinvested in people and shifted his own focus away from selling toward "learning how to become a real CEO." Historically he was the sole rainmaker doing all outbound (networking, calls, conferences); conferences got too expensive post-COVID ("pay to play, pay to speak"), so lead flow fell. Average deal is worth ~$75–80k. Alex diagnoses him as **demand-constrained** (he confirms he can handle more customers), so the whole prescription is about generating more leads, not more delivery capacity. Alex's frame: outbound is what he's good at, so either keep going one-on-one or "go hunt where there's fish in a barrel" — conferences, where you "fish with a net." He maps the lone-operator dynamic as a **crisscross**: outbound rises between events, then you lean into an event, close a burst, leads deplete, outbound climbs again. The fix for stability is to hire an **SDR** (sales development representative) to set and qualify appointments so the owner just closes — cheap and the owner's biggest time constraint. Notably, the recruiter already embedded in the business is a good SDR candidate: recruiting is largely outbound, so SDR work is "the closest comparable role." Alex also endorses **LinkedIn thought leadership** as a natural fit — "all you do to be a thought leader is take the stuff you're doing privately and just talk about it publicly," i.e. talk about client work. Sequencing: run conferences now (fast, he has the cash flow to fund it) to fill the pipeline, and concurrently run ads for an SDR so recurring outbound leads stack on top of event leads over time. He caps the plan at a realistic two conferences a month given the owner's family constraints. Ends with the roadmap promo.
