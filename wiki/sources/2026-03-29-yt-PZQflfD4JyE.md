# What is ignorance debt?

## Summary
Responding to an interviewer's questions, Alex Hormozi defines "ignorance debt" as the gap between the behavior that would maximize a person's trajectory toward a goal and their current behavior — the difference between "actual and should," which he equates with the unknown unknown. He argues the debt is largest early in a journey and far from the top of a field, and that the fastest way to close it is to spend time directly with people who have already reached the top. He illustrates measurement with the pasta-tower exercise and the Silicon Valley "zero-billion-dollar business" idea, and shares his consulting-derived research method of interviewing the smartest people in a domain until the referral names start repeating.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex defines ignorance debt as the delta between the behavior that maximizes your trajectory toward a goal and your current behavior set — the difference between "actual and should," and another framing of the unknown unknown.
- As of March 2026, Alex says the size of the debt is roughly proxied by how far you are from the top of a field; it is typically largest early in the journey, with the caveat that some people are on the right path and simply need more time.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues wealthier people (among those who want to make money) tend to have a more accurate view of reality, and that a distorted view impairs the poorer person's ability to get what they want because their actions don't yield expected outcomes.
- As of March 2026, Alex favors entrepreneurship because the marketplace provides objective, fast feedback loops that reveal errors quickly.
- As of March 2026, Alex claims the fastest way to build a $10M business differs from the fastest way to build a billion-dollar business, invoking the "zero-billion-dollar business" (same foundation as a zero-million one) and citing OpenAI as valued in the hundreds of billions despite arguably making no money.
- As of March 2026, Alex warns advice is context-dependent — software/billion-dollar-company advice can be disadvantageous for, e.g., a lawn-care service — and that internet clips strip context because context "kills the clip's reach," making the ability to judge advice increasingly important.
- As of March 2026, Alex's method for closing ignorance debt in a new domain: spend time with the top performers, who reveal the unknown unknowns; he learned this as a management consultant.
- As of March 2026, Alex describes his research method: ask the smartest person for the five smartest people they know, interview each with every question, and keep asking for five more until the names repeat and the network maps out.
- As of March 2026, Alex says he used this exact method over two weeks to hold ~30 conversations with people who had exited between $30M and $3B while considering an investment in banking/payments/credit cards, concluding he did not want to enter that space.
- As of March 2026, Alex notes people fear that absorbing a field's behaviors will turn them into someone else, but says you can always delete or add behaviors based on your own personality.
