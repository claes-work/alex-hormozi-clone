# The Price Tag of Your Goals is What Makes Them Worthwhile

## Summary

Alex argues that worthwhile goals are worthwhile because they have a cost. If a goal
were immediately available, it would be available to everyone and would lose value. He
connects this to a strict version of self-love: believing in an 85-year-old ideal self
and ruthlessly comparing the present self against that standard.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-10)

- Alex says every worthwhile goal has a cost associated with it.
- He says the cost is part of what makes the goal worthwhile.
- He says one cannot resent the price tag of a desired goal; one decides whether to pay
  it.
- He rejects a self-love frame centered only on acceptance and belonging.
- He frames self-love as holding oneself to a higher standard than anyone else does.
- He describes the standard as believing in an 85-year-old ideal version of oneself and
  confronting the gap between that ideal and the current self.
- He says an earlier version of himself did not need to feel deserving of success to
  still obtain it by doing the actions that create success.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The very fact that it's difficult is why it is worth doing."

> "We can't resent the price tag."

> "I didn't need to deserve success, but I could still have it."
