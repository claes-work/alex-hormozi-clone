# The Hiring Trick That Filters Out 90% of Candidates

## Summary

Alex explains how Acquisition.com uses real work samples instead of purely hypothetical
cases. For senior leadership, he prefers real company problems because the candidate's
frameworks and experience become visible. For repeated roles such as sales or editing,
he uses structured tests: a script read, coaching and retry, or raw footage edited into
a clip, so the team can assess skill, coachability, ego, and learning speed.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Consulting-style case questions test how a candidate thinks, not just the answer.
- Acquisition.com uses real cases for higher-level leadership roles.
- Real cases can produce useful insight for the business and reveal whether the
  candidate can implement the solution.
- Repeated roles need structured interview processes.
- For sales candidates, Alex sends a script and has candidates perform a short sound
  bite in a group setting.
- The test filters for skill or work ethic quickly.
- Giving feedback and asking the candidate to retry reveals coachability, ego, and speed
  of learning.
- For editors, Alex prefers seeing edited output from raw footage rather than judging
  interview performance.
- Interviewing is a skill, but the job may require a different skill.
- Some smart people communicate poorly, and the importance of communication depends on
  the role.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Either they don't have the skill or they don't have the work ethic."
