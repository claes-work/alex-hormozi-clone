# How to Get an Online Coaching Business to $1M/Month

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises the same overseas-Chinese entrepreneur community founder covered in [[2026-06-19-yt-AgsBAVcC2ls]]. The clip repeats the central recurring-revenue lesson: education is valuable once, while continuity requires consumable inputs, network, access, discounts, or other value that renews. It also repeats Alex's credibility answer: a partner spreading his message would need a large proof point because the messenger supplies most of the context in which a message is believed.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Education has high one-time value and weak continuity unless recurring value is added.
- Recurring value should be consumable, such as fresh tested assets, deals, news, network access, negotiated discounts, or other inputs.
- Gym Launch's monthly tested ads are Alex's example of recurring inputs that kept the business sticky.
- Niching enables recurring input creation because the provider can test and provide specific assets for a specific group.
- A smaller recurring component can be viable if it remains sticky and delivers a multiple of its price in value.
- More members should ideally increase community value rather than dilute it.
- Partnership or message distribution requires bulletproof proof, not just repeating the words.
- Authority is compelling because it lowers perceived risk and cognitive load.
- Alex says high-agency people should separate messenger from message, while most audiences do not.
