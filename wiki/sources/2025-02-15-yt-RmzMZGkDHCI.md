# The Secrets to building an Online Community | ft.Alex Hormozi

## Summary

Alex gives a direct-response Skool pitch built around how to design an online community business. He starts with "who is it for," recommends shared interest/problem/goal communities, explains the expert vs. crowdsourced model, argues that beginners can start free and later switch to paid, and maps community value to access, content, events, and the $100M Offers value equation.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- **2025-02-15 - Community avatar**: Alex says the first design question is who the community is for: people with a shared interest, shared goal, or shared problem.
- **2025-02-15 - Scratch your own itch**: Alex says founders can start with a problem they personally want solved, even if they are not already the expert.
- **2025-02-15 - Expert vs. crowdsourced model**: Alex distinguishes expert-led communities from crowdsourced communities, where members help find answers and bring them back to the group.
- **2025-02-15 - Community as leverage**: Alex says a community lets the owner "punch above" their individual status because the group provides distribution to outside experts.
- **2025-02-15 - Free-to-paid path**: Alex recommends starting free if confidence is low, learning what the audience values, and later switching to paid once the community has momentum.
- **2025-02-15 - Community value stack**: Alex says paid communities can provide access, exclusive content, and events; these should be scheduled or cadenced so members know what they receive.
- **2025-02-15 - Value equation for communities**: Alex applies the value equation to communities: make the desired outcome less risky, faster, and easier or lower-sacrifice.
- **2025-02-15 - Audience asset**: Alex says the audience is the most valuable asset built over time, and that doing the community right can matter more than monetizing immediately.
- **2025-02-15 - Promotional statistics**: Alex claims one out of two paid Skool communities make money and that the average paid community owner earns $1,360 per month. Treat these as platform promotional claims, not independently verified statistics.
