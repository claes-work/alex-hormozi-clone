# Helping a Marketing Agency Scale | Hormozi Hotline

## Summary
Hotline call. The caller runs a ~12-year-old digital marketing agency (started 2012 doing Arabic SEO after living in the Middle East for four years) that now helps clinics attract Arabic-speaking patients for elective procedures — plastic surgery, cosmetic dental/veneers — in Gulf markets like Saudi Arabia and the UAE. He does ~$300k revenue, ~$150k take-home, churns ~50% of clients per year, and feels the market is too small: his outbound (Instantly, Apollo) exhausts the entire prospect list — maybe 150 cosmetic surgeons in a country — within a week. Alex diagnoses **two separate problems**. First, a **retention/structural-churn** problem: the caller sells to very small businesses, whose own volatility becomes the agency's volatility, so some churn is baked in. Second, an **acquisition** problem: outbound isn't a bad channel, but the caller has simply run out of a reliable way to source enough leads. Alex's primary fix is to **switch from outbound to paid ads** — with modern targeting ("Andromeda") you can stay specific yet keep targeting open by running one campaign per procedure type (dental cosmetics, ocular-facial, facial, noses, necks, breast augmentation, etc.). There are far more than 150 of each, and tens of millions of people in the region. His bigger strategic reframe attacks the caller's core assumption: the caller sees low Gulf competition as an advantage, but Alex says he's doing **"reverse market arbitrage"** — living in a high-cost market (US) and selling into a cheaper one, so he's constantly undercut by cheap local freelancers ("I could hire five people for the price I'm paying you"). The ideal is the opposite: live cheap, sell to the rich market. Alex's decision rule on niching: **if you're not good, niche down to win a tiny pond; if you're genuinely good, go where the competition is, because that's where all the money is.** Since the caller says he's really good and has 12 years of skill, Alex tells him he may have "leveled up" and should consider expanding to the US — the market with the most buying power — and just compete. Closes with the acquisition.com roadmap promo (excluded from claims).
