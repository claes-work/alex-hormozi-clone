# If You're in Your 20s or 30s, Here's How to Win (at Anything)

## Summary

Solo talk in which Alex Hormozi argues that "more" (sheer volume) is the highest
risk-adjusted return move available in business and in life. He contends that most
entrepreneurs wildly underestimate both the volume required to win and their own
capacity for it, and that they sabotage themselves by constantly changing things —
change has a fixed cost (~20% output decrement) and only a variable reward. He frames
winning as becoming "the exception," backs it with anecdotes (Chiron scaling REAL from
$200M to $1.2B via 260 events/year, his own high-school/college conversion from
optimizer to maximizer, and the 2,800 ads behind his $105M "$100M Money Models" book
launch), and closes with a repeatable decision loop: pick the volume goal, ask what
resources it requires, ask if it's worth it, then remove what's stopping you.

## Key claims

- "More" is the highest risk-adjusted return move in business: once something works, it
  is statistically unlikely that a different new thing will also work, so the smart bet
  is to jam more volume into the machine that already works rather than roll the dice on
  something new.
- The big unlocks in volume occur precisely at the point where people say "there's no
  way I can do more." Napoleon (possibly misattributed): "quantity has a quality unto
  itself."
- Most business owners wildly underestimate both the volume required and the volume they
  are capable of; capability is always higher than what is required.
- Maximizers beat optimizers on returns. Optimizers ask "how do I get as much as I can
  out of as little as I can?"; maximizers ask "how do I get as much as I possibly can?"
- Diminishing returns are still returns (still output); absolute returns matter more
  than relative returns. He'd rather spend $1M to get $2M back (2:1) than spend $10K to
  get $100K back (10:1) — because it's more money net.
- Change has a fixed cost and a variable reward: changing something (with people
  involved) typically causes ~20% output decrement first, with only maybe ~5% eventual
  upside, and it might not work at all. Doing nothing yields ~1-3% month-over-month
  gains as people naturally get more skilled.
- His rule: only take a change/bet if projected impact exceeds the guaranteed ~20% loss;
  he picks roughly one big bet per year. Evaluate bets with the ICE frame — Impact
  (how big), Confidence (how likely), Ease (resources required).
- Business strategy analogy (credited to Chiron): don't cook only with what's already
  in the fridge — decide what you actually want to eat, then go get the ingredients.
- To win you must sacrifice a normal life; being top 0.1% / 0.01% requires an
  exceptional (abnormal) life that most people will reject — and you must reject them in
  return. "Oil and water do not mix."
- Secret to success: (1) get better, (2) never stop. On a long enough time horizon you
  win. "The work begins when your motivation ends."
- The four ways to get customers ("Core Four"): warm outreach, cold outreach, content,
  paid ads. Do "more" of each — for ads: more creative, more money, more platforms,
  attacked in reverse order of risk (creative first, then spend, then platforms).
- The $105M+ "$100M Money Models" book launch was driven by volume: ~2,800 ads made
  (2,000+ banked before the 6-week window), spending up to $500K/day at the end;
  scaling from 25 ads/day (5 editors) to the needed pace required contracting 10 more
  editors (15 total).
- To beat someone ahead of you, don't match their visible volume (3x) — do 10-30x,
  because they also do volume you can't see. If someone is doing better, they are better
  in some way; learn from them rather than throwing rocks.

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)

- "The fundamental question every single business owner needs to answer... is why can't
  I do more? And for most people, doing more is the answer."
- "Your capability is always higher than what is required. But the thing is is that you
  might not know it yet."
- "You cannot make yourself exceptional and live a normal life. To make yourself
  exceptional, you must live an exceptional life... You must become the exception."
- "Number one, get better. Number two, never stop. If you do only those two things, you
  will win on a long enough time horizon."
- "The work begins when your motivation ends. Just win."
- "If necessity is the mother of invention, repetition is the father of skill."
- "The times I've made the most money in my life have not been when I've been changing
  the most. It's actually when the business has been really boring and we're just
  blocking and tackling and doing it over and over again."
- "A lot of people use the business to satisfy their own ADD, to satisfy their own need
  for novelty when the business thrives on same."
- "I would take a million in to get two back every day of the week and twice on Sunday.
  Why? Because it's more. It's still more. It's absolute returns."
- "Sometimes you just have to do a violent unreasonable amount of work for an extended
  period of time."
- "If someone is doing better than you, they are better than you in some way. And in
  that, you can learn of them."
