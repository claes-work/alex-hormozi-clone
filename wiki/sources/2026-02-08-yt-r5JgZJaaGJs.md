# How Intuition Actually Works

★ L3-candidate — a compact, distinctive mental model of intuition (as accumulated pattern-recognition over identifiable-but-unlabeled variables, not magic) that sharpens the decision-making thread in mindset/ and beliefs.

## Summary
This short MoreMozi clip captures Alex Hormozi answering how much he trusts intuition when making a decision. His core argument: intuition is not a mystical power but "a really amorphous word for having a history of reinforcement with variables that you can identify or have trouble identifying." A gut feeling — e.g. a bad vibe about a person walking in — is the subconscious matching present cues (dress, hair, makeup, speech, mannerisms, the way someone carries themselves) against past experiences that ended badly, without your being able to consciously name the matching variable. He will pay attention to intuition but refuses to treat it as magic; instead he sees it as a signal pointing at a variable he simply hasn't yet identified, and one he could often surface by deliberately thinking it through ("it wasn't this, it wasn't this... this is what it was"). The clip closes with a promotion for his free 10-stage zero-to-$100M+ roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap.

- (2026-02-08) A gut feeling (e.g. "I've got a bad feeling about her") arises because you have seen many similar cases before, and your subconscious is matching present cues against a past negative experience.
