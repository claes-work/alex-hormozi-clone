# The Best Offer for a Construction Company

## Summary
Live coaching clip in which Alex designs an ideal offer for a custom home builder / general contractor. He opens with his value framework — every deal is priced on **risk, speed, ease** (people want the outcome low-risk, fast, and easy) — then constructs the offer around it: the **on-time, on-budget guarantee**. The builder promises to deliver the home on time and on budget or refunds his profit on the deal (translated to "20% of the deal"), and this guarantee is **paired with a 20% price increase**. The key structural insight: the guarantee has a hard carve-out — it only holds **as long as the homeowner doesn't change their mind** about the scope. Since homeowners almost always change something, the builder is "almost never on the hook," yet the guarantee still closes the deal at a premium. Alex then scripts the sales pitch, anchoring against the industry norm: most custom home jobs go over budget by 10–20% (on a $2M home, a 15% overage is $300k) and run 3–9 months late, costing the buyer both the overage and lost home appreciation during the delay. Against that baseline, a builder who is genuinely on-time/on-budget is "already saving you $300,000," so his "measly $250,000 fee is nothing — you're already in the black." He adds an appraisal/value-engineering angle (building the right things for a better appraisal-to-cost ratio) and a comedic scope-change illustration ("if you want gold toilets, I can't do on-time on-budget"). He closes by noting the math works even in the rare case the builder is held to the guarantee: what you make on the other 95% of deals more than covers the occasional payout. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
