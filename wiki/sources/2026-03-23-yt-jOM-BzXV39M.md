# Helping a Marketing Agency Scale

## Summary
In this short coaching clip, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of an influencer-relations agency (B2B on both sides: sells influencers to marketing agencies/companies, and manages influencers on the back end) who wants to grow from ~$1M/year to $1M/month. The owner says their main constraint is awareness — companies and influencers can't find out who they are, partly because satisfied clients on both sides have an incentive not to refer competitors. Hormozi diagnoses word-of-mouth as a weak growth channel for this B2B model and recommends immediately running paid ads, using one of their own influencers to tell their story in the creative.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex argues that a two-sided B2B business (selling to companies and to influencers) is a poor fit for word-of-mouth growth, because success on either side gives clients an incentive NOT to tell competitors — so its runway for referrals is limited and it will likely need a cold-traffic sales motion.
- As of March 2026, Alex's default recommendation for this agency is to run paid ads, and to feature one of their own influencers telling their story in the ad, then target that ad at other prospects (e.g. audiences over a follower threshold).
- As of March 2026, Alex frames the agency's offer as unusually easy to advertise because it is simple and "something for almost nothing" — the pitch to influencers is "you have following, I give you money," and to marketers "you have no brand; we have people with brand and audience."
- As of March 2026, Alex notes that if a business had a working referral strategy he would "juice" that first, but absent one, cold traffic is the more reliable path for this type of company (stated as a generalization from having seen many similar companies).
- Caller claims (unverified): the agency did ~$250K revenue the prior year and ~$1M the following year, grew mostly organically via word of mouth, and profited ~$900K on the $1M — used by the owner to argue they can afford to fund an ads push.

## Notes
Synthesis candidate: The heuristic that two-sided B2B businesses where clients have an incentive to hide their vendor are structurally weak for word-of-mouth and should default to cold-traffic ads — a specific referral-vs-paid diagnostic worth capturing in marketing topics.
