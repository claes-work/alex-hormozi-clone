# 5 Ways To Increase Lead Quality

## Summary

In this 2020 Gym-Launch-era tactical video, Hormozi lays out five marketing levers a
business owner can pull to raise lead quality, while stressing that each lever raises
quality but usually lowers volume. His overarching argument is that most people should
fix the monetization model behind the leads rather than tweak the front end: start with
the widest possible net to get flow, then engineer offers, upsells, and follow-up to
afford the traffic.

## Key claims

*All paraphrased from Alex Hormozi, 2020-10-13, unless quoted.*

- (2020) There are five ways to increase lead quality; each increases quality but
  typically **decreases volume**, so they are trade-off variables you tune for optimal
  sales throughput, not a checklist to apply all at once.
- (2020) The five ways:
  1. **Targeting** — narrow by zip code, income level, or profession (e.g. only rich
     zip codes). Higher quality, but those segments are more competitive, more expensive,
     and lower volume.
  2. **Number of steps** — more steps/hoops before the prospect reaches you means they
     took more action, so they are more in pain, more compelled by the offer, and more
     qualified.
  3. **Nature of the offer** — a free giveaway opt-in vs. a "click here to schedule a
     sales call" produces very different prospects; giveaways yield far more volume,
     direct-to-call yields more qualified leads. "Not all leads are created equal."
  4. **Application** — adding an application (a variant of adding steps); long paragraph
     answers screen harder than checkbox/ABC questions. Use application psychology: make
     them state their goal, admit a problem, name their biggest unknowns, set the
     precedent of expense, and optionally ask a minimum-spend question (e.g. "are you
     willing to spend at least $500?").
  5. **Provide value first** — the ad/video content itself; a genuinely valuable,
     tactical video gives authoritative positioning so prospects arrive already trusting
     him and more likely to pay.
- (2020) His personal preference: **start with the biggest, widest net to get flow**,
  then work backwards — build offers, upsells, indoctrination sequences, and preframes to
  monetize that flow. The worst thing is having no flow at all.
- (2020) You should know your monetization curve per dollar in (e.g. "$1 in → $1 back at
  day 15 → $3.50 at day 30 → $7 at day 60"); without those numbers, marketing decisions
  are much harder.
- (2020) The right time to apply these qualifiers is when volume is so high and quality
  so low that you willingly sacrifice a third or half of throughput — either to raise
  ticket price or to fit your **operational capacity** (e.g. deliberately doubling lead
  cost to "put a fence up" when the team can't handle the flow).
- (2020) "Put the onus on the model rather than on the marketing and typically you'll
  make more money." He says he rinse-and-repeated this same model across the gym,
  supplement company, Gym Launch, and the new online fitness model — where owners keep
  the same average price point but get roughly **one-fifth the cost per lead** and
  unlimited lead flow.
