# Helping a $1.3M Service Business Scale to $10M

## Summary

Alex treats a guarantee for a TEDx-booking service as expected-value math rather than a
binary risk. He illustrates with a home-service contractor who raised price by 20% and
promised to return the profit if the job missed time or budget. If the guarantee improves
conversion more than its expected payouts cost, it increases profit despite occasional
claims.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says guarantee design is a math problem built from conversion
  lift, claim frequency, and payout size.
- In his self-reported contractor example, eight of ten projects finished on time and
  budget; one changed scope and one suffered material delay.
- He recommended a 20% price increase paired with returning the entire 20% profit if the
  contractor failed to deliver on time and budget.
- Alex self-reports that the change increased both close volume and profit; the result is
  not independently verified here.
- Fear of a guarantee can be justified, but the decision should compare expected gains
  from more sales with expected refund cost.
- The TEDx business's revenue, success rate, and booking-time figures are owner-reported.
