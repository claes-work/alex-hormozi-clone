# The Highest ROI Investment an Entrepreneur Can Make

## Summary

Alex says talent is the highest-return capital allocation entrepreneurs can make. He
describes settling as the core hiring mistake, explains why great hires feel like "I
have to have this person," and gives a practical firing filter based on role need and
the person's level of incompetence.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says the easy answer to common hiring mistakes is settling.
- He says the right senior hire often creates a feeling of needing the person even if
  the role is not already perfectly defined.
- He says he will pay more than expected if the ROI of the role pencils out.
- He says talent is one of the highest-return investments for entrepreneurs, with the
  highest arbitrage often higher in the organization.
- He says firing speed depends partly on whether the role is the current business
  constraint.
- He uses yellow, orange, and red categories: incomplete competence, inability to do
  the current role, and inability in a role required for growth.
- He says some people improve with feedback and coaching.
- He frames slow firing as choosing local comfort over global harm to the company and
  the people who trusted it with their careers.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The highest returns on capital we get as entrepreneurs is talent."
