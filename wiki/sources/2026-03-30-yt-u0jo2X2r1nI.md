# Helping a Financial Services Business Scale

## Summary
In this short coaching clip, Alex Hormozi advises the owner of a business-loan company whose only lead source is referrals from previous clients and who has plateaued. The owner reports low-ticket loans (~$7,000 average, ~$1,500–$2,000 gross profit each) and wants a scalable acquisition method. Hormozi recommends paid ads or, preferably, referral partners (affiliates) such as credit-repair companies, and explains the unit economics of why affiliates beat cold outbound at that margin. The clip ends with the standard invitation to Acquisition.com's Vegas workshop.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends that a low-ticket business-loan company acquire customers through paid ads or referral partners, calling these the two channels most likely to succeed at that ticket price.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues the winning referral-partner strategy is to convert a higher percentage of a partner's customers than competitors do, which lets you pay the partner more and out-compete other referral recipients.
- As of March 2026, Alex frames credit-repair companies as natural referral partners, since their customers become loan-ready after repair, letting you extend the partner's LTV by getting those customers bigger loans and paying the partner.
- As of March 2026, Alex says cold outbound is very tight economically at ~$1,500–$2,000 gross profit per loan, whereas an affiliate costing ~$10,000 to acquire but delivering ~20 customers/month ($40,000/month) makes the metrics work.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises telling referral partners exactly what customer you want (e.g. $50,000+ loans) and paying "fatter slices" to attract those larger deals.
- Caller claim (unverified): the owner's average loan is ~$7,000 with ~$1,500–$2,000 gross profit, they have three or four clients at the $50,000–$100,000 level, and all current business comes from past-client referrals.

## Notable verbatim quotes
> "For that business, I would probably, especially the ticket price you're at, I would probably go ads or referral partners. So those would be the two channels that I would see as the most likely to succeed."

> "The way that you can win there is if you convert a higher percentage of their customers than other people do, then you'll be able to pay them more. Because a lot of people waste leads."

> "There's plenty of companies that just like send the leads and don't even ask for anything back. And so you can be more aggressive on the acquisition to them."

> "Maybe it cost me 10 grand to acquire an affiliate, but that affiliate is going to send me 20 customers a month. And so it cost me 10 to make 40,000 a month. And then the metrics make amazing sense."

> "If you have referral partners, you tell them who you're looking for. We specialize in people with $50,000 loans or higher. ... And then you can pay them even fatter slices, so they might be interested in that."
