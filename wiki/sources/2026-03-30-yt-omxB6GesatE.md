# Helping a Home Services Business Get More Leads

## Summary

In this short coaching exchange, Alex Hormozi advises a roofing-and-windows business owner who has burned through three ad agencies without getting usable leads. Hormozi lays out the common growth levers for home services (referral partners, PPC, social, plus word of mouth), gives a checklist for vetting agencies, and argues that tangential-business referral partnerships are the fastest path given the owner's economics ($8,000 margin per customer supports a ~$1,000 referral fee). The caller's own business relies mostly on in-person organic networking.

## Key claims

- As of March 2026, Alex frames the most common home-services growth channels as: referral partners (tangential home-services businesses whose reps go into customers' homes), PPC, and social — besides word of mouth.
- As of March 2026, Alex advises evaluating agencies by whether they share best practices; an agency that can't articulate the funnel, offer, lead-response timing, and scripts is a red flag ("if an agency does not give you best practices, they suck").
- As of March 2026, Alex says the highest-converting agencies emphasize calling leads within 5 minutes, and that many good agencies will also work the leads because business owners are generally bad at working leads themselves.
- As of March 2026, Alex recommends talking to about 10 agencies before buying, rather than "dating two people and choosing one," and negotiating a 30-day results trial.
- As of March 2026, Alex's litmus test for being ready to decide is when you stop learning new things on the vetting calls; he says it takes him about 15 calls with experts to understand a space.
- As of March 2026, Alex argues referral partnerships with tangential home-services businesses (carpet cleaning, landscaping) are the fastest, easiest path here: a $1,000 referral fee is attractive when a customer yields $8,000 in margin.
- Alex references "the agency chapter" (from his own book) as recommended reading on the buying process.
- Unverified caller claims: the business grew mostly via in-person organic networking; three agencies failed to produce results; leads that came in were irrelevant, disputed the opt-in, or some didn't even own a home; average customer is worth $8,000 in margin.
