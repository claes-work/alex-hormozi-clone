# How Do I Manage a Business When I Can't Be In Person?

## Summary
Live Q&A clip. A viewer asks how to hire and set up systems for a liquidation/resale business located ~100 miles away, where they can't be present full-time but could "stop by occasionally." Alex reframes the problem around what a **system** actually is: not people remembering to do things or "checklists that exist in your head," but **a prompt that triggers a behavior or action and does not rely on memory**. To design systems you have two routes — automation that prompts people, or a person that prompts people — and either way, at a location you're not at, you need **someone on the ground to observe what needs to occur and watch the system** get built. So his first move is to put a **general manager in place** to watch the staff and build the systems, even though he's not always in favor of hiring that role from the very start. His deeper point ties to leadership presence (he mentions writing a book on leadership): **the presence of a leader matters because you have to demonstrate what good looks like** — if you can't be there to set that bar, someone else has to demonstrate and set it, and only once that person has established the standard can systems (automation, reminders, checklists) keep prompting "what good looks like." Bottom line: "if you're not going to be there, somebody needs to be there." Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo (the 10-stage zero-to-$100M+ roadmap).

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex defines a system as "a prompt that triggers a behavior or an action and does not rely on memory" — he concedes it's not the most technically correct definition, but "people get it."
- As of March 2026, what is NOT a system: people remembering to do something and doing it because they remembered, "checklists that exist in your head," and people who know how to do things but follow no guidance.
- As of March 2026, there are two routes to designing a system: automation that prompts people, or a person that prompts people.
- As of March 2026, to run a location you're not physically at, you need someone on the ground to observe what needs to occur and to watch the system being built — the first hire he'd consider is a general manager to watch the staff and build the systems.
- As of March 2026, he is not always in favor of hiring a general manager from the very start, but for an absentee owner far from the location it becomes necessary.
- As of March 2026, leadership presence matters because a leader has to demonstrate what good looks like; if the owner isn't there to demonstrate it, someone else must be there to demonstrate and set that bar.
- As of March 2026, systems (automation, reminders, checklists) only work to keep prompting "what good looks like" AFTER a present person has understood and set the standard for it.
- As of March 2026, Alex noted he is writing a book on leadership.
