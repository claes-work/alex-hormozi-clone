# Helping a Wholesale Business Doing $22M/Year Get to $50M/Year

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client runs a ~$22M/year cannabis/hemp **distribution** business — buying flower from California farms and distributing it to hemp dispensaries across the country (a distributor, not a grower) — and wants to reach $50M. His stated blocker is **regulatory uncertainty**: a few states that are key markets are changing their rules, and that fear is stopping him from reinvesting and expanding the sales team. He floats a hedge: maybe pivot into other cannabis-space product categories he could sell into his existing customer base rather than just flower. Alex reframes the whole thing as a decision-making problem about **acting on known information vs. trying to guess the future**. His diagnosis: regulation is the #1 concern in heavily-regulated spaces (like healthcare too), but everyone's fear is that it ends *tomorrow*, when in reality you almost always have far more runway than you think — bills get advance notice, they have to be voted on, there's an effective date after the vote, and effective dates usually carry a **phase-in** because regulators don't want to abruptly disrupt business. So he operates on a "run it this way until we *know*" basis; otherwise you get frozen in uncertainty indefinitely. He invokes Ray Dalio (whom he admires) as the cautionary case — predicting the world's end for ~14 years and being "almost right" the whole time, while anyone who fled to gold missed one of the best stock-appreciation runs in US history. The prescription: **capitalize on the opportunity while it's there**; keep aggressively expanding the sales team and distribution footprint as business-as-usual; and only when you get a real heads-up that a market is closing do you aggressively push the other products into the same distribution base. He notes that sourcing additional products and training the reps to sell them is probably not that hard, so the pivot is available *if and when* it's needed — no need to pre-hedge against a future that may not come. Ends with the standard acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
