# The Ruthless Focus That Got Me to My First Million

## Summary

Alex Hormozi recounts the mindset that carried him to his first million: a "ruthless focus on dollars per hour." He describes anchoring on his first $500 sale (made in 30 minutes) and thereafter outsourcing or eliminating anything that earned him less than that rate. His central productivity thesis is inverted: rather than writing to-do lists, write a "to-sacrifice" list — cut everything non-essential so only the one money-making activity remains. He closes with a promotional CTA for the acquisition.com scaling roadmap.

## Key claims

- The lever that got him to his first million was a "ruthless focus on dollars per hour" — trading away everything possible for more time on the single money-making activity.
- After his first $500 sale (30 minutes of work), he set a rule: anything earning less than ~$250/hour ($500 per 30 min) gets handed to someone else.
- One sale could pay for a month of hired help (cooking, grocery shopping, cleaning), so he outsourced all of it.
- The goal is to optimize the "input to output being money equation" — pour everything into the input, eliminate everything else.
- Writing down what you're *willing to sacrifice* is more useful than a to-do list; if you sacrifice everything, nothing is left except the one thing that matters.
- Cites Jerry Seinfeld's writing discipline (sits for ~2 hours daily; not required to write, but not allowed to do anything else) as a model for forced focus.
- Most people who aren't achieving what they want are undisciplined with their time and overestimate their real output — "you don't even know how to work."
- The ultimate productivity hack is "no" — stop doing non-essential things, create space, then fill it with what matters. Adding more to a schedule you won't cut from is why you're not doing what you need to do.
