# You Don't Have a Brand, You Have an Arbitrage

## Summary

In this short coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a DTC skincare founder doing about $10M with 15% margins who wants to reach $100M and build for exit. Alex says the low margins come from lacking brand, not merely from model or subscription mechanics. The founder is stuck in the direct-response doom loop: spending more to reach less profitable audiences while margins compress. Alex argues the strategic fix is learning brand as the core e-commerce skill: building positive associations, extending attribution windows to 6-12 months, and thinking like a capital allocator rather than a 30-day ROAS performance marketer.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **DTC skincare founder** - the coachee. His revenue, margin, products, subscription idea, rebrand, and exit goal are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- In DTC physical-product e-commerce, the core skill is brand; it cannot be fully outsourced to a branding agency.
- Scaling direct-response spend without brand raises CPA and compresses margins because the business reaches less profitable audiences.
- The founder is in the direct-response / ROAS doom loop: spend more, make less, then spend more again until revenue exists without profit.
- A business that is only media arbitrage and white-label product is not meaningfully sellable long term.
- To scale long-term, roughly 70% of advertising may need to become brand-driven rather than direct-to-buy.
- Brand advertising builds founder story, narrative, differentiation, top-of-funnel awareness, and associations with things the ideal avatar values.
- Branding means making associations with positive things in the audience's mind; eventually the borrowed association remains attached to the product.
- Brand can let a company charge more, sell more often, get higher CTRs, and improve conversion rates.
- CRO, subscriptions, cart-value work, and LTV tactics can create a one-time lift, but they do not solve the front-end commoditization problem.
- Brand requires a longer time horizon, roughly 6-12 months, and capital-allocation thinking rather than 30-60 day direct-response attribution.
