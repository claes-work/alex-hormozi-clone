# How to Find Your Ideal Customer (Most Businesses Miss This)

## Summary

Alex gives his process for narrowing an avatar: examine all customers, find the top
10-20% who got the best outcomes, and identify the one to four traits that distinguish
them from everyone else. He recommends looking across demographic, psychographic,
geographic, and activity-based filters. The practical version is to identify the clients
the business loves and helps most, then say no to others.

## Key claims

- Alex says avatar narrowing is one of the biggest value unlocks in businesses he buys.
- Start with customers already sold and identify the top 10-20% by outcome.
- Find what those best customers have or lack that others do not.
- Useful filters include demographics, psychographics, geographics, and activities.
- A business should select one to four distinguishing characteristics.
- Narrowing the avatar allows more specific messaging, higher conversion, and higher
  pricing.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Look at all the customers that you have sold."

> "What do all these people have in common?"

> "Be willing to say no to people who aren't that."
