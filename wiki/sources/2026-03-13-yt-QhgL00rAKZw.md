# Helping a Medical Practice Get to $10M/year

## Summary
Live coaching clip. A physician (Dr. Anne Trunk) running a sexual-health medical practice at $3.8M/year wants to reach $10M+. Her in-office treatment is the core high-ticket offer ($15k–$20k); traffic is almost all organic YouTube (150k subscribers). After a prior conversation with Alex she added an application funnel with qualifying/disqualifying questions feeding a calendar to her sales team — but she reports she now has "too few" qualified people on calls and asks whether the application process is broken. Alex's first move is **diagnostic reframing**: he walks her through the input/output logic until she concedes that adding friction *raised the percentage* of qualified bookings and *raised the close rate* (quality up), while *lowering the absolute number* (volume down). His verdict: "mission accomplished" — the sales process is fixed; the remaining problem is a **traffic problem**, not an acquisition-funnel problem. He then prescribes two levers to raise absolute throughput without sacrificing quality. **Lever 1 — better-targeted content (raise traffic quality):** figure out which content attracts the right avatar by (a) emailing existing customers with a prize/giveaway incentive to reply which content bucket they came in on, and (b) adding a "what video got you here?" question on the sales call. He illustrates with his own audience: broad mindset videos get more views but a narrower "cash guys" business-breakdown format fills a room of $4M/year operators — quality over quantity. **Lever 2 — paid ads (raise traffic quantity):** solicit the same audience more times in more places. YouTube retargeting is low-hanging fruit (nudges people who already clicked) but the real unlock is paid ads on Meta and/or YouTube. He predicts Meta will be easier to crack even for a YouTube-native creator because those viewers also have Instagram/Facebook accounts; her prior failed ad test doesn't count because it ran before the friction-adding funnel fix, i.e. open targeting into a leaky process. On creative: use **save-worthy over share-worthy** content — process/list/step-teaching shorts have the highest purchase intent; pull the top ~10% by saves-to-likes ratio over the last year and run those as Meta ads down the now-fixed funnel. Since clients fly in from all over, he greenlights running it as a **national play**. He also flags her Google-ad shutdown (sexual-health content flagged) and advises auditing the page for compliance before relaunching. Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex reframes the client's complaint: adding friction that *raises the qualified percentage and close rate* while *lowering absolute volume* is not a broken funnel — it is "mission accomplished" on quality; the leftover issue is a traffic problem, not an acquisition problem.
- As of March 2026, he insists on separating percentage from absolute numbers before solving: if half of applicants self-disqualify, the qualified *percentage* of remaining bookings goes up even as the *absolute* count falls — you must "talk about reality" and agree on which metric moved before diagnosing.
- As of March 2026, once the throughput quality is fixed, the growth move is to drive more volume into the front end so the higher-quality-percentage process yields a higher absolute number of qualified sales.
- As of March 2026, to raise traffic *quality*, identify which content attracts the target avatar by emailing existing customers with a prize/giveaway to get them to name the content bucket they came in on, and by adding a "what video got you here?" question on the sales call.
- As of March 2026 (his own example): broad mindset content gets more views but converts a smaller share of high-value buyers; a narrower business-breakdown format ("cash guys" videos) draws fewer views yet fills a room averaging ~$4M/year revenue — narrower content trades quantity for quality.
- As of March 2026, to raise traffic *quantity*, solicit the audience more times in more places by running paid ads; YouTube retargeting is low-hanging fruit (nudges people who already clicked), but the real unlock is paid ads on Meta and/or YouTube.
- As of March 2026, Meta is likely easier to crack than YouTube ads even for a YouTube-native audience, because those same viewers also have Instagram and Facebook accounts.
- As of March 2026, a prior failed Meta-ad test should be discounted if it ran *before* the funnel had qualifying friction — that was open targeting into a process with no friction, so it doesn't invalidate paid ads now that the funnel is fixed.
- As of March 2026, the best ad creative is **save-worthy rather than share-worthy**: process/list/step teaching content that did "medium well" typically carries the highest purchase intent; select the top ~10% by saves-to-likes ratio over the last year and run those as ads.
- As of March 2026, for a high-ticket service whose clients travel in, ads can be run as a national play rather than geo-restricted.
- As of March 2026, a sexual-health page shut down by Google's ad platform should be audited for compliance and relaunched, since retargeting warm clickers is valuable low-hanging fruit.
