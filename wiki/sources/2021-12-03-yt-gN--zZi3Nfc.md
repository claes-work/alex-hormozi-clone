# Why entrepreneurs stay broke and sad [trigger warning]

## Summary

Alex argues that many entrepreneurs already know the tactics but do not have enough hunger to do the obvious work consistently. He uses gym-owner examples to show how people make money only under pressure, then stop once they feel relief. His sharper point: if a founder only wants enough to live on, they will stop at comfort.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2021-12-03)

- Alex says tactics stop mattering when the owner is no longer hungry enough to do obvious work.
- He criticizes gym owners who restrict sales availability to one inconvenient hour and then complain about show rates.
- He says people often already know what to do and should instead study why they are not doing it.
- He says many owners work hard only when payroll, family, or crisis forces the issue.
- He says making enough to live on creates relief, and relief can kill the drive to keep growing.
- He self-reports that fear and insecurity, not money, were major internal drivers for him.
- He says if the goal is only to satisfy one person or one family, the business will stay small.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You already know how to make money."

> "Figure out why you're not hungry."

> "If you're only trying to make enough to live on..."
