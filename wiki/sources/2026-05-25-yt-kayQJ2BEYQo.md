# The #1 Trait That Almost Guarantees Success

## Summary

Alex Hormozi argues that consistency is one of the hardest and most powerful virtues
because people are attracted to novelty. He says people should stop complaining about
results they did not earn from work they did not do, then illustrates consistency with
diet adherence, content, punctuality, side hustles, and Gym Launch's value of doing the
boring work.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- People often complain about missing outcomes despite not doing the required work.
- Consistency is difficult because humans are attracted to novelty.
- Asking "did you follow the plan?" is too vague; ask how many exact meals/actions were
  completed out of the planned total.
- The same consistency test applies to diet, content, showing up to work, and side-hustle
  time.
- One useful "muscle" is learning to do the same thing repeatedly.
- Alex says Gym Launch had a value around doing the boring work because boring work makes
  people rich.
- Boring work includes follow-up sequences, repeated split tests, and meeting preparation.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Stop complaining about the results you didn't get from the work you didn't put in."

> "Boring is what makes you rich."
