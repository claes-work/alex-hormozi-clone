# How to Turn Your Content Into a Business

## Summary

Alex says creators should choose based on their goal. If the goal is making content, keep
improving content. If the goal is making money, learn the money game. He applies theory of
constraints to creators: a creator who is already excellent at content but weak at
monetization does not have an editor problem; they have a monetization problem.

## Key claims

- Alex says creators should decide whether their goal is content creation or money.
- If the goal is money, he says to learn the money game rather than keep polishing content.
- He applies theory of constraints: a system grows until constrained and no further.
- He says a creator who is 97/100 on content and 0/100 on monetization should not treat
  better editors as the constraint.
- If making videos about a niche already satisfies the goal, he says the creator has won.
- If the creator wants money, they need to learn the business/monetization game.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "If your goal is to make money, then learn the money game."

> "If your goal is to make content, then... just keep making content."

> "Your constraint is not that you're a 97 out of 100 on content. It's that you're a zero out of 100 on monetization."

> "If that's the case, then you got to learn the game."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: creator constraint is monetization, not content quality; goal determines constraint; theory of constraints for creators.
