# The Real Reason Your Business Isn't Scaling (It's Not CAC)

## Summary

Alex coaches a mobile IV-drip business and says the constraint is not CAC but LTV. He
recommends shifting from emergency/survive demand to thrive demand, using Meta to
reach colder wellness buyers, turning every customer into ad creative, and converting
one-off packs into prepaid memberships.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says the business has an LTV problem more than a CAC problem.
- He recommends changing the upsell hook to ask whether the customer wants today's
  visit free, then crediting the visit toward a recurring plan.
- He says the business must move from the survive/emergency market into the
  thrive/wellness market.
- He says search and PPC capture high-intent emergency demand, while Meta can reach
  colder people who want to feel better.
- He says interruption-based demand can reduce price shopping compared with
  intent-based search demand.
- He recommends switching from six-packs to memberships, while still collecting cash up
  front through prepaid quarters or longer commitments.
- He says local targeting will depend more on messaging than platform targeting.
- He says the current business should be treated as an experiment until the model is
  nailed; marketing exists to create reps for the sales motion.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I don't think you have a CAC issue."

> "You have to get out of the survive business and into the thrive business."

> "The business as it currently exists is an experiment until you get it right."
