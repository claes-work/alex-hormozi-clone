# Why I Tell Beginners to Cold Email Before Running Ads

## Summary

In this tactical Q&A, Alex Hormozi compares cold email/outbound, Meta ads, Google ads, and content for fastest early ROI. He says ads are fastest if skill is already maxed, but outbound is the better beginner play because it teaches volume and selling to cold prospects. He recommends maintaining enough content to look alive and nurture outbound leads, while learning to sell warm leads as if they were cold so the process remains rigorous.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Ads can be fastest when the operator already has skill, but that assumption is unrealistic for many beginners.
- Outbound is the beginner's play because it teaches volume and cold selling.
- Baseline content should make the founder look alive and credible; early content is lead nurture, not necessarily top of funnel.
- Social profiles increasingly function as the default website prospects check before responding.
- Warm traffic can spoil sellers because it closes despite weak process.
- Learning to sell cold makes warm conversion stronger; treating warm leads like cold leads maximizes conversion.
- A warm buyer will not be bothered by the proof and framing needed by a cold buyer.
