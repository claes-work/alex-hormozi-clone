# I make $50M/Year Selling Supplements but I Feel Lost With My Messaging

## Summary
A doctor who sells genetic reports and supplements (≈$51M/year, ~22% annual growth, distributed ~22% retail / 40% wholesale / rest Amazon, grown almost entirely by word of mouth despite ~$50–60K/month on Google ads) tells Alex he feels lost on messaging: he has a "headful" of genetic knowledge and treats many conditions (infertility, pregnancy, neurotropic issues, depression, anxiety, ADHD) and wants a single unified message to hand his team and a director of marketing. Alex's core diagnosis is that the member is not actually confused — his message is already clear and can be "chunked up" into a simple frame: your problems come from your genetics/genetic vulnerabilities interacting with environment and lifestyle; we test your genes, three things influence them, you control some, and we add the supplement. Alex reframes the ask (the member wants clarity/unified message, not to niche down) and prescribes a concrete build: a quiz-based decision-tree funnel that routes people through the ~10 most common issues into videos and then product recommendations — cheap and fast to build ("an afternoon," a jot form with quiz questions). For traffic and scale, Alex prescribes recruiting "scientistic" health-and-wellness influencers as affiliates, using the genetic test as a low-barrier front-end offer, and giving affiliates 100% of the front-end profit so they push it hard while the business monetizes through back-end segmentation/follow-up sequences and ancillary word-of-mouth. The clip closes with Alex's standard promo for his free 10-stage zero-to-$100M+ roadmap at acquisition.com/roadmap.
