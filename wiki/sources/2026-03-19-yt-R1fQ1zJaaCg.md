# The 3 Ways to Build a Business

## Summary
Short monologue laying out three ways to build a business. (1) Copy what's already working and do it yourself — "a wildly underestimated path"; just pick one, start, and learn from there. (2) Have a strong advantage in *one* of the two levers — acquiring customers cheaper *or* delivering better — in which case you don't need much advantage in the other. (3) Have an advantage so strong in one lever that it overcomes a deficit in the other. He notes this asymmetry: a true product-driven business can be poor at customer acquisition yet solve acquisition anyway by making the product so good it goes viral — but he doesn't believe the reverse works (you can't overcome a weak product purely with unbelievable distribution). Ends with the roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex names three ways to build a business: (1) copy what's already working and do it yourself; (2) hold a strong advantage in one of the two levers (cheaper customer acquisition *or* better delivery); (3) hold an advantage strong enough in one lever to overcome a deficit in the other.
- As of March 2026, he calls "just look at what's already working and do that for yourself" a "wildly underestimated path" — pick one, start, and learn.
- As of March 2026, he frames the two core levers as customer *acquisition* (cheaper) and *delivery* (better); a strong edge in one means "you don't really need much of an advantage in the other."
- As of March 2026, the asymmetry claim: a true product-driven business can be weak at acquisition yet solve it "vis-à-vis a better product" (so good it goes viral) — but the reverse doesn't hold: you *cannot* do the same "by just having unbelievable distribution" over a weak product.
