# How a 22 Year Old Built a $2.4M Pizza Shop

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a 22-year-old operator of a single pizza-and-ice-cream shop doing about $2.4M with a 30% profit margin. The owner is considering opening a second location. Alex says the owner is likely on the right path but should "nail then scale": install tracking and data before expansion, give the GM profit-aligned incentives, and test whether the first location can sustain or grow for three to six months without the owner before committing to location two.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- For single-location brick-and-mortar businesses, the rule is "nail then scale."
- Before adding locations, the owner should identify unmaxed levers at the existing location, including pricing and data/tracking.
- Tracking should be installed early because each later decision without data carries higher stakes and is harder to untangle.
- Privately owned brick-and-mortar expansion usually needs a manager with profit share.
- A general manager's all-in target earnings should be high enough to attract someone who can truly run the store.
- The litmus test for opening another location is whether the current location sustains or grows for three to six months while the owner is effectively absent.
- Overexpansion is hard to undo because problems appear in both locations at once.
- If taking an extra year now creates a replicable model, it can save years later.
