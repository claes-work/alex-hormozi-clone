# Helping a $300K Coaching Business Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches a salon-business consultant whose derivative Profit First book already
attracts clients. He says the business model is scalable and the constraint is
acquisition, not the model. He recommends a book funnel: ads to the book, a thank-you
page, a low-pressure call to apply the relevant chapters, and testimonial-driven hooks
from clients' worst pre-solution moments.

## Key claims

- Alex says the founder's model is scalable and the real problem is no acquisition.
- He says a Profit First for Salons book "screams book funnel" because the book creates
  a brand halo and makes acquisition easier than a cold call funnel.
- He recommends ads to the book, then a thank-you page call offer that helps the buyer
  apply the right chapters in the right order.
- He says hooks should come from the client's highest-pain moment before working with
  the company.
- He says the owner should collect both internal life-change stories and external stats
  from current customers.
- He says books often have poor direct return but strong brand-building power.
- He says a $30K advice/coaching offer with about $90K three-year LTV should support
  aggressive acquisition spend.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "This just screams book funnel."

> "The book is the front end."

> "Selling $30,000 advice is very scalable."

Synthesis candidate: book funnel for coaching acquisition; hook from highest-pain moment; books as brand halo despite poor direct return; high-LTV advice supports ad spend.
