# Your ADHD Isn't the Problem, Your Environment Is

## Summary

Asked whether to medicate a recent ADHD diagnosis or rely on willpower, Alex explicitly
says he is not a doctor and leaves medication to the individual and their qualified
medical guidance. His non-medical focus is environmental design: reduce competing stimuli
until the intended task is the most interesting remaining option, exercise, and begin the
work rather than waiting for perfect conditions.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex frames intense focus associated with ADHD as potentially useful
  in the right context, based on personal observation rather than clinical evidence.
- He explicitly says he is not a doctor and does not decide whether the questioner should
  use medication.
- Alex says his own response to easy distraction is removing stimuli from the work
  environment.
- He defines focus as subtraction: eliminate alternative activities so the work becomes
  the most salient remaining option.
- He claims exercise and stimulus reduction may help throughput more than medication for
  some people; this is non-expert opinion and must not be treated as medical advice.
- Alex says people often delay starting longer than the task itself takes and may delay
  the first 20 hours of practice by years.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I'm not a doctor."

> "Focus is through subtraction, not addition."

> "The best way to make sure that you do nothing but the work is to make sure there's nothing else to do but the work."
