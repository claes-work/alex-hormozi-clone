# What Does Culture Mean?

★ **L3-candidate** — Introduces a compact, operational definition of company culture ("the rules, spoken and unspoken, that govern reinforcement in an organization") plus a concrete two-path method for codifying values and a heuristic for transposing culture to a new location. This framing may be novel to the persona's leadership/culture material.

## Summary

In this short solo clip, Alex Hormozi argues that most entrepreneurs use the word "culture" without being able to define it, which makes it impossible to improve. He offers his own definition — culture is the set of spoken and unspoken rules that govern reinforcement (reward, nothing, or punishment) in an organization — and reframes company values as "chunked-up rules." He then gives two methods for building a value/culture system (a thorough "codify everything" approach he prefers, and a fast "three values, describe them" approach), a practical prompt for surfacing rules (look at the conditions in which behavior occurs, e.g. rules of a meeting), and a method for transposing culture to a new location by relocating the biggest culture champion.

## Key claims

Paraphrased unless quoted. All statements Alex-attributed (solo video).

- (2026-02-26) Many entrepreneurs use the word "culture" without knowing what it means; you cannot improve something you cannot define. *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) Hormozi defines culture as the spoken and unspoken rules that govern reinforcement in an organization. *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) A "rule" is a conditional if-then statement: if you do X, you get rewarded; if you do Y, nothing happens; if you do Z, you get punished. *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) Culture is hard to transpose from one place to the next because there are so many rules (100+) governing behavior; "soaking in" a culture really means observing many rules and reinforcement conditions over an extended period. *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) People rarely codify the 100+ rules of behavior, which is why culture stays tacit and hard to move. *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) Two paths to defining culture: (1) his preferred, thorough path — codify all the behaviors that are rewarded and punished, then chunk them into ~three values/statements that, when unpacked, imply the underlying set of behaviors; (2) the fast-and-dirty path — pick three values and describe each as well as you can. *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) Values are "chunked-up rules": each value, when unpacked, should map to a specific set of behaviors that follow from believing it. *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) The easiest way to surface rules is to think about the conditions in which behavior occurs and the rules of engagement for each interaction (e.g. within a meeting). *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) Example rules at acquisition.com: if you are not talking, you are muted; talking (to someone other than the group) while in a meeting signals inattention and is close to grounds for termination. *(paraphrase)*
- (2026-02-26) To transpose culture to a new location: ensure the leader actually adheres to the rules and behaviors, and move the biggest culture champion to the new site — provided the origin culture can still reinforce itself without that leader. *(paraphrase)*
