# Closing 3.3% of Leads? Here's the Fix

## Summary

Alex diagnoses an outdoor-lighting company with Meta leads, high appointment volume, low
close rate, and one sales rep leaving. He first protects cash flow, then identifies the
real problem as close rate and offer structure, not lead volume. His fixes: confirm the
spouse will attend, add pre-frame VSLs before and during the appointment, and convert the
"featured home" offer into a giveaway with a public second-place prize that can be sold
to many people.

## Key claims

- If cash flow depends on sales capacity, a leaving sales rep is a red-alert issue even
  while the sales motion is imperfect.
- Meta leads are colder than search/referrals, but a $140 appointment against ~$5,500
  lifetime gross profit can work if the close rate improves.
- Require spouse attendance when spouse objections kill deals; confirm it in reminders.
- Use VSLs to pre-frame the offer before the visit and again while the rep calculates the
  proposal.
- A giveaway should sell the second-place prize: one winner gets the grand prize free;
  others get the same desired thing at a discount.
- State the facts and tell the truth about first-place and second-place examples.
