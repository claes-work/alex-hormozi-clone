# How to build a Skool Community with Alex Hormozi

## Summary

A direct-response promotional VSL (video sales letter) narrated entirely by Alex Hormozi advertising the "Skool Games" — a free competition/onboarding funnel that walks beginners through starting a paid online community on the Skool platform. Alex pitches the paid-community business as the fastest, lowest-skill, lowest-risk online business to start (make money in hours, no audience or expertise needed), cites a roster of member success stories and platform statistics, and closes with a free-trial call-to-action promising a live personal onboarding call. The content is sales/marketing copy, not a teaching interview.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** — the sole speaker. The entire video is his first-person monologue. All claims below are Alex-attributed.
- No guests or interviewers. The channel is @TheSkoolSchool (a Skool-affiliated channel), but the voice throughout is Alex's.

## Key claims (Alex-attributed)

- He was once stuck and afraid to fail; he only felt comfortable starting his own business after **paying another business owner to teach him** how to run a local business — and he frames the Skool Games as "repaying the favor."
- He scaled his local gym chain, then started **Gym Launch, which he sold years later for $46.2 million**.
- He claims to be **the person with the largest community on Skool**.
- A paid community is pitched as the online business that takes the **least time to start** ("make money in hours"), requires the **least skill** ("if you can invite people to a party you can make this business work"), and needs **no expertise and no audience**.
- Analogy: running a paid community is like **hosting a party** — the host just provides the place, sets the rules, and picks the theme/guests; the guests (members) create the value, and the host ("doorman") collects the money.
- Analogy: it's like **Wikipedia** — you don't need to be the expert, you just bring the people together and they become the experts.
- Claimed platform statistic: **54.1% of people who start a paid community on Skool make money** ("more than one out of two").
- Claimed statistic: the **average paid-community owner earns $1,360 per month**.
- Member success stories cited (his examples): Hakeem (ex-barber teaching barbers, $16k/mo); Levi & partner "Music Money VIP" ($30k/mo); Evelyn (234 members in first month, sourced organically from Skool's existing users); Christian (phone-flipping community); Kai (YouTube thumbnails/headlines community, $57k/mo on his first offer); Nate & brother ("anime fitness," $91k in first month); Kenneth "Creditpreneur" (credit-score community, $70k/mo); plus low-key examples — Jed (acrylic painting), Antonio (cologne for men), Ryan (a $1/month fun community with hundreds of members), and Kyle ($466/mo, never won, never top-10).
- Of the winning communities' owners he cited, **only two of them had an organic following** before starting — used as proof audience isn't required.
- Everything needed (calendars, payment processing, tech) is **built into Skool**; you can pick your community topic in **under 20 minutes**, and members can be crowdsourced from Skool's existing "millions of users."
- Offer mechanics: sign up this month → follow the process → build a paid community → if you reach the **top 10 on the leaderboard**, join Alex and other winners in **Vegas**; if you don't win, you keep competing every month.
- Risk-reversal framing: worst case you quit and "walk away with thousands of dollars worth of knowledge"; there is an **unconditional money-back guarantee** on the free trial (forget to cancel → they refund you).
- CTA: if you join this month, **Alex personally onboards you live** on a Monday onboarding call.
