# Helping a Marketing Agency Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches a small pay-per-show marketing agency. He says the model can work but the
sample size is too small, the cost per show may be too high relative to revenue, and
the agency should go upmarket rather than add sales services for weak clients. He also
warns that pay-per-show clients churn when bills are unpredictable.

## Key claims (Alex's advice, dated 2026-05-08)

- Alex says the agency needs more data because five clients is too small a sample.
- He says the pay-per-show model is not inherently flawed and has scaled for other
  agencies.
- He flags cost per show relative to revenue per show as the major margin risk.
- He says efficiency may worsen at scale, compressing margins.
- He recommends going upmarket to companies that can spend much more on ads.
- He warns against adding sales services for clients who cannot close; better clients
  should already understand that showed appointments do not guarantee sales.
- He says customers retain better when they can predict what they will pay.
- He recommends giving clients range estimates, budget discussions, and regular
  billing updates as the tab grows.
- He says not to raise prices until delivery has been stressed and proven under more
  client load.
