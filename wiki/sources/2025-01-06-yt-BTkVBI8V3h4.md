# Ditch the Broke Life. Get to Work! (2025-01-06)

## Summary

Solo talk in which Alex Hormozi argues that "unscalable" is almost always a
skill deficiency, not a physics problem — you simply lack the resources and
context early on. His core prescription for beginners: you can't be busy and
broke, so if you're broke, get busy doing the unscalable work (one-on-one
calls, onboarding, handwritten cards), because early on you learn more than you
earn, and every unscalable process has a 2.0/3.0 version you scale later.

## Key claims

- "Unscalable" is really "unskillable": unless physics literally forbids it,
  an inability to scale onboarding/outreach is a skill deficiency, not proof
  it can't scale. (paraphrase)
- Andy Frisella (First Form) started writing handwritten cards to every buyer
  and still does — the process evolved from writing, to signing, to others
  writing them, keeping ~80% of the value. (paraphrase, cites Frisella)
- You can't be busy AND broke — if you're making $0 you have the time, so get
  on the calls below your ticket price. (paraphrase)
- Early one-on-one contact teaches pain points, hooks, and product changes;
  Paul Graham's point that you can solve nearly any business problem by talking
  to customers more. (paraphrase, cites Graham)
- Two magic customer questions: which single feature they'd keep if all others
  were cut, and which one feature they'd drop with no change to their life. (paraphrase)
- Scaling path: one-on-one → one-on-six → group onboarding (1-on-30) →
  training someone else through the same deck; he learned sales via ~4,000
  one-on-one closes, not any sales course. (paraphrase, self-reported)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)
