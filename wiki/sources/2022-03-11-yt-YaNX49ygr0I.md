# "How to get what you want…" (2022-03-11)

## Summary

A solo video presenting Hormozi's keystone self-change habit: when facing any decision, ask
"what would [this type of person] do?" — a wise man, a billionaire, an ideal husband — and
act accordingly. Built on the be-do-have / identity-as-voting-system idea (crediting James
Clear's *Atomic Habits*), he argues identity drives behavior which drives outcomes, so you
should "fix for the being" and let habits follow, rather than chasing goals directly.

## Key claims

- **Winners and losers have the same goals.** Goals aren't the differentiator; the
  activities you commit to are. "Committing to the activities — that is the goal." (paraphrase)
- **The single hack: "what would this type of person do?"** A repeatable refrain (his own
  is "what would a wise man do?" / "what would a billionaire do?") beats memorizing a
  50-point checklist. (paraphrase)
- **Identity is repeated beingness.** He unpacks the etymology (entity = being; identical =
  repeated) to argue actions literally become who you are, so changing actions changes
  identity — a self-reinforcing loop. (paraphrase)
- **Affirmations don't work; evidence does.** Saying "I am a lion" changes nothing; you must
  give yourself evidence via activity that you are the type of person you claim to be. (paraphrase)
- **We need reminding, not teaching.** Most people already know what to do and aren't doing
  it; attacking *why you're not doing it* is more fruitful than seeking secrets. "Do the
  uncommonly obvious thing for an inordinate period of time." (paraphrase)

## Verbatim quotes (voice training data)
