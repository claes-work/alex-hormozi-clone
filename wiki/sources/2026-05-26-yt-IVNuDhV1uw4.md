# Why Your Sales Team Tanks After Every Big Month

## Summary

Alex Hormozi diagnoses a real-estate wholesaler's post-big-month sales crashes as a sales
operations and culture problem. If lead quality is stable but close rates swing, he says
the likely issue is script adherence, daily training, management cadence, and a leader who
allows the process to sit below individual players. He recommends making the script
mandatory, training daily, screening for coachability before hire, and treating sales
process IP as the company's trade secret.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- If the leads are the same but sales output swings wildly, the problem is likely sales
  discipline and sales operations.
- The sales manager or sales leader is accountable for the culture of consistency.
- Sales feedback loops are fast, so culture problems become visible quickly.
- Script adherence should be mandatory; a script is not a suggestion if the team wants
  consistent outcomes.
- Daily training, memorization, morning meetings, career-goal management, and consequences
  create the operating cadence.
- No player should sit above the process.
- Many sales teams create volatility by hiring closers, seeing who sells, firing the rest,
  and tolerating mercenary behavior.
- A valuable sales organization can take lower-skill but high-work-ethic people and train
  them into consistent output quickly.
- Hiring should screen for preparation and coachability before live one-on-one time:
  send the script, ask for a short video, run group drills, give feedback, and watch how
  candidates respond.
- The recruiting process itself sets the frame for the company's discipline and standards.
