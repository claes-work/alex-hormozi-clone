# You Don't Need to Write Your Goals Down If You Can't Stop Thinking About Them

## Summary

Asked when he began consciously planning his life, Alex says he did not tie ambitions to
age. He recalls that a former girlfriend predicted he would reach a financial goal
because it was all he talked about. He says he rarely writes goals down because he
thinks about little else, while cautiously declining to claim that this is universally
right or wrong.

## Key claims

- As of April 2026, Alex says his goals were organized around desired outcomes rather
  than age-based milestones.
- He self-reports that, before meeting Leila, a girlfriend observed that he was likely
  to achieve his financial goal because it dominated his conversation.
- Alex says he has rarely written down his goals because they remain continuously in
  his attention.
- He does not present his approach as a universal prescription, but says persistent
  focus likely raises the probability of achieving a goal.
- The exact financial goal in the anecdote is uncertain: Alex recalls it may have been
  $100,000 but explicitly says he cannot remember.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "I actually really haven't written many of my goals down at all."

> "It's just that I literally think of nothing else."

> "Is that the right way? Is that the wrong way? I have no idea."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: goals held through persistent attention rather than age milestones or written reminders.
