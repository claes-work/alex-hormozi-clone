## Summary

Short-form clip in which Alex Hormozi answers the question of how important raw
intelligence is to entrepreneurial success. His core argument: intelligence
matters mostly at the extreme high end (e.g. building an AI company), and even
there its value is primarily in **recruiting** — being smart enough that a
top engineer respects you — not in doing the technical work yourself. For the
vast majority of businesses (his recurring examples: lawn care, gyms), you can
succeed without being "brilliant" or above-average IQ. He argues the traits
that actually matter more are **courage**, **high agency** (making your own
decisions without being swayed by others), and **perseverance**. He frames
entrepreneurship with a lottery-ticket metaphor: you pay in time to get
effectively unlimited tickets, most people never cash any in, and those who
persevere get enough "shots on goal" that eventually one big win lands and
changes their life. The clip ends with a CTA for his free acquisition.com/roadmap
(0–100M, 10-stage) lead magnet.

★ **L3-candidate** — contains a durable, clearly-stated view (intelligence is
overrated vs. courage / high agency / perseverance; intelligence matters mainly
for recruiting at the high end) worth promoting into `persona/beliefs.md` and a
mindset topic page.

## Key claims

_All paraphrased from Alex Hormozi, 2026-02-20, unless quoted below._

- (2026-02-20) The importance of intelligence depends on the level of success
  you're targeting; for something like building an AI company you probably need
  to be really smart.
- (2026-02-20) Even in high-intellect fields, the value of intelligence is
  mostly in recruiting — being smart enough that an engineer who couldn't do the
  work themselves is impressed by you — not in personally being the engineer.
- (2026-02-20) You can succeed in most businesses without being brilliant or
  above ~average IQ; his examples are lawn care and gyms.
- (2026-02-20) Traits people describe as more important than intelligence:
  **courage**, **high agency** (the ability to make your own decisions without
  being influenced by others), and **perseverance / the ability to endure**.
- (2026-02-20) Entrepreneurship gives you effectively unlimited "lottery tickets"
  that you buy with time; you can always keep cashing them in.
- (2026-02-20) Most people never cash their tickets in — or never even grab them
  to begin with; perseverance is what earns you more shots on goal.
- (2026-02-20) You only need one really big win to change your life forever, so
  sticking with it long enough gives you so many chances that eventual luck
  becomes likely.
- (2026-02-20) CTA: free acquisition.com/roadmap — a 10-stage map from 0 to
  $100M+ that fewer than 1% of companies finish, broken down across eight
  business functions, drawn from doing it multiple times across software,
  physical products, services, and brick-and-mortar.
