# How I Think About Goals

## Summary
Short interview clip (off-screen interviewer, marked `>>`; only Alex's words are voice data). Asked whether getting wealthy is a good goal for *everyone*, Alex rejects the framing: he "wholly rejects shoulds in general" — nobody *should* want anything; do what you want. His operative model of a goal is **action-step-based**: whatever you want (get in shape, get married, make money) "has clear action steps that you can do to pursue them," and by definition "everything that is not those steps is distracting you from them." Goals are therefore a lens for sorting activity into pursuit vs. distraction. On his own younger motivation he's blunt — he wanted to make money; Gym Launch's mission was jokingly "not be broke" (a deliberately low bar). But he stresses **sequencing**: you have to "put your air mask on before you can think really big" — he's never met anyone planning a 10-year vision while they can't pay rent, so immediate problems get solved first. This is why keeping a job can be advantageous: "it's the 5 to 9 where everyone loses everything, not the 9 to 5" — the ~8 non-work hours a day plus weekends are more hours than you spend at work, and your job isn't what's blocking you (assuming you don't already have what you want). Ends with the acquisition.com/roadmap promo.
