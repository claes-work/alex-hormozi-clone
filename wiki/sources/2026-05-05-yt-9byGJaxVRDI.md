# How to Pick the Right Price for Your Paid Community

## Summary

Alex answers a pricing question for a low-ticket, one-to-many paid community. He says a
community could plausibly sit anywhere from $9 to $99 depending on audience affinity, but
the real decision should be made by testing price points and solving for the highest
effective earnings per click (EPC), including conversion rate and churn. His practical
preference is to start lower if uncertain, then move up while the economics improve.

## Key claims

- The right price for a low-ticket community depends on audience affinity and measured
  churn, not a universal number.
- Price changes affect two variables: conversion rate and churn.
- The decision metric is effective EPC over the customer lifespan, not just initial
  conversion.
- Test upward through price points; if conversion/churn economics keep improving, keep
  raising price.
- If a later price drop is needed, let existing customers leave and come back at the new
  price rather than hiding the change.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "It's going to depend on the affinity of the audience with you."

> "You're going to solve for the highest EPC."
