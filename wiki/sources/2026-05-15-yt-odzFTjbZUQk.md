# Don't Follow the Crowds for a Life You Actually Want

## Summary

Alex explains how his father's approval once functioned as the single dominant voice in
his head. He says his father was most proud of him when Alex was saddest, which began a
six-month journey from deciding to change his life to actually changing it. He frames
agency as compressing the time between decision and action, and says the first big wall
creates evidence for handling later walls.

## Key claims (Alex's self-report and advice)

- Alex says many people hear many judgment voices, but he primarily heard one: his
  father's.
- He says his father disapproved of the path Alex wanted to take.
- The moment his father approved of his life most was, according to Alex, when Alex was
  saddest.
- That mismatch started a six-month path from deciding to change his life to changing it.
- Higher agency compresses the time between deciding to change something and doing it.
- Alex says he left Baltimore and did not tell his father until he was already several
  states away because he feared disapproval.
- After that first hard act, selling strangers felt easier because he had a larger proof
  point.
- The bigger the first wall someone gets through, the more evidence they have for the
  next wall.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "His approval was literally everything."

> "Maybe his approval isn't the right way to feel good about life."

> "The bigger the wall that you have to get through... the more evidence you have."
