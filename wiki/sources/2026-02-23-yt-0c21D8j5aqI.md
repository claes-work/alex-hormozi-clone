# How to Make Offers People Never Churn From

★ **L3-candidate** — concrete anti-churn mechanics: the promise↔churn trade-off, the "consumable deliverable / education-plus-news" continuity model (razor-and-blades applied to communities), and the "stay-for-peers vs. stay-for-you" qualification lens. Worth promoting into `topics/business/` retention + offers pages and `persona/beliefs.md`.

> Format note: Q&A. A second speaker (an audience member running a community) asks the questions; all framework content below is **Alex-attributed** and paraphrased unless quoted.

## Summary

Alex answers a community-operator's question about retention after a time-boxed promise ("90 days to lose 10 kg") — i.e. what keeps people past day 91. His core claim: promise strength is a trade-off. Bigger promises convert better (lower bar to say yes) but churn harder (higher expectations, more reason to leave); the theoretical zero-churn extreme is promising nothing and selling only to a rare, self-qualifying buyer. For durable retention he gives two levers: (1) **qualification** — whether members stay for each other (peer-generated value, sustainable) or for you (you must keep generating forever), and (2) **continuity via the next problem** — every solved problem creates a new problem, and naming that next problem clearly is what turns a one-time win into ongoing subscription. The strongest low-churn mechanic he's observed in high-delivery (non-high-qualification) communities is a **consumable deliverable that functionally expires**: teach the system once as a "black box," then keep them subscribed by supplying a perishable input they still need to run it — a weekly "hot list," fresh data, current news. Illustrated by a 3D-printing community at 2–3% monthly churn whose operator's team scrapes the currently top-selling 3D-printed products each week; members already know how to print, so they stay for the news. The retention formula he names: blend **education + news** into a recurring consumable.

## Key claims

All dated 2026-02-23, paraphrased (Alex-attributed):

- **Promise strength is a trade-off between conversion and churn.** The more you promise, the lower the bar to say yes (better conversion) — but higher expectations give members more reason to say no later, so they churn more. Bigger promise = converts better *and* churns higher.
- **The theoretical zero-churn extreme is promising nothing.** "Perfect world" offer: promise absolutely nothing, tell buyers to purchase only if they're a very rare type of person, and warn it may or may not suck. Someone who buys under those terms has far lower churn likelihood than someone sold "I'll save your life in 30 days," because expectations are set very low. Lowering the expectation is the trade you make to lower churn.
- **Retention lever 1 — qualification (stay for peers, not for you).** Ask whether members stay for the other people or for you. If they stay *for you*, you must perpetually generate content/value to hold everyone. If they stay *for each other*, the members do the value-generation themselves, which is sustainable. This is the "broadcast community" (you provide the value/deliverable) vs. peer community (everyone provides value for each other) distinction.
- **Retention lever 2 — continuity via the next problem.** Whatever problem the first 90 days solved, solving it creates a new problem — all problems lead to more problems. As long as you are clear about what that new problem is, that clarity becomes the basis for continuity (the reason to keep paying).
- **Consumable-deliverable mechanic (strongest observed anti-churn for high-delivery communities).** The front-end one-time thing (the 90-day course / the "system") should be a self-contained "black box": *this is the system to lose weight / fix your marriage / make money.* But the best driver of stick is to hand over that system once — they learn it — and then require a perishable input to keep running it. Analogy: you got the printer, but to run the machine you still have to keep buying ink from me (razor-and-blades applied to a community).
- **Education + news is the retention formula.** Blend a one-time education deliverable with a recurring, consumable, news-like feed (data that is fresh this week and different next week). The education is done once; the news is why they stay subscribed.
- **Worked example — 3D-printing community at 2–3% monthly churn.** The operator had already taught everyone *how* to 3D print (education, done once); his own team then scraped and identified the top-ranked, currently best-selling 3D-printed products each week. Members stayed to get that weekly news, producing 2–3% monthly churn.
- **Example inputs of consumable value:** a weekly "hot list" of new wholesaling / real-estate addresses (different this week vs. next), or the current top-selling products in a niche — perishable data the member must keep coming back for.
