# You Must Endure...

## Summary

In this short solo clip, Hormozi argues that endurance is the core skill of becoming who you want to be, and that the fastest path there is to put yourself in a situation with no choice but to become that person. He illustrates with his own gym launch — signing a $5,000 lease with only $5,000 in the bank — and frames constraints as the engine of innovation ("necessity is the mother of invention"). He closes with a promotion for a free acquisition.com 10-stage roadmap from zero to $100M+.

## Key claims

- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) When instructing new business owners starting Skool communities, the one instruction he gave them was to "learn to endure."
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) The fastest way to become the person you want to be is to put yourself in a situation where you have no choice but to become them.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) People can endure far more than they expect once they have no alternative.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) When he signed the lease for his gym, he had $5,000 in the bank and the rent was $5,000; he had never really made money before. Paying 100% of his prior job income would have covered rent with nothing left for food or anything else.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) Constraints create the innovation required to escape the constraint and the hard time — invoking the Magic: The Gathering flavor text "necessity is the mother of invention."
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) The hardships people feel today are generally much easier than what other humans throughout history have endured; they endured because the only choice was to die or endure.
- (2026-02-25, paraphrase) Promotes a free acquisition.com 10-stage roadmap from zero to $100M+ that fewer than 1% of companies finish, which he says he has done multiple times; it breaks each stage down by eight business functions — what the constraint feels like, its symptoms, and the steps taken to graduate — validated across software, physical products, service, and brick-and-mortar businesses.
