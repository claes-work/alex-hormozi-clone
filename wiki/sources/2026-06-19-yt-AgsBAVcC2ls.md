# How an Online Coach Can Scale to $1M/Month

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises an overseas-Chinese entrepreneur community founder who sold founding lifetime memberships and wants to reach $1 million per month. Alex explains that education has high one-time value but poor continuity unless the business creates recurring consumable value, such as fresh tested inputs, deal flow, news, discounts, access, or network effects. When asked what it would take to become Alex's Chinese partner, Alex says the person would need bulletproof credibility because the messenger is most of the context through which the message is received.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- Education has high one-time value but often drops to zero once the learner knows the thing.
- Continuity is hard when education is the primary value proposition.
- Recurring businesses need consumable value: new or time-sensitive inputs that keep being useful.
- Gym Launch stayed sticky partly by giving gym owners newly tested winning ads every month.
- Alex distinguishes the one-time "black box" system from recurring inputs to the machine.
- Niching makes recurring inputs easier because the provider can create and test specific assets for a specific audience.
- For more advanced business owners, recurring value may come from network, access, negotiated discounts, or other non-course benefits.
- A small recurring component can be priced much lower than the upfront offer if it remains sticky and delivers three to four times its monthly price in value.
- Additional members should ideally add value rather than dilute it; that is a network effect.
- A global partner would need strong proof and credibility, not merely the ability to repeat Alex's words.
- Alex says authority reduces perceived risk and cognitive load for the audience.
