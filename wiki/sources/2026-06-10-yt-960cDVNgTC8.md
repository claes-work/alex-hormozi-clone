# How to Find Motivated Sellers in a Frozen Real Estate Market

## Summary

In this coaching segment, Alex Hormozi brings in Sharran Srivatsaa to advise a Los Angeles multifamily commercial real-estate broker. The business has listings but lacks motivated sellers in a frozen market. Sharran gives most of the tactical advice: target extremely motivated buyers such as 1031-exchange buyers, build the largest community of multifamily owners in Los Angeles County, and consider recurring consulting income with discounted transaction fees. Alex adds that a gated owner community can turn podcast access into scarce status and authority.

## Key claims (attributed)

- **Sharran** says the market is hard because sellers remember higher-equity conditions and are reluctant to accept current pricing.
- **Sharran** recommends finding extremely motivated buyers, especially 1031-exchange buyers with timelines, as a workaround when sellers are not motivated.
- **Sharran** says a community of multifamily owners could become an enduring business asset and a defensible CRM.
- **Alex** says a podcast guest community can become a scarce resource: past guests get access, the host gates the room, and the broker gains status by controlling collective access.
- **Alex** says that controlling a resource of higher-status owners can flip the sales conversation because the broker is no longer just an agent asking for a listing.
- **Sharran** suggests pulling revenue forward by creating recurring consulting income and discounting transaction fees, because commission income is tied to completed transactions.
- **Sharran** says the founder's extra time should go toward the future asset he wants to be able to name in two years: the dominant Los Angeles multifamily owner community.
