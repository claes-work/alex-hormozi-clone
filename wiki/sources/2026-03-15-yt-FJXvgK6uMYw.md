# I Run a Marketing Agency. How Do I Hire Better Talent?

## Summary
Live coaching clip. The client owns a social-media agency selling to sports/entertainment whale clients (Netflix, NBC Sports; ~$12M revenue) and wants to reach $25–30M, but says the bottleneck is "a trained bench of talent" to service surging demand (sponsors like Verizon, Visa, State Farm wanting to up their social game). Alex reframes the hiring problem as a **math problem, not a manpower problem**: owners stay understaffed because they never actually calculate what a specialized hire is worth versus what it costs to acquire. He walks the numbers on the client's "director of social" role — ~$1.25M/year in gross profit per such hire (with the ~$550k pod cost already netted out), and asks the pivotal question: what would you spend one time to add $1.25M/year? He introduces the **talent-side mirror of LTGP:CAC** — lifetime gross profit per employee to **cost of acquiring talent** — noting everyone already knows LTV:CAC on the front (customer) end and the same discipline belongs on the back (hiring) end. Extending to lifetime: a 4-year tenure = ~$5M brought in, and at an 8x multiple that hire adds ~$10M of tax-free enterprise value, so he'd "spend 125k one time to add an extra $1.25M per year all day." A blue-chip recruiting firm typically charges ~30% of salary (~$60k on a $200k role), an easy trade to make $1.25M. He backs it with two stories: the **dentist** doing ~$20M/$4M profit who needed 3 specialized dentists (~$100k recruiting each, $300k total) and could pull a 2-year hiring goal into 6 months by "only" making $3.7M instead of $4M this year; and a **portfolio company that built a 50-person sales team in 90 days** by hiring 80 and keeping 50, running 15 headhunting firms simultaneously plus internal recruiters. Closing rule of thumb: for **repeated roles** (big sales / CS / editing / cleaning teams) build true end-to-end recruiting infrastructure and treat each role like a customer with an acquisition process; for a **one-off role**, just pay the money to a recruiter. Ends with the roadmap promo.

## Key claims
- As of March 2026, Alex reframes a hiring/staffing bottleneck as a math problem: owners under-hire because they "haven't done the math" on what a hire produces versus what acquiring that hire costs, not because talent is truly unavailable.
- As of March 2026, he applies **LTGP:CAC to talent** — the hiring-side mirror of front-end LTV:CAC — where the numerator is (lifetime) gross profit per employee and the denominator is the **cost of acquiring talent**.
- As of March 2026 (worked example), the client's specialized "director of social" produces ~$1.25M/year in gross profit, with the person's ~$200k comp and the ~$350k pod beneath them (≈$550k total) already netted out of that gross profit.
- As of March 2026, the right question isn't the ongoing salary but "what are you willing to pay **one time** to attract" the person who unlocks that $1.25M/year — Alex says he'd spend ~$125k one-time to add $1.25M/year "all day."
- As of March 2026, extend the value to a lifetime: at ~4-year tenure that hire brings in ~$5M, and at an ~8x business multiple adds ~$10M in **tax-free enterprise value**, which dwarfs any reasonable recruiting fee.
- As of March 2026, a blue-chip / "blue chip" recruiting firm typically charges ~30% of salary (~$60k to place a $200k role); paying $60k to secure $1.25M/year in gross profit is an obvious yes "as long as you're profitable."
- As of March 2026 (dentist story), a practice at ~$20M revenue / ~$4M profit making ~$700k gross profit per dentist could hire 3 needed dentists at ~$100k recruiting each ($300k total) and pull a 2-year hiring goal into ~6 months — trading making $3.7M instead of $4M this year for the acceleration.
- As of March 2026 (portfolio story), his team built a **50-person sales team in ~90 days** by hiring 80 and keeping 50, spinning up **15 headhunting firms simultaneously** plus internal recruiters to source candidates fast.
- As of March 2026, the decision rule: for **repeated/high-volume roles** (large sales, customer-service, editing, or cleaning teams) build real end-to-end recruiting infrastructure and "think about every role as a new customer that you have to build an acquisition process for"; for a **one-off, one-time role**, "I just pay the money" to a recruiter.
