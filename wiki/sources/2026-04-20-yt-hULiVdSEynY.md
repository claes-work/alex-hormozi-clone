# How to Buy Back 36 Years of Your Life

## Summary

Alex says he would rather compress 40 years of normal career work into four years and
buy back the remaining 36 years with greater resources. He recalls an early conversation
with Leila about helping people and money, arguing that making money can expand the
ability to help.

## Key claims

- Alex says a normal career contains a certain amount of work required to generate
  income, and he would rather compress that work into a shorter period.
- He frames hard early work as a way to buy back decades of freedom later.
- He self-reports that on his first date with Leila, she said she wanted to help people,
  and he asked whether making money would let her help more people.
- He says limited resources limit how many people someone can help.
- He says if someone wants to help many people, they need to learn the game of making
  money.
- He warns that working hard and smart does not mean the work will be exciting.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "Cram it into four."

> "Have the other 36 years of my life."

> "You got to learn the game."
