# Most People Will Lose Their Jobs to AI in 2026

## Summary

In this coaching Q&A, Alex Hormozi advises a founder selling fund-launch and CFO services to entrepreneurial GPs. The founder feels like the delivery bottleneck and thinks about productizing the service. Alex says 2026 requires shifting from role-based thinking to workflow-based thinking: break every function into granular actions, identify what AI agents can perform, and train clients through action-only instructions rather than long theoretical explanations.

## Speakers & attribution

- **Alex Hormozi** - the coach. The advice below is Alex-attributed and persona-eligible.
- **Coachee** - fund-launch/CFO services founder. His revenue, target customer, delivery bottleneck, and GP-training work are context only.

## Key claims (Alex's advice only)

- In 2026, organizations should shift from role-based thinking to workflow-based thinking.
- Future organizations will be less about org charts and more about flows.
- Every function in a business is a series of actions at the granular level.
- Entrepreneurs should break workflows down into steps so AI agents or prompts can take over parts of the work.
- Alex says Acquisition.com's media output recently tripled without added headcount because prompts now handle a meaningful share of the workload.
- Training should often skip theory and give people only the actions they need to take.
- Shortening the distance between words and actions improves comprehension and follow-through.
- Building AI agents for the repetitive parts of the fund-launch workflow is directionally correct.
