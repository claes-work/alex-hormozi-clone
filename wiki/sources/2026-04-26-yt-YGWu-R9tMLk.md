# 'Alex, how do I find my niche? It looks like I'm not good at anything.'

## Summary

Alex answers a question from someone who feels they are not good at anything. He separates
being bad at everything from not yet being good at anything immediately monetizable. His
advice is to chunk up to general skills, such as reading, writing, speaking, typing, and
learning quickly, then look for things other people want and learn those faster than they
can.

## Key claims

- Alex says the caller might be right or wrong that they are not good at anything, but the
  more useful diagnosis is that they may not yet be good at anything people want to buy.
- He frames basic capacities -- reading, writing, speaking, typing, learning -- as
  transferable skill foundations.
- He suggests moving one level closer to the market: ask what other people want, then learn
  how to provide that thing faster than they can learn it themselves.
- The implied business advice is to acquire monetizable skill through demand contact rather
  than waiting to discover an innate niche.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You probably just aren't good at something that is immediately as monetizable as you would like."

> "You're just not good at anything that people want to buy from you yet."

> "So, we just go learn that stuff."

## Notes

Synthesis candidate: niche as monetizable skill acquisition; chunk-up general skills; learn what the market wants.
