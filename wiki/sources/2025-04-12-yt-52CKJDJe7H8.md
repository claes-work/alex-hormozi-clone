# Try this 5 Step-Program to Start Your First Business

## Summary

A solo teaching video in which Alex Hormozi walks through a five-step framework (plus two bonuses) for picking and launching a first business, aimed at people who are broke or don't know what business to start. The framework moves from choosing what the business is about (the "three Ps"), to narrowing the customer avatar, to articulating the upside and downside of the offer, to combining it all into a single-sentence positioning statement. Bonuses cover the "unique mechanism" and a warm-outreach script for landing the first five customers. Throughout he promotes Skool (school.com/games), where he co-owns the platform and claims more than one in two people who start a paid community make their first dollar.

## Key claims

The five-step framework:

- **Step 1 — The What (Three Ps framework):** Almost all businesses are born from one of three sources — **Pain** (something you personally went through and overcame), **Profession** (your day job or past work / a skill people pay for), or **Passion** (what you're inherently interested in and consume in your spare time). You don't need the perfect pick on the first try; picking begins the feedback/iteration loop. Goal is to reach your "best bad idea" as fast as possible and make it "less wrong over time."
- **Step 2 — The Who:** Pick who you serve from three categories — **people like you**, **people you've helped before** (paid or as a favor), or **people who are underserved** (formal market/trend analysis). He recommends "people like you" because you already know their pains. Then build a **call-out** and narrow the **avatar** using five frames (age, gender, profession, problems/pains, interests/passions) — pick at least three. Specificity claim: the more specific the niche, the more you can charge (time-management PDF ~$19 → for salespeople ~$199 → for outbound salespeople ~$1,999 → full system for garden/power-tools outbound ~$10,000), because niche specificity means less competition ("blue ocean").
- **Step 3 — The How, Part 1 (upside / good stuff):** Describe the good outcomes using the four elements of the **value equation** written in the positive — easier, guaranteed, faster, and their dream scenario (and the way they want to get it). B2B example from Gym Launch.
- **Step 4 — The How, Part 2 (downside / bad stuff):** Motivation comes from only two levers — remove bad stuff or add good stuff. Invert the value equation: risks avoided, slowness avoided, pain/sacrifice avoided, plus things they like that they won't have to give up and things they hate that they won't have to start doing. Gather this material via the **three-letter method** / customer interviews (functions like a sales call) if you aren't the customer yourself.
- **Step 5 — Combine (the "Mr. Miyagi moment"):** Drop everything into a Mad Lib: "I help [who] get [good stuff] without [bad stuff]." Examples given. This one sentence can be used immediately.
- **Bonus 1 — Unique mechanism:** A named, proprietary process (a checklist or an ordered sequence of steps) that differentiates your solution and adds "special sauce / sexiness." Examples: P90X "muscle confusion," Weight Watchers points system. Tied to his content formula "promise, proof, plan." Name it (rhyme or alliteration).
- **Bonus 2 — First five customers (warm outreach):** Greet the person, give a specific compliment, insert your one-sentence positioning statement, then ask if they know anybody who could benefit. Do this four hours a day or until you reach 100 outreaches per day; keep going daily and you'll get five customers. Attributed to the "warm outreach" chapter of his Leads book.
