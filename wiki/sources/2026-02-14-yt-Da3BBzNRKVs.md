# How Will AI Affect Business?

## Summary

A short solo segment (framed as part of a "TLDDR") in which Alex Hormozi gives his view on AI's effect on business. His thesis: AI will **not** change the fundamentals that make business business, but it **will** change the tools available to the players — and eventually, in the future, even the players themselves. He argues opportunity is greater now than ever because AI raises the *leverage* of any opportunity (leverage = output relative to input), which is why he expects the first one-person, ten-person, etc. billion-dollar companies. He dismisses the "AI can do everything so nothing matters" argument as unproductive (you could say that about literally anything), and instead asks the more useful question: what does this knowledge actually change about my behavior? His organizing frame: "since the dawn of time it has been man-plus-tools against man-plus-tools" — right now we simply have man-plus-*better*-tools; nothing fundamentally changes until the day it becomes man against machine and the machine wins. Until then it's the same game, "just more fierce."

## Key claims (2026-02-14, paraphrased)

- AI will not change the fundamentals that make business business, but it will change the tools available to the players — and eventually, in the future, even the players themselves.
- There is more opportunity now than ever, because AI increases the *leverage* of any opportunity. He defines leverage as the difference between what you put in and what you get out; get more out for the same input and you have higher leverage.
- ★ AI leverage is why the first one-person billion-dollar companies (and ten-person billion-dollar companies, etc.) will emerge — businesses with more AI-enhanced levers will get more out for what they put in.
- The "no one will do anything because AI can do everything" argument is unproductive: taken to its extreme (AI can do anything a human can), you could say that about literally everything, so it changes nothing actionable.
- His practical test for any prediction: reverse it to "what will this knowledge change about my behavior?" — i.e. only act on framing that alters what you actually do.
- ★ Core frame: "since the dawn of time it has been man-plus-tools against man-plus-tools"; today we just have man-plus-*better*-tools. Nothing has really changed. It only fundamentally changes on the day it becomes man against machine and the machine wins.
- Until we go head-to-head against machines (and lose), it's still the same competitive game it's always been — "just more fierce."
