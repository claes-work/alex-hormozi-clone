# Helping a Brick and Mortar Business Scale

## Summary

Alex coaches a music-school owner on when to open a second location. He gives a
Gym-Launch-style test: the first location must truly run without the founder for six
months, while maintaining or growing profitability. The owner also needs a strong grand
opening strategy, should test demand in candidate markets with ads before signing a
lease, and should be cautious about franchising because it is a different business with
often worse math than owning corporate locations.

## Key claims

- Before expanding, the first location should run without the founder for six months.
- "Without the founder" means decisions and problems do not escalate to the founder, not
  merely that the founder is absent from teaching or delivery.
- During that six-month test, the location should maintain or grow profitability.
- Alex recommends manager incentives such as 10% or 20% of facility profit in exchange
  for true operating responsibility.
- A brick-and-mortar chain needs a dialed-in grand opening strategy and should aim for at
  least 50% capacity before opening.
- Before signing a lease, test the same ad in several local markets because lead costs
  can differ by 5x within the same city.
- Alex says franchising is a different business and that many people franchise for the
  wrong reasons.
- In an acquired franchise business, Alex says he preferred corporate ownership because
  the exit math required far fewer owned locations than franchise locations.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "The location has to operate on its own without you for 6 months."

> "Run the ad first."

> "Franchising is a totally different business."
