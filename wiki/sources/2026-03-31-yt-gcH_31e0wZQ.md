# Clients Find Out My Real Age and It Hurts My Business

## Summary

Alex tells a young real-estate content-agency owner that age is not the hiring constraint;
demonstrated expertise quickly overrides surface judgment. For geographic expansion, he
rejects franchising because the model needs little capital and instead proposes centralized
editing with locally hired videographers managed on clients' behalf.

## Key claims

- The owner self-reports $150,000 revenue and a $1 million goal; these figures are unverified.
- As of March 2026, Alex says prospective clients care about age only briefly before
  evidence of expertise changes the judgment.
- Franchising is primarily a capital-raising structure that trades most local upside for
  a royalty while retaining meaningful support obligations; it is unnecessary here.
- Alternative delivery models include flying clients in for a concentrated content day,
  sending a company shooter out, or sourcing local shooters while editing centrally.
- His preferred scalable option is to hire local videographers on the client's behalf,
  add a management margin, and retain control of a relationship the client finds harder
  to replace than an ordinary vendor.
- If a client cancels, the local shooter can serve a competitor, creating a credible
  scarcity and retention dynamic.

## Notable verbatim quotes

> "You just have to demonstrate expertise."

> "Franchising is just a model for raising money."
